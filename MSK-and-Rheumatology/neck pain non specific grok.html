üíä 3. Treatment (Grade I & II)
First-Line Advice & Self-Management
  • Reassurance ‚Äì Usually benign, good prognosis
  • Encourage early return to normal activities & work
  • Advise firm supportive pillow (1 pillow)
  • Avoid collars/immobilisation
  • Posture advice, ergonomic assessment if work-related
  • Heat/ice as preferred
Pharmacological
  • Paracetamol and/or ibuprofen regular (first choice)
  • Short course weak opioid (codeine or co-codamol) if needed
  • Topical NSAID (effective and safer GI profile)
  • Avoid long-term opioids and diazepam (removed 2023)
  • Consider amitriptyline or gabapentin if neuropathic features
Non-Pharmacological (Strong Evidence)
  • Physiotherapy ‚Äì multimodal (exercise + manual therapy)
  • Exercise programmes (strengthening, stretching, yoga, Pilates, Tai Chi)
  • McKenzie method or specific directional exercises if beneficial
Referral Options
  • Persistent >6‚Äì12 weeks ‚Üí community physiotherapy or pain clinic
  • Significant psychological distress ‚Üí CBT/psychological therapy
  • Work-related ‚Üí occupational health
  • Radicular symptoms ‚Üí see cervical radiculopathy topic
