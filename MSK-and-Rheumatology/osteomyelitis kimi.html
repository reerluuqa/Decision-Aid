üè• Imaging Modalities

  • Ultrasound: Drainage guidance, pediatric use
  • FDG-PET/CT: Implants, equivocal MRI
üéØ Diagnostic Criteria

  • Purulent drainage with radiographic evidence
  • Positive culture from bone/deep tissue
  • Histology showing acute/chronic inflammation

ü¶∂ Diabetic Foot Special Considerations

  • X-ray first, MRI if inconclusive
  • IV antibiotics 48h then review
  • Strict glycemic control essential

‚ö†Ô∏è Complications to Watch For
  • Drug reactions: Rash, nephrotoxicity, hepatotoxicity
  • Fracture: Risk at surgical site (3-14%)
  • Neurological: Cord compression, neuropathy
  • Growth disturbance: Children - physeal arrest
  • Recurrence: Usually within 2 years
üìà Prognosis & Outcomes
Acute osteomyelitis: >90% cure rate with prompt treatment
  • Class A host (no comorbidities): 96% success
  • Class B host (comorbidities): 73% success
  • Chronic: Complex management, 60-80% cure in specialist centers
  • Recurrence: Less likely after 2 years, but can occur decades later
