Red Flags - Immediate Action

Neurological signs: Unilateral, sudden onset with weakness/numbness
Systemic symptoms: Weight loss, night sweats, fever
Severe intensity: VAS 8-10, interfering with sleep
Duration: >6 weeks without clear cause
‚úÖ

Immediate Actions

First-line: Full blood count, liver/renal function, TSH
Topical: Menthol 1-2%, pramoxine, cooling gels
Systemic: Non-sedating antihistamines (double dose)
Refer: Dermatology if uncertain, neurology if focal

Diagnostic Flow

1. Skin examination - primary lesions?
2. Systemic review - weight loss, fever?
3. Basic labs - FBC, U&E, LFT, TSH
4. Targeted tests based on findings
5. Consider psychogenic if all negative
