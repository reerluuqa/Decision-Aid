Sudden Vascular/Visceral Event

Suspect Vascular EDS (vEDS) if patient presents with:

  • Sudden severe abdominal/flank pain
  • Thoracic pain (dissection)
  • Stroke-like symptoms at young age
  • Organ rupture (colon, uterus)
!

Family History of Sudden Death

Ask specifically about:

  • Aortic dissection or rupture
  • Carotid artery dissection
  • Spleen or bowel rupture
  • Unexplained sudden collapse
!

High-Risk Anatomy

Examine for:

  • Thin, translucent skin with visible venous map
  • Characteristic facial features (thin nose/lips, prominent eyes)
  • Easy, extensive bruising

üìã Overview & Definition

EDS: A group of inherited connective tissue disorders affecting collagen, fibrillin, and matrix proteins.

Clinical Triad: Hypermobility Skin Hyperextensibility Tissue Fragility

Major Subtypes Comparison

üîç Differentials

  • Marfan Syndrome: Aortic root dilation is hallmark (vs dissection in vEDS), Lens dislocation.
  • Loeys-Dietz: Hypertelorism, bifid uvula, arterial tortuosity. More aggressive arterial disease.
  • Fibromyalgia: Widespread pain without the tissue fragility or hypermobility signs.

ü©∫ Management Approach

Immediate / Acute

  • Protect unstable joints (splinting if needed)
  • Avoid unnecessary invasive procedures if vEDS suspected
  • Cardiovascular workup (Echo, MRI) for vEDS/cEDS

Long-term / Multidisciplinary

  • Physiotherapy: Proprioceptive training & strengthening (focus on stability not hypermobility).
  • Pain Management: Avoid opioids if possible; consider neuropathic agents.
  • Wound Care: Close tension-free, deeper sutures, leave longer.
  • Lifestyle: Avoid contact sports, heavy lifting, straining.

‚ö° Systemic Red Flags

Cardiovascular (POTS/Dysautonomia)
Tachycardia >30bpm on standing, palpitations, orthostatic intolerance. Treat with fluids, salt, compression.
Gastrointestinal
Gastroparesis, IBS, functional dysmotility. Exclude perforation in vEDS.
Craniocervical Instability (CCI)
Headaches, "heavy head", dysphagia, nystagmus. Requires flexion/extension imaging.
Gynecological
Dysmenorrhea, pelvic floor dysfunction, higher miscarriage rate.
