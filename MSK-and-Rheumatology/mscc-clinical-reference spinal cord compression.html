Malignant Spinal Cord Compression Clinical Guide

MEDICAL EMERGENCY

Acute spinal cord compression is a time-critical condition. Immediate diagnosis and treatment are essential to prevent irreversible neurological damage.

Red Flags - Urgent Assessment Required

Back Pain (>95% of patients)

Often first symptom. Worse at night, worse when lying down, worse with coughing/straining. Present weeks before neurological symptoms.

Motor Weakness / Paralysis (35-85%)

Limb weakness, unsteady gait, difficulty walking/standing/transferring. Worsens over days to weeks.

Sensory Loss / Paresthesias (~60%)

Numbness, tingling, pins and needles, loss of pinprick/temperature/vibration sense. Often in toes and fingers.

Autonomic Dysfunction - Bladder/Bowel

Late sign but serious. Urinary retention, incontinence, constipation. Rarely the only symptom.

Hyperreflexia + Babinski Sign

Early neurological signs. Check Oppenheimer or Chaddock signs if patient tickle-sensitive.

Emergency Actions - Time Critical

1

MRI Whole Spine Within 24 Hours

Gadolinium-enhanced MRI preferred. If contraindicated, use CT myelography. Image entire spine - multiple lesions common.

2

Dexamethasone Within 12 Hours

Reduce tumor-associated edema and prevent further neurological deterioration.

Dexamethasone 10mg IV loading, then 16mg/day PO/IV in divided doses
3

Multidisciplinary Team Referral

Urgent referral to neurosurgery, radiation oncology, and medical oncology for definitive treatment planning.

4

Spinal Stability Assessment

Calculate SINS score (Spinal Instability Neoplastic Score). Score >7 requires surgical evaluation.

Treatment Algorithm

1

Start Corticosteroids

Dexamethasone as soon as MSCC suspected. Continue until definitive treatment begins, then taper gradually.

2

MRI + Diagnosis Confirmed

Whole spine MRI with gadolinium. Extradural tumor causing cord compression visualized.

3

Assess for Surgery

Indications: Life expectancy >6 months, MRC grade ‚â•3, spinal instability, radio-resistant tumor, need for tissue diagnosis.

4

Definitive Treatment

Surgery + RT: Decompression, stabilization. RT alone: Radiosensitive tumors, poor surgical candidates, limited prognosis.

5

Supportive Care

VTE prophylaxis, bladder/bowel management, pressure ulcer prevention, rehabilitation.

Prognosis - Key Predictors

Favorable Prognostic Factors

  • Ambulatory status at presentation (strongest predictor)
  • Duration of symptoms < 24 hours before treatment
  • Limited metastatic disease
  • Slow progressive deficit vs. sudden onset
  • Radio-responsive primary tumor

Differential Diagnosis - Important Mimics

Condition Key Differentiating Features Key Tests
Spinal Epidural Abscess Fever, IV drug use, diabetes, recent spinal surgery. Often has elevated inflammatory markers. Gadolinium-enhanced MRI shows abscess; blood/CSF cultures positive
Osteoporotic Fracture Kyphosis, loss of height, minimal trauma. No significant neurological deficit usually. MRI shows hypointense fracture (vs hyperintense tumor); wedge fracture on X-ray
Transverse Myelitis Prodrome of viral illness 1-4 weeks prior. Often bilateral leg weakness. Arms involved in minority. MRI shows focal demyelination; serum aquaporin-4 antibodies often positive
