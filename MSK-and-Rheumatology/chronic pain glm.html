Chronic Pain Quick Reference

Clinical Assessment & Management Tool
Last Revised: January 2025

RED FLAGS - Urgent Referral Required

Cauda Equina Syndrome

  • Saddle anesthesia
  • Urinary retention
  • Fecal incontinence
  • Bilateral leg weakness

Cancer Indicators

  • Unexplained weight loss
  • Fever, sweats, chills
  • History of cancer
  • Pain that worsens at night

Infection Signs

  • Fever, sweats, chills
  • Immunosuppression
  • History of IV drug use
  • Localized tenderness

Other Serious Conditions

  • Giant cell arteritis
  • Subarachnoid hemorrhage
  • Multiple myeloma
  • Bone/soft tissue sarcoma
Assessment Approach

Quick Assessment

Identify red flags first, then conduct person-centered biopsychosocial assessment

1 Pain Characteristics: Location, intensity, type
2 Impact Assessment: Daily activities, sleep, work
3 Psychological Factors: Mood, coping, trauma
4 Social Context: Relationships, work, housing
Definition & Classification

Chronic Pain: Pain that persists or recurs for more than 3 months

Primary Pain
Secondary Pain

Chronic Primary Pain: Pain in one or more anatomical regions with significant emotional distress or functional disability

  • Chronic widespread pain (CWP)
  • Complex regional pain syndrome (CRPS)
  • Chronic primary headache/orofacial pain
  • Chronic primary visceral pain
  • Chronic primary musculoskeletal pain

Chronic Secondary Pain: Pain where an underlying condition is identified

  • Chronic cancer-related pain
  • Chronic post-surgical/post-traumatic pain
  • Chronic secondary musculoskeletal pain
  • Chronic secondary visceral pain
  • Chronic neuropathic pain
Risk Factors
Demographic: Female sex, age >45, lower socioeconomic status
Lifestyle: Smoking, alcohol use, inactivity
Clinical: Multimorbidity, psychological distress, sleep disorders
Occupational: Prolonged standing, repetitive movements
Protective: Active coping strategies, good social support
Investigations

Investigations should be guided by history and examination findings:

Blood Tests: FBC, ESR/CRP, thyroid function
Imaging: X-ray, ultrasound, MRI (as indicated)
Cancer Screening: When red flags present
Common Causes of Chronic Pain
Category Conditions Key Features
Musculoskeletal Osteoarthritis, Rheumatoid arthritis, Low back pain Pain with movement, localized tenderness
Neurological Neuropathic pain, Post-herpetic neuralgia, Trigeminal neuralgia Burning, shooting pain, sensory changes
Headache Migraine, Medication overuse headache Associated symptoms, triggers
Systemic Cancer pain, Diabetes complications, Fibromyalgia Widespread pain, systemic features
