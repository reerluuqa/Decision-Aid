Soft-Tissue Sarcoma Quick Reference

Last updated: Nov 11, 2025

EMERGENCY ACTION

Any suspicious soft tissue mass >5 cm or deep to fascia requires immediate specialist referral and biopsy

Red Flags

Mass >5 cm

Significant indicator of malignancy

Deep to fascia

Fixed to underlying structures

Rapid growth

Increasing size over weeks-months

Pulmonary symptoms

May indicate metastasis

Key Diagnostic Steps

Cross-sectional imaging

MRI for extremities, CT for retroperitoneal

Core needle biopsy

Essential for histological diagnosis

Expert pathology review

Specialist sarcoma pathologist required

Chest CT

Mandatory for staging

High-Risk Factors

Genetic syndromes

NF1, Li-Fraumeni, Familial retinoblastoma, FAP

Prior radiation

Especially angiosarcoma, UPS, leiomyosarcoma

Chronic lymphedema

Stewart-Treves syndrome

Chemical exposure

Vinyl chloride, arsenic, thorotrast

Treatment Pathways

Extremity STS
Retroperitoneal/Abdominal
GIST

Stage I (Low Grade)

  • Primary Wide local excision with clear margins
  • Consider Observation if margins wide

Stage II (Resectable)

  • Option 1 Neoadjuvant RT → Surgery
  • Option 2 Surgery → Adjuvant RT
  • Consider Observation if <5cm with wide margins

Stage III/IV (Resectable)

  • Multimodal Neoadjuvant RT/Systemic therapy → Surgery
  • Chemotherapy Doxorubicin + Ifosfamide

Standard Approach

  • Primary Complete surgical resection
  • Consider Multi-organ resection for clear margins
  • Controversial Role of adjuvant RT

Unresectable

  • Systemic Doxorubicin-based chemotherapy
  • Clinical trials Targeted therapies based on molecular profile

Localized Disease

  • Primary Surgical resection
  • High risk Imatinib 3 years adjuvant

Metastatic Disease

  • First-line Imatinib 400mg daily
  • Progression Sunitinib or Regorafenib
  • SDH-deficient Consider alternative approaches
