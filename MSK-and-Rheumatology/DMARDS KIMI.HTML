Assessment

Patient Eligibility

Age: Adults aged 18 years and onwards

Scope: Ongoing monitoring of adults on DMARDs in primary care

Exclusions: Children/young people, pre-treatment testing (secondary care), pharmacological actions, drug interactions

Key Risks

Myelosuppression Infection Risk Hepatotoxicity Renal Toxicity Pulmonary Toxicity Retinal Damage

General Principles

  • DMARD initiation and initial monitoring by specialist
  • GPs may monitor stable patients under shared care protocol
  • Follow local guidelines where they differ
  • Trends are as important as absolute values
  • Monitor based on DMARD requiring most frequent monitoring
  • Ensure patient awareness of adverse effects

Baseline Requirements

  • Full Blood Count (FBC)
  • Renal Function (Creatinine/eGFR)
  • Liver Function Tests (ALT/AST, Albumin)
  • Blood Pressure
  • Weight (for Leflunomide)
  • Eye Assessment (for Hydroxychloroquine >5 years)

Follow-up & Referral

Urgent Referral Criteria RED FLAGS

STOP Treatment & Urgent Referral if:
WBC < 3.5 × 10⁹/L
Neutrophils < 1.6 × 10⁹/L
ALT/AST > 100 U/L
Platelets < 140 × 10⁹/L
Albumin < 30 g/L (unexplained)
Creatinine ↑ >30% over 12 months & eGFR <60
Blood pressure >140/90 mmHg (on Ciclosporin)
Urinary protein 2+ or more (persistent)
Clinical Symptoms Requiring Urgent Referral:
  • Skin/mucosal reaction (rash, ulceration)
  • Sore throat, fever, unexplained bruising/bleeding
  • Nausea, vomiting, diarrhoea, weight loss
  • Breathlessness, cough, infection
  • Peripheral neuropathy
  • Biologics: TB symptoms, heart failure, interstitial lung disease

Vaccination Schedule

Monitoring Trends

Action required for:
• Rapid fall/rise in any parameter
• Consistent downward/upward trends
• Gradual decreases in WBC or albumin
• Increasing liver enzymes
Infection Risk: Higher in first 6 months. Advise avoidance of contact with chickenpox/shingles and seek urgent advice if exposed.
