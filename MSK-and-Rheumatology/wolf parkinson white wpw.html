Key Diagnostic Factors Essential

Symptoms: Palpitations, dizziness, SOB, chest pain, syncope
Family History: 3.4% incidence in families, 0.55% in first-degree relatives
Red Flags: Syncope, aborted SCD, AF with rapid ventricular response Urgent

Risk Factors Screen

Ebstein's Anomaly: Most common association (7-20% of WPW patients) High Risk
Other Associations: HOCM, MVP, ASD, VSD, TGA, Coarctation, Dextrocardia
Occupational Risk: Athletes, pilots, bus drivers require risk stratification

Physical Examination

  • Vital signs: HR, BP (unstable if BP <90/60 mmHg)
  • Signs of heart failure or hemodynamic compromise
  • Murmurs suggesting structural heart disease
  • Signs of congenital heart disease

1st Test to Order Immediate

12-lead ECG: Look for delta waves, PR interval <120ms, QRS >110ms

Key ECG Features

  • Delta wave: Slurred upstroke at QRS onset
  • PR interval: Short (<120 ms)
  • QRS: Wide (>110 ms) with secondary ST-T changes
  • Intermittent pre-excitation: Indicates lower risk

Ongoing Management Long-term

Asymptomatic Patients

Monitoring: Every 2-3 years if low-risk features
Risk Stratification: Consider EPS with isoproterenol
Catheter Ablation: Consider for high-risk APs or high-risk occupations

Symptomatic Patients

1st Line: Catheter ablation (high efficacy, low risk)
2nd Line: Antiarrhythmic drugs if ablation fails or refused

Pharmacologic Options Maintenance

Class IC: Flecainide 50-150 mg BID, Propafenone 150-300 mg q8h (no structural heart disease)
Class III: Sotalol 80-160 mg BID, Amiodarone, Dofetilide (structural heart disease)
QTc Check: Must be <440 ms before dofetilide (<500 ms with conduction abnormality)
üìã Follow-up & Prognosis

Monitoring Schedule Routine

Post-Ablation: Visit at 3 months, then annually
Medical Therapy: Every 2-3 years if asymptomatic
Symptom Recurrence: Immediate reassessment

Complications Awareness

Sudden Cardiac Death: 0.9-2.4 per 1000 person-years untreated; accounts for 10% of SCD in youth
Ablation Risks: 4.4% overall complication rate, <1% major (AV block, tamponade, vascular)

Prognosis Excellent

Post-Ablation: >95% success rate, low recurrence
Asymptomatic Low-Risk: Excellent prognosis with monitoring
Pregnancy: Higher incidence of SVT; consider pre-pregnancy ablation

Patient Education Important

  • Recognize symptoms requiring emergency care (syncope, severe palpitations)
  • Avoid competitive sports until risk stratification complete
  • Report any recurrence of symptoms immediately
  • Understand that successful ablation is curative
