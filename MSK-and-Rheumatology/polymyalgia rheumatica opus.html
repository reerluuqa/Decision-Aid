Step 3: Treatment

üíâ

Prednisolone Reduction Schedule

‚ñº
üíä

Typical treatment duration: 1-2 years (may be 3-5 years or lifelong)

Response often rapid - symptoms resolving within 24-72 hours

Week 1-3: Initial Dose

Prednisolone 15 mg daily until symptoms fully controlled (usually 3 weeks)

Starting dose

Week 4-6: First Reduction

Reduce to 12.5 mg daily for 3 weeks

Week 7-12: Second Reduction

Reduce to 10 mg daily for 4-6 weeks

Ongoing: Gradual Tapering

Reduce by 1 mg every 4-8 weeks until stopped

Individualize based on response

Important Notes

Smaller dose reductions and longer durations may be needed to avoid relapse. Up to 50% of patients may not respond adequately within 4 weeks.

Relapse Management

‚ñº
‚ö†Ô∏è

Relapse is common but usually responds to dose increase

üîÑ If Relapse Suspected:

  1. Reassess the diagnosis
  2. Increase prednisolone to previous dose that controlled symptoms
  3. Monitor response
  4. If symptoms settle: continue on this dose, then taper more cautiously
  5. If symptoms don't settle: seek specialist advice

Prognosis

Overall prognosis is good. PMR is not associated with increased mortality, but morbidity may occur due to steroid adverse effects. Minimum treatment 9-12 months; some cases may be lifelong.
