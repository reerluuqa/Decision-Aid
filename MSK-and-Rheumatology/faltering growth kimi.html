Emergency Criteria

Admission Indicators:
  • Severe malnutrition
  • Dehydration
  • Outpatient failure
  • Safety concerns
Immediate Actions:
  • Document intake precisely
  • Multidisciplinary consult
  • Consider tube feeding
  • Child protection if neglect

Positive Indicators

Good Prognosis Signs:
  • Catch-up growth potential
  • Responsive feeding
  • Family engagement
  • No organic cause
Target Outcomes:
  • Weight gain 200-250g/week (0-3mo)
  • 100-125g/week (3-6mo)
  • 50-70g/week (6-12mo)
  • Sustained growth velocity

Diagnostic Criteria

Parameter Threshold Flag
Weight-for-age <-2 SD from median Underweight
Length-for-age <-2 SD from median Stunting
Weight-for-length <-2 SD from median Wasting
BMI-for-age <-2 SD from median Wasting

Key Risk Factors

High Risk:
SGA, Poverty, GI disorders, Poor carer knowledge
Medium Risk:
Prematurity, Lack of routine, Carer depression
Consider:
Food allergies, Autism, Chronic illness
Remember: Can occur at all socioeconomic levels

Assessment Steps

  1. Plot on correct growth chart (WHO 0-2y, CDC 2-19y)
  2. Document feeding history: 24h recall, mealtime behaviors
  3. Social assessment: Food security, family stressors
  4. Targeted investigations only (guided by history)
  5. Developmental screening (at-risk population)

Management Priority

Immediate:
  • Increase caloric density
  • Responsive feeding
  • Consistent routines
  • Family support
Follow-up:
  • Weekly weights initially
  • Multidisciplinary team
  • Transition to family diet
  • Monitor development

Pharmacological Options

Cyproheptadine:
  • Short-term appetite stimulant
  • Consider after non-pharm failed
  • Consult specialist for dosing
Tube Feeding:
  • Last resort only
  • Multidisciplinary decision
  • Severe/recalcitrant cases
