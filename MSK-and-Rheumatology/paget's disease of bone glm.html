Paget's Disease of Bone

Quick Clinical Reference Tool
RED FLAGS: Sudden worsening of pain, increased local temperature, or new neurological symptoms may indicate pathological fracture or malignant transformation (osteosarcoma). Immediate evaluation required.

Key Information

Chronic localized bone remodeling disorder characterized by increased bone resorption, formation, and remodeling, leading to deformities.

2nd most common bone disorder Prevalence: 1-2% Mean onset: 55 years

Red Flags

Sudden onset or worsening of chronic pain
Skull involvement with neurological symptoms
Spinal stenosis with neurological deficits
High-output cardiac failure (rare)
Elevated ESR with localized pain (possible osteosarcoma)

Diagnostic Criteria

Test Expected Finding
X-ray Thickened, coarse trabeculae; cortical thickening; bone expansion
Alkaline Phosphatase Elevated (85-100% of cases)
Bone Scan Intense uptake in affected areas
View X-ray Examples

When to Treat

Symptomatic patients (bone pain, deformities)
Disease in facial bones (malocclusion, deformities)
Skull involvement (cranial nerve compression)
Vertebral involvement (compression fractures)
Locations adjacent to joints (functional impairment)
Prior to surgery on pagetic bone

Treatment Algorithm

1
Observation

Regular follow-up, patient education, preventive measures

2
Monitor

Periodic assessment of alkaline phosphatase and symptoms

1
First-Line Treatment

Bisphosphonates:

  • Zoledronic acid: 5 mg IV single dose
  • Alendronic acid: 40 mg orally daily for 6 months
  • Risedronate sodium: 30 mg orally daily for 2 months
2
Alternative Treatment

Calcitonin-salmon: 100 units SC/IM day 1, then 50-100 units every 1-3 days

3
Supportive Therapy

Physiotherapy, orthoses, walking aids, hearing aids, analgesics

1
Immediate Treatment

Bisphosphonates (same as symptomatic patients)

2
Close Monitoring

Regular assessment of symptoms and bone turnover markers

3
Specialist Referral

Consider referral to endocrinology or orthopedics for management

Monitoring

Marker When to Check
Alkaline Phosphatase After 6 months of treatment, then every 6-12 months
Bone-specific Alkaline Phosphatase If available, same schedule as above
P1NP For localized disease with normal alkaline phosphatase
Symptoms At each follow-up visit

Complications

Pathological Fractures
Common in weakened pagetic bone, especially femur/hip or tibia
Arthritis
Secondary to mechanical changes around joints
Hearing Loss
Common in skull involvement, often irreversible
Spinal Stenosis
Due to narrowing of spinal canal or blood-supply-steal mechanism
Osteosarcoma
Rare but serious complication

Clinical Pearls

Ensure calcium and vitamin D levels are normal before bisphosphonate treatment
Consider bisphosphonates prior to surgery on pagetic bone to reduce bleeding
Pain may initially worsen when therapy is initiated due to increased metabolic activity
Advise patients to avoid high-impact activities to prevent pathological fractures
Early use of hearing aids is recommended for patients with hearing deficits
Family history is significant - first-degree relatives have up to 7x increased risk
