Raynaud's Phenomenon

NICE CKS Management Algorithm (Oct 2024 Update)

Assessment & History
[cite_start]

Goal: Suspect diagnosis if skin color changes in digits are precipitated by cold, stress, or emotion[cite: 193]. Differentiate Primary from Secondary causes.


Check Clinical Features

    [cite_start]
  • Symptoms: Cold sensitivity, color changes (White → Blue → Red), numbness, paresthesia, pain on rewarming [cite: 197, 211-213].
    • [cite_start]
  • Distribution: Usually digits; can affect nose, ears, lips[cite: 198].
    • [cite_start]
  • Triggers: Cold, emotion, vibration, drugs, injury[cite: 216].
    • [cite_start]
  • Photos: Ask patient to photograph attacks[cite: 223].

Assess for Underlying Causes

    [cite_start]
  • Connective Tissue Signs: Sclerodactyly (tight skin), calcinosis, telangiectasia, rashes, joint swelling/stiffness, heartburn/dysphagia [cite: 215, 225-227].
    • [cite_start]
  • Examination: Check peripheral pulses (radial, brachial, feet) and BP in both arms[cite: 228].
    • [cite_start]
  • Nail Folds: Check for abnormal capillaries using a dermatoscope (early sign of systemic sclerosis)[cite: 225].

[cite_start]Investigations: Consider FBC, CRP, ESR, ANA, Thyroid function to screen for secondary causes[cite: 232]. [cite_start]If ANA positive (>1:40) or high suspicion, consider ENA testing[cite: 233].
Diagnosis Stratification

Differentiate between Primary (Idiopathic) and Secondary Raynaud's.


Primary Raynaud's Low Risk

  • Onset typically < [cite_start]30 years (teens/20s)[cite: 219].
    • [cite_start]
  • Symmetrical attacks affecting both hands (usually spares thumbs)[cite: 203].
    • [cite_start]
  • No digital ulceration or necrosis[cite: 203].
    • [cite_start]
  • Normal physical exam between attacks[cite: 222].
    • [cite_start]
  • Negative ANA / No underlying condition[cite: 205].

Secondary Raynaud's Caution

  • Onset typically > [cite_start]30 years[cite: 199].
    • [cite_start]
  • Asymmetrical, intense, or painful attacks[cite: 200].
    • [cite_start]
  • Symptoms persist between attacks (numbness/pain)[cite: 200].
    • [cite_start]
  • Digital pitting, ulceration, or abnormal nail fold capillaries[cite: 202].
    • [cite_start]
  • Signs of connective tissue disease (Systemic Sclerosis, SLE, RA, etc.)[cite: 119].
[cite_start]Differentials: Chilblains, Acrocyanosis, Erythromelalgia, Frostbite, Vibration White Finger [cite: 240-256].
Triage: Urgent & Specialist Referral

Urgent / Emergency

Refer to Vascular Surgery or Rheumatology ASAP (Admission may be required) if:
    [cite_start]
  • Severe critical digital ischaemia (persistent color change/severe pain)[cite: 269].
    • [cite_start]
  • Digital ulceration, necrosis, or gangrene[cite: 269].
    • [cite_start]
  • Severe tissue infection[cite: 270].
    • [cite_start]
  • Proximal large vessel occlusive disease suspected[cite: 271].

Specialist Referral

Refer to Rheumatology, Occupational Medicine, or Paediatrics if:
  • Children: Age ≤ [cite_start]12 years (Paediatrician)[cite: 275].
    • [cite_start]
  • Teens (13-17): If considering drug treatment (Paediatrician/Rheum)[cite: 276].
    • [cite_start]
  • Secondary Causes: Connective tissue disease suspected[cite: 273].
    • [cite_start]
  • Occupational: Hand-arm vibration syndrome suspected[cite: 274].
    • [cite_start]
  • Severe/Uncontrolled: Frequent episodes or poor control despite primary care management[cite: 274, 295].
Primary Care Management

1Lifestyle & Conservative Measures (All Patients)

  • Warmth: Keep whole body warm, layered clothing, gloves/mittens, head covering. [cite_start]Use hand warmers with caution[cite: 282, 283].
    • [cite_start]
  • Triggers: Avoid sudden temp changes, vibration, cold exposure[cite: 281].
    • [cite_start]
  • Cessation: Stop smoking (refer to cessation services)[cite: 284].
    • [cite_start]
  • Diet/Stress: Reduce caffeine, manage stress/anxiety[cite: 284, 285].
    • [cite_start]
  • Medication Review: Stop aggravating drugs (e.g., beta-blockers) if possible[cite: 280].
    • [cite_start]
  • Info: Signpost to Scleroderma and Raynaud's UK[cite: 278].

2Pharmacological Treatment

[cite_start]

Indicated if symptoms are bothersome despite lifestyle measures[cite: 286].

First Line: Calcium-Channel Blocker (Adults ≥ 18)
  • Nifedipine Immediate Release: Start 5mg TDS. [cite_start]Increase slowly to max 20mg TDS[cite: 287].
  • OR Nifedipine Sustained Release (off-label): Start 10mg BD. [cite_start]Increase slowly to max 40mg BD[cite: 287].
Second Line (if Nifedipine not tolerated/ineffective)
  • Amlodipine (off-label): Start 5mg OD. [cite_start]Increase to max 10mg OD[cite: 289].

3Follow Up

    [cite_start]
  • Monitor response and adverse effects[cite: 290].
    • [cite_start]
  • Primary Raynaud's: Consider intermittent use (e.g., winter only)[cite: 291].
    • [cite_start]
  • Periodically trial stopping medication (remission is possible)[cite: 292].
    • [cite_start]
  • Refer Back: If signs of ulceration, connective tissue disease emerge, or symptoms worsen [cite: 293-295].
Nifedipine Prescribing Details

Contraindications & Cautions

    [cite_start]
  • Stop if: Ischaemic pain occurs or worsens shortly after starting[cite: 310].
    • [cite_start]
  • Contraindications: Cardiogenic shock, significant aortic stenosis, unstable angina, within 1 month of MI[cite: 309].
    • [cite_start]
  • Pregnancy/Breastfeeding: Avoid (inhibits labour / manufacturer advises avoid in breastfeeding)[cite: 311, 312].
    • [cite_start]
  • Caution: Severe hypotension, heart failure, hepatic impairment (dose reduction), elderly [cite: 313-315].

Adverse Effects & Interactions

    [cite_start]
  • Common SEs: Headache, flushing, dizziness, oedema, palpitations, constipation[cite: 316].
  • Interactions:
      [cite_start]
    • Increased levels: Macrolides, protease inhibitors, diltiazem, grapefruit juice (avoid)[cite: 319, 322].
      • [cite_start]
    • Decreased levels: Rifampicin, carbamazepine, phenytoin[cite: 320].
      • [cite_start]
    • Hypotension risk: Alcohol, ACEi, diuretics, beta-blockers, sildenafil[cite: 321].
