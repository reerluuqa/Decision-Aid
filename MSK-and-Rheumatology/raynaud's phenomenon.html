Primary vs Secondary RP Key Decision
Primary: No underlying disease, normal labs, age <30, symmetric attacks. Secondary: Associated with CTD, abnormal labs, older onset.
Laboratory Tests Routine
ANA, ESR/CRP, CBC, urinalysis. Consider anti-centromere, anti-Scl-70 if systemic sclerosis suspected.
ANA positive with nucleolar pattern?
Nailfold Capillaroscopy Specialist
Dilated/destroyed capillaries suggest secondary RP. Normal pattern supports primary RP.

Treatment
‚ñº
Lifestyle Measures First-line
Keep warm, smoking cessation, stress reduction, avoid triggers, moisturize skin, protect digits.
Pharmacological Therapy Second-line
Calcium channel blockers (nifedipine first-line), ARBs (losartan), topical nitrates, PDE-5 inhibitors for severe cases.
Digital ulcers present?
Severe Disease Management Specialist
IV prostacyclins, surgical sympathectomy, wound care, pain management, treat infections.

Monitoring Schedule Routine
Primary RP: Annual review. Secondary RP: 3-6 monthly, or as clinically indicated based on disease activity.
Complication Surveillance Important
Watch for digital ulcers, infections, gangrene, progression to systemic disease. Monitor treatment side effects.

Prognosis Counseling Education
Primary RP generally benign. Secondary RP requires ongoing monitoring. 13% risk of developing CTD over time.
