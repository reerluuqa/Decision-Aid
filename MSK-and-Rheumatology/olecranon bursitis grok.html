Olecranon Bursitis Interactive Algorithm

Based on NICE CKS – Last revised May 2024 • Age ≥16 years

Assessment – When to suspect olecranon bursitis

Typical presentation

  • Swelling over the olecranon (appears over hours to days)
  • Fluctuant, movable, compressible
  • May be tender/warm or painless
  • Elbow movement painless except at full flexion
  • History of trauma (single blow or repetitive pressure) or systemic disease (RA, gout)

Red flags suggesting septic bursitis

  • Rapidly worsening pain, redness, heat
  • Cellulitis surrounding bursa
  • Fever, tachycardia, systemic illness
  • Skin breach / abrasion over bursa
  • Immunocompromised (diabetes, steroids, alcohol excess, HIV)
URGENT: If any red flag → treat as septic until proven otherwise
Diagnosis & Investigations

Differential diagnosis

  • Rheumatoid nodules (firm, other joints involved)
  • Septic arthritis (whole joint swollen, painful ROM)
  • Gout / pseudogout (previous attacks, tophi)
  • Fracture (significant trauma)
  • Tumour (rare, rapid growth, weight loss)

When to aspirate

Aspirate if:

  • Any suspicion of septic bursitis
  • Persistent/large non-septic bursitis causing functional impairment

Send fluid for: Gram stain, culture, sensitivities, crystal examination, cell count

Fluid appearance guide (not definitive):
Pus → highly likely septic
Blood-stained → trauma, gout, RA, or septic
Milky → gout/pseudogout
Straw-coloured → usually non-septic
Treatment

Confident NON-SEPTIC bursitis

  • Conservative (first-line): Rest, ice (10 min every few hours), compressive bandage, elbow pad protection
  • Analgesia: Paracetamol ± NSAID (ibuprofen ≤1.2 g/day or naproxen ≤1 g/day)
  • Aspiration if large/painful (therapeutic + diagnostic)
  • Intramuscular or intrabursal corticosteroid only if experienced and confident non-septic
  • Most resolve within weeks–months
Good prognosis with conservative measures

Suspected/PROVEN SEPTIC bursitis

  • Aspirate (before antibiotics if possible)
  • Empirical antibiotics (7–14 days minimum):
    Flucloxacillin 500 mg QDS (1 g QDS if >70 kg)
    Penicillin allergy → Clarithromycin 500 mg BD
    Pregnant/breastfeeding → Erythromycin 500 mg QDS
  • Review every 3–4 days
  • Repeat aspiration if re-accumulates
  • Continue antibiotics 1–4 weeks according to response/culture
Caution: longer duration often needed
Follow-up & When to Refer

Primary care follow-up

  • Non-septic: review if no improvement after 4–6 weeks
  • Septic: review every few days until clear improvement
  • Recheck if swelling recurs after initial resolution

Urgent admission

  • Suspected septic arthritis (painful ROM, whole joint swollen)
  • Systemic sepsis / toxicity
  • Extensive cellulitis or pointing abscess
  • Immunocompromised + severe infection

Urgent/soon referral

  • No response to oral antibiotics in septic bursitis
  • Recurrent septic bursitis → consider bursectomy after infection cleared
  • Non-septic, no improvement after 2 months of conservative ± injection
