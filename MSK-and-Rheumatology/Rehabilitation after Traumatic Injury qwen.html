Assessment

Initial Evaluation

Conduct a comprehensive assessment of the patient's physical, cognitive, psychological, and social needs following traumatic injury.

  • Evaluate injury type: musculoskeletal, visceral, nerve, soft tissue, spinal, limb reconstruction/loss
  • Assess functional limitations in mobility, self-care, and daily activities
  • Screen for psychological impact including acute stress reactions
  • Identify pre-existing conditions that may affect rehabilitation

Severity Indicators

Use these indicators to determine urgency of intervention:

Urgent Referral Caution Required Stable Condition

Key Considerations

  • Trauma affects all age groups - median age 63.6 years (TARN data)
  • Falls are the primary mechanism of injury (57.8% of cases)
  • Psychological problems commonly accompany physical trauma
  • Cognitive functioning may be impaired even without brain injury
Diagnosis

Diagnostic Criteria

Diagnosis is based on comprehensive assessment findings and established criteria:

  • Complex rehabilitation needs: Require coordinated input from ≥2 allied health disciplines
  • Traumatic injury definition: Any injury requiring hospital admission at time of injury
  • Functional impact assessment: Evaluate ability to perform ADLs and participate in society

Differential Considerations

  • Exclude traumatic brain injury as primary cause (covered in separate CKS topic)
  • Distinguish from complications of minor injuries requiring admission
  • Consider psychosocial factors affecting recovery trajectory

Severity Classification

Classify based on impact and complexity:

  • Urgent: Life-threatening complications, severe psychological distress affecting engagement
  • Caution: Moderate functional limitations, emerging psychological symptoms
  • Stable: Mild limitations, good coping mechanisms, strong support system
Treatment

Rehabilitation Plan Components

Develop an individualized plan including:

  • Short-term and long-term rehabilitation goals
  • Programme of therapies and treatments
  • Information on returning to vocational/leisure activities
  • Details of rehabilitation coordinator/key worker
  • Follow-up arrangements and review schedule

Primary Care Interventions

  • Provide educational materials on injury management and self-care
  • Discuss expected recovery pathway and potential complications
  • Offer psychological support tailored to rehabilitation goals
  • Facilitate access to community resources and support services
  • Monitor progress using patient-reported outcome measures (PROMs)

Specialist Referrals

Refer when indicated:

  • Urgent: Psychology services if psychological problems impede rehabilitation
  • Caution: Physiotherapy, occupational therapy, dietetics
  • Stable: Community falls service, peer support groups
Follow-up

Monitoring Schedule

  • Review rehabilitation prescription every 4-6 weeks
  • Functional reassessment at 2-3 months post-discharge from critical care
  • Regular psychological screening for anxiety, depression, and PTSD
  • Ongoing assessment of cognitive functioning and decision-making capacity

Evaluation Parameters

Assess across multiple dimensions:

  • Physical and sensory functioning
  • Communication abilities
  • Social care and equipment needs
  • Psychological well-being (anxiety, depression, PTSD)
  • Cognitive and behavioral functioning
  • Psychosocial adjustment and community participation

Adjustment Triggers

Modify plan when:

  • Recovery progresses slower than anticipated
  • New physical or non-physical morbidities emerge
  • Patient reports significant barriers to engagement
  • Changes in living situation or support system occur
