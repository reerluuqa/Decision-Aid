Key Presentation: Low back pain starting before age 45, lasting longer than 3 months, PLUS 4 or more additional criteria below.

üìã Diagnostic Criteria Checklist

  • Low back pain starting before age 35 (increased likelihood)
  • Symptoms waking patient during second half of night
  • Buttock pain (variable location, may alternate with hip pain)
  • Improvement with movement and exercise, not relieved by rest
  • Improvement within 48 hours of taking an NSAID
  • Family history of spondyloarthritis in first-degree relative
  • Current/past history of peripheral arthritis (asymmetrical oligoarthritis)
  • Current/past history of peripheral enthesitis
  • Current/past history of psoriasis (including nail changes)
Criteria selected: 0/9

ü©∫ Clinical Examination

  • Back pain characteristics: insidious onset, morning stiffness ‚â•30 minutes
  • Signs of inflammatory arthritis (tender/swollen joints)
  • Enthesitis (tender, stiff, or swollen Achilles tendon/plantar fascia)
  • Dactylitis (diffuse swelling of whole finger/toe)
  • Psoriasis skin lesions
  • Limitation of spinal movement (typically late stages)

üìä Routine Review Components

  • Assess symptom control: pain, stiffness, fatigue, physical function
  • Review flare episodes and current disease activity
  • Assess quality of life and psychosocial impact
  • Screen for extra-musculoskeletal manifestations
  • Review medication adherence and adverse effects
  • Cardiovascular risk assessment and management
  • Osteoporosis screening (every 2 years)
