CLINICAL PRESENTATION

Typical Features:

  • Acute onset (<2 weeks)
  • Single joint (knee most common)
  • Pain + swelling + restriction
  • Fever (60% cases)

Atypical Features:

  • Polyarticular (22% cases)
  • Prosthetic joint
  • Immunocompromised
  • Insidious onset

RISK FACTORS

Strong Risk:

  • Pre-existing joint disease
  • Prosthetic joint
  • Age >80 years
  • IV drug use

Moderate Risk:

  • Diabetes
  • Immunosuppression
  • Recent joint surgery
  • Skin infection

SPECIAL SITUATIONS

Prosthetic Joint:

  • DO NOT aspirate in ED
  • Immediate orthopedic referral
  • Requires theater aspiration
  • May need surgical management

Hip Joint:

  • Ultrasound-guided aspiration
  • Orthopedic referral
  • Consider surgical drainage
