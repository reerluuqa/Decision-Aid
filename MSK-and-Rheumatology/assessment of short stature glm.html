Short Stature Assessment - Quick Reference

Emergency Considerations

Short stature may be the presenting sign of serious or life-threatening conditions. Urgent evaluation required when:

Red Flag Conditions
  • Congenital primary hypothyroidism - cause of short stature and mental delay
  • Craniopharyngioma - diplopia, vision loss, headaches, and short stature
  • CNS malignancies - require urgent neurological and endocrinological evaluation
  • Crossing height percentiles in downward direction always needs evaluation
Immediate Action: Refer to pediatric endocrinology and arrange brain imaging if CNS tumor suspected
Urgent Considerations
  • Cardiac defects - may present with short stature
  • Inflammatory bowel disease - growth failure with gastrointestinal symptoms
  • Cystic fibrosis - recurrent respiratory infections with faltering growth
  • Chronic kidney failure - growth failure with urinary symptoms
  • Poorly controlled diabetes mellitus - polyuria, polydipsia, weight loss
Key Definitions

Short stature: Height that is two or more standard deviations (SD) below the mean for age and sex within a population (below the 2.5th percentile).

Growth deceleration: Growth velocity below the 5th percentile for age and sex (e.g., <5 cm/year after age 5), or height drop across two or more percentiles on growth chart.

Normal Variants
Condition Key Features Action
Familial Short Stature Steady growth below 5th percentile, normal puberty
Constitutional Delay Growth deceleration first 2 years, delayed bone age
Idiopathic Short Stature No organic cause, healthy but short
Genetic Syndromes
Turner Syndrome
  • 45 XO chromosomal abnormality
  • Webbed neck, short stature
  • Ovarian dysgenesis
Noonan Syndrome
  • Short stature with dysmorphic features
  • Heart defects (pulmonary valve stenosis)
  • Learning problems
Russell-Silver
  • SGA with dysmorphism
  • Triangular face, asymmetry
  • Feeding problems
Trisomy 21
  • Chromosomal abnormality
  • Hypotonia, cardiac defects
  • Delayed development
History & Examination
Key History Points
  • Birth weight, length, gestation
  • Perinatal complications
  • Medical symptoms (dyspnea, diarrhea, etc.)
  • Parental heights (calculate target height)
  • Dietary and medication history
Physical Examination
  • Dysmorphic features
  • Proportionate vs disproportionate stature
  • Weight-to-height ratio
  • Pubertal status
Growth Assessment
Accurate Measurements
  • Length supine until 2 years
  • Standing height after 2 years
  • Use appropriate growth charts
  • Calculate growth velocity

Laboratory Evaluation
First-line Tests
  • FBC
  • Complete metabolic profile
  • ESR
  • Tissue transglutaminase
  • Urinalysis
  • Thyroid function tests
  • IGF-1 and IGFBP-3
  • Karyotype in girls
Subsequent Tests
  • GH stimulation tests
  • MRI brain
  • Cortisol levels
  • X-ray wrist for bone age

Other Treatments
  • Hypothyroidism: Levothyroxine replacement
  • Celiac Disease: Gluten-free diet
  • Chronic Illness: Treat underlying condition
  • Psychosocial Deprivation: Remove from adverse environment
Monitoring & Follow-up
Growth Monitoring
  • Measure height every 3-6 months
  • Plot on appropriate growth chart
  • Calculate growth velocity
  • Assess pubertal development
Treatment Monitoring
  • GH therapy: IGF-1 levels every 6-12 months
  • Thyroid function tests annually
  • Bone age assessment annually
  • Monitor for treatment side effects
