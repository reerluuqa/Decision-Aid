Follow-up & Monitoring

‚ñº

Monitoring Schedule

1
Laboratory Monitoring: FBC, LFTs, renal function every 3-4 months during treatment
2
Ophthalmologic Screening: Every 3 months (high-risk) or 6-12 months (low-risk)
3
Disease Activity: Assess joints, inflammatory markers, patient/physician global scores

Complications to Monitor

Joint Complications

  • Joint erosions
  • Leg length discrepancy
  • C-spine fusion/C1-C2 subluxation
  • TMJ involvement

Extra-articular

  • Uveitis (10-20% risk)
  • Macrophage activation syndrome
  • Growth disturbances
  • Osteoporosis

Treatment Tapering

When to Consider Tapering: When disease is clinically inactive for at least 6-12 months
Tapering Sequence: Corticosteroids first, then biological agents, then DMARDs. Always under specialist supervision.

Prognosis

Good Prognosis Factors

  • Shorter disease duration before treatment
  • Good initial response to therapy
  • Oligoarticular subtype
  • Early aggressive treatment
2/3 achieve remission

Poor Prognosis Factors

  • RF-positive polyarticular subtype
  • Systemic-onset subtype
  • High disease activity at baseline
  • Joint damage at presentation
May have moderate-severe disability

Vaccination Guidelines

Live vaccines are contraindicated during immunosuppressive therapy. Annual influenza vaccination recommended for all patients.
