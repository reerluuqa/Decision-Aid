When to Suspect RA

  • Persistent synovitis (weeks, not days) with no obvious cause
  • Symmetrical joint involvement, especially small joints of hands/feet
  • Morning stiffness >1 hour
  • Joint pain worse at rest/inactivity
  • Boggy swelling on palpation

Poor Prognosis Indicators

  • Multiple joints affected
  • Small joint swelling/tenderness (especially PIP/MCP joints)
  • Positive MCP squeeze test
  • Inability to make fist/flex fingers

Differential Diagnosis

  • Connective tissue disorders (SLE)
  • Fibromyalgia
  • Infectious arthritis
  • Osteoarthritis
  • Polyarticular gout
  • Polymyalgia rheumatica
  • Psoriatic arthritis
  • Reactive arthritis
  • Septic arthritis (urgent if single hot, swollen joint + systemic signs)

Investigations

Diagnosis is clinical - no specific diagnostic test

Consider to expedite:

  • Rheumatoid factor (60-70% positive in RA)
  • Anti-CCP antibodies (if RF negative, ~80% positive in RA)
  • X-rays hands/feet
  • FBC, LFTs, renal function
  • CRP/ESR (usually elevated)
  • Ultrasound/MRI (if locally available)

Do not delay referral for investigations

Management

Suspected RA (Primary Care)

  • Consider NSAID + PPI at lowest effective dose until specialist appointment
  • DO NOT prescribe glucocorticoids before specialist assessment
  • Do not delay referral if blood tests normal/results pending

Confirmed RA (Primary Care Role)

  • Annual review
  • Comorbidity screening
  • Medication monitoring
  • Vaccinations
  • Patient education
  • Specialist liaison
