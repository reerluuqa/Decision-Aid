Immediate Red Flags & Emergencies
Priority 1
›
Respiratory Distress: Restrictive lung disease is a major cause of mortality. Look for pectus deformities, scoliosis >30°, and recurrent rib fractures.
›
Basilar Invagination: Headaches, numbness/weakness in extremities, or signs of spinal cord compression. Requires neurosurgical eval.
›
Perinatal Lethality (Type II): Multiple in utero fractures, severe hypomineralization, blue sclera. Counsel regarding prognosis.
›
Hyperplastic Callus (Type V): Can mimic osteosarcoma. Be aware of distinct clinical picture to avoid misdiagnosis.
Diagnostic Checklist
History/Exam
Absence of one feature (e.g., blue sclera) does NOT rule out OI.
Action Algorithm
Order Now

1. Skeletal Survey

Skull, long bones, spine, chest. Assess deformities & fractures.

Required for diagnosis

2. Genetic Testing

Massively parallel sequencing panel (COL1A1/A2 + others).

Confirm molecular cause

3. DXA Scan

Quantify bone mineral density. Only if safe positioning is possible.

Baseline tracking

4. Baseline Audiology

Essential at diagnosis, then every 3 years (annual if abnormal).

5. Spirometry & Echo

Lung function (esp. if scoliosis) and cardiac valvular assessment.

Sillence Types (Quick)
Reference
Type Severity Key Features
I Mild No deformity. Blue sclera. Fractures post-walking.
II Lethal Perinatal death. Severe hypomineralization.
III Severe Progressive deforming. "Popcorn" epiphyses. Short stature.
IV Moderate Mild-mod deformity. White/grey sclera.
V Mod-Sev Hyperplastic callus. Interosseous membrane calcification.
Management Overview
Multidisciplinary
Pharmaceutical Bisphosphonates (mainstay) to increase BMD. Analgesia for chronic pain.
Orthopaedic Intramedullary rodding for long bones. Corrective osteotomy.
Rehabilitation Physiotherapy (muscle strengthening). Occupational therapy. Safe mobility.
Dental Regular exams. Manage dentinogenesis imperfecta. Orthodontics.
ENT/Audiology Stapedectomy (if indicated). Hearing aids. Regular audiograms.
Pulmonology Monitor restrictive lung disease. Treat infections aggressively.
Aetiology Quickview

85-90%: COL1A1 / COL1A2 (Autosomal Dominant).

Type I collagen quantity (mild) vs quality (severe).

Rare Types: Autosomal Recessive (AR).

Genes: CRTAP, P3H1, SERPINF1, FKBP10, etc.

Founder Variants: Type VII (First Nations, Ont), Type VIII (West Africa), Type XI (Turkey).
