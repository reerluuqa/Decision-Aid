Initial Assessment

History Taking Essential

Collect comprehensive history including onset, duration, and characteristics of pain.

Mechanism of injury
Previous knee problems
Activity level and occupation
Physical Examination Careful

Perform systematic examination of both knees and surrounding structures.

Inspection for swelling/deformity
Palpation for tenderness
Range of motion assessment
Red Flag Screening Urgent

Identify signs requiring immediate specialist referral.

Severe trauma with deformity
Hot, swollen joint with fever
Inability to weight-bear

Differential Diagnosis

Mechanical Disorders Common

Evaluate for structural and mechanical problems.

Meniscal tears
Ligament injuries
Patellofemoral disorders
Inflammatory Conditions Systemic

Consider inflammatory causes of knee pain.

Rheumatoid arthritis
Gout or pseudogout
Septic arthritis
Degenerative Changes Chronic

Assess for age-related changes.

Osteoarthritis
Meniscal degeneration

Treatment Strategy

Conservative Management First Line

Initial non-surgical approach for most cases.

NSAIDs and analgesics
Exercise therapy
Weight management
Interventional Procedures Secondary

Consider when conservative measures fail.

Corticosteroid injections
Hyaluronic acid injections
Surgical Options Definitive

Reserved for severe or refractory cases.

Arthroscopic procedures
Joint replacement
