Falls Assessment - Clinical Reference

Quick reference for healthcare professionals
Last revised: June 2025
IMMEDIATE REFERRAL REQUIRED
Patients who have experienced loss of consciousness, unable to get up independently after a fall, or have had 2+ falls in the past year
Red Flags
  • Loss of consciousness related to fall
  • Unable to get up independently after a fall
  • Two or more falls in the last year
  • Significant injury requiring medical treatment
  • Acute medical cause suspected (arrhythmia, stroke, TIA)
Who to Assess
  • People aged 65+ years
  • People aged 50-64 with risk factors
  • Those with cognitive impairment
  • Those with visual impairment
  • Those with mobility/balance conditions

Assessment Questions
Question Why It Matters
How many falls in the past year? History of falls is strongest predictor of future falls
What were the circumstances of the fall? Helps distinguish simple falls from collapse due to acute medical issues
Were there any injuries? Indicates severity and need for intervention
Were you able to get up independently? Inability indicates higher risk and need for comprehensive assessment
High Risk Factors
  • Previous fall 66% chance of another fall within a year
  • Multiple risk factors Risk doubles with each additional factor
  • Polypharmacy (4+ medications)
  • Psychoactive medications
  • Medications causing postural hypotension
Medical Conditions
  • Cognitive impairment
  • Visual impairment
  • Muscle weakness
  • Poor balance
  • Arthritis
  • Diabetes
  • Stroke
  • Parkinson's disease

COMPREHENSIVE ASSESSMENT INDICATORS
Refer to specialist falls service if patient meets any of these criteria: living with frailty, injured in fall requiring treatment, loss of consciousness, unable to get up independently, or 2+ falls in last year
Comprehensive Assessment Components
  • History of falls
  • Gait, balance and mobility assessment
  • Muscle weakness evaluation
  • Osteoporosis risk assessment
  • Footwear and foot condition
  • Functional ability and fear of falling
  • Visual and hearing assessment
  • Cognitive, neurological and cardiovascular evaluation
  • Home hazards assessment
  • Medication review
Falls Prevention Exercise Programme
  • Delivered by appropriately trained professionals
  • Progressive and tailored to individual needs
  • Focus on balance, coordination, strength and power
  • Include regular exercise progress reviews
  • Duration sufficient to bring about behavior change
Medication Review
High-Risk Medications
Psychotropic drugs, benzodiazepines, antidepressants
Postural Hypotension
Antihypertensive medications
Action
Review, consider discontinuation with specialist input
HIGH RISK OF SERIOUS CONSEQUENCES
40-60% of falls result in major lacerations, traumatic brain injuries, or fractures. 5% result in fracture or hospitalization.
Fractures
  • 90% of hip fractures caused by falls
  • 18-33% increased one-year mortality after hip fracture
  • 20% enter long-term care after hip fracture
  • Significant impact on daily living activities
Psychological Impact
  • Fear of falling (>25% of cases)
  • Activity avoidance
  • Social isolation
  • Depression
  • Loss of independence
Financial Burden
  • Total cost of fragility fractures to UK: £4.4 billion
  • Social care costs: £1.1 billion
  • Hip fracture costs: £2 billion
  • Unaddressed home hazards: £435 million to NHS England
