Back Pain Assessment

Quick Clinical Reference Tool
EMERGENCY ACTIONS
Cauda Equina Syndrome

Immediate MRI and urgent surgical referral for patients with saddle anesthesia, bowel/bladder dysfunction, or bilateral sciatica.

Spinal Cord Compression

Urgent MRI for patients with progressive neurological deficit, especially with history of malignancy.

Spinal Epidural Abscess

Urgent MRI with contrast for patients with fever, back pain, and neurological deficit, especially with risk factors (IVDU, diabetes).

Trauma

CT imaging for patients with midline tenderness, high-energy mechanism, or >60 years with thoracolumbar injury mechanism.

Red Flags
Sphincter Disturbance

Bladder/bowel dysfunction, acute urinary retention, new onset incontinence

Neurological Deficit

Progressive or profound weakness, sensory changes, altered reflexes

Systemic Symptoms

Fever, chills, night sweats, unexplained weight loss

Significant History

History of malignancy, IV drug use, immunosuppression, prolonged steroid use

Trauma

Recent trauma, especially in older adults or with osteoporosis

Initial Assessment
History

Onset, duration, location, radiation, aggravating/relieving factors, prior episodes

Physical Examination

Inspection, palpation, range of motion, neurological exam, gait observation

Laboratory Tests

Only if infection, malignancy, or inflammatory arthritis suspected (FBC, ESR, CRP)

Imaging

Not routinely required. Consider if red flags present or symptoms persist >6 weeks

Management Approach
Non-Specific Mechanical Back Pain

Reassure, advise staying active, simple analgesics, consider physical therapy. Review in 4-6 weeks if not improving.

Pharmacological Management

NSAIDs first-line, consider acetaminophen, muscle relaxants for short-term use. Opioids only if severe pain and functional impairment.

Follow-up

Review in 4-6 weeks if not improving. Consider imaging if symptoms persist >6 weeks or if red flags develop.

Risk Factors for Chronicity

Maladaptive pain coping, non-organic signs, functional impairment, poor general health, sleep disturbance, psychiatric comorbidities.
