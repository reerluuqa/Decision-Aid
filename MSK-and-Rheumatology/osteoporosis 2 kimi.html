Clinical Assessment

Comprehensive evaluation for osteoporosis risk factors and fracture risk assessment.

Key Diagnostic Factors

Risk Factors

Strong Risk Factors

  • Prior fragility fracture
  • Female sex
  • White ancestry
  • Older age (>50 women, >65 men)
  • Low BMI (<20 kg/m¬≤)
  • Postmenopause
  • Glucocorticoid use
  • Smoking
  • Excessive alcohol use

Moderate Risk Factors

  • Proton-pump inhibitor use
  • Hyperthyroidism
  • Heparin use
  • Anticonvulsant use
  • Androgen deprivation therapy
  • Aromatase inhibitor treatment
  • Fall-risk increasing drugs

Physical Examination

Measure height Assess BMI Check for kyphosis Evaluate gait & balance Vision assessment
Red Flag: Any low-impact fragility fracture (including asymptomatic vertebral fractures) indicates osteoporosis regardless of BMD

Treatment Classes

Antiresorptives

  • Bisphosphonates: Alendronate, Risedronate, Zoledronic acid
  • Denosumab: RANKL inhibitor (6-monthly)
  • SERMs: Raloxifene, Bazedoxifene
  • Hormone: Estrogen (limited use)

Anabolic Agents

  • Teriparatide: PTH(1-34) - up to 2 years
  • Abaloparatide: PTHrP analogue - up to 2 years
  • Romosozumab: Sclerostin inhibitor - 12 months
Sequential therapy required
Critical: Never discontinue denosumab without starting bisphosphonate (delayed start leads to rapid bone loss and multiple vertebral fractures)

Monitoring & Follow-up

Regular monitoring to assess treatment response, adherence, and complications.
