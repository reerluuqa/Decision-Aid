Urgent Signs - Refer Immediately

Signs of Rickets:

  • Painful wrist swelling
  • Rachitic rosary (costochondral swelling)

  • Muscle pain and weakness
  • Difficulty climbing stairs
  • Delayed walking or waddling gait

üîÑ Differential Diagnosis

‚ñ∂

  • Hyperparathyroidism - hypercalcaemia symptoms
  • Thyroid disease - fatigue, muscle weakness
  • Juvenile idiopathic arthritis - joint symptoms
  • Muscular dystrophies - progressive weakness

üìù Lifestyle Advice

‚ñ∂

Safe Sun Exposure:

  • Infants <6 months: Keep out of direct strong sunlight
  • Short periods in sunlight with forearms, hands, or lower legs uncovered
  • Longer exposure may be needed for dark-pigmented skin
  • Avoid prolonged exposure leading to burning/tanning
  • Avoid sunbeds - ineffective and harmful

Dietary Sources of Vitamin D:

  • Oily fish (salmon, mackerel, sardines)
  • Egg yolks
  • Fortified foods (cereals, margarine, formula milk)
  • Red meat, liver
üí°

Key Message

It is difficult to obtain sufficient vitamin D from food sources alone. Long-term supplementation is usually needed to prevent recurrence.
