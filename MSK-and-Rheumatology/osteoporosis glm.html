Last updated: April 2025
Emergency: Immediate action required for patients with vertebral fractures or severe pain. Refer to specialist care immediately.
Who to Assess Immediately

All patients with:

  • Previous fragility fracture
  • Current or recent oral corticosteroid use
  • History of falls
Schedule DXA Scan
Routine Assessment

  • Men ≥75 years

DXA Scan Indications

Offer DXA scan to:

  • People >50 years with fragility fracture
  • People <40 years with major risk factors

Offer DXA scan to:

  • People with 10-year fracture risk ≥10%
  • People with risk close to threshold
  • People with risk factors underestimated by FRAX®
First-Line Treatment

For most high-risk patients:

  • Oral bisphosphonates (alendronate, risedronate)
  • Calcium and vitamin D supplementation
  • Lifestyle modifications
Initiate Treatment
Specialist Referral

Consider referral for:

  • Severe renal impairment (eGFR <30-35)
  • Younger patients with major risk factors
  • Fractures despite treatment
Refer to Specialist

Lifestyle Interventions
  • Weight-bearing aerobic exercise (walking, jogging)
  • Muscle-strengthening activities (2+ times/week)
  • Balance training to reduce falls
  • Calcium intake ≥1000mg/day
  • Vitamin D supplementation (800-1000 IU/day)
  • Protein intake 1-1.2g/kg body weight
Major Risk Factors
Previous fracture Corticosteroids Family history Low BMI Smoking Alcohol excess
Secondary Causes
Rheumatoid arthritis Hyperthyroidism Diabetes Malabsorption COPD Chronic kidney disease
Medication Risk Factors
  • Oral corticosteroids (dose and duration dependent)
  • Antiepileptics (especially enzyme-inducing)
  • SSRIs
  • Proton pump inhibitors
  • Aromatase inhibitors
  • Gonadotropin-releasing hormone agonists
  • Thiazolidinediones
Fall Risk Factors
  • Impaired vision
  • Neuromuscular weakness
  • Cognitive impairment
  • Use of alcohol or sedatives
  • Environmental hazards
  • History of previous falls
