1. Assessment – When to suspect / test?
Do NOT routinely test asymptomatic patients

Test serum 25(OH)D only if:
  • Suspected osteomalacia (bone pain + proximal muscle weakness)
  • Chronic widespread pain WITH features of osteomalacia
  • Osteoporosis or fragility fracture
  • Starting potent antiresorptive (zoledronate, denosumab, teriparatide)
  • Paget’s disease before bisphosphonate
  • Symptoms that may respond to vitamin D (e.g. severe persistent musculoskeletal pain)
At-risk groups (advise maintenance Vit D without testing):
Age ≥65, limited sun exposure, dark skin, malabsorption, obesity, CKD, liver disease, drugs (anticonvulsants, rifampicin, HAART, etc.)
2. Diagnosis – Interpreting 25(OH)D level
Serum 25-hydroxyvitamin D thresholds (SACN/ROS 2022):

< 25 nmol/L → DEFICIENCY Treat with loading ± maintenance
25–50 nmol/L → INSUFFICIENCY Treat if symptomatic OR osteoporosis OR before potent antiresorptive
> 50 nmol/L → SUFFICIENT Lifestyle advice only (unless high-risk → maintenance)
Additional tests if deficiency confirmed:
  • Bone profile (Ca, PO₄, ALP)
  • PTH (usually raised in deficiency)
  • FBC, ferritin, B12/folate, U&E, LFTs, coeliac screen (as indicated)
Differential diagnosis (consider if symptoms persist despite treatment):
Fibromyalgia, myeloma, metastatic cancer, hyperparathyroidism, polymyalgia rheumatica, thyroid disease, etc.
3. Treatment & Prevention
URGENT SPECIALIST ADVICE / REFERRAL needed if (red badge cases):
Renal stones / active stones  Granulomatous disease (sarcoid, TB)  Primary hyperparathyroidism  Severe CKD / liver disease  Pregnancy
Treatment regimens (Colecalciferol = Vitamin D₃ preferred)

Rapid correction needed (symptomatic or before potent antiresorptive):
Loading total ≈ 300,000 IU over 6–10 weeks, e.g.
• 50,000 IU weekly × 6 weeks or
• 40,000 IU weekly × 7 weeks or
• 1,000 IU four times daily × 10 weeks

Then maintenance 800–2,000 IU daily (OTC for most; prescribe if osteoporosis/malabsorption)
Slower correction (oral bisphosphonate co-prescription):
Start maintenance 800–2,000 IU daily without loading
Prevention (no testing needed):
All adults ≥65 years and at-risk groups: 400 IU (10 µg) daily all year
Everyone else: Consider 400 IU daily especially Oct–Mar
Healthy Start vitamins free for eligible patients
Calcium: Aim ≥700 mg/day dietary (≥1,000 mg if osteoporosis).
Supplement if diet inadequate (not with Adcal-D3/Calcichew-D3 if high-dose Vit D needed)
Lifestyle advice (give to everyone):
• Safe sun exposure (arms/face 10–15 min midday spring/summer)
• Dietary sources limited – oily fish, eggs, fortified foods
• Continue maintenance Vit D long-term in at-risk groups
4. Follow-up
After loading regimen:
Check adjusted calcium 1 month after last dose
• Hypercalcaemia → stop Vit D ± calcium, investigate
• Hypocalcaemia → increase dietary/supplemental calcium
Repeat 25(OH)D only if:
• Persistent symptoms
• Malabsorption
• Suspected non-compliance
• Before next zoledronate/denosumab dose AND baseline very low
Target >50 nmol/L sufficient for most
If still <50 nmol/L at 3–6 months → check adherence ± refer for malabsorption
