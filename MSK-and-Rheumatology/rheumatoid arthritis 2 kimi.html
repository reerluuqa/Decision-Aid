Baseline Investigations

Essential tests for diagnosis and monitoring:

Rheumatoid factor (RF) - positive in 60-70% of patients
Anti-CCP antibodies - more specific, present in 70% of patients
Inflammatory markers: ESR and CRP
Baseline imaging: X-rays of hands and feet
Ultrasound or MRI for early disease detection

Poor Prognostic Factors

Identify patients needing aggressive early treatment:

Clinical Factors

High baseline disease activity, early functional limitation

Serological Factors

High RF or anti-CCP titers, elevated ESR/CRP

Radiographic Factors

Early erosions, high baseline damage scores

Patient Factors

Smoking, comorbidities, low socioeconomic status

Treatment Strategy

Treat-to-target approach with DMARD therapy

‚ñº

Initial Treatment - Non-pregnant Patients

Treatment target: Low disease activity or remission within 3-6 months

Methotrexate

First-line DMARD for moderate-to-high disease activity. Start 7.5-15mg weekly, titrate up to 20-25mg.

‚ö° Consider subcutaneous route for better efficacy

Hydroxychloroquine

Preferred for low disease activity. Better tolerated, favorable risk profile.

Sulfasalazine

Alternative DMARD, particularly useful if methotrexate contraindicated.

Combination Therapy

Methotrexate + biological agent or targeted synthetic DMARD for poor prognostic factors.

üî• Use in presence of poor prognostic factors

Glucocorticoid Bridge Therapy

Short-term corticosteroids for rapid symptom control:

Prednisolone 5-10mg daily (max 10mg)
Intra-articular injections for individual inflamed joints
IM methylprednisolone for acute flares
Taper and discontinue within 3-6 months
‚ö†Ô∏è Minimize dose and duration üíä Use as bridge while DMARDs take effect

Escalation Strategy (3-month review)

If target not achieved after 3 months of conventional DMARD:

Switch to subcutaneous methotrexate if on oral
Add or switch to biological DMARD (TNF inhibitor, IL-6 inhibitor, abatacept, rituximab)
Consider JAK inhibitors for severe disease
Triple therapy (MTX + SSZ + HCQ) as alternative

Biological and Targeted Therapies

TNF Inhibitors

Adalimumab, Etanercept, Infliximab, Golimumab, Certolizumab

First-line biologics

IL-6 Inhibitors

Tocilizumab, Sarilumab - particularly effective in high disease activity

JAK Inhibitors

Tofacitinib, Baricitinib, Upadacitinib, Filgotinib - oral targeted therapy

Pregnancy Considerations

Preferred: Continue hydroxychloroquine, sulfasalazine
Corticosteroids: Safest option for symptom control
Avoid methotrexate, leflunomide (teratogenic)
Biologics: Continue if necessary, avoid in 3rd trimester

Laboratory Monitoring

Regular safety monitoring for DMARD therapy:

FBC & LFTs: 2-4 weekly initially, then 3-monthly when stable
ESR/CRP: Every visit to monitor disease activity
Creatinine: Regular monitoring with agents affecting renal function

Complications to Monitor

Cardiovascular Disease

RA increases CV risk - monitor blood pressure, lipids, glucose

Infections

Increased risk with immunosuppressive therapy - screen and vaccinate

Osteoporosis

RA and corticosteroids increase fracture risk - consider DEXA scan

Malignancy

Regular cancer screening, especially with long-term immunosuppression

Interstitial Lung Disease

Screen for respiratory symptoms, consider baseline lung function tests

Depression & Fatigue

Screen for mental health issues and provide support
