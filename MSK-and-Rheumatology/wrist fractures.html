Physical Examination

Key Findings

  • 'Dinner Fork' Deformity: Suggests displaced Colles' fracture (dorsal angulation).
  • Swelling & Tenderness: Over distal radius (hallmark). Check for skin tenting.
  • Anatomic Snuff-box: Tenderness = Suspect Scaphoid fracture.

Neurovascular Check

Assess Radial, Median, and Ulnar nerves before and after manipulation.

Epidemiology & Risk Factors
Group A: Older Women (60s-70s)

Low-energy (Fall from standing). Osteoporosis link. Female:Male 4:1.

Group B: Young Men (20s-30s)

High-energy (Sport, MVC). Denser bone.

Diagnostic Summary
Non-Displaced Fracture:

No deformity. Normal radiographic contours.
Treatment: Immobilization.

Displaced Fracture:

'Dinner fork', loss of anatomy.
Treatment: Reduction ¬± Surgery.

Analgesia & Initial Care
  • Adults: Oral Paracetamol (Mild) ‚Üí Paracetamol + Codeine (Mod) ‚Üí IV Paracetamol + Morphine (Severe).
  • Children (<16): Ibuprofen/Paracetamol (Mild/Mod) ‚Üí Nasal/IV Opioids (Severe).
  • Regional: Hematoma block or nerve block for reduction.
  • Avoid: NSAIDs in frail/elderly.
Complication Alerts
  • EPL Rupture: Rare (<5%), occurs within 12 weeks. Check thumb extension.
  • Malunion: Loss of motion/grip strength in younger patients.
  • CRPS: Monitor for disproportionate pain/swelling.
Fracture Classifications

AO/ASIF Classification

  • Type A: Extra-articular (No joint involvement).
  • Type B: Partial intra-articular.
  • Type C: Complete intra-articular.

Fernandez Classification

Based on mechanism:

  • I: Bending of metaphysis
  • II: Shearing of joint surface
  • III: Compression of joint surface
  • IV: Avulsion / Fracture-dislocation
  • V: Combined high-velocity
Follow-up & Prevention

Monitoring

Radiographic monitoring for loss of position (especially in elderly/osteoporotic).

Prevention

  • Primary: Fall prevention strategies.
  • Secondary: Assess for Osteoporosis (DXA scan) in low-energy fractures.
  • Lifestyle: Calcium, Vitamin D, Weight-bearing exercise.
