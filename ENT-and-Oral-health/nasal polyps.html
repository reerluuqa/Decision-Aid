Definition & Pathology

Benign swellings of the paranasal sinus mucosa.

  • Invariably associated with Chronic Rhinosinusitis (CRSwNP).
  • Strong association with Asthma (esp. eosinophilic).
  • Histology: Loose connective tissue, oedema, inflammatory cells (mostly eosinophils).

Investigations for Red Flags

CT Sinuses (Essential)

Modality of choice to assess extent and underlying anatomy. Look for soft-tissue opacifications, bone erosion, or expansion.

Biopsy

Indicated if unilateral or concerning features present. Histology changes diagnosis in ~1% of surgically removed polyps.

Diagnostic Approach

  1. History: Symptoms > 12 weeks. Use VAS to score (0-10).
  2. Examination:
    • Anterior rhinoscopy (large polyps visible).
    • Nasal Endoscopy (Gold standard for small polyps).
  3. Imaging: CT Scan if surgery planned or red flags present.

Labs & Tests

  • FBC w/ Diff: Check eosinophils (0.4 - 1.5 x 10^6). High levels suggest EGPA if >1.0.
  • Nasal Smear: Eosinophils typical.
  • Skin Prick/IgE: Rule in/out allergic rhinitis (not CRSwNP specific).
  • Aspirin Challenge: If history unclear (Specialist only).

Mild to Moderate (VAS 0-7)

First-line treatment. Reassess at 3 months.

  • Intranasal Corticosteroid (Primary)
    Fluticasone (93-186mcg) or Mometasone (100-200mcg) daily.
  • Nasal Saline Irrigation (Adjunct)
    Isotonic saline or Ringer's lactate.
  • Doxycycline (Adjunct - Optional)
    200mg day 1, then 100mg daily for 3-12 weeks.

Severe (VAS >7)

Aggressive inflammation management.

  • Intranasal Corticosteroid (As above)
  • Oral Corticosteroid (Consider Short Course)
    7-21 days. Reduces total symptom score significantly.
  • Review at 1 Month

Refractory Cases (No Improvement @ 3 Mo)

Surgical Polypectomy

Required pre-op CT scan.

  • Endoscopic Sinus Surgery (ESS)
  • Post-op: Saline douching + continued intranasal steroids.
  • Risk of revision surgery higher in asthmatics.

Aspirin Therapy After Desensitisation (ATAD)

Indication: Patients with CRSwNP and NSAID-exacerbated respiratory disease (NERD) / Aspirin hypersensitivity.

Action: Desensitisation followed by daily aspirin therapy.

Preventive Measures:
  • H. pylori eradication
  • Proton-pump inhibitors
  • H2 antagonists
