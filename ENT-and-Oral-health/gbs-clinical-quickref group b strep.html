üö® RED FLAGS - Immediate Action Required

NEONATAL EMERGENCY SIGNS

  • Temperature instability - hypothermia more common than fever in neonates
  • Respiratory distress - grunting, nasal flaring, intercostal retractions
  • Hemodynamic instability - hypotension, tachycardia, poor perfusion
  • Altered mental status - lethargy, irritability, poor feeding
  • Seizures or bulging fontanelle - signs of meningitis

ADULT EMERGENCY SIGNS

  • Fever ‚â•38¬∞C (100.4¬∞F) - especially in elderly or immunocompromised
  • Hypotension - systolic BP <90 mmHg or MAP <65 mmHg
  • Altered consciousness - confusion, especially in elderly
  • Signs of meningitis - neck stiffness, photophobia, headache
  • Rapid deterioration - GBS infections can progress rapidly despite antibiotics

Treatment Algorithm - Confirmed GBS Infection
1

CONFIRM DIAGNOSIS

Isolate GBS from normally sterile site (blood, CSF, joint fluid, urine in neonates). Consider PCR if cultures negative but clinical suspicion high.

2

START TARGETED THERAPY

Benzylpenicillin is preferred (narrower spectrum) or Ampicillin. Add Gentamicin for synergy in meningitis or severe sepsis per local guidelines.

3

ADJUST FOR SPECIAL SITUATIONS

Penicillin allergy: Cefazolin (low risk) or Vancomycin/Ceftriaxone (high risk). Consult ID specialist for meningitis or severe allergy.

4

DURATION OF THERAPY

UTI/Bacteremia/Pneumonia: 10 days. Meningitis: 14-21 days. Septic arthritis: 3-4 weeks. Adjust based on clinical response.

Universal Screening Recommended (US)

Vaginal & rectal swab at 36-37 weeks for all pregnant women

  • Single swab from lower vagina AND rectum
  • Culture in enrichment broth + selective media
  • Process within 24 hours (keep at 4¬∞C if delay)
  • Self-sampling is accurate alternative

Supportive Care - All Patients

AIRWAY & BREATHING

  • Supplemental O2 for hypoxia or respiratory distress
  • Intubate if: decompensated shock, severe RDS, altered consciousness, raised ICP
  • Follow pediatric/adult advanced life support principles

CIRCULATION

  • Large-bore IV access for fluid resuscitation
  • Fluid boluses for hypotension/poor perfusion
  • Vasopressors if no response to fluids (MAP target ‚â•65 mmHg)
  • Caution: fluids if raised ICP, myocardial dysfunction, or ARDS

Critical: GBS infections can progress rapidly

Clinical deterioration may continue despite prompt antibiotic therapy. Continuous monitoring essential, especially in first 24-48 hours.
