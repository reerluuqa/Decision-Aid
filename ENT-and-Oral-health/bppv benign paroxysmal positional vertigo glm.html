Definition

Disorder of the inner ear characterized by repeated episodes of positional vertigo. Symptoms occur with changes in head position relative to gravity.

Risk Factors
Etiology
  • Increasing age (5th-7th decade)
  • Female sex (F:M ratio ~2:1)
  • Head injury / Prolonged recumbent position
  • Ear surgery
  • Migraine / Recent viral URI
  • Vitamin D deficiency
Prognosis
Outcome
  • Spontaneous recovery: ~20% @ 1 month, 50% @ 3 months.
  • Recurrence: Common (~15% annually). 50% recur within 3-5 years.
  • Risks for recurrence: Age >65, Diabetes, Hypertension, Migraine, Osteoporosis.

Key Symptoms
Feature Typical Presentation
Trigger Lying down, turning over in bed, looking up, bending over.
Duration < 1 minute
Between Attacks Asymptomatic (patient may overestimate duration).
Hearing NOT affected (No tinnitus/hearing loss).
Positive Sign: Torsional (rotatory) upbeating nystagmus.
Dix-Hallpike Manoeuvre
Diagnostic
1
Patient sits upright, head turned 45¬∞ to the test side.
2
Lie patient down rapidly (2 seconds) until head is extended 20-30¬∞ over end of couch. Chin points slightly up.
3
Observe eyes closely for up to 30 seconds. Note latency (usually 5-20s), duration, and direction.
4
Support head, sit patient up slowly. Observe for reversal of nystagmus.
5
Repeat on opposite side.
Note: Do not repeat solely to confirm fatiguability due to unpleasant symptoms.
Patient Advice
Recovery takes days/weeks. Get out of bed slowly. Avoid looking up. Warn re: driving/falls.

Epley Manoeuvre
First Line

Contraindications: Neck/back issues, unstable cardiac disease, vertebrobasilar disease, carotid stenosis, morbid obesity.

1
Sit upright. Turn head 45¬∞ to affected side.
2
Lie back quickly (head dependent 30¬∞). Wait 30s+ (until vertigo subsides).
3
Rotate head 90¬∞ to opposite side (face upwards). Wait 20s.
4
Roll patient onto that side (body follows head). Nose 45¬∞ below horizontal. Wait 30s.
5
Sit up sideways. Rotate head to center, chin down 45¬∞.

Outcome: No post-procedure positional restrictions needed.

Semont Manoeuvre
Alternative

Use if skills available. Less common in primary care.

  • Sit, turn head away from affected side.
  • Lie on affected side rapidly (head turned up). Wait 20s after nystagmus stops.
  • Move quickly through sitting to opposite side (head faces down). Wait 30s.
  • Sit up slowly.
Brandt-Daroff Exercises
Home Use

Use if Epley not possible or inappropriate.

  • Sit on edge of bed.
  • Lie down quickly on side, head rotated 45¬∞ up.
  • Wait 30s or until vertigo stops.
  • Sit up 30s. Repeat other side.
  • Frequency: 3-4 reps, 3-4 times/day for 2 symptom-free days.

Driving & DVLA
DVLA states: Stop driving if "liable to sudden unprovoked disabling dizziness". However, BPPV is usually provoked. Most people continue to drive. Advise not to drive during acute episodes.
