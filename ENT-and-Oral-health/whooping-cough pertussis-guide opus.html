Clinical Phases

Phase 1: Catarrhal (1-2 weeks)

Incubation: 7-10 days (range 4-21 days)

Symptoms: Rhinorrhoea, malaise, mild cough, sore throat, conjunctivitis

‚ö†Ô∏è MOST INFECTIOUS STAGE

Phase 2: Paroxysmal (1-6 weeks, up to 10 weeks)

Classic features: Violent coughing fits with inspiratory 'whoop'

Note: 'Whoop' less common in adults and infants <3 months (may present with apnoea alone)

Associated: Post-tussive vomiting, cyanosis in children, cough syncope in adults

Pattern: Average 15 attacks/24 hours, worse at night

Phase 3: Convalescent (2-3 weeks)

Gradual improvement in cough frequency and severity

Note: Paroxysms can recur with subsequent respiratory infections for months

üì¢ Patient Advice

  • Rest, adequate fluid intake, paracetamol/ibuprofen for symptom relief
  • Even with antibiotics, expect protracted non-infectious cough (weeks to resolve)
  • Symptoms less severe if previously immunized
  • Seek medical advice if complications develop
  • Arrange outstanding vaccinations after recovery

üè´ Exclusion from Nursery/School/Work

Asymptomatic contacts: No exclusion required
