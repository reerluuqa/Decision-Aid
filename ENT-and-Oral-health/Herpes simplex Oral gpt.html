1) Diagnose

Herpes labialis (cold sores)

Gingivostomatitis

  • Primary infection common in children; fever, malaise, sore throat, lymphadenopathy.
  • Excess salivation/drooling (esp. children).
  • Multiple painful vesicles/ulcers on oral mucosa (palate, buccal, tongue, gingiva, floor of mouth).
Assessment prompts
  • Location, frequency, duration; pain severity; triggers (UV, stress, fatigue, fever, menses, trauma/dental work, temperature extremes, immunosuppression).

3) Management

  • Reassure: usually self‚Äëlimiting.
  • Analgesia/antipyretic: paracetamol and/or ibuprofen if no contraindications.
  • Encourage fluids; postpone elective dental care until healed.
Topicals (not routinely prescribed)
  • OTC topical antivirals (aciclovir/penciclovir) may help if started at prodrome.
  • OTC topical anaesthetics/analgesics/mouthwashes may be used (check paediatric licensing).

  • Healthy: not routine. Consider for primary infection or severe/frequent/persistent recurrences.

5) Advice & Prevention

Reduce transmission & autoinoculation

  • Avoid kissing and oral sex until fully healed.
  • Do not share items contacting lesions (lip balms, makeup, utensils, towels).
  • Avoid touching lesions; if applying topicals, dab not rub; wash hands after.
  • Contact lenses: take care (risk of ocular HSV).
Children who are otherwise well do not need exclusion from nursery/school.

Triggers & prevention

  • Identify/avoid triggers (UV, stress, fatigue, trauma, extremes of temperature).
  • If sunlight triggers: use SPF ‚â•15 lip balm/sunscreen.
