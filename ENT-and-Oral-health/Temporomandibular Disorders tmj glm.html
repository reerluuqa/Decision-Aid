Temporomandibular Disorders (TMDs) Clinical Decision Support

Definition & Classification

  • TMDs: Musculoskeletal conditions affecting masticatory muscles, TMJ, and associated structures
  • Main symptoms: TMJ pain, headache, limited jaw movement, joint sounds (clicking/popping/crepitus)
  • Classification:
    • Myogenous: Involving muscles of mastication
    • Arthrogenous: Involving TMJ bones and/or articular disc
    • Headache: Temporal region pain exacerbated by jaw function

When to Suspect TMD

  • Pain in/around TMJ and/or masticatory muscles (may radiate to ear, temple, teeth)
  • Reproducible joint noise with jaw movements
  • Headache limited to temporal region + worsened by jaw function
  • Otalgia without ear disease
  • Limited mouth opening (<35mm)

Assessment Approach

History

  • Pain characteristics: Location, radiation, onset, quality, intensity, duration
  • Precipitating factors: Trauma, dental procedures, stress
  • Exacerbating factors: Chewing, talking, yawning
  • Impact on QoL: Sleep, mood, concentration, daily activities
  • Screening tool: 3-question TMD screening questionnaire

Examination

  • Inspect: Facial asymmetry, swelling
  • Palpate: TMJ (anterior to tragus), masticatory muscles
  • Assess: Mouth opening (normal: 35-55mm), joint noises, tenderness
  • Exclude: Dental pathology, lymphadenopathy

Red Flags (Urgent Referral)

  • Malignancy history
  • Persistent neck lump/lymphadenopathy
  • Progressive/worsening pain
  • Pain with exertion/coughing/sneezing
  • Jaw pain with bisphosphonate use
  • Neurological symptoms/signs
  • Facial asymmetry/mass/profound trismus
  • Unexplained fever/weight loss
  • New unilateral headache/scalp tenderness (>50 years)
  • Persistent hoarseness/dysphagia/oral ulcers (>3 weeks)

Differential Diagnosis

  • Dental: Caries, periodontal disease, tooth abscess
  • Orofacial pain: Giant cell arteritis, trigeminal neuralgia
  • Headache disorders: Migraine, tension-type headache
  • Salivary gland: Sialadenitis, stones
  • ENT conditions: Otitis media, sinusitis
  • Neurological: Postherpetic neuralgia

Initial Management

  1. Reassurance and education about benign nature of most TMDs
  2. Self-care advice: Soft diet, avoid extreme jaw movements, heat/cold therapy, gentle jaw exercises
  3. Pain management: Simple analgesics (paracetamol), short-term NSAIDs (lowest effective dose), consider PPI co-prescription if GI risk factors
  4. Stress management techniques
  5. Referral considerations: Persistent symptoms, severe functional limitation, red flags present, diagnostic uncertainty

Based on NICE Clinical Knowledge Summaries (May 2024) and other referenced guidelines. Always use clinical judgment in individual patient management.
