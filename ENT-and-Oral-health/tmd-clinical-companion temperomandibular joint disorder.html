Temporomandibular Disorders - Quick Reference

Rapid clinical decision support for TMD assessment, diagnosis, and management

CRITICAL RED FLAGS - IMMEDIATE ACTION REQUIRED

Recent Trauma/Fracture
History of facial trauma, mandibular fracture, or recent surgery
URGENT OMFS REFERRAL
Signs of Infection
Fever, swelling, trismus, warmth, erythema, purulent discharge
INFECTION WORKUP
Neurological Deficit
Sensory changes, CN palsy, paresthesia, unilateral hearing loss
NEUROLOGICAL ASSESSMENT
Unexplained Weight Loss/Mass
Persistent mass, unexplained weight loss, night sweats, lymphadenopathy
ONCOLOGY REFERRAL
Persistent Locking (>3 months)
Chronic closed lock with limited opening, failed conservative treatment
SURGICAL EVALUATION
Atypical Pain Patterns
Pain not reproducible on palpation, constant pain, pain at rest
DIFFERENTIAL DIAGNOSIS

Diagnostic Categories (DC/TMD)

Myalgia

ROUTINE
Clinical Presentation
  • Dull, aching muscle pain
  • Pain modified by jaw function
  • Regional pain referral patterns
  • Associated headache (temporal)
Key Examination Findings
  • Tender to palpation of masticatory muscles
  • Pain with resistive jaw movements
  • No joint sounds (typically)
  • Normal ROM (may be limited by pain)
Self-care, soft diet, NSAIDs, PT referral

Disc Displacement

ROUTINE
Clinical Presentation
  • Clicking, popping, or snapping sounds
  • Joint tenderness and pain
  • Deviation on opening/closing
  • Intermittent locking episodes
Types
Type Characteristics
With Reduction Click on opening & closing, normal ROM
Without Reduction Limited opening, no click, deviation
Observation if painless; splint if painful

Arthralgia

ROUTINE
Clinical Presentation
  • Pre-auricular joint pain
  • Pain reproduced on palpation
  • Pain with lateral/p protrusive movements
  • May have associated limited ROM
Associated Conditions
  • Parafunction (bruxism)
  • Trauma (micro/macro)
  • Systemic arthritis
  • Hypermobility syndromes
Anti-inflammatories, soft diet, avoid wide yawning

Degenerative Joint Disease

FOLLOW-UP
Clinical Presentation
  • Crepitus (grating sensation)
  • Progressive limited ROM
  • Joint pain and tenderness
  • May have bony enlargement
Imaging Findings
  • Joint space narrowing
  • Osteophyte formation
  • Subchondral sclerosis
  • Erosion or flattening of condyle
Monitor progression; imaging if worsening

Treatment Protocol

Conservative Self-Care (First-Line)

Diet & Nutrition
  • Soft diet for 2-4 weeks (avoid chewing gum, tough meats, hard vegetables)
  • Cut food into small pieces
  • Avoid wide yawning (support chin with fist)
  • No nail biting, teeth clenching, or pen chewing
  • Maintain adequate hydration
Heat/Cold Therapy
  • Apply moist warm towels to jaw muscles for 10-15 minutes, 2-3x daily
  • Ice packs (wrapped in cloth) for acute inflammation, 10-15 minutes
  • Alternate heat and cold for chronic conditions
  • Apply before jaw exercises to reduce discomfort
Posture & Ergonomics
  • Maintain neutral head position (ear over shoulder)
  • Avoid forward head posture when using devices
  • Use ergonomic workstation setup
  • Take frequent breaks from sustained positions
  • Sleep position: avoid stomach sleeping, use supportive pillow
Stress Management
  • Identify and reduce stressors
  • Relaxation techniques: deep breathing, meditation
  • Progressive muscle relaxation
  • Yoga or tai chi for overall stress reduction
  • Biofeedback training for awareness of parafunction

Clinical Examination Steps

History Taking
  • Onset, duration, and location of pain
  • Character of pain (dull/aching vs sharp/throbbing)
  • aggravating and relieving factors
  • History of trauma, dental procedures
  • Parafunctional habits (bruxism, clenching)
  • Medical history (arthritis, fibromyalgia)
  • Previous treatments and their efficacy
Physical Examination
  • Inspection: Facial symmetry, swelling, deviation
  • Palpation: TMJ, masticatory muscles (masseter, temporalis, pterygoids)
  • ROM: Measure interincisal opening (normal: 35-45mm)
  • Joint sounds: Auscultation with stethoscope or finger on joint
  • Provocation tests: Pain on lateral/palatal pressure
  • Neurological: CN V motor and sensory distribution
Diagnostic Criteria (DC/TMD)

Axis I (Physical):

  • Pain-related TMD: Myalgia, Arthralgia, Headache
  • Intra-articular disorders: Disc displacement, DJD

Axis II (Psychosocial):

  • Depression screening (PHQ-9)
  • Anxiety screening (GAD-7)
  • Pain catastrophizing (PCS)
  • Oral behaviors questionnaire
Imaging Indications
  • Limited range of motion >3 months
  • Suspected structural pathology
  • History of trauma
  • Failed conservative treatment
  • Pre-surgical evaluation
  • Unexplained pain or dysfunction

Modalities: Panoramic (screening), CBCT (detailed bone), MRI (soft tissue/disc position)

SNRI Duloxetine 30-60mg daily 8-12 weeks Hepatic impairment, uncontrolled glaucoma, MAOI
Topical Capsaicin cream 0.025-0.1% QID As needed Avoid broken skin, avoid eye contact

Therapeutic Exercises

Goldfish Exercise (Lateral)
  • Place tongue on roof of mouth
  • Slowly open and close mouth
  • Goal: 6 repetitions, 6x daily
  • Repeat for both sides
Controlled Opening
  • Place thumb under chin
  • Slowly open against gentle resistance
  • Hold for 3-5 seconds
  • 10 repetitions, 3x daily
Resistance Opening
  • Place fist under chin
  • Push fist down while opening mouth
  • Hold for 5 seconds
  • 10 repetitions, 3x daily
Resistance Closing
  • Place fingers on upper teeth
  • Palm against lower jaw
  • Push jaw closed against resistance
  • Hold for 5 seconds, 10 reps
Side-to-Side Movement
  • Place tongue on roof of mouth
  • Move jaw slowly to the right
  • Hold for 3-5 seconds
  • Return to center, repeat left
Forward Protrusion
  • Slowly move lower jaw forward
  • Jut lower teeth past upper teeth
  • Hold for 3-5 seconds
  • 10 repetitions, 3x daily
