Critical Red Flags ‚Äî Immediate Action Required

üö® Life-Threatening Manifestations URGENT

Immediate recognition and intervention is critical for these presentations:

  • Diffuse Alveolar Hemorrhage (DAH): Hemoptysis, hypoxemia, new bilateral infiltrates, falling hemoglobin
  • Rapidly Progressive Glomerulonephritis (RPGN): Rising creatinine, RBC casts, proteinuria
  • Central Nervous System Vasculitis: Stroke, seizures, altered mental status
  • Cardiac Involvement: Myocarditis, pericarditis, heart failure
  • Mesenteric Ischemia: Abdominal pain, GI bleeding
  • Limb/Digit Ischemia: Critical limb threatening

Immediate Actions (Within Minutes):

  1. Methylprednisolone 1g IV daily √ó 3 days (or 500mg if weight <50kg)
  2. Urgent Nephrology & Rheumatology consult
  3. Consider Plasma Exchange if: Cr >5.7mg/dL OR DAH requiring ventilation
  4. Airway protection if DAH with respiratory failure
  5. Start empiric broad-spectrum antibiotics after cultures

Reference: PEXIVAS Trial, ACR/EULAR 2021 Guidelines

ü´Å Airway Emergency ‚Äî Subglottic Stenosis CRITICAL

Clinical Presentation:

  • Stridor (inspiratory/expiratory)
  • Hoarseness, persistent cough
  • Dyspnea on exertion, orthopnea
  • Wheeze unresponsive to bronchodilators

Key Point: Laryngoscopy reveals circumferential red friable tissue narrowing the subglottis. Unlike asthma, flow-volume loop shows fixed upper airway obstruction.

Immediate Management:

  1. ENT emergency consultation ‚Äî secured airway if Stridor at rest
  2. Systemic immunosuppressive therapy for inflammatory component
  3. Local therapy: Intralesional glucocorticoid injection + dilatation
  4. Tracheostomy only if definitive airway cannot be secured otherwise

Note: Fibrotic stenoses respond poorly to immunosuppression alone.

‚ö†Ô∏è Don't Miss ‚Äî Easily Overlooked VIGILANCE
  • Venous Thromboembolism (VTE): 16x increased risk during active disease. Any limb swelling/pain requires duplex ultrasound
  • Mononeuritis Multiplex: Asymmetric sensorimotor neuropathy ‚Äî classic "foot drop" presentation
  • Silent Renal Involvement: Normal creatinine does NOT exclude active glomerulonephritis ‚Äî always check UA
  • Subclinical Lung Disease: 1/3 have asymptomatic lung involvement ‚Äî always image chest
  • Medication-Induced ANCA: Propylthiouracil, minocycline, cocaine ‚Äî review medication history

Clinical Presentation ‚Äî Classic Features

üéØ The Classic Triad >90%
  1. Upper Respiratory Tract (>90%)
    • Chronic sinusitis, nasal crusting, epistaxis
    • Otitis media, conductive hearing loss
    • Nasal septal perforation ‚Üí saddle nose deformity
    • Subglottic stenosis (can cause airway emergency)
  2. Lower Respiratory Tract
    • Cough, dyspnea, hemoptysis
    • Nodules (often cavitary) on imaging
    • Diffuse alveolar hemorrhage (life-threatening)
  3. Renal Involvement (80%)
    • Pauci-immune glomerulonephritis
    • Often asymptomatic until advanced
    • Rapidly progressive if untreated

Treatment Protocols ‚Äî Induction & Maintenance

üíä Remission Induction ‚Äî Severe Disease LIFE/ORGAN THREATENING

All patients require:

  1. High-dose corticosteroids +
  2. Immunosuppressive agent (CHOOSE ONE):
Agent Dosing
Rituximab (Preferred) 375 mg/m¬≤ weekly √ó 4 OR 1g √ó 2 (Day 0, 14)
Cyclophosphamide IV pulse (15mg/kg q2-3wks) OR oral (2mg/kg/day)

Rituximab preferred over CYC per ACR guidelines (less toxic, equivalent efficacy). RAVE trial established non-inferiority.

Phase Duration Dose
Pulse Days 1-3 Methylprednisolone 500-1000mg IV daily
High-dose oral Weeks 1-4 Prednisone 1mg/kg/day (max 80mg)
Taper Months 2-6 Gradual reduction to 5mg/day
Target ~5 months Prednisone 5mg/day or discontinue

Reduced-dose regimen (PEXIVAS) showed 60% less steroid exposure with non-inferior renal outcomes.

üíä Remission Induction ‚Äî Non-Severe Disease NON-LIFE THREATENING

When to use: No renal insufficiency, no DAH, no CNS involvement

Agent Notes
Methotrexate + Prednisone First-line (if Cr normal). Avoid if renal impairment.
Rituximab + Prednisone Alternative, especially if concerns about MTX toxicity
Mycophenolate + Prednisone Higher relapse rate; alternative if MTX/RTX contraindicated

Contraindication: Avoid cyclophosphamide in non-severe disease due to toxicity profile.

üõ°Ô∏è Remission Maintenance 24-48 MONTHS

Start after successful remission induction (typically 3-6 months)

Agent Dosing Notes
Rituximab 500mg IV q6 months Superior to AZA (MAINRITSAN trials)
Azathioprine 2mg/kg/day Standard oral option
Methotrexate 15-25mg weekly If renal function preserved

Duration: 24-48 months. Consider indefinite therapy for patients with relapsing disease.

üíâ Infection Prophylaxis MANDATORY

Required for all patients on immunosuppression:

Indication Prophylaxis
PCP (Pneumocystis) TMP-SMX 1 DS daily (or 3x/week) for all RTX/CYC patients
High-dose steroids Consider TMP-SMX if >20mg prednisone + another agent
Osteoporosis prevention Calcium + Vitamin D; bisphosphonate if prednisone >3 months

Vaccinations: Give inactivated vaccines before B-cell depletion when possible. Avoid live vaccines during immunosuppression.

üìä Drug Monitoring & Toxicity SAFETY

Cyclophosphamide:

  • Hemorrhagic cystitis (UA, symptoms)
  • Bone marrow suppression (CBC q2weeks initially)
  • Infertility (discuss sperm banking/ovarian suppression)
  • Bladder protection: Mesna with IV pulses

Rituximab:

  • Hypogammaglobulinemia (check IgG q3-6months)
  • Late-onset neutropenia
  • Infusion reactions (premedicate)
  • Reactivation: HBV, PML (rare)

Maximum CYC lifetime exposure: 25g ‚Äî beyond this, switch to RTX to limit toxicity.
