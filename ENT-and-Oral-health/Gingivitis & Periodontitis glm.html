Gingivitis & Periodontitis

Clinical Reference Guide for Healthcare Professionals

Initial Assessment

Red Flags (URGENT REFERRAL)

  • Unexplained gingival enlargement → Leukemia workup
  • Rapid periodontitis in young adults → Aggressive periodontitis/Immunosuppression
  • Oral ulceration >3 weeks → Cancer pathway (2-wk wait)
  • Oral lump/red-white patch → Urgent dental cancer referral
Patient presents with gum symptoms
Assess for red flags
Red flags present?
Yes → Urgent Referral No → Continue Assessment

Diagnostic Features

Condition Key Symptoms Key Signs
Gingivitis Bleeding on brushing/eating Red/swollen margins, no pockets
Periodontitis Halitosis, loose teeth, root sensitivity + Pocketing, pus, mobility, recession
ANUG Acute severe pain, fever, malaise Ulcers with pseudomembrane, lymphadenopathy

Routine Management

1
Oral Hygiene Advice
  • Brush 2min 2x/day (powered brush preferred)
  • Daily interdental cleaning (brushes > floss for periodontitis)
  • Fluoride toothpaste ("spit don't rinse")
2
Referral

All cases to dentist/hygienist

3
Avoid

Routine mouthwashes/antibiotics

Special: Diabetics → urgent dental review

ANUG Management

1
Urgent Dental Referral
2
While Awaiting Dentist:
  • Metronidazole 400mg TDS x3d
  • Analgesia (paracetamol/ibuprofen)
  • Chlorhexidine/H2O2 mouthwash

Drug Note

Metronidazole: QT prolongation risk (avoid with other QT-prolonging drugs)

Special Considerations

Risk Factor Action
Smoking Cessation support
Diabetes Reinforce dental urgency
Immunosuppression Lower threshold for antibiotics
Pregnancy Monitor for worsening gingivitis

When to Suspect Malignancy

Immediate 2-week Cancer Referral For:

  • Ulcers: Non-healing >3 weeks
  • Lumps: Any persistent oral mass
  • Patches: Red/white lesions (erythroplakia/leukoplakia)

Key Drug Notes:

  • Metronidazole: QT prolongation risk
  • Analgesia: Paracetamol/ibuprofen first-line
  • Antibiotics: Only for ANUG with systemic features or immunocompromise
