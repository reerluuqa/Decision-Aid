Cholesteatoma

Clinical Decision Support Tool
Updated: Apr 2024

Urgent Referral Required

Refer urgently to ENT if patient presents with:

  • Facial Palsy (CN VII)
  • Meningitis / Intracranial Abscess
  • Semicircular Canal Fistula (Vertigo)
  • Sigmois Sinus Thrombosis
Definition: Accumulation of keratinising squamous epithelium in the middle ear/mastoid.
Key Feature: Benign but destructive (bone erosion).

History & Exam

  • Common: Malodorous ear discharge (often scant).
  • Common: Conductive hearing loss.
  • Common: Attic crust or retraction pocket (Pars flaccida).
  • Common: Discharge resistant to antibiotics.
  • Uncommon: Tinnitus, otalgia (pain), dizziness.
  • Uncommon (Red Flag): Altered taste (Chorda Tympani) or facial weakness.

Investigations

1st Line
  • Pure Tone Audiogram: Determine hearing status (Conductive loss typical).
  • Otoscopy/Microsuction: Visualize keratin/debris. Clean ear for drops.
Imaging
  • High-Res CT Temporal Bone: Standard for extent, bone erosion (scutum/ossicles).
  • MRI (Diffusion Weighted): If intracranial complications suspected or to check for recurrence post-op.

Management Overview

Definitive treatment is surgical removal. Goal: Dry, safe ear.

Pre-Operative Adjuncts
  • Aural Cleaning: Remove debris/wax.
  • Topical Antibiotics: Quinolones (Ciprofloxacin/Dexamethasone) effective for discharge.
  • Wick: Insert if canal swelling prevents drop application.
Procedure Pros/Cons Follow-up
Canal Wall Up
Preferred in Kids		 Avoids mastoid cavity. Requires 2nd look surgery for recurrence check. 2nd Look: 9-12 months OR MRI Diffusion Weighted.
Canal Wall Down
Atticotomy / Mod Radical		 Direct visualization possible. Creates open cavity (may need care swimming). Cavity exam. If attic reconstructed, needs 2nd look/MRI.

Risk Factors

  • Eustachian Tube Dysfunction: Leads to retraction pockets.
  • Congenital: Cleft palate, craniofacial abnormalities (Down/Turner).
  • History: Prior otologic surgery, traumatic blast injury.
  • Pediatric: More aggressive disease pattern than adults.
Recurrence Rate:
Residual/Recurrent disease is a significant risk. Regular follow-up is essential.

Patient Discussion Points

Recurrence

Risk persists for years. Warn patient to return if discharge recurs or hearing worsens.

Swimming

Open mastoid cavity may cause vertigo with water entry (temp change). Advise swim plugs.

Surgery

Goal is safe, dry ear. Hearing improvement is secondary and may need 2nd procedure.
