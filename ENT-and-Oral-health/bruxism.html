Suspected Sleep Apnoea (OSA)

Strong Link: Snoring, witnessed apnoeas, or excessive daytime sleepiness.

Action: Refer for Sleep Study (Polysomnography). Rule out OSA before treating solely as bruxism.

Neurological/Medication Induced

Triggers: SSRIs, Dopamine Antagonists, Ecstasy, Cocaine, or Neuro disorders (Dystonia, Parkinson's).

Action: Review medication list. Liaise with prescribing physician. Do not treat solely with oral appliances.

Classification: Sleep vs. Awake

Sleep Bruxism (SB)

Night

Masticatory muscle activity during sleep. Rhythmic (phasic) or non-rhythmic (tonic). Not a movement disorder in healthy people.

  • Partner reports grinding sounds.
  • Associated with sleep arousals.
  • Can be a protective factor for airway patency.

Awake Bruxism (AB)

Day

Repetitive/sustained tooth contact, bracing, or thrusting during wakefulness.

  • Often linked to stress/precision tasks.
  • Mostly clenching (tonic) rather than grinding.
  • Patient is usually aware of the habit.

Diagnostic Approach

1
History (Non-Instrumental)

Questionnaire/Interview. Ask about grinding sounds (from partner), jaw fatigue upon waking, and stress levels.

2
Clinical Exam (Probable Bruxism)
Tooth Wear Facets on canines/incisors, wear to dentine (at least 1 sextant).
Muscle Signs Hypertrophy (masseter), tenderness on palpation.
Mucosa Linea alba (cheek biting), Tongue scalloping.
TMJ Pain on dynamic/static tests (low association but relevant).
3
Instrumental (Definite Diagnosis)

Polysomnography (PSG): Gold standard for SB. Criteria: >4 episodes/hr of sleep.
EMG: Ambulatory portable devices.
Note: Only needed for severe cases, medico-legal reasons, or sleep apnoea suspicion.

Management Plan

Treatment is symptomatic. Target clinically relevant consequences, not the bruxism activity itself.

Counselling & Education
Explain the benign nature vs consequences. Advise on sleep hygiene. First-line therapy.
Oral Appliances
Stabilization Splints (Occlusal Splints): Reduce tooth wear and muscle activity.
Not to "correct the bite."
CBT & Physio
Cognitive Behavioral Therapy for stress/anxiety (especially AB). Physiotherapy for muscle pain relief.
Pharmacology (Rare)
Muscle relaxants or Botulinum Toxin only for short-term pain relief. Not recommended for long-term management.
