Classic Tetrad of Symptoms
V
VERTIGO Unprovoked, sudden spinning, incapacitating. Lasts minutes to hours. Nausea/vomiting common.
H
HEARING LOSS Low-frequency SNHL early, fluctuating, worsens during attacks. May become constant over time.
T
TINNITUS Roaring quality, usually unilateral. May be severe and fluctuate with attacks.
A
AURAL FULLNESS Sensation of pressure/fullness in affected ear. Often increases before attack onset.
Stepwise Treatment Algorithm
1
Dietary Modification + Lifestyle Low salt (1500-2300mg/day), limit caffeine/alcohol, smoking cessation, stress management
2
Diuretics (Maintenance) Triamterene/HCTZ 37.5/125mg daily OR Acetazolamide 250mg BID. Taper after 6 months if symptom-free.
3
Intratympanic Injections Steroids (dexamethasone/methylprednisolone) for hearing loss OR Gentamicin for intractable vertigo (hearing risk!)
4
Surgical Options (Refractory Vertigo) Endolymphatic sac surgery (non-destructive) ‚Üí Vestibular nerve section (hearing-preserving) ‚Üí Labyrinthectomy (last resort)
Chronic Hearing Loss Management
  • Digital Hearing Aids Modern devices with directional mics, wireless tech, noise processing
  • Assistive Listening Devices For situational difficulties, not ready for hearing aids
  • Intensive Audiological Counseling Essential for adjustment to and acceptance of amplification
Tinnitus Management
  • Tinnitus Retraining Therapy (TRT) Counseling + white noise generators. Benefits may take up to 18 months.
  • Neuromonics Customized neural stimulus + music therapy
  • Medications (Last Line) Amitriptyline 25-75mg/day OR Alprazolam 0.25-0.5mg TID
Required Workup

Must Order

  • MRI with Gadolinium ALL patients with asymmetric/unilateral SNHL
  • Pure Tone Audiometry Air + bone conduction, speech audiometry
  • Tympanometry Middle ear function assessment

Consider Based on Clinical Suspicion

  • TSH, Lyme, RPR For acute hearing decrease
  • ANA, ANCA, RF Bilateral symptoms + no response to treatment
  • ECochG, VEMP, ENG/VNG Specialized vestibular testing
