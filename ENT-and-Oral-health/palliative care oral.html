Palliative Oral Care Clinical Algorithm

A comprehensive guide for managing oral symptoms in palliative care patients

Rapid Assessment

Patient in Palliative Care
Oral Symptoms Present?
Assess: Dry Mouth, Pain, Ulcers, Halitosis, Taste Change, Excess Saliva
Examine: Mucosa, Tongue, Dentures, Saliva Flow
Identify Specific Problem
Key Action: If no symptoms, initiate regular oral care protocol

Symptom-Specific Management

Dry Mouth (Xerostomia)

  • Non-Pharmacological:
    • Frequent sips of water
    • Avoid alcohol/tobacco
    • Humidify oxygen
  • Pharmacological:
    • Artificial saliva (spray/gel)
    • Pilocarpine 5mg TDS*
    • Avoid anticholinergics
Pilocarpine: Avoid in asthma, COPD, cardiovascular disease

Oral Candidiasis

  • Pseudomembranous:
    • Nystatin suspension 4x/day
    • OR Miconazole gel 2x/day
  • Erythematous:
    • Fluconazole 50mg daily*
    • OR Nystatin
  • Denture-Related:
    • Clean dentures + Miconazole gel
Check drug interactions (especially fluconazole with opioids)

Mouth Ulcers

Type Features Management
Aphthous Round, yellow base, red rim Triamcinolone paste 4x/day
Herpetic Vesicles → ulcers, painful Aciclovir 400mg 5x/day x5 days
Mucositis Diffuse erythema, post-chemo Benzydamine rinse + Analgesia
Neutropenic Deep, necrotic, fever URGENT: Hematology referral

Herpes Simplex Infection

  • Aciclovir: 400mg 5x/day x5 days
  • OR Valaciclovir 500mg BD
  • Severe/Painful? → Add Analgesia

Halitosis

  • Improve Oral Hygiene:
    • Brush teeth/tongue
    • Antiseptic mouthwash
  • Malignant Ulcer? → Metronidazole 400mg TDS
Metronidazole: QT prolongation risk - check interactions

General Oral Care

Daily Care Protocol

  • Soft brush/floss teeth
  • Baking soda rinse
  • Denture removal at night
  • Regular lip moisturizing

Prevention Strategies

  • Avoid irritants (alcohol, tobacco, spicy foods)
  • Maintain adequate hydration
  • Regular dental review
  • Early intervention for symptoms

Critical Considerations

Drug Interactions:
• Fluconazole + opioids → respiratory depression
• Metronidazole + QT-prolonging drugs → arrhythmia risk
Dysphagia: Use liquids/sprays, avoid tablets
Swab Safety: Avoid foam-headed swabs (inhalation risk)
Red Flags:
• Neutropenic ulcers (ANC <0.1×10⁹/L)
• Suspected malignancy (hard, rolled edges)
• When in doubt: Refer to palliative care team/dentist

Based on NICE NG31 (End-of-life care) and CKS Palliative Oral Care (April 2025)

Algorithm designed for rapid clinical decision-making
