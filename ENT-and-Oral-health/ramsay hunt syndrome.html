URGENT

Before diagnosing RHS, exclude CVA and Tumors if these features are present:

üî¨ Confirmation

1st Line: Clinical

History & Physical Exam is usually sufficient if triad is present.

Uncertainty? PCR Swab

Swab vesicular lesions for VZV DNA PCR. Sensitivity ~100%.

Note: Consider Lyme Serology if patient has tick exposure, erythema migrans, or traveled to endemic area.

START WITHIN 72H

Prompt combination therapy is recommended for all patients.

    High-Dose Corticosteroids
    Prednisolone: 1 mg/kg/day orally (Max 60mg) for 7 days.
    Taper gradually over 5-7 days.
    Reduces inflammatory oedema. Consider PPI for gastric protection.
  • ‚úì

    Add high-dose antiviral (e.g., Aciclovir or Valacyclovir) per local guidelines.
    Reduces acute pain, improves lesions, reduces risk of post-herpetic neuralgia.

Prognosis

Majority recover movement at least partially with treatment. Improvement typically seen within weeks to months. Full stabilization can take up to 1 year.
