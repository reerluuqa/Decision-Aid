PHARMACOLOGY
Drug Dose Key Side Effects
Valganciclovir (PO) 900mg PO q12h (induction)
900mg PO daily (maintenance)		 Neutropenia, thrombocytopenia, GI upset
Ganciclovir (IV) 5mg/kg IV q12h Neutropenia, nephrotoxicity, headache
Foscarnet (IV) 60mg/kg IV q8h OR 90mg/kg q12h Nephrotoxicity, electrolyte abnormalities
Cidofovir (IV) 5mg/kg IV weekly Nephrotoxicity (requires probenecid + hydration)
Maribavir (PO) 400mg PO q12h Dysgeusia, nausea, elevated LFTs. Preferred for refractory/resistant CMV
Letermovir (PO/IV) 480mg PO daily (prophylaxis only) Headache, nausea. Not for treatment of established disease

‚óè Clinical Summary - What To Do Next

ACTION ITEMS
1
Identify Patient Risk Category

Determine immune status: immunocompetent vs transplant vs AIDS vs congenital

2
Check for Red Flags

Pneumonitis, severe GI disease, retinitis (AIDS), neurological symptoms (congenital) = urgent intervention

3
Order Appropriate Tests

Quantitative CMV PCR for all. Tissue biopsy for organ-invasive disease. Fundoscopy for visual symptoms.

4
Initiate Treatment

Immunocompetent: Supportive only. Immunocompromised: Valganciclovir PO or Ganciclovir IV. Maribavir for refractory disease.

5
Monitor & Follow Up

Serial viral loads, CBC, renal function. Continue until viral clearance + symptom resolution.
