Ataxia Assess

Clinical Overview

Definition: Neurological syndrome characterised by clumsy and uncoordinated movement of limbs, trunk, and cranial muscles.


Origin: Pathology in the cerebellum (and connections) or proprioceptive sensory pathways.

Last Updated: Aug 2025
Urgent Considerations
High Priority
Cerebellar Stroke / Haemorrhage

Can result in increased intracranial pressure due to oedema over days. Look for lethargy and signs of brain stem compression.

Cerebellar Haemorrhage: Risk of upward/downward herniation.

ICU Monitoring / Urgent Decompression
Acute Cerebellitis

Rare complication in children (post-viral/infectious). Oedema can cause raised pressure in posterior fossa.

ICU / Ventriculostomy if oedema
Miller-Fisher Syndrome

Variant of GBS. Can evolve to affect swallowing muscles and respiratory muscles rapidly.

Monitor Resp Function / Intubation
Wernicke-Korsakoff

Rapid onset of confusion, amnesia, nystagmus, oculomotor palsies, and ataxia. Associated with alcohol misuse/nutritional deficiency.

High-Dose Parenteral Vitamin B1
Diagnostic Approach
Clinical Exam

Key Signs

Gait Ataxia Unsteady gait with tendency to falls.
Limb Incoordination Dysmetria (inaccuracy of targeted movements) on finger-chase test.
Bulbar Signs Dysarthria or dysphagia may be present.
Ocular Symptoms Abnormal control of eye movements, nystagmus.

Investigations

Imaging
MRI Brain

Gold standard. Detects acute ischaemia, haemorrhage, tumours, atrophy, and demyelination. Use diffusion-weighted sequences.


Lab Work
Metabolic & Toxicology

B1, B12, E levels. Drug levels (Lithium, Phenytoin, Chemotherapy). Thyroid function.

Aetiology & Causes
Toxic
Alcohol & Drugs

Alcoholic cerebellar degeneration (midline). Anticonvulsants (Phenytoin, Carbamazepine), Chemotherapy (Cytarabine, 5-FU). Lithium, Amiodarone.

Vascular / Hypoxic
Stroke & Hypoxia

Ischaemic/Haemorrhagic stroke. Sequelae of hypoxia/heat stroke. Vascular malformations (von Hippel-Lindau).

Infectious
Infections / Post-infectious

Meningoencephalitis (VZV, Lyme, Listeria). Acute cerebellar ataxia of childhood (post-viral). HIV. Prion disease (CJD).

Neoplastic
Tumours & Compression

Medulloblastomas, Astrocytomas, Metastases. Non-neoplastic: Arnold-Chiari malformation, Basilar invagination.

Autoimmune
Immune-Mediated

Multiple sclerosis. Paraneoplastic cerebellar degeneration (Lung, Ovary, Breast). Opsoclonus-myoclonus-ataxia (OMA). Coeliac disease (gliadin antibodies).

Nutritional
Vitamin Deficiencies

Vitamin B1 (Wernicke's). Vitamin B12 (Posterior column dysfunction). Vitamin E deficiency (fat malabsorption).

Autosomal Recessive
Friedreich's Ataxia

Most common recessive ataxia. Onset 10-15 yrs. Frataxin gene mutation. Associated with cardiomyopathy, diabetes, scoliosis.

Autosomal Recessive
Ataxia Telangiectasia

Onset <5 yrs. Oculocutaneous telangiectasias, immunodeficiency, high cancer risk, radiation sensitivity. High Alpha-fetoprotein.

Autosomal Recessive
RFC1 Associated (CANVAS)

Likely most common cause of late-onset inherited ataxia. Syndrome of Cerebellar ataxia, Neuropathy, and Vestibular areflexia.

Autosomal Dominant
Spinocerebellar Ataxias (SCA)

Over 40 types. SCA3 (MJD) is most common. Often trinucleotide repeat expansions. See table below for specific breakdown.

Dominant / Episodic
Episodic Ataxia

EA1 (Brief mins, KCNA1 mutation, myokymia). EA2 (Hours, CACNA1A mutation, nystagmus/weakness).

X-Linked
Fragile X Tremor-Ataxia (FXTAS)

Late adult life. Intentional tremor, cerebellar ataxia, peripheral neuropathy. Premutation in FMR1 gene.

Common Spinocerebellar Ataxias (SCA)

Type Gene / Mutation Key Clinical Features
SCA3 (MJD) ATXN3 (CAG) Most common. Slow saccades, lid retraction, dystonia, parkinsonism, neuropathy.
SCA1 ATXN1 (CAG) Upper motor neuron signs, hypermetric saccades, dysarthria. Onset young adulthood.
SCA2 ATXN2 (CAG) Very slow saccades, peripheral neuropathy, hyporeflexia. Large Cuban founder.
SCA6 CACNA1A (CAG) Pure cerebellar syndrome. Nystagmus. Slow progression. Calcium channel.
SCA7 ATXN7 (CAG) Retinal degeneration with vision loss. Rapidly progressive. Seizures if childhood onset.
SCA10 ATXN10 (ATTCT) Mexican/Central American descent. Ataxia and seizures.
SCA17 TBP (CAG) Cognitive decline, extrapyramidal symptoms (chorea, dystonia), dementia.
DRPLA Atrophin (CAG) Ataxia, seizures, chorea, myoclonus, dementia.
