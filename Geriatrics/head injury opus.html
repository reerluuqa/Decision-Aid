üö® IMMEDIATE TRANSFER TO ED
Any ONE of these = Call ambulance if needed
  • GCS <15 on initial assessment
  • Any loss of consciousness
  • Any post-traumatic seizure
  • Focal neurological deficit
  • Suspected skull fracture (open/depressed/basal)
  • Penetrating injury or visible skull trauma
  • Amnesia >5 minutes (pre or post injury)
  • Persistent headache since injury
  • Any vomiting (clinical judgement in <12y)
  • On anticoagulants (warfarin, DOACs)
  • Bleeding/clotting disorder
  • Previous brain surgery
  • Current intoxication (alcohol/drugs)
  • Dangerous mechanism of injury
  • Safeguarding concerns
Children-specific red flags:
  • Bruise/swelling/laceration >5cm (if <1 year)
  • Tense fontanelle
  • Irritability or altered behaviour (<5 years)

üìã RETURN TO ED IF...
Advise patient/carer to return immediately if:
  • Unconsciousness or lack of consciousness
  • Confusion or unusual behaviour
  • Severe or worsening headache
  • Vomiting
  • Seizure
  • Clear fluid from ear/nose
  • Bleeding from ear
  • Weakness or sensory changes
  • Vision or hearing problems
  • Difficulty speaking or understanding
  • Balance problems
