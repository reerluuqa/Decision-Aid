TIA vs Stroke Differentiation

Feature TIA (Query) Stroke (Suspected)
Neurological Deficit COMPLETELY RESOLVED PERSISTING
Duration Typically <1 hour (seconds-minutes) >24 hours persisting
Immediate Action Aspirin 300mg + 24h specialist review Immediate admission + scanning
Key Point: 20-25% of ischemic strokes are heralded by TIA. Nearly 50% of 90-day stroke risk after TIA occurs within first 48 hours.

TIA Symptom Patterns

Definite TIA Features:
‚Ä¢ Motor weakness in 2 limbs or 1 limb + face
‚Ä¢ Sensory deficit in 2 limbs or 1 limb + face
‚Ä¢ Homonymous hemianopia or monocular blindness
‚Ä¢ Aphasia or dysarthria
Possible TIA:
‚Ä¢ Unsteady gait, diplopia, vertigo, dysphagia
‚Ä¢ Single feature = possible; 2+ features = definite
Usually NOT TIA:
‚Ä¢ Amnesia, confusion, transient LOC
‚Ä¢ Headache, incoordination
‚Ä¢ Partial sensory deficit only
Atypical TIA Warning: Patients with atypical symptoms present later, have more recurrent events, and are less likely to receive immediate treatment. When uncertain, act as if it's TIA!

Diagnosis

‚ñº

Imaging Strategy

Stroke: Non-contrast CT within 1 hour (before aspirin!)
TIA: MRI preferred over CT if scanning needed. Not everyone needs scanning.
Scan Type When to Use Purpose
Non-contrast CT All suspected strokes Exclude hemorrhage, inform thrombolysis decision
MRI (preferred for TIA) Selected TIA cases Confirm infarct, exclude differentials
CTA/MRA Within 48h if macrovascular cause likely Identify large vessel occlusion
Carotid Ultrasound All candidates for endarterectomy Measure stenosis (NASCET/ECST criteria)
Carotid Endarterectomy Indicated:
‚Ä¢ 50-99% stenosis on NASCET criteria
‚Ä¢ <50% NASCET or <70% ECST: NOT indicated

Thrombolysis Criteria TIME-CRITICAL

0-4.5 hours: Alteplase OR tenecteplase (NCGS 2023)
4.5-9 hours: Alteplase IF salvageable tissue on CT perfusion/MRI DWI-FLAIR mismatch
Requirements: Hemorrhage excluded, premorbid function considered, location/timing appropriate

Thrombectomy Criteria

Standard Window: Within 6 hours of symptom onset, proximal anterior circulation occlusion
Extended Window (6-24h): "Wake-up" strokes, known well 6-24h ago. Thrombectomy alone or with thrombolysis depending on vessel location.

Treatment

‚ñº

Immediate Management ACUTE

For Suspected TIA:

Aspirin 300mg daily START IMMEDIATELY
Unless contraindicated
PLUS: Secondary prevention to be started by secondary care (clopidogrel usually started after 2 weeks)

For Suspected Stroke:

DO NOT GIVE ASPIRIN until scan confirms ischemic stroke
Before Scan: Supportive care, BP management only if hypertensive emergency
After Hemorrhage Excluded: Aspirin 300mg within 24h, continue for 2 weeks
After Thrombolysis: Start antiplatelet after 24h (if no contraindication)

Antiplatelet Therapy PROTOCOL

High-Risk TIA/Minor Stroke (within 24h):
‚Ä¢ Clopidogrel 300mg load ‚Üí 75mg daily + Aspirin 300mg load ‚Üí 75mg daily for 21 days
‚Ä¢ Then clopidogrel monotherapy
‚Ä¢ OR: Aspirin + Ticagrelor for 30 days

Other Acute Treatments

  • Statins: Start after 48h (or immediately if swallowing safe per NCGS 2023)
  • Blood Glucose: Maintain 4-11mmol/L (NICE) or 5-15mmol/L (NCGS 2023)
  • Nutrition: Swallowing assessment on admission, weekly malnutrition screening
  • Mobilization: Early mobilization as tolerated

Lipid Management

Ischemic Stroke with Atherosclerosis:
Atorvastatin 80mg daily
Target: LDL <1.8 or non-HDL <2.5mmol/L
If Target Not Met: Add ezetimibe, bempedoic acid, or PCSK9 inhibitors
Hemorrhagic Stroke: Statin based on cardiovascular risk (QRISK) - primary prevention approach

Stroke Rehabilitation ESSENTIAL

Who: Specialist stroke MDT (PT, OT, SLT, dietitian, psychologist, nurses, social worker)

Key Rehabilitation Elements:

  • Minimum 3 hours/day, 5 days/week
  • Cognitive, swallowing, continence assessment
  • Communication, mobility, balance training
  • Vision, hearing checks
  • Spasticity management
  • Social care needs assessment
Activity: 30-40 minutes cardiorespiratory/mixed training, 3-5x/week for 10-20 weeks
