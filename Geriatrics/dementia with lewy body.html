CRITICAL WARNING: Antipsychotic Sensitivity

DO NOT PRESCRIBE TYPICAL ANTIPSYCHOTICS. Patients with DLB have severe sensitivity. Risk of neuroleptic malignant syndrome, increased mortality, and severe parkinsonism. Use low-dose atypical antipsychotics with extreme caution only if absolutely necessary.

Management & Treatment
Goal: Stabilize & Safety

Cognitive Impairment

1st Line: Cholinesterase Inhibitors (e.g., Rivastigmine, Donepezil).

May help hallucinations and cognition.

Motor Symptoms

Agent: Levodopa/Carbidopa.

Use with caution. Adverse effects may be restrictive and response limited. Monitor for hallucinations worsening.

RBD (Sleep Disturbance)

Agents: Melatonin (Low dose) or Clonazepam.

Ensure safety of sleeping environment to prevent injury.

Avoid

Anticholinergics (worsen cognition), Typical Antipsychotics (High Mortality Risk), High-dose Dopamine agonists (Psychosis risk).
