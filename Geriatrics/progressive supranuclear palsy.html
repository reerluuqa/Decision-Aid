Assessment
‚ñº

Key Diagnostic Features High Priority

  • Postural instability with unexplained falls (often backwards) within first 3 years
  • Slow vertical saccades or vertical supranuclear gaze palsy
  • Axial-predominant akinetic-rigid parkinsonism with poor levodopa response
  • Early dysphagia and/or dysarthria
  • Frontal executive dysfunction (apathy, impulsivity)

Physical Examination Checklist

Eye Movements

Test vertical saccades (slow, "round-the-houses" sign), doll's head maneuver, square wave jerks, optokinetic nystagmus

Postural Instability

Perform pull test, observe gait (wide-based, en bloc turning), sit-to-stand assessment

Cognitive Assessment

Use MoCA (preferred), evaluate executive function, verbal fluency, apathy scale

Speech & Swallowing

Assess dysarthria, apraxia of speech, water swallow test for dysphagia

MDS-PSP Diagnostic Criteria (2017) Gold Standard

Mandatory Inclusion: Age ‚â•40 years, gradual progression, sporadic occurrence

Four Functional Domains:

Ocular Motor

O1: Vertical supranuclear gaze palsy
O2: Slow vertical saccades
O3: Macro square wave jerks/eyelid-opening apraxia

Postural Instability

P1: Repeated unprovoked falls within 3 years
P2: Falls on pull-test within 3 years
P3: >2 steps backward on pull-test

Akinesia

A1: Progressive gait freezing within 3 years
A2: Axial-predominant, levodopa-resistant parkinsonism
A3: Parkinsonism with tremor/asymmetry/levodopa-responsive

Cognitive Dysfunction

C1: Speech/language disorder (non-fluent aphasia/apraxia)
C2: Frontal behavioral presentation
C3: Corticobasal syndrome

Key Differential Diagnoses

  • Idiopathic Parkinson's Disease: Excellent levodopa response, limb-predominant symptoms, hyposmia common
  • Multiple System Atrophy: Early autonomic dysfunction (orthostatic hypotension, severe urinary symptoms), cerebellar atrophy
  • Dementia with Lewy Bodies: Visual hallucinations, fluctuating cognition, REM sleep behavior disorder
  • Corticobasal Degeneration: Asymmetric limb dystonia, alien limb, myoclonus, cortical sensory loss
  • Vascular Parkinsonism: Stepwise progression, lower-body parkinsonism, MRI infarcts

Treatment
‚ñº

Multidisciplinary Team Approach Essential

Core Team: Movement disorder neurologist, physiotherapist, occupational therapist, speech-language pathologist, social worker/case manager, neuropsychologist

Goals: Symptomatic management, rehabilitation, fall prevention, optimize quality of life, advance care planning

Symptom-Specific Management Algorithm

1. Parkinsonism Levodopa Trial Required

  • Carbidopa/Levodopa: 25/100 mg TID, titrate to max 2000 mg/day levodopa over 4 weeks
  • Maintain minimum 1-3 months before assessing response (‚â§30% improvement defines resistance)
  • If no benefit, taper over 2 weeks and discontinue
  • Avoid dopamine agonists, MAO-B inhibitors, COMT inhibitors, anticholinergics

2. Gait & Balance Impairment Fall Prevention Priority

  • Daily physiotherapy: Resistance training, balance exercises, gait training (60-80 min/day, 5 days/week)
  • Assistive devices: Weighted, wide-based walker with reverse brakes (U-Step¬Æ)
  • Visual/auditory cueing for freezing of gait
  • Trial amantadine 100-300 mg/day (weak evidence, monitor for psychosis)
  • CoQ10 trial for 2 months (minimal evidence, low risk)

3. Dysphagia & Drooling Aspiration Risk

  • SLP evaluation: Modified barium swallow if silent aspiration suspected
  • Postural maneuvers (chin-tuck), thickened liquids, soft/pureed foods
  • Consider Lee Silverman Voice Treatment (LSVT)
  • PEG tube: If aspiration all textures + weight loss >10% or pneumonia
  • Drooling: Glycopyrronium (no CNS penetration) or botulinum toxin (risk of worsening dysphagia)

4. Speech Impairment Rehabilitation Focus

  • Speech therapy for dysarthria/apraxia
  • LSVT may improve voice quality
  • Augmentative communication devices (alphabet board, eye-gaze systems)
  • Pacing techniques for palilalia

5. Behavioral Symptoms Caution with Medications

  • Depression: SSRI (citalopram, escitalopram, sertraline) - avoid TCAs
  • Emotional lability/PBA: Dextromethorphan/quinidine (most effective), SSRIs as alternative
  • Impulsivity: Reduce/stop dopaminergics, trial non-dopaminergic SSRIs
  • Apathy: Generally pharmacoresistant, focus on behavioral strategies

6. Cognitive Impairment Limited Options

  • NO proven symptomatic therapy for executive dysfunction
  • If Alzheimer's-type memory impairment predominates: cautious trial of donepezil (worsens mobility)
  • Avoid memantine (may worsen cognition)
  • Occupational therapy: structured routines, daily planners, environmental modifications

7. Dystonia Botulinum Toxin

  • Botulinum toxin injection: Limb, cervical, facial (blepharospasm) dystonia - repeat q3 months
  • Avoid anticholinergics (cognitive impairment)
  • Muscle relaxants (baclofen, clonazepam) only as last resort
  • Eyelid-opening apraxia: neuro-ophthalmology referral

8. Sleep Disturbance Standard Approaches

  • Insomnia: Melatonin (also for REM behavior disorder)
  • Daytime sleepiness: Modafinil/armodafinil 200-400 mg, methylphenidate 10-60 mg/day
  • ‚ö†Ô∏è AVOID benzodiazepines - associated with faster PSP progression
  • OBSTRUCTIVE SLEEP APNEA: CPAP, positioning, weight loss

9. Constipation Standard Management

  • Non-pharmacological: Hydration, fiber, physical activity
  • Docusate stool softener, osmotic laxatives (PEG, magnesium citrate)
  • Avoid stimulant laxatives long-term

10. Urinary Symptoms Urology Input

  • Obstruction: Alpha-blockers (terazosin, doxazosin, tamsulosin)
  • Overactive bladder: Beta-3 agonist (mirabegron)
  • Avoid antimuscarinics (cognitive effects)

Prognosis & Disease Monitoring Poor Prognosis

PSP-RS (Classic)

Median survival: 6-8 years from symptom onset
Median survival: 5.3 years from diagnosis
Rapid progression: wheelchair <3 years, severe dysphagia <4 years

PSP-P (Parkinsonism)

Mean survival: 11.2 years
Slower progression, better levodopa response initially

PSP-PGF (Gait Freezing)

Mean survival: 11.5 years
Can survive up to 19 years

Poor Prognostic Factors Early Palliative Care Trigger

  • Early falls (within first year)
  • Early cognitive impairment/dementia
  • Early dysphagia (within 2 years)
  • PSP-RS phenotype
  • Older age at onset
  • Rapid symptom progression

Monitoring Tools

  • PSP Rating Scale (PSPRS): Recommended by ERN-RND to track progression
  • Clinical milestones: Walking aid requirement, wheelchair dependence, severe dysphagia, cognitive decline, recurrent falls
  • Cognitive monitoring: MoCA every 6-12 months, neuropsychological evaluation if declining
  • Functional assessment: ADLs, swallowing function, speech intelligibility

Palliative Care & Advance Planning Start Within 1st Year

  • Initiate discussions at diagnosis: Disease trajectory, prognosis, goals of care
  • Advance directives: Decision-making capacity may decline within 2-3 years
  • Feeding decisions: PEG timing, aspiration risk management
  • End-of-life planning: Preferred place of care, resuscitation status
  • Support services: PSP Association (UK), CurePSP (US), caregiver respite
