Osteoporosis Prevention Algorithm

Prevention of Fragility Fractures - Quick Reference Guide for Healthcare Professionals

Based on NICE guidelines (2017), NOGG (2021), SIGN (2020) | Last revised: April 2025

Who to Assess

  • Assess all:
    • Women ≥65 years
    • Men ≥75 years
  • Assess if younger with risk factors:
    • Previous fragility fracture
    • Current/frequent oral corticosteroids
    • History of falls
    • Family history of hip fracture
    • BMI <18.5 kg/m²
    • Smoking
    • Alcohol >14 units/week
    • Secondary causes of osteoporosis
  • Consider assessment for:
    • SSRIs, antiepileptics, PPIs
    • Aromatase inhibitors, GnRH agonists

How to Assess

  • Exclude non-osteoporotic causes:
    • Metastatic bone disease
    • Multiple myeloma
    • Osteomalacia, Paget's disease
  • Direct to DXA scan without risk calculation:
    • >50 years with fragility fracture
    • <40 years with major risk factors
  • For others:
    • Calculate 10-year fracture risk (QFracture® or FRAX®)
    • DXA scan if high risk or close to threshold
    • Assess vitamin D deficiency and calcium intake
    • Identify fall risk factors

Interpret Risk Scores

  • QFracture®: ≥10% = high risk
  • FRAX®:
    • Red zone = high risk
    • Amber zone = intermediate risk
    • Green zone = low risk
  • Threshold for DXA scan: 10% fracture risk
  • Note: FRAX® may underestimate risk with:
    • Corticosteroid use
    • Multiple fragility fractures
    • High alcohol intake
    • Heavy smoking

Management

High Risk (≥10%)

  • DXA scan → T-score ≤-2.5: offer bone-sparing treatment
  • T-score >-2.5: modify risk factors, repeat DXA within 2 years

Glucocorticoid Therapy

  • Offer bisphosphonates without BMD assessment if:
    • Prior fragility fracture
    • Women ≥70 years
    • High-dose glucocorticoids

Lifestyle Advice

  • Calcium intake ≥1000 mg/day
  • Address vitamin D deficiency
  • Fall prevention strategies
  • Smoking cessation
  • Reduce alcohol consumption

Follow Up

  • Monitor adherence to treatment
  • Assess for adverse effects
  • Review need for continuing bisphosphonate therapy (especially long-term use)
  • Regular reassessment of fracture risk
  • Consider drug holidays after 3-5 years of treatment

This reference algorithm is based on clinical guidelines from NICE, NOGG, and SIGN. Always use clinical judgment when applying this information to individual patients.
