Immediate Red Flags

Change in Alertness

Sudden confusion, LOC, or behavior changes. Suspect stroke, seizure, or infection.

Head Trauma + Anticoagulants

High risk for Subdural Haematoma. Immediate imaging required.

Signs of Fracture

Persistent pain, inability to bear weight, anatomical deformity. Ortho consult.

Key Statistics

  • Mortality: Leading cause of unintentional injury death worldwide in older adults.
  • Hip Fracture: Associated with 18-33% 1-year mortality.
  • Trend: Fall deaths increasing ~3% per year (US data).

Prevention Goal

Reduce risk by identifying multifactorial causes. Primary focus: Exercise & Medication Review.

Exercise Home Safety Vision

Diagnostic Approach

Step 1

History & Circumstances

Location: Identify environmental hazards (stairs, rugs, lighting).
Activity: Standing, walking, turning?
Associated Symptoms: LOC, chest pain, palpitations, vertigo?
Medication Review: Psychotropics, Opioids, Polypharmacy (≥5).
Comorbidities: Parkinson's, Diabetes, Osteoporosis.
Step 2

Physical Examination

Cardio

Rate/Rhythm (Arrhythmia?), Murmurs (AS?), Orthostatics.

Neuro

Mental status (delirium/dementia), Motor, Romberg, Dix-Hallpike.

Vision

Acuity, fields, Fundoscopy (Papilloedema?).

Musculoskeletal

Feet (pain/deformity), Gait (Up & Go test), Strength.

Step 3

Targeted Testing

Labs

FBC, B12, Glucose/HbA1c, Electrolytes, TSH.

Imaging

CT Head (trauma/LOC), X-ray (pain/bearing weight), MRI Spine (stenosis).

Cardiac

ECG (All syncope), Echo (Structural disease), Holter/Event monitor.

Specialized

DXA Scan (Osteoporosis), EMG (Neuropathy), EEG (Seizure).

Aetiology & Risk Factors

Neuropsychiatric

  • • Visual impairment
  • • Peripheral neuropathy
  • • Vestibular dysfunction (BPPV)
  • • Cognitive impairment (Dementia/Delirium)
  • • Parkinsonism (Shuffling, tremor)
  • • Fear of falling

Cardiovascular

  • • Syncope (Cardiac/Vasovagal)
  • • Orthostatic Hypotension
  • • Post-prandial Hypotension
  • • Carotid Sinus Syndrome

Medications & Toxic

  • High Risk: Benzos, Z-drugs, Opioids, Antipsychotics.
  • • Antihypertensives (Diuretics, Alpha-blockers)
  • • Polypharmacy (≥5 meds)
  • • Alcohol/Marijuana use

Musculoskeletal

  • • Joint instability/Arthritis
  • • Foot problems (pain, deformity)
  • • Deconditioning
  • • Sarcopaenia/Frailty

Environmental

  • • Loose rugs/tiles
  • • Poor lighting
  • • Uneven floors/Clutter
  • • Living alone / Recent cane/walker use

Differentials

Condition Key History Key Exam 1st Test / Action
Stroke Sudden numbness/weakness, aphasia, vision loss. Focal deficits, cranial nerve issues. CT Head (Diff ischaemic vs haemorrhagic).
Joint/Mechanical Gait Prior injury/athletics. Quadriceps weakness, pain with movement. Timed Up-and-Go Test.
Deconditioning Sedentary lifestyle, immobility. Difficulty rising, proximal weakness. Timed Up-and-Go Test.
Osteosarcopaenia High falls/year, fracture history. Muscle weakness, kyphosis. DXA Scan.
Meds / Polypharmacy Use of 5+ meds, Benzos, Opioids. Usually normal (diagnosis of exclusion). Chart Review / Deprescribing.
Peripheral Neuropathy Diabetes, unsteady walking. Romberg+, sensory loss. Blood Glucose, B12, EMG.
Condition Key History Key Exam 1st Test / Action
Vestibular Dysfunction Vertigo, dizziness. Nystagmus, Dix-Hallpike +. Clinical (Epley Manoeuvre).
Dementia Gradual change in function/mood. Memory loss, gait issues. MMSE / MoCA.
Delirium Acute confusion, fluctuating. Inattention, disorganized thinking. 4AT Tool, Confusion Assessment Method.
Seizure Witnessed episode, postictal state. Often normal if postictal. EEG, CT Head.
Syncope LOC, prodromal nausea/sweating. Arrhythmia, murmur. ECG (All syncope patients).
Orthostatic Hypotension Symptoms on standing. Blood pressure drop on standing. Orthostatic challenge (lie/stand 3 min).

Prevention & Management

Exercise Interventions

Strong Evidence

USPSTF recommends exercise to prevent falls in community-dwelling adults ≥65 at increased risk.

  • Most effective: Balance and functional exercises + Resistance training.
  • Tai Chi: Highly effective for balance.
  • Uncertain benefit: Resistance alone, walking, dancing.

Vitamin D & Calcium

Conditional

USPSTF Conclusion: Insufficient evidence to recommend daily Vit D >400 IU & Calcium >1g for primary fracture prevention in postmenopausal women.

Note: Low dose (800-1000 IU) may reduce falls in deficient persons. Shared decision-making required.
