Elder Abuse & Neglect

BMJ Best Practice Reference • Updated Oct 2025
High Index of Suspicion Required: Prevalence is ~15.7% (1 in 6). If suspected, ask screening questions directly.

RED FLAGS Critical

  • Unexplained injuries (bruises, burns, fractures)
  • Inconsistent history between patient & carer
  • Delay in seeking treatment
  • Carer dominance: Refuses to leave patient alone
  • Physical signs: Poor hygiene, pressure ulcers, malnutrition
  • Volume depletion / Unexplained weight loss
  • Withdrawn / Agitated behavior

Screening Questions (AMA)

Ask if "Yes" to any:

  • Has anyone ever hurt you?
  • Has anyone touched you without consent?
  • Has anyone taken things without asking?
  • Are you afraid of anyone at home?
  • Has anyone failed to help you when needed?

Risk Factors Checklist

Tap items to track clinical assessment

  • Age > 75 years
  • Cognitive impairment / Dementia
  • Dependence on carer for personal care
  • Depression/Mental illness in CARER
  • Substance misuse (Patient or Carer)
  • Financial dependence of CARER on patient
  • Social isolation / Loneliness

Diagnostic Tools

EASI
6 questions, <5 mins. Validated for family practice.
H-S/EAST
Cross-cultural adaptation. Emergency/Outpatient.
VASS
Identifies risk in older women specifically.

Initial Investigations (1st Line)

Test Rationale / Indication
FBC + Platelets + Clotting Evaluate bruising/bleeding causes, exclude leukemia/anaemia.
Clinical Photograph Record extent/site of bruises or wounds.
Basic Metabolic Profile Check electrolyte abnormalities, volume depletion.
X-Rays Detect fractures (look for multiple or various healing stages).
Toxicology Screen Identify non-prescribed substances or missing medications.
Consider Secondary Imaging if: Neurological signs → CT Head; Abdominal signs → CT Abd/LFTs/Amylase.
Global Definition (WHO): "Single or repeated act, or lack of appropriate action, occurring within any relationship where there is an expectation of trust, which causes harm or distress."

Treatment Algorithm Overview

1. Suspected

Action: Social Services Evaluation.

Ensure safety, dignity, and patient preferences.

2. Confirmed

Action: Report to Authorities.

Mandatory reporting varies by state/country. Inform Law Enforcement.

3. Treatment

Action: Intervention + Injury Management.

MDT approach (Day care, respite, legal aid). Treat injuries physically.

Intervention Strategies

Use a multi-disciplinary team (MDT) approach.

  • Linkages: Home health, adult day care, respite care.
  • Guardianship (if appropriate).
  • Referral to counseling / support groups.
  • Financial / Legal assistance.
  • Housing assistance / Convalescent home.
  • Emergency responses (Food, physical/mental health).
  • Counselling the abuser.

Prognosis & Complications

Mortality Risk:
13-year survival rate:
• Abused: 9%
• Self-Neglect: 17%
• Control: 40%
  • Physical: Pressure ulcers, fractures (Hip, Wrist, Spine), Brain injury.
  • Psychological: Depression, Delirium, PTSD, Learned helplessness.

Differential Diagnosis

Condition Key Differentiators
Dementia Paranoia/delusions. May falsely accuse carers. Home health eval helps.
Falls Injury consistent with type of fall. Check neurological/sensory deficits.
Med Non-adherence Features of underlying illness return (HTN, Thyroid). Previous history?
Substance Misuse Behavioral symptoms from use/withdrawal. Urine/blood tox screen.
Depression Cognitive disturbance. Use Geriatric Depression Scale.
