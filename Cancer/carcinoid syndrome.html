Life-threatening catecholamine surge during stress/surgery. Prophylactic octreotide required. Features: hypotension, flushing, bronchospasm, arrhythmias.

Definition & Pathophysiology

Carcinoid syndrome occurs due to release of serotonin (5-HT) and vasoactive peptides into systemic circulation from functional neuroendocrine tumors (NETs).

Key Mechanism

When liver metastases are present, amines drain into systemic circulation before hepatic degradation ‚Üí symptoms develop. 95% of patients with carcinoid syndrome have liver mets.

Occurrence Rates

  • Midgut NETs: 20-30%
  • Bronchial carcinoids: ~5%
  • Pancreatic NETs: ~1%

Incidence (Western)

  • NETs: ~7 per 100,000
  • ~19% of NETs have carcinoid syndrome

Clinical Presentation

  • Diarrhoea 75%
  • Flushing (face/trunk) Up to 90%
  • Abdominal pain/cramps 40%
  • Palpitations Common
  • Wheeze/bronchospasm 10-20%
  • Pellagra (dermatitis, diarrhoea, dementia) ~5%

Flushing Triggers

Stress, tyramine-rich foods (chocolate, bananas, walnuts), alcohol, large meals

üö® Red Flags & Complications

1. Carcinoid Crisis (LIFE-THREATENING)

During surgery/intervention: Hypotension (occasionally hypertension), tachycardia, arrhythmias, bronchospasm, flushing. Mortality significant without prophylaxis.

2. Carcinoid Heart Disease (20-70% with mets)

Right-sided valve fibrosis (tricuspid regurgitation, pulmonary stenosis). Right heart failure signs. 3-year survival: 31% vs 69% without cardiac involvement.

3. Bowel Obstruction/Perforation

Mesenteric fibrosis causing sub-acute or acute obstruction. May require emergency surgery. Present with severe abdominal pain, distension.

    Lu-177 DOTA-octreotate. Symptomatic improvement 60-80%. PFS benefit confirmed.

    Consider prophylactic cholecystectomy (60% develop gallstones on SSA).

üõ°Ô∏è Perioperative Octreotide Protocol

MANDATORY for All Procedures

Prophylactic octreotide prevents carcinoid crisis during surgery, biopsy, hepatic embolisation, chemotherapy initiation.

European Guidelines Protocol

  • Start: 12 hours pre-op
  • Continue: Throughout surgery
  • Duration: Until clinically stable post-op
Typical Regimen
Octreotide 500mcg/hr IV infusion
Consult specialist for dosing. Follow local protocols.

If Carcinoid Crisis Occurs

Give IV bolus octreotide 500-1000mcg, then infusion. Add IV hydrocortisone if refractory. Aggressive IV fluids for hypotension.
