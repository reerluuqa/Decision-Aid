Any unexplained swelling or lump in the thyroid gland.
↓
SUSPECTED CANCER REFERRAL

Clinical Context

  • Over 2500 new cases/year in UK.
  • ~70% of diagnoses in females.
  • 5-year survival rate is > 90%.

Presentation Overview
  • Laryngeal: Hoarseness is most common. Diagnosis via laryngoscopy in secondary care.
  • Oral: Persistent ulceration, mass, or bleeding. Diagnosis via biopsy in secondary care.
  • Thyroid: Nodule or diffuse swelling. May present with lymphadenopathy.
Safety Netting
  • Explain which symptoms to look out for and when to return.
  • Provide written safety netting information if appropriate.
  • Consider a review for people with low-risk symptoms that don't meet referral criteria.
  • Ensure investigation results are reviewed and acted upon.
Patient Information & Support
Communication: Discuss preferences for involvement in decision-making.
Reassurance: Explain that most people referred will not have cancer, but discuss alternatives.
Details: Cover location of referral, wait times, tests involved, and contact info if appointment is missed.
