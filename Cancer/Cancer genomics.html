Family History is Our Main Tool in Primary Care Genomics

ðŸ“‹ Key Terminology

Genomics

The study of all our DNA (genes + the DNA in between genes)

Germline Variant

A DNA change present in all cells that can be inherited (e.g., BRCA1/2)

Somatic Variant

A DNA change present only in specific tissue/cells (e.g., HER2 in breast cancer)

Pathogenic Variant

The new term replacing "mutation" - a DNA change with clinical consequences

Use the "Rule of Thumb":
2 relatives with same cancer < age 60
OR
3 relatives with same cancer at any age
  • Young age at diagnosis (e.g., bowel cancer at 35)
  • Multiple primary cancers in one person
  • Bilateral cancers
  • Rare cancer types
  • Specific cancer combinations (breast+ovarian, bowel+endometrial)
  • Certain ethnic backgrounds (e.g., Ashkenazi Jewish for BRCA)

ðŸ‘¥ Family History Essentials

  • Always ask about family history - it's our main genomic risk assessment tool
  • Document ages at diagnosis, types of cancer, and relationship to patient
  • Advise patients: "Come back if family history changes"
  • For referral to genetics, try to include an affected relative's details
  • Use A&G if unsure about referral criteria
Note: Only ~5% of cancers have inherited predisposition

ðŸ©º Clinical Scenarios

Trudy (35, bowel cancer)
  • Young age + fit lifestyle = red flags
  • Diagnosed with Lynch syndrome
  • Increases risk of bowel, endometrial, ovarian cancers
  • Consider contraception advice
Simon (74, lung cancer, smoker)
  • Environmental etiology likely
  • No strong inherited suspicion
  • Somatic testing for targeted therapy organized by oncology

Remember: Our role is identifying those at above population risk

ðŸ’¡ Practical Tips

  • Distinguish "genetic cancer" (somatic) from "inherited cancer" (germline)
  • Be aware of Lynch syndrome (bowel, endometrial, ovarian cancer link)
  • Know BRCA associations (breast, ovarian, prostate, pancreatic)
  • For direct-to-consumer genetic tests: advise patients to go back to provider for interpretation
  • Consider risk reduction: healthy lifestyle, screening, symptom vigilance
