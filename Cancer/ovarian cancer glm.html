Ovarian Cancer Referral

Last Revised: April 2025 (NICE CKS)
Primary Care Tool
Alert for Women >50: New onset IBS symptoms in the last 12 months require ovarian cancer investigation. Do not assume IBS.

When to Suspect (Red Flags)

Abdominal Distension
Feeling bloated or full.
Persistent / Frequent (>12x/mo)
Early Satiety
Feeling full quickly / Loss of appetite.
Persistent / Frequent (>12x/mo)
Pelvic or Abdominal Pain
Persistent discomfort in lower torso.
Persistent / Frequent (>12x/mo)
Urinary Urgency
Increased frequency or need to rush.
Persistent / Frequent (>12x/mo)

Management Pathway

Examination Findings

If Ascites or Pelvic/Abdominal Mass present:

  • Ensure mass is NOT uterine fibroids.
  • Do not wait for blood results.
↓
Normal Examination?

Proceed to blood test.

Serum CA125 Test
Cut-off: 35 IU/mL
≥ 35 IU/mL (Raised)

Arrange urgent Ultrasound (Abdomen & Pelvis).

If US suggests cancer → Urgent 2WW Refer.

< 35 IU/mL (Normal)

Consider other causes (e.g., IBS, UTI). Review if symptoms persist/worsen.

Risk Factors Snapshot

Increased Risk (↑)

  • Age: Risk peaks in 8th decade.
  • Genetics: BRCA1/2 mutations (Lifetime risk ~28-44%).
  • Family History: Mother/sister with ovarian cancer.
  • HRT: Small increased risk.
  • Endometriosis: Associated with increased incidence.
  • Other: Nulliparity, early menarche, late menopause.

Reduced Risk (↓)

  • Pregnancy: Higher number of pregnancies.
  • Contraception: Use of combined oral contraceptive pill.
  • Breastfeeding.
  • Surgery: Tubal ligation or hysterectomy.

Key Differential Diagnoses

Symptom Consider
Bloating / Distension Uterine fibroids, Ascites (Heart failure/Cirrhosis), Adenomyosis.
Altered Bowel Habit IBS, Constipation, Coeliac disease, IBD.
Urinary Frequency Recurrent UTIs.
Raised CA125 Endometriosis, PID, Fibroids, Pregnancy, Peritoneal irritation.
