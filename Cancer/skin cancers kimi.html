üìã Presentation

Melanoma

Pigmented lesion on skin. Rarely nodular or amelanotic. May present after lymph node spread.

Squamous Cell Carcinoma

Raised lesion with typical features. Often diagnosable visually but confirmed by biopsy.

Basal Cell Carcinoma

Ulcer with raised rolled edge, prominent vessels, or pearly/waxy nodules. Visual diagnosis possible.
