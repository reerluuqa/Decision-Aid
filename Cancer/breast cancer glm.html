Symptom Action Cards

Presentation & Diagnosis

Common Presentations

  • Breast lump (most common). Usually painless but can be painful.
  • Nipple symptoms: Change in shape, bleeding, discharge.
  • Skin changes: Tethering, dimpling, peau d'orange.

Diagnostic & Referral Process

Discuss with a specialist (phone/email) if uncertain about interpretation of symptoms.

Safety Netting: Explain symptoms to look out for. Reassure low-risk patients but ensure they know when to return. Review results promptly (beware of false negatives).

Referral Timelines

  • Suspected Cancer Pathway Diagnosis or ruling out of cancer within 28 days of urgent referral.
  • Urgent To happen/be performed within 2 weeks.
  • Very Urgent To happen within 48 hours.
  • Non-Urgent Standard timescale (e.g., < 30 breast lump).
