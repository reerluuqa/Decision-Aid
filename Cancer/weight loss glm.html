Does the patient meet the clinical definition of significant weight loss? (>5% in 6-12 months)

Definition & Overview

Definition: Loss of ≥ 5% of usual body weight within 6–12 months, not due to treatment of known illness.

High Mortality Risk Cancer Marker Organ Failure Sign

Emergencies & Red Flags

Adrenal Crisis

ACT NOW

Signs:

  • Sudden hypotension
  • Reduced organ perfusion
  • Acute abdominal pain

Action: Immediate IV fluids, Hydrocortisone/Dexamethasone. Check electrolytes.

Thyroid Storm

ICU REQUIRED

Signs:

  • Fever, Altered mental status
  • Tachyarrhythmia
  • Gastrointestinal symptoms

Action: Beta-blockers, Glucocorticoids, Anti-thyroid drugs.

DKA / Hyperosmolar State

METABOLIC

Signs:

  • Polyuria, Polydipsia
  • Altered mental status
  • Kussmaul breathing (DKA)

Action: IV Fluids, Insulin, Electrolyte correction.

Severe Starvation

SUICIDE RISK

Signs:

  • Severe Bradycardia
  • Hypothermia
  • Electrolyte imbalance

Action: Psych assessment, Re-feeding precautions, Cardiac monitoring.

History Checklist

Social & Functional

  • Access to food (Poverty/Isolation)
  • Dental issues / Dysphagia
  • Alcohol or Substance use

Medications (Review List)

Topiramate Metformin/GLP-1 SSRIs Donepezil Laxatives/Diuretics

Red Flag Symptoms

  • Dysphagia (Odynophagia)
  • Hematochezia/Melena
  • Night sweats/Fever

Physical & Baseline Labs

Physical Exam Focus

Oral Cavity: Stomatitis, Thrush
Respiratory: COPD signs, Clubbing
Lymph Nodes: Lymphadenopathy
Neuro: Cognitive decline, Tremor

First-Line Investigations

  • CBC (Anemia, Infection)
  • ESR/CRP (Inflammation/Malignancy)
  • TSH, Glucose
  • LFTs, Renal Function, Ca2+
  • Urinalysis
  • CXR (Lung cancer, TB, Infection)

Common Aetiologies

Malignancy

High risk if rapid loss + smoking history.

  • GI: Pancreatic, Gastric, Colon
  • Lung: Smoker/Non-smoker
  • Heme: Lymphoma, Leukemia, Myeloma
  • Other: Renal, Ovarian, Breast

Psychiatric

Highly common, rule out organic causes first.

  • Depression: Anorexia common
  • Anxiety: Somatic overlap
  • Eating Disorders: Anorexia/Bulimia
  • Dementia: Forgetting to eat

Gastrointestinal

Often associated with diarrhea or pain.

  • Coeliac Disease
  • Pancreatic Insufficiency
  • IBD (Crohn's/UC)
  • Mesenteric Ischemia (Post-prandial pain)

Endocrine

Check TSH and Glucose routinely.

  • Hyperthyroidism: Increased appetite + loss
  • Diabetes T1: Polyuria, Polydipsia
  • Adrenal Insufficiency: Fatigue

Chronic Disease

Look for signs of Cachexia.

  • Heart Failure: Cardiac cachexia
  • COPD: Increased work of breathing
  • Renal Failure: Renal cachexia
  • HIV/AIDS: Opportunistic infections

Infections

Consider travel and immune status.

  • Tuberculosis
  • Endocarditis: Subacute
  • Parasites: Giardia, Cryptosporidium
  • Hepatitis B/C
