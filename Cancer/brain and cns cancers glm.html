Brain & CNS Cancers

NICE CKS Quick Ref

Adults (18+)

Trigger Symptom: Progressive, sub-acute loss of central neurological function.

Urgent Direct Access MRI Or CT if MRI contraindicated
≤ 2 Weeks

Children & Young People

Trigger Symptom: Newly abnormal cerebellar or other central neurological function.

Very Urgent Referral Suspected cancer service
≤ 48 Hours

Recognition & Presentation

Look for these common signs, especially if new-onset or progressive:

New-onset Seizures
Headache
Nausea / Vomiting
Drowsiness
Visual Change
Personality Change

Referral Timelines

Term Target
Immediate Acute admission / few hours
Very Urgent Within 48 hours
Urgent Within 2 weeks
Suspected Cancer Pathway Diagnosis/Rule-out within 28 days

Safety Netting & Advice

  • Inform: Patient they are being referred to a cancer service. Most referrals are not cancer.
  • Discuss: Potential risks/benefits and alternative diagnoses.
  • Review: If symptoms don't meet criteria but risk persists, arrange planned or patient-initiated review.
  • Support: Provide written info on what to expect and who to contact if appointment is missed.
