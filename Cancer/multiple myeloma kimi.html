ü©∫ Initial Investigations
‚ñº
Urgent if indicated Within 48 hours

For persistent bone pain/fracture (age ‚â•60):

  • Full blood count
  • Serum calcium
  • Plasma viscosity or ESR
  • Paraproteins (serum protein electrophoresis)
  • Serum free light chains (or Bence-Jones urine if unavailable)

For hypercalcaemia/leukopenia with myeloma features:

Very urgent (within 48 hours)
  • Serum electrophoresis
  • Serum-free light-chain assay
  • Bence-Jones protein urine assessment

For abnormal PV/ESR/incidental findings:

Very urgent (within 48 hours)
  • Same panel as above
Note: 1-5% are "non-secretory" (negative electrophoresis)

Additional investigations:

  • Peripheral blood film (rouleaux formation)
  • Renal & liver function (renal impairment, uric acid)
  • X-rays of symptomatic areas (pathological fractures)

üè• Confirmed Multiple Myeloma
‚ñº
MDT Management Regular Review

Secondary Care Management:

  • Managed by haematologist + multidisciplinary team
  • Symptomatic myeloma: review at least every 3 months
  • Smouldering myeloma: review every 3 months for first 5 years, then based on stability

Primary Care Role:

Infection Management
Prompt antibiotics, avoid aminoglycosides
Pain Control
Paracetamol/codeine; avoid NSAIDs
Vaccinations
Annual influenza, one-off pneumococcal
Mental Health
Screen for depression/anxiety

Secondary Care Treatments:

  • Bisphosphonates: Zolendronic acid, pamidronate, clodronate
  • Anaemia treatment: Erythropoietin analogues, transfusion
  • Chemotherapy: Cyclophosphamide, doxorubicin, melphalan
  • Corticosteroids: Dexamethasone, prednisolone
  • Proteasome inhibitors: Carfilzomib, bortezomib
  • Immunomodulators: Thalidomide, lenalidomide, pomalidomide
  • Monoclonal antibodies: Daratumumab
  • Stem cell transplant
  • DVT prophylaxis: Aspirin, LMWH (with immunomodulators)
‚ö∞Ô∏è End-Stage Multiple Myeloma
‚ñº
Palliative Focus End-of-life care
Suspect end-stage when:
‚Ä¢ Symptoms refractory to treatment
‚Ä¢ Multiple treatment lines exhausted
‚Ä¢ Rapid deterioration
‚Ä¢ Bone pain, renal failure, bone marrow failure
‚Ä¢ Infection (common cause of death)
‚Ä¢ Increasing fatigue, anorexia, weight loss

Management Approach:

  • Coordinate care with MDT, district nurses, palliative care team
  • Early palliative care referral improves outcomes & reduces admissions
  • Optimize symptom control (pain, breathlessness, secretions, insomnia)
  • Adjust drug doses for deteriorating renal function
  • Consider hospice admission if needed

Communication & Planning:

  • Discuss disease process and prognosis
  • Explain what dying might be like and symptom management
  • Advance care planning (if patient wishes)
  • Review plans at clinical events or social changes

üìÖ Monitoring Schedule
‚ñº
Risk-stratified 3-12 month intervals

Symptomatic (Active) Myeloma:

  • Review by haematologist at least every 3 months
  • Frequency based on risk factors for progression

Asymptomatic (Smouldering) Myeloma:

  • Every 3 months for first 5 years
  • Then frequency based on long-term stability

Primary Care Review:

  • Regular assessment of symptoms and complications
  • Vaccination status check annually
  • Pain assessment at each visit
  • Mental health screening as needed
