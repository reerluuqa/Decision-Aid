üí§ Management: Shift Work
Sleep Environment Dark, cool, quiet room. Blackout blinds/earplugs essential.
Napping Strategy Short protected naps (< 30 mins) before/during shifts.
Commute Home Wear sunglasses to avoid morning light (prevents wakefulness).
Light Exposure Seek bright light during the night shift.
Caffeine Use just before and during shift. Avoid 3 hrs before sleep.
Work Schedule Clockwise rotation (Day ‚Üí Evening ‚Üí Night) is best.
Occupational Health: Night workers (3hrs between 11pm-6am) are entitled to regular free health assessments.
üåç Management: Jet Lag
Melatonin (Licensed) Short-term treatment for jet lag in adults. Taken at destination bedtime.
Eastbound Travel Harder. Advance sleep. Seek morning light, avoid evening light.
Westbound Travel Easier. Delay sleep. Seek evening light.
General Advice: Maintain hydration, avoid excess alcohol, and adopt local time immediately upon arrival.
üíä Pharmacology Summary
SAFE / LICENSED

Melatonin: Licensed for short-term jet lag treatment. Also available for insomnia in ADHD children (Circadin/Melatonin Orion).

AVOID / RESTRICTED

Modafinil: Do NOT use for shift work (Cardiovascular/Psychiatric risks). Only for Narcolepsy.

üîé Differential Diagnosis
Condition Distinguishing Feature
Travel Fatigue General fatigue/headache from any long journey. Settles after one normal sleep. No circadian mismatch.
Insomnia Chronic difficulty sleeping not linked to shift/travel timing.
Obstructive Sleep Apnoea Snoring, witnessed apnoeas, daytime sleepiness unrelated to work schedule.
Depression/Anxiety Low mood, anhedonia, worry. May coexist with shift work disorder.
Delayed Sleep-Wake Phase Persistent pattern of late sleep/late wake, not caused by external shifts.
üìâ Risk Factors & Complications
Increasing Risk:
  • Shift Work: Older age, short recovery between shifts, rapid rotation, high number of night shifts.
  • Jet Lag: Number of time zones, Eastbound travel, older age, dehydration, excess alcohol/caffeine.
Potential Complications:
  • Metabolic: Obesity, Type 2 Diabetes, Cardiovascular disease.
  • Safety: Work errors, accidents (Chernobyl, Exxon Valdez cited examples).
  • Psychological: Depression, cognitive impairment, 'sleep debt'.
