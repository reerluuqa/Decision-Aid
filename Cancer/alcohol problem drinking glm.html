Alcohol - Problem Drinking

CKS Summary
Last Revised: May 2025
Low-Risk Guidelines
Safe Limit

≤ 14 units per week (Men & Women)

  • Spread evenly over 3 or more days.
  • Pregnancy: Safest approach is to avoid alcohol completely.
  • 1 Unit = 10ml (8g) pure ethanol.

Risk Definitions
  • Hazardous (Increasing Risk):
    Men: >14 & <50 units/wk
    Women: >14 & <35 units/wk
  • Harmful (Higher Risk):
    Causing health problems (psychological or physical).
  • Dependence:
    Craving, tolerance, preoccupation, continued drinking despite harm.

Screening & Diagnosis

Who to Screen?

Routine screening in primary care for:

  • New patient registration
  • Chronic disease management
  • Medication review
  • Antenatal appointments
  • Mental health problems (anxiety/depression)
  • Minor injuries/Assault
Incidental findings: Raised GGT/MCV, dilated capillaries, hand tremor, smelling of alcohol, frequent accidents.

Neurological Emergency

Wernicke-Korsakoff Syndrome

Thiamine Deficiency. Treat urgently.

  • Wernicke's: Ocular motility disorders, ataxia, confusion.
  • Korsakoff's: Anterograde/retrograde amnesia, confabulation (often follows low-dose tx).

Withdrawal Signs

  • Tremor, hallucinations, seizures.
  • Refer/Admit if moderate/severe dependence.

Management Goals
1. Identification

Screen and identify harmful drinkers.

2. Reduction

Help reduce or stop drinking to prevent harm.

3. Referral

Refer for specialist assessment/treatment if dependent.

4. Abstinence

Encourage continued abstinence where appropriate.
