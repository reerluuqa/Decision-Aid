Platelets - Abnormal Counts & Cancer

NICE CKS Quick Reference • Last Revised April 2025
Clinical Tool

High Cancer Risk Detected

Thrombocytosis (>450) + Age > 40 has a PPV of 11.6% (men) / 10.1% (women) for cancer.
Lung and Colorectal are common sites. 1/3 of patients may have NO other symptoms.

Thrombocytosis

Definition: Count > 450 × 10⁹/L

Cancer Red Flags
  • Age > 40 (High risk threshold)
  • Unexplained cough or haemoptysis
  • Change in bowel habit
  • Rectal bleeding
  • Unexplained weight loss
  • Dysphagia
  • Persistent dyspepsia (>55 yrs)
  • Post-menopausal bleeding (>55 yrs)

Common Non-Cancer Causes:

Infection/InflammationRheumatoid arthritis, SLE
Iron DeficiencyMost common cause
DrugsCorticosteroids
Surgery/TraumaRecent bleeding
SplenectomyHyposplenism

How to assess Thrombocytosis

History Taking

  • Recent infection or inflammation
  • History of cancer / family history of cancer
  • Prior splenectomy
  • Medications (e.g., steroids)
  • Cardiovascular risk factors

Investigations

  • Repeat FBC (Trend?)
  • Blood Film (Reactive usually have small platelets; Primary may have large platelets)
  • CRP / ESR (Inflammatory markers)
  • Iron studies (Ferritin)
  • JAK-2 test (if Essential Thrombocythaemia suspected)
  • Chest X-ray (Age >40)
  • FIT test (Colorectal rule out)

How to assess Thrombocytopenia

History Taking

  • Alcohol intake, dietary habits
  • Medications: Quinine, Heparin, NSAIDs, Penicillins
  • Recent infections / vaccinations
  • Symptoms of autoimmune disease
  • Travel history (Malaria)
  • Pregnancy status (Pre-eclampsia risk)

Investigations

  • Review Blood Film (Exclude Pseudothrombocytopenia)
  • Reticulocyte count
  • Coagulation screen (PT/APTT)
  • LFTs / Renal Function
  • HIV / Hep C / EBV serology if indicated
  • B12/Folate levels
  • Autoimmune screen (ANA, Antiphospholipid)

Management & Referral Principles

Urgent Referral (Suspected Cancer)

Refer urgently (within 2 weeks) if:

  • Thrombocytosis > 450 in patients aged >40.
  • Accompanied by red flag symptoms (Cough, weight loss, change in bowel habit, dyspepsia).

Immediate Actions

  • Severe Thrombocytopenia (<50): Seek specialist haematology advice immediately, especially if bleeding present.
  • Pregnant women >20wks: Rule out Pre-eclampsia / HELLP syndrome immediately.
  • Stop potential culprit drugs if safe to do so.
