Pancreatic Cancer

Quick-Scan Clinical Reference

IMMEDIATE CONSIDERATION

Suspect Pancreatic Cancer in patients aged ≥60 with weight loss + ANY of: new-onset diabetes, back pain, abdominal pain, or nausea.

Epidemiology

Peak Age 65-74 years
5-Year Survival 13.3% (US)
Median Survival 4.6 months
3rd leading cause of cancer death in the US. Usually presents late with advanced disease.

Red Flag Symptoms

  • Painless Obstructive Jaundice (Head tumor)
  • Unexplained Weight Loss & Anorexia
  • New-onset Diabetes (>50 yrs)
  • Epigastric pain radiating to Back
  • Trousseau's Sign (Migratory thrombophlebitis)

Diagnostic Pathway

1
Pancreatic Protocol CT
Triphasic, thin slices. Gold standard.
Diagnostic rate 97%. Predicts resectability 80-90%.
2
CA 19-9 Biomarker
Not for screening. Monitor progression.
FP in benign jaundice/pancreatitis. Sensitivity 70-90%.
3
EUS / EUS-FNA
If CT negative or needs tissue.
High sensitivity for small tumors (2-3mm). Risk of seeding.

Risk Factors

Strong

  • Smoking (1.7x risk)
  • Family History
  • Hereditary Syndromes

Weak

  • Chronic Pancreatitis
  • Obesity
  • Diabetes Mellitus
  • Peutz-Jeghers (STK11)
  • Hereditary Pancreatitis (PRSS1)
  • Familial Breast Cancer (BRCA1/2, PALB2)
  • Lynch Syndrome

Management: Curative

Surgical Resection

Only hope for cure. Only possible in 5-10% of patients.

  • Adjuvant Chemo: Prolongs survival.
  • Resectable Criteria: No SMA/CHA/CA involvement; Patent SMV/PV.

Prognosis & Staging

Metastatic at Dx 50-55%
Survival (Metastatic) 3-6 months
Borderline Resectable: May benefit from neoadjuvant therapy.
Locally Advanced: Unresectable arterial contact >180°.

Differentials

Chronic Pancreatitis Pain, malabsorption
Cholangiocarcinoma Painless jaundice
Bile Duct Stones Painful jaundice
Ampullary Carcinoma Waxing/waning jaundice

Emergency / Palliative Actions

Biliary Obstruction

If surgery not elected/delayed: ERCP Stenting for palliation.

Thromboembolism

High risk of DVT/PE. Manage with anticoagulation per protocol.

Severe Pain

Celiac plexus block or strong analgesics required for back pain.
