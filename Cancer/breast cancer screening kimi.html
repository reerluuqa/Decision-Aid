Risk Stratification

Determine Management Pathway

‚ñº

Primary Care Management Suitable for GP Management

Criteria: Patients can be managed in primary care if they have:
‚úÖ
Single Affected Relative One first-degree OR second-degree relative diagnosed with breast cancer at age 40+ years

AND none of the following additional risk factors:

  • Bilateral breast cancer
  • Male breast cancer
  • Ovarian cancer
  • Jewish ancestry
  • Sarcoma in relative <45 years
  • Two+ relatives with breast cancer on father's side

Secondary Care Referral Urgent Referral Required

Family History Services Referral Criteria

Refer to secondary care family history service if any of the following are present:

üö®
Early-Onset Breast Cancer One first-degree female relative diagnosed under age 40 years
üë®
Male Breast Cancer One first-degree male relative diagnosed at any age
üîÑ
Bilateral Breast Cancer One first-degree relative with bilateral disease where first primary was diagnosed under age 50
üë•
Multiple First-Degree Relatives Two first-degree relatives, OR one first-degree and one second-degree relative diagnosed at any age
‚ö†Ô∏è
Breast + Ovarian Cancer One first-degree or second-degree relative with breast cancer AND one with ovarian cancer (one must be first-degree)
üìä
Three or More Relatives Three first-degree or second-degree relatives with breast cancer at any age

Specialist Genetics Referral High Risk

üß¨
Known Gene Mutation Refer directly to specialist genetics service if a high-risk predisposing gene mutation has been identified (e.g., BRCA1, BRCA2, TP53)

Specialist Advice Seek Advice

Seek specialist advice from secondary care if:
  • Any additional risk factors present beyond the referral criteria above
  • Uncertain whether to refer
  • Patient not reassured by information provided
  • Complex family patterns that don't fit standard criteria
