Dyspepsia Management Algorithm

Clinical Decision Support for Unidentified Cause Dyspepsia

Definition

Dyspepsia: Upper GI symptoms (≥4 weeks) including:

  • Epigastric pain/discomfort
  • Heartburn
  • Acid reflux
  • Nausea/vomiting

Key Point

In patients without prior endoscopy

Initial Assessment

Alarm Symptoms

  • Dysphagia
  • Age ≥55 with weight loss
  • Persistent vomiting
  • GI bleeding
  • Palpable mass
  • Iron deficiency anemia

History

  • Symptom frequency/duration/pattern
  • Lifestyle factors (obesity, trigger foods)
  • Smoking/alcohol status
  • Stress/anxiety/depression
  • Medication review (NSAIDs, aspirin)
  • Family history of upper GI malignancy

Examination

  • Weight/BMI trend
  • Signs of anemia
  • Abdominal examination

Initial Management

Lifestyle Modifications

  • Weight loss if overweight
  • Avoid trigger foods (coffee, chocolate, tomatoes)
  • Smaller meals, avoid eating 3-4h before bed
  • Smoking cessation
  • Reduce alcohol consumption
  • Stress management

Medication Review

  • Stop/reduce exacerbating medications if possible
  • Consider short-term antacids/alginates

Choose One Strategy

Option A: Full-dose PPI for 1 month
Option B: Test for H. pylori
  • Use ¹³C-urea breath test or stool antigen test
  • No PPI for 2 weeks or antibiotics for 4 weeks prior
  • If positive, offer first-line eradication therapy

H. pylori Management

First-line Eradication (7 days)

Standard: PPI (twice daily) + amoxicillin 1g (twice daily) + clarithromycin 500mg (twice daily) OR metronidazole 400mg (twice daily)
Penicillin Allergy: PPI (twice daily) + clarithromycin 500mg (twice daily) + metronidazole 400mg (twice daily)
Penicillin Allergy + Previous Clarithromycin: PPI (twice daily) + metronidazole 400mg (twice daily) + tetracycline 500mg (four times daily) + bismuth subsalicylate 525mg (four times daily)

PPI Dosing

  • Lansoprazole 30mg
  • Omeprazole 20-40mg
  • Esomeprazole 20mg
  • Pantoprazole 40mg
  • Rabeprazole 20mg

Refractory or Recurrent Symptoms

Reassess

  • New alarm symptoms?
  • Adherence to initial management?
  • Consider alternative diagnoses

Switch Strategy

  • If initial PPI trial → test for H. pylori
  • If initial H. pylori testing → try PPI trial

Consider

  • H₂-receptor antagonist
  • Long-term acid suppression at lowest effective dose
  • NSAID/aspirin management

H. pylori Re-testing (Selective)

  • Poor compliance with initial therapy
  • Initial test affected by PPI/antibiotics
  • Aspirin/NSAID indicated (especially with ulcer history)
  • Family history of gastric malignancy
  • Severe/persistent symptoms
  • Patient request

Second-line Eradication

Based on previous regimen and antibiotic exposure

Referral Indications

Urgent (2-week wait)

  • Dysphagia
  • Age ≥55 with weight loss

Non-urgent Endoscopy

  • Age ≥55 with treatment-resistant symptoms
  • Refractory symptoms despite optimal management

Specialist Referral

  • Persistent unexplained symptoms
  • Diagnostic uncertainty

Annual Review

For patients on long-term treatment:

  • Review symptoms and treatment
  • Step down or stop if possible
  • Use lowest effective dose
  • Consider as-needed therapy

Clinical Tip

Regularly reassess need for continued therapy
