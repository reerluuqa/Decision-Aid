Dyspepsia - Proven Peptic Ulcer

Clinical Algorithm for Primary Care Management

1
Initial Assessment
  • Assess for alarm symptoms (complications/malignancy)
  • Provide written information & lifestyle advice:
    • Weight loss if overweight
    • Avoid trigger foods (coffee, chocolate, tomatoes, fatty/spicy foods)
    • Smaller meals, evening meal 3-4 hours before bed
    • Stop smoking, reduce alcohol
    • Address stress/anxiety/depression
  • Review medications:
    • Stop NSAIDs if possible
    • Consider reducing/stopping other ulcer-inducing drugs (aspirin, bisphosphonates, corticosteroids, SSRIs)
2
H. Pylori Testing
  • Test if status unknown/uncertain:
    • First choice: Carbon-13 urea breath test or stool antigen test
    • Ensure NO PPI in past 2 weeks, NO antibiotics in past 4 weeks
    • Alternative: Laboratory serological testing (if locally validated)
Decision Point

Result determines management pathway:

  • H. pylori POSITIVE → Eradication therapy
  • H. pylori NEGATIVE → PPI therapy
3
Management Pathway
H. pylori POSITIVE
  • NSAID-associated ulcer:
    • Full-dose PPI for 2 months → THEN first-line eradication therapy
  • Non-NSAID-associated ulcer:
    • First-line eradication therapy
H. pylori NEGATIVE
  • Full-dose PPI for 4-8 weeks
4
First-line Eradication (7 days)
Standard Regimen
  • PPI (twice-daily) + amoxicillin 1g (twice-daily) +
    • Either clarithromycin 500mg (twice-daily) OR metronidazole 400mg (twice-daily)
Penicillin Allergy
  • PPI (twice-daily) + clarithromycin 500mg (twice-daily) + metronidazole 400mg (twice-daily)
Penicillin Allergy + Previous Clarithromycin
  • Quadruple therapy: PPI + metronidazole + tetracycline + bismuth subsalicylate
5
Follow-up
  • Gastric ulcers: Repeat endoscopy at 6-8 weeks to confirm healing
  • H. pylori re-testing:
    • Carbon-13 urea breath test (first choice) or stool antigen test
    • If positive → second-line eradication therapy
  • Unhealed ulcers: Exclude non-adherence, malignancy, inadvertent NSAID use, Zollinger-Ellison syndrome
6
Second-line Eradication (7 days)
Standard
  • PPI (twice-daily) + amoxicillin 1g (twice-daily) +
    • Alternative antibiotic (whichever not used first-line)
Previous Exposure to Both Antibiotics
  • PPI (twice-daily) + amoxicillin 1g (twice-daily) +
    • Either levofloxacin 250mg (twice-daily) OR tetracycline 500mg (four times daily)
Penicillin Allergy Options
  • Various regimens based on previous antibiotic exposure
7
Long-term Management
  • Annual review for people on long-term treatment
  • Step down/stop treatment if possible
  • Use lowest effective dose or "as-needed" basis
8
Referral Criteria
Refer to Gastroenterologist When:
  • Refractory/recurrent symptoms despite optimal management
  • Unsuccessful second-line eradication therapy
  • Limited antibiotic options
  • Unhealed gastric ulcer after appropriate therapy
  • Suspected Zollinger-Ellison syndrome or other non-peptic causes

PPI Doses: Lansoprazole 30mg, omeprazole 20-40mg, esomeprazole 20mg, pantoprazole 40mg, or rabeprazole 20mg
