IBS Management Algorithm

One-Page Reference for Clinical Decision Making

I. Definition & Diagnosis

Definition

Chronic gut-brain interaction disorder with abdominal pain associated with altered stool form/frequency

Diagnostic Criteria (symptoms ≥6 months)

  • Abdominal pain/discomfort relieved by defecation OR associated with altered bowel frequency/stool form
  • PLUS ≥2 of:
    • Altered stool passage (straining, urgency, incomplete evacuation)
    • Abdominal bloating/distension
    • Symptoms worsened by eating
    • Passage of mucus

Subtypes (based on Bristol stool form)

D
IBS-D: Diarrhea predominant
C
IBS-C: Constipation predominant
M
IBS-M: Mixed bowel habits
U
IBS-U: Unclassified

II. Assessment

History

  • Symptom onset, duration, severity
  • Diet, triggers (food, stress, medications)
  • Impact on daily functioning
  • Comorbidities (especially psychological)

Examination

  • Weight/BMI (unexplained loss = red flag)
  • Abdominal palpation (masses = red flag)
  • Rectal examination (with consent)

Initial Investigations

  • FBC (anemia)
  • ESR/CRP (inflammation)
  • Coeliac serology (if diarrhea-predominant)
  • Faecal calprotectin (if diarrhea and ≤45 years)

III. Red Flags (Refer Urgently)

  • Unintentional weight loss
  • Rectal bleeding
  • Positive FIT test
  • Change in bowel habit >60 years
  • Raised faecal calprotectin
  • Iron deficiency anaemia
  • Persistent bloating in females (>50 years)
  • Abdominal/rectal mass
  • Family history of bowel/ovarian cancer, coeliac, IBD

IV. Management

1
General Advice (All Patients)
  • Explain IBS as gut-brain axis disorder
  • Reassure: no increased cancer risk
  • Regular meals, balanced diet
  • Adequate fluid intake
  • Regular physical activity (30 min, 5 days/week)
  • Probiotics (trial for 12 weeks)
  • Address stress/anxiety/depression
2
Symptom-Specific Diet Advice

IBS-D/Bloating:

  • Reduce insoluble fiber (bran, whole grains)
  • Limit caffeine, alcohol, carbonated drinks
  • Reduce gas-producing foods

IBS-C:

  • Increase soluble fiber (ispaghula, oats, linseed)
  • Gradual increase to minimize bloating
3
Follow-Up
  • Review within 2 months
  • If persistent symptoms: consider drug treatment or referral

V. When to Refer

  • Atypical presentations (nocturnal diarrhea)
  • Features of defecatory disorder
  • Faecal incontinence
  • Diagnostic uncertainty
  • Refractory symptoms despite appropriate management
