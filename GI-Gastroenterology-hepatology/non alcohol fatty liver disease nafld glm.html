Red Flags: Immediate Referral Required

Refer to Hepatology if: Signs of advanced liver disease (jaundice, ascites), FIB-4 > 2.67, or diagnostic uncertainty.

When to Suspect NAFLD

Suspect if incidentally found abnormal LFTs or fatty liver on ultrasound, PLUS:

  • Risk Factors: Metabolic syndrome features (Obesity, T2DM, Hypertension, Hyperlipidaemia).
  • Persistent Elevation: ALT/AST raised for >3 months (usually ALT > AST).
  • No Excess Alcohol: <20g/day (women) or <30g/day (men).
  • Exclusion: Negative viral hepatitis, autoantibodies, normal ferritin/transferrin saturation.
Note: Routine LFTs can be normal in NAFLD. Do not rely on them alone to rule out disease.
Primary Assessment
  • History: Alcohol intake, fatigue, RUQ pain, drug history (e.g., amiodarone, tamoxifen).
  • Examination: BMI, Waist Circumference, BP. Check for stigmata of chronic liver disease.
  • Bloods: FBC, Clotting, HbA1c, Lipid Profile, Ferritin, TSH, Tissue Transglutaminase.
  • Imaging: Ultrasound sensitivity ~85% for moderate-severe steatosis.

Interpretation (NAFLD):

  • Low Risk (<1.3): Rule out advanced fibrosis. Manage in primary care.
  • High Risk (>2.67): Indeterminate rule-in. Refer to hepatology.
  • Indeterminate (1.3 - 2.67): Consider ELF test or specialist opinion based on clinical judgement.
Lifestyle & Diet First-line Treatment
  • Weight Loss: Target 5-10% loss over 6 months. (Deficit of ~600kcal/day).
  • Diet: Mediterranean diet is strongly recommended. Reduce sugar-sweetened beverages.
  • Exercise: Moderate intensity aerobic exercise 150-200 mins/week (3-5 sessions).
  • Alcohol: Advise within national limits. Alcohol + Obesity synergistically increases liver damage.
Pharmacology
Statins: Safe to use in NAFLD. Do not stop existing statin therapy due to abnormal LFTs. Statins may improve steatosis.

Insulin Sensitizers (Pioglitazone) / Vitamin E:

May be considered in biopsy-proven NASH in secondary care. Not first-line in primary care without specialist advice.

Monitoring & Follow-Up

Annual Review (Low Risk)

  • Repeat LFTs.
  • Monitor BMI & Weight.
  • Check HbA1c & Lipids.
  • Reassess CVD risk.

3-Yearly Reassessment

Repeat non-invasive fibrosis assessment (e.g., FIB-4) every 3 years for those with initial low risk.

Action: If signs of advanced liver disease appear or FIB-4 score increases to high risk, Refer.
Definition & Scope

NAFLD: Excess fat accumulation in the liver (steatosis in >5% of hepatocytes) unrelated to alcohol consumption.

  • Spectrum ranges from simple steatosis to Non-alcoholic Steatohepatitis (NASH).
  • NASH involves inflammation and hepatocyte injury, carrying a risk of fibrosis/cirrhosis.
  • Secondary causes: Drugs (amiodarone, tamoxifen), Hepatitis C, Endocrine disorders (PCOS, Hypothyroidism).
Prognosis
  • Simple Steatosis: Good prognosis. Very slow progression.
  • NASH: Increased risk of cirrhosis, liver failure, and HCC.
  • Mortality: Cardiovascular disease is the most common cause of death in people with NAFLD, not liver disease.
Complications

Hepatic

  • Portal Hypertension
  • Variceal Haemorrhage
  • Liver Failure
  • Hepatocellular Carcinoma (HCC)

Metabolic

  • Hypertension
  • Chronic Kidney Disease
  • Type 2 Diabetes (2x increased risk)
  • Atrial Fibrillation / CVD events
