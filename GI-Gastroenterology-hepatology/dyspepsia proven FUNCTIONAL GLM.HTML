Functional Dyspepsia Management Algorithm

Evidence-based clinical decision support for primary care

Based on NICE CKS guidance • Last revised May 2024

Definition & Assessment

Functional dyspepsia: Symptoms ≥4 weeks (upper abdominal pain/discomfort, heartburn, acid reflux, nausea/vomiting) with normal endoscopy
Subtypes: Epigastric pain syndrome OR Post-prandial distress syndrome (or both)
Initial assessment:
  • Check for alarm symptoms (dysphagia, age ≥55 with weight loss) → urgent referral
  • Review medications (NSAIDs, aspirin, etc.)
  • Assess for stress, anxiety, depression

Initial Management

Lifestyle modifications:
  • Weight loss if overweight
  • Avoid trigger foods (coffee, chocolate, tomatoes, fatty/spicy foods)
  • Smaller meals, evening meal 3-4 hours before bedtime
  • Smoking cessation
  • Reduce alcohol consumption
Medication review:
  • Consider reducing/stopping exacerbating drugs
  • Short-term antacids/alginates only (not long-term)

H. pylori Testing & Treatment

Test if status unknown:
  • Carbon-13 urea breath test or stool antigen test (no PPI 2 weeks, no antibiotics 4 weeks prior)
  • Laboratory serology if unavailable
If H. pylori POSITIVE:
First-line eradication (7 days):
PPI twice-daily + Amoxicillin 1g twice-daily +
Either Clarithromycin 500mg twice-daily OR Metronidazole 400mg twice-daily
If H. pylori NEGATIVE:
Low-dose PPI OR standard-dose H2-receptor antagonist for 1 month

Refractory or Recurrent Symptoms

Reassess for new alarm symptoms
Consider alternative diagnoses
Check adherence to initial management
H. pylori re-testing if:
  • High local antibiotic resistance
  • Poor compliance with first-line therapy
  • Aspirin/NSAID use (especially with ulcer history)
  • Family history of gastric malignancy
  • Severe/persistent symptoms
  • Patient request
If re-testing POSITIVE:
Second-line eradication (7 days):
PPI twice-daily + Amoxicillin 1g twice-daily +
Alternative antibiotic (clarithromycin or metronidazole, whichever not used first-line)
If negative or not tested:
Low-dose PPI OR H2-receptor antagonist for 1 month
Consider switching acid suppression therapy
Long-term acid suppression at lowest effective dose
Annual review for long-term treatment

Referral Criteria

Refer to gastroenterologist if:

  • Refractory/recurrent symptoms despite optimal management
  • Unsuccessful second-line H. pylori eradication
  • Limited antibiotic options due to hypersensitivity/resistance
  • Need for specialist investigations (gastric scintigraphy, hydrogen breath tests)
