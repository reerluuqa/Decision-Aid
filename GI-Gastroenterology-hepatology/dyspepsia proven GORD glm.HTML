GORD Management Algorithm

Endoscopically-Proven Gastro-Oesophageal Reflux Disease

1

Initial Assessment

  • Confirm endoscopically-proven GORD (oesophagitis or endoscopy-negative reflux disease)
  • Age: 18+ years
  • Red flags: Dysphagia, weight loss (especially age 55+) → urgent referral
  • Risk factors: Obesity, smoking, alcohol, stress, certain medications (CCBs, nitrates, benzodiazepines, NSAIDs)
2

Initial Management

  • Lifestyle modifications: Weight loss, avoid trigger foods, smaller meals, smoking cessation, reduce alcohol, elevate head of bed
  • Medication review: Reduce/stop exacerbating medications if possible
  • Pharmacological treatment: Full-dose PPI × 4 weeks (proven GORD) or × 8 weeks (severe oesophagitis)
  • Do NOT test for H. pylori
3

Refractory/Recurrent Symptoms

  • Check adherence to initial management
  • With oesophagitis: Additional 1 month full-dose PPI, OR double-dose PPI × 1 month, OR add H2RA at bedtime
  • Endoscopy-negative: Switch to H2RA × 1 month
  • Severe oesophagitis: High-dose PPI × 8 weeks, OR switch to alternative PPI
4

Long-Term Management

  • Recurrent symptoms: Use lowest effective PPI dose or on-demand PPI
  • Annual review for long-term treatment
  • Consider stepping down if no comorbidities requiring continued PPI
⚠️

Referral Criteria

  • Refractory, persistent, or unexplained symptoms
  • Symptoms controlled but patient declines long-term treatment
  • Acid suppression therapy not tolerated
  • Risk factors for Barrett's oesophagus
