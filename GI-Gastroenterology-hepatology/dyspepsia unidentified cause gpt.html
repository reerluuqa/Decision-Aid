3) H. pylori Eradication — First Line (7 days)

Standard Triple

  • PPI BD + Amoxicillin 1 g BD + Clarithromycin 500 mg BD or Metronidazole 400 mg BD.

Penicillin Allergy

  • PPI BD + Clarithromycin 500 mg BD + Metronidazole 400 mg BD.

Penicillin Allergy & Prior Clarithromycin Exposure

  • Quadruple: PPI BD + Metronidazole 400 mg BD + Tetracycline 500 mg QID + Bismuth (subsalicylate) QID.
Emphasise adherence; tailor regimen to prior antibiotic exposure.

4) If Symptoms Persist / Recur

  • Switch to the alternate initial strategy if not yet tried.
  • Reinforce adherence & lifestyle; review drugs and consider stopping NSAIDs/aspirin where possible (or add gastroprotection).
  • Consider H2RA if inadequate response to PPI.
  • Think differentials (cardiac/hepatobiliary/pancreatic/coeliac/IBS/SIBO).
Do not routinely re-test for H. pylori after first-line therapy unless: poor compliance; test done within 2w of PPI or 4w of abx; NSAID/aspirin indicated (esp. prior PUD); family history gastric cancer; severe/persistent/recurrent symptoms; or patient-request.
