Crohn's Disease CKS

Updated May 2024
NICE CKS Summary

IMMEDIATE RED FLAGS

Suspected Complication
  • Severe colicky abdominal pain + distention
  • Vomiting + no flatus (Obstruction)
  • Passing gas/feces in urine or vagina (Fistula)
  • Significant haemorrhage
Refer Urgently (Non-Elective)
  • Systemic upset (fever, tachycardia, anaemia)
  • Weight loss or failure to thrive (children)
  • Severe perianal disease (abscess/fissure)

At a Glance

Chronic, relapsing-remitting inflammation of the GI tract.

  • Skip lesions: Discrete affected areas.
  • Transmural: Full-thickness wall inflammation.
  • Location: Terminal ileum (45%), Colon (32%), Ileocolon (19%).
Incidence: 83/million/yr Peak onset: 15-30 yrs

Primary Actions

  • Refer to secondary care for diagnosis & management.
  • Do not start biologics in primary care.
  • Encourage smoking cessation (reduces relapse risk).
View Referral Criteria

Risks & Causes

Smoking High Risk Increases risk & relapse
Family History 25-40% in children
NSAIDs Caution May trigger flare
Oral Contraceptives Slight increased risk

History: When to Suspect

Persistent Symptoms > 4-6 Weeks
  • Diarrhoea: Frequent loose stools. Nocturnal is a key sign.
  • Abdominal Pain: Right lower quadrant or general discomfort.
  • Weight Loss: Unexplained or in children (faltering growth).
  • General: Fatigue, malaise, anorexia, fever.

Examination Signs

General
  • Pallor
  • Clubbing
  • Mouth Ulcers
Abdominal/Perianal
  • RLQ Mass/Tenderness
  • Anal Skin Tags
  • Fissure/Fistula
Note: Investigation results may be normal in active disease.

Differential Diagnosis

Irritable Bowel Syndrome Ulcerative Colitis Coeliac Disease GI Cancer Infectious Gastroenteritis Appendicitis Anal Fissure (Idiopathic)

Intestinal

  • Strictures: Narrowing causing obstruction.
  • Fistulae: Abnormal tracks (bowel-bladder, bowel-vagina, skin).
  • Perianal Disease: Fissures, abscesses.
  • Acute Dilation/Perforation.
  • Haemorrhage.

Cancer Risk

Risk of small and large intestine cancer increases with duration.

DurationColorectal Cancer Risk
10 Years~2%
20 Years~8%
30 Years~18%
Similar to UC with same colonic extent.

Systemic

  • Anaemia: Iron, B12, or Folate deficiency / Chronic disease.
  • Malnutrition: Reduced intake, malabsorption.
  • Children: Faltering growth, delayed puberty.
  • Osteoporosis: Due to steroids or disease activity.

Extra-Intestinal Manifestations (EIMs)

EIMs are more common in Crohn's colitis. May present before GI symptoms.

Active Disease Related

  • Arthritis (Pauci-articular): <5 large joints (ankles/knees). Asymmetric.
  • Erythema Nodosum: Tender red nodules on shins.
  • Aphthous Ulcers: Mouth ulcers.
  • Episcleritis: Red, painful eye.

Independent of Activity

  • Axial Arthritis: Sacroiliitis / Spondylitis (Buttock/back pain).
  • Polyarticular Arthritis: >5 joints, symmetrical, damaging.
  • Pyoderma Gangrenosum: Deep ulcers (often on shins).
  • Uveitis: Painful red eye, photophobia.
  • PSC: Primary Sclerosing Cholangitis.

Other

  • Psoriasis
  • Gallstones
  • Kidney Stones
  • Venous Thromboembolism (DVT/PE)
