üî¨ Understanding FIT (Screening)

  • Test: Faecal Immunochemical Test (quantitative).
  • Process: Single stool sample sent via post.
  • Result Time: ~2 weeks.
  • Outcome:
    Normal Routine recall (2 yrs).
    Abnormal Nurse assessment & Colonoscopy.

‚ö†Ô∏è Harms & Limitations

  • False Negatives: Screening is not 100% effective. Cancers can be missed (interval cancer).
  • Colonoscopy Risks: Small risk of perforation or bleeding.
  • Anxiety: False positives lead to unnecessary invasive procedures.
Primary Care Actions

üö® Symptomatic Presentation

If patient has symptoms, ignore screening status.

  • Red Flags: Rectal bleeding, change in bowel habit, abdominal pain, weight loss.
  • Action: Refer via 2-week wait suspected cancer pathway.
  • Note: Symptomatic FIT thresholds are significantly lower than screening thresholds (e.g., <10 ¬µg/g). A "Normal" screening FIT does NOT rule out cancer in a symptomatic patient.

‚úÖ Promoting Uptake & Prevention

  • Encourage Participation: Emphasize early detection improves survival (Stage A ~90% vs Metastatic ~10%).
  • Lifestyle Advice:
    • Maintain healthy weight.
    • Diet high in fiber/veg; low red/processed meat.
    • Limit alcohol & stop smoking.
    • Regular physical activity.
