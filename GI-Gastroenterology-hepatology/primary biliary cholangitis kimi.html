Laboratory investigations and diagnostic criteria

First-Line Investigations

Cholestatic Pattern: Elevated ALP & GGT >> ALT
  • Alkaline phosphatase (ALP) - elevated Required
  • Gamma-glutamyl transferase (GGT) - elevated Required
  • Bilirubin - normal or elevated in advanced disease Prognostic
  • ALT/AST - normal or mildly elevated Typical

Autoantibody Profile Diagnostic Hallmark

  • Antimitochondrial antibody (AMA) - present in 90-95%
  • PBC-specific ANA (anti-sp100, anti-gp210)
  • ELISA titres >1:40 considered significant

Imaging Studies

  • Abdominal ultrasound - exclude biliary obstruction Essential
  • MRCP - if ultrasound inconclusive Alternative
  • Transient elastography - assess fibrosis stage Non-invasive

Diagnostic Criteria AASLD Guidelines

Diagnosis requires 2 of 3 criteria:

1
Cholestatic biochemistry (elevated ALP)
2
AMA or PBC-specific ANA positivity
3
Compatible liver histology (if biopsy performed)

Note: Liver biopsy not routinely required - only if diagnostic uncertainty or suspicion of autoimmune hepatitis overlap

Differential Diagnoses

  • Obstructive bile duct lesion Exclude first
  • Small-duct primary sclerosing cholangitis Different demographics
  • Drug-induced cholestasis History key
  • Infiltrative malignancy If systemic features

Disease Progression Modification

First-Line Therapy All Patients

Ursodeoxycholic Acid (UDCA)
Dose: 12-16 mg/kg/day in 3 divided doses
Continue indefinitely; assess response at 12 months
  • Very benign safety profile
  • Can cause nausea or weight gain
  • Space 4 hours from colestyramine
  • Up to 40% inadequate responders Consider second-line

Long-term Complications

Common

  • Osteoporosis

Serious

  • Portal hypertension
  • Hepatocellular carcinoma

Prognosis

Overall: Slowly progressive in majority
Standardized mortality ratio: 2.8 (doubled risk)
Many patients die of other causes before end-stage liver disease
  • Liver-related mortality: doubled Major cause
  • Non-liver mortality: also doubled Cardiovascular/malignancy
  • Younger age (<45) & male sex: poorer prognosis High-risk groups
