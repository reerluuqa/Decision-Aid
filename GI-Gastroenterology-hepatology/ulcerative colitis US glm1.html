Ulcerative Colitis Quick Reference

EMERGENCY: Toxic megacolon is a life-threatening complication requiring immediate intervention. Watch for colonic dilation ≥6 cm, fever, tachycardia, and abdominal distension.

Overview

Ulcerative colitis (UC) is an inflammatory bowel disease characterized by diffuse inflammation of the colonic mucosa with a relapsing-remitting course.

Key symptoms: Bloody diarrhea, lower abdominal pain, fecal urgency, and extraintestinal manifestations.

Classification

Severity Clinical Features
Mild (S1) ≤4 stools/day (with or without blood), no systemic illness, normal ESR
Moderate (S2) >4 stools/day with minimal signs of systemic toxicity
Severe (S3) ≥6 bloody stools/day, pulse ≥90 bpm, temperature ≥37.5°C, Hb <105g/L, ESR ≥30 mm/h
Extent Description
E1 (Proctitis) Limited to rectum (distal to rectosigmoid junction)
E2 (Left-sided) Limited to colorectum distal to splenic flexure
E3 (Extensive) Extends proximal to splenic flexure

Key Diagnostic Factors

  • Rectal bleeding (present in >90% of patients)
  • Bloody diarrhea
  • Lower abdominal pain
  • Fecal urgency and tenesmus
  • Extraintestinal manifestations (erythema nodosum, arthropathy, uveitis)

Diagnostic Approach

Diagnosis requires endoscopy with biopsy and negative stool culture

Treatment Algorithm

First-line: 5-ASA (mesalamine) - oral or rectal depending on extent
  • Proctitis: Rectal 5-ASA suppositories
  • Left-sided: Rectal 5-ASA enemas
  • Extensive: Oral 5-ASA
First-line: Oral corticosteroids to induce remission
  • Oral prednisone 40-60 mg/day
  • Taper over 6-8 weeks
  • Consider immunomodulators (azathioprine) for steroid-sparing
  • Biologics for refractory cases
Hospital admission required for IV steroids
  • IV methylprednisolone 40-60 mg/day
  • If no response in 3-5 days: Rescue therapy
  • Rescue options: IV infliximab or cyclosporine
  • Surgery if no response to rescue therapy

Monitoring & Follow-up

  • Clinical assessment every 3 months in remission
  • Colonoscopy with biopsies 8-12 weeks after treatment initiation
  • Surveillance colonoscopy for dysplasia after 8-10 years of disease
  • Monitor bone density with long-term steroid use
  • Vaccinations: Influenza annually, pneumococcal every 5 years
