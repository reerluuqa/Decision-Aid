Diagnostic Approach

Recommended Investigations
  • Abdominal Ultrasound (First-line)

    Confirms presence of stones. Note: Absence of stones does not exclude them if clinical suspicion is high.

  • Liver Function Tests (LFTs)

    Abnormal LFTs suggest Common Bile Duct stones.

  • MRCP / Endoscopic Ultrasound

    If Ultrasound normal but clinical suspicion remains high (e.g., dilated bile duct or abnormal LFTs).

Key Differential Diagnoses
Condition Key Features
Acute Hepatitis Liver inflammation, viral markers.
Gastritis / Dyspepsia Burning epigastric pain, related to meals.
Peptic Ulcer Proven GORD, H. pylori positive.
IBS Variable bowel habits, bloating.
Non-biliary Pancreatitis Alcohol, hypertriglyceridemia.

Patient Advice

While waiting for surgery:

Avoid food and drinks that trigger symptoms (e.g., fatty foods).

Post-surgery:

Most people can eat a normal diet. No permanent restrictions needed.

Warning signs:

Seek help if symptoms return (new pain, jaundice, fever) after recovery.
