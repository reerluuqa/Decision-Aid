Chronic Pancreatitis Quick Reference

RED FLAGS: Jaundice, significant weight loss, or new-onset diabetes in older patients require immediate evaluation for pancreatic cancer.

Overview

Chronic pancreatitis is characterized by recurrent or persistent abdominal pain and progressive injury to the pancreas, resulting in scarring and loss of function.

Most commonly associated with chronic alcohol ingestion (>75%)
Diagnosis based on clinical findings and imaging
No definitive therapy; treatment aimed at symptoms and complications

Key Symptoms

Abdominal pain: Epigastric, dull, radiates to back, worse postprandially
Steatorrhoea: Occurs in 8-22% at diagnosis
Diabetes mellitus: Occurs in >80% after 25 years
Jaundice: Occurs in ~10% due to bile duct compression
Weight loss: Due to fear of food, malabsorption, poor intake

Management Approach

Treat underlying causes
Pain management
Pancreatic enzyme replacement
Nutritional support
Management of diabetes
Treatment of complications

Diagnostic Approach

1
History and physical examination
2
Cross-sectional imaging (CT or MRI)
3
EUS if diagnosis in question after imaging
4
s-MRCP if still unclear
5
Functional tests for exocrine insufficiency
