üìã Rome IV Criteria
Definition

Diagnosis of exclusion (Birth to <5 months).

  • Age: Starts & stops before 5 months.
  • Symptoms: Recurrent/prolonged crying, fussing, or irritability.
  • Cause: No obvious cause & cannot be prevented/resolved by caregiver.
  • Status: No failure to thrive or fever/illness.
Common Signs: Crying late afternoon/evening, knees drawn up, clenched fists, back arching.
üîç Assessment
History & Exam
  • History: Onset, duration, frequency, pitch of cry.
  • Feeding: Pattern, method (breast/bottle), reconstitution, winding, other fluids.
  • Parental: Coping strategies, anxiety/depression, support network.
  • Exam: Weight/gain (Faltering growth?), temperature, hydration, skin (rash/itch?), oral anatomy (tongue-tie?), trauma check.
‚úÖ Management
First Line
  • Reassure: Common & self-limiting. Resolves by 3-4 months (latest 5-6).
  • Support: Signpost to NHS Leaflet, Cry-sis, ICON campaign, Health Visitor.
  • Soothe: Holding, reduce stimuli, gentle motion (pram/rocking), white noise, warm bath.
  • Technique: Ensure optimal winding technique.
  • Parent Care: Ask for help, rest when baby sleeps, put baby down safely if need "time out".
  • Feeding: Encourage continued breastfeeding if applicable.
Review: Arrange review based on clinical judgment.
