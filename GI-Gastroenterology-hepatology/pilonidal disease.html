Etiology

Definition

Chronic, suppurative condition involving the natal cleft. Caused by loose hairs trapped in the natal cleft penetrating the skin, leading to a granulomatous foreign body reaction.

Pathophysiology

1. Empty hair follicle fills with keratin/debris.
2. Loose hair gets trapped.
3. Inflammation leads to granuloma.
4. Sinus tract forms.
5. Superinfection causes acute disease.

Strong Risk Factors

Male Sex Age 15-45 Prolonged Sitting (>6hrs) Hirsutism (9x risk) Poor Hygiene (<2 baths/wk)

Diagnosis

Clinical assessment is key. Imaging generally not required.

Acute Presentation (Abscess)

Urgent

Symptoms:

  • Sudden onset mild-to-severe pain in natal cleft.
  • Swelling and/or purulent/bloody discharge.
  • Fever or malaise indicates undrained abscess.

Physical Exam:

  • Cellulitis in natal cleft.
  • Tender, fluctuant mass (often lateral to midline).
  • Superior aspect of natal cleft.

Chronic Presentation

Routine

History: Prior abscess (drained or spontaneous). Persistent intermittent/constant drainage.

Exam: Sinus openings visible. Macerated deep skin. Hair protruding from sinus.

Asymptomatic

Safe

Incidental finding of midline pit without tenderness, erythema, induration, fluctuance, or drainage.

Acute Abscess Action

Priority

Incision & Drainage

Ideal Procedure: Unroofing and Curettage.

Why Curettage? Healing in 10 weeks: 96% (vs 79% drainage alone)
Recurrence: 11% (vs 42% drainage alone)

Treatment Protocols

Asymptomatic

Do not operate. Morbidity outweighs risk.

  • Hair removal (Laser preferred)
  • Improved perineal hygiene
  • Weight loss
  • Avoid prolonged sitting

Acute (Abscess)

Primary: Incision & Drainage + Curettage.

Adjunct: Hygiene + Hair Removal.

When to use Antibiotics?

Not routinely recommended. Use only if:

  • Significant cellulitis post-drainage
  • Immunosuppression
  • High risk of endocarditis
  • Systemic illness

1st Line:

Amoxicillin/Clavulanate (500mg TDS)

Alt (If Penicillin Allergy):

Ciprofloxacin (750mg BD) + Metronidazole (500mg TDS)

*Fluoroquinolone black box warning applies.

Chronic / Recurrent

Surgery is mainstay. Goal: Remove sinus tracts & pits.

Off-Midline Closure (Flap): Lowest recurrence (0-6%). E.g., Karydakis, Bascom Cleft-lift.
Excision + Secondary Intention: Lower recurrence than midline closure, but long healing time.
Sinusectomy: Less invasive, faster recovery.

Differentials

Distinguishing Pilonidal Disease from other conditions.

Condition Key Differentiating Signs Location
Perianal Fistula Opening in perianal region, tracks towards anus. Normal natal cleft skin. Perianal
Perianal Abscess Severe pain near anal/rectal area. Normal natal cleft skin. Perianal
Hidradenitis Suppurativa Chronic follicular occlusive disease. Natal cleft usually SPARED. May involve axilla/inframammary. Perineal / Inguinal / Axilla
Skin Abscess / Furuncle Pus collection in dermis. Commonly away from midline. No sinus tract. Various (usually away from midline)
Folliculitis Infection within follicles. Multiple small (<5mm) raised pruritic lesions. Pustule in center. Various

Confirming Pilonidal

Diagnosis is clinical. No diagnostic tests required.

Look for: Midline pits, sinus tracts, hair protruding from opening, location at superior aspect of natal cleft.
