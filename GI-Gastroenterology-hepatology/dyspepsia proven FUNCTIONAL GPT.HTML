Functional Dyspepsia – One‑Page Algorithm

Adults ≥18y Primary Care NICE CKS (May 2024)

Rapid Care Pathway

Use during consultations to streamline assessment, first‑line management, and escalation.

Step 1Initial assessment
  • Exclude alarm features → urgent referral if present (e.g., dysphagia, GI bleeding, unexplained weight loss, persistent vomiting, abdominal mass; age ≥55 with new onset + weight loss).
  • Review medications that exacerbate symptoms (NSAIDs, steroids, bisphosphonates, CCBs, nitrates, theophyllines, TCAs, etc.).
  • Lifestyle: weight loss, avoid triggers (coffee, chocolate, tomatoes, fatty/spicy foods), smaller meals; evening meal ≥3–4h before bed; stop smoking; reduce alcohol; manage stress.
Step 2Test & treat for H. pylori (if status unknown)
  • Cease PPI ≥2 weeks and antibiotics ≥4 weeks before testing.
  • Preferred test: 13C‑urea breath test; alternative: stool antigen; locally validated serology if others unavailable.
If H. pylori positive → 7‑day eradication
  • PPI bid + Amoxicillin 1 g bid + Clarithromycin 500 mg bid or Metronidazole 400 mg bid
  • Penicillin allergy: PPI bid + Clarithromycin 500 mg bid + Metronidazole 400 mg bid
  • Pen allergy + prior clarithromycin: Quadruple → PPI bid + Metronidazole 400 mg bid + Tetracycline 500 mg qid + Bismuth 525 mg qid
If H. pylori negative
  • Trial: low‑dose PPI or standard‑dose H2RA for 1 month.
Step 3Refractory or recurrent symptoms
  • Re‑check for alarm features and alternative diagnoses (cardiac/hepatobiliary, etc.).
  • Confirm adherence; reinforce lifestyle measures.
  • Re‑test H. pylori only if: poor compliance/timing; high local resistance; NSAID/aspirin indicated (esp. PUD history); family history of gastric cancer; severe or persistent symptoms; or patient request. Perform ≥4 weeks after therapy (ideally 8 weeks), off PPI ≥2 weeks and antibiotics ≥4 weeks.
If re‑test positive → 7‑day second‑line
  • PPI bid + Amoxicillin 1 g bid + (Clarithromycin or Metronidazole not used first‑line)
  • If prior clarithro + metro: PPI bid + Amoxicillin 1 g bid + Levofloxacin 250 mg bid* or Tetracycline 500 mg qid
  • Pen allergy, no prior levo: PPI bid + Metronidazole 400 mg bid + Levofloxacin 250 mg bid*
  • Pen allergy + prior levo: PPI bid + Bismuth 525 mg qid + Metronidazole 400 mg bid + Tetracycline 500 mg qid
If re‑test not indicated / negative
  • Switch PPI ↔ H2RA; consider long‑term control at lowest effective dose.
  • Annual review; consider PRN PPI and short‑term antacid/alginate (avoid continuous long‑term use).

* Fluoroquinolones: prescribe only if commonly recommended alternatives are inappropriate (regulatory safety advice).

Step 4Refer / specialist input
  • Refractory or recurrent symptoms despite optimal primary‑care management.
  • Failed second‑line H. pylori eradication.
  • Limited antibiotic options (hypersensitivity, resistance, multiple prior exposures).
  • Possible investigations: barium swallow/meal; gastric scintigraphy (suspected delayed emptying); hydrogen breath tests (SIBO, lactose/fructose intolerance).
  • Specialist therapies may include TCAs (for pain modulation/anxiety/depression) and adjunct probiotics (variable evidence).

Acid suppression trial
  • PPI (low dose) or H2RA (standard dose) × 1 month.
  • Stop before endoscopy (≥2 weeks) to avoid masking pathology.
Long‑term management
  • Consider lowest effective dose; PRN PPI where suitable.
  • Annual medication + symptom review.
Adjuncts
  • Psychological therapies; relaxation strategies.
  • Low‑dose TCAs for symptom modulation in selected patients.
  • Probiotics as adjunct (evidence inconsistent).

Safety & Interactions (selected)

  • PPIs: monitor for hypomagnesaemia in long‑term use; consider fracture risk in elderly/high‑dose/long‑term; avoid with clopidogrel (ome/esome preferred to avoid) by selecting alternative PPI where appropriate; stop ≥2 weeks pre‑endoscopy.
  • H2RAs: dose‑adjust in CKD; stop ≥2 weeks pre‑endoscopy.
  • Clarithromycin: numerous interactions (e.g., statins, warfarin, QT‑prolonging agents); avoid with ivabradine; use caution with edoxaban.
  • Amoxicillin: check true penicillin allergy; adjust in advanced CKD.

MHRA reminder: Systemic fluoroquinolones can cause serious, potentially long‑lasting adverse effects; reserve for when commonly recommended antibiotics are inappropriate.

Designed as a rapid aide‑mémoire for primary care. This summary is based on NICE CKS functional dyspepsia (May 2024). Always apply local antimicrobial guidance.
