üî¨ What is Hepatitis C?
  • Blood-borne RNA virus (Flaviviridae family)
  • 6 genotypes ‚Äî UK mainly genotypes 1 & 3
  • Acute: First 6 months post-infection (mostly asymptomatic)
  • Chronic: Persists >6 months (in 55-85% of cases)
  • Often undiagnosed ‚Äî symptoms may take decades
‚úì Good news: Hepatitis C is CURABLE with direct-acting antivirals (DAAs)

ü©∫ Clinical Features

Acute HCV (often asymptomatic >60%)

  • Fatigue, night sweats, fever
  • Aches, pains, poor concentration
  • Loss of appetite, nausea, vomiting
  • RUQ discomfort, abdominal pain
  • Jaundice (dark urine, pale stools)

Symptoms 2 weeks to 6 months after infection ‚Äî often not connected to exposure

Chronic HCV

Usually asymptomatic until serious liver damage develops (decades later)

üíä Specialist Treatment Overview

Direct-Acting Antivirals (DAAs)

  • First-line treatment for chronic HCV
  • >90% cure rate
  • Once-daily oral tablet
  • 8 or 12 weeks duration
  • Well tolerated, minimal side effects
  • Most effective before onset of cirrhosis
Cure = HCV RNA negative 12 weeks after treatment completion
