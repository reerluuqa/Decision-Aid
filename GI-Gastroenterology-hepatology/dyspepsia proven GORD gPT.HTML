1 · Initial Assessment

  • Screen for alarm symptoms (see red box above).
  • Review medicines that aggravate reflux: NSAIDsCCBsNitratesTCAsAnticholinergicsBenzodiazepinesBisphosphonates etc.
  • Ask about OTC antacids/alginates used.

2 · Lifestyle & Self‑Care

  • Weight loss if overweight/obese.
  • Avoid triggers: coffee, chocolate, tomatoes, fatty/spicy foods; smaller meals; avoid food 3–4h pre‑bed.
  • Raise head of bed by 10–20 cm (blocks), not extra pillows.
  • Stop smoking; reduce alcohol; address stress/anxiety.
Education
Provide written info on GORD and self‑care; agree follow‑up plan.

3 · Pharmacological Management

Proven GORD (endoscopy confirmed)

  • PPI full dose × 4 weeks (for healing).
  • If severe oesophagitis: full‑dose 8 weeks then long‑term maintenance.
  • Do not test for H. pylori.

4 · If Symptoms Recur / Persist

  • Reassess for new red flags; check adherence & lifestyle.
  • Options (1 month each unless stated):
  • Repeat full‑dose PPI.
  • Double‑dose PPI.
  • Add H2RA at bedtime (short intermittent course, e.g., 2 weeks) for nocturnal symptoms.
  • For endoscopy‑negative reflux: consider H2RA switch for 1 month.

5 · Long‑Term Maintenance

  • Annual review if on long‑term therapy.
  • Encourage step‑down/stop if suitable; or use lowest effective PPI dose / PRN.
  • Self‑treatment with antacid/alginate PRN (not continuous long‑term).
  • Do not step down if: severe/complicated oesophagitis, Barrett’s, NSAID gastroprotection, or prior bleeding ulcer with persistent H. pylori.

6 · When to Refer

  • Symptoms refractory, persistent or unexplained despite optimized therapy.
  • Responds to PPI but not tolerated / patient declines long‑term therapy.
  • Risk factors for Barrett’s: male, long duration/frequency of reflux, hiatus hernia, prior stricture/ulcers → consider endoscopy.
  • Considering anti‑reflux surgery.

7 · Specialist (for info)

Investigations

  • 24‑h oesophageal pH monitoring (symptom correlation).
  • Oesophageal manometry (exclude motility disorders; pre‑surgery).
  • Barium swallow/meal (structural & motility assessment).

Management

  • Laparoscopic fundoplication (gold standard; counsel risks: gas‑bloat, dysphagia, stricture, recurrence; small mortality risk).
  • Other options in specialist centres: endoscopic radiofrequency, magnetic bead band, bulking agents.

Safety Pointers (Compact)

PPIs — Cautions
  • Stop 2 weeks before endoscopy (may mask malignancy).
  • Consider bone health (osteoporosis risk), magnesium monitoring if at risk.
  • Long‑term rare risks: hypomagnesaemia, fractures, C. difficile, rebound acid.
Key Interactions
  • Clopidogrel: avoid with omeprazole/esomeprazole.
  • Warfarin/Phenytoin/Digoxin: monitor if starting/stopping certain PPIs.
  • Protease inhibitors, azole antifungals: seek advice / adjust.
H2RAs — Notes
  • Hold 2 weeks pre‑endoscopy.
  • Renal dose adjustments (famotidine, nizatidine).
  • Adverse effects uncommon; CNS effects rare.
