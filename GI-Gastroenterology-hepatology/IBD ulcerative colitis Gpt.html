Ulcerative Colitis – Primary Care Quick Algorithm

1. Suspected UC

  • Admit if red flags: Severe bloody diarrhoea, fever, tachycardia, hypotension.
  • If not systemically unwell:
    • Urgent referral to gastroenterology.
    • Avoid anti-diarrhoeals (risk toxic megacolon).
    • Refer to specialists if extra-intestinal features present.

2. Confirmed UC – Ongoing Review

  • Regular reviews, depending on clinical judgement.
  • Assess impact on daily life & mental health.
  • Encourage exercise, healthy weight, smoking cessation.
  • Ensure colorectal cancer surveillance (≥10 yrs disease, except proctitis alone).
  • Children: monitor growth & puberty.
  • Monitor osteoporosis risk, nutritional deficiencies.
  • Vaccinations: avoid live vaccines on immunosuppressants/biologics.

3. Specialist-Initiated Treatments

  • Aminosalicylates: mild/moderate, first-line (topical ± oral).
  • Corticosteroids: short course for induction, not maintenance.
  • Thiopurines: for steroid-dependent/refractory disease.
  • Biologics / JAK inhibitors / Ozanimod: severe, refractory cases.
  • Ciclosporin: acute severe, steroid-resistant.
  • Children: consider enteral nutrition for remission induction.

4. Flare-Up Management

  • Admit if:
    • >6–8 bloody stools/day
    • Severe pain, obstruction, fever, tachycardia, hypotension
    • BMI <18.5 or rapid weight loss
    • Raised inflammatory markers or anaemia
  • If not admitted:
    • Exclude infection (esp. C. difficile).
    • Check adherence, urgent gastro review.
    • Short course oral corticosteroids (per shared care).
    • Dietitian referral if malnourished.
    • Recurrent flares → escalate or consider surgery.

5. Pregnancy, Fertility & Breastfeeding

  • Pre-conception: optimise remission, review meds.
  • Unplanned pregnancy on methotrexate, mercaptopurine, infliximab, adalimumab → urgent specialist advice.
  • Most meds safe for breastfeeding – confirm with specialist if unsure.
  • Note: increased risk of post-partum flare.

6. Symptom Management in Primary Care

  • Diarrhoea: exclude infection, bile salt malabsorption; avoid loperamide unless specialist advised.
  • Pain: paracetamol first-line; avoid NSAIDs & opiates.
  • Fatigue/Anaemia: check FBC, ferritin, B12, folate, vitamin D.

Key Reminders

  • Always escalate suspected acute severe UC.
  • No NSAIDs, no loperamide if diagnosis uncertain.
  • Maintain close shared-care with gastroenterology.
