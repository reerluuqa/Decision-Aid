1

Initial Assessment

For adults (≥18y) with endoscopy‑proven gastric or duodenal ulcer.

Screen for Alarm Symptoms

Consider urgent action if weight loss, bleeding/anaemia, progressive dysphagia, persistent vomiting, or other red flags.

Medication Review

  • Stop NSAIDs if possible to allow healing.
  • Consider other ulcerogenic drugs: aspirin, steroids, bisphosphonates, SSRIs, potassium, recreational cocaine.
  • Note previous antibiotic courses (impacts eradication choice).

Lifestyle & Support

  • Weight loss if overweight/obese; smaller meals; avoid late meals.
  • Avoid triggers (coffee, chocolate, tomatoes, fatty/spicy foods); reduce alcohol; stop smoking.
  • Address stress/anxiety; consider psychological support.

OTC Symptom Relief

Short‑term antacid/alginate is reasonable; avoid long‑term continuous use.
2

H. pylori Testing

If status is unknown or uncertain.

Preferred Tests

  • 13C‑Urea breath test (first‑line)
  • Stool antigen (second‑line)
  • Locally validated serology only if the above are unavailable.

Pre‑test Washout

Hold PPI for 2 weeks and antibiotics for 4 weeks before testing.
3

Treatment Overview

If H. pylori positive

  • Non‑NSAID ulcer: Start first‑line eradication immediately.
  • NSAID‑associated: Full‑dose PPI for 2 months, then start eradication.

If H. pylori negative

  • Full‑dose PPI for 4–8 weeks (clinical judgement).
4

First‑line H. pylori Eradication (7 days)

Use a PPI at standard dose BID + antibiotic combination below. Emphasise adherence.

Standard Triple

  • PPI (BID) + Amoxicillin 1 g BID +
  • Clarithromycin 500 mg BID or Metronidazole 400 mg BID

Penicillin Allergy

  • PPI (BID) + Clarithromycin 500 mg BID + Metronidazole 400 mg BID

Penicillin Allergy + prior Clarithromycin

  • Quadruple: PPI (BID) + Metronidazole 400 mg BID + Tetracycline 500 mg QID + Bismuth 525 mg QID
5

Second‑line H. pylori Eradication (7 days)

Use different antibiotics than first course; continue PPI (BID).

After failure of first‑line

  • PPI + Amoxicillin 1 g BID + Clarithromycin 500 mg BID or Metronidazole 400 mg BID (whichever not used before).

Prior Clarithro and Metro exposure

  • PPI + Amoxicillin 1 g BID + Levofloxacin 250 mg BID or Tetracycline 500 mg QID

Penicillin Allergy (no prior levofloxacin)

  • PPI + Metronidazole 400 mg BID + Levofloxacin 250 mg BID

Penicillin Allergy + prior levofloxacin

  • Quadruple: PPI + Bismuth 525 mg QID + Metronidazole 400 mg BID + Tetracycline 500 mg QID
6

Follow‑Up

Confirm Healing & Eradication

  • All gastric ulcers: repeat endoscopy at 6–8 weeks.
  • Re‑test H. pylori (urea breath first‑line; stool antigen 2nd) with pre‑test washout.

If Ulcer Unhealed

Exclude non‑adherence, missed H. pylori, ongoing NSAIDs/other ulcerogenic meds, malignancy, or Zollinger‑Ellison.

Recurrent Symptoms after Healing

  • PPI at lowest effective dose, consider as‑needed use.
  • Consider switch to H2RA; consider long‑term suppression if NSAID/antiplatelet required.

Long‑term Review

  • Annual review for those on long‑term therapy; step down or stop where possible.
  • OTC antacid/alginate can be used intermittently; avoid continuous use.
7

Referral Criteria

  • Refractory or recurrent symptoms despite optimal primary‑care management.
  • Failure of second‑line eradication.
  • Limited antibiotic choices (hypersensitivity, local resistance, prior macrolide/nitroimidazole/quinolone use).
  • Unhealed gastric ulcer on repeat endoscopy.
  • Suspected Zollinger‑Ellison or other non‑peptic cause.
