Rare but Serious Rule-Out

Usually benign. However, if patient presents with granulomatous cheilitis and facial nerve paralysis, consider Melkersson-Rosenthal syndrome. Associated with Down's syndrome.

Appearance: Numerous grooves or fissures on the dorsal surface. May be isolated or cover entire dorsum (lobulated appearance).
History: Usually asymptomatic. Often present since childhood, becoming more prominent with age.
Common Complaints: Halitosis (bad breath) due to trapped food debris. Mild burning sensation if food is trapped.

Rule Out in Immunocompromised

HIV Positive / Immunosuppressed? Differentiate from Oral Hairy Leukoplakia (Epstein-Barr virus).

Key Differentiator: Leukoplakia typically involves the lateral borders of the tongue. Hairy tongue is midline dorsal.

[Visual: Elongated hair-like projections, Yellow/Black color]
Appearance: Diffuse hair-like projections on dorsal midline. Color ranges from yellow to brown/black.
Pathophysiology: Elongation of keratinized filiform papillae due to disrupted shedding (accumulation of keratin).
Risk Factors: Smoking, poor oral hygiene, coffee/tea, oxidising mouthwashes, hyposalivation, drug abuse.
‚úÖ Management & Action Hygiene

Goal: Mechanical debridement & Risk Factor Modification.

  • Use tongue scraper or toothbrush regularly
  • Stop smoking, reduce coffee/tea
  • Improve general oral hygiene
  • Manage hyposalivation or oral discomfort

Clinical Association

Significant association with Psoriasis. Consider localised papillary atrophy secondary to anaemia or B12 deficiency as a differential.

Appearance: Well-delineated areas of erythema with raised white circinate (scalloped) edges. Predominantly lateral margins, tip, and dorsum.
Key Symptom: Central red atrophic zone (loss of filiform papillae). Lesions appear to migrate over time.
Patient Report: Increased sensitivity to hot/spicy foods. Burning sensation.

Reassurance is primary. No treatment required unless symptomatic.

  • Reassure: Benign condition
  • Avoid trigger foods (hot/spicy/acidic)
  • Symptomatic relief: Topical hyaluronic acid or Benzydamine
