Definition

Chronic immune-mediated systemic disorder triggered by dietary gluten (wheat, barley, rye) in genetically predisposed individuals.

  • Small bowel enteropathy with villous atrophy
  • Variable GI and/or systemic symptoms
  • Presence of coeliac-specific autoantibodies
  • Improves with gluten removal

Higher Risk Groups - Offer Testing

  • Type 1 Diabetes (at diagnosis)
  • Autoimmune thyroid disease (at diagnosis)
  • First-degree relative with coeliac disease
  • Down's, Turner, Williams syndrome
  • Autoimmune liver disease
  • Selective IgA deficiency
  • Dermatitis herpetiformis

Key Principle

Patient must be eating gluten-containing foods (wheat/barley/rye in >1 meal/day) for minimum 6 weeks before testing

üî¥

SUSPECT Coeliac Disease If:

  • Persistent unexplained GI symptoms: diarrhoea, steatorrhoea, weight loss, abdominal pain, bloating, constipation
  • Irritable bowel syndrome (adults)
  • Faltering growth / delayed puberty (children)
  • Prolonged fatigue / lethargy
  • Persistent/recurrent mouth ulcers
  • Unexplained iron/B12/folate deficiency or anaemia not responding to treatment
  • Type 1 DM or autoimmune thyroid (at diagnosis)
  • 1st-degree relative with coeliac disease
  • Dermatitis herpetiformis
üü°

CONSIDER Coeliac Disease If:

  • Unexplained depression or anxiety
  • Osteomalacia / osteopenia / osteoporosis
  • Fragility fractures
  • Unexplained peripheral neuropathy or ataxia
  • Recurrent miscarriage / subfertility / amenorrhoea
  • Unexplained raised transaminases (ALT/AST)
  • Dental enamel defects
  • Hyposplenism / functional asplenia

Recognising Dermatitis Herpetiformis

Characteristics:
  • Intensely itchy vesicular rash
  • Symmetrical distribution
  • Small vesicles, papules, erythema
  • Erosions, excoriations, crusts common
Common Sites:
  • Elbows
  • Knees
  • Shoulders
  • Buttocks / sacral region

Core Treatment Principle

Lifelong gluten-free diet is the only effective treatment. Untreated disease leads to continuing ill-health and serious complications.

üè•

Initial Management (Post-Diagnosis)

  • Ensure referral to dietitian with coeliac expertise
  • Educate on gluten-free diet importance
  • Consider prescribable GF products (ACBS endorsed)
  • Provide Coeliac UK information (www.coeliac.org.uk)
  • Assess for nutritional deficiencies
  • Consider DEXA scan for osteoporosis risk (adults)
  • Offer vaccinations if hyposplenism (flu, pneumococcal, meningococcal)

Annual Review Checklist

  • Symptom assessment - persistent/recurrent despite GFD?
  • Dietary adherence - inadvertent gluten exposure?
  • Weight, height, BMI (growth in children)
  • Nutritional status - need for supplements?
  • Complications screening
  • Mental health - depression, anxiety, eating disorder?
  • Osteoporosis risk / DEXA need (adults)
  • Vaccination status (if hyposplenism)

AVOID - Gluten-Containing Foods

  • Wheat products: bread, flour, pasta, cakes, pastries, biscuits, breakfast cereals
  • Wheat varieties: spelt, kamut, semolina, triticale
  • Barley and rye products
  • Hidden gluten: sausages, ready meals, soups, sauces, battered foods
  • Cross-contamination: chips fried in same oil as battered items
  • Malt products: most beers
‚úÖ

SAFE - Can Be Eaten

  • Alternative starches: corn, rice, potatoes
  • 'Gluten-free' labelled products
  • 'Very low gluten' labelled products
  • Crossed Grain Symbol products
  • Codex wheat starch products (labelled as containing wheat/wheat starch)
  • Pure uncontaminated oats - most tolerate, but monitor (~5% react)

URGENT REFERRAL (2WW if Malignancy Suspected)

  • Suspected malignancy: lymphoma, small bowel adenocarcinoma, pancreatic cancer
  • Refractory coeliac disease (symptoms persist despite confirmed GFD compliance)
  • Faltering growth in children (paediatric referral)
üè•

Gastroenterology Referral

Urgency based on clinical judgement

  • Positive serology - for biopsy to confirm diagnosis
  • Strong suspicion + negative serology
  • Non-responsive/refractory disease:
    • Persistently high titres after 12 months
    • Persistent symptoms (diarrhoea, pain, weight loss, fatigue, anaemia)
  • Suspected co-existing condition (bacterial overgrowth, microscopic/inflammatory colitis)
