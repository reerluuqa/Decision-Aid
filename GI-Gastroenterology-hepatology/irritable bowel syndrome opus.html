üìã What is IBS?

Chronic, relapsing disorder of gut-brain interaction

  • Abdominal pain associated with altered stool form/frequency
  • May be related to defaecation
  • Often associated with bloating
  • Symptoms fluctuate over years
Prevalence: 5-20% of general population
Peak age: 20-39 years | Female:Male ~1.5-1.8:1

‚ö° Risk Factors / Triggers
  • Post-infectious (~10% after enteric infection)
  • Genetic / family history (OR 2.75)
  • GI inflammation
  • Diet: caffeine, alcohol, spicy/fatty foods
  • Antibiotics
  • Stress, anxiety, depression
‚úÖ Reassurance Points
  • Not associated with increased cancer risk
  • No increased mortality
  • 30-50% symptoms unchanged over time
  • 12-38% improve
  • Post-infectious: 50% resolve in 6-8 years

üîç Diagnostic Criteria
üìã NICE CKS Criteria (Primary Care)
Abdominal pain/discomfort for ‚â•6 months that is:
  • Relieved by defecation, OR
  • Associated with altered bowel frequency, OR
  • Associated with altered stool form
+ At least 2 of the following:
  • Altered stool passage (straining, urgency, incomplete evacuation)
  • Bloating, distension, tension or hardness
  • Made worse by eating
  • Passage of mucus
