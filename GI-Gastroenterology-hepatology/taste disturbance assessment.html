Initial Assessment

Patient History Essential
Comprehensive evaluation of taste dysfunction onset, duration, and characteristics
Determine if disturbance is quantitative or qualitative
Assess ability to distinguish basic tastes (sweet, sour, salty, bitter, umami)
Identify triggers and relieving factors
Medical History Review Critical
Evaluate medications, recent procedures, and underlying conditions
Review current medications (ACE inhibitors, antibiotics, antifungals)
Document recent dental procedures or surgeries
Assess cancer treatment history (chemotherapy, radiotherapy)
Physical Examination Standard
Complete oral cavity and neurological assessment
Examine oral mucosa for lesions or inflammation
Assess salivary flow and gland function
Evaluate cranial nerve function

Diagnostic Evaluation
‚ñº
Taste Testing Objective
Chemical gustometry and electrogustometry for quantitative assessment
Apply taste strips with increasing concentrations
Test anterior 2/3 and posterior tongue separately
Document threshold levels for each taste quality
Olfactory Testing Important
Rule out concomitant smell disorders affecting flavor perception
Use validated olfactory testing methods
Assess odor identification and threshold
Differentiate taste from flavor disorders
Laboratory Investigations Targeted
Based on clinical suspicion from history and examination
FBC, ferritin, B12, folate for nutritional deficiencies
Serum zinc levels
Renal and liver function tests

Treatment Approach
‚ñº
Address Underlying Cause Priority
Target specific etiology identified through diagnostic workup
Discontinue offending medications when possible
Treat underlying infections or inflammatory conditions
Manage systemic diseases (diabetes, thyroid disorders)
Symptomatic Management Supportive
Provide relief while addressing underlying pathology
Zinc supplementation trial (if deficiency suspected)
Sialogogues for dry mouth symptoms
Dietary counseling and flavor enhancement
Specialized Interventions Advanced
For persistent or complex cases requiring specialist care
Referral to taste and smell disorder centers
Consider neurological evaluation for central causes
ENT consultation for structural abnormalities
