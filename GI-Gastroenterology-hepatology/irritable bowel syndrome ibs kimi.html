IBS Quick Reference

Irritable Bowel Syndrome - Clinical Decision Support

RED FLAGS - Immediate Action Required
⚠️ Refer urgently if any present
  • Unintentional weight loss
  • Rectal bleeding
  • Iron deficiency anemia
  • Family history of bowel/ovarian cancer
  • Change in bowel habit >60 years
  • Palpable abdominal/rectal mass
  • Nocturnal symptoms
POSITIVE IBS DIAGNOSIS
Rome IV Criteria:
  • Abdominal pain ≥1 day/week for 3 months
  • Associated with ≥2 of:
    • Related to defecation
    • Change in stool frequency
    • Change in stool form
Onset ≥6 months before diagnosis
SUBTYPES
Subtype Stool Pattern Tag
IBS-C ≥25% hard/lumpy stools Constipation
IBS-D ≥25% loose/watery stools Diarrhea
IBS-M Both patterns ≥25% Mixed
INITIAL INVESTIGATIONS
Routine:
  • FBC
  • CRP/ESR
  • Coeliac serology
If diarrhea:
  • Faecal calprotectin
  • FIT if indicated
FIRST-LINE MANAGEMENT
Lifestyle:
  • Regular meals
  • Adequate fluids
  • Exercise 30min × 5/week
  • Reduce stress
Dietary:
  • Adjust fibre intake
  • Limit caffeine/alcohol
  • Consider probiotics (12-week trial)
  • Food diary
PHARMACOLOGICAL OPTIONS
Symptom Drug Notes
Constipation Bulk laxative Avoid lactulose
Severe IBS-C Linaclotide ≥12 months, review 12wk
Diarrhea Loperamide Max 16mg/day
Pain/Spasm Antispasmodic Mebeverine/Peppermint oil
Refractory TCA (low dose) Amitriptyline 5-30mg
REFERRAL CRITERIA
  • Diagnostic uncertainty
  • Severe/refractory symptoms
  • Failed therapeutic trial (12 months)
  • Patient request
  • Psychological comorbidity
