1) Suspect IBS (≥ 6 months, no red flags)

Typical features
  • Abdominal pain/discomfort ± bloating
  • Change in bowel habit (frequency or form)
  • Related to defecation
Use Bristol Stool Scale Consider IBS‑D / IBS‑C / IBS‑M / IBS‑U
Alarm features → Urgent referral
  • Unintentional weight loss, rectal bleeding, iron‑deficiency anaemia
  • Age > 60 with new change in bowel habit; positive FIT; raised faecal calprotectin
  • Abdominal/rectal mass; persistent or frequent bloating in women > 50
  • Family history of bowel/ovarian cancer, coeliac disease, or IBD

2) Initial Workup

History
  • Onset, duration, severity; stool description
  • Diet, fibre intake; known triggers (caffeine, alcohol, spicy/fatty foods)
  • Antibiotics, meds (incl. opioids); comorbidities; stress/anxiety/depression
  • Impact on daily function; activity levels
Exam
  • Weight/BMI; assess for weight loss
  • Abdominal palpation; consented rectal exam
Tests to exclude organic disease
  • FBC; ESR/CRP
  • Coeliac serology
  • Faecal calprotectin (esp. ≤45y with diarrhoea)

3) Make a Positive Diagnosis

Explain & reassure
  • Gut–brain axis; chronic, relapsing, benign
  • Not linked to increased cancer or mortality
  • Aim: symptom control & quality of life (may not be complete relief)

4) First‑line Management (all)

Lifestyle
  • Regular meals; balanced diet; adequate fluids
  • Physical activity ≥ 30 min moderate, 5 days/week
  • Weight management if overweight/obese
  • Address stress, anxiety, depression
Dietary pointers
  • Constipation → increase soluble fibre (e.g., ispaghula, oats, linseed)
  • Diarrhoea/bloating → reduce insoluble fibre; limit caffeine, alcohol, fizzy drinks; avoid gas‑producing foods
  • Probiotics: consider trial ≥ 12 weeks; stop if no benefit
Review
  • Plan review within 2 months; tailor next steps to symptoms

5) If Persistent Symptoms → Dietitian

Low FODMAP / Exclusion diet (time‑limited, supervised)
  • Reinforce general advice; consider single‑food avoidance
  • Supervise to avoid nutritional gaps & microbiome impact
  • Re‑introduce FODMAPs per tolerance after restriction phase

6) Drug Therapy (targeted; review at 3 months)

IBS‑C: Constipation
  • Start bulk‑forming laxative; titrate to comfortable soft stool
  • Avoid lactulose (gas/bloating)
  • Severe & ≥12 mo refractory → Linaclotide 290 mcg OD* (often secondary‑care initiation)
IBS‑D: Diarrhoea
  • Loperamide PRN; titrate (usual 6–8 mg/day; max 16 mg/day)
  • Counsel not to exceed dose or duration; rare cardiac risks with overdose
Pain / Spasm
  • Antispasmodics PRN: mebeverine, alverine, peppermint oil
  • If ineffective → low‑dose TCA (e.g., amitriptyline 5–30 mg nocte, off‑label)
  • If TCA not tolerated/ineffective → SSRI (citalopram, fluoxetine, sertraline, off‑label)

* Review linaclotide response at 4–12 weeks; stop if ineffective. Observe cautions (electrolytes/diarrhoea risk).

Safety snippets
  • Check contraindications in BNF/eMC (e.g., loperamide: avoid in UC flare, C. difficile, bacterial enterocolitis)
  • Explain off‑label use for TCAs/SSRIs (pain modulation, GI effects)

7) Refractory or Severe Symptoms

Re‑assess
  • Recheck for alternative diagnoses / new red flags
Refer
  • Gastroenterology: uncertainty, severe disease, refractory to optimal primary care, or patient request
  • Mental health: persistent ≥ 12 months and/or psychological comorbidity; consider CBT/psychological therapies earlier per availability/preferences

Follow‑up & Review

  • Agree follow‑up with patient; initial review in 2–3 months
  • Ongoing if on meds: every 6–12 months; adjust per response/tolerability
