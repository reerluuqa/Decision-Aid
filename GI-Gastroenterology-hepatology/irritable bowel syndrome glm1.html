Diagnosis & Assessment

Red Flags (Refer/Investigate)

Exclude serious conditions (Cancer, IBD, Ovarian) before diagnosing IBS.

  • Unintentional / Unexplained weight loss
  • Rectal bleeding
  • Positive Faecal Immunochemical Test (FIT)
  • Change in bowel habit over age 60
  • Raised faecal calprotectin
  • Iron deficiency anaemia
  • Family history of bowel/ovarian cancer/IBD
  • Abdominal or rectal mass

Positive Diagnosis (NICE)

Diagnose if red flags excluded AND:

  • Abdominal pain/discomfort present for at least 6 months.
  • Relieved by defecation OR associated with altered stool frequency/form.

PLUS at least TWO of:

  • Altered stool passage (straining, urgency, incomplete evacuation)
  • Bloating, distension, tension
  • Symptoms made worse by eating
  • Passage of mucus
ROME IV Pain avg 1 day/week in last 3 months, onset >6 months ago.

Initial Investigations (Primary Care)

Standard Labs:
  • FBC: Anaemia, Platelets
  • ESR/CRP: Inflammatory markers
  • Coeliac Serology: Especially if diarrhoea/mixed symptoms
  • Faecal Calprotectin: If diarrhoea & aged ≤45 (exclude IBD)
Refer to Gastroenterology for:
  • Nocturnal diarrhoea or atypical pain
  • Defecatory disorder (incomplete/blocked evacuation)
  • Faecal incontinence

Management Plan

General Advice

  • Explain: Gut-brain axis, chronic fluctuating nature, lack of cancer risk.
  • Goal: Symptom improvement & QoL, not necessarily cure.
  • Activity: Regular physical activity (30 min, 5 days/week).
  • Mental Health: Assess for stress/anxiety/depression (manage appropriately).

Diet: Diarrhoea/Bloating Predominant

  • Reduce Insoluble fibre: Wholemeal bread, bran cereals, brown rice.
  • Reduce Triggers: Caffeine, alcohol, carbonated drinks, gas-producing foods.
  • Reduce Spicy and fatty foods.

Diet: Constipation Predominant

  • Increase Soluble fibre: Ispaghula (psyllium), oats, linseed.
  • Action Gradually increase fibre intake + fluids.
  • Drink adequate fluid intake.

General Diet

  • Eat regular meals with a healthy, balanced diet.
  • Food diary to identify triggers (up to 90% report food triggers).
  • Probiotics: Try for at least 12 weeks if chosen. Stop if no improvement.

Differential Diagnosis

Condition Key Indicators / Reason to Suspect
Malignancy Red flags present (weight loss, bleeding, mass, change >60).
Inflammatory Bowel Disease Nighttime symptoms, weight loss, raised calprotectin/ESR/CRP, blood in stool.
Coeliac Disease Bloating, diarrhoea, malabsorption signs. (Check serology).
Microscopic Colitis Common in females >45, watery diarrhoea, nocturnal symptoms. Link to NSAIDs/SSRIs/PPIs.
Bile Acid Malabsorption History of cholecystectomy or right hemicolectomy.
Thyroid issues Hypo (constipation) or Hyper (diarrhoe) systemic signs.
Gynaecological Endometriosis, ovarian cancer (persistent bloating >50), premenstrual syndrome.

Prescribing Updates (2022-2025)

New Products & Alerts

  • Enterosgel® (2023): Licensed OTC for IBS with diarrhoea.
  • Loperamide: Caution in drug abuse, Brugada syndrome. Risk of pancreatitis. Do not exceed dose (cardiac issues).
  • Linaclotide: Updated adverse effects (2021).

Common Options

Tailor to subtype (IBS-D vs IBS-C).

  • Antispasmodics: For pain/cramps.
  • Laxatives: For IBS-C (e.g., Linaclotide, PEG).
  • Antimotility: For IBS-D (e.g., Loperamide).
  • Low-dose TCAs or SSRIs: If psychosocial factors or pain refractory.
