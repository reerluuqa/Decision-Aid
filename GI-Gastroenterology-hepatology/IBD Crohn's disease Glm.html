Crohn's Disease Clinical Reference Algorithm

Fast decision-making guide for primary care physicians based on NICE CKS guidance (May 2024)

When to Suspect Crohn's Disease

Red Flag Symptoms

  • Persistent diarrhoea (>4-6 weeks), including nocturnal
  • Abdominal pain/discomfort
  • Unexplained weight loss or faltering growth (children)
  • Fatigue, malaise, anorexia, or fever

Key Examination Findings

  • Pallor, clubbing, or aphthous mouth ulcers
  • Abdominal tenderness/mass (especially RLQ)
  • Perianal abnormalities (tags, fissures, fistulae, abscesses)
  • Extra-intestinal manifestations (joints, eyes, skin, liver)

Complication Recognition

Urgent Referral Indicators

  • Fistulae (bowel-bladder, bowel-vagina, perianal)
  • Intestinal obstruction (colicky pain, distension, vomiting)
  • Significant bleeding
  • Severe malnutrition or failure to thrive

Risk Assessment

Poor Prognostic Factors

  • Early age of onset
  • Perianal disease
  • Steroid dependence at presentation
  • Severe initial presentation
  • History of surgery or complications

Modifiable Risk Factors

  • Smoking (strongly advise cessation)
  • NSAID use (avoid if possible)
  • Oral contraceptive use (discuss risks/benefits)

Primary Care Actions

Initial Investigations

  • FBC (anemia), CRP/ESR (inflammation)
  • Stool calprotectin (if available)
  • Basic biochemistry (nutritional status)
  • Consider stool cultures (exclude infection)

Referral Pathway

  • URGENT: Suspected obstruction, fistula, or severe malnutrition
  • ROUTINE: All suspected cases for definitive diagnosis
  • SPECIALIST: All confirmed cases for ongoing management

Monitoring Requirements

Regular Assessment

  • Disease activity symptoms
  • Nutritional status (especially children)
  • Medication side effects
  • Psychosocial impact
  • Growth and development (pediatric patients)

Quality Standards (NICE)

  • Specialist assessment within 4 weeks of referral
  • Age-appropriate multidisciplinary team support
  • Monitoring for adverse effects of drug treatment

This algorithm is based on NICE CKS guidance (May 2024). Diagnosis and specialist management should occur in secondary care. This reference supports primary care recognition and initial management.
