Lacerations – Quick Scan

Red Flags → Immediate Action
  • Arterial bleeding, loss of pulse, poor perfusion
  • Signs of tetanus: trismus, spasms, rigidity
  • Systemic infection / sepsis
  • Deep structure injury: nerve, tendon, vessel, bone
  • Facial lac crossing lip/ear/eyelid margins
  • High-risk tetanus-prone wound + no HTIG available

Safe to Close Now

  • Clean, non-infected, <6 h old
  • Edges easily approximated
  • No contamination/devitalised tissue
  • Low-tension area (<5 cm)

Refer / Admit

Condition Destination
Vascular/nerve/tendon/bone injury A&E
Complex, gaping, devitalised A&E
Systemic infection / unwell Admit
HTIG not available A&E

Closure Quick Pick

Sutures
  • >5 cm, high tension, cosmetic areas
  • Remove 5–7 d (head) 10–14 d (extremity)
Adhesive / Strips
  • <5 cm, low tension, clean
  • Leave 5–8 d (glue peels itself)

High Risk of Infection

  • Contaminated (soil, faeces, pus)
  • >5 cm, stellate, jagged
  • Devitalised tissue / foreign body
  • Presentation >6 h
  • Age >65, diabetes, immunosuppression

Tetanus Prophylaxis

Immune status Clean wound Tetanus-prone High-risk
Full (<10 y) — — —
Full (>10 y) — Td Td + HTIG
Unknown/incomplete Td Td + HTIG Td + HTIG

Review  •  2–5 d

  • Re-check for infection
  • Swab if pus/spreading erythema
  • Close if no infection
