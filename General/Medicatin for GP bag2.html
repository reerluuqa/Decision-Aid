💧 Diluents
💧 Water for Injection
Most drugs come as solutions. Where they don't, the diluent is specified with each drug below.
💧 Sodium Chloride 0.9% for Injection
Used as diluent where specified (e.g. chlorphenamine).
❤️ Acute Coronary Syndrome
💊 Aspirin 300mg tablets Oral
Dose: 300mg orally as a single dose
⚠️ Unless known allergy to aspirin.
💊 GTN 400mcg Sublingual Spray Sublingual
Dose: 1–2 puffs every 3–5 min · Max 3 doses in 15 min
If GTN fails to relieve pain, consider giving an opioid analgesic with cover for nausea and vomiting (see Pain section).
🔴 Anaphylaxis
💉 Adrenaline 1 in 1000 (1mg/ml) IM ONLY
As ampoule or pre-filled 500mcg pen (e.g. Mini-Jet NOT Epipen)
Dose: 0.5mg (500mcg) intramuscularly
🚫 Do NOT give adrenaline intravenously in anaphylaxis.
🚫 Do NOT use Epipen or equivalent — these contain only 0.3mg/300mcg.
Monitor: BP, pulse, O₂ saturations, ECG as soon as possible.
⚠️ IMPORTANT: If any asthma-like symptoms present, treat as per acute asthma as well.
💉 Chlorphenamine 10mg/ml IM Slow IV
Dose: 10mg IM or slowly IV over 1 min
Can be given after initial resuscitation.
Diluent: Sodium Chloride 0.9%
💉 Hydrocortisone 100mg (Efcortesol pre-mixed*) IM Slow IV
Dose: 200mg (2 ampoules) IM or slowly IV
Can be given after initial resuscitation.
🫁 Asthma
💨 Salbutamol pMDI Inhaler + Large Volume Spacer Inhaled
Dose: 4 puffs then 2 puffs every 2 min up to max 10 puffs
Can be repeated 10–20 min later if needed. 5 gentle tidal breaths per puff. Shake inhaler before each puff.
💨 Salbutamol Nebules Nebulised
Dose: 2.5–5mg · Ideally drive nebuliser with oxygen
⚠️ If severe/life-threatening asthma: consider continuous nebulised salbutamol.
💨 Ipratropium Nebules Nebulised
Dose: 500mcg (0.5mg)
Use combined with nebulised salbutamol if poor response to initial salbutamol or if life-threatening asthma.
🫁 Oxygen High-flow
Give if sats <94% · Target sats 94–98%
High-flow via close-fitting face mask or nasal cannula.
💊 Prednisolone 5mg tablets Oral or Hydrocortisone IV
Dose: 40–50mg prednisolone orally OR 100mg hydrocortisone IV
🩸 Hypoglycaemia
🍬 Quick-Acting Carbohydrate (e.g. Glucogel, Dextrogel) Oral
Dose: One tube
After treatment, if admission not indicated: give oral carbohydrate (e.g. toast ± jam) when patient recovers. Ensure they are left in the care of a responsible adult who can watch for relapse.
💉 Glucagon for Injection IM SC
Dose: 1mg IM or SC
Use if impaired consciousness or unable to take oral glucose.
💉 Glucose 20% 50ml IV
Dose: 50ml of 20% glucose into a large bore vein
If no response to glucagon after 10 min.
⚠️ Very irritant — use large bore vein only.
⚡ Hypo-Adrenalism
💉 Hydrocortisone 100mg (Efcortesol pre-mixed*) IM Slow IV
Dose: 100mg IM or slowly IV
Presents with vomiting, hypotension and shock in someone who has abruptly stopped oral steroids (especially if unwell with intercurrent illness).
💧 Pulmonary Oedema
💉 Furosemide 10mg/ml IV
Dose: 40mg IV over 10 min
Relieve cardiac pain if present (sublingual nitrates, opioids). Alternative: oral furosemide 40mg.
🧠 Bacterial Meningitis & Meningococcal Disease
💉 Ceftriaxone 2g IV infusion IV inj Deep IM
Dose: 2g
For strongly suspected meningococcal disease (don't delay hospital transfer) or strongly suspected bacterial meningitis if significant delay in transfer expected.
Always check BNF.
IM: Doses >1g must be divided between sites.
IV infusion (preferred): Dilute 2g in 40ml of 5%/10% glucose or 0.9% NaCl. Give over ≥30 min.
IV injection: Give over 5 min.
💉 Benzylpenicillin 600mg IV Slow IM
For strongly suspected meningococcal disease / bacterial meningitis. Ceftriaxone preferred if available.
If given IM: give as proximally as possible to an area still warm (better perfusion), e.g. deltoid.
Diluent: Water for Injection
IM: 600mg dissolved in 1.6–2.0ml water.
IV infusion: ≥4ml water for 600mg, ≥8ml for 1200mg (risk of Na⁺ overload in renal/heart failure if diluted in NaCl 0.9%).
🤢 Nausea & Vomiting
💉 Cyclizine 50mg/ml IV IM
Dose: 50mg IV or IM
🚫 Not recommended in Acute Coronary Syndrome (peripheral vasoconstriction) or Heart Failure.
💉 Metoclopramide 5mg/ml IM Slow IV
Dose: 10mg IM or slow IV over 3 min
⚠️ Can cause oculogyric crisis, especially in young and elderly. Reverse with procyclidine.
💉 Prochlorperazine 12.5mg/ml Deep IM
Dose: 12.5mg deep IM
⚠️ Can cause oculogyric crisis, especially in young and elderly. Reverse with procyclidine.
💉 Procyclidine 5mg/ml IV IM
Not an antiemetic — for oculogyric crisis caused by antiemetics (sustained maximum, usually upward, deviation of eyes).
Dose: 5–10mg IV or IM
Usually takes 5–10 min to work, may take up to 30 min.
💊 Opioid Overdose
💉 Naloxone 400mcg/ml IV preferred SC IM
Dose: 400–2000mcg IV · Repeat every 2–3 min · Max 10mg
Most patients only need 400–800mcg (1–2 ampoules). Best given IV. SC/IM have slower response times.
🩹 Pain
💊 Paracetamol, Ibuprofen, Codeine Oral
For mild to moderate pain.
💉 Diclofenac IM PR
Dose: 75mg IM or 100mg suppository
💉 Diamorphine 2.5–10mg IV IM SC
⚠️ Always give with an antiemetic. Controlled drug regulations apply.
Diluent: Water for Injection
Acute Coronary Syndrome: 5mg slow IV. Further 2.5–5mg if needed.
Acute Pulmonary Oedema: 2.5–5mg slow IV.
Acute Pain: 5mg IM or SC (up to 10mg in heavier patients). Half to quarter dose if given IV.
🕊️ Palliative Care
Plan ahead and have 'just in case' drugs in the house.
💉 Hyoscine Butylbromide 20mg/ml SC
Dose: 20mg SC
For bowel colic and excessive respiratory secretions.
Diluent: Sodium Chloride 0.9%
🚫 Do NOT confuse with HYOSCINE HYDROBROMIDE — same indications, very different doses.
💉 Midazolam 2mg/ml SC
Dose: 2.5–5mg SC
For restlessness or mild agitation. For seizures, see Seizures section.
💊 Dexamethasone 2mg tablets Oral
Spinal cord compression: 16mg stat and admit. Early use may save cord function.
Superior vena cava obstruction: 8–12mg daily.
⚡ Seizures
💉 Midazolam Oromucosal Solution 5mg/ml Buccal
Dose: 10mg for buccal absorption
💉 Diazepam Rectal Solution 10mg/2.5ml PR
Dose: 10–20mg rectally (10mg in elderly)
Alternative to buccal midazolam. Repeated once after 10–15 min if needed.
