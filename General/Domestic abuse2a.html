‚ö° Risk Stratification (RCGP Framework)
üö® LEVEL 1: Immediate Safety Risk to Life
Indicators: Person doesn't feel safe to go home, fears immediate serious harm ("I think they're going to kill me"), planning to leave the relationship

Actions:
  • Contact police 999 / call on their behalf
  • Initiate urgent safeguarding (child/adult)
  • Consider MARAC referral (with consent)
  • Always discuss with colleague
‚ö†Ô∏è LEVEL 2: Possible High-Risk DA (No Immediate Safety Risk)
Risk Factors: Severe/escalating abuse, weapons, stalking, sexual assault, attempted strangulation/suffocation/drowning, pregnancy, perpetrator threats (kill victim, harm children, suicide), access to weapons, drug/alcohol use, harm to pets, assault history

Actions:
  • Assess immediate safety
  • Share 999 silent solution
  • Refer to local DA agency
  • Consider MARAC referral
  • Child safeguarding if appropriate
  • Arrange follow-up
‚úÖ LEVEL 3: No Current Immediate or High-Risk Indicators
Consider: Type and severity of abuse, what support patient wants, children/vulnerable people in household, adult's care/support needs

Actions:
  • Provide information about support services
  • Signpost to local DA services
  • Safety planning discussion
  • Arrange follow-up
