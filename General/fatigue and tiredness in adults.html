1-Minute Fatigue Algorithm – Adults (≥18 yr)

1 First moments – “What do you mean by tired?”

  • Sleepiness → think OSA / insomnia
  • Weakness → think neuro-muscular
  • Exhaustion not relieved by rest → think CFS/ME

2 Red-flags (immediate action)

SymptomAction
Weight loss ≥5 % / 6–12 mo?malignancy / Addison / DM / thyrotoxicosis
Fever / night sweats / nodes?infection / lymphoma
Focal neurology?brain lesion / MS
Travel + rash / bites?tropical disease / Lyme

3 60-second screen

Sleep, mood, stress, drugs, alcohol, shift work, chronic disease.

4 6-week rule: investigations to tick

If no obvious cause & fatigue ≥6 wks → order “FERRITIN-C” panel:

  • FBC + Ferritin
  • ESR or CRP
  • Renal & Liver profile
  • Infection screen (Monospot <40 yr, HIV, Hep B/C if risk)
  • TFTs, HbA1c, Coeliac (TTG-IgA)
  • Urinalysis, CK (if muscle pain)
  • Vit D, B12, folate as indicated

5 When to refer

ConditionWhereTiming
Suspected CFS/MESpecialist CFS serviceAfter 3 mo unexplained fatigue + core features
Any red-flag or unclear causeRelevant specialtyASAP

6 Primary-care pearls while waiting

  • Explain biopsychosocial model.
  • Sleep hygiene, stress / mood care.
  • Give leaflet: NHS “Sleep & tiredness”.
  • Annual review for CFS/ME patients.

Quick CFS/ME checklist (all ≥6 wks)

  • Debilitating fatigue worsened by activity & not relieved by rest
  • Post-exertional malaise: delayed, disproportionate, prolonged
  • Unrefreshing sleep / sleep disturbance
  • Cognitive difficulties (memory, concentration, word-finding)

No test confirms CFS; diagnosis is clinical after exclusion.
