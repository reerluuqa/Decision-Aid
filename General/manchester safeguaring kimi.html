Initial Assessment
Immediate actions and threshold evaluation

For all safeguarding concerns - Children & Adults
1 Immediate Danger Assessment 999 First
If there is immediate risk to life, call 999 before any other action. For non-emergency police concerns, call 101. Document your decision-making process.
2 Determine Threshold Level Consult Guidelines
Children: Use "Right Intervention - Right Time" framework (Levels 1-4/5)
Adults: Apply Care Act 2014 Section 42 criteria
Key Question: Is there actual or potential significant harm?
3 Gather Essential Information
Before calling, prepare:
  • Full name, DOB, address, NHS number
  • Family composition and key relationships
  • Specific concerns (what, when, where, who)
  • Relevant history and previous concerns
  • Parents'/adult's awareness and response
  • Your risk assessment justification
‚ö†Ô∏è Confidentiality Note: Safeguarding concerns override confidentiality. You CAN share information without consent if someone is at risk of significant harm. Document your reasoning for information sharing decisions.

Making effective safeguarding referrals

üìû CALL MANCHESTER MASH: 0161 234 5001
Single point of contact for Children & Adults - 24/7
1 Make the Referral Phone First
Always phone first for urgent concerns. Online forms are for non-emergencies only.
Children: Manchester Children's Services Contact Centre ‚Üí MASH
Adults: Adult Social Care Contact Centre ‚Üí Adults MASH
Key Message: State clearly if you believe threshold for significant harm is met
2 Information Sharing Protocol GDPR Compliant
Children: Share info without consent if at risk of significant harm. Inform parents unless it increases risk.
Adults: Obtain consent where possible. Share without consent if:
  • Serious harm to adult or others
  • Coercion or incapacity present
  • Life-threatening neglect
3 Online Referral Forms (Non-Urgent)
Children: Professional Referral Form (Manchester City Council website)
Adults: Adult Safeguarding Referral Form

Post-Referral Actions
What happens next and your ongoing role
‚ñº
1 Children's Cases - Possible Outcomes
  • No Further Action: Redirected to other services
  • Early Help / Child in Need: Section 17 assessment and support
  • Child Protection Enquiry: Section 47 investigation
    • Strategy Discussion/Meeting within 1 working day
    • Initial Child Protection Conference within 15 working days
    • GPs invited to conferences - attend or send report
2 Adult Cases - MASH Risk Ratings
  • RED (High Risk): Same-day multi-agency discussion ‚Üí Section 42 Safeguarding Enquiry
  • AMBER (Moderate): MASH enquiry to gather more information ‚Üí decision on next steps
  • GREEN (Lower): Signposted to other services or no further action
3 Your Ongoing Responsibilities Stay Involved
  • Provide additional information when requested
  • Contribute to Safeguarding Plans (medical oversight, capacity assessments)
  • Attend multi-agency meetings when invited
  • Maintain clear documentation in patient records
  • Follow up if no feedback within 3 working days
4 Escalation Process If Concerns Remain
If you are dissatisfied with the response or decision, use the Escalation and Resolution Protocol (2025). Contact the Designated Safeguarding Professionals for advice on next steps.
‚ö†Ô∏è Professional Allegations (LADO): If concern involves a professional or volunteer who works with children, you must inform the Local Authority Designated Officer (LADO) via Contact Centre.

Documentation & Training
Record keeping and ongoing competency
‚ñº
1 Essential Documentation Legal Requirement
In patient records, document:
  • Time and date of referral
  • Name of person you spoke to at MASH
  • Summary of concerns and evidence
  • Advice given andactions taken
  • Follow-up arrangements
  • Your decision-making rationale (especially if sharing without consent)
2 Required Training Levels Level 3 Expected
GPs should maintain Level 3 competency:
  • Attend MSP multi-agency training (free for Manchester practitioners)
  • Complete e-learning modules via Virtual College platform
  • Topics: Domestic Abuse, Child Exploitation, MCA, FGM, Prevent
  • Regular refreshers and updates
Access Training: MSP Training Booking Portal
