Assessment
Evaluate patient age, risk factors & vaccination history
Age Assessment

Determine the patient's chronological age and vaccination status:

  • Infants & Children: Under 2 years (routine schedule)
  • Children: 2-10 years (clinical risk groups)
  • Adolescents & Adults: 10-64 years (clinical risk groups)
  • Older Adults: 65 years and over (routine vaccination)
Clinical Risk Factors
High Risk Groups
Asplenia or splenic dysfunction
Chronic respiratory disease (COPD, bronchiectasis, CF)
Chronic heart disease (IHD, CHF)
Chronic kidney disease (stage 4-5, nephrotic syndrome)
Chronic liver disease (cirrhosis, biliary atresia)
Diabetes mellitus (insulin/oral therapy)
Immunosuppression (HIV, chemotherapy, steroids)
Cochlear implants
CSF leaks (post-trauma/neurosurgery)
Occupational exposure (welders)

Note: Asthma is NOT an indication unless requiring oral corticosteroids ≥20mg/day for >1 month (or ≥1mg/kg/day for children <20kg)

Vaccination History

Verify previous pneumococcal vaccinations:

  • Confirm dates and vaccine types (PCV13, PCV15, PPV23)
  • Check if partially vaccinated or unvaccinated
  • Review timing of last dose (re-vaccination intervals)
  • If no reliable history → assume unvaccinated
Minimum 8-week interval required between PCV and PPV23
Contraindications & Precautions
Absolute Contraindication

Confirmed anaphylactic reaction to the vaccine or any component

Postpone Vaccination

Acute illness: Postpone if fever or significant systemic upset. Minor illness without fever is NOT a contraindication.

Safe in Pregnancy/Breastfeeding

Treatment & Vaccination Schedule
Implement appropriate vaccination protocol
Available Vaccines

PCV13 (Prevenar 13®)

13-valent conjugate vaccine

Use: Children <2 years, high-risk groups

Schedule: 3-dose primary series + booster

PCV15 (Vaxneuvance®)

15-valent conjugate vaccine

Use: From 6 weeks of age

Coverage: PCV13 serotypes + 22F, 33F

PPV23 (Pneumovax®)

23-valent polysaccharide vaccine

Use: Children ≥2 years, adults

Schedule: Single dose (re-vaccination for select groups)

Children Under 1 Year (Clinical Risk)
Condition Schedule Vaccines
Standard Risk 12 weeks → 1 year PCV13/PCV15 booster
High Risk* 6 weeks → ≥8 weeks → 1 year → ≥8 weeks → 2 years PCV13/PCV15 (3 doses) → PPV23

*High risk: Asplenia, splenic dysfunction, complement disorder, severe immunocompromise

Children 1-2 Years (Clinical Risk)
  • Standard schedule completed: PPV23 at 2 years (≥8 weeks after last PCV)
  • Unvaccinated/Partial: PCV13/PCV15 now → PPV23 ≥8 weeks later
  • High risk groups: Additional PCV13/PCV15 dose after 1-year booster
Children 2-10 Years (Clinical Risk)
  • Vaccinated: PPV23 at least 8 weeks after last PCV
  • Unvaccinated/Partial: PCV13/PCV15 → PPV23 ≥8 weeks later
  • Severe immunocompromise: PCV13/PCV15 regardless of history → PPV23 ≥8 weeks later
10+ Years & Adults (Clinical Risk)
  • Standard risk: Single PPV23 dose
  • Unvaccinated/Partial: PCV13/PCV15 → PPV23 ≥8 weeks later
  • Severe immunocompromise: PCV13/PCV15 → PPV23 ≥8 weeks later
  • Asplenia/Complement disorder: PPV23 (PCV first if unvaccinated)
Re-vaccination every 5 years for asplenia, CKD
Timing Considerations
Critical Timing
  • Minimum interval: 8 weeks between PCV and PPV23
  • Pre-splenectomy: 4-6 weeks before (minimum 2 weeks)
  • Post-splenectomy: Delay ≥2 weeks after operation
  • Pre-chemotherapy: 4-6 weeks before (minimum 2 weeks)
  • Post-chemotherapy: Delay ≥3 months after completion
  • Leukaemia: PCV from 6 months after chemotherapy completion
  • Bone marrow transplant: PCV 9-12 months after transplantation
Follow-up & Monitoring
Post-vaccination care and long-term management
Adverse Reactions

Common (usually mild):

  • Pain, swelling, redness at injection site
  • Mild fever
  • Myalgia, headache
Manage with paracetamol/ibuprofen if needed

Do NOT use prophylactic paracetamol for infants (reduces antibody response)

Anaphylaxis is extremely rare - observe for immediate reactions

Documentation

Record all vaccination details:

  • Date of administration
  • Vaccine product name and type (PCV13/PCV15/PPV23)
  • Batch number and expiry date
  • Dose administered and site of injection
  • Vaccinator name
  • Patient consent (written or verbal for ≥16 years)
Update patient records and immunization registry
Patient Advice
  • Explain benefits of vaccination (prevents serious illness, meningitis)
  • Reassure about safety (serious adverse effects very rare)
  • Provide PHE leaflet "What to expect after vaccinations"
  • Advise on managing mild reactions (paracetamol/ibuprofen if needed)
  • Provide information about red flags requiring medical attention
NHS website provides comprehensive vaccination information
Herd Immunity

PCV vaccination contributes to population protection by:

  • Reducing pneumococcal carriage in vaccinated individuals
  • Preventing onward transmission to unvaccinated contacts
  • Reducing overall invasive pneumococcal disease in the community
High vaccine coverage benefits entire population
