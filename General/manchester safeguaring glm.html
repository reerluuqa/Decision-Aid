Referral Checklist

  • Is there immediate danger? Call 999.
  • For Children: Consult "Right Intervention – Right Time" (Thresholds).
  • For Adults: Check Care Act S42 criteria (Care needs + Abuse risk + Inability to protect).
  • Have Consent? Ideally yes. If NO but risk is high -> Share anyway (Public Interest).

Before You Call/Refer

Demographics

Name, DOB, Address, NHS No., Family composition.

The Concern

What happened? When? Who? Specific details of disclosure/observation.

Safety Measures

Immediate actions taken? Police involved? Capacity/Consent status?

Thresholds: "Right Intervention"

Level Action
Early Help Support below statutory threshold. No Social Care referral needed.
Child in Need (S17) Significant needs, no serious harm yet. Social work assessment required.
Child Protection (S47) Significant Harm. Immediate strategy discussion & investigation.

*If unsure, call MASH to consult.

MASH Process (Children)

  • Referral Received: Screened by Contact Centre.
  • MASH Triage: Multi-agency info share (Police/Health/Edu).
  • Decision (1 Working Day):
    • No further action / Signpost
    • Child in Need (S17)
    • Section 47 Enquiry
  • If S47: Strategy Meeting within timescales. GPs usually invited to CP Conference.

Consent & Information Sharing

Rule of Thumb: You can share info without consent if a child is at risk of significant harm.

  • Tell parents/carers unless it increases risk to child or compromises police investigation.
  • Document your decision-making clearly in notes.

Care Act Section 42 Criteria

Referral required if ALL 3 apply:

  1. Has care & support needs (18+)
  2. Experiencing (or at risk of) abuse/neglect
  3. Unable to protect themselves due to those needs

Principle: Making Safeguarding Personal. Adult's wishes are central, unless serious risk/crime overrides.

Adults MASH Flow

RED (High Risk)
Same-day "MASH Huddle" -> Section 42 Enquiry.
AMBER (Moderate)
MASH Enquiry -> Gather info -> Decision on S42.
GREEN (Low)
Signpost to support / No formal action.

Mental Capacity & Consent

Has Capacity?

Respect their refusal unless risk to others is critical.

Lacks Capacity?

Best Interest decision. If abuse/neglect suspected, S42 enquiry usually triggered.
