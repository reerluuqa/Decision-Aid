Core Concept

What is Social Prescribing?

Referring patients to non-medical support to improve health & wellbeing. It links patients to community groups, charities, and services.

  • Focus: "What matters to you?"
  • Free & Confidential
  • Key part of NHS Personalised Care
Local Service

Manchester: Be Well Service

Run by Big Life Group in collab with NHS/Council. Accepts anyone living in Manchester or registered with a Manchester GP.

Benefits

Key Success Metrics

  • Reduced Demand: 42.2% drop in GP appointments (Tameside eval).
  • Reduced A&E: 15-24% reduction in attendances in some regions.
  • Patient Confidence: Improved self-management for long-term conditions.
  • Cost Saving: Significant reduction in secondary care costs.

Patient Engagement Tips

1. Normalize it: "It's part of our NHS care."

2. Warm handoff: Introduce the link worker personally if possible.

3. Sell the benefit: "They can help with the things I don't have time for."

4. Follow up: Ask how the group/activity went at next visit.

Manchester COVID-19 Response Case Study

The Be Well service demonstrated exceptional agility during the pandemic.

Challenge: Vulnerable patients isolated, needing meds and food.
Action: Shifted focus to welfare. Mobilized community networks to deliver essentials.
Result: Supported 1,100+ people in a single week. Proved the strength of NHS-Voluntary partnership.
