DO NOT advise a victim to leave their partner without a safety plan.

Leaving is the time of highest risk for serious injury or homicide. Always ensure support (police/specialist agencies) is in place first.

üìû The Silent Solution
DIAL 999 -> Press 55

If the victim cannot speak, advise them to dial 999. If prompted by the operator, pressing 55 alerts police to a genuine emergency.

üìä RCGP Risk Assessment
Level Action Required
1. Immediate Risk to Life
Fear of killing/harm, or plans to leave.
  • Call 999 (or help pt call)
  • Urgent Safeguarding (Adult/Child)
  • MARAC Referral (consent not req if high risk)
2. High Risk
Escalating abuse, weapons, strangulation, pregnancy.
  • Assess immediate safety
  • Share Silent Solution (999+55)
  • Refer to local DA agency
  • Consider MARAC
3. Standard Risk
No immediate threats.
  • Signpost to support
  • Discuss needs
  • Document
üìù Safe Documentation
Code: "History of Domestic Abuse"
  • Visibility: HIDE consultation from online access.
  • Content: Use patient's own words. Objective.
  • Perpetrator: NEVER record in perpetrator's notes unless they self-disclose.
  • Linking: Link records (victim/child) if possible/safe.
üß† Why they stay? (Barriers)
  • Fear (escalation/homicide)
  • Financial dependence
  • Shame / Low self-esteem
  • Immigration status
  • Fear of Social Services
  • Cultural/Religious pressure
  • "Love" / Hope for change
  • Don't recognize it as abuse
üë• Special Considerations

Children: Recognized as victims under DA Act 2021. Witnessing abuse = Safeguarding concern. Watch for CAPFA (Child to Parent Violence).

Health Professionals:

  • Higher risk than general population.
  • Barriers: Fear of GMC, professional shame, "it shouldn't happen to me".
  • Perpetrator may sabotage work (sleep deprivation, false accusations).
üì¢ Sharing Without Consent

Permissible if:

  1. Risk of serious harm or homicide.
  2. Safeguarding concern (Child or Vulnerable Adult).
  3. MARAC Referral (high risk assessed).
