Acid-Base & Electrolyte Disorders Quick Reference

Last updated: Mar 12, 2024
CRITICAL: Immediate intervention required for severe hyperkalaemia (>6.0 mmol/L), severe hypocalcaemia, DKA, and HHS

Respiratory Acidosis

High
Definition:
pH < 7.35, PCO₂ > 6.0 kPa (45 mmHg)
Causes:
COPD, pneumonia, drug use, foreign body
Clinical:
Hypoventilation, respiratory muscle fatigue
Emergency Actions
Treatment

Respiratory Alkalosis

Medium
Definition:
pH > 7.45, PCO₂ < 4.7 kPa (35 mmHg)
Causes:
Pulmonary embolism, sepsis, hyperventilation
Clinical:
Light-headedness, paraesthesia, syncope
Red Flags
Management

Metabolic Acidosis

High
Definition:
pH < 7.35, HCO₃⁻ < 22 mmol/L
Causes:
DKA, renal failure, lactic acidosis, toxins
Clinical:
Kussmaul breathing, nausea, altered mental status
Emergency Actions
Treatment

Metabolic Alkalosis

Hyponatraemia

Variable
Definition:
Na⁺ < 135 mmol/L (severe: <120)
Causes:
SIADH, hypovolemia, thiazides, heart failure
Clinical:
Headache, confusion, seizures, coma
Emergency Actions
Treatment

Hypernatraemia

Medium
Definition:
Na⁺ > 145 mmol/L
Causes:
Water loss, diabetes insipidus, sodium gain
Clinical:
Thirst, weakness, confusion, seizures
Red Flags
Management

Hypokalaemia

High
Definition:
K⁺ < 3.5 mmol/L (severe: <2.5)
Causes:
Diuretics, vomiting, diarrhoea, aldosteronism
Clinical:
Muscle weakness, arrhythmias, ECG changes
Emergency Actions
Treatment

Hyperkalaemia

High
Definition:
K⁺ > 5.0 mmol/L (severe: >6.0)
Causes:
Renal failure, ACE inhibitors, tissue breakdown
Clinical:
Muscle weakness, palpitations, ECG changes
Emergency Actions
Treatment

Hypocalcaemia

High
Definition:
Ca²⁺ < 2.15 mmol/L
Causes:
Hypoparathyroidism, vitamin D deficiency, pancreatitis
Clinical:
Paraesthesia, tetany, seizures, Chvostek's sign
Emergency Actions
Treatment

Hypercalcaemia

Medium
Definition:
Ca²⁺ > 2.50 mmol/L (severe: >3.0)
Causes:
Hyperparathyroidism, malignancy, vitamin D excess
Clinical:
Stones, bones, groans, psychiatric overtones
Red Flags
Management

Magnesium Deficiency

Medium

Diabetic Ketoacidosis

High
Definition:
Glucose >13.9, pH <7.3, ketones
Causes:
Insulin deficiency, infection, stress
Clinical:
Polyuria, polydipsia, nausea, Kussmaul breathing
Emergency Actions
Treatment

Hyperosmolar HHS

High
Definition:
Glucose >33, osmolality >320, pH >7.3
Causes:
Infection, inadequate fluid intake
Clinical:
Extreme dehydration, altered mental status
Emergency Actions
Treatment
