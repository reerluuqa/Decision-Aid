Ensuring Access to Primary Care

Key Principle

GP practices have a duty to ensure PEH can access appropriate health and social care. No fixed abode is NOT a barrier to registration.

  • Registration: Register all patients at first consultation. No ID required
  • Homelessness Lead: Appoint a dedicated staff member to coordinate care
  • Private Areas: Offer confidential discussion spaces away from reception
  • Extended Appointments: Provide longer slots for complex needs Addiction/Mental Health
  • Reminders: Use appointment cards, text/phone reminders if possible
  • Support Workers: Allow PEH to bring advocates to appointments
  • No Penalties: Do not discharge for missed appointments Critical

Comprehensive Health Assessment

Assess at each contact:

  • Housing Circumstances: Current situation and stability
  • QNI Health Assessment: Template covering physical health, LTCs, substance use, mental health, sexual health
  • Children/Dependents: Identify safeguarding issues Follow local protocols
  • Historical Context: Past trauma, adverse childhood experiences, service experiences
  • Capacity: Assess decision-making capacity and autonomy

Mental Health & Dual Diagnosis

  • Depression & Anxiety: Almost twice as likely as general population Screen routinely
  • PTSD: Common due to trauma history
  • Bipolar Disorder: Higher prevalence
  • Conduct Disorders: In younger PEH
  • Trimorbidity: Physical + Mental + Substance misuse co-occurring Complex Care Needed
  • Neurodiversity: Higher rates of unrecognized conditions

Substance Misuse

Integration with Mental Health

Substance misuse often co-occurs with mental health problems. Integrated treatment approaches are essential.

  • Alcohol Dependence: High prevalence
  • Opioid Dependence: Requires specialist support
  • Benzodiazepines: Common misuse, withdrawal risk
  • Drug-related Deaths: Leading cause of mortality Naloxone provision

Special Populations - Children & Young People

  • Mental Health: Increased risk of depression, PTSD, conduct disorders
  • Development: Lower educational attainment, developmental delays
  • Nutrition: Malnutrition, iron deficiency anemia
  • Infections: Vaccine-preventable diseases, asthma, ear infections
  • Safety: Higher risk of abuse, exploitation Safeguarding Priority

Safeguarding & Risk Assessment

Immediate Action Required for:

  • Immediate risk of harm to self or others
  • Safeguarding concerns for children/dependents
  • Domestic violence or abuse
  • Exploitation or modern slavery
  • Severe malnutrition or untreated acute illness
Treatment & Management

Specialist Referral Pathways

When to Refer

Refer to Specialist Primary Healthcare Centres for PEH (SPHCPH) or homelessness multidisciplinary teams when locally available and based on patient preference.

Available Services:
GP Appointments Mental Health Triage Substance Misuse Dental Services Outreach Programs Drop-in Services

Trauma-Informed Care Approach

Best Practice Psychologically Informed Environment:

  • Build organizational awareness of psychological needs
  • Create safe physical and social spaces
  • Provide staff training and ongoing support
  • Implement reflective practice and service evaluation

Best Practice Trauma-Informed Care Principles:

  • Understanding, recognizing, and responding to trauma effects
  • Emphasize physical, relational, and emotional safety
  • Help rebuild narratives of connection, control, and empowerment
  • Use recovery-oriented language (avoid jargon/acronyms)
  • Shared decision-making and strengths-based approaches

Housing & Social Care Support

Homelessness Reduction Act 2017

Public authorities must notify local housing authority of potential homelessness. While not mandatory for primary care, good practice to refer to LHA.

Medication Considerations

  • Storage: Consider lack of refrigeration/storage facilities
  • Adherence: Support with medication management Complex regimens
  • Supervised Consumption: For high-risk medications
  • Multicompartment Aids: May help with organization
  • Review: Regular medication reviews essential

Peer Advocacy & Support

Evidence-Based Peer support improves engagement:

  • Involve peers/advocates in consultations
  • Refer to peer advocacy services if available
  • Promote self-reliance and empowerment
  • Build trust through shared experiences
Follow-up & Long-term Support

Review & Monitoring

Review Timing

Reassess needs whenever circumstances change OR when requested by patient. Avoid standard review periods.

  • Flexible Reviews: Patient-led timing
  • Deterioration Monitoring: Watch for increasing needs Early Intervention
  • Recovery Journey: Non-linear progress is normal Hidden Risks
  • Family Support: Encourage child development checks & immunizations

Housing Transition Support

Moving to independent accommodation can be stressful and isolating:

  • Practical Support: Tenancy skills, budgeting, bills
  • Emotional Support: Loneliness and isolation risk
  • Continued Care: Maintain healthcare engagement
  • Community Links: Connect to local resources
  • Safety Net: Ensure know how to access help if at risk of re-homelessness
High Relapse Risk Period

Multidisciplinary Team Coordination

Best Practice Coordinate across sectors:

  • Primary Care: Ongoing health management
  • Secondary Care: Hospital liaison, discharge planning
  • Social Care: Support packages, safeguarding
  • Housing: Tenancy support, housing options
  • Voluntary Sector: Day centres, outreach, advocacy

Re-engagement Strategies

For patients who disengage from services:

  • Active Outreach: Proactive contact attempts
  • Flexible Methods: Phone, text, email, face-to-face
  • Third-party Involvement: Through support workers, peers, family
  • Reduce Barriers: Simplify appointment processes
  • Build Trust: Patience and persistence

Special Considerations

Maternity Care:
  • Direct access to maternity services preferred
  • Enhanced support throughout pregnancy
  • Postnatal care planning
  • Safeguarding for mother and baby Priority
Palliative & End-of-Life Care:
  • Identify early: death likely within 6-12 months
  • Coordinate multidisciplinary palliative care
  • Address homelessness during end-of-life
  • Spiritual and cultural support
Frailty & Premature Aging:
  • PEH may experience frailty earlier than general population 40s-50s
  • Comprehensive geriatric assessment principles
  • Cognitive and physical decline monitoring
  • Adapted long-term care planning

Outcome Measures & Audit

Track progress in:

  • Registration Rates: % PEH registered with GP
  • Engagement: Appointment attendance rates
  • Screening: Participation in national programs
  • Vaccination: Up-to-date immunization rates
  • Hospital Use: A&E attendance, admission rates
  • Housing Stability: Maintenance of accommodation
  • Patient Experience: Satisfaction surveys
