Lipid Modification for CVD Prevention

A quick reference algorithm based on NICE CKS guidance (last revised July 2025). This is a memory aide and not a substitute for clinical judgment.

Primary Prevention

1. Identify Patient

  • Offer Statin: QRISK3 ≥10%, T1DM (>40y, >10y duration, or nephropathy), CKD, or all people with HIV (≥40y).
  • Consider Statin: Age ≥85, or QRISK3 <10% with clinical concern or patient preference.

2. Pre-Treatment Assessment

  • Discuss lifestyle modifications.
  • Baseline Bloods: Lipid Profile, LFTs, eGFR, HbA1c.
  • CK: Only for unexplained muscle symptoms.
  • Refer to Specialist if: Total-C >9.0, non-HDL-C >7.5, or TGs >20 (urgent).

3. Initiate & Monitor

First-line: Atorvastatin 20 mg once daily.

TARGET: >40% reduction in non-HDL Cholesterol

Review with lipid profile at 2-3 months.

4. Optimize / Escalate

  1. Discuss adherence & lifestyle.
  2. Titrate Atorvastatin up to 80 mg daily.
  3. If target still not met, add Ezetimibe 10 mg daily.

Statin Intolerance

Stop & restart, reduce dose, switch statin, or try alternate-day dosing. If still intolerant, offer Ezetimibe 10 mg. Consider adding Bempedoic Acid if needed.

Secondary Prevention

1. Identify Patient

  • Offer statin to all adults with established CVD (MI, Angina, Stroke/TIA, PAD, Revascularization).

2. Pre-Treatment Assessment

  • Do not delay treatment. Discuss lifestyle concurrently.
  • Baseline Bloods: Lipid Profile, LFTs, eGFR, HbA1c.
  • CK: Only for unexplained muscle symptoms.
  • Refer to Specialist if: Total-C >9.0, non-HDL-C >7.5, or TGs >20 (urgent).

3. Initiate & Monitor

First-line: Atorvastatin 80 mg once daily.

(Use a lower dose for CKD, high AE risk, or patient preference).

TARGET: LDL-C ≤2.0 mmol/L or non-HDL-C ≤2.6 mmol/L

Review with lipid profile at 2-3 months.

4. Optimize / Escalate

  1. Discuss adherence; ensure max tolerated statin dose.
  2. Add Ezetimibe 10 mg daily.
  3. If target still not met, consider specialist options: Icosapent Ethyl (if TGs high), or injectables (PCSK9i/Inclisiran). Referral is appropriate.

Statin Intolerance

Offer Ezetimibe 10 mg monotherapy. If target not met, consider adding Bempedoic Acid or an injectable based on eligibility. Referral is appropriate.

Universal Monitoring & Safety

Test When to Check Action Required
Full Lipid Profile Baseline, 2-3 months after change, then annually. Guide treatment decisions based on targets.
LFTs (ALT/AST) Baseline, 2-3 months after change, then at 12 months. If >3x ULN: Stop statin. Re-check in 4 weeks. Do not restart if still elevated.
Creatine Kinase (CK) Only if patient has persistent, unexplained muscle symptoms. If >5x ULN: Stop statin. Re-check after 7 days. Do not restart if still elevated.
