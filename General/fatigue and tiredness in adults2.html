Tiredness/Fatigue in Adults – One‑Page Primary Care Algorithm

UK primary care · ≥18 yrs · based on NICE CKS “Tiredness/fatigue in adults” and NG206 (last full review Oct 2021; minor update Mar 2025 – topic under review)
Fast memory aide for 10‑minute consultations • Use search, print to A4

Stepwise Algorithm

1) Initial Assessment

Clarify fatigue vs sleepiness vs weakness; determine duration, severity, impact.
  • Onset sudden (infection/toxin/drug/PTSD) vs gradual (renal/hepatic failure, DM, thyroid, electrolytes, anaemia, depression)
  • Sleep: quality/quantity, snoring, witnessed apnoea, restless legs, nocturia, naps
  • Mood & psychosocial: depression, anxiety, stress, loss, PTSD, social supports, carer burden
  • Lifestyle: work/shift pattern, alcohol, drugs, diet, activity level
  • Medical: chronic diseases (asthma, COPD, CKD, HF), infection risks, travel/ticks/rash, CO exposure
  • Medicines: sedatives, antidepressants, muscle relaxants, opioids, antihypertensives, antihistamines, OTC/herbals
  • CFS-associated symptoms: post‑exertional malaise, cognitive issues, unrefreshing sleep, chronic pain; autonomic dysfunction; temperature/sensory hypersensitivities; myoclonus; flu‑like symptoms; food/chemical intolerance
  • Functional impact: ADLs, work/education, risk factors (female sex, high BMI, low activity/SES)

2) Screen for Red Flags (consider urgent referral)

  • Unintentional weight loss ≥5% in 6–12 months
  • Fever, night sweats, lymphadenopathy
  • Focal neurological signs / neurodegenerative concern
  • Inflammatory joint/connective tissue disease features
  • High‑risk travel/tick exposure ± rash
  • Suspected malignancy; HIV; acute hepatitis; active TB; Addison’s; uncontrolled DM/thyrotoxicosis

3) Examination

  • General and targeted system exam based on history
  • Mental state exam (mood, cognition)

4) Investigations (if no obvious cause; especially if >6 weeks)

First‑line — FBC + ferritin; ESR/CRP; LFT; U&E; TFTs; HbA1c; IgA tissue transglutaminase; Urinalysis; CK (if myalgia/weakness); Bone profile + myeloma screen (≥60y)
Consider Vitamin D, B12, folate; Monospot (<40y); HIV/Hep B/C (risk); AM cortisol; CXR ± sputum (TB risk); Lyme; EBV/CMV if suggested.

5) Suspect Chronic Fatigue Syndrome (provisional)

  • Persistent fatigue ≥6 weeks, disabling, not lifelong; not explained by another illness; not relieved by rest
  • All of: post‑exertional malaise (delayed, disproportionate, prolonged); unrefreshing sleep/sleep disturbance; cognitive difficulties (attention, memory, word‑finding, slowed response)
  • No specific diagnostic test for CFS (clinical diagnosis)

6) Primary Care Management

  • Treat reversible causes & contributory factors; share a biopsychosocial explanation
  • Sleep hygiene: avoid long daytime naps; regular schedule
  • Activity: pacing/energy management; avoid boom‑and‑bust
  • Mood: manage anxiety/depression/PTSD if present
  • Diet: balanced intake; refer dietitian if weight loss, gain, or restrictive diet
  • Mobility: refer physio/OT for graded activity within energy envelope
  • Orthostatic intolerance: consider secondary care advice
  • Pain: follow chronic pain guidance
  • CBT: offer to those who would find it useful to support symptom management and functioning
  • CFS has no proven curative treatment; symptoms can be managed; course fluctuates

7) Referral

  • Underlying cause present → Refer to appropriate specialty if not manageable in primary care
  • Suspected CFS meeting criteria and symptoms persist ≥3 months → Refer to specialist CFS service
  • Persistent unexplained fatigue (doesn’t meet CFS criteria): consider specialty referral if diagnostic uncertainty, need for second opinion, access to CFS service care models, or for occupational/disability assessments

8) Follow‑Up

  • CFS: review at least annually: condition course & impact; new symptoms; self‑management & activity plans; support needs; psychological wellbeing; upcoming changes/challenges
  • Seek specialist input if deterioration or uncertainty
  • Investigate new or changed symptoms to exclude other pathology

Scope & Notes

Adults (≥18). Covers assessment/initial management of tiredness/fatigue in UK primary care, incl. suspected CFS. Excludes detailed management of specific causes & cancer‑related fatigue.
Primary care UK/NICE NG206‑aligned

Red Flags (summary)

  • Weight loss · Fever/night sweats · Lymphadenopathy
  • Focal neurology; inflammatory joint/connective tissue disease
  • High‑risk infections (HIV, hepatitis, TB)
  • Endocrine/metabolic crises (Addison’s, severe DM/thyroid)
  • Malignancy concern
  • Travel/tick exposure ± rash

First‑Line Tests

  • FBC + ferritin
  • ESR/CRP
  • LFT · U&E (electrolytes)
  • TFTs
  • HbA1c
  • IgA tTG (coeliac)
  • Urinalysis (protein/blood/glucose)
  • CK (if myalgia/weakness)
  • Bone profile + myeloma screen (≥60y)
Extras if indicated: Vit D, B12/folate, Monospot (<40y), HIV, Hep B/C, AM cortisol, CXR/TB tests, Lyme, EBV/CMV.

CFS – Core Diagnostic Features

  • Fatigue ≥6 weeks, disabling, not explained by another disease
  • Post‑exertional malaise (delayed, prolonged)
  • Unrefreshing sleep / sleep disturbance
  • Cognitive impairment (attention, memory, processing)
  • Clinical diagnosis; no single test
