Palliative Oral Care — 1‑Page Algorithm

Adults ≥ 16 NICE CKS · Apr 2025 Primary Care Quick‑Ref

Assess fast · Act on red flags

Ask
Dry mouth, pain, halitosis, altered taste, excessive saliva, chewing/speaking issues, dysphagia, bleeding.
Look
Dryness, erythema, plaques/membranes, ulcers/vesicles, tongue coating/depapillation, gingival swelling/bleeding, denture/teeth debris; saliva absent/ropey; painful swallow/voice change.
Investigate (if indicated)
FBC if neutropenia suspected; platelets if spontaneous bleeding with mucositis; iron/folate/B12 if recurrent aphthae (not terminal); targeted swab for Candida (heavy growth) ± speciation if persistent.

Red flags → same‑day specialist/urgent care

  • Severe mucositis limiting intake / treatment tolerance or spontaneous gingival bleeding.
  • Suspected neutropenia or neutropenic ulcers.
  • Severe/persistent Candida; severe HSV; bleeding oral ulcers.
  • Nutrition risk (reduced intake), uncontrolled pain, or communication/swallow safety concerns.

Prevention & routine care

  • Brush with soft brush + fluoride twice daily; warm water rinse after meals.
  • Choice of mouthwash: water or 0.9% saline. Chlorhexidine (≤ 2×/day) if infection risk—may sting (alcohol) and alters taste; avoid at same time as nystatin.
  • Increase frequency: q2h if high risk; hourly if severe problems (e.g., infections, mucositis, dehydration, immunosuppression, diabetes, oxygen therapy).
  • Dentures: remove nightly; clean with soft brush + unperfumed soap/denture paste; soak plastic in dilute sodium hypochlorite; metal in chlorhexidine; rinse well.
  • Before chemo/RT: pretreatment dental assessment; allow 10 days healing after extractions; avoid abrasive foods; stop smoking/alcohol.
  • Consider antifungal prophylaxis in high‑risk patients—specialist advice.

Products containing sugar/acid hasten caries in dry mouth; avoid acidic saliva products (e.g., Glandosane®) in people with own teeth.

At‑a‑glance notes

  • Manage reversible causes where possible; otherwise prioritize comfort & dignity.
  • Humidify oxygen if used; use water‑soluble lip lubricant if on oxygen (avoid petroleum jelly in that case).
  • Some advocate pineapple chunks for mucosal comfort; consider sugar/acid risks.
  • Foam swabs: MHRA alert—risk of head detaching/choking; if used, extreme caution per manufacturer.

Symptom‑based management

Dry mouth (xerostomia)

  • Treat cause Review meds (many anticholinergic), correct dehydration, manage anxiety, treat Candida; consider humidifying oxygen.
  • Relief Frequent sips/sprays of water; ice chips/ice lollies; sugar‑free gum/boiled sweets; topical saliva stimulants or artificial saliva substitutes.
  • Avoid acidic foods/products in people with own teeth.

Oral pain

  • Topical (local) Choline salicylate gel (avoid excess under dentures), benzydamine spray, lidocaine 5% ointment/10% spray (avoid pre‑meal pharyngeal anaesthesia).
  • Topical (diffuse) Benzydamine mouthwash (dilute 1:1 to reduce stinging).
  • Systemic Paracetamol or NSAID → add weak opioid → strong opioid for severe pain; consider 24‑h CSCI if cannot swallow. Seek specialist help if difficult.

Oral Candida (thrush)

  • Address risk factors (hygiene, dentures, xerostomia, antibiotics).
  • Immunocompetent 1st‑line topical nystatin or miconazole; if persistent → oral fluconazole.
  • Immunocompromised High‑dose oral fluconazole usually preferred 1st‑line.
  • Dentures: clean with chlorhexidine; chlorhexidine may be adjunctive.
  • Persistent cases → specialist advice.

Mouth ulcers & mucositis

  • Aphthous ulcers Correct iron/folate/B12 deficits; topical steroids (hydrocortisone lozenges early; beclomethasone spray or betamethasone soluble tablets if extensive); chlorhexidine mouthwash (not with nystatin).
  • Mucositis (chemo/RT) Meticulous oral care; urgent specialist if severe or gingival bleeding. Use sterile saline rinses; ice chips; treat infections (e.g., Candida). Avoid spicy/acidic foods, alcohol, tobacco, hot/fizzy drinks; prefer soft low‑salt foods. Opioids may be required.
  • Herpes simplex Fluids/antipyretics; immunocompetent early lip lesions → topical aciclovir 5% q4h ×5 days or penciclovir 1% q2h (awake) ×4 days. Early intra‑oral → oral aciclovir 200 mg 5×/day ×5 days. Immunocompromised → oral aciclovir 400 mg 5×/day ×5 days. Prevent secondary infection with mouth care ± short chlorhexidine course. Admit if severe or severely immunocompromised.
  • Malodorous malignant ulcers Regular cleansing; if anaerobic (foul smell) consider metronidazole (long‑term may be needed) — dose & duration with specialist. Bleeding ulcers → specialist. Manage pain as above.
  • Neutropenic ulcers Suspect? → urgent specialist assessment.

Halitosis · Excessive salivation · Altered taste

  • Halitosis (oral cause) Rigorous oral hygiene incl. tongue cleaning; fluids; diet modification (avoid garlic/onion); antimicrobial gargle (e.g., chlorhexidine); artificial saliva if very dry.
  • Excessive salivation Uncommon; address cause (painful ulcers, oral cancer, dysphagia). Seek specialist advice if troublesome or aspiration risk.
  • Altered taste Treat xerostomia/Candida; consider stopping taste‑altering drugs if possible; diet tips: tart foods if no ulcers; foods served cold/room temp; flavouring; manage urea taste (e.g., marinade, stronger seasoning, liquids). After RT, reassure: taste often partially returns 20–60 days and usually within 2–4 months.

End‑of‑life oral care

  • Include dry‑mouth care in personalized plan; stop/change xerogenic meds if appropriate.
  • Conscious: moisten q30 min (spray/drops/ice chips). Unconscious: at least hourly.
  • Use water‑soluble lip lubricant if on oxygen; consider room humidifier.
  • Assist with teeth/denture care as tolerated; provide symptomatic analgesia (stop burdensome disease‑directed treatments).
