Pulmonary Embolism Quick Reference

Clinical decision support tool for rapid assessment and management

Last Updated:
September 2023

IMMEDIATE ADMISSION REQUIRED

For patients with hemodynamic instability or who are pregnant/postpartum (within 6 weeks)

Key Symptoms & Signs
Dyspnea

Most common symptom (50% of cases)

Pleuritic Chest Pain

Present in 39% of cases, typically localized

Haemoptysis
Syncope or Pre-syncope
Tachypnea

Present in 21-39% of cases

Tachycardia

Heart rate > 100 bpm

Hypotension

Systolic BP < 90 mmHg

Fever
Assessment Tools
Clinical Feature Points
Clinical signs and symptoms of DVT 3
PE is #1 diagnosis or equally likely 3
Heart rate > 100 bpm 1.5
Immobilization ≥ 3 days or surgery in previous 4 weeks 1.5
Previous DVT/PE 1.5
Haemoptysis 1
Cancer 1
Interpretation:
• PE Likely: Score > 4 points
• PE Unlikely: Score ≤ 4 points

Use PERC rule if clinical suspicion is LOW and patient is not pregnant:

If all criteria are met: PE can be safely ruled out without further testing
Management Approach
Immediate Admission

Hemodynamic instability or pregnancy/postpartum (within 6 weeks)

Assess Clinical Probability

Use two-level PE Wells score to determine likelihood

Initial Investigations

Chest X-ray, ECG, D-dimer if PE unlikely (Wells ≤ 4)

Note: Do not delay management for results of chest X-ray or ECG. These are not diagnostic for PE but may help with differential diagnosis.
Anticoagulation

Start immediately if no contraindications

Follow-up

Review at 3 months, then at least annually if long-term treatment

Screening

Consider cancer screening and thrombophilia testing if indicated

Special Considerations: Management during pregnancy requires specialist input and modified treatment approaches.

Quick Facts
  • Incidence: 39-115 per 100,000 population
  • Mortality: 5-10% present hemodynamically unstable with >15% early mortality
  • CTEPH Risk: 0.1-9.1% within 2 years after PE
  • Recurrence Rate: Up to 10% in first year after stopping anticoagulation
  • Maternal Deaths: Leading cause of direct deaths within 42 days postpartum
