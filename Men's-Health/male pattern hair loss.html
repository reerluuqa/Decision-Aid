Male Pattern Hair Loss – Quick Reference

Last validated: February 2022 | NHS CKS condensed

1. Confirm Correct Topic
  • Patient: ♂ ≥ 18 yr
  • Pattern: bitemporal recession → vertex → crown
  • Exclude: female pattern, alopecia areata, telogen effluvium, scarring alopecia
2. 30-Second Triage

History

Onset, rate, family, meds, diet, psych impact

Exam

Hamilton-Norwood stage; look for scarring

Hair-pull test

>3 hairs = active shedding

Red Flags

Patchy, scarring, systemic illness → investigate TFT/FBC/Ferritin/Vit D
3. Management Pathway
Patient Choice / Severity First-Line Options Key Counselling
Mild or accepts cosmetic No Rx OR wigs, fibres, styling Hair loss continues
Moderate–severe, wants Rx • Minoxidil 5 % foam (18–49 yr NHS)
• Minoxidil 5 % soln (≤ 65 yr OTC/private)
• Finasteride 1 mg (private) 		• 2–4 mo to benefit (minoxidil)
• 3–6 mo to stabilise (finasteride)
• Stop → loss within 6–12 mo
Non-response / atypical → Dermatology Specialist Rx/transplant
4. Safety Checklist
  • Minoxidil: avoid <18 yr, >65 yr (foam >49 yr), HTN, scalp disease
  • Finasteride: counsel on sexual dysfunction, mood changes; pregnant partners use condom; monitor PSA (×2 after 6 mo)
  • Combo: specialists may combine despite PIL warnings
