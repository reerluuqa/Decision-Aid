Trichomoniasis Quick-Reference Algorithm

NICE CKS – December 2024

1. Is Trichomoniasis Likely?

  • Women: frothy green-yellow discharge, vulval itch, dysuria, odour (↑pH > 4.5)
  • Men: urethral discharge ± dysuria (often asymptomatic)
  • Risk flags: new/multiple partners, co-existing STI, BV, inconsistent condom use

2. Immediate Action

1

All symptomatic or exposed patients → offer GUM referral

2

If declined / unavailable → proceed with primary-care testing

3. Point-of-Care Tests

Women Men
Sample Self-taken vaginal swab (preferred)
OR high vaginal swab		 First-void urine or urethral / penile-meatal NAAT swab
Lab NAAT (gold); wet microscopy if available

Add-ons: Triple STI screen (Chlamydia, Gonorrhoea, HIV ± Syphilis, Hep B/C if high-risk)

4. Treatment – START SAME DAY

Patient Group Regimen Notes
Non-pregnant / non-breastfeeding Metronidazole 400–500 mg PO BD × 7 d
or 2 g single dose		 Avoid alcohol 48 h post-dose
Pregnant + symptomatic Metronidazole 400 mg PO BD × 7 d High-dose single dose avoided
Breastfeeding Same as pregnant Dose after feed, discard milk 12–24 h
HIV co-infection Metronidazole 500 mg PO BD × 7 d

Partners: Treat all sexual partners within 4 weeks simultaneously

5. Follow-Up & Red Flags

  • Abstain from sex until 1 week after treatment finished for patient and partner(s)
  • Test-of-cure only if symptoms persist / recur (≥48 h post-treatment)
  • Persistent symptoms → check adherence + reinfection → GUM referral
  • Pregnant / recurrent / treatment-failure cases → GUM specialist review
