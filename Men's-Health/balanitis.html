NICE CKS – Balanitis Quick Reference Guide

Overview

This page distils the NICE CKS balanitis topic (May 2024) into an at-a-glance algorithm for primary-care clinicians.

4-Step Algorithm

  1. Suspect – Typical symptoms + risk factors.
  2. Assess – History, examination, red-flag check.
  3. Test – Swab / STI screen / HbA1c as indicated.
  4. Treat & Review – Empiric therapy below; refer if recalcitrant or red-flag.

Empiric Management

Probable CauseFirst-Line Therapy
Irritant / non-specificGood hygiene, lukewarm wash, emollient
Dermatitis / allergyHydrocortisone 1 % od ≤ 14 d + imidazole if Candida
CandidalImidazole cream 2–3×/d 2 wk
OR fluconazole 150 mg single dose (severe)
Bacterial – mildMupirocin 2 % topical 2–3×/d 7–10 d
Bacterial – severePhenoxymethylpenicillin 500 mg qds 10 d
Clarithromycin 250 mg bd 7 d if penicillin-allergic
Anaerobic (Gardnerella)Metronidazole 400 mg bd 7 d

Referral Indications

  • Diagnosis uncertain / biopsy needed
  • Recalcitrant or recurrent disease
  • Suspected lichen sclerosus, Zoon’s, neoplasia
  • Penile mass / persistent ulcer → 2-week wait
  • Severe phimosis / paraphimosis

Quick Pearls

  • Retract & gently dry foreskin daily.
  • Avoid soap, bubble bath, latex condoms during flare.
  • Consider circumcision for repeated lichen sclerosus.
  • Screen partners for streptococcal balanitis.
