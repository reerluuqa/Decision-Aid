Screening / Case‑finding

Who to Test

Use NAAT screening proactively in higher‑risk people and with any compatible symptoms.

  • All sexually active women <25 y annually; consider more often with a new partner.
  • Sexual partners of people with confirmed/suspected chlamydia.
  • Anyone worried about a recent exposure — test now; if exposure was within 2 weeks and negative, repeat at 2 weeks.
  • People <25 y treated for chlamydia in the last 3 months; people with ≥2 partners in the past 12 months.
  • All women seeking TOP; all attendees at GUM clinics.
Clinical features

When to Suspect

Women
  • Increased or purulent vaginal discharge; mucopurulent cervicitis; friable cervix.
  • Post‑coital / inter‑menstrual bleeding; deep dyspareunia.
  • Dysuria, pelvic pain/tenderness, cervical motion tenderness.
Men
  • Dysuria; mucoid/mucopurulent urethral discharge; urethral discomfort/urethritis.
  • Epididymo‑orchitis; reactive arthritis.
Other sites / syndromes
  • Rectal: anal discharge, proctitis, anorectal discomfort — often asymptomatic.
  • LGV features: tenesmus, bloody discharge, anorectal pain; consider especially in MSM.
  • Conjunctivitis: typically unilateral low‑grade irritation (may be bilateral).
  • Oropharynx: pharyngitis/sore throat — usually asymptomatic.
Diagnostics

How to Test

Confirm with NAAT; choose the most sensitive specimen for the site.

  • Women: Self‑taken vulvovaginal swab (preferred) or endocervical swab; FCU if preferred (hold urine ≥1 h; collect first ~20 mL).
  • Men: First‑catch urine (preferred); urethral swab alternative (insert 2–4 cm, rotate once).
  • Extra‑genital: Take rectal swabs for symptomatic people; test for LGV if proctitis, and in HIV‑positive MSM with CT detected at any site.
Antibiotic regimen

Treatment (Uncomplicated)

Pregnancy — options
  • Azithromycin: 1 g once, then 500 mg daily × 2 days.
  • Erythromycin: 500 mg QID × 7 days or 500 mg BID × 14 days.
  • Amoxicillin: 500 mg TID × 7 days.

Avoid doxycycline. Discuss with midwife/obstetrician and/or GUM if uncertain.

Context

Special Groups & Referral

  • Safeguarding: If an STI is suspected/diagnosed in a child or young person, consult GUM/paediatrics early and follow local safeguarding policies.
  • Urgent GUM referral for non‑response to 1st‑line therapy or if PID suspected.
  • Offer written information on transmission, treatment, complications, and prevention.
Prevention of reinfection

Partner Management

  • Treat current partner(s) empirically to reduce reinfection and onward transmission.
  • Avoid sexual contact (including oral sex) until patient and partners have completed therapy (or 7 days after azithromycin).
  • Encourage GUM attendance for full STI screen (gonorrhoea, HIV, hepatitis B, syphilis) and partner notification support. With consent, share details to GUM solely for partner notification if attendance is not possible.
Aftercare

Follow‑up

  • Check adherence and symptom resolution; reinforce condoms/regular testing.
  • Test of cure (TOC) is not routine. Indicated in pregnancy, poor adherence, or persistent symptoms.
  • When needed, defer TOC ≥3 weeks after treatment to avoid false positive NAAT.
  • Re‑test all people <25 y at 3–6 months (reinfection check). Consider in >25 y if high risk.
Key prescribing checks

Safety & Interactions — Quick Cues

  • Pregnancy/Breastfeeding: Avoid tetracyclines (doxycycline).
  • QT prolongation: Macrolides (azithro/erythro) — check cardiac history, electrolytes, and QT‑prolonging meds.
  • Fluoroquinolones: Reserve ofloxacin for when other recommended antibiotics are inappropriate; contraindicated in pregnancy/children; counsel on rare but serious adverse effects.
  • Common interactions: Warfarin (monitor INR), digoxin/statins (macrolides), antacids/iron/zinc (separate from doxycycline by 2–3 h), retinoids (avoid with tetracyclines).
Think broader

Differential Diagnosis

Gonorrhoea, BV, candidiasis, trichomoniasis, PID, UTI.
