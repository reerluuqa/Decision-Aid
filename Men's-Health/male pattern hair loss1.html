Male Pattern Hair Loss Clinical Algorithm

Comprehensive diagnostic and management guide for primary care physicians

Last Updated: February 2022

Definition & Pathophysiology

Definition

  • Patterned, progressive hair loss from scalp
  • Front and sides affected initially → progresses to back
  • Most common cause of non-scarring alopecia

Pathophysiology

  • DHT shortens anagen phase
  • Genetic predisposition (polygenic)
  • Miniaturization of hair follicles
  • Normal androgen levels in most cases

Assessment

History

  • Onset & progression rate
  • Family history (strongest risk factor)
  • Medications (antidepressants, anticoagulants)
  • Psychological impact

Examination

  • Pattern: Bitemporal recession + vertex thinning
  • Hamilton-Norwood Scale (Grade 1-7)
  • Hair pull test: Positive if >3 hairs extracted
  • Scalp inspection (rule out inflammation/scarring)

Investigations

  • Generally not needed for typical presentation
  • Consider if atypical:
  • - Thyroid function tests
  • - FBC, ferritin, vitamin D

Differential Diagnosis

Non-scarring

  • Telogen effluvium (diffuse shedding)
  • Alopecia areata (patchy areas)
  • Traction alopecia (mechanical pulling)
  • Trichotillomania (self-induced)

Scarring

  • Tinea capitis (itchy, scaly)
  • Discoid lupus (scaly plaques)
  • Dermatomyositis (rash + weakness)

Systemic Causes

  • Hypothyroidism
  • Iron deficiency
  • Vitamin D deficiency
  • Drug-induced

Management

Conservative

Reassurance & Counseling
  • Shampooing doesn't worsen loss
  • Natural course explanation
Cosmetic Options
  • Hairpieces & wigs
  • Hair styling techniques

Pharmacological

Topical Minoxidil
  • Effect: 2-4 months (peak at 4 months)
  • Must continue indefinitely
  • Reverses in 3-6 months if stopped
Oral Finasteride 1mg
  • Effect: 3-6 months (peak at 2 years)
  • Counsel on adverse effects
  • Reverses in 6-12 months if stopped

Referral

Consider referral to:
  • Dermatologist: Atypical presentation
  • Endocrinologist: Hormonal disorders
  • Mental health: Significant distress
  • Hair transplant surgeon: Surgical assessment

Prognosis

  • Progressive but rate varies significantly
  • Earlier onset predicts more extensive loss
  • Treatments slow progression but don't achieve complete reversal
  • Younger patients with smaller bald areas respond better
  • Peak hair growth: 4 months (minoxidil), 2 years (finasteride)
