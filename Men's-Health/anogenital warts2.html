Step¬†5

Special Groups

Pregnancy

  • May enlarge; often delay therapy until postpartum (frequent spontaneous resolution within 6¬†weeks postpartum).
  • Avoid topicals (podophyllotoxin, imiquimod, sinecatechins). Prefer cryotherapy or TCA if needed.
  • Low risk of vertical HPV transmission / recurrent respiratory papillomatosis.

Immunocompromised (incl. HIV)

  • Higher burden, lower spontaneous clearance, higher SCC risk, more recurrences.
  • May need longer or combined regimens; consider surgical then adjunct therapy.

Children / Adolescents

  • Assess for non‚Äësexual vs sexual transmission context; safeguarding as indicated; refer to paediatrics/sexual health.

Consider

Key Differentials

  • Pearly penile papules (benign, corona/sulcus rings).
  • Molluscum contagiosum (umbilicated papules).
  • Condylomata lata (secondary syphilis; moist plaques + systemic features).

  • Other benign: skin tags, seborrhoeic keratoses, epidermoid cysts, hidradenoma papilliferum, sebaceous glands (foreskin/vulva).
