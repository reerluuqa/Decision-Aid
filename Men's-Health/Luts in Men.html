LUTS in Men

Quick Reference Algorithm for Primary Care
Assessment Management Red Flags Quality Standards

Assessment

History

  • Symptom type: Storage (urgency, frequency, nocturia, incontinence), Voiding (hesitancy, weak stream, straining), Post-micturition (dribble, incomplete emptying)
  • Severity and impact on quality of life
  • Possible underlying causes (comorbidities, medications)
  • Ask about ketamine use (especially in younger patients)

Examination

  • Abdominal: Check for distended bladder
  • External genitalia: Look for phimosis, meatal stenosis
  • Digital rectal examination: Assess prostate (size, symmetry, firmness)
  • Neurological: Perineum and lower limbs if indicated

Investigations

  • Urine dipstick (blood, glucose, protein, leucocytes, nitrites)
  • Consider serum creatinine/eGFR if renal impairment suspected
  • Consider PSA testing if:
    • Symptoms suggest bladder outlet obstruction
    • Abnormal DRE
    • Patient concerned about prostate cancer

Assessment Tools

  • Urinary frequency-volume chart (for bothersome symptoms)
  • IPSS questionnaire (for baseline symptoms)

Management by Symptom Type

Voiding Symptoms

  1. Mild symptoms (IPSS 1-7):
    • Reassurance and lifestyle advice
    • Monitor
  2. Moderate-severe symptoms (IPSS 8-35):
    • First-line: Alpha-blocker (e.g., tamsulosin)
    • If inadequate response: Consider combination therapy (alpha-blocker + antimuscarinic)
    • If persistent symptoms: Refer to specialist

Storage Symptoms

  1. Lifestyle advice:
    • Fluid management
    • Bladder training
    • Caffeine/alcohol reduction
  2. Pharmacological:
    • First-line: Antimuscarinic (e.g., oxybutynin, solifenacin)
    • Alternative: Mirabegron (if antimuscarinics unsuitable)
    • New option: Vibegron (if antimuscarinics unsuitable, ineffective, or not tolerated)

Post-micturition Dribble

  • Advice on urethral milking technique
  • Reassurance if no other symptoms

Urinary Retention

  1. Acute retention:
    • Emergency catheterization
    • Refer to urology
  2. Chronic retention:
    • Refer to urology for assessment

Red Flags (Refer Urgently)

Refer for suspected urological cancer if:

  • Unexplained hematuria
  • Lower back pain
  • Bone pain
  • Weight loss
  • Hard/irregular prostate on DRE

Refer for suspected urological infection if:

  • Fever
  • Loin pain
  • Abnormal urine dipstick

Refer for suspected cauda equina syndrome if:

  • Bilateral sciatica
  • Saddle anesthesia
  • Urinary/bowel dysfunction
  • Neurological deficit in legs

Refer for complicated LUTS if:

  • Recurrent UTI
  • Renal impairment
  • Suspected neurological cause

Quality Standards

Full physical examination including DRE for initial assessment
Urinary frequency-volume chart for bothersome symptoms
Lifestyle advice for non-bothersome symptoms
Offer containment products for incontinence
Information on urethral milking for post-micturition dribble
Regular medication review for those on drug treatment
Consider flow rate and post-void residual measurement in specialist assessment
Surgery only for severe voiding symptoms or failed conservative management
