Haematospermia – One-Page Decision Algorithm

Last revised March 2022 | NHS CKS Condensed

1. Immediate Triage

  • Age ≥ 40 y OR red flags → Refer Urology ≤ 2 weeks (cancer pathway)
  • Age < 40 y, single episode, no red flags → Reassure & watch

2. Red Flags (same-day referral if any)

  • Recurrent / persistent haematospermia
  • Elevated PSA or suspicious DRE
  • Painless visible haematuria
  • Testicular mass / swelling
  • Systemic symptoms (weight-loss, night sweats)
  • Recent GU-procedure (expect resolution ≤ 3–4 weeks)

3. Rapid History & Exam

Key PointAction
Duration & frequencyLonger/recurrent → higher risk
ColourBright red = fresh bleed
LUTS / pain / dischargeCheck UTI, STI, prostatitis
Coagulopathy / anticoagulantsReview meds / bleeding history
GU trauma / recent proceduresNote iatrogenic cause

Physical: BP, temp, abdomen, testes, prostate (DRE if ≥ 40 y or symptoms)

4. Bedside & First-Line Tests

  • Urinalysis + MSU C&S (all patients)
  • PSA (all ≥ 40 y or high-risk)
  • STI screen if risk factors → refer GUM
  • FBC / coagulation / LFT / eGFR if systemic signs
  • Scrotal USS if testicular symptoms

5. Management Pathways

ScenarioAction
Single episode, < 40 y, no cause foundReassure, advise return if recurs
UTI suspectedEmpiric antibiotics guided by culture
STI suspectedRefer GUM for contact tracing
HypertensionTreat BP
ProstatitisSee CKS prostatitis topics
Any red flag or > 40 y with no causeDirect referral to urology ≤ 2 weeks

6. Patient Information

  • Most cases are benign & self-limiting
  • Fertility generally unaffected (except untreated STI)
  • Leaflets: NHS “Blood in semen”, BAUS website
