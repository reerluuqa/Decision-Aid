Pubic Lice Quick-Reference Algorithm

Primary care – CKS Feb 2023 update

1. Suspect

  • Symptom: Genital / night itching
  • Risk: Sexual contact, shared clothing/bedding
  • Differentials: Scabies, folliculitis, seborrhoeic dermatitis, dermatophytes

2. Examine

  • Look for: Live lice (2 mm, crab-shaped) or yellow-white nits
  • Signs: Blue macules, red papules, excoriation, rust-coloured faeces
  • Areas: Pubic, peri-anal, axillary, chest, eyelashes, beard, scalp margins
  • Aids: Dermoscope, magnifier, fine-toothed comb

3. Test & Trace

  • Sexual acquisition? → Refer to GUM for partner tracing (± 3 months) & STI screen
  • Children: Consider sexual abuse; non-sexual spread possible

4. Treat Same day

Body / pubic Permethrin 5 % cream
OR malathion 0.5 % aqueous		 Whole body (pubic → knees, trunk, beard). 8–12 h overnight, wash off Day 7
Eyelashes Simple eye ointment BP or paraffin ointment BID 8–10 d
OR permethrin 1 % lotion 10 min once		 Keep eyes closed during application, wash off —
<18 yrs, pregnant, breastfeeding Prefer ointment for eyelashes

5. Counsel & Prevent

Patient education
  • Transmission & adherence
  • Itching may persist after successful treatment
Decontamination
  • Wash ≥ 50 °C or seal bag 2 weeks
  • Avoid close contact until partners treated

6. Review

  • Day 7–10: Re-examine
  • Live lice?
    • Technique poor → repeat same drug correctly
    • Technique correct → switch drug class
  • Dead nits ≠ failure; comb out
  • Failure of both drugs / atypical → GUM referral

Red Flags for Referral

Failure of both insecticides Diagnostic doubt Atypical presentation Severe secondary infection / chronic skin changes
