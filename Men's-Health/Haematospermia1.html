Haematospermia – 1-Page Quick Reference

1At Presentation

  • Age ≥ 12 years (male)
  • Confirm blood in ejaculate (bright red = fresh; brown = old)

2Red Flags → 2-week Urology Referral

  • Age ≥ 40 yrs
  • Recurrent / persistent haematospermia
  • Painless visible haematuria
  • Elevated PSA or suspicious DRE
  • Testicular lump / swelling
  • Signs of systemic malignancy (lymphadenopathy, bruising)

3Focused History & Examination

  • Duration / frequency (single vs recurrent)
  • Associated symptoms: dysuria/frequency (UTI), perineal pain (prostatitis), urethral discharge (STI)
  • Risk factors: recent urological procedure, anticoagulants, STI risk, TB/schistosomiasis travel
  • Exam: BP, temperature, abdomen, testes, DRE (≥ 40 yrs or red flags), skin bruising

4Bedside Tests

  • Urinalysis & MSU culture
  • PSA (all ≥ 40 yrs or red flags)
  • FBC, clotting, U&E, LFT (if systemic illness)

5Management Pathway

Scenario Action
Single episode, age < 40, no red flags Reassure; return if recurs
Suspected STI Refer to GUM clinic
Suspected UTI Antibiotics per culture/local guidelines
Hypertension Treat per NICE HTN guidance
Prostatitis suspected Manage per CKS prostatitis topic
Red flags OR age ≥ 40 no cause 2-week wait urology referral

6Patient Reassurance & Leaflets

  • Fertility: Most causes do not affect fertility (except STIs)
  • Post-procedure: Bleeding settles within 3–4 weeks
