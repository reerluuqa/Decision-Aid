NICE CKS – Male Urethritis Quick Guide

May 2024 • One-page modern summary for front-line clinicians

1. Rapid Screen

  • Target: males ≥ 16 y with muco-/purulent discharge ± dysuria/itch
  • Exclude: confirmed chlamydia, gonorrhoea, trichomoniasis, herpes, UTI (see separate CKS)

2. 60-Second Assessment

A
History: last sex < 3 mo, partners, condoms, STI history, trauma/irritants
B
Exam: check underwear, retract foreskin, milk urethra, ± prostate
C
Tests: 1st-void urine NAAT (chlamydia + gonorrhoea); add Mycoplasma genitalium, HIV, syphilis as indicated

3. Decide – Refer vs Treat In-House

Patient able/willing to attend GUM?

4. Empiric Treatment (Primary Care)

Scenario1st-lineAlternatives
Non-gonococcal urethritis Doxycycline 100 mg bid × 7 d Azithromycin 1 g day 1 → 500 mg od × 2 d
Ofloxacin 200 mg bid × 7 d*
Gonorrhoea suspected Follow Gonorrhoea CKS
Trichomonas suspected Add Metronidazole 400 mg bid × 5 d
*Fluoroquinolones only if other agents inappropriate (MHRA warning).

5. Follow-Up & Treatment Failure

Review at 1–2 w Symptoms resolved? Partner treated?
If persistent:
  1. Confirm adherence & no re-infection
  2. Switch regimen (e.g., doxy → azithro + metro, or azithro → moxifloxacin + metro)
  3. Still symptomatic → urgent GUM referral

Red Flags – Immediate GUM

  • Systemic symptoms, severe testicular pain
  • Joint/eye involvement (possible reactive arthritis)
  • Pregnant partner or epididymo-orchitis suspected
