Erectile Dysfunction

Quick Consultation Algorithm  |  Updated Oct 2024 (NICE CKS)

1. Doorway Questions

  • Age ≥ 18 y male? ✅ proceed
  • Priapism (painful erection ≥ 4 h)? → Admit / Urology NOW

2. 30-Second Triage – When to Refer

  • Urology: Young (<40 y) lifelong ED; pelvic/genital trauma; structural abnormality.
  • Endocrinology: Low testosterone or abnormal T/LH/FSH/Prolactin.
  • Cardiology: High cardiac risk (unstable angina, MI <2 w, NYHA IV, uncontrolled HTN/arrhythmia) – STOP sex/PDE-5.
  • Mental health: Severe psychogenic / relationship issues.

3. Rapid Work-up

  • History: Onset, libido, morning erections, drugs, cycling >3 h/wk.
  • Exam: BP, BMI, waist, genitalia ± DRE if LUTS or >50 y.
  • Bloods: HbA1c/FBG, lipids, 9-11 am testosterone (± LH/FSH/Prolactin), PSA/TFT/LFT/U&E as indicated.
  • Risk: Calculate 10-y CVD risk.

4. First-line Management (Low/Intermediate Cardiac Risk)

  1. Lifestyle: Weight loss, exercise, stop smoking, cut alcohol, trial off cycling.
  2. Optimise comorbidities (DM, HTN, lipids).
  3. PDE-5 Inhibitor choice:
    DrugOnsetDurationFood delayNHS Rx
    Sildenafil 50 mg1 h4-5 h~60 minUnrestricted
    Tadalafil 10 mg (or 5 mg daily)30 min36 hNoneSLS only*
    Vardenafil 10 mg25-60 min4-5 h~60 minSLS only*
    Avanafil 100 mg15-30 min6 h~75 minSLS only*
    *SLS criteria: diabetes, MS, Parkinson’s, prostate cancer, severe pelvic injury/surgery, dialysis/transplant, pre-1998 NHS ED Rx.
  4. Counsel: Needs sexual stimulation; try max tolerated dose ≥4-8 attempts before switching.
  5. Follow-up: 6-8 wks review.

5. If Inadequate Response

  • Re-check correct use, increase to max dose.
  • Sequential trial of ≥2 PDE-5 inhibitors.
  • Still failing → Urology referral (VED, intracavernosal injections, MUSE, prosthesis).
