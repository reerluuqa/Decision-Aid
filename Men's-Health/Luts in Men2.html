LUTS in Men – Quick Reference Card

1. Triage & Red-Flags

Red-flag symptom/signImmediate action
Visible haematuria, weight loss, hard/irregular prostate2-week cancer referral
Acute painful retention / palpable bladderAdmit & catheterise
Bilateral sciatica, saddle numbnessCauda equina – same-day MRI
Fever, loin pain, positive dipstickTreat infection (see UTI topic)

2. 90-Second Assessment

  • History: storage vs voiding vs dribble, bother score, meds, fluid/caffeine, ketamine
  • Exam: abdomen (bladder), DRE, external genitalia, focused neuro
  • Chart: 3-day frequency-volume chart if bothersome LUTS

3. Bedside Investigations

Urine dipstickAll patients
Creatinine / eGFRIf palpable bladder, nocturnal enuresis, recurrent UTI, stones
PSA discussionObstructive symptoms, abnormal DRE, patient concern
