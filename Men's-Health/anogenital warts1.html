Anogenital Warts – 1-Page Ref Card

Last updated May 2024 – BASHH 2024

Goal in 10 s

Benign HPV 6/11 lesions → remove warts, screen for STIs, counsel, refer if red-flags.

3-Step Algorithm

  1. Spot it – Clinical Dx: Cauliflower-like lesions anywhere anogenital; rule out mimics.
  2. Triage – Refer or Treat:
    • Refer if uncertain dx, intra-meatal/anal, pregnancy, immunosuppressed, child <13 y.
    • Treat in primary care only if dx certain, patient declines referral, and you can screen STIs.
  3. Treat – Choice Tree:
    Lesion typeFirst-line
    Soft / non-keratinisedPodophyllotoxin 0.5 % soln/cream
    Keratinised or mixedImiquimod 5 % cream OR cryotherapy
    Large / resistantTCA 80-90 % OR excision
    PregnancyCryotherapy / TCA (avoid topicals)

Dosing Cheat-Sheet

  • Podophyllotoxin: 0.5 % soln/cream BID × 3 d, stop 4 d, repeat weekly × 4-5 (max area 4 cm²).
  • Imiquimod: 3×/wk at bedtime, wash off 6–10 h later, up to 16 wks.
  • Sinecatechins: 3×/day until clear ≤ 16 wks (max 250 mg/dose).

Red-Flags = Instant Referral

Bleeding / ulcerated lesions, haematuria, distorted urine stream, recurrent perianal warts, any child <13 y (abuse screen).

30-Second Counsel

  • 30 % clear spontaneously in 6 m.
  • Treatments take 1–6 m; high failure/relapse rates.
  • Use condoms, stop smoking to improve response.
  • Partners may benefit from check-up.
  • No change to cervical smear schedule.

Follow-Up

Review after each treatment course. < 50 % response at 4–5 wks (8–12 wks for imiquimod) → change plan. Immunocompromised need closer watch.
