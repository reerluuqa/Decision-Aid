Common Cold Quick Reference

Revised Feb 2022
URGENT: Admit / Refer Immediately

Signs of Serious Complication / Alternative Diagnosis

  • Upper Airway Obstruction: Stridor, drooling, inability to swallow (Suspect Epiglottitis/Abscess).
  • Meningism: Stiff neck, photophobia, bulging fontanelle (infants), non-blanching rash.
  • Severe Respiratory Distress: Grunting, chest indrawing, tachypnea, O2 sat < 92%.
  • Risk Groups: Immunocompromised, significant comorbidities (e.g., CF, heart failure).

High-Risk Fever in Children

  • Age < 3 months: Fever ≥ 38°C (High Risk).
  • Age 3–6 months: Fever ≥ 39°C (Intermediate Risk).
ACTION: Arrange Emergency Admission

Clinical Diagnosis

Adults & Older Children
Onset Rapid (1-2 days)
Key Symptoms Sore throat (first), Nasal discharge/stuffiness, Sneezing, Cough (often develops later).
Fever Unusual / Low grade if present.
Exam Exclude complications (Chest, Ears). Investigations rarely needed.
Younger Children & Infants
Onset Rapid.
Key Symptoms Nasal congestion (may affect feeding), Rhinorrhoea, Cough, Fever (Common).
Temp 38–39°C common in preschool children.
Exam Check Fontanelle (bulging?), Ears (OM), Throat (Strep?). No swabs needed.
Key Differential Diagnoses

Consider these if symptoms deviate from typical cold pattern:

Influenza Prominent myalgia/fever, sudden onset.
COVID-19 Loss of smell/taste, continuous cough, shortness of breath.
Strep Pharyngitis Severe sore throat, absence of cough/sneeze.
Allergic Rhinitis Nasal symptoms > 2 weeks, no fever, itchiness.
Whooping Cough Severe coughing fits, "whoop" sound.

Management Plan

Symptom Relief (Safe)
  • Reassurance: Self-limiting (7 days adult, 14 days child). Cough may persist 3 weeks.
  • Fluids & Food: Adequate fluids. No special diet needed; don't force feed children.
  • Rest: Normal activity does not prolong illness.

Pharmacological

  • Paracetamol / Ibuprofen: For fever, headache, or myalgia.
    Kids <5yrs: Only if distressed. Do NOT give simultaneously.
  • Vapour Rubs: Chest/Back application (avoid nostrils).
  • Saline Drops: For infant congestion/feeding issues.
Important Warnings

What to AVOID

  • Antibiotics: Ineffective for common cold. Adverse effects likely.
  • Antihistamines: Generally ineffective for colds.
  • OTC Cough & Cold Meds:
    Do NOT use in children under 6 years (MHRA advice).

Hygiene

  • Wash hands frequently.
  • Avoid sharing towels/toys.

Source: NICE Clinical Knowledge Summaries (CKS) - Common Cold. Last revised February 2022.

Target Audience: Healthcare professionals within the NHS.
