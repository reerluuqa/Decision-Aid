Primary Care Lab Guide

Quick reference for Containers, Technique & Rejection Criteria

Urine (MC&S) Red Top Monovette (Boric Acid)

How to Take

  • Collect "Clean Catch" or MSU.
  • Fill the Red Top Monovette to the fill line (10ml).
    • [cite_start]
  • Screening: Only send if Dipstick positive for Leukocytes or Nitrites (unless pregnant, >65, or child). [cite: 1545]

Important

  • Microscopy is not routine. [cite_start]If urgent microscopy is needed, call the lab (0161 276 8788) before sending. [cite: 1537, 1555]
REJECTION CRITERIA
    [cite_start]
  • White top universal containers are REJECTED (unless paediatric <3 months). [cite: 1539]
  • Under-filled samples (boric acid concentration becomes toxic to bacteria).
    • [cite_start]
  • Samples >48 hours old. [cite: 1553]
Wound / Skin / Pus Liquid eSwab (Pink/White Cap)

How to Take

    [cite_start]
  • Clean debris/slough with saline before swabbing. [cite: 1584]
    • [cite_start]
  • Sample the deepest part of the wound or advancing edge. [cite: 531]
  • Place swab into the liquid tube and snap off at the break point.
    • [cite_start]
  • Pus is better: If aspirate is available, send in a sterile universal container. [cite: 1582]
REJECTION CRITERIA
    [cite_start]
  • Dry swabs (no liquid). [cite: 1587]
    • [cite_start]
  • Liquid discarded (must have >1ml liquid remaining). [cite: 1587]
    • [cite_start]
  • Superficial/Dry crusted areas (low yield). [cite: 1583]
MRSA Screening Double eSwab (Pink + White)

How to Take

  • Use the Double eSwab pack.
  • Step 1 (Pink Swab): Swab Groin/Perineum. [cite_start]Place in liquid, swirl for 5 seconds, then DISCARD THE SWAB into bin. [cite: 1244]
  • Step 2 (White Swab): Swab Nose (both nostrils). [cite_start]Place in the SAME liquid tube, snap off, and cap. [cite: 1257]
  • Send the tube with the white swab inside.
REJECTION CRITERIA
    [cite_start]
  • Sending the pink swab to the lab (it should be discarded). [cite: 1258]
  • No liquid in tube.
Faeces (Stool) Blue/Green Top Pot (with spoon)

How to Take

    [cite_start]
  • Fill pot to 1/3 or use spoon to collect walnut size (solid) or 2ml (liquid). [cite: 933]
  • Do not mix with urine or toilet water.
    • [cite_start]
  • History: State travel history (triggers Vibrio/Giardia testing) or food poisoning suspicion. [cite: 931]

Parasites

    [cite_start]
  • Routine culture covers Salmonella, Shigella, Campylobacter, E.coli O157, Crypto, Giardia. [cite: 924, 1360]
    • [cite_start]
  • For other parasites (OCP), send 3 samples over 10 days. [cite: 1361]
REJECTION CRITERIA
  • C. difficile: Formed (solid) stool is REJECTED. [cite_start]Must be liquid (Bristol stool chart 5-7). [cite: 724]
    • [cite_start]
  • Samples collected within 24hrs of a previous sample. [cite: 929]
  • Leaking containers.
Chlamydia / Gonorrhoea Roche Cobas PCR Tube (Yellow)

How to Take

Urine (Male/Female):

  • First catch urine (initial stream).
    • [cite_start]
  • Fill Cobas tube strictly between the two black lines. [cite: 682]

Swabs (Vaginal/Throat/Rectal):

    [cite_start]
  • Use Woven swab from dual kit. [cite: 686]
  • Place ONE swab in Cobas tube. Break off shaft.
REJECTION CRITERIA
    [cite_start]
  • Under/Over-filled urine: Must be between black lines. [cite: 683]
    • [cite_start]
  • Two swabs in one tube. [cite: 690]
    • [cite_start]
  • Using the "flocked" swab (skinny one) for vaginal samples (Flocked is for endocervix only). [cite: 689]
  • White top universal urine container (Lab will not process for PCR).
Sputum & Viral Swabs Sterile Universal (Sputum) Green VTM (Viral)

Viral (Flu/COVID/RSV)

  • Use Green VTM (Virus Transport Medium) tube.
  • Nose & Throat swab: Throat first, then Nose. [cite_start]Place both in same tube. [cite: 761]

Bacterial Sputum

  • Early morning sample is best.
  • Patient must rinse mouth, then cough deeply.
    • [cite_start]
  • Container: Sterile Universal (Screw cap). [cite: 1411]
REJECTION CRITERIA
  • Saliva: Sputum must be purulent/mucopurulent. [cite_start]Saliva is rejected. [cite: 1407]
    • [cite_start]
  • Trap tubing attached / Push-top lids (Leak hazard). [cite: 1410]
  • Dry swabs for viral PCR.
General Rejection Rules Applies to All
[cite_start]

Labelling [cite: 366, 458]

  • 4 Identifiers Mandatory: Forename, Surname, DOB, NHS/District Number.
  • Sample label must match Request form.
    • [cite_start]
  • "Pre-labelling": Do not label empty pots before seeing patient. [cite: 369]

Transport

    [cite_start]
  • One Bag = One Patient: Do not mix samples from different patients in one bag. [cite: 480]
    • [cite_start]
  • High Risk (HIV/TB/Hep): Must have "Danger of Infection" sticker. [cite: 446]
