CRITICAL ALERTS & RED FLAGS

Immediate action required for these complications. Mortality risk increases if untreated.

Intestinal Obstruction

  • Severe colicky abdominal pain
  • Nausea & Vomiting
  • Distension
  • Absent bowel sounds
ACTION: Piperazine (Paralysis) OR Surgery if no improvement in 24h.

Hepatobiliary / Pancreatic

  • Biliary Colic / Cholangitis
  • Acute Pancreatitis
  • RUQ Tenderness & Jaundice
  • Fever
ACTION: ERCP (Endoscopic Removal) + Broad-spectrum Antibiotics.

Perforation / Peritonitis

  • Rebound tenderness
  • Hypotension
  • Tachypnoea
  • Signs of abdominal sepsis
ACTION: Immediate Surgical Laparotomy.

Overview & Theory

Key Facts

  • Causative Agent: Ascaris lumbricoides (Largest nematode).
  • Transmission: Ingestion of eggs from soil/contaminated food (fecal-oral).
  • At Risk: Children 5-15y, Asia/Africa/Latin America, poor sanitation.
  • Complications: Malnutrition, growth retardation (chronic), obstruction (acute).

Diagnosis

First-Line: Stool Microscopy
Investigation When to Order Key Findings
Stool Microscopy Standard for all suspected cases. Returning travelers, immigrants, GI symptoms. Positive Fertilised eggs (pebbled, yellow-brown, 60-70Œºm).
Note: False negative in single-sex infection or early phase.
Chest X-Ray New asthma, fever, cough (Loeffler's syndrome) after travel. Patchy pulmonary infiltrates (migratory phase).
Abdominal X-Ray Suspected obstruction (pain, vomiting, distension). Dilated loops, air-fluid levels, linear images of worms.
Ultrasound / CT Obstruction or biliary pain (not fully cleared by X-ray). Mass of worms in tract, single worm in biliary tree.
ERCP Cholangitis, biliary colic, pancreatitis (Therapeutic & Diagnostic). Direct visualization of worms in ducts (allows removal).

Symptomatic Clues

Lungs: Cough, wheezing, dyspnoea, fever (Loffler's).
Gut: Abdominal pain, nausea, anorexia.
Children: Growth retardation, malnutrition.

Management & Treatment

Select the clinical presentation to see the algorithm.

Uncomplicated

Asymptomatic or mild GI symptoms. No obstruction.

OPTIONS (Choose One)
Albendazole
>2yrs: 400mg PO x1
12mo-2yrs: 200mg PO x1
Mebendazole
>2yrs: 500mg PO x1 OR 100mg BID x3 days
Ivermectin
>15kg: 150-200 mcg/kg PO x1
Note: Avoid 1st trimester pregnancy. 2nd/3rd trimester: Use Albendazole/Mebendazole.

Intestinal Obstruction

Nausea, vomiting, colicky pain, distension.

1ST LINE (Medical)
Piperazine (Paralyses worms)
Dose: Consult specialist/Local guidelines

+ Supportive Care: NG suction, NPO, IV Hydration, Electrolytes.

IF NO IMPROVEMENT (24h)
Surgery
Laparotomy & manual removal/Enterotomy
  • Suspected peritonitis
  • Bowel strangulation
  • Complete obstruction
  • Free air on imaging

Hepatobiliary / Pancreas

Biliary colic, cholangitis, pancreatitis.

PROCEDURE
ERCP
Endoscopic removal of worms.
ADJUNCTS
  • Analgesia: Morphine for colic.
  • Antibiotics: Broad-spectrum (if secondary bacterial infection/sepsis suspected).

Loffler's Syndrome (Migratory Phase)

Usually supportive only. Anthelmintics do not kill migrating larvae. Use bronchodilators, corticosteroids, antihistamines. Re-check stool 2-3 weeks later to treat adult worms once they reach the gut.
