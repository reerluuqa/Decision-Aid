Balanitis: Quick-Scan Reference

Based on NICE CKS (May 2024) Ages: 1 month+
Red Flags & Immediate Actions
Urgent Referral / Specialist Review Required:
  • Penile intraepithelial neoplasia suspected (Erythroplasia of Queyrat, Bowen's disease).
  • Suspected penile cancer (persistent ulcer, nodule, mass).
  • Paraphimosis (retracted foreskin cannot be returned) - Urological Emergency.
  • Urinary retention or significant meatal stenosis.
  • Severe systemic infection (fever, lymphadenopathy) unresponsive to oral antibiotics.

Diagnostic Uncertainty

Refer if diagnosis is unclear or persistent despite treatment (e.g., possible Lichen Sclerosus, Psoriasis).

Sexual Health Context

Refer to GUM clinic if STI is strongly suspected (ulcers, multiple partners, purulent discharge) or if primary care management fails.

Assessment & Diagnosis

History Taking

Hygiene & Irritants Ask about soaps, bubble baths, wipes, latex condoms, or over-washing. Mechanical irritation (sex, manipulation).
Medical History Diabetes risk (HbA1c if severe/persistent), HIV/Immunosuppression, Atopy (Eczema).
Sexual History New partners? STI symptoms? Partner symptoms?

Examination & Differential

Sign / Type Common Causes
Infection Candida (Blotchy red, sparing meatus), Streptococcus (Painful, purulent), Anaerobes (Foul smell).
Dermatological Contact Dermatitis (Irritant/Allergic), Zoon's (Orange/red glossy), Lichen Sclerosus (White atrophic patches).
STI Herpes (Vesicles/Ulcers), Gonorrhoea (Discharge), Syphilis (Erosive).
Investigations: Swab if severe/uncertain (Candida, Bacterial). Screen for STIs if high risk. HbA1c if persistent/severe.
Management Protocols

First Line: Hygiene & Advice

  • Wash daily with lukewarm water.
  • Keep foreskin retracted until dry (if retractable).
  • Avoid: Soap, bubble bath, baby wipes.
  • Use emollient as soap substitute.
  • Frequent nappy changes (infants).

Suspected Non-specific Dermatitis

Prescription
  • Hydrocortisone 1% cream/ointment OD (once daily) for up to 14 days.
  • Imidazole cream (e.g., Clotrimazole) until symptoms settle or 14 days.

Specific Infections

Candida (Confirmed/Suspected)

Imidazole cream (frequency per prep) up to 14 days.

Add Hydrocortisone 1% if inflamed/itchy.

Bacterial Infection

Mild: Mupirocin 2% ointment 2-3 times daily (7-10 days).

Severe: Oral Phenoxymethylpenicillin for 10 days.

If Penicillin Allergy: Clarithromycin 7 days.

Review: If symptoms severe or not improving.

Hygiene & General Care

  • Avoid irritants (soaps, perfumes, spermicides).
  • Keep area clean and dry.
  • Use emollient as soap substitute.

Pharmacological Management

Topical Steroid (for inflammation/dermatitis)

Hydrocortisone 1% cream/ointment OD for up to 14 days.

Antifungal (for Candida)

Imidazole cream (e.g., Clotrimazole) until settle or 14 days.

Infection & Screening

Severe Bacterial Infection

Oral: Phenoxymethylpenicillin for 10 days.

Topical (Mild): Mupirocin 2% ointment.

STI Screening: If high risk or signs of STI (ulcers, discharge, lymphadenopathy), arrange swabs and refer to GUM/Sexual Health.

Specialist Causes

  • Lichen Sclerosus: Potent topical steroid (Ultra-potent) - usually secondary care initiation.
  • Zoon's: Circumcision usually curative.
