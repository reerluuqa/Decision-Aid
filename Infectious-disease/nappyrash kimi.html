üìãRisk Factors

  • Infrequent nappy changes
  • Non-absorbent/non-breathable nappies
  • Fragranced/alcohol-based wipes
  • Diarrhoea
  • Oral antibiotics
  • Underlying skin conditions (eczema)
  • Age 9-12 months (peak prevalence)
  • No nappy-free time

üîÑTreatment Failure & Referral

Causes of Treatment Failure

  • Non-adherence to regimen
  • Secondary infection (candidal/bacterial)
  • Alternative diagnosis
  • Immunocompromised host

When to Refer

üè•Paediatric Dermatology Referral

üìäPrognosis

Typical Course

  • Acute cases: 2-4 days to resolve
  • Candidal infection: 10-21 days
  • Most cases don't require medical review

Key Points

  • 25% of infants in first 4 weeks
  • Up to 100% at some point in infancy
  • Decreased incidence with modern nappies
