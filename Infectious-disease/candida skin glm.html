Candida - Skin

CKS NICE Reference
Last Revised
April 2025

Immediate Action Required

Admit to hospital if systemic candidiasis is suspected (e.g., peritonitis, meningitis) or if the patient is severely immunocompromised with signs of sepsis.

1. Assessment

  • Is the infection localized and patient not significantly immunocompromised?
  • If YES → Proceed to Topical Treatment.
  • If NO (Widespread or Immunocompromised) → Consider Oral Fluconazole.

2. First-Line: Topical

Adults:
Imidazole (Clotrimazole, Econazole, Miconazole, Ketoconazole) or Terbinafine
Children:
Clotrimazole Econazole Miconazole

Continue for at least 1-2 weeks after clinical healing.

3. Inflamed/Itchy?

If inflammation/itch is severe, add a mildly potent corticosteroid:

Hydrocortisone 1%
  • Use 1-2 times daily for max 7 days.
  • Review after 7 days: Stop if resolved, continue 7 more if improved, Stop if no response.

4. Systemic / Failed Topical

Indication: Widespread infection, immunocompromised, or topical failure.

Adults (16+):
Fluconazole 50mg once daily for 2 weeks.
  • If resolved: Stop.
  • If not resolved: Extend 2 weeks OR seek specialist advice.
  • Children (<16): Seek specialist advice.

Key Clinical Features

Appearance: Variable. Usually sore and itchy.
Texture: Scales may accumulate, producing a white-yellow, curd-like substance.
Intertrigo (Skin folds): Red, moist skin. Look for fringed, irregular edges and satellite lesions (pustules).
Web spaces: Marked maceration with thick, crusty layer.

Differential Diagnosis

  • Atopic Eczema: Chronic, relapsing, usually flexural but more dry/scaly.
  • Dermatophyte infections: More dry/scaly, prominent border, NO satellite lesions.
  • Erythrasma: Red-brown patches (Corynebacterium minutissimum).
  • Flexural Psoriasis: 'Glazed' appearance, fissuring in fold depth, sharply demarcated edges.

When to Swab/Culture

Not routine. Swab if:

  • Suspected secondary bacterial infection.
  • Patient is immunocompromised.
  • Diagnosis is uncertain.

Topical Antifungals Safety

Drug Cautions & Interactions
Clotrimazole Interaction Increases Tacrolimus levels.
Latex Damages condoms/diaphragms.
Miconazole WARFARIN Enhances anticoagulant effect. Monitor INR.
Latex Damages condoms/diaphragms.
Econazole Anticoagulants Monitor effect (rare but manufacturer advises caution).
Latex Damages condoms/diaphragms.
Terbinafine Minimal systemic absorption. No known interactions.

Oral Fluconazole (50mg)

DO NOT PRESCRIBE:

  • Pregnant women (Washout ~1 week before conception).
  • Acute porphyria.
  • Concurrent drugs with significant interactions (check BNF).

CAUTION:

  • Cardiac: Risk of QT prolongation (e.g., cardiomyopathy, arrhythmias, hypokalaemia).
  • Hepatic: Risk of necrosis. Discontinue if abdominal pain/jaundice.
  • Renal: Halve dose if eGFR < 50 mL/min.

Patient Advice

  • Hygiene: Wash skin regularly with a soap substitute (e.g., emulsifying ointment).
  • Drying: Ensure skin is dried thoroughly, especially in skin folds.
  • Clothing: Avoid occlusion. Wear loose, breathable clothing. Change pads/nappies frequently.
  • Weight: If obese, advise weight loss to reduce friction.
