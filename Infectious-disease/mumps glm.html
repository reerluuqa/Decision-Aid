Mumps Reference

CKS Dec 2023
Based on NICE CKS
Last Revised: Dec 2023

Immediate Admission Required

If patient presents with signs of Encephalitis or Meningitis.

Neurological Red Flags

  • Altered consciousness / Loss of consciousness
  • Focal neurological signs
  • Seizures
  • Indicates: Mumps Encephalitis (Mortality 1–5%).

Meningitis & Fertility

Mumps Meningitis Symptoms:

Severe headache, neck ache, high fever, lethargy, vomiting.

Note: May occur without parotitis. Admit if in doubt.

Fertility Issues (Post-Epididymo-orchitis):

If abnormal semen analysis or persistent fertility issues.

Clinical Presentation

Mumps is an acute infectious disease caused by a paramyxovirus.

  • Parotitis (95% of cases): Usually bilateral. One side first (max 2-3 days), other follows.
  • Ear Lobe Sign: Ear lobe deflected upward/outward; angle of mandible obscured.
  • Prodrome: Low-grade fever, headache, malaise, muscle ache (1 day before swelling).
  • Tenderness: Gland is tender to touch.
Incubation: 14–18 days (range 12–25).
Infectious: 1–2 days before to 9 days after onset.

Assessment & Differential

Consider likelihood if:

Not Fully Immunized Age > 18 years Contact History

Note: Mumps is unlikely in infants <1 year (passive immunity) or previously infected.

Differential Diagnosis:

  • Other Viral: EBV, Parainfluenza, Adenovirus, HIV.
  • Bacterial: Staphylococcus aureus (Acute suppurative parotitis).
  • Obstruction: Salivary stones.
  • Autoimmune: Sjogren's, Sarcoidosis.

Potential Complications (Incidence)

Epididymo-orchitis (Men)
~25% of post-pubertal men.

Usually unilateral. Rarely causes infertility unless bilateral.

Aseptic Meningitis
Up to 25% (3x more common in men).

Usually benign. Complete recovery.

Encephalitis
~0.1%

Serious. Mortality 1-5%.

Pancreatitis
~4%

Usually mild/transient.

Management of Cases

Mumps is a Notifiable Disease.

  • Notify Local HPT: Do not wait for lab confirmation. HPT will arrange saliva swab (IgM).
  • Advice: Rest, fluids, warm/cold packs for discomfort.
  • Analgesia: Paracetamol or Ibuprofen. Avoid Aspirin <16yrs.
Exclusion Advice:
Stay off school or work for 5 days after initial development of parotitis.

Management of Contacts

Manage anyone who has been in the same room for 15+ mins or face-to-face contact.

  • Offer MMR Vaccine: If not fully immunized (less than 2 doses). Offer as soon as convenient.
  • Surveillance: Advise seeking medical advice if symptoms develop.

What NOT to do:

  • Do not give Human Normal Immunoglobulin (HNIG).
  • Do not give antibiotics.
  • Do not give corticosteroids (routine).

Managing Epididymo-orchitis

Symptomatic Relief:
  • Bed rest
  • Scrotal support
  • Warm/Cold packs
  • Analgesia
Prognosis & Fertility:

Symptoms usually resolve in 2 weeks. Sub-fertility is possible but rare unless bilateral.

If bilateral/severe: Offer semen analysis at least 3 months after resolution.

Pregnant Women

Management of Suspected Case:

  • Manage the same as otherwise healthy people (supportive care).
Vaccination Contraindication:

The combined MMR vaccine is a live vaccine and is contraindicated in pregnancy.

Management of Exposure (Contact):

  • No post-exposure prophylaxis available.
  • Advise to seek medical advice if symptoms develop.

Immunocompromised

Management of Suspected Case:

  • Manage the same as otherwise healthy people.

Management of Exposure (Contact):

  • Do NOT routinely give MMR.
  • May be considered in people with HIV with or without moderate (but not severe) immunosuppression. Follow local protocols.
  • Seek specialist advice.
