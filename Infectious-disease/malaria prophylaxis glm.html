Malaria Prophylaxis

NICE CKS Quick Reference Tool
Source: UKHSA / NaTHNaC / CDC
Last Revised: Jan 2024
Drug Availability Update: Proguanil has been discontinued in the UK (June 2023). Do not prescribe.

RED FLAG: Immediate Action

Malaria can progress from mild to life-threatening within hours.

  • If fever develops during travel or up to 1 year after return: Seek urgent medical attention.
  • Request an urgent blood test for malaria (do not wait).
  • Children & Pregnant Women: Deteriorate very rapidly. Inform schools/carers about travel history.

Primary Prevention: Bite Avoidance

No regime is 100% effective. Bite prevention is the first line of defense.

SKIN Use DEET 50% insect repellent (safe from age 2 months).
CLOTHING Cover up at dusk/dawn (long sleeves, trousers).
SLEEP Use insecticide-treated nets in unscreened rooms.

Risk Assessment Essentials

  • Itinerary: Rural vs Urban, Season, Altitude.
  • History: Splenectomy, HIV, Epilepsy, Psoriasis, Psych Hx.
  • Drugs: Check interactions (e.g., Amiodarone, Anticonvulsants, Bupropion).
  • Beliefs: Correct "I am immune" misconceptions (VFR - Visiting Friends & Relatives).

Pregnancy & Breastfeeding

High Risk: Increased risk of severe/fatal malaria.

  • Avoid travel to malarious areas if possible.
  • Chemoprophylaxis: Check specific drug contraindications (some are unsafe).
  • Folic Acid: Supplement recommended (5mg daily) if taking proguanil (historically relevant) or general prenatal care.
  • Strict adherence to bite avoidance is critical.

Comorbidities & Contraindications

Condition Clinical Note
Psychiatric Hx Mefloquine may be contraindicated if personal/family history of severe mental health disorder.
Epilepsy Chloroquine lowers seizure threshold; Mefloquine is contraindicated.
Psoriasis Chloroquine/Mefloquine may exacerbate condition.
Renal/Liver Impairment Affects dosage choice. Doxycycline usually safe; adjust others.

Drug Safety Highlights & Warnings

Review these specific alerts before prescribing.

Chloroquine + Macrolides
Potential cardiovascular risk. Avoid combination.
Psychiatric Effects
Mefloquine & Chloroquine: Suicidal behavior and disorders added to adverse effects.
Doxycycline
Caution: Photosensitivity. Advise sun avoidance. Esophageal irritation - take with water.
Atovaquone/Proguanil
Generally well tolerated. Requires daily dosing.

Standby Emergency Treatment

Consider only if visiting a remote area >24 hours from medical care.

  • Not a substitute for rigorous chemoprophylaxis.
  • Ensure patient is educated on how to take it.
  • Seek medical help immediately regardless.
