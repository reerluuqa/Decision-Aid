Transmission
  • Sexual: Vaginal, anal, oral (especially with oral ulcers).
  • Vertical: Pregnancy, childbirth, breastfeeding.
  • Blood: Needles, transfusions (unscreened), occupational sharps.
NOT transmitted by: Casual contact, hugging, sharing utensils, insects.
Prognosis & Prevention
U = U (Undetectable = Untransmittable)

People with undetectable viral load on ART cannot sexually transmit HIV.

Life Expectancy: Near-normal with timely diagnosis and ART adherence.

Primary Care Management
Action Details
Testing 4th generation Ag/Ab test (can detect from 4 weeks). Repeat 3 months post-exposure if negative.
PEP Post-Exposure Prophylaxis. Start within 24h (up to 72h) of high-risk exposure. Refer to GUM/A&E immediately.
PrEP Pre-Exposure Prophylaxis. Refer to sexual health service for at-risk individuals.
Referral All positive diagnoses require urgent referral to HIV specialist. Refer for complications (e.g., TB, shingles).
