Campylobacter Infection

Quick-Scan Clinical Reference Tool

Updated: Apr 24, 2024

CRITICAL FLAGS & COMPLICATIONS

Immediate Concerns

Guillain-Barre Syndrome (GBS) Common antecedent. Watch for ascending paralysis/areflexia.
Acute Appendicitis Mimic Severe RLQ pain common; misdiagnosis risk.
Extra-intestinal Infection Meningitis, endocarditis, septic arthritis (esp. C. fetus or immunocompromised).

High Risk Groups

Immunocompromised HIV/AIDS, hypogammaglobulinaemia. High risk of bacteraemia.
Pregnant Patients Risk of perinatal infection/fetal death. Active rule-out required.
Extremes of Age Infants & older adults (esp. with liver disease).

INTERACTIVE: TREATMENT DECISION SUPPORT

Guideline: Antibiotics are NOT indicated for uncomplicated infections. Use this checklist to determine if antimicrobial therapy is required.

Criteria for Antibiotics:

Supportive Care Only
Fluid & Electrolyte Replacement

Recommended Regimen

Preferred: Azithromycin
Adults: 500mg once daily (3-5 days)
Children: 10mg/kg once daily
Alternative (Pregnancy): Erythromycin
Adults: 500mg four times daily
Children: 30-50mg/kg/day divided
Fluoroquinolones (e.g. Ciprofloxacin)
High resistance (~28%). Use only if inappropriate to use Macrolides.

Key Presentation

  • Common Abdominal Pain Often severe, continuous, radiates to RLQ (mimics appendicitis).
  • Common Diarrhoea Watery (up to 8-10/day). Becomes bloody in 20-25% cases. Inflammatory type (leukocytes/blood present).
  • Uncommon Prodrome Headache, myalgias, chills, fever (1-3 days prior).
  • Uncommon Vomiting Mostly infants.

Key Risk History

Ask about: Undercooked poultry, unpasteurised milk, untreated water, travel to Asia/S. America/Africa, contact with farm animals.

Diagnostics

Investigation Key Indication
Stool Culture (Campy-BAP/Skirrow) Gold Standard / Diagnostic
Stool Microscopy RBCs & Neutrophils (Inflammatory)
Serology Only for late complications (e.g., GBS)
Blood Culture Only if bacteraemia suspected
Note: PCR panels (Infectious Diarrhea Panel) recommended if severe or >7 days duration.

Routine Care & Prevention

Primary Treatment

Fluid & Electrolyte Replacement is essential for ALL patients.

  • Oral: ORS (glucose or polymer-based).
  • IV: If nausea/vomiting prevents oral intake.

Prevention

Cook poultry to internal temp of 74°C (165°F).
Avoid unpasteurised milk & untreated water.
Hand hygiene (esp. after handling raw meat).

Quick Differentials

Campylobacter often presents with more severe abdominal pain than other bacterial causes.

Salmonella
Bloody stool less common in non-enteric.
Shigella
Similar presentation.
Acute Appendicitis
RLQ pain overlap. Use Ultrasound/CT.
Ulcerative Colitis
Chronic history. Stool culture negative.
Viral Gastroenteritis
More upper GI symptoms (nausea/vomiting).
