Antibiotic-Associated Diarrhoea

Quick-Scan Reference Tool (NICE CKS)
Last Revised: May 2025
Target: Adults 18+
EMERGENCY FLAGS Admit

Suspect Fulminant C. difficile or severe complications if present:

  • Hypotension or Septic Shock
  • Ileus or Toxic Megacolon
  • Bowel Perforation
  • Pseudomembranous colitis signs
  • Severe abdominal distension (absence of diarrhoea is a red flag)
  • Rapid deterioration
IMMEDIATE ACTION: Arrange urgent hospital admission.
HIGH RISK SUSCEPTIBILITY

Be extra vigilant if patient has:

Age > 65 High mortality risk
Recent Antibiotics Clindamycin, Cephalosporins, Fluoroquinolones
Current PPI Use Acid suppression increases risk
Immunosuppression Chemo, transplant, HIV
Prior CDI 20% recurrence rate
Immediate Triage Steps
1. Assess Severity

Check for dehydration, shock, or acute abdomen.

2. Send Stool Sample

Must take shape of container. Do not wait for results to treat if high suspicion.

3. Stop Antibiotic

Stop inciting antibiotic if clinically safe to do so.

Severity Calculator (C. diff)

Use criteria to classify infection severity.

Investigations
Stool Sample Criteria
  • Symptoms cannot be explained by other causes.
  • Stool takes shape of container (liquid enough).
  • Label with: Recent abx, PPI use, hospital contact.
Bloods Required
  • FBC (WBC Count)
  • Serum Creatinine (assess renal function & baseline comparison)
  • U&Es
Note: Re-test only if first test negative but strong clinical suspicion remains. Do not re-test to confirm cure (can remain positive).
Negative / Not Suspected C. diff
Action: Stop the inciting antibiotic if appropriate.

If the C. diff toxin test is negative:

  • Manage as acute gastroenteritis (fluids).
  • Consider specialist advice if severe but stopping abx is not possible.
  • Re-test only if clinical suspicion remains high.
Confirmed / Suspected C. diff
Initial Episode (Non-Severe) 1st Line: Vancomycin 125 mg orally

Four times a day for 10 days.

(Seek specialist advice before prescribing. Fidaxomicin is 2nd line if local protocol dictates).

Recurrent Episode

Defined as symptoms resolving and recurring.

Relapse (< 12 weeks):
Fidaxomicin 200 mg orally twice a day for 10 days.
New Infection (> 12 weeks):
Vancomycin 125 mg qds OR Fidaxomicin 200 mg bd for 10 days.

Hygiene & Transmission Control
CRITICAL: Alcohol hand rubs are NOT effective against C. diff spores.
  • Wash hands with soap and water thoroughly and regularly.
  • Clean toilet areas with diluted bleach (chlorine-containing) agent.
  • Isolate if in care home setting to prevent outbreaks.
