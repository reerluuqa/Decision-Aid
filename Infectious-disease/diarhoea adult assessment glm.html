Quick Decision Aid

Based on duration and key features

Scenario: Acute Diarrhoea (< 4 weeks)

‚ÑπÔ∏è
Definition Lasting less than 14 days (up to 4 weeks for this assessment context).
Key Assessment Points
  • Onset & frequency (Pre-formed toxin if < 6hrs post-food)
  • Character: Watery vs. Bloody
  • Signs of dehydration
  • Recent travel or food sources
  • Sick contacts (Outbreaks?)
  • New medications (especially laxatives, PPIs, antibiotics)
Send Stool Sample If...
  • Systemically unwell / needs admission
  • Blood or pus in stool
  • Immunocompromised
  • Recent antibiotics/hospital (Request C. difficile)
  • Foreign travel (Request Ova, Cysts & Parasites)
  • Public health risk (Food handlers)
Consider Referral

If diagnosis uncertain and acute causes excluded, suspect early chronic cause (see Chronic tab). Also use FIT testing if cancer criteria not fully met but clinical concern exists.

Scenario: Chronic Diarrhoea (> 4 weeks)

‚ÑπÔ∏è
Definition Lasting more than 4 weeks. Differential is broad.
Red Flag Indicators
  • Unexplained weight loss
  • Unexplained rectal bleeding
  • Abdominal/Rectal mass
  • Iron deficiency anaemia
  • Family history of IBD/Coeliac
  • Nocturnal symptoms
Common Causes
  • Irritable Bowel Syndrome (IBS)
  • Inflammatory Bowel Disease (Crohn's/UC)
  • Coeliac Disease
  • Microscopic Colitis
  • Bile Acid Malabsorption
  • Medication side effects
Blood Tests (All Patients)
  • FBC (Anaemia)
  • U&E
  • LFTs + Albumin
  • Calcium
  • B12 & Folate
  • Ferritin (Iron status)
  • TFTs
  • ESR / CRP
Crucial: Test for Coeliac Disease (IgA tTG or IgA EMA) while patient is on a gluten-containing diet.

Background & Causes

Types of Diarrhoea
Acute < 14 days
Persistent 14 - 28 days
Chronic > 4 weeks
Mechanisms
  • Osmotic: Stops with fasting (e.g., laxatives, malabsorption).
  • Secretory: Persists with fasting (e.g., infections, bile acids).
  • Inflammatory: Blood/pus present (e.g., IBD).
  • Motility: Increased frequency (e.g., hyperthyroidism).
Infectious Causes (Acute)
Viruses:
  • Norovirus (Common)
  • Rotavirus
  • Sapovirus
Bacteria:
  • Campylobacter jejuni
  • Salmonella
  • Shigella
  • E. Coli
  • C. difficile (Antibiotic assoc.)

Investigation Summary

Stool Samples
Scenario Tests to Request
Systemically unwell / Bloody Microbiology culture
Post-Antibiotics / Hospital C. difficile toxin
Foreign Travel Microbiology + Ova, Cysts & Parasites (x3 samples)
Chronic + Travel Risk Microbiology + Ova, Cysts & Parasites
Specialist Tests
  • Faecal Calprotectin: Differentiates IBS vs IBD in <40s (if cancer not suspected). Do not use if rectal bleeding present.
  • FIT (Faecal Immunochemical Test): For colorectal cancer triage in primary care.
  • CA125: Consider if symptoms suggest ovarian cancer.
  • HIV Serology: Consider if unexplained chronic diarrhoea or risk factors.
