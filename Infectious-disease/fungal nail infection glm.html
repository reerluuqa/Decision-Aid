Fungal Nail Infection

Last Revised: August 2023 | Age 12+

Critical Actions & Red Flags

Review before prescribing or if diagnosis is uncertain.

Itraconazole Warning

Negative inotropic effect.

DO NOT PRESCRIBE if ventricular dysfunction or history of heart failure.

Terbinafine Warning

Hepatotoxicity Risk.

STOP IMMEDIATELY if unexplained nausea, fatigue, jaundice, right upper abdominal pain, or dark urine.

Refer / Red Flag

  • Diagnosis uncertain (psoriasis, melanoma).
  • Subungual melanoma (black pigmentation extending to nail fold).
  • Immunocompromised patient.
  • Child requiring oral antifungal.

Contraindications

  • Terbinafine: Hepatic impairment, severe renal impairment (caution).
  • Itraconazole: Acute porphyria, heart failure.

Diagnosis & Assessment

Confirm clinically AND via microscopy/culture if considering antifungal treatment.

Distal/Lateral Common

White/yellow opaque streaks. Nail lifts up at free edge, erodes. May have 'spikes' spreading proximally.

Superficial White Children

Small flaky white patches/pits on top of plate. Roughens and spreads deeper.

Proximal

White/yellow spots in lunula. Debris accumulates, causing onycholysis spreading distally.

Total Dystrophy Severe

Marked thickening, hyperkeratosis. Plate destroyed. Common in immunocompromised (Candida).

Severe Dermatophytoma

Granulated mass of hyphae/necrotic keratin under nail plate. "Spikes" from distal to proximal.

With Paronychia

Painful redness/swelling of periungual skin. Likely Candida. Treat paronychia too.

Differential Diagnosis (Rule Out)

  • Psoriasis: Pitting, onycholysis, 'oil drop sign'.
  • Lichen Planus: Thinned/grooved nails, longitudinal ridges.
  • Trauma: Distal onycholysis, history of injury.
  • Bacterial (Pseudomonas): Green/black nails.
  • Yellow Nail Syndrome: Green-yellow, hard, elevated curvature.
  • Onychogryphosis: Thickened, ram's horn nails (older age).
  • Subungual Melanoma: Irregular black pigmentation extending to nail fold. URGENT REFERRAL.

Management Protocol

Self-Care (First Line for All)

Advise on these measures regardless of pharmacological treatment.

  • Keep nails trimmed short and filed down.
  • Wear well-fitting, breathable shoes/cotton socks.
  • Treat concurrent athlete's foot (Tinea pedis).
  • Avoid communal showers barefoot.
  • Do not share nail clippers.
  • Replace old contaminated footwear.
1

Is treatment required?

No: Asymptomatic & not bothered by appearance.

Yes: Pain, discomfort, psychological distress, high risk of complications (diabetes, PAD), or source of skin infection.

2

Assess Severity & Type

Mild: Very early distal/superficial infection.

Mod/Severe: Matrix involvement, >50% nail, thickening (>2mm), or lateral edge involvement.

3

Select Treatment

Topical Treatment

Mild Cases Only

Indication: Distal/Superficial infection OR contraindication to oral antifungals.

Drug: Amorolfine 5% Nail Lacquer.

Dose: Apply once or twice weekly after filing.

Duration: Fingernails 6mo, Toenails 9-12mo.

Warning: Cure rates are very low.

Oral Treatment

Moderate/Severe

Indication: If self-care/topical fails, matrix involvement, or significant symptoms.

1st Line (Dermatophyte): Terbinafine.

1st Line (Candida/Yeast): Itraconazole.

Alternative: Itraconazole if Terbinafine contraindicated/not tolerated.

Prescribing Information

Terbinafine 1st Line

Indication: Dermatophyte nail infection.
Dose 250 mg once a day
Duration (Fingernails) 6 weeks
Duration (Toenails) 12 - 24 weeks
Improvement seen Fingers: ~2mo | Toes: ~3mo

Safety: Check LFTs at baseline and after 4-6 weeks. Stop if symptomatic (nausea, fatigue, jaundice).

Renal: Use 50% dose if eGFR < 50 mL/min (or avoid per manufacturer).

Key Interactions:

  • Avoid Warfarin (Monitor INR).
  • Avoid Tamoxifen.
  • Increases levels of Tricyclics, some Beta-blockers, SSRIs.

Itraconazole Candida / Alt

Indication: Candida / Yeast infection, or if Terbinafine unsuitable.
Dose 200 mg TWICE a day
Regimen Pulse Therapy: 1 week on, 3 weeks off.
Duration (Fingernails) 2 Pulses
Duration (Toenails) 3 Pulses

Safety: Negative inotropic effect - AVOID in heart failure.

Key Interactions:

  • Statins: Avoid Simvastatin, Lovastatin.
  • DO NOT take with St John's Wort.
  • Avoid Ergot alkaloids.
  • Increases effects of Warfarin, Digoxin, Calcium Channel Blockers, Benzodiazepines.

Amorolfine (Topical)

Usage: OTC or Prescription. Apply 1-2 times weekly after filing. Avoid nail varnish during treatment.

Adverse Effects: Local burning, erythema, itching. Contains ethanol (flammable).

Follow-Up & Failure

Monitoring Success

The Notch Technique: File a notch at the base of the abnormal nail before starting treatment.

  • Success: Normal nail grows out distal to the notch.
  • Failure: Abnormal nail moves proximal to the notch (ongoing infection).

Review 3-6 months after starting oral treatment.

Managing Failure

If normal nail regrowth does not occur:

  • Treat concurrent Tinea pedis (athlete's foot).
  • Re-sample for microscopy/culture.
  • Consider combination therapy (Topical + Oral).
  • Refer to Podiatrist: If thick nails cause pain/trauma.
  • Refer to Dermatologist: If treatment fails, co-existent nail disease, or diagnosis uncertain.
