üíä Ciprofloxacin - Detailed Information ‚ñº

‚ùå Contraindications

  • ‚úó History of tendon disorders with quinolone use
  • ‚úó Previous serious adverse reactions to quinolones
  • ‚úó Concurrent corticosteroid use

‚ö†Ô∏è Cautions

  • Family history of aneurysm disease
  • Pre-existing aortic aneurysm/dissection
  • Epilepsy or seizure predisposition
  • Diabetes mellitus - monitor glucose
  • G6PD deficiency
  • QT prolongation risk factors
  • History of psychotic disorder
  • Myasthenia gravis
  • Age >60, renal impairment, transplant

Key Drug Interactions

Antacids (take 2h before or 4-6h after), ciclosporin, domperidone, methotrexate, NSAIDs (seizure risk), phenytoin, theophylline, tizanidine (avoid), warfarin (monitor INR), zolmitriptan (max 5mg/24h), QT-prolonging drugs.

‚ö†Ô∏è Cautions

  • Acute porphyrias
  • Folate deficiency (elderly, those on antifolates)

ACE inhibitors/ARBs (hyperkalaemia), azathioprine/mercaptopurine, ciclosporin, coumarins (monitor INR), digoxin (elderly), potassium-sparing diuretics, methotrexate (monitor FBC), phenytoin.

MHRA FLUOROQUINOLONE WARNING (2024)

Systemic fluoroquinolones must now only be prescribed when other commonly recommended antibiotics are inappropriate.

üö® Serious Adverse Effects - Fluoroquinolones

Can cause long-lasting (months to years), disabling, and potentially IRREVERSIBLE side effects affecting multiple systems. Estimated 1-10 per 10,000 patients.

STOP IMMEDIATELY if:

  • ! Tendonitis or tendon rupture
  • ! Muscle pain or weakness
  • ! Joint pain or swelling
  • ! Peripheral neuropathy
  • ! CNS effects
‚ö†Ô∏è Additional Safety Concerns

Cardiovascular:

  • Increased risk of aortic aneurysm and dissection (particularly elderly)
  • QT interval prolongation

Tendon Injury Risk Increased:

  • Age >60 years
  • Concurrent corticosteroid use (CONTRAINDICATED)
  • Renal impairment
  • Solid organ transplant recipients

Psychiatric:

  • Suicidal thoughts or self-endangering behaviour reported
  • Caution in history of psychotic disorder
‚ö†Ô∏è Co-trimoxazole Specific Warnings
  • Severe respiratory toxicity with potential progression to Adult Respiratory Distress Syndrome
  • Hyperkalaemia very common
  • Avoid with clozapine - increased risk of fatal agranulocytosis
