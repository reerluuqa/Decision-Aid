Immediate Risk Indicators
Prioritise these patients immediately.

People presenting with cross-infection risk:

  • Diarrhoea, vomiting, unexplained rash, fever.
  • Known multi-drug resistant organism (MDRO) e.g., MRSA.
  • Recent inpatient in UK/abroad hospital (last 12 months).
  • Close contact with Carbapenemase-producing Enterobacterales.
What is an HCAI?

Infection resulting from contact with healthcare settings or medical intervention.

Common Causes:
MRSA, MSSA, C. difficile, E. coli.
Transmission: Contact, Droplet, Airborne, Inoculation (sharps).
Risk Assessment Actions
1. Assess Promptly Evaluate risk to/from individual on arrival.
2. Determine Controls Identify steps to reduce/control risks.
3. Isolate if Necessary Separate by >1 metre from others if infectious.
Hand Hygiene
Use Alcohol Rub UNLESS visibly soiled or caring for diarrhoea/vomiting.

Decontaminate Immediately:

  • Before direct contact or aseptic procedure.
  • After direct contact or body fluid exposure.
  • After contact with surroundings.
Technique: Rub vigorously 10-15 seconds. Allow alcohol to dry.
Personal Protective Equipment (PPE)
Gloves:

Use for invasive procedures, contact with non-intact skin, or body fluids. Change between patients.

Aprons/Gowns:

Single-use fluid repellent gown if risk of extensive splashing.

Face Protection:

Worn if risk of splashing to face/eyes. Regular glasses are NOT protection.

Respiratory & Cough Hygiene
Cover nose/mouth with disposable tissue when coughing/sneezing.
Dispose of tissues promptly into clinical waste.
Decontaminate hands immediately after disposal.
Sharps Safety
DO NOT RECAP NEEDLES. DO NOT BEND/BREAK.
  • Discard immediately at point of use into correct container.
  • Use sharps safety devices if risk assessment indicates.
  • Do not pass directly hand-to-hand.
Container Limits: Dispose when fill line is reached. Label with date/source.

Offensive/Hygiene

Segregate immediately. Hold bags by neck. Do not open bags to check contents.

Blood/Body Fluid Spillage
PPE First Wear gloves and apron.
Contain Cover spill with chlorine-releasing granules/spill kit.
Dispose Treat spillage as infectious waste.
Catheters & Devices

Core Principle: Remove as soon as no longer needed.


  • Urinary Catheters: Link to UTIs. Maintain closed system.
  • Vascular Access: Link to bloodstream infections. Aseptic technique essential.
Educate patient/carers on safe management of the device to prevent infection.
Venepuncture Steps
  1. Wash hands / Alcohol rub.
  2. Put on apron.
  3. Decontaminate hands again after tourniquet.
  4. Put on gloves. Clean skin (70% alcohol, 30s).
  5. Discard sharps immediately. Apply pressure.
  6. Remove PPE. Wash hands.
MRSA
  • Risk: Underlying illness, older age, open wounds.
  • Action: Standard precautions + assessment of colonization risk.
C. difficile
  • Risk: Antibiotic use, hospital stay.
  • Action: Soap and water (alcohol ineffective). Environ cleaning with chlorine.
Blood-borne Viri (HIV/Hep B/C)
  • Risk: Percutaneous injury (sharps), mucous membrane splash.
  • Action: Immediate first aid. Report occupational health exposure immediately.
PVL-SA
  • Risk: Younger age, close contact sports, shared towels.
  • Action: Advise on hygiene, avoid sharing items.
