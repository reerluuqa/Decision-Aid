Management Protocol

Step-by-Step

1. Immediate Conservative Care

For all minor infections or early presentation.

  • Warm Soaks: 10-15 mins, 3-4 times daily (helps localize infection & drain pus).
  • Analgesia: Paracetamol or NSAID as required.
  • Hygiene: Keep clean and dry. Avoid trauma/manipulation.

2. Incision & Drainage (I&D)

Required if fluctuant pus collection is present.

  • Perform: If expertise/facilities exist in primary care.
  • Refer: To Surgery/ED if fluctuance is extensive or not manageable in primary care.
  • Note: Antibiotics alone are often insufficient for an abscess.

3. Antibiotic Indications

Prescribe oral antibiotics if:

  • I&D not performed AND significant symptoms.
  • I&D performed but extensive tissue involvement, fever, or comorbidities exist (e.g., Diabetes).

Prevention & Recurrence

For Recurrent Acute Paronychia:

  • Swab to confirm organism & sensitivities.
  • Check for Staphylococcal carriage.
  • Consider X-ray if refractory to rule out osteomyelitis.

Patient Advice:

  • Avoid biting nails/sucking fingers.
  • Do not trim cuticles aggressively.
  • Wear cotton-lined gloves if hands frequently in water.
  • Dry hands thoroughly after washing.
