Immediate Action / Admission Required
  • AKI Warning Stage 3 with High Probability (Acute illness context) → Immediate Hospital Admission.
  • Hyperkalaemia: Severe (K+ ≥ 6.5 mmol/L) or Moderate (K+ 6–6.4 mmol/L) with symptoms (e.g., weakness, arrhythmias).
  • Symptoms: Confusion, lethargy, severe drowsiness, anuria, or signs of fluid overload (pulmonary oedema).
  • History: Known CKD Stage 4/5, renal transplant, or previous AKI with acute illness.

Diagnosis & Assessment

When to Suspect AKI

AKI is often asymptomatic. Be alert in people with:

  • Nausea, vomiting, diarrhoea, suspected dehydration
  • Reduced urine output or colour change
  • Confusion, fatigue, drowsiness
  • Acute illness + Risk Factors (Age >65, CKD, Sepsis)
  • Recent use of Nephrotoxic drugs (NSAIDs, ACEi, ARBs)

Establishing Baseline Creatinine

  1. Use lowest value within last 7 days.
  2. OR review older results: Use lowest or mean value from 7 days to 1 year ago.
  3. OR if no baseline: Repeat creatinine in 48–72 hrs (don't delay treatment if unwell).
Note: Creatinine is a slow-changing surrogate. It may take 24-72 hours to reflect true GFR decline.

  • Hypovolaemia (bleeding, GI losses)
  • Reduced cardiac output (HF, Sepsis)
  • Drugs: ACEi, ARBs, NSAIDs, Diuretics
  • Toxins: Antibiotics, PPIs, Contrast
  • Vascular: Vasculitis, TTP

  • Blocked Catheter

Risk depends on susceptibility and exposure to insult.

Age ≥ 65 Chronic Kidney Disease (CKD) History of AKI Heart Failure Liver Disease Diabetes Mellitus Sepsis Hypovolaemia/Dehydration Neurological Impairment Iodine Contrast Exposure Immunocompromised
Medication Risk: Use of NSAIDs, ACE inhibitors, ARBs, Diuretics, or Aminoglycosides in the last week, especially if hypovolaemic.

Life-Threatening

  • Hyperkalaemia: Arrhythmias, paralysis.
  • Metabolic Acidosis: Hyperventilation, circulatory collapse.
  • Fluid Overload: Pulmonary oedema, heart failure.
  • Uraemia: Pericarditis, encephalopathy, seizures.

Long-Term

  • Chronic Kidney Disease (CKD): Progressive loss of function.
  • End-Stage Kidney Disease: Need for dialysis.
  • Hypertension: Increased cardiovascular risk.

Prevention & Follow-Up

Prevention Advice:
  • Ensure adequate hydration during acute illness.
  • Review nephrotoxic medications in at-risk patients.
  • Monitor creatinine after starting or dose-adjusting drugs.
Post-AKI Follow-Up (NICE):
  • Monitor for CKD development for at least 3 years after episode (longer if Stage 3 AKI).
  • Adults discharged after AKI need clinical review within 3 months (or sooner if high risk).
