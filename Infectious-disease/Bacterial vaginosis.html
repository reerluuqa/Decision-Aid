Bacterial Vaginosis – 1-Page Quick Reference

Last updated July 2023 • NICE CKS

1. Screen & Confirm Diagnosis

  • Symptoms: Fishy, thin, grey/white discharge; no itch/soreness.
  • Key questions: sexual hx, douching, IUD, new partner, smoking.
  • Exam: speculum → homogeneous coating; test pH > 4.5.
  • Swab only if: atypical, pregnant, post-procedure, or diagnostic doubt.

2. Treat (Non-Pregnant)

First-LineDoseNotes
Oral Metronidazole 400 mg PO BID × 5–7 d Avoid alcohol
or Intravaginal Metronidazole Gel 0.75 % 1 applicator OD × 5 d Off-label <18 yrs
or Intravaginal Clindamycin Cream 2 % 1 applicator OD × 7 d Oil-based → ↓ condom efficacy
  • Asymptomatic: treat only pre-gynae procedure.
  • Persistent/Recurrent: 7-day oral metronidazole; remove IUD; treat female partner if WSW.
  • Suppressive: metronidazole gel 2×/wk (off-label) if ≥3 recurrences/year.

3. Pregnant Women

  • Symptomatic: Oral metronidazole 400 mg BID × 5–7 d (avoid 2 g stat).
  • Asymptomatic: discuss with obstetrician (treat if high-risk for PTB).
  • Topical: metronidazole gel or clindamycin cream (2nd/3rd trimester only).
  • Re-test: 1 month if still symptomatic.

4. Red Flags & Escalate

  • Recurrent BV → GUM referral.
  • Pelvic pain/fever → exclude PID.
  • Pregnancy complications → obstetric review.

⚠️ Pearls

  • Consistent condom use reduces recurrence.
  • No need to treat male partners.
  • Re-test only if symptoms persist (pregnant: re-test @ 1 month).
