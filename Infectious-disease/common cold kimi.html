üí° Key Diagnostic Points

  • Symptoms are self-limiting - no treatment alters course
  • Common viruses: Rhinovirus (50%), Coronavirus (10-15%), Influenza (5-15%)
  • Transmission: direct contact, aerosols, contaminated surfaces
  • Infectious period: several weeks; children are key reservoirs

üö´ Important Warnings

‚ùåIneffective or Harmful Treatments

Antibiotics Antihistamines OTC Cough/Cold meds (children <6y)

‚úÖ Recommended Symptom Relief

‚úì Analgesics

Paracetamol or Ibuprofen for headache, muscle pain, or fever

  • Adults & children ‚â•5 years: if needed
  • Children <5 years: only if fever and distressed
  • Use lowest effective dose, shortest duration
‚úì Self-Care

Adequate fluids, rest, healthy food (no special diet needed)

‚úì Nasal Relief

Saline drops for congestion (especially infants)

Steam inhalation (avoid scalding)

‚úì Throat Relief

Salt water gargle or menthol sweets

Vapor rubs (chest/back, not nostrils)

üë∂ Age-Specific Guidance

Adults & Children >6 years
Children ‚â§6 years
  • OTC combination products may provide limited benefit
  • Intranasal decongestants: short-term use only (avoid >7 days)
  • Oral decongestants: small benefit, consider cardiovascular risk
  • Cough medicines: limited benefit but generally safe
  • ‚ö†Ô∏è Caution: Avoid multiple products containing paracetamol

üö´Children ‚â§6 Years

AVOID: Antitussives, expectorants, decongestants, antihistamines

  • Use simple cough remedies (glycerine, honey, lemon)
  • Honey & lemon warm drink (children >1 year)
  • Saline nasal drops for congestion
  • Paracetamol/Ibuprofen for fever/distress only

üßº Infection Control

  • Wash hands frequently with soap and hot water
  • Avoid sharing towels, toys, or utensils
  • Normal activity is fine - doesn't prolong illness
  • Stay home only if severely unwell
