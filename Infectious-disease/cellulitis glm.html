Cellulitis - Acute

Primary Care Management
Last revised: Oct 2025
Red Flags Diagnosis Management Prescribing & Safety
Immediate Action / Admit

Refer urgently or admit if:

Signs of sepsis
Suspected Necrotizing Fasciitis
Orbital or Periorbital Cellulitis
Systemic illness (confusion, tachycardia)
Severe immunocompromise / unstable comorbidity
Failure to respond to oral antibiotics

Diagnosis & Assessment

History

Duration & severity of pain
Systemic symptoms (fever, malaise)?
Recent trauma/surgery?
Risk factors (Diabetes, PVD, Lymphoedema)?
Water/Freshwater exposure or animal bites?

Examination

Unilateral redness, heat, swelling, tenderness
Mark extent with pen to monitor
Look for portal of entry (ulcer, tinea pedis)
Check for fluctuance (abscess)
Observations (Temp, BP, Pulse)

Management (Adults 18+)

Standard First-Line

For uncomplicated cellulitis

Flucloxacillin

500–1000 mg four times a day

Duration: 5–7 days

Advise: Take on empty stomach. Space evenly.
Caution: Cholestatic jaundice/hepatic dysfunction.

Penicillin Allergy

If Flucloxacillin unsuitable

Clarithromycin

500 mg twice a day

Duration: 5–7 days

Or
Doxycycline

200 mg day 1, then 100 mg daily

Interaction: Macrolides increase statin levels (risk of myopathy). Check QT prolongation.

Facial / Near Eyes

Consider specialist advice

Co-amoxiclav

500/125 mg three times a day

Duration: 7 days

If Penicillin Allergy:

Clarithromycin 500mg BD + Metronidazole 400mg TDS

Known Lymphoedema

Longer course usually required.

Flucloxacillin

500–1000 mg four times a day

Duration: 14 days

Note: Skin changes (discolouration) may persist for months; this does not necessarily require further antibiotics.

Patient Advice

  • Elevate affected limb to reduce oedema.
  • Avoid compression garments during acute episode.
  • Paracetamol or Ibuprofen for pain/fever.
  • Keep hydrated.
  • Seek help if symptoms worsen rapidly or do not improve after 2–3 days.

Reassessment

Review after 2–3 days:

Is erythema reducing?
Is systemic toxicity resolving?
Any severe pain disproportionate to infection?

Safety & Interactions

Flucloxacillin

Severe Interaction: High-dose Flucloxacillin + Paracetamol can cause High Anion Gap Metabolic Acidosis (HAGMA).

Be alert for signs especially in renal impairment, sepsis, or malnutrition.
Warfarin: May decrease INR.

Clarithromycin / Erythromycin

Statins: Stop Simvastatin (Contraindicated). Use caution with Atorvastatin.
QT Prolongation: Avoid if history of arrhythmias or electrolyte imbalance.
Warfarin: May increase INR.

Doxycycline

Contraindicated: Pregnancy and children under 12 years (risk of teeth staining).

Photosensitivity: Advise sun protection.

Metronidazole

Alcohol: Avoid during and for 48 hours after (disulfiram-like reaction).

Warfarin: Enhances anticoagulant effect.
