Granuloma Annulare

Quick-Scan Tool
Associations: Rule out underlying diabetes, thyroid disease, or lipid disorders. Consider malignancy screening in older patients with atypical presentation.

What is it?

Benign & Self-Limiting

Typically asymptomatic. 50% resolve within 2 years without treatment. Recurrences are common but harmless.

Key Demographics

Who gets it?
  • Localised: Women in their 30s (most common).
  • Subcutaneous: Children < 5 years.
  • Generalised: Older adults (watch for malignancy).

Clinical Presentation Summary

Appearance

Small grouped papules in an annular (ring) configuration. Flesh-coloured, pink, or violaceous.

Common Sites

Dorsum of hands, feet, elbows, knees, ankles. 60% limited to hands/arms.

Symptoms

Usually asymptomatic. Non-itchy. Systemically well.

Clinical Variants

Localised 75%
Site: Hands, feet, elbows.
Look: Classic annular rings. Asymptomatic.
Generalised
Site: Torso, limbs symmetrical.
Look: Hundreds of small macules/papules. Rarely annular.
Subcutaneous
Site: Feet, fingertips, scalp (Kids <5).
Look: Deep, painless, solitary soft-tissue nodule.
Perforating
Site: Hands, fingers.
Look: Superficial papules with central umbilicated crust/ulcer. Can be painful/itchy.
Patch
Site: Intertriginous areas (axilla, groin).
Look: Erythematous plaques with palpable border.

Diagnostic Approach

First-Line Strategy

Diagnosis is typically clinical. Tissue biopsy is rarely needed unless the diagnosis is uncertain or a malignant process (e.g., lymphoma, sarcoma) is suspected (rapid growth/pain).

Action Required: Red Flags & Screening

Potential Malignancy Association

While evidence is challenged, an association is postulated in specific atypical cases.

  • Triggers: Older patients (>65), Atypical presentation (palmar plaques, erythroderma), or Generalised GA.
  • Associated Cancers: Hodgkin's disease, CLL, other haematological malignancies.
  • Action: Perform age-appropriate cancer screening. Evaluate for systemic symptoms (weight loss, lymphadenopathy).
Diabetes Mellitus

Evidence: Weak association reported.

Screen if: Strong family history, symptoms, Generalised GA, or patient age >40.

Test: Fasting Blood Sugar
Metabolic / Thyroid

Associations: Thyroid disease and Hyperlipidaemia.

Action: Check fasting lipid profiles. Thyroid function tests if symptomatic.

TSH Lipid Panel
Infectious Triggers

HIV (generalised or atypical sites like penis), Hepatitis B/C, Herpes Zoster.

Drug-Induced

Check TNF-alpha inhibitors, checkpoint inhibitors (PD-1/PD-L1), vemurafenib, allopurinol, amlodipine, BCG vaccination.

Management Strategy

General Rule: Treatment is usually NOT required as the condition is self-limiting. Intervention is patient-driven (cosmetic concerns) or symptom-driven (pain/itch in perforating type).
