Mimics & Differentials Rule out conditions that can mimic Pityriasis Rosea, specifically:
  • Secondary Syphilis: Non-itchy, may involve palms/soles.
  • HIV Seroconversion: Symmetrical maculopapular rash, often acute illness.
  • Drug Reaction: No herald patch, usually violet-red, severe itch.

‚úÖ Management Plan

1

Reassurance

Rash usually resolves within 2-6 weeks (up to 3 months). No treatment needed for the rash itself. Not contagious.

2

Symptomatic Treatment (If Itchy)

  • Emollients: Regular use.
  • Antihistamines (Sedating): e.g., Chlorphenamine at night if sleep affected. Stop if no relief after 2 weeks.
  • Topical Corticosteroids: Mild (Hydrocortisone 1%) or Moderate (Clobetasone 0.05%). Use sparingly for up to 4 weeks.
3

Referral (Dermatology)

Refer if diagnosis uncertain, atypical forms suspected, severe itch uncontrolled, or persists >3 months.

Condition Key Differentiating Features
Guttate Psoriasis Small round scaly papules all over body. Usually more abrupt onset following strep infection.
Tinea Corporis Annular patches with active red scaly advancing edge and clear center. Fungal scraping positive.
Secondary Syphilis Non-itchy maculopapular rash. Often involves palms and soles.
Drug Eruption No herald patch. Lesions often violet-red. History of new medication (e.g., ACE inhibitors, Metronidazole, Omeprazole).
Erythema Migrans Lyme disease. Single expanding lesion (often >5cm) following tick bite.
Lichen Planus Flat-topped purple polygonal papules. Wrists/ankles common. May have Wickham's striae.

Emollients

Use regularly to soothe dry skin and itch.

Safety Warning: Advise patient not to smoke or go near naked flames when using paraffin-based emollients (fire risk).

Topical Corticosteroids

Mild: Hydrocortisone 1%

Moderate: Betamethasone valerate 0.025% or Clobetasone 0.05%

Apply once or twice daily for up to 4 weeks based on itch severity.

Oral Antihistamines

Sedating: Chlorphenamine (at night)

Use only if itching disturbs sleep. Discontinue if no relief after 2 weeks.
