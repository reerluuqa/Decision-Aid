SAFE START: General Measures
Avoid irritants (soap, over-washing). Use soap substitutes (aqueous cream). Wear white cotton underwear. Treat infections promptly.
Definition

Inflammation of the glans penis and prepuce. It is a descriptive term, not a diagnosis. Requires specific causative identification.

Cannot occur in circumcised males
(though balanitis can).
Broad Categories
  • Inflammatory Dermatoses (Eczema, Psoriasis, Lichen Sclerosus)
  • Infective Candida, Gonorrhoea, HSV, Anaerobes
  • Pre-Cancerous Erythroplasia of Queyrat (PeIN)
Assessment & Diagnosis
Key Diagnostic Factors

Symptoms

  • Pruritus: Common in eczema, contact dermatitis, scabies.
  • Pain/Soreness: Infection, trauma.
  • Dribbling: Incontinence risk factor (irritation).

Investigations

1st Line Tests

Micro Swab Gram stain/culture (Bacteria, Candida, Gonorrhoea)
NAAT/PCR C. trachomatis, Mycoplasma, N. gonorrhoeae, T. pallidum

Other / Specialist Tests

  • Skin Biopsy: If refractory to treatment, to exclude PeIN/Cancer.
  • Dark-field Microscopy: If syphilis suspected.
  • Patch Testing: If allergic contact dermatitis suspected.
Key Differentials
Condition Key Signs Diagnostic Path
Lichen Sclerosus White atrophic patches, telangiectasia, purpura. Meatal stenosis. "Tight" foreskin. Clinical. Biopsy if atypical/eroded.
Zoon Balanitis Well-demarcated, glistening, bright red/brown patches. "Cayenne pepper" spots. Biopsy often needed (excludes PeIN/LS).
Psoriasis Silvery-scaled patches on shaft (no scale on glans). Check nails, scalp, umbilicus. Clinical. Inverse pattern common.
Candidiasis Burning/soreness > Itch. Coalescent red patches with superficial erosions. KOH prep / Culture.
Erythroplasia of Queyrat (PeIN) Persistent, well-demarcated red velvety plaque on glans (uncircumcised). Biopsy mandatory. High cancer risk.
Gonorrhoea Purulent discharge, meatal oedema. Red patches similar to Zoon. Urethral swab / NAAT.

Inflammatory
1
Atopic / Contact Dermatitis

Adj: Oral Antihistamine for itch
1
Seborrhoeic Dermatitis
Topical Antifungal + Mild/Moderate Steroid. If severe (HIV/Folliculitis) -> Oral Itraconazole/Fluconazole.
1
Psoriasis / Reactive Arthritis
Topical Steroid or Calcineurin Inhibitor. Severe: Systemic agents (Methotrexate, Biologics).
Reactive Arthritis: +/- Oral Retinoids if HIV+

If Refractory -> Circumcision
1

Intermittent Moderate/High Potency Steroid +/- Antibiotic/Antifungal.
