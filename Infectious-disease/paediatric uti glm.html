Paediatric UTI

CKS April 2024
Age Range: Birth - 16 years
AGE < 3 MONTHS? REFER URGENTLY to paediatric specialist for parenteral antibiotics.

Suspect Pyelonephritis (Upper UTI)

High Risk Indicators:

  • Unexplained fever of 38¬∞C or higher
  • Loin pain or tenderness
  • Systemic illness (lethargy, vomiting)

ACTION REQUIRED:

Send urine sample for microscopy & culture within 24 hours.

Consider referral to paediatric specialist.

Source: NICE [NG144]

Suspect Cystitis (Lower UTI)

Common Symptoms (Age > 3mo):

  • Fever (may be absent or low grade)
  • Dysuria (Painful urination)
  • Frequency / Urgency
  • Abdominal pain
  • New bedwetting
  • Offensive/Cloudy urine

Note: In children < 3mo, symptoms are non-specific (vomiting, lethargy, poor feeding).

Urine Dipstick Interpretation & Actions (Age > 3 months)

Leukocyte Esterase Nitrite Action
Positive Positive Treat as UTI (Prescribe antibiotics) + Send Culture.
Negative Positive Send Culture. Treat as UTI only if sample fresh.
Positive Negative Send Culture. Treat only if clinical evidence strong (e.g. obvious symptoms).
Negative Negative UTI unlikely. Do not treat. Consider differential diagnosis.

* Send culture anyway if: High/Intermediate risk of serious illness, history of previous UTI, or pyelonephritis suspected.

Atypical UTI / Red Flags

Investigate/Refer urgently if UTI is Atypical:

  • Seriously ill (Sepsis)
  • Reduced or poor urinary flow
  • Abdominal or bladder mass
  • Raised creatinine
  • Failure to respond to treatment within 48 hours
  • Infection with non-E. coli organisms

CKS Topic Exclusions

This specific tool does NOT cover management of:

  • Recurrent UTI in sexually active girls
  • Children with indwelling urinary catheter
  • Neurogenic bladder / Intermittent catheterization
  • Significant uropathy / Underlying renal disease
  • Immunosuppression

Definitions & Causes

Common Pathogens (85-90%): E. coli

Others: Proteus mirabilis (uncircumcised boys), Klebsiella, Enterococcus, Staphylococcus saprophyticus (adolescent females).

Risk Factors:

  • Constipation / Voiding dysfunction
  • Vesicoureteral reflux (VUR)
  • Family history of VUR/Renal disease
  • Uncircumcised infant boys
