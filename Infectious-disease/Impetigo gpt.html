Impetigo — Rapid Reference Algorithm

CKS (Last revised July 2024) · For quick use during consultations
Primary care · All ages

1) Diagnosis — clinical

  • Non‑bullous: golden‑brown crusts (face/limbs), minimal erythema, usually no systemic features.
  • Bullous: large flaccid bullae → collarette of scale after rupture; trunk/flexures; systemic features if extensive.
  • Swab if recurrent/widespread, poor response, diagnostic uncertainty, or suspected MRSA.
Hx: onset, sites, trauma Comorbids (eczema/DM) Systemic Sx Check differentials

2) Red flags / Referral

Admit / urgent referral if: sepsis or deep soft tissue infection; immunosuppressed + widespread.
Consider referral / advice if: systemically unwell, frequent recurrence, high‑risk of complications, uncertain diagnosis. Suspected MRSA → discuss with microbiology.

3) Universal measures

  • Hygiene: wash lesions, avoid scratching, keep covered; hand hygiene; no sharing towels/linens; clean toys/equipment.
  • Laundry ≥ 60 °C daily for first few days.
  • Exclude from school/work until lesions healed & crusted or 48 h after starting treatment (including H2O2 cream).
  • Treat predisposing skin disease (eczema, lice, scabies, insect bites).

4) Treatment pathway

  • Non‑bullous — Localized, not unwell: Hydrogen peroxide 1% cream 2–3×/day × 5 days → if unsuitable Fusidic acid 2% TDS × 5 days → if resistance suspected/confirmed Mupirocin 2% TDS × 5 days.
  • Non‑bullous — Widespread, not unwell: Topical or oral antibiotic (do not combine).
  • Non‑bullous — Systemically unwell / high‑risk: Oral antibiotic.
  • Bullous impetigo: Oral antibiotic (5 days; consider 7 days if severe/very numerous lesions).

Typical course 5 days; may extend to 7 days by clinical judgement. Follow BNF/eMC for cautions, interactions and renal/hepatic dosing.

6) Reassess / Follow‑up

  • Reassess if rapid worsening or no improvement after a completed course.
  • Check adherence + hygiene; revisit differential diagnoses and complications.
  • Consider swab for C&S (esp. after oral therapy / recurrent cases).
  • Adjust to narrow‑spectrum per C&S; consider decolonisation if recurrent (nasal swab, local protocol).

Memory aide

  • Localized non‑bullous → topical (H2O2 first).
  • Widespread or bullous → oral antibiotic.
  • No dual topical + oral in routine cases.
  • Always hygiene + temporary exclusion.

Differentials (think)

  • Cellulitis, erysipelas, SSSS, necrotising fasciitis.
  • Tinea/candidiasis, scabies, HSV, VZV.
  • Contact/atopic dermatitis, insect bites, burns.
  • Pemphigus, bullous pemphigoid, EM, Sweets.
