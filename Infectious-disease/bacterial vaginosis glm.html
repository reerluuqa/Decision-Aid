Critical Red Flags (Immediate Action)

If present, consider urgent referral or GUM assessment:

  • Pelvic/Abdominal Pain
  • Fever
  • Pregnant
  • Recent Gynae Procedure
  • Post-termination
  • Post-natal
  • High STI Risk
  • Foreign Body Suspected

Diagnosis & Signs

Typical Presentation: Asymptomatic (50%) or thin, grey/white, homogeneous discharge with a fishy smell. No itching or soreness (key differentiator).

pH Test > 4.5 is suggestive (but not specific). Use narrow-range paper.
Microscopy Gram stain (Gold Standard) or Clue cells.

Management: Pregnant

Risks: BV in pregnancy is associated with late miscarriage, pre-term labour, and low birthweight.

Recommendation: Discuss with Obstetrician before initiating treatment.

Treatment:

  • Oral Metronidazole 400mg BD for 5-7 days is standard.
  • Avoid high-dose stat regimens in pregnancy.
