üìö Background Information

Deeper understanding for educational purposes

What is Nappy Rash?
An acute inflammatory reaction of the skin in the nappy area. It is a form of irritant contact dermatitis caused by the interaction of several factors.

Pathophysiology:

  • ‚úì Skin maceration due to excessive hydration
  • ‚úì Prolonged contact with urine and faeces
  • ‚úì Friction between skin and nappy
  • ‚úì Increased skin pH activates faecal enzymes which irritate skin
  • ‚úì Barrier disruption leads to increased percutaneous absorption of irritants
  • ‚úì Altered pH predisposes to Candida and bacterial colonization
Prevalence
Varies 16‚Äì65% depending on study population. Most common skin condition in infants.
Peak Age
9‚Äì12 months. Common in first 2 years. Up to 25% affected in first 4 weeks; up to 100% at some point in infancy.
Demographics
Both sexes affected equally. No ethnic variation.
Trends
Incidence and severity have decreased with super-absorbent disposable nappies and hypoallergenic products.
