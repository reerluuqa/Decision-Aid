Pubic Lice – Quick Scan

Red Flags & Emergencies
  • Severe secondary bacterial infection (cellulitis, impetigo)
  • Ocular involvement: conjunctivitis / blepharitis
  • Treatment failure after 2 correctly-applied insecticide courses
  • Child with infestation <6 months old

First-line Treatment
Permethrin 5% cream
Apply to whole body (neck-down) & dry naturally 8–12 h → wash off
Re-apply day 7
≥2 months, caution <18 y seek advice
Malathion 0.5% aqueous
Same technique; 12 h contact
Flammable – avoid hair-dryers/heat
≥6 months, max 1×/wk ×3 wks
Eyelash Infestation
  • Inert occlusive ophthalmic ointment (simple eye ointment BP) BD × 8–10 d
  • Do NOT use regular petrolatum (irritates eyes)
  • Permethrin 1% lotion 10 min single application (eyes closed) is an alternative
Prevention / Contact Tracing
  • Screen & treat sexual contacts within last 3 months
  • Hot wash (≥50 °C) clothing/bedding; seal unwashable items 2 weeks
  • Abstain from close body contact until both partners treated
Follow-up
  • Review 1 week after completion
  • If live lice: confirm technique → repeat or switch agent
  • Dead nits ≠ failure; remove with fine nit comb
  • Refer GUM if 2 failures or atypical picture
Pregnancy / Breast-feeding
  • Permethrin or malathion permitted (short contact)
  • Oral agents contraindicated
Children <6 months
  • Refer to specialist
  • Do NOT use topical insecticides
Consider Co-infection
  • Offer STI screen: chlamydia, gonorrhoea, HIV, syphilis
  • Refer to GUM if sexual acquisition
