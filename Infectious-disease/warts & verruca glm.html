Warts & Verrucae

Last Revised: Oct 2024
Source: NICE CKS

Visual Identification Guide

Common Wart

Firm, raised papules. Rough "cauliflower" surface. Knuckles, knees, fingers.

Verruca (Plantar)

Sole of foot. Often painful. Central dark dots (thrombosed capillaries).

Plane Wart

Flat-topped, round. Skin-coloured or greyish-yellow. Face, hands, shins.

Filiform Wart

Finger-like fronds. Narrow stalk. Face/Neck.

Key Diagnostic Checks

  • Diagnosis is usually clinical (visual).
  • Light paring: Reveals black dots (capillaries). Warning: Bleeding may spread virus.
  • Dermoscope: Helpful if diagnostic doubt.
  • Atypical? Refer for specialist assessment.

Differential Diagnosis

  • Molluscum contagiosum: Umbilicated, pearly white.
  • Corns/Calluses: Smooth surface, no black dots on paring.
  • Seborrheic keratosis: "Stuck on" appearance, greyscale.
  • Actinic keratosis: Sun-exposed, white/yellow plaques.

Specific Considerations

Plantar Warts (Verrucae)

  • Difficult to treat; Cryotherapy less effective.
  • Paring: Helps relieve pain caused by thick skin.
  • Careful paring required to avoid spreading virus.

Plane Warts (Back of hands)

  • High risk of scarring.

Secondary Care Options

Available if referral is made:

  • Physical Ablation: Surgery, laser, microwave, photodynamic.
  • Antimitotics: Podophyllin, Bleomycin, Retinoids.
  • Immunomodulators: Imiquimod 5%, Sensitizers (DPCP).
  • Virucidal: Formaldehyde/Glutaraldehyde.

Contraindications (Do NOT use)

  • On the face or anogenital regions.
  • On moles, birthmarks, or mucous membranes.
  • On open wounds, infected skin, or diabetic/neuropathic feet.
  • Children under 2 years.

Application Technique

  1. Soak area in warm water (2-5 mins).
  2. Dry thoroughly.
  3. Gently debride surface with emery board/pumice.
  4. Apply directly to wart, avoid surrounding skin.
  5. Apply once daily, usually at night.

Patient Advice & Hygiene

To minimize transmission and auto-inoculation:

  • Cover warts with waterproof plaster when swimming.
  • Avoid barefoot in public places (showers/pools).
  • Do not pick, scratch, or bite warts/nails.
  • Keep feet dry; change socks daily.
  • Dispose of skin filings hygienically.

Exclusion from Activities

No need to exclude. Children with warts/verrucae can continue sports and swimming, provided they take precautions to minimize transmission.
