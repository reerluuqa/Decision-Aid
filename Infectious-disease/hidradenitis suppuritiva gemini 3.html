Busy Clinic Mode

URGENT Referral & Red Flags

  • Immediate/Urgent Referral: Severe pain, signs of infection/sepsis, or severe suicidal ideation.
  • Routine Referral (Dermatology): Hurley Stage II or III disease.
  • Uncertain diagnosis or failure to respond to primary care treatments.

Diagnosis (Dessau Criteria)

  • 1. Typical Lesions: Nodules, abscesses, sinus tracts (tunnels), fibrotic scars, double-ended comedones.
  • 2. Typical Distribution: Axillae, groin, breasts, buttocks, perineum (intertriginous areas).
  • 3. Chronicity: Recurrence ≥2 episodes in 6 months.
  • Swabs are usually negative (sterile abscesses) but can rule out secondary staph infection.
  • Misdiagnosis as simple "boils" or "folliculitis" is common.

Hurley Staging (Severity)

  • Stage I (Mild): Single/multiple abscesses. NO sinus tracts or scarring.
  • Stage II (Moderate): Recurrent abscesses with sinus tracts and scarring. Separated by normal skin.
  • Stage III (Severe): Diffuse involvement, multiple interconnected tracts and abscesses.
  • Staging determines treatment intensity (Topical vs Systemic vs Biologic).

General & Lifestyle Management

  • Smoking Cessation: Crucial. Up to 60% of patients smoke.
  • Weight Loss: Obesity is a major risk factor. Consider GLP-1 agonists if eligible.
  • Wound Care: Non-adhesive absorbent dressings. Avoid tight clothing.
  • Pain Management: NSAIDs are first-line. Opioids use with caution.
  • Hygiene: Reassure patient it is not due to poor hygiene. Not contagious.

Medical Therapy Steps

  • Step 1 (Mild/Localized): Topical Clindamycin 1% BD or Chlorhexidine 4% wash.
  • Step 2 (Moderate/Stage I-II): Oral Tetracyclines (Lymecycline/Doxycycline) for 12 weeks.
  • Step 3 (Refractory/Severe): Clindamycin + Rifampicin combination (Specialist initiation often required).
  • Step 4 (Biologics): Adalimumab, Secukinumab, Bimekizumab (Secondary Care).
  • Others: Metformin (insulin resistance), Spironolactone (anti-androgen).

Acute & Surgical Options

  • Acute Flare: Incision & Drainage (I&D) provides relief but recurrence is ~100%. Intralesional steroids (Triamcinolone) may reduce inflammation.
  • Deroofing: "Unroofing" sinus tracts. Used for persistent nodules/tracts. Allows secondary healing.
  • Wide Excision: For extensive disease. Higher success but longer healing.

Associated Comorbidities

  • Metabolic: Diabetes T2, High Cholesterol, Obesity, PCOS. Screen BP/Hba1c/Lipids.
  • Mental Health: Depression, Anxiety, Suicide risk.
  • Autoimmune: IBD (Crohn's/Colitis), Inflammatory Arthritis.
  • Other: Acne, Pilonidal Sinus, Dissecting Scalp Cellulitis (Follicular Occlusion Tetrad).

Patient Advice
  • Not infectious
  • Not due to poor hygiene
  • Warm compresses for flares
  • Avoid shaving affected area
Screening Checklist
  • Smoking status
  • BMI / Waist circumference
  • Depression/Anxiety (PHQ9)
  • HbA1c & Lipids
  • PCOS symptoms (females)
