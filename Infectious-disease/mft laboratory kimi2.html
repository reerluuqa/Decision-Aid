B
10ml Monovette with BORIC ACID
CRITICAL: Use boric acid container

How to Collect:

  1. Patient cleans genital area
  2. Pass initial urine into toilet
  3. Collect midstream into container (fill to line)
  4. Refrigerate if delay >4 hours
Dipstick first: Only send if leucocytes OR nitrites positive, or clinical concern

WILL BE REJECTED:

  • Catheter tip (send urine from bag/port instead)
  • Specimen >48 hours old
  • No patient ID or mismatch
  • Form not filled correctly
Blood Cultures (Bacteriology)
A
Aerobic Bottle (Silver/Blue Top)
8-10ml for adults
An
Anaerobic Bottle (Purple Top)
8-10ml for adults

How to Collect:

  1. Clean skin with alcohol, allow to dry
  2. Take blood BEFORE antibiotics
  3. Fill bottles in order: Aerobic first, then Anaerobic
  4. Fill to 8-10ml mark (not below minimum line)
  5. Mix gently (don't shake vigorously)
CRITICAL: DO NOT REFRIGERATE Incubate within 4 hours of collection

WILL BE REJECTED:

  • Bottle past expiry date or damaged
  • Volume below minimum line
  • Bulging/broken seal (may indicate contamination)
  • No patient ID/wrong bottle
  • Refrigerated or delayed >4 hours
Clotted Blood (Serology/Virology)
Red Top Clotted Blood Tube
6ml adult, 2ml minimum

How to Collect:

  1. Standard venepuncture technique
  2. Allow to clot upright for 30-60 minutes
  3. DO NOT shake or invert
  4. Keep at room temperature
Tests: Hepatitis serology, HIV, CMV IgG/IgM, Measles, etc.

WILL BE REJECTED:

  • Haemolysed sample (pink/red serum)
  • Insufficient volume (<2ml)
  • Wrong tube type (EDTA, heparin)
  • No patient ID or mismatch
  • Sample >48 hours old
EDTA Blood (Molecular/PCR)
E
Purple Top EDTA Tube
6ml adult, 3ml minimum

How to Collect:

  1. Standard venepuncture
  2. GENTLY invert tube 8-10 times immediately
  3. Keep at room temperature
  4. Send to lab within 6 hours (can refrigerate overnight)
Tests: HIV viral load, CMV PCR, HBV DNA, BKV, etc.

WILL BE REJECTED:

  • Clotted sample (inadequate mixing)
  • Insufficient volume (<3ml)
  • Wrong tube type (clotted, heparin)
  • Haemolysed sample
  • Delayed >24 hours (not processed in time)
Swabs (General)
ACCEPTED: Liquid eSwabs (1ml fluid)
REJECTED: Dry swabs, charcoal swabs (except specific requests)

General Rules:

  1. Use aseptic technique
  2. Sample the active lesion/deepest part
  3. DO NOT squeeze out the liquid from eSwab
  4. Ensure visible fluid in tube (>1ml)
Swab Types:
• Wound: eSwab, sample pus not surface
• Throat: eSwab, tonsillar area
• Nose: eSwab, anterior nares
• MRSA Screen: Double-headed swab (nose + groin)

WILL BE REJECTED:

  • Dry swabs (no visible liquid)
  • Fluid <1ml in eSwab
  • Charcoal swabs (unless specified)
  • Wrong swab site for test
  • No patient ID
Faeces/Stool Samples
Clean, dry universal container
No preservative needed for routine tests

How to Collect:

  1. Pass into clean, dry bedpan (no soap/disinfectant residue)
  2. Transfer 2g (pea-sized) or 2ml to container
  3. Don't mix with urine
  4. Keep at room temperature, refrigerate if delay
Tests: Culture, C.diff, OCP (if requested)
NOTE: Formed stools will be REJECTED for C.diff testing

WILL BE REJECTED:

  • Formed stool for C.diff (must be loose/liquid)
  • Specimen in dirty/contaminated container
  • Sample >48 hours old
  • Multiple samples in same 24hrs (unless specific request)
  • No patient ID
TOP 10 REJECTION REASONS
  1. Wrong bottle/container type
  2. No patient ID or mismatch between form & sample
  3. Insufficient volume
  4. Haemolysed clotted blood
  5. Clotted EDTA blood
  6. Dry swabs (no visible fluid)
  7. Formed stool for C.diff
  8. Refrigerated blood cultures
  9. Sample >48 hours old
  10. Leaking or damaged container
