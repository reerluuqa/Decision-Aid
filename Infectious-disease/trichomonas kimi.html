Trichomoniasis Management

Interactive Clinical Decision Support Algorithm | NICE CKS Guidelines (December 2024)

Assessment

History taking and physical examination

1History Taking

For Women:

  • Ask about vaginal discharge (70% symptomatic), vulval itching, dysuria, or offensive odour
  • Note discharge characteristics: frothy, yellow-green in 10-30% cases
  • Up to 50% of women are asymptomatic
  • Sexual history: new partners, number in last year, condom use, previous STIs
50% asymptomatic Pregnancy status critical

For Men:

  • Ask about urethral discharge (20-60% symptomatic) and/or dysuria
  • About 15-50% of men are asymptomatic
  • Symptoms typically mild: urinary frequency, urethral irritation
  • Rarely: copious purulent discharge, prostatitis

2Physical Examination

Women (if GUM referral declined/not possible):

  • Abdominal palpation: assess for tenderness or mass
  • Vulval inspection: lesions, discharge, ulceration, inflammation
  • Speculum examination: visualize cervix and vagina
    • Classic finding: yellow-green frothy discharge with fishy odour
    • Strawberry cervix in 5-15% cases
  • Vaginal pH testing: normal 3.5-4.5, >4.5 suggests trichomoniasis
Offer chaperone 5-15% have no abnormalities

Men:

  • Usually no signs even with symptoms
  • Rarely: balanitis/balanoposthitis

3Risk Factors

Key Risk Factors:

  • Current gonorrhoea or chlamydia infection (women)
  • Two or more sexual partners in previous year
  • Lack of consistent condom use
  • Bacterial vaginosis
  • Black Caribbean/black ethnicity (higher prevalence)
Always offer: Tests for chlamydia, gonorrhoea, HIV, syphilis, and hepatitis (if at high risk)

Diagnosis

Laboratory confirmation and differential diagnosis

1Sample Collection

Women:

  • High vaginal swab from posterior fornix
  • Transport in Amies medium with charcoal
  • Refrigerate at 4°C if delay >2 hours (max 48 hours)
  • Mark request as "suspected trichomoniasis"
  • Alternative: self-administered vaginal swab for NAAT

Men:

  • Urethral swab or self-taken penile-meatal swab for NAAT
  • Note: Results may be less reliable than GUM clinic testing

2Diagnostic Tests

Primary Test: Nucleic Acid Amplification Testing (NAAT)

  • Gold standard - highest sensitivity and specificity
  • Can be performed on vaginal or urethral swabs
  • Results typically available in 24-48 hours
Preferred Method

Microscopy (wet mount):

  • Direct visualization of motile trichomonads
  • Sensitivity: 51-65% (lower than NAAT)
  • Requires immediate examination
  • Not routinely performed in all labs

Culture:

  • More sensitive than microscopy
  • Results take 3-7 days
  • Limited availability

3Differential Diagnosis

Women - Other Causes of Vaginal Discharge:

  • Infective: Bacterial vaginosis, vulvovaginal candidiasis, chlamydia, gonorrhoea, PID
  • Non-infective: Retained foreign body, irritant/allergic vaginitis, genitourinary syndrome of menopause
  • Serious: Gynaecological cancers, fistula

Men - Other Causes of Urethritis:

  • Infective: Gonorrhoea, chlamydia, HSV, Mycoplasma genitalium, UTI
  • Non-infective: Trauma, irritation from soaps/spermicides
Important: Consider co-infection with other STIs in all cases

Treatment

Antimicrobial therapy and partner management

1First-Line Treatment

Metronidazole

Dosing Regimens:

Adults (non-pregnant, not breastfeeding):
• 400-500 mg PO twice daily for 7 days
• OR 2 g PO as a single dose
Pregnancy & Breastfeeding:
• 400 mg PO twice daily for 7 days only
• Avoid high-dose (2g) regimen
HIV-positive individuals:
• 500 mg PO twice daily for 7 days
Cure Rate: 90-95% Pregnancy: 400mg BD only Avoid alcohol during treatment

2Partner Management

Critical Steps:

  • Treat current partner(s) simultaneously
  • Treat partner(s) from within the 4-week period prior to presentation
  • Contact tracing: arrange via GUM clinic or health advisor
  • Ensure partner(s) receive same treatment regimen
Partner treatment essential 4-week look-back period

Sexual Abstinence Advice:

  • Abstain from sexual intercourse for at least 1 week after starting treatment
  • Continue abstinence until both patient and partner(s) have completed treatment and follow-up
  • Use condoms consistently after resuming sexual activity

3Special Populations

Pregnant Women:

  • Symptomatic: 400 mg PO twice daily for 7 days
  • Asymptomatic: Seek specialist GUM advice
  • Inform obstetrician and midwife of diagnosis
  • Potential complications: preterm delivery, low birthweight
Specialist advice for asymptomatic

Breastfeeding Women:

  • 400 mg PO twice daily for 7 days
  • Metronidazole enters breast milk and may affect taste
  • Consider timing doses to minimize infant exposure

Children & Young People (<16 years):

  • Low threshold for GUM/paediatric discussion
  • Follow local child safeguarding procedures
  • Consider possibility of child maltreatment
Safeguarding alert

4Metronidazole Important Information

Contraindications:

  • Known metronidazole or nitroimidazole hypersensitivity
  • Cockayne syndrome (severe hepatotoxicity risk)

Key Interactions:

  • Alcohol: Disulfiram-like reaction - avoid during treatment and 48 hours after
  • Warfarin: Enhanced anticoagulant effect - monitor INR
  • Lithium: Levels may increase - monitor levels and toxicity
  • Phenytoin/Phenobarbital: Increased metabolism - may need higher dose

Follow-up

Monitoring, test of cure, and prevention

1Arrange Follow-up

Purpose:

  • Review for persistent symptoms (treatment failure indicator)
  • Confirm contact tracing completed
  • Discuss STI screen results
  • Provide sexual health advice and prevention education

Timing:

  • Schedule 1-2 weeks after treatment completion
  • Earlier if symptoms persist or worsen
  • Ensure patient has completed full course

2Test of Cure

Test of Cure Indications:

  • ONLY if patient remains symptomatic after treatment
  • If symptoms recur after treatment
  • If poor treatment adherence suspected
  • NOT ROUTINELY REQUIRED for asymptomatic patients

Test of Cure Protocol:

  • Wait at least 48 hours after completing treatment before re-testing
  • Use NAAT for confirmation
  • Consider repeat treatment if positive
  • Seek GUM specialist advice if second course fails

3Treatment Failure

If Symptoms Persist After First-line Treatment:

  • Check adherence: Did patient complete full course?
  • Exclude vomiting: If medication vomited within 2 hours
  • Exclude reinfection: Check partner(s) treated simultaneously
  • Reconsider diagnosis: Rule out other causes (see Differential Dx)
  • Repeat testing: NAAT at least 48 hours after treatment

Second-Line Treatment:

Repeat 7-day course of metronidazole 400-500 mg BD
• Use same dose as initial treatment
• Ensure better adherence counseling
• Consider directly observed therapy if available

Specialist Referral Indicators:

  • Failed second-line treatment
  • Failed high-dose (2g) single-dose regimen
  • Pregnant/breastfeeding with persistent infection
  • Confirmed metronidazole allergy
  • Complex or complicated cases

4Prevention & Education

Recommendations for Patients:

  • Consistent condom use to reduce STI risk
  • Regular STI screening if at risk
  • Notify sexual partners of positive result
  • Avoid sexual activity until treatment completed
  • Follow-up if symptoms persist or recur

Complications to Discuss:

  • Women: PID, infertility, increased HIV transmission risk, cervical cancer association
  • Men: Prostatitis, infertility, increased HIV transmission risk
  • Pregnancy: Preterm delivery, low birthweight
Serious complications possible

Key Follow-up Success Criteria:

  • ✓ Symptoms resolved
  • ✓ Partner(s) treated
  • ✓ STI screening completed
  • ✓ Prevention advice provided
  • ✓ Patient knows when to return if symptoms recur
