Primary Care Sample Collection Guide

This guide provides information on specimen collection for microbiology tests in primary care. It includes the appropriate collection medium, collection technique, and common reasons for rejection.

Important Note

This is a general guide. Always verify specific requirements with your local laboratory as protocols may vary.

Urine Tests

Common
Mid-Stream Urine (MSU)
Catheter Specimen
Paediatric Urine
Container
Collection
Rejection Reasons

Required Container

  • Sterile universal container (borosilicate glass or plastic)
  • Red-top boric acid preservative container if delayed transport (>4 hours)
  • Minimum volume: 5-10ml

Collection Instructions

  • Label container with patient details, date, and time of collection
  • Provide patient with a sterile container and written instructions
  • Instruct patient to clean genital area with soap and water
  • Patient should begin urinating into toilet, then collect mid-stream portion
  • Avoid touching inside of container or lid
  • Secure lid tightly after collection

Common Rejection Reasons

  • Insufficient volume (<5ml)
  • Leaking container
  • Incorrect labeling or unlabeled specimen
  • Specimen older than 24 hours (unless in preservative)
  • Contamination with pubic hair or fecal material
  • Specimen collected from inappropriate site

Rejection Reasons

Required Container

  • Paediatric sterile urine collection bag or sterile universal container
  • Red-top boric acid preservative container if delayed transport (>4 hours)
  • Minimum volume: 1-2ml

Collection Instructions

Swab Tests

Common
Wound Swab
Throat Swab
Eye Swab
Ear Swab
Nasal Swab (MRSA)
Container
Collection
Rejection Reasons

Required Container

  • Standard transport medium swab (e.g., Amies, Stuart)
  • For anaerobic culture: anaerobic transport swab
  • For viral PCR: viral transport medium (VTM) swab

Collection Instructions

  • Label swab with patient details, date, and time of collection
  • Clean wound area with saline (not antiseptic)
  • Remove exudate or crust with sterile gauze
  • Rotate swab over area of infection, applying enough pressure to obtain cellular material
  • For deep wounds, use a second swab or tissue sample if possible
  • Place swab in transport medium, break off swab stick, and secure cap

Common Rejection Reasons

  • Dry swab (no transport medium)
  • Swab broken in transport medium
  • Incorrect labeling or unlabeled specimen
  • Specimen older than 48 hours
  • Insufficient material on swab
  • Wrong transport medium for requested test

Sputum Tests

Common
Routine Sputum Culture
Tuberculosis (AFB)
Fungal Culture
Container
Collection
Rejection Reasons

Required Container

  • Sterile, wide-mouth, screw-capped container
  • Minimum volume: 2-5ml
  • Container should be leak-proof

Collection Instructions

  • Label container with patient details, date, and time of collection
  • Collect first morning specimen if possible
  • Rinse mouth with water before collection
  • Instruct patient to cough deeply to produce sputum from lungs
  • Avoid saliva or postnasal drip
  • Secure lid tightly after collection

Common Rejection Reasons

  • Saliva specimen (no sputum)
  • Insufficient volume (<2ml)
  • Leaking container
  • Incorrect labeling or unlabeled specimen
  • Specimen older than 24 hours
  • Contamination with food particles or oral flora

  • Sterile, wide-mouth, screw-capped container
  • Minimum volume: 2-5ml
  • Container should be leak-proof
  • No preservatives or fixatives

Collection Instructions

  • Label container with patient details, date, and time of collection
  • Collect stool in a clean, dry container or bedpan
  • Avoid contamination with urine or toilet water
  • Use spoon attached to container lid to transfer specimen
  • Aim for bloody, mucoid, or liquid portions
  • Secure lid tightly after collection

  • Insufficient volume (<2ml)
  • Leaking container
  • Incorrect labeling or unlabeled specimen
  • Specimen older than 24 hours (unless refrigerated)
  • Contamination with urine or toilet water
  • Dried specimen

Blood Tests

Urgent
Blood Culture
Serology (Antibodies)
Blood PCR
Container
Collection
Rejection Reasons

Required Container

  • Blood culture bottles (aerobic and anaerobic)
  • Pediatric bottles for children
  • Volume: 5-10ml per bottle (adults), 1-3ml per bottle (children)

Collection Instructions

  • Label bottles with patient details, date, and time of collection
  • Perform strict aseptic technique
  • Clean skin with 70% isopropyl alcohol or chlorhexidine
  • Allow skin to dry completely before venipuncture
  • Collect 2 sets from different sites for optimal detection
  • Draw blood into bottles without changing needles
  • Mix bottles gently after collection
  • Transport to lab immediately - do not refrigerate

Common Rejection Reasons

  • Insufficient volume
  • Incorrect labeling or unlabeled specimen
  • Leaking bottle
  • Contamination during collection
  • Delayed transport (>2 hours)
  • Refrigerated specimen
Container
Collection
Rejection Reasons

Required Container

  • Serum separator tube (SST) or plain red-top tube
  • Minimum volume: 5-10ml
  • For some tests: specific tube may be required

Collection Instructions

  • Label tube with patient details, date, and time of collection
  • Perform standard venipuncture technique
  • Allow blood to clot if using plain tube (30 minutes at room temperature)
  • Centrifuge if possible to separate serum
  • Transport to lab within 24 hours
  • If transport delayed, refrigerate (2-8C)

Common Rejection Reasons

  • Insufficient volume
  • Incorrect labeling or unlabeled specimen
  • Hemolysed specimen
  • Specimen older than 7 days (unless frozen)
  • Wrong tube type for requested test

Quick Tips

  • Always label specimens at the time of collection, not before
  • Ensure patient identifiers match on request form and specimen
  • Include clinical information on request form to guide testing
  • Transport specimens to the lab promptly to ensure viability
  • Store specimens appropriately if transport is delayed
