RED FLAGS - Immediate Actions

  • Secondary bacterial infection (impetigo, furunculosis) - consider antibiotics
  • Severe hypersensitivity reaction to louse feces - extensive rash with lymphadenopathy
  • Heavy infestation (>100 lice) - may indicate immunosuppression
  • Treatment failure after 3 properly applied treatments - consider resistance

Transmission

  • Requires head-to-head contact
  • Cannot jump, fly, or swim
  • Not transmitted via pets
  • Unlikely via shared items (hats, combs, pillows)
  • Survive only 1-2 days off human host

FIRST-LINE TREATMENT OPTIONS

1. Wet Combing (Bug Buster¬Æ Kit)

Regimen: Days 1, 5, 9, 13

Success Rate: 38-52%

Best for: Pregnancy, breastfeeding, children 6mo-2y, asthma, eczema

2. Physical Insecticides

Dimeticone 4% (Hedrin¬Æ)

Success Rate: 70-97%

Resistance: Unlikely (physical action)

Treatment Failure

  • Check: Complete course, correct technique, sufficient volume
  • Assess all household contacts
  • Consider resistance (especially if malathion used)
  • Switch treatment class or repeat wet combing

Patient Education

  • Children can attend school during treatment
  • No preference for clean/dirty hair
  • No special cleaning of clothes/bedding needed
  • Weekly screening for primary school children
  • Treat all affected household members same day
