Influenza Seasonal

Circulating: YES
ADMISSION CRITERIA (Severe)

Complicated Influenza - Refer Immediately

  • ✅ Signs/Symptoms requiring hospital admission
  • ✅ Lower respiratory tract infection (hypoxaemia, dyspnoea)
  • ✅ Central nervous system involvement
  • ✅ Significant exacerbation of underlying condition
Action: Do not delay antivirals for lab confirmation in these cases.
UNCOMPLICATED INFLUENZA (Primary Care)

Manage at home unless 'At Risk'

  • ✅ Coryza & Nasal discharge
  • ✅ Fever (typically 38–40°C)
  • ✅ Headache, Malaise, Myalgia
  • ✅ Sore throat, Cough
Note: Onset is abrupt (approx 2 days post-exposure).
Key Differential Diagnoses

Common Cold

More nasal problems; fever, fatigue, and myalgia are less common/severe.

COVID-19

Similar signs. Co-infection increases mortality risk. Rule out with PCR/LFD.

Meningitis

Baby: High fever, bulging fontanelle. Adult: Stiff neck, photophobia, purpuric rash.

The Golden Window

Treatment must be started within 48 hours of symptom onset (36 hours for Zanamivir in children).

Note: Starting treatment after this window is an off-label use and requires clinical judgement.

Oseltamivir (Oral)
First Line Pregnancy Safe
Adults 75 mg twice daily for 5 days
Children Dose by weight (See BNF/BNFC)
Neonates 3 mg/kg twice daily (Licensed)

Preferred over Zanamivir for pregnant women, respiratory disease, or patients who struggle with inhalers.

Zanamivir (Inhaled)
Caution: Asthma/COPD
Adults 2 inhalations (10mg) twice daily for 5 days
Children (5+ yrs) Same as adults

Contraindicated in patients with asthma or COPD. Monitor closely if used.

General Self-Care Advice

Rest: Advise plenty of rest and sleep.

Hydration: Drink plenty of fluids to prevent dehydration.

Analgesia: Paracetamol or Ibuprofen for fever/pain.

Hygiene: Use tissues to catch germs, bin tissues, wash hands.

Post-Exposure Prophylaxis (PEP)

Note: Prophylaxis is not a replacement for immunization.

Indication for Antiviral Prophylaxis

Consider if all the following apply:

  • Close contact with a clinically diagnosed case.
  • The contact is in an 'at risk' group.
  • Influenza is circulating in the community.
  • The contact has not been effectively immunized (or if exposed before immunity develops).
Dosage for Prophylaxis
Drug Duration
Oseltamivir Once daily for 10 days (or up to 6 weeks in outbreaks)
Zanamivir Once daily for 10 days
