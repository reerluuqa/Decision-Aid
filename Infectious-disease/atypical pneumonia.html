Atypical Pneumonia

Last Updated: Sep 10, 2024 | BMJ Best Practice Source

üí°
Quick Definition: Community-acquired infection by organisms difficult to detect on Gram stain (e.g., Mycoplasma, Chlamydophila, Legionella). Constitutional symptoms often outweigh respiratory findings.

Epidemiology & Risk Factors

ü¶† Common Pathogens

  • Mycoplasma pneumoniae (Most common in children/young adults, boarding schools)
  • Chlamydophila pneumoniae
  • Legionella pneumophila (Severe, water-source exposure)
  • Coxiella burnetii (Zoonotic/Livestock)

Clinical Presentation & Diagnosis

Key Symptoms

  • ‚úÖ Persistent, dry cough (Slow onset)
  • ‚úÖ Headache
  • ‚úÖ Malaise
  • ‚úÖ Low-grade fever (often)
  • ‚úÖ Pharyngitis/Hoarseness

üö® Red Flags / Severity

  • Severe Disease: Suggests Legionella
  • ICU Indicators: Saturation < 92% (in air), Confusion, Urea > 19.8 mg/dL
  • Legionella specific: Non-bloody diarrhea, Hyponatremia, Elevated LFTs
  • CNS: Seizures, confusion (rare)

Physical Exam

  • General: Mild signs vs. symptoms
  • Lungs: Rales/Crepitations (often minimal)
  • Rare Signs: Bullous myringitis (M. pneumoniae)
  • Skin: Maculopapular rash (Mycoplasma)

üî¨ Key Investigations (1st Line)

Test Expected Result / Significance
Chest X-Ray Infiltrates (often more extensive than exam suggests)
Oxygen Saturation Low (<90-92%) indicates severity -> Admit
WBC Count Slightly elevated (<13,000) or Normal
LFTs (AST/ALT) Elevated (Suggests Mycoplasma or Legionella)
RT-PCR (SARS-CoV-2) Rule out COVID-19 (mandatory in many settings)

Management & Treatment

üöë
Severe Community-Acquired Pneumonia Detected:
Admit to Hospital. Start Beta-Lactam + Macrolide/Fluoroquinolone.
üè†
Mild/Outpatient Presentation:
Treat as outpatient with Macrolide or Doxycycline.

1st Line: Outpatient (Mild/Mod)

Option A: Macrolide
  • Azithromycin: 500mg Day 1, then 250mg Days 2-5
  • Clarithromycin: 500mg BD x 14-21 days
  • Erythromycin: 500mg QID x 14-21 days
Option B: Tetracycline
  • Doxycycline: 100mg BD x 14 days
  • Note: First-line for zoonotic atypicals (Psittacosis, Q fever)
‚Üì

Severe Disease / Hospitalization

Add: Beta-Lactam Antibiotic (e.g., Ceftriaxone/Ampicillin-Sulbactam)

PLUS Macrolide (above) OR Fluoroquinolone:

  • Levofloxacin: 750mg daily x 5 days
  • Moxifloxacin: 400mg daily x 7-14 days

1st Line: Outpatient

Macrolide (Preferred)
  • Azithromycin (Child): 10mg/kg Day 1, then 5mg/kg Days 2-5
  • Erythromycin: 40mg/kg/day divided q6h
  • Clarithromycin: 7.5mg/kg BD

Severe Disease / Hospitalization

Add: Beta-Lactam Antibiotic

Severe / Macrolide Resistance: Consider Fluoroquinolone (Levofloxacin)
Only if benefits outweigh risks & no other options.

Safety & Emerging

‚ö†Ô∏è Fluoroquinolone Warning

Systemic fluoroquinolones (Levofloxacin, Moxifloxacin) may cause disabling, potentially irreversible adverse events:

  • Tendinopathy / Tendon Rupture
  • Peripheral Neuropathy
  • CNS Effects (Seizures, Psychosis)
  • Aortic Aneurysm / Dissection

Restrict to serious, life-threatening infections where no alternative exists.

üíâ Emerging Therapy

Lefamulin:

First-in-class pleuromutilin. Covers typical and atypical pathogens (incl. MRSA).
Warning: QT interval prolongation.

üíä Corticosteroids

Severe CAP: May reduce time to clinical stability. Consider in refractory septic shock.
Non-Severe: Not recommended.

Key Differentials

Condition Differentiating Factor
COVID-19 Contact history, Positive RT-PCR. Imaging often indistinguishable.
Typical Bacterial Pneumonia Acute onset, high fever, pleuritic pain, purulent sputum. Positive Gram stain/Culture.
Pulmonary Embolism No fever, no response to antibiotics. Positive V/Q scan or CTPA.
Tuberculosis Immunosuppression, chronic course, night sweats. AFB stain positive.
