Bed Bugs Clinical Quick Scan

Updated: Jul 12, 2024

RED FLAG: Anaphylaxis

Rare but Life-Threatening

  • Widespread urticaria (wheals)
  • Wheezing / Stridor
  • Hypotension

Immediate Action

Administer Adrenaline (IM)

Supportive care: Oxygen, IV fluids, secure airway.

Clinical Presentation & Diagnosis

Key Presentation

Pruritic Painless Bite

Visual Pattern Cue:

Irregular Linear/Curved Papules

  • Size: 1-5 mm erythematous papules.
  • Location: Exposed skin during sleep (face, neck, arms, shoulders).
  • Timing: New lesions noted in the morning; bites painless.
  • Center: Small haemorrhagic punctum may be visible.

Risk Factors & History

Diagnosis is clinical. Lab tests rarely needed.

Sign/Question Significance
Recent Travel High Risk
High-turnover housing (Hotels, Hostels) High Risk
Second-hand furniture Common Source
Sighting bugs / "Ink dots" on sheets Confirmatory

Scabies: Burrows in interdigital spaces, nocturnal itch, family history. Skin scrape positive.

Urticaria: More blanchable, shorter duration, triggers (food/drug).

Fleas/Chiggers: Usually lower legs only.

Management Algorithm

1. Symptomatic Relief

Goal: Minimize pruritus. Lesions resolve in 1-2 weeks if re-exposure stops.

First Line (Mild)

  • Oral Antihistamine: Hydroxyzine (sedative effect helps).
  • Topical Antipruritic: Pramocaine/Camphor/Calamine or Doxepin cream.

Second Line (Discomfort)

  • Topical Corticosteroid: Triamcinolone or Hydrocortisone (low-to-mid potency).
  • Max duration: 2 weeks.

Severe / Widespread

  • Systemic Corticosteroid: Prednisolone 40-60mg daily (7-14 days).

2. Eradication (Essential)

Re-infestation will occur without environmental control. Professional extermination usually required.

Heat Protocol (Laundering)

  • Wash in water > 55°C (130°F)
  • OR Dry in hot air for ≥ 30 mins

Alternative: Freezing

  • -20°C (-5°F) for 8 hours per 2.5kg of item.

Professional Measures

  • Vacuum furniture/mattresses.
  • Seal cracks in walls/furniture.
  • Pest control trained in insecticide resistance.
Complications & Special Cases

Anemia

Rare. Caused by massive infestations. Check CBC if clinical suspicion.

Asthma

Induced by bites. Treat with bronchodilators/corticosteroids.

Infection Risk

Secondary infection (S. aureus/S. pyogenes) due to scratching.

Rx: Cefalexin or Mupirocin
Note: Vector transmission of Hep B/HIV is highly unlikely / theoretical. No evidence of disease transmission role.
