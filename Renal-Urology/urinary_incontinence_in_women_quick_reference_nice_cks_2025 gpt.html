Urinary Incontinence in Women

Clean, rapid algorithm for assessment and management in clinic. Built from NICE CKS (last revised Jan 2025). Avoid dark‑mode; print-friendly.

Includes Red Flags & Quick Flow

At‑a‑Glance Types

Stress UI Leakage on effort/cough/sneeze.

Urgency UI / OAB Leakage with urgency ± frequency/nocturia.

Mixed UI Combination of stress and urgency.

Other Continuous (consider fistula/diverticulum), Overflow.

Initial Assessment

  • History → classify type (Stress / Urgency(OAB) / Mixed / Other).
  • Bladder diary ≥ 3 days covering work & leisure days.
  • Urinalysis in all; culture if recurrent UTI with +leucocytes & nitrites.
  • Pelvic exam (prolapse, atrophy, diverticulum); assess pelvic floor contraction (Oxford scale).
  • Consider renal function if obstruction suspected.

Red Flags – Refer

  • Suspected cancer pathway (2‑week): ≥45y with visible haematuria (unexplained or persistent post‑UTI); ≥60y with non‑visible haematuria + dysuria or raised WCC.
  • Urgent/Non‑urgent specialist: persistent bladder/urethral pain, voiding difficulty, chronic retention, palpable post‑void bladder, clinically benign pelvic mass, fecal incontinence, suspected neuro disease or urogenital fistula, prior incontinence/pelvic cancer surgery or radiation; recurrent unexplained UTIs in ≥60y (consider non‑urgent cancer referral).

Initial Management (All Types)

  • Weight loss if BMI > 30.
  • Adjust fluids (avoid too high/low); reduce caffeine.
  • Smoking cessation.
  • Manage reversible contributors: constipation, uncontrolled diabetes.
  • Address complications: anxiety/depression, fall risk, sleep disturbance.
Containment products only for temporary coping, adjunct to treatment, or long‑term after options explored; review at least annually (6‑monthly if >75y).

Stress UI – Management

  • First‑line: ≥3 months supervised PFMT (≥8 contractions, ≥3×/day).
  • If persistent → refer (urogyn/gynae/urology) to consider colposuspension, autologous rectus fascial sling, retropubic mid‑urethral mesh sling or urethral bulking.
  • If surgery unsuitable/declined → duloxetine (discuss AEs; taper if stopping).

Urgency UI / Overactive Bladder

  • First‑line: ≥6 weeks bladder training; continue if helpful.
  • Consider antimuscarinic with lowest acquisition cost (e.g., oxybutynin IR, tolterodine IR, darifenacin OD). Avoid oxybutynin IR in frail older women.
  • If contraindicated/ineffective/not tolerated → β3‑agonist (mirabegron or vibegron per local formulary).
  • Other oral options (if switching/adjusting): fesoterodine, oxybutynin ER or transdermal, propiverine (IR/ER), solifenacin, tolterodine ER, trospium (IR/ER).
  • Do not offer: flavoxate, propantheline, imipramine.
  • If refractory or patient prefers → specialist referral (botulinum toxin A, sacral neuromodulation, augmentation cystoplasty, urinary diversion).
Adjuncts: topical vaginal oestrogen for atrophy; desmopressin for troublesome nocturia in carefully selected women.

Mixed UI – Management

Treat the predominant symptom:

  • Predominant stress → follow Stress UI pathway before surgery; consider OAB meds if needed.
  • Predominant urgency → follow Urgency/OAB pathway.

Review & Monitoring

  • Medicines: review ~4 weeks after start/change; then annually (6‑monthly if >75y).
  • Containment products (if long‑term): yearly review covering continence, skin integrity, changes to comorbidities/meds/BMI/mobility, and alternative options.

Prescribing Safety Highlights

  • Antimuscarinics: monitor AEs (dry mouth, constipation, cognitive effects in older/frail). Avoid oxybutynin IR in older women at risk of sudden decline.
  • β3‑agonists (mirabegron/vibegron): consider hypertension/contraindications per local formulary.
  • Duloxetine for SUI if surgery unsuitable: counsel on nausea, fatigue; avoid in hepatic impairment, severe renal impairment; taper to stop.
  • Desmopressin for nocturia: fluid restriction; monitor sodium (especially ≥65y); avoid in hyponatraemia risk states.

One‑Screen Summary Flow

1. Assess Classify type; UA for all; bladder diary ≥3 d; pelvic exam.
2. Exclude Red Flags Cancer pathway/urgent specialist as listed.
3. Universal Lifestyle; manage constipation/diabetes; address anxiety/falls/sleep.
4a. Stress UI PFMT ≥3 mo → if persistent: surgical referral ± duloxetine.
4b. Urgency/OAB Bladder training ≥6 wk → antimuscarinic → β3‑agonist → specialist.
4c. Mixed Treat predominant symptom (stress vs urgency).
5. Adjuncts Vaginal oestrogen for atrophy; desmopressin for selected nocturia.
6. Review 4‑week med review; annual long‑term review (6‑mo if >75y).
