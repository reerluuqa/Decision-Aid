üîç Causes of AKI

  • Hypovolaemia (diarrhoea, vomiting, bleeding, diuretics)
  • Cardiac failure, CCF, MI
  • Hepatic cirrhosis, nephrotic syndrome
  • Sepsis, SIRS, anaphylaxis
  • Drugs (ACEi, ARBs, NSAIDs, diuretics)
  • Acute Tubular Necrosis (ischaemia, toxins)
  • Glomerulonephritis (RPGN, IgA, Lupus)
  • Interstitial nephritis (drugs, infections)
  • Atheroembolic disease (post-procedure)
  • Rhabdomyolysis, malignant hypertension
  • Bladder outlet obstruction (BPH, cancer)
  • Ureteric obstruction (stones, strictures, malignancy)
  • Neurogenic bladder, urethral stricture
  • Pregnancy-related obstruction

üíä STOP AKI - Drug Review

Immediate action: Review and withhold nephrotoxic medications

D

Diuretics

Stop: Furosemide, Bendroflumethiazide, Spironolactone

Monitor electrolytes closely
A

ACE Inhibitors

Stop: Lisinopril, Ramipril, Enalapril, Perindopril

Hold for 48h post-contrast
M

Metformin

STOP immediately if eGFR <30 or acute illness

Risk of lactic acidosis
N

NSAIDs

Stop: Ibuprofen, Naproxen, Diclofenac, Aspirin

Avoid all NSAIDs including topical

1
