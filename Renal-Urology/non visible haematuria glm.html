AUA Risk Stratification (3 Categories)

Use age, smoking history, and RBC count to determine the workup intensity.

Low Risk

  • Women: Age <50 years
  • Men: Age <40 years
  • Never smoker or <10 pack-years
  • 3-10 RBC/HPF on single microscopy
  • No risk factors for urothelial cancer
Recommended Action

Repeat urine microscopy in 6 months.

If persistent, reclassify as Intermediate/High.

Intermediate Risk

  • Women: Age 50-59 years
  • Men: Age 40-59 years
  • 10-30 pack-year smoking history
  • 11-25 RBC/HPF
  • Low risk patient with persistent 3-10 RBC on repeat
  • Additional risk factors (e.g., irritative LUTS, pelvic radiation)
Recommended Action

Renal Ultrasound + Cystoscopy.

High Risk

  • Men & Women: Age ≥60 years
  • >30 pack-year smoking history
  • >25 RBC/HPF
  • History of visible haematuria
Recommended Action

Multi-phasic CT Urography + Cystoscopy.

Diagnostic Testing Logic

Step 1: Confirmation & Basic Workup

Urine Microscopy (Gold Standard)

Confirms NVH (≥3 RBC/HPF). Checks for dysmorphic RBCs (Glomerular source) and casts.

History & Physical

Exclude menses, trauma, exercise. Assess smoking, occupational exposure, family history.
