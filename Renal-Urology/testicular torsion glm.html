Ultrasound with Doppler

Primary Goal: Confirm diagnosis if uncertain, BUT do not delay surgery for high suspicion.

  • Absent flow on affected side
  • Whirlpool sign (Twisted cord)
  • Increased Resistive Index (Arterial)

Power Doppler is more sensitive than Color Doppler.

Key Differentials

Surgical Management

Orchidopexy (Fixation)

Viability Present

If testis is viable after detorsion. Fix both testes (bilateral) to prevent recurrence.

Orchiectomy (Removal)

Non-Viable / Necrotic

If necrosis is confirmed. Must still fix contralateral testis.

Neonatal:

If torsion present at birth: Semi-elective exploration ± contralateral fixation.
