BMJ Best Practice Summary

MEDICAL EMERGENCY: Requires rapid evaluation to prevent glans loss.
Check for signs of necrosis/ischemia immediately.

Definition & Theory

Foreskin retracted behind glans, left in place causing vascular engorgement and oedema.

Key Risk Factors:
Iatrogenic (Catheter) Phimosis Poor Hygiene

Quick Diagnosis Checklist

History:
  • Penile Pain
  • Duration of retraction
  • Indwelling catheter?
  • Piercings/Trauma
Exam:
  • Swollen Glans
  • Constricting Band
  • Black Tissue? (Necrosis)
  • Non-pliable Glans

Management Algorithm Select Patient Status

1. Manual Reduction

Goal: Reduce swelling, replace foreskin.

  • Analgesia: Topical or Penile Block.
  • Adjuncts: Ice packs, compression, or fine granulated sugar (osmotic agent).
  • Technique: Pull phimotic ring down, push glans proximally.

2. Puncture (Dundee)

Use if Manual fails.

  • Multiple punctures (22G needle) in oedematous foreskin.
  • Allow fluid exudation.
  • Manual compression afterwards.
  • Adjunct: Hyaluronidase injection.

3. Surgical Reduction

Use if above fail.

  • Dorsal Slit: Incise phimotic ring dorsally.
  • Followed by Circumcision.
  • Requires Urology consult.

URGENT: Ischemia or Necrosis Detected

Patient requires immediate Urology involvement and operative management.

Emergency Surgery

  • Procedural sedation or General Anaesthesia.
  • Immediate invasive reduction.
  • Debridement of devitalized tissue.

Chronic Presentation (>24-48h)

Fibrotic ring likely. Manual reduction often unsuccessful.

Surgical Reduction & Circumcision

  • 1st Line: Surgical reduction.
  • Usually performed under GA.
  • Dorsal slit or formal circumcision.

1. Preparation

  • Provide analgesia (Lidocaine cream or dorsal nerve block).
  • Wrap penis in plastic, apply ice packs.
  • Apply liberal amounts of fine granulated sugar (osmotic) for 5-10 mins to reduce oedema.

2. The "Squeeze & Push"

  • Place thumbs on glans.
  • Place fingers on phimotic ring.
  • Squeeze glans to reduce oedema.
  • Push glans distally while pulling foreskin proximally.
Adjunct: Hyaluronidase can be injected into oedematous foreskin to degrade hyaluronic acid and reduce swelling faster.
  • Clean area with antiseptic.
  • Use 22-gauge hypodermic needle.
  • Make multiple punctures (approx 10-20) through the oedematous foreskin.
  • Gently milk fluid out of punctures.
  • Proceed with manual compression and reduction.

Indication: Failed conservative reduction or chronic paraphimosis.

  • Anesthetize penis (Block or GA).
  • Clamp phimotic ring vertically to crush tissue (haemostasis).
  • Use sharp scissors to cut through crushed tissue along midline (dorsally).
  • Release clamp, foreskin should spring open.
  • Suture edges with 4-0 chromic.
  • Plan for definitive circumcision later.
