Testicular Cancer – Quick Reference

Red Flags

Hard, non-transilluminating testicular mass
→ Urgent urology referral & scrotal US within 2 weeks
Acute testicular pain + swelling
→ Rule out torsion first (Doppler US)
Gynaecomastia, neck mass, lung/back pain
→ Metastatic disease until proven otherwise

Initial Actions

Scrotal US + tumour markers (AFP, hCG, LDH)
Inguinal (not scrotal) radical orchiectomy – pathological diagnosis
CT chest/abdomen/pelvis for staging

Stage-specific Rx

Seminoma

  • I: Surveillance
  • IIA/B ≤3 cm: RT or 3×BEP
  • IIB >3 cm: 3×BEP
  • IIC/III: 4×BEP

Non-seminoma

  • I (low risk): Surveillance
  • I (high risk): 1×BEP or RPLND
  • IIA/B: 3×BEP or RPLND
  • IIC/III: 4×BEP

Relapse / Salvage

Conventional: TIP or VeIP
High-dose: TI-CE + auto-HSCT
Late relapse (>2 y): Surgery preferred if resectable

Follow-up

  • Year 1: H&P + markers + imaging every 2–3 mo
  • Year 2: every 3–4 mo
  • Years 3–5: every 6 mo
  • Teach testicular self-exam (↑ contralateral risk ~2%)
