Red Flags All Ages

  • Suspected type 1 diabetes (same-day referral)
  • Urinary tract infection
  • Daytime symptoms with primary bedwetting
  • Secondary bedwetting (previously dry >6 months)
  • Signs of child maltreatment
  • Structural abnormalities on examination
  • Recurrent UTIs
‚úÖ

First-line Actions

  • Reassure: Not the child's fault
  • Adequate fluid intake (spread through day)
  • Regular toileting (4-7 times daily)
  • No fluid restriction before proven necessary
  • Positive reward systems for dry nights
  • No punishment for wetting

Assessment Priorities

  • Type of bedwetting (primary/secondary)
  • Daytime symptoms present?
  • Pattern: frequency, timing, volume
  • Fluid intake diary (2 weeks)
  • Sleep quality & arousal difficulties
  • Constipation assessment
  • Family history

Desmopressin Precautions

  • Fluid restriction: 1 hour before to 8 hours after dose
  • Stop if vomiting/diarrhea
  • Avoid NSAIDs
  • Monitor for hyponatremia
  • Not for long-term use without review
