Pre-Test Checklist

  • No active UTI (wait 6 wks)
  • No ejaculation (48 hrs)
  • No vigorous exercise (48 hrs)

Digital Rectal Examination (DRE)

Malignant Signs: Hard gland, palpable nodules, irregular surface.

Benign Signs: Smooth, firm, enlarged gland.

NOTE: A normal DRE does NOT exclude tumour.

Immediate Action:
Refer via suspected cancer pathway (2WW) if prostate feels malignant on DRE.

Cambridge Prognostic Groups (CPG)

Used for localized prostate cancer to determine mortality risk and management.

CPG 1

Low Risk

Criteria:

  • Gleason 6 (Grade Group 1)
  • PSA < 10
  • Stage T1–T2

10 yr Mortality: 1.9%

CPG 2

Intermediate

Criteria:

  • Gleason 3+4=7 (GG 2)
  • OR PSA 10–20
  • AND Stage T1–T2

10 yr Mortality: 4.2%

CPG 3

Intermediate

Criteria:

  • Gleason 3+4=7 (GG 2) & PSA 10–20
  • OR Gleason 4+3=7 (GG 3)

10 yr Mortality: 8%

CPG 4

High

Criteria (One of):

  • Gleason 8 (GG 4)
  • PSA > 20
  • Stage T3

10 yr Mortality: 13.5%

CPG 5

Very High

Criteria:

  • Two or more of: GG4, PSA>20, T3
  • OR Gleason 9–10 (GG 5)
  • OR Stage T4

10 yr Mortality: 31.9%

Primary Care Management

Setting: Shared Care (Initiated in Secondary Care).

  • Watchful Waiting: Annual PSA. No routine DRE if stable. Deferred hormone therapy.
  • Active Surveillance:
    • Yr 1: PSA q3-4mo, DRE @12mo, MRI @12-18mo.
    • Yr 2+: PSA q6mo, DRE q12mo.
  • Radical Treatment: PSA @6wks, then q6mo x 2yrs, then yearly.

Managing Complications in Primary Care

Hot Flushes

1st Line: Medroxyprogesterone 20mg/day (10wks).

2nd Line: Cyproterone acetate.

Sexual Dysfunction

Offer PDE-5 inhibitors (e.g., Sildenafil).

Refer for psychosexual counselling.

Osteoporosis Risk

Assess fracture risk if on androgen deprivation therapy.

Offer bisphosphonates if osteoporosis confirmed.

Prescribing Information

Dose: 20 mg per day for 10 weeks (off-label).

Contraindications: Active thromboembolism, liver disease, history of meningioma, breast cancer.

Caution: Asthma, depression, diabetes, epilepsy.

Adverse Effects: Weight gain, fluid retention, thrombosis. Stop if meningioma diagnosed.

Dose: 50 mg twice daily for 4 weeks.

Contraindications: Liver diseases, meningioma, existing thrombosis, wasting diseases.

Caution: Diabetes, Sickle cell anaemia, depression.

Adverse Effects: Hepatotoxicity (monitor LFTs), gynaecomastia, weight change.

Indication: Osteoporosis in men on androgen deprivation therapy.

Note: Not routinely offered to prevent osteoporosis.

Note: Can be considered for pain relief in hormone-refractory cancer if other analgesia failed.
