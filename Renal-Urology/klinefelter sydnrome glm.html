Klinefelter Syndrome

Ref: Jun 2024

Critical Diagnostic Cluster

Immediate suspicion warranted in males presenting with Small Testes + Infertility (Azoospermia). Do not wait for full phenotypic expression.

1:660
Prevalence in Males
Often Undiagnosed (~75%)
Core Definition
47,XXY

Sex chromosome aneuploidy. Extra X chromosome leads to primary hypogonadism and testicular degeneration.

Test Result in KS
Testosterone Low (or low-normal)

Karyotype 47,XXY (Definitive)
Presentation
  • Expressive Speech Delay: Slow to start talking (common 40%).
  • Developmental Delay: Learning disabilities (>75%), walking delays.
  • Tall Stature: Long legs (SHOX gene effect).
  • Behaviour: Shy, passive, or frustration tantrums.
Interventions
  • Speech Therapy: Early intervention for language.
  • Educational Support: Specialized teaching plans.
  • Monitor Growth: Check height centile shift.
Exam Flags
  • Small penis (10-25%)
  • Cryptorchidism (rare but diagnostic)
Presentation
  • Stalled Puberty: Starts normally then arrests (Tanner stage 5).
  • Small Testes: Firm/soft, volume <6mL (Almond size).
  • Gynaecomastia: Common (~1/3).
  • Body Composition: High abdominal fat, low muscle mass.
Action Required

Consider Testosterone Therapy if:

  • Testosterone fails to rise in late puberty (>14 yrs).
  • High LH/FSH levels present.
  • Psychosocial distress due to hypogonadism.
Investigate: Karyotype & Bone Age.
Primary Complaint

Infertility

90% are Azoospermic.

Prevalence: 10-15% of non-obstructive azoospermia cases.

  • Small Testes (<6mL bi-testicular).
  • Reduced facial/pubic hair.
  • Fatigue / Low Libido.
Management Options
Testosterone Replacement Therapy (TRT)

Lifelong therapy. Improves muscle mass, bone density, mood, and libido.

Fertility (TESE/mTESE)

Sperm extraction possible in ~50%. Best done in young adulthood before testicular fibrosis advances.
