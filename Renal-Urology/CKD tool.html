CKD Management Algorithm

Evidence-based guidelines for chronic kidney disease diagnosis and management

Assessment & Screening

Identify at-risk patients and initial investigations

Targeted Screening

  • Previous acute kidney injury (AKI)
  • Incidental dipstick findings (blood or protein)
  • Diabetes mellitus
  • Hypertension or cardiovascular disease
  • Long-term NSAID use
  • Prostatic hypertrophy or renal stones

Initial Investigations

  • Urea & Electrolytes (U&Es)
  • Full Blood Count (FBC) - especially if eGFR ≤30
  • Albumin-Creatinine Ratio (ACR)
  • Urine dipstick (specifically for blood)
  • Liver Function Tests (LFT) & Bone profile
  • Blood Pressure measurement

Key Practice Points

  • DO NOT dip urine for protein (send for ACR instead)
  • DO dip urine for BLOOD
  • If eGFR is low, repeat within 2 weeks to exclude AKI
Routine Screening ACR 3-70: Repeat early morning sample

Diagnosis & Classification

Confirm CKD and determine stage

Diagnostic Criteria

CKD is diagnosed if any of the following are present:

  • eGFR <60 ml/min on 2 occasions ≥3 months apart
  • ACR ≥3 mg/mmol (repeat early morning if 3-70)
  • Structural or genetic kidney disease

Risk Calculation

Calculate 5-year risk of renal replacement therapy:

  • Use 4-variable kidney failure risk equation
  • Download from NICE website (Implementation support)
  • Refer if 5-year risk >5%

CKD Classification (GFR Stages)

Stage eGFR (ml/min) Category Action Required
G1 >90 Normal/High Monitor if other evidence of kidney damage
G2 60-89 Mild reduction Monitor if other evidence of kidney damage
G3a 45-59 Mild-moderate reduction 6-monthly monitoring if ACR >30
G3b 30-44 Moderate-severe reduction 4-6 monthly monitoring
G4 15-29 Severe reduction 3-4 monthly monitoring, consider referral
G5 <15 Kidney failure Urgent referral, 3 monthly monitoring

Ultrasound Indications

  • eGFR <30 ml/min
  • Accelerated CKD (see below)
  • Visible or persistent non-visible haematuria
  • Symptoms suggesting obstruction
  • Age >20y with family history of polycystic kidneys

Treatment (Think ALPHA)

Comprehensive management framework

A - Atorvastatin

20mg for ALL patients (primary & secondary prevention)

  • 1° prevention: Aim for >40% reduction in non-HDL cholesterol
  • 2° prevention: Aim for LDL ≤2mmol/l or non-HDL ≤2.6mmol/l
  • If targets not met: Increase dose if eGFR ≥30 (seek specialist advice if eGFR <30)

L - Lifestyle

Optimization essential for all patients

  • BMI optimization
  • Smoking cessation
  • Dietary modifications (low salt, protein moderation)
  • Regular exercise

P - Proteinuria

ACEi/ARB therapy key

  • Start ACEi/ARB if ACR ≥70, or ACR ≥3 if diabetic
  • Up-titrate to maximum tolerated dose
  • Add GLIFLOZIN if T2DM & ACR >30 (consider if ACR 3-30)

H - Hypertension

Targets based on ACR, regardless of age

  • ACR <70: Target BP <140/90 mmHg
  • ACR ≥70: Target BP <130/80 mmHg
  • Manage according to NICE hypertension guidance

A - Anaemia

Investigate and manage appropriately

  • eGFR >60: CKD unlikely cause
  • eGFR 30-60: CKD may be the cause
  • eGFR <30: CKD often the cause

GLIFLOZIN Use (NICE Guidance)

  • OFFER if T2DM and ACR >30 (on max ACEi/ARB)
  • Consider if T2DM and ACR 3-30
  • Technology appraisals outline other situations where dapagliflozin/empagliflozin are 'an option'

Practice Pearls

  • ACEi/ARB can worsen eGFR initially - monitor potassium and creatinine
  • GLIFLOZINS have renal protective effects beyond glucose control
  • Statins reduce cardiovascular risk significantly in CKD

Structural/genetic causes of CKD

Accelerated CKD Definition

Over 12 months:

  • Absolute sustained fall in eGFR of 15 ml/min
  • OR Relative eGFR fall ≥25% which results in worse CKD staging
Requires urgent referral

Primary Care Management

  • Stable G1-G3a with ACR <30
  • Well-controlled hypertension
  • No concerning features
  • Patient stable on current therapy

Enhanced Monitoring

  • ACR 30-70 (check for progression)
  • eGFR declining but not accelerated
  • New onset anaemia
  • Rising BP despite treatment
