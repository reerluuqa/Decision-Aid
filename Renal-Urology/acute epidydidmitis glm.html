Acute Epididymitis

BMJ Best Practice Summary • Updated: Oct 28, 2025

Do Not Miss: Testicular Torsion

Sudden onset pain? No infection signs? Urgent surgical exploration required if torsion cannot be excluded.

Clinical Triage
Patient Type Most Likely Cause
Sexually Active Men STI Chlamydia, Gonorrhea, Mycoplasma
MSM (Insertive Anal) STI + Enteric (E. coli)
Men >35 yo Uropathogens (E. coli, Proteus)
Post-instrumentation Enteric (Common)
Immunosuppressed Atypical (Candida, TB, etc.)
Treatment Protocol
Gonorrhoea / Chlamydia (Suspected)

Ceftriaxone 500mg IM (single dose) (1000mg if >150kg) PLUS

Doxycycline 100mg PO BID x 10 days

*If Gonorrhoea likely (discharge, MSM, contact): Add Azithromycin 1g single dose.
Enteric / Uropathogens (>35 or Instrumented)

Levofloxacin 500mg PO daily x 10 days

*If MSM (insertive anal): Ceftriaxone 500mg IM + Levofloxacin 500mg PO x 10 days.
Mycoplasma genitalium (Confirmed/Suspected)

Moxifloxacin 400mg PO daily x 14 days

*Use if macrolide resistance or first-line failure. Partner must be treated.
Fluoroquinolone Safety Warning (Levofloxacin/Moxifloxacin)

May cause disabling adverse events: Tendon rupture, peripheral neuropathy, CNS effects. Restrict to serious infections where no alternatives exist.

Diagnostics
  • NAAT (First-void urine or swab)
    For Chlamydia trachomatis, Neisseria gonorrhoeae, M. genitalium.
  • Gram Stain
    Urethral secretions (≥5 PMNs/HPF suggests urethritis).
  • Urine Culture
    Mid-stream specimen. Dipstick for Leukocyte Esterase (WBC).
  • Scrotal Colour Duplex US
    If: Abscess suspected OR Torsion cannot be excluded.
  • HIV & Syphilis Screen
    Recommended for all STI-confirmed cases.
Supportive Care & Advice

Patient Measures

  • Bed rest
  • Scrotal elevation
  • Ice packs (optional)

Analgesia

  • Paracetamol 500-1000mg q4-6h
  • NSAIDs (Ibuprofen/Naproxen) for pain/inflammation
Sexual Health Advice:
Avoid unprotected intercourse until patient and partner(s) complete treatment and symptoms resolve.
