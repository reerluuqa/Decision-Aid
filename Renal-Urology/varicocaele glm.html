Varicocele Clinical Reference

Quick-Scan Point of Care Tool

Overview

Abnormal dilation of internal spermatic veins/pampiniform plexus.

15% Incidence
90% Left Side
40% Infertility Link
Immediate Action Required
Isolated Right-sided Varicocele: Rare. Suspect retroperitoneal/pelvic mass.
Action: Order CT/MRI Abdomen & Pelvis.
Non-draining Varicocele (Supine): Does not diminish when lying down.
Action: Rule out abdominal mass.
Clinical Grading
Grade Findings
Subclinical Detectable only by Doppler US
I Palpable only with Valsalva
II Palpable without Valsalva
III Visible ("Bag of worms")
Diagnosis Key Points
  • • Exam: Perform standing & supine. Valsalva for Grade I.
  • • Scrotal US: Use for obese patients or if exam difficult.
  • • Labs: Semen analysis (x2-3) & FSH/Testosterone for adults.
Management Protocol

Goal: Prevent testicular growth arrest & catch-up growth.

✓
Grade I (Subclinical)
No treatment necessary. Reassurance.
⏱
Grade II or III: Symmetrical
Testes symmetrical (<20% difference). Observation with yearly exams.
⚠
Indication for Repair
Asymmetry >20% (or >2cm³), Significant Pain, or Abnormal Semen Analysis.

Goal: Improve fertility parameters or relieve pain/hypogonadism.

✓
Subclinical / Grade I + Normal Semen
No treatment. Reassurance.
⏱
Palpable Asymptomatic + Normal Semen
Observation with serial semen analysis every 1-2 years.
⚠
Indication for Repair
1. Palpable varicocele + Abnormal semen parameters (Infertility).
2. Scrotal Pain.
3. Hypogonadism (Low Testosterone).
Repair Techniques
Microsurgical Subinguinal
Preferred
Low recurrence (1.1%), Low hydrocele (0.4%)
Laparoscopic
Recurrence (4.3%), Hydrocele (2.8%)
Percutaneous Embolization
Recurrence (12.7%), Less post-op pain
Open Retroperitoneal
High recurrence (15%), Hydrocele (8.2%)
Note: Microsurgical is preferred in adults. If prior inguinal surgery exists, use microsurgical to preserve testicular artery.
Complications & Follow-up
POST-OP
  • • Hydrocele (most common)
  • • Recurrence
  • • Testicular atrophy (rare)
MONITORING
  • • Semen analysis: Every 3 mo (1st year)
  • • Improvement: Usually 6-9 mo
  • • Adolescents: Yearly exam until growth complete
