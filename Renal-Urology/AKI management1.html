üö® Immediate Referral Criteria

Consider IMMEDIATE admission for potential dialysis if:

  • Fluid overload or pulmonary oedema Urgent
  • Hyperkalaemia (K+ >6.5 mmol/L or ECG changes) Urgent
  • Metabolic acidosis (pH <7.2) Urgent
  • Uraemia or complications (pericarditis, encephalopathy) Urgent
  • AKI Stage 3 with acute illness Immediate

üìû Nephrology Consultation

Discuss ALL cases of AKI with a nephrologist as soon as possible (most within 24h)

  • To advise on management Expert Input
  • To consider need for renal replacement therapy Dialysis?
  • To investigate for intrinsic renal disease Diagnosis

üö´ Medication Management

Consider WITHHOLDING drugs that could exacerbate AKI:

  • Diuretics (if hypovolaemic) Volume Depletion
  • NSAIDs ‚Üì Renal Perfusion
  • Antihypertensives including ACEi/ARBs ‚Üì GFR
  • Metformin (risk of lactic acidosis) Contraindicated
Review: Check if any new nephrotoxic drugs have been started (PPIs, NSAIDs, certain antibiotics, allopurinol).

üíß Fluid Management

Assess fluid status thoroughly:

  • Fluid intake and output record I/O Chart
  • Pulse, BP (including postural drop) Haemodynamics
  • JVP, capillary refill, dry axillae Clinical Signs

Note: AKI can present with oliguria OR polyuria. Treat the patient, not just the numbers.

üè• Post-Hospital AKI Follow-up

All patients who have had AKI require structured follow-up:

Within 3 months of AKI episode:

  • Clinical review with doctor/nurse/pharmacist Required
  • Medication review (including restarting drugs withheld) Medication Review
  • Advice on maintaining kidney health Education
  • Plan for ongoing monitoring of renal function Monitoring Plan

üî¨ Long-term Monitoring

Monitor for CKD development/progression for at least 3 years after AKI

  • Even if eGFR returns to baseline All Patients
  • Periodic eGFR and ACR testing Renal Monitoring
  • Frequency not specified by NICE ‚Äì use clinical judgment Individualized
At 3 months: Repeat renal function to check for evolving CKD. This is a critical checkpoint that should not be missed.

üíä Medication Restart

Key considerations when restarting medications:

  • ACE inhibitors/ARBs: Restart only if eGFR and potassium stable Renal Function
  • Diuretics: Start at lower dose, titrate slowly Titration
  • Metformin: Restart only when eGFR >30 ml/min and patient stable Contraindication
  • NSAIDs: Consider avoiding if recurrent AKI Risk Assessment

Evidence suggests AKI increases long-term CV risk:

  • Lifestyle advice and support Lifestyle

‚ö†Ô∏è Predictors of Poor Outcomes

Creatinine rise on starting ACEi/ARB may predict poor outcomes:

  • Creatinine rise ‚â•30%: 3.4x greater risk of end-stage renal disease High Risk
  • 1.5x greater risk of MI, 1.4x greater risk of heart failure CV Risk
  • 1.8x greater all-cause mortality Mortality
Clinical implication: Patients with creatinine rise on ACEi/ARBs may be high-risk group requiring closer monitoring and CV risk factor management.

üí° Key Take-Home Messages

  • Always think kidneys ‚Äì consider AKI in all acutely unwell patients
  • Be particularly alert in elderly, CKD patients, and those on DAMN-Glif drugs
  • Don't miss AKI in CKD patients ‚Äì a rise in creatinine may be AKI, not just CKD progression
  • Follow NHS Think Kidneys guidance for retesting and management
  • Ensure all AKI patients have 3-month follow-up and long-term monitoring
