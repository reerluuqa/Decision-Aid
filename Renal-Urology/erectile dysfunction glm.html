EMERGENCY ALERT: PRIAPISM
Contraindications

DO NOT PRESCRIBE PDE5 Inhibitors if:

  • Patient uses organic nitrates.
  • Recent MI (<90 days).
  • Unstable angina or coital angina.
  • Uncontrolled hypertension (>170/100).
  • Hypotension (<90/50).
  • Stroke (<6 months).
Immediate Action: Priapism

Erection > 3 hours is a medical emergency (Risk of tissue ischaemia).

Action: Emergent evaluation & management required.

Definition

Inability to achieve or maintain an erection sufficient for sexual performance.

Prevalence: 50% of men aged 40-70 report some difficulty.

Patient Vibe Check

Psychogenic vs Organic Clues:

Psychogenic
Acute onset, situational, morning erections preserved.
Organic
Gradual onset, constant course, poor non-coital erections.

Diagnosis Workup

History & Physical

Sexual History: Onset, morning erections, self-stimulation ability?
Medication Review: Antihypertensives, Antidepressants, Anti-androgens?
Genital Exam: Check for plaques (Peyronie's), testes size, gynaecomastia.
Screening: SHIM (IIEF-5) Questionnaire.

Key Labs

Test Indication
Fasting Glucose / HbA1c Screen for Diabetes
Lipid Profile CV Risk Assessment
Testosterone (AM) If low libido / signs of hypogonadism
Prolactin / TSH If Testosterone low (Rule out pituitary/thyroid)

Management Strategy

1
Primary Therapy: PDE5 Inhibitors
Common Agents
  • Sildenafil (Viagra)
  • Tadalafil (Cialis)
  • Vardenafil (Levitra)
  • Avanafil (Spedra)
Key Consideration

Safety & efficacy similar for all.

Note Tadalafil has longer half-life (36 hrs), allows for spontaneity/daily dosing.

Side Effects

Headache, flushing, dyspepsia, nasal congestion, dizziness.
Mild visual effects (3-11%).

2

Second Line: Intracavernosal Injections

For non-responders to PDE5i.

  • Primary: Alprostadil (PGE1). FDA Approved.
  • Secondary: BiMix (Papaverine + Phentolamine) or TriMix (includes Alprostadil).
  • Risk: Priapism, Penile Fibrosis.
Caution: Contraindicated in sickle cell anaemia or history of priapism.
3+

Alternative & Surgical Options

Vacuum Device Effective if other treatments fail.
Intraurethral Suppository Alprostadil. Rarely used (cost/rigidity issues).
Penile Prosthesis Surgical option when meds fail. Permanent.
Peyronie's Sx Intralesional Collagenase or surgery if curvature impairs coitus.

Special Scenarios

Post-Prostatectomy

Consider early daily dosing of PDE5i. Recovery takes 6-24 months. Intracavernous injection may help.

Psychogenic ED

First line is Psychosexual therapy (individual/couples). Can be adjunctive to pharmacotherapy.

Pelvic Trauma

Refer for vascular evaluation. Consider penile revascularization in young, healthy patients.
