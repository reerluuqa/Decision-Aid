Renal Cell Carcinoma (RCC)

Clinical Companion • Updated: Dec 2025

Red Flags / Emergency
  • The Classic Triad
    Hematuria + Flank Pain + Palpable Mass
    Only < 5% of cases, but signals aggressive/advanced disease.
  • Acute Metastatic Symptoms
    Bone pain, persistent cough, neurological deficits.
  • Paraneoplastic Syndromes
    Hypercalcemia, Stauffer's (hepatic dysfunction without mets), Polycythemia.

Definition: Malignancy arising from renal parenchyma/cortex.

Most cases are sporadic. Assessment of primary tumour, regional lymph nodes, and distant metastasis (TNM) is paramount for treatment decisions.

Note: Small renal masses (<4cm) have low metastatic potential. Active surveillance is a valid option for select patients.
Diagnostic Workup
Investigation Key Findings / Significance
CT Abdomen/Pelvis (w/ contrast) Definitive initial test. Characterizes lesions, assesses invasiveness, lymph nodes, venous involvement. (100% specificity for malignant characterization).
CT Chest Required for staging (pulmonary mets are common). CXR only if CT unavailable.
MRI Abdomen/Pelvis Alternative if CT contraindicated (allergy/renal dysfunction). Superior for venous thrombus extent.
Biopsy Not routine for localized masses (go straight to surgery).
Indicated: Metastatic disease, or if active surveillance/ablation is being considered.
Labs (LDH, Ca, Hb) Prognostic markers for metastatic disease (MSKCC criteria). High LDH, Hypercalcemia (>10mg/dL) = poor prognosis.
Ultrasound Often the initial incidental finding. Not sufficient for complex cysts or solid mass characterization.
Risk Factors & Genetics

Modifiable

Smoking (20-30% attributable) Obesity (BMI ≥35) Hypertension

Non-Modifiable

Male Sex Age >55 Family History (2.8-4.3x risk) ESRD / Dialysis

Genetic Red Flags

Refer for genetics if:

  • Age ≤ 46
  • Bilateral/Multifocal tumors
  • Papillary or Chromophobe histology
  • Personal/Family history of pheochromocytoma
Key Genes: VHL (Clear Cell), MET (Papillary), FH (HLRCC), FLCN (Birt-Hogg-Dubé).
Localized Disease (Stage I-II)
Partial Nephrectomy (Nephron-Sparing)
Gold standard for T1 tumors. Curative >90%.
Radical Nephrectomy
If tumor is not amenable to partial nephrectomy (e.g., anatomic position).
Active Surveillance
For Small Renal Masses (<4cm) or comorbid patients. Monitor for growth >5mm/yr.
Advanced / Metastatic Disease
Systemic Therapy (1st Line)
Combination Therapy Recommended:
1. Dual Immune Checkpoint Inhibitors (ICI)
2. ICI + VEGF-TKI (Tyrosine Kinase Inhibitor)
Cytoreductive Nephrectomy
Consider in select patients with good performance status and low metastatic burden.
Palliative Care
Integrate early for symptom management (bone pain, hemorrhage).
