Angio-oedema & Anaphylaxis

CKS Ref
Last Revised: Aug 2024

Suspected Anaphylaxis?

Use the ABCDE approach. Anaphylaxis is likely when ALL 3 criteria below are met. Act immediately.

Diagnostic Criteria (Must Meet ALL 3)

  • Sudden Onset & Rapid Progression
    Person feels unwell/impending doom. Minutes to hours.
  • Life-threatening Airway/Breathing/Circulation
    Airway Swelling/Stridor Breathing Wheeze/Respiratory Arrest Circulation Shock/Collapse/Hypotension
  • Skin and/or Mucosal Changes
    Erythema, urticaria, or angio-oedema. (Present in >80%).

Immediate Actions

1. IM ADRENALINE

Administer intramuscular adrenaline immediately. Do not delay.

2. ABCDE Assessment

Call for help. Secure airway, breathing, circulation.

3. Remove Trigger

Stop IV drug if implicated, remove stinger if present.

CRITICAL WARNING: Skin changes without life-threatening Airway, Breathing, or Circulation problems do NOT signify an anaphylactic reaction. Skin changes can be subtle or absent in up to 20% of reactions.

Biphasic Reaction Awareness

Potentially life-threatening recurrence of symptoms without re-exposure. Less likely in food-induced, higher risk if idiopathic or with initial hypotension. Monitor patient closely.

Diagnosing Angio-oedema

Angio-oedema WITH Urticaria

Superficial swelling (weals) + Deep swelling. Usually allergic or histamine-mediated.

Scenario A: Allergic (Acute)
  • Occurs within 1â€“2 hours of exposure.
  • Triggers: Foods (nuts, shellfish), Drugs (penicillin, NSAIDs), Latex, Insect stings.
Action: Identify & Avoid Allergen
Scenario B: Idiopathic (Chronic)
  • Recurrent episodes (>6 weeks).
  • Associated with autoimmune disorders (SLE, Thyroiditis).
Action: Refer Dermatologist/Immunologist

Angio-oedema WITHOUT Urticaria

Deep swelling only. Often bradykinin-mediated. High airway risk.

Suspect: ACE Inhibitor Reaction
  • Onset days to months after starting.
  • Can be life-threatening.
Action: WITHDRAW ACEI Immediately
Suspect: Hereditary (HAE)
  • Family history? (though 25% are new mutations).
  • Recurrent, non-pitting, painless swelling (24h to peak).
  • Sx: Abdominal pain, nausea, laryngeal swelling.
Action: Refer Immunologist
Suspect: Acquired (AAE)
  • History of Lymphoma or Connective Tissue Disorder (e.g., SLE).
  • No family history.
Action: Treat Underlying Disorder + Refer

Background & Prognosis

Definitions

  • Angio-oedema: Swelling of deep dermis, subcutaneous, or submucosal tissue. Affects face, genitalia, hands, feet, bowel, airway.
  • Anaphylaxis: Severe, life-threatening, generalized hypersensitivity reaction. Rapidly developing ABC problems + skin/mucosal changes.

Prevalence

  • Angio-oedema: ~40% with urticaria. ~10% isolated.
  • ACEi related: 0.1-0.7% (Higher in black people).
  • HAE: Rare (1 in 10,000 - 1 in 150,000).
  • Anaphylaxis: Lifetime prevalence 0.05% - 2.0%.

Prognosis & Complications

Angio-oedema

  • Acute Allergic: Self-limiting (1-3 days).
  • ACEi: Can become life-threatening if not stopped (20% risk).
  • HAE: Requires lifelong treatment.

Anaphylaxis

  • ~20 deaths/year in UK (Underestimate).
  • 50% fatalities due to shock, 50% asphyxia.
  • Death usually occurs rapidly (IV meds < 5min, Stings < 15min, Food < 30min).
  • Risk factor: Poorly controlled asthma.
