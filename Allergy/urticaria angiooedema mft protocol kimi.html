Evidence-based management approach

First-Line Management

Non-sedating antihistamines as required for infrequent symptoms
Regular antihistamines if symptoms are weekly or more
Consider dose escalation (double, triple, or quadruple)
Loratadine 10mg, cetirizine 10mg, or fexofenadine 180mg as first choice

Patient Education

Condition Explanation

Spontaneous urticaria/angioedema is a benign nuisance affecting 1-20% of population. Most cases resolve within 3 years. No known deaths globally from spontaneous urticaria.

‚ö†Ô∏è
Trigger Avoidance

Identify and avoid co-factors: stress, trauma, infection, exercise, temperature changes, pressure, NSAIDs, alcohol, fatigue.
