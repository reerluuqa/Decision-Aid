Quick Triage & Overview

Immediate clinical decision support for Chronic Rhinosinusitis with Nasal Polyps (CRSwNP).

RED FLAGS (Urgent)

Suspect Neoplasia (e.g., Nasopharyngeal cancer) or invasive fungal sinusitis if:

  • Unilateral obstruction/polyps
  • Bloodstained nasal discharge / Epistaxis
  • Facial pain/pressure (poorly correlated with polyps)
  • Periorbital edema, diplopia, vision loss
  • Severe frontal headache/swelling
  • Neurological signs (Cranial nerve palsy)
  • Systemic signs (Weight loss, night sweats)
Key Symptoms (Prevalence)
Nasal obstruction 92%
Hyposmia (Reduced smell) 84%
Discoloured discharge / PND 80%
Facial pressure/congestion 67%
Definition & Theory

Definition

Benign swellings of paranasal sinus mucosa. Invariably associated with Chronic Rhinosinusitis (CRSwNP).

Histology: Loose connective tissue, edema, inflammatory cells (mostly eosinophils).

Strong Associations

Asthma Cystic Fibrosis Aspirin Sensitivity (Samter's Triad) Allergic Fungal Rhinosinusitis

Diagnosis & Investigation

Based on symptoms + endoscopic findings. Imaging if red flags present.

Criteria (Adults)

≥ 2 symptoms for ≥12 weeks:

  • Nasal blockage/obstruction/congestion OR nasal discharge (Anterior/Posterior drip)
  • ± Facial pain/pressure
  • ± Reduction/loss of smell

Severity (VAS Score)

0-3 (Mild) >3-7 (Mod) >7-10 (Severe)
Examination

Direct visualization is essential.

Anterior Rhinoscopy / Endoscopy

Look for: Grey polyps, insensitive to touch, no connection to lateral wall.

Endoscopic Grading:
  • Grade 0: None
  • Grade 1: Middle meatus only
  • Grade 2: Below middle turbinate
  • Grade 3: To inferior turbinate
  • Grade 4: Complete obstruction
Labs & Imaging
CT Sinus Pre-op Red Flag
FBC + Diff Eosinophilia (0.4 - 1.5 x10^6/L)
Nasal Smear Eosinophils (Typical)
Biopsy If unilateral/suspicious
Differentials
Condition Key Differentiator
Nasopharyngeal Cancer Neck mass, unilateral symptoms, epistaxis, cranial nerve palsy, EBV risk.
Antrochoanal Polyp Usually unilateral, single, larger, children > adults.
EGPA (Churg-Strauss) Severe asthma + blood eosinophilia > 1.0 + vasculitic rash/palpable purpura.

Management Algorithm

Stepwise approach based on severity (VAS score).

Mild to Moderate VAS 0-7
1

Intranasal Corticosteroids

Duration: 3 months course.

Options: Fluticasone (93-186mcg bid) or Mometasone (100mcg qd/bid).

Review at 3 months. Continue at lowest effective dose if improved.

2

Refractory / Minimal Improvement

Action: Surgical Polypectomy (after pre-op CT).

Adjuncts: Post-op nasal saline irrigation + Oral Doxycycline (3-12 weeks) or Macrolide.

3

Advanced (Severe Recurrence)

Biologics (if indicated): Dupilumab, Mepolizumab, Omalizumab.

OR ATAD: Aspirin Therapy After Desensitisation (if NERD).

Severe VAS >7
1

Intranasal + Oral Corticosteroids

Add: Short course (7-21 days) of systemic steroid.

Then: Continue Intranasal Corticosteroids.

Max 1-2 courses/year generally recommended alongside maintenance.

2

Refractory / Uncontrolled

Action: Surgical Polypectomy.

Post-op: Saline irrigation + Doxycycline/Macrolide + Intranasal steroid.

3

Biologics

Consider in patients contraindicated to systemic steroids or needing frequent courses.

Biologics Criteria (3 of the following required)
Type 2 Hypersensitivity (Eosinophils ≥10/hpf or blood ≥250/µL)
Contraindication/Need for systemic steroids
Impaired QoL (SNOT-22 ≥40)
Anosmia
Comorbid Asthma needing regular inhaled steroids

Special Scenarios & Comorbidities

Specific considerations for high-risk groups.

Pediatric Polyps

Nasal polyps in children are rare.

Investigate for:
  • Cystic Fibrosis (Strong association)
  • Primary Ciliary Dyskinesia
Asthma & Aspirin (NERD)

Samter's Triad: Asthma + Nasal Polyps + Aspirin Sensitivity.

Management:

  • Avoid all COX-1 inhibitors.
  • COX-2 inhibitors generally safe (test first dose in hospital).
  • Consider Aspirin Therapy After Desensitisation (ATAD) if severe or requiring aspirin for cardiac reasons.
Additional Reference Values
Test Significance in CRSwNP
Skin Prick / IgE Used for allergic rhinitis diagnosis. Not required for CRSwNP diagnosis (atopy unrelated).
ANCA If EGPA suspected. Positive in ~35% of EGPA patients.
Peak Nasal Inspiratory Flow Quick objective measure of airflow. Used pre/post treatment in specialist clinics.

Urgent Action Plan

If red flags (unilateral symptoms, bleeding, visual changes) are present:

  1. Refer immediately to Otorhinolaryngology (ENT).
  2. Order CT Scan of Sinuses (Soft tissue opacifications + bone erosion check).
  3. Prepare for Biopsy to exclude neoplasia.
  4. If visual/neuro deficits: Emergency referral to rule out intracranial extension.
