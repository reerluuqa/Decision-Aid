Differential Diagnosis

Don't miss:

  • Adults: COPD, Heart Failure, Vocal Cord Dysfunction, Lung Cancer.
  • Children: Inhaled foreign body, Cystic Fibrosis, Congenital heart disease.

Adults (12+) Treatment Options

Choose ONE of the following as first-line:

  • A
    Anti-inflammatory Reliever (AIR) Therapy
    ICS-Formoterol taken ONLY as needed.
  • B
    Maintenance & Reliever Therapy (MART)
    ICS-Formoterol regularly + as needed.
  • !
    SABA PRN (Short-acting beta agonist)
    Not recommended as monotherapy due to safety risk.

    Consider LABA if symptoms uncontrolled on ICS.

NEW Acute Exacerbation Management (Adults)

Self-Management Plan (PAAP)

If symptoms deteriorate:

  • Quadrupling ICS dose (Adults only)
  • Temporarily increase OCS (if prescribed)
  • Seek urgent help if no improvement
Lifestyle & Non-Pharm
  • Smoking cessation (Critical)
  • Avoid allergens (if sensitized)
  • Weight management
  • Exercise (if triggered, use reliever 15min prior)
Annual Review Checklist

Ensure these 4 pillars are addressed at every review:

1. Symptom Control Ask about daytime symptoms, night waking, activity limitation.
2. Exacerbations Record number of attacks needing OCS/hospital admission in last 12mo.
3. Inhaler Technique Ask patient to demonstrate device use. Correct if errors found.
4. Personalized Action Plan (PAAP) Written plan agreed with patient on adjusting meds.
