Asthma Clinical Reference

Diagnosis & Management (Adults & Children)
Risk Factors for Death
  • History of near-fatal asthma (intubation/ventilation)
  • Hospital admission or emergency care in past 12 months
  • Currently using or recently stopped oral corticosteroids
  • Not using ICS or poor adherence
  • Over-use of SABAs (short-acting beta-2 agonists)
  • History of adverse psychosocial issues
  • Food allergy or anaphylaxis with asthma
Acute Complications
  • Pneumomediastinum & Pneumothorax
  • Pneumonia
  • Hypercapnic respiratory failure
  • Severe exacerbations (potentially life-threatening)
  • Preventable death: UK has one of the highest mortality rates in Europe.
Occupational Warning
  • Account for ~1 in 6 adult cases.
  • If cause is identified & exposure stops: 25-30% fully recover in 3-5 years.
  • High Risk Jobs: Baking, chemical processing, foam/plastic, welding, woodworking, healthcare.
Scope
CKS
Age 1 month+

Covers primary care diagnosis and management of asthma in adults, young people, and children.

Exclusions: COPD, Bronchiolitis, Anaphylaxis (see separate topics).
Management Goals
  • Diagnose & assess severity
  • Treat to manage symptoms
  • Self-management strategies
  • Provide Personalised Action Plan
  • Regular primary care review
  • Emergency admission where needed
Diagnosis Approach
1
Symptom Profile Cough, wheeze, chest tightness, shortness of breath. Variable airflow limitation over time.
2
Objective Tests Essential for confirmation. Tests vary by age (BTS/NICE/SIGN 2024).
3
Monitor Asthma control monitored at every review.
QOF Indicator (2025/26): New diagnosis must have record of objective test (3m before/after).

First-line Therapy (12y+)
NG245 2024

Anti-inflammatory Reliever (AIR) Therapy OR Maintenance and Reliever Therapy (MART) using ICS/formoterol combination inhaler.

Reduces risk of severe exacerbations compared to SABA-only.
Children (5-11y)

Consider a MART regime with ICS/formoterol.

Note: Off-label indication in this age group.
Prevalence (UK)

Boys vs Girls (Child) 8% vs 4%

Prognosis
  • Many children experience remission by adolescence (infrequent wheezers).
  • Severe/frequent childhood symptoms often persist into adulthood.
  • Adult-onset: 15.9% remission within 5 years.
  • Risk of persistence: Airway hyper-responsiveness, nasal polyps.
Definitions
Uncontrolled: Lifestyle impact, exacerbations needing OCS, night waking 1+/week, reliever use 3+/week.
Severe: Uncontrolled despite high-dose ICS/LABA or OCS. Distinct from "difficult to treat" (often adherence issues).
Complete Control: No symptoms, no night waking, normal lung function, no rescue meds.
