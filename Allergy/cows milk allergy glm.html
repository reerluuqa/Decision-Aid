üí° Clinical Judgement

Be aware that clinical features are variable and overlap with other common conditions.

Suspect CMA if:

  • One or more symptoms in Table 1.
  • Multiple, persistent, severe, or treatment-resistant symptoms (e.g., GORD, eczema).
  • Symptoms involve multiple organ systems (Up to 1 in 4 infants).
üö´ Do NOT Use

The following alternative diagnostic tools are not recommended:

  • Serum-specific IgG testing.
  • Vega testing (electroacupuncture).
  • Applied kinesiology.
  • Hair analysis.
  • Atopy patch testing.

Assessment Checklist

Allergy-Focused History
Core
Examination & Tests

Examine for:

  • Nutritional status & growth (Weight, length/height, BMI).
  • Signs of current reaction.
  • Comorbidities (Asthma, Eczema, Rhinitis).

Diagnostic Tests:

  • Suspected IgE: Offer Skin prick test OR serum-specific IgE (if local pathways allow).
  • Suspected Non-IgE: No reliable allergy tests exist. Diagnosis is via elimination/reintroduction.
Definition

Reproducible immune-mediated allergic response to one or more proteins in cow's milk.

Key Distinction:

  • Food Allergy: Immune-mediated (IgE or Non-IgE).
  • Food Intolerance: Non-immune (e.g., enzyme deficiency, pharmacological). NOT covered in this CKS topic.
Prevalence
  • Most common food allergy in early childhood.
  • 1-2 years UK: Adjusted incidence approx 1.28%.
  • 56% of cases are non-IgE-mediated.
  • Almost all present before 1 year of age.
  • Self-reported rates are significantly higher than confirmed rates.
Risk Factors
  • Comorbid atopy (Asthma, Rhinitis, Eczema).
  • Early-onset severe eczema (<6 months).
  • Existing food allergy (Multiple common).
  • Family history of atopy/food allergy.
  • Male sex (2-fold higher risk).
Complications
  • Anaphylaxis: Rare but life-threatening.
  • Growth issues: Faltering growth, micronutrient deficiencies (Iron, Vit D, Calcium).
  • Atopic March: Risk of developing asthma/rhinitis later.
  • Severe Non-IgE: FPIES, Enteropathy, Eosinophilic oesophagitis (Not fully covered here).
Prognosis
  • Most children outgrow allergy by adulthood.
  • Non-IgE: Resolves faster (100% tolerant in EuroPrevall study at 1yr).
  • IgE: 56% tolerant by 1yr in same study.
  • Likely to persist if: High initial IgE levels, Asthma/Rhinitis present, Multiple food allergies.

Differential Diagnosis

Alternative diagnoses which may present similarly include:

Lactose Intolerance Non-immune adverse reaction due to enzyme deficiency. Symptoms may overlap with non-IgE CMA if cow's milk-induced enteropathy exists.
GORD / Reflux Common physiological issue in infants, but can be treatment-resistant if caused by CMA.
Infection Viral gastroenteritis, UTI, or respiratory infections may mimic GI or respiratory symptoms.
