Immediate Action Required

If systemic symptoms or suspected anaphylaxis: Arrange immediate ambulance transfer to Accident and Emergency.

üõë Immediate IgE-Mediated Reaction

Onset: Seconds to 1-2 hours after ingestion.

Systemic (Anaphylaxis) Respiratory distress, wheeze, hypotension, collapse, confusion, loss of consciousness.
Skin Pruritus, urticaria (hives), angioedema (lips/eyes), erythema, flushing.
Respiratory Cough, chest tightness, wheeze, stridor, hoarseness, rhinitis.
Gastrointestinal Nausea, vomiting, diarrhoea, abdominal pain.

üåº Pollen Food Syndrome (Oral Allergy)

Often Mild

Localized reaction due to cross-reactivity between aeroallergens and raw foods.

  • ‚û§ Mild, transient oropharyngeal pruritus & angioedema.
  • ‚û§ Trigger: Raw fruit/veg (Apple, carrot, celery, kiwi, stone fruits).
  • ‚û§ History of allergic rhinitis usually present.
Note: Severe reactions possible in birch pollen season, especially with asthma. Primary nut allergy can coexist.

üßê When to Suspect Food Allergy

Consider allergy if symptoms do not respond to standard treatment for:

  • Atopic eczema
  • Gastro-oesophageal reflux disease
  • Chronic constipation
  • Faltering growth (children)

üß™ Testing & Referral

Arrange

Skin Prick Test and/or Serum-specific IgE for suspected food allergens and cross-reactive foods.

Refer

Refer to allergy specialist or dietitian depending on clinical judgment and local pathways.

Do NOT use in primary care:
  • Atopy patch testing
  • Oral food challenges
  • Vega testing / Applied kinesiology / Hair analysis
  • Serum-specific IgG testing

  • Previous severe reaction

  • Teenagers / Young Adults

Persistent Allergy:
