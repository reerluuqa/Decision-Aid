Antenatal Care – Quick Reference Uncomplicated Pregnancy
Scope

Who this applies to

Healthy, singleton pregnancies needing routine care; typically ages 18–40, community‑managed. Excludes medical/obstetric complications or complex social factors.

  • Scans: Dating 11+2–14+1w • Anomaly 18+0–20+6w
  • Nulliparous: Booking, 16, 25, 28, 31, 34, 36, 38, 40, 41w • Parous: Booking, 16, 28, 34, 36, 38, 41w
Timeline

Routine schedule

Booking (≤10 wks)
  • History (medical/obstetric/family/social/mental health); private domestic abuse screen.
  • BMI, BP, urine protein; FBC, group, rhesus D.
  • Risk assess: GDM, pre‑eclampsia (aspirin/consultant‑led if at risk), FGR, VTE, FGM.
  • Offer screens: HIV, syphilis, hep B; sickle/thalassaemia; fetal anomaly.
  • Advice: folic acid 400 µg (5 mg if high‑risk) to 12w; vitamin D 10 µg; diet/exercise; meds safety.
  • Immunisations info: influenza, pertussis, COVID‑19; arrange dating scan.
16 wks
  • Review results; BP, urine; reassess pre‑eclampsia risk; mental health; discuss birth preferences.
25 wks (nullip)
  • BP, urine, SFH; counsel about fetal movements.
28 wks
  • BP, urine, SFH; FBC, group & antibodies; anti‑D if Rh‑negative; sleep position advice.
  • Discuss labour prep, newborn care.
31 wks (nullip)
  • BP, urine, SFH; confirm birth preferences; reinforce movement advice.
34 wks
  • BP, urine, SFH; second anti‑D if two‑dose regimen; birth & postnatal planning.
36 wks
  • BP, urine, SFH; palpate abdomen; USS if breech suspected; finalise plan.
38 wks
  • As at 36w; discuss membrane sweep/induction; prolonged pregnancy risks.
40 wks (nullip)
  • Repeat 38w review; ensure access day/night if concerns.
41 wks
  • Discuss induction (41+0–42+0) vs expectant management per local policy.
What to do

Appointment content (fast checklist)

First contact (pre‑booking)

  • Identify need for additional care; urgent med review if on teratogens.
  • Vitamins (folate, vit D); lifestyle (diet, smoking, alcohol, drugs). UKTIS/bumps for meds.

Booking ≤10 w

  • Comprehensive history; medicines & allergies; occupation risks; support network.
  • Private domestic abuse screen; document partner/next‑of‑kin.
  • Baseline tests; risk tools (GDM, pre‑eclampsia, FGR, VTE, FGM).
  • Offer infectious disease, haemoglobinopathy & anomaly screening; immunisations.
  • Education: development, emotions, partner support, services/classes.

16 w

  • Update history/meds; mental health; BP/urine; reassess pre‑eclampsia; start birth plan.

25 w (nullip)

  • BP/urine/SFH; fetal movement counselling; follow up results/referrals.

28 w

  • BP/urine/SFH; FBC, group & antibodies; anti‑D if Rh‑neg; sleep position.
  • Discuss labour/birth plan, newborn screening & vitamin K.

31 w (nullip)

  • BP/urine/SFH; confirm birth preferences; reinforce movement advice.

34 w

  • BP/urine/SFH; 2nd anti‑D if two‑dose; birth & postnatal planning.

36 w

  • BP/urine/SFH; presentation check; USS if breech suspected; finalise plan.

38–41 w

  • As above; discuss sweep/induction; prolonged pregnancy risks; ensure access.
Always

Ongoing at every visit

  • BP & urine protein; SFH from 24 w (scan if large/small for dates).
  • Assess VTE risk after any admission/significant health event.
  • Fetal movements: urgent same‑day assessment if reduced.
  • Mental health, domestic abuse, social concerns (provide private space).
  • Education: emotions, partner support, resources, bonding.
Key supplementation

Folic acid & Vitamin D

  • Folic acid 400 µg daily until 12 w; use 5 mg if higher NTD risk (previous NTD, certain antiepileptics, diabetes, coeliac, sickle‑cell/thalassaemia).
  • Vitamin D 10 µg daily throughout pregnancy; consider Healthy Start vitamins if eligible.
  • Avoid vitamin A supplements (teratogenic).
Urgent

Red flags → same‑day secondary care

  • Pre‑eclampsia symptoms: severe headache; visual changes; severe epigastric/RUQ pain; vomiting; breathlessness; sudden swelling of face/hands/feet (incl. up to 4 w postpartum).
  • Hypertension/proteinuria; abnormal SFH or malpresentation; vaginal bleeding; significant pain.
  • Reduced fetal movements at any time (24/7 access to maternity services).
Lifestyle & safety

Diet, exercise, medicines & exposures

Diet

  • Balanced diet; don’t “eat for two”. Encourage 5‑a‑day, wholegrains, lean protein, dairy.
  • Avoid: soft mould‑ripened cheeses; unpasteurised products; pâté; undercooked meats/ready meals; raw shellfish; liver; shark/swordfish/marlin; limit tuna (≤4 cans or 2 steaks/wk).
  • Caffeine <200 mg/day (≈ instant 100 mg; tea 75 mg; cola ≤40 mg; energy drink ≤80 mg).
  • Peanuts are fine unless allergic.

Medicines & complementary therapies

  • Check with GP/pharmacist before OTC use; use lowest effective dose if required.
  • Paracetamol preferred analgesic; avoid herbal products with unknown safety.

Exercise

  • Continue/start moderate activity; include strength; minimise sedentary time.
  • Avoid contact/fall‑risk sports and scuba diving.

Alcohol, smoking, drugs

  • Alcohol: no known safe level → advise abstinence.
  • Smoking: refer to stop‑smoking support; include partners.
  • Recreational drugs: disclose for care planning; consider specialist referral; MDT care.

Toxoplasmosis prevention

  • Hand/food hygiene; thoroughly cook meats/ready meals; garden with gloves.
  • Avoid cat litter/faeces; avoid lambing/newborn lamb contact.

Other

  • Sex is safe in uncomplicated pregnancy.
  • After 28 w, avoid falling asleep supine; use pillows to maintain position.
  • Offer vaccinations at routine visits (flu, pertussis, others per guidance).
