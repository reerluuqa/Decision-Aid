Pre‑Conception Care — One‑Page Clinical Algorithm
NICE CKS (Last revised: June 2025) · Fast memory aide for consultations

1) Rapid Triage & Assessment

Ask, tick, and act in a single pass.

  • Pregnancy plans & timing; previous obstetric history.
  • Height, weight, BMI; diet & activity.
  • Folic acid knowledge & dose need (NTD risk).
  • Cervical screening status.
  • Lifestyle: smoking, alcohol, recreational drugs.
  • Infection risks: HBV/HIV; immunity to rubella & varicella.
  • Occupational / environmental exposures & radiation; travel plans (e.g., Zika).
  • All medicines incl. OTC & herbal; need for substitutions or washout.
  • Chronic conditions & mental health; genetic risk concerns.

2) Advice for All

Folic acid

  • 400 mcg OD for all trying to conceive (including BMI ≥25 and ↑PE risk).
  • 5 mg OD if ↑NTD/congenital risk: personal/partner/family NTD; prior affected pregnancy; diabetes; haemoglobinopathies; anti‑epileptics; HIV meds.
  • In sickle cell disease / thalassaemia / trait: use 5 mg daily throughout pregnancy.

Lifestyle & weight

  • Balanced diet (Eatwell); target BMI 18.5–24.9.
  • If BMI ≥30: support weight loss; even 5–10% reduces risk & improves fertility.
  • Smoking: advise stop; offer services. NRT if needed; avoid varenicline/bupropion when pregnancy possible.
  • Alcohol & illicit drugs: advise avoid; refer if unable to reduce/stop; test injectors for HBV/HCV/HIV.

Conception & fertility

  • Sex every 2–3 days; >80% conceive within 1 year (if <40y).
  • No need to time precisely to ovulation.
  • After progestogen‑only injection, fertility may be delayed up to 12 months.
  • Discuss age & inter‑pregnancy interval (often 18–59 months; personalise).

Screening & immunisation

  • Cervical smear if due before pregnancy.
  • Rubella: vaccinate seronegative; avoid pregnancy ×1 month.
  • Varicella: check history/antibodies; vaccinate eligible non‑immune; avoid pregnancy ×1 month after last dose.
  • Hepatitis B vaccine if high risk (e.g., IDU, multiple partners, CKD, liver disease, close contacts).

Medicines

  • Do not stop key meds without specialist input; consider safer alternatives.
  • Minimise number/dose; identify drugs needing washout.
  • Avoid non‑essential OTC/herbal remedies.

Hazards & travel

  • Review work/home chemical/radiation exposures; involve Occ Health where available.
  • Zika: avoid pregnancy during travel to affected areas; see “Infection risks”.

3) Chronic & Mental Health — Refer / Optimise

Diabetes mellitus

Avoid conception if HbA1c > 86 mmol/mol (10%).
  • Aim pre‑conception HbA1c < 48 mmol/mol (6.5%) if safe; monthly checks.
  • SMBG targets: waking 5–7 mmol/L; pre‑meal 4–7 mmol/L (balance hypo risk).
  • Folic acid 5 mg OD until 12 weeks once pregnant.
  • Arrange retinal assessment before rapid optimisation; renal assessment (consider nephrology if Cr ≥120 µmol/L, uACR >30 mg/mmol, or eGFR <45).
  • In Type 1: check TSH, FT4, TPO antibodies.

GLP‑1 agonists

Stop before planned pregnancy. Use recommended washout intervals:

AgentWashout
Tirzepatide1 month
Semaglutide2 months
Exenatide12 weeks
LiraglutideNo washout required
On tirzepatide + oral contraception: switch to non‑oral or add barrier for 4 weeks after start and after each dose increase.

Mental health

  • Do not stop meds abruptly; discuss risks of untreated illness vs. treatment.
  • Severe disorders (bipolar, psychosis, severe depression): refer to perinatal psychiatry.
  • Consider prolactin if on prolactin‑raising antipsychotics; consult UKTIS data when prescribing.
  • Maintain effective contraception until specialist review if changing therapy.

Other long‑term conditions

  • Thyroid disease: optimise; follow condition‑specific guidance; refer if needed.
  • Cardiac disease, chronic HTN, renal disease: specialist assessment; continue meds until advised otherwise.
  • Asthma: keep well‑controlled; continue usual therapy; oral steroids if indicated—do not withhold.
  • VTE history / thrombophilia: seek thrombosis‑in‑pregnancy expertise; if on warfarin, plan switch per risk.
  • IBD / Rheumatology: gastro/rheum review; contraception until reviewed; written info (e.g., CCUK); ensure folate as appropriate.
  • Haemoglobinopathies (SCD/thal/trait): haematology review; folic acid 5 mg throughout pregnancy.

4) Genetic Risk Assessment

  • Family history (e.g., SMA, CF, haemoglobinopathies, Fragile X, Tay‑Sachs); ethnicity‑linked risks.
  • Consanguinity; recurrent pregnancy loss; prior affected pregnancy.
  • Refer couples with any of the above for genetics assessment/screening.

5) Infection & Travel Risks

Zika — defer conception

  • If female only travelled: avoid conception & consider condoms for 2 months after symptoms/departure.
  • If male partner travelled: avoid conception & consider condoms for 3 months after symptoms/departure.
  • Advise daytime & dusk mosquito precautions.

Viral hepatitis / HIV

  • Offer HBV/HCV/HIV testing to people who inject drugs and others at risk.
  • Offer HBV vaccine if at high risk (see Immunisation section).

Quick doses & cut‑offs

  • Folic acid: 400 mcg OD (most) · 5 mg if ↑risk/haemoglobinopathies (throughout pregnancy).
  • Diabetes: avoid conception if HbA1c > 86; aim < 48 mmol/mol if safe.
  • Type 1 SMBG: waking 5–7; pre‑meal 4–7 mmol/L.
  • POI Depo‑Provera: fertility delay up to 12 months.
  • MMR/Varicella vaccine: avoid pregnancy ×1 month.
  • Zika: Female 2 mo · Male partner 3 mo.
