Overview

Have I got the right topic?

This CKS topic covers the management of widespread itch, with or without rash, that is specific to pregnancy.

It does NOT cover the management of rash without itch in pregnancy.

Target Audience Healthcare Professionals NHS UK Primary Care

Background Information

Definition

Itch (pruritus) is an unpleasant sensation that triggers a desire to scratch.

Causes of Itch

Itch in pregnancy may be caused by:

  • A pre-existing or coincidental condition
  • A condition specific to pregnancy:
    • Obstetric cholestasis (no rash)
    • Polymorphic eruption of pregnancy (with rash)
    • Atopic eruption of pregnancy (with rash)
    • Pemphigoid gestationis (with rash)

Prevalence

  • Overall itch: 18-40% of pregnancies
  • Obstetric cholestasis: 0.7% (UK), higher in Asian ethnicity
  • Polymorphic eruption: 0.5% (single), up to 17% (triplet)
  • Atopic eruption: 43-49% of pruritic rashes
  • Pemphigoid gestationis: 1 in 50,000 pregnancies

Prognosis

  • Obstetric cholestasis: Resolves postpartum, 45-90% recurrence
  • Polymorphic eruption: Resolves within week postpartum, rarely recurs
  • Atopic eruption: Resolves after delivery, may recur
  • Pemphigoid gestationis: Resolves in weeks-months, 50-70% recurrence

Diagnosis

Itch Without Rash

Suspect obstetric cholestasis if itch typically:

  • Starts from 28 weeks gestation onwards
  • Affects soles and palms primarily
  • Worse at night, causing insomnia
  • May have jaundice (10%), dark urine, pale stools
Diagnosis confirmed by: Elevated serum bile acids and/or abnormal LFTs

Itch With Rash

Polymorphic Eruption

  • Third trimester
  • Urticarial papules/plaques on abdomen
  • Spares umbilicus
  • May spread to buttocks/thighs

Atopic Eruption

  • First trimester
  • History of atopy
  • Eczematous lesions on face/neck/chest
  • Papules/nodules on trunk/extensor surfaces

Pemphigoid Gestationis

  • Second/third trimester
  • Intense itch, then urticarial papules/plaques
  • Includes umbilicus, may progress to blisters
  • Urgent dermatology referral needed

Management

Itch Without Rash (Obstetric Cholestasis)

1
Same-day referral to maternity unit for assessment of bile acids, LFTs, and fetal wellbeing
2
Symptomatic relief:
  • Emollients (liberal and regular)
  • Menthol 0.5-1% in aqueous cream
  • Sedating antihistamine at night (chlorphenamine/promethazine)
3
Self-care advice: Cool baths, ice packs, loose cotton clothing
4
Postnatal: LFTs at 2 weeks (45-90% recurrence in future pregnancies)

Itch With Rash

Polymorphic Eruption

  • Reassurance (no serious risk)
  • Emollients liberally
  • Moderately potent topical corticosteroids
  • Sedating antihistamine if sleep affected
  • Refer if severe/not responding

Atopic Eruption

  • Emollients and topical corticosteroids
  • Sedating antihistamine if needed

Pemphigoid Gestationis

  • Urgent dermatology referral
  • Associated with preterm delivery
  • Low birth weight risk
