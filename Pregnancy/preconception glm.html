Pre-conception Care Algorithm

Clinical decision support for healthcare professionals

Women 16-45 years
Planning pregnancy
NICE CKS June 2025

Patient Assessment

  • Basic Assessment: Pregnancy timing, obstetric history, diet, weight/BMI, medications
  • Lifestyle: Smoking, alcohol, recreational drugs
  • Screening: Cervical smear status
  • Risk Factors: Chronic conditions, mental health, family history
  • Infection Risk: Hepatitis B, HIV
  • Immunity: Rubella, varicella
  • Exposures: Occupational/environmental, travel to Zika areas

General Recommendations

Folic Acid
  • Standard risk: 400 mcg daily (first 12 weeks)
  • High risk: 5 mg daily (NTD, diabetes, family history)
  • BMI >25 alone doesn't require higher dose
Lifestyle
  • Healthy weight (BMI 18.5-24.9)
  • Smoking cessation (offer referral)
  • Avoid alcohol completely
  • Stop illicit drugs
  • Balanced diet (Eatwell Guide)
Conception
  • Regular intercourse (every 2-3 days)
  • No need to time with ovulation
  • Fertility delay up to 1 year after progestogen-only injection

Condition-Specific Care

Chronic Conditions
  • Diabetes: Optimize glucose control, refer to joint team
  • Epilepsy: Review meds, switch to lowest risk regimen
  • Thyroid: Ensure TSH in pregnancy range
  • Hypertension: Switch to pregnancy-safe antihypertensives
  • Other conditions: Specialist referral
Mental Health
  • Severe illness: Refer to perinatal mental health service
  • Continue contraception until specialist assessment
  • Do not stop medication without specialist input
  • Balance risks vs benefits
Medication Review
  • Discontinue unnecessary medications
  • Switch to pregnancy-safe alternatives
  • Some require washout period before conception

Preventive Measures

Immunizations
  • Rubella: Check immunity, MMR if needed (avoid pregnancy 1 month after)
  • Varicella: Check immunity, vaccinate if eligible
  • Hepatitis B: Vaccinate if at risk
Screening
  • Up-to-date cervical smear before pregnancy
  • Hepatitis B, syphilis, HIV testing if indicated
Travel Precautions
  • Avoid pregnancy during travel to Zika areas
  • If travel occurs: avoid conception for 2 months (female) or 3 months (male) after return

Referral Indications

Specialist Referral
  • Severe mental health conditions
  • Poorly controlled chronic medical conditions
  • History of recurrent miscarriage
  • Known/suspected genetic disorders
  • Inability to stop high-risk medications
Genetic Counseling
  • Family history of genetic disorders
  • Consanguinity
  • Advanced maternal age (>35 years)
