Endometrial Cancer Reference

Updated: May 2025

RED FLAG: Clinical Presentation

Post-Menopausal Vaginal Bleeding (PVB)

Any PVB is significant. 5% to 10% of women with PVB have Endometrial Cancer.

1 Assess
Rule out cervical cause or HRT source.
2 Transvaginal Ultrasound
Check endometrial thickness.
3 Bold Action
If stripe > 4mm → Biopsy.

Diagnostic Algorithm

Trigger / Sign Action Required Urgency
PVB + Endometrial thickness > 4 mm Outpatient Endometrial Biopsy (Pipelle) or Hysteroscopy & D&C Urgent
PVB + Endometrial thickness ≤ 4 mm Observation (unless high risk/symptoms persist) Routine
Cervical Cytology: Atypical Glandular Cells Immediate Endometrial Sampling Urgent
Pre-menopausal Abnormal Bleeding (Age >35) Endometrial Biopsy to rule out cancer Standard

Immediate Management Strategy

1. Staging

Surgical staging is standard (replaced clinical staging).

2. Specialist Referral

Refer to Gynecological Oncologist immediately upon biopsy confirmation.

3. Molecular Testing

Mandatory IHC for MMR status (Lynch Syndrome screen) for all tumors.

Type I (Endometrioid) - 80%

Hormone Dependent

  • Associated with unopposed estrogen.
  • Common in obesity, hyperlipidemia.
  • Prognosis: More favorable.
  • Genetics: PTEN mutation, Microsatellite instability.

Type II (Non-Endometrioid) - 20%

Aggressive

  • Serous, Clear Cell, Carcinosarcoma.
  • Arise in atrophic endometrium.
  • Older women, higher risk of early spread.
  • Genetics: TP53 mutation, HER2 amplification.

Key Genetic Drivers

Gene/Marker Association
PTENTumor suppressor loss (Early event in Type I).
K-rasMutation in 24% of endometrioid adenocarcinomas.
p53Mutated in >90% of serous tumors (Type II).
HER-2/neuAmplification in serous cancers (poor prognosis).
MMR GenesMLH1, MSH2, MSH6, PMS2 defects (Lynch Syndrome).

Treatment Summary

  • Surgery: Total hysterectomy + bilateral salpingo-oophorectomy is primary treatment.
  • Radiotherapy: Adjuvant vaginal brachytherapy/pelvic EBRT improves progression-free survival in high-intermediate/high-risk disease (but not overall survival).
  • Chemo/Immunotherapy: For Stage III/IV or recurrent disease.
  • Hormonal: Palliative option for recurrent disease or non-endometrioid carcinomas if receptor positive.

Key Risk Factors

  • Obesity: The most important factor. Risk triples with BMI ≥30.
  • Age > 50: 75% of diagnoses are ≥55 years.
  • Tamoxifen Use: 2-7x increased risk in post-menopausal women.
  • Unopposed Estrogen: HRT or anovulation (PCOS).
  • Family History: Lynch Syndrome (Colorectal/Endometrial).

Genetics & Prevention

Lynch Syndrome Screening

Screen for Lynch syndrome if significant family history of colorectal/endometrial/ovarian cancer.

Action: Immunohistochemistry for MMR proteins (MLH1, MSH2, MSH6, PMS2).

Primary Prevention

  • Weight management.
  • Combined oral contraceptives (reduce risk).
