Vaginal Discharge – Rapid Reference

NICE CKS 2024 • One-page clinical aide

1. Physiological Discharge? Normal

  • Clear / white, non-offensive, cyclical variation
  • No itch, pain, odor or bleeding
→ Reassure + hygiene advice only

2. Red Flags URGENT

  • PID suspected: bilateral lower abdominal pain ± fever, cervical motion tenderness
  • Cancer suspected: post-menopausal bleeding, persistent unexplained bloody discharge
→ Admit / 2-week-wait gynae referral

3. High STI Risk?

Age < 25 New/casual partners Multiple partners Previous STI Black ethnicity
If YES → Offer full STI screen (GUM)

6. Swab or Refer to GUM

  • High STI risk or partner notification
  • Pregnancy / postpartum / post-surgery / IUD ≤3 wks
  • Recurrent, treatment failure, mixed picture
  • Unable to attend GUM → follow individual CKS protocols

7. Non-infective Causes

Foreign body
Remove + review
Contact dermatitis
Stop product, emollients
Menopause
Topical oestrogen
Lesions
Biopsy / gynae referral

8. Safety-netting Script

“Return if discharge persists >2 weeks, worsens, new pain, bleeding, fever, or pregnancy symptoms.”
