Definition Physiology
  • Monthly cycle of blood loss via vagina.
  • Result of uterine lining breakdown when implantation does not occur.
  • Indicates functioning hormonal controls and reproductive tract response.
  • Not a direct sign of ovulation.
Normal Ranges Safe
Cycle Length: 21 - 35 Days
Duration: 4 - 5 Days (Range 3-7)
Blood Loss: 5 - 80 ml per menses
Menarche Age: Avg 13 (Normal: 8 - 16)
Hormonal Axis
  • Hypothalamus: GnRH (LHRH, FSHRH)
  • Pituitary: Gonadotrophins (FSH, LH)
  • Ovary: Oestrogen & Progesterone
  • FSH rise ‚Üí Follicles mature. LH surge ‚Üí Ovulation (16-32h). Progesterone ‚Üí Prepares endometrium.

Immediate Attention Required
Pregnancy complications, malignancy, or systemic disorders require specific investigation.
Puberty Flags Investigate
  • Precocious: Before 8 years old.
  • Delayed: After 15 (or 16) years old.
  • Possible causes: Leukemia, eating disorders, genetic conditions (Turner syndrome), or hormonal failure.
Structural/Reproductive Risks High Priority
  • Pregnancy Complications: Threatened/missed abortion, Ectopic pregnancy.
  • Trophoblastic Disease: Consider if recent pregnancy.
  • Malignancies: Endometrial, cervical, ovarian carcinoma.
  • Postcoital Spotting: Cervical lesions/polyps.
Systemic/Clotting Medical
  • Blood Clotting Failure: Von Willebrand's disease, prothrombin deficiency.
  • Hypersplenism / Leukemia: Can affect platelets/clotting.
  • Hypothyroidism: May cause menorrhagia or IMB.
  • Cirrhosis: Reduced estrogen metabolism + hypoprothrombinaemia.
Types of Disorders
  • Menorrhagia: Excessive loss (>80ml) interfering with QoL. Can cause iron-deficiency anaemia.
  • Polymenorrhoea: Too frequent (>1 per calendar month).
  • Oligomenorrhoea: Infrequent.
  • Amenorrhoea: Absent.
  • Dysmenorrhoea: Painful periods.
Structural Causes
  • Uterine Fibroids.
  • Endometrial Polyps.
  • Adenomyosis.
  • Endometritis: Usually presents as intermenstrual spotting.
  • Cervical Lesions: Erosions, polyps, cervicitis.
Other Contributing Factors
  • Iatrogenic: Contraceptives (COC/POP breakthrough bleeding), HRT, Chemo/Irradiation.
  • Lactation: Delays return of menstruation.
  • Weight & Stress: Rapid weight change, anorexia, emotional stress, significant illness.
  • Medication: Psychotropics (e.g. risperidone).
DUB (Dysfunctional Uterine Bleeding) Diagnosis of Exclusion

Abnormal uterine bleeding in the absence of organic disease. Can only be diagnosed after all other causes (structural, pregnancy, systemic) have been excluded. Pathophysiology largely unknown.

Routine Coping
Pain Relief Period Pains: Respond well to anti-inflammatories (e.g., Mefenamic acid).
Hygiene Wide range of towels/tampons. Use combination overnight.
Postponement

To delay cycle (e.g., holidays):

  • Medroxyprogesterone acetate: 10 mg bd or tds. Start 3 days before anticipated onset. Bleeding occurs ~3 days after stopping.
  • Extended COC: Run packs together, omit pill-free week (Tricycling). Safe, but watch for breakthrough bleeding.
Investigation Strategy
  • Pregnancy Test: Exclude pregnancy first.
  • Bloods: FBC (Anaemia), Clotting screen, Thyroid function.
  • Imaging: Ultrasound for structural pathology (fibroids/polyps).
  • Biopsy: If malignancy suspected or age appropriate.
