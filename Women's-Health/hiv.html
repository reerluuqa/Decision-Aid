Post‚Äëexposure prophylaxis (PEP)

Exposure to HIV is a medical emergency ‚Äî act fast

Start as soon as possible (ideally within 24h; within 72h maximum) and arrange specialist follow‚Äëup & baseline tests (HIV, HBV, HCV).

Occupational exposure ‚Äî immediate first aid

  • Wash skin/wounds with soap & water; irrigate mucous membranes with water.
  • Irrigate eyes with saline/water; remove contact lenses first.
  • Encourage bleeding from puncture wounds; do not suck. Clean small wounds with antiseptic.
  • Follow local 24/7 pathway (usually via A&E) for urgent advice/PEP; report to HSE (RIDDOR) per policy.
