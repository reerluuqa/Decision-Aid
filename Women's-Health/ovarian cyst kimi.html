Red Flags

  • • Acute abdomen/shock
  • • Elevated CA-125 >35 U/mL (post-menopausal)
  • • Solid/complex cysts in post-menopausal
  • • Nodular/fixed pelvic mass
  • • Ascites or metastasis

Assessment

Comprehensive evaluation of ovarian cyst presentation and risk factors

History & Examination

  • Pelvic Pain - Dull, pressure-like, exacerbated by movement/intercourse Common
  • Bloating & Early Satiety - <12 months duration, >12 times/month Malignancy Risk
  • Palpable Adnexal Mass - Bimanual exam sensitivity 28% Variable
  • Urinary Frequency - Bladder compression symptoms Common
  • Acute Abdomen - Consider torsion/rupture Emergency

Risk Factors

Strong Risk Factors

  • Pre-menopausal age group Protective
  • Early menarche
  • First trimester pregnancy
  • Infertility treatment / PCOS
  • Increased gonadotrophins
  • Tamoxifen therapy
  • Endometriosis Cancer Risk

Weak Risk Factors

  • Cigarette smoking 3x risk mucinous tumors

Investigations

  • Transvaginal Ultrasound - First-line imaging Essential
    Characterize size, consistency, location, nodularity
  • CA-125 - Tumor marker Post-menopausal >35 U/mL
    Elevated in 80% epithelial ovarian cancer
  • Color Doppler - Assess vascularity
    Resistive & pulsatility indices
  • MRI/CT - Complex cases Selective
    CT for metastases, MRI for soft tissue
  • Germ Cell Markers - Women <40 years
    AFP, β-hCG, LDH
  • Laparoscopy - Diagnostic & therapeutic If US non-diagnostic

Differential Diagnoses

  • Ovarian Cancer - Multiloculated, solid areas, CA-125 ≥35 Rule Out
  • Ovarian Torsion - Absent venous flow on Doppler Emergency
  • Polycystic Ovary Syndrome - ≥12 follicles 2-9mm Benign
  • Uterine Fibroids - Solid mass projecting from uterus Common
  • Pelvic Inflammatory Disease - Purulent discharge, tenderness Infection
  • Endometrioma - Endometrial-like lining cells Cancer potential
  • Ectopic Pregnancy - Positive β-hCG, empty uterus Emergency

Diagnosis

Risk stratification tools and diagnostic criteria

Risk of Malignancy Index (RMI)

RMI = U × M × CA-125

U = Ultrasound Score 0 = 0, 1 = 1 point, 3 = 2-5 points
Points for: multilocular, solid areas, metastases, ascites, bilateral lesions
M = Menopausal Status 1 = Pre-menopausal
3 = Post-menopausal
CA-125 Serum level in U/mL

Risk Categories:

Low: RMI <25 (<3% cancer risk) Moderate: RMI 25-250 (20% cancer risk) High: RMI >250 (75% cancer risk)

DePriest Morphology Index

  • Tumor Volume - <10cm³, 10-50cm³, >50-200cm³, >200cm³ Score 0-4
  • Wall Structure - Smooth <3mm, smooth >3mm, papillary <3mm, papillary >3mm Score 0-4
  • Septal Structure - No septa, thin <3mm, thick 3-10mm, solid area >10mm Score 0-4
  • Total Score - Range 0-12 <5: Benign (100% NPV)
  • Post-menopausal - Score ≥5 PPV 45% for malignancy

ACOG Referral Guidelines

Post-menopausal Women

  • CA-125 >35 U/mL
  • Ascites present
  • Nodular or fixed pelvic mass
  • Evidence of metastasis
  • Family history of ovarian/breast cancer

Pre-menopausal Women

  • CA-125 >200 U/mL
  • Ascites present
  • Evidence of metastasis
  • Family history of ovarian/breast cancer

Genetic Risk Assessment

>20-25% Risk - Refer

  • Personal history of breast AND ovarian cancer
  • Ovarian cancer + close relative with ovarian/pre-menopausal breast cancer
  • Ashkenazi Jewish ancestry with ovarian cancer
  • Breast cancer ≤50 years + close relative with ovarian/male breast cancer
  • Known BRCA-1/BRCA-2 mutation in family

5-10% Risk - Consider

  • Breast cancer ≤40 years
  • High-grade serous ovarian cancer at any age
  • Bilateral breast cancer (first case ≤50 years)
  • Two+ close relatives with breast cancer

Treatment

Management algorithms based on patient group and cyst characteristics

Treatment Algorithm Overview

Acutely Unwell / Acute Abdomen Emergency

• Laparoscopy or laparotomy with resuscitation

• IV broad-spectrum antibiotics (cyst rupture/abscess)

• Consider ovarian torsion/rupture/hemorrhage

Pre-menopausal Simple Cyst Conservative

• Expectant management if <5-7cm and asymptomatic

• Serial ultrasound every 2-6 months for characterization

• Laparoscopy if persistent/enlarging >3 months

Pre-menopausal Complex/Solid Cyst Monitor

• Repeat ultrasound in 2-3 months if benign appearance

• Laparoscopy if persistent (<10cm, low suspicion)

• Laparotomy + oncology referral if solid or suspicious

Post-menopausal Simple Cyst Conservative

• Serial US + CA-125 every 2-3 months if <10cm + normal CA-125

• No follow-up needed for cysts ≤3cm per SRU guidelines

• Surgical evaluation if increasing size or morphology

Post-menopausal Complex/Solid Cyst Oncology Referral

• Refer to gynecological oncologist for surgical evaluation

• Laparotomy by experienced oncologist

• Complete staging and lymph node exploration

Pregnancy Most Resolve

• Conservative observation for simple/complex <8cm

• Laparoscopy for symptomatic or >8cm cysts

• Laparotomy in 2nd trimester if malignant features

• 70% spontaneous resolution rate for complex cysts

Medical Management

  • Oral Contraceptives - Do NOT hasten resolution No Effect
  • Antibiotics - For tubo-ovarian abscess/PID Cefoxitin + Doxycycline
  • Progesterone Support - If corpus luteum removed <12 weeks Pregnancy
  • Analgesia - NSAIDs for pain management Symptomatic

Surgical Options

  • Laparoscopy - Minimally invasive, low morbidity Benign-appearing <10cm
  • Mini-laparotomy - Alternative to laparoscopy Obesity, pulmonary disease
  • Laparotomy - Open surgery for malignant suspicion Oncology cases
  • Cystectomy - Cyst removal preserving ovary Pre-menopausal
  • Oophorectomy - Ovary removal when necessary Post-menopausal/malignant

Follow-up

Monitoring protocols and prognosis indicators

Monitoring Protocols

Pre-menopausal

  • Simple cysts >5-7cm: US every 2-6 months
  • Complex cysts: US every 2-3 months
  • Pelvic exam at each visit

Post-menopausal

  • Simple cysts 3-5cm: US every 3-6 months
  • CA-125 at each follow-up visit
  • Rectovaginal exam (if feasible)

Pregnancy

  • Serial US to monitor growth/torsion
  • Absent venous flow → urgent intervention
  • Most resolve by 16-20 weeks

Complications

  • Cyst Spillage - 18% laparoscopy vs 1% laparotomy Medium Risk
  • Cyst Rupture - Intraperitoneal bleed (corpus luteum) Low Risk
  • Ovarian Torsion - 14.8% of surgically treated masses 14.8% Rate
  • Dyspareunia - Pain with intercourse Low Risk
  • Malignant Transformation - Small percentage in endometriomas Rare

Prognosis & Resolution Rates

Pre-menopausal

  • Simple cysts <6cm: 50% resolution at 6 months, 75% at 75 months
  • Complex cysts: 8.3% spontaneous resolution
  • Recurrence after laparoscopy: 7.6%

Post-menopausal

  • Simple cysts <10cm: 69.4% resolution, <0.1% malignancy risk
  • Complex cysts: 36-39% malignancy frequency
  • Multiloculated solid tumors: Do not manage expectantly

Pregnancy

  • Complex cysts: 70% spontaneous resolution
  • Simple cysts: Nearly 100% resolution
  • Malignancy risk: 1 in 12,000 to 47,000

Patient Education

  • Symptom Monitoring - Report bloating, early satiety, urinary changes, pelvic pain Essential
  • Activity Restrictions - Avoid vigorous exercise/intercourse with large cysts Prevention
  • Combined Oral Contraceptives - 40% relative risk reduction for ovarian cancer Long-term use benefit
  • Prophylactic Oophorectomy - BRCA-1/BRCA-2 carriers High-risk groups
