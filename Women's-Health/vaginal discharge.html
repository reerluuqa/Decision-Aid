Vaginal Discharge Clinical Algorithm

A quick reference guide for busy physicians

Initial Assessment

History

  • Characteristics: onset, duration, color, smell, consistency
  • Associated symptoms: itch, pain, dysuria, bleeding
  • Exacerbating factors (e.g., after intercourse)
  • Previous treatments and response
  • Vaginal products used
  • STI risk factors: <25 years, new/multiple partners, previous STI, Black ethnicity

Examination

  • May be omitted if:
    • History suggests BV or candidiasis
    • Low STI risk
    • No upper genital tract symptoms
  • If indicated: inspect vulva, speculum exam, pH testing (normal <4.5)

Diagnostic Approach

Vaginal Discharge
PHYSIOLOGICAL

White/clear, mucus-like, varies with cycle

PATHOLOGICAL
INFECTIVE CAUSES
  • Non-STI: Bacterial vaginosis (pH ≥4.5), Vulvovaginal candidiasis (pH <4.5)
  • STI: Chlamydia, Gonorrhoea, Trichomoniasis, Pelvic inflammatory disease
NON-INFECTIVE CAUSES
  • Inadequate hygiene, Foreign body, Irritant/allergic vaginitis
  • Behçet's syndrome, Desquamative inflammatory vaginitis
  • Erosive lichen planus, Vaginal fistula, Gynaecological cancers
  • Genitourinary syndrome of menopause

RED FLAGS

  • Pregnancy + suspected PID → Urgent hospital admission
  • Severe systemic symptoms (fever >38°C, nausea, vomiting) → Urgent hospital admission
  • Adnexal mass → Urgent hospital admission
  • Suspected gynaecological cancer → Urgent suspected cancer referral

CONFIRMED CONDITIONS

  • Chlamydia, gonorrhoea, trichomoniasis → Refer to GUM clinic
  • Pelvic inflammatory disease → Refer to GUM clinic
  • Bacterial vaginosis → See specific CKS topic
  • Vulvovaginal candidiasis → See specific CKS topic
  • Cervicitis → Treat for chlamydia while awaiting swab results

NO IDENTIFIABLE CAUSE

  • Reassure likely physiological
  • Advise on hygiene
  • Avoid feminine hygiene products
  • Return if symptoms persist/worsen

REFER TO GUM CLINIC

  • Persistent or recurrent symptoms
  • Doubt about diagnosis
  • High STI risk

Investigations

High STI Risk

Test for chlamydia, gonorrhoea, trichomoniasis, HIV, syphilis

Consider Pregnancy Test

To exclude pregnancy in relevant cases

Urine Dipstick

To exclude urinary tract infection

High Vaginal Swabs

If symptoms inconsistent, pregnant/postpartum, recurrent symptoms, or poor response to treatment
