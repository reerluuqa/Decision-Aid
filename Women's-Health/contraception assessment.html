Contraception Assessment Algorithm

A Quick Reference Aide-Memoire for Clinicians

1Initial Patient Assessment & Discussion

Establish Patient Needs

  • Discuss preferences, reproductive goals, lifestyle, and partner's views.
  • Provide information on all available methods, highlighting the high efficacy of Long-Acting Reversible Contraception (LARC): Implants, IUDs, Injectables.
  • Compare methods based on efficacy (perfect vs. typical use), side effects, risks/benefits, and return to fertility.

Screening & Safeguarding

  • STI Risk: Assess and offer screening if appropriate. Promote condom use for dual protection.
  • Vulnerability: Assess for sexual abuse or coercion.
  • Under 16: Assess using Fraser Criteria (competence to consent). Document clearly.

2Exclude Pregnancy

You can be "Reasonably Certain" a patient is NOT pregnant if they have no signs/symptoms of pregnancy AND one of the following is true:

  • It is within the first 5 days of a normal menstrual period.
  • They are <21 days postpartum (and not breastfeeding).
  • They are <6 months postpartum, fully breastfeeding, and amenorrhoeic.
  • It is within the first 5 days after an abortion or miscarriage.
  • They have been correctly and consistently using a reliable contraceptive method.
  • They have not had intercourse for >21 days and have a negative high-sensitivity pregnancy test.

If pregnancy cannot be excluded: Offer Emergency Contraception (EC). Consider "Quick Starting" a suitable method (e.g., implant, POP, CHC) after discussing risks and advising a follow-up pregnancy test.

4Assess for Key Drug Interactions

Enzyme-Inducing Drugs

(e.g., Carbamazepine, Phenytoin, Rifampicin, St John's Wort)
Significantly reduce the effectiveness of CHC, POP, and the Implant.
Recommended Methods: Cu-IUD, LNG-IUD, or the Progestogen-Only Injectable.

Teratogenic Drugs

(e.g., Sodium Valproate, Retinoids, Lithium)
Pregnancy prevention is critical. Strongly advise a highly effective LARC method (Implant or IUC). The injectable is also suitable if compliance is documented. Avoid relying on user-dependent methods.

5Final Method-Specific Considerations

  • Intrauterine Contraception (IUC): Requires a bimanual pelvic exam prior to insertion. Assess for STI risk before fitting.
  • Progestogen-Only Injectable (DMPA): Associated with a temporary reduction in bone mineral density. Use with caution in those with significant osteoporosis risk factors. Consider alternatives for adolescents unless other methods are unsuitable.
  • Sterilization (Male or Female): Counsel thoroughly on permanence and the risk of regret, especially in those <30 years old or without children. Offer LARC methods as highly effective, reversible alternatives.
