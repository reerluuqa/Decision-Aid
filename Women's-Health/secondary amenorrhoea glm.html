Secondary Amenorrhoea

Clinical Assessment & Decision Support
ALWAYS ASSESS FOR PREGNANCY FIRST

Definition

No menses for >3 months in a female who previously had regular cycles, or no menses for >6 months in a female with previous irregular cycles.

Key Stats

Prevalence: ~3-4% (higher in athletes: 20-56%). Common causes: PCOS, Hypothalamic dysfunction, POI, Hyperprolactinaemia.

Urgent Considerations & Red Flags

Pregnancy Rule out in any patient of reproductive age. This is the #1 priority.
Neurological Symptoms Headache, visual field changes? Requires MRI to rule out pituitary adenoma/prolactinoma.
Rapid Virilisation Sudden onset male-pattern hair, clitoromegaly, deepening voice? Rule out Androgen-secreting tumour (Ovarian/Adrenal).
Acute Menorrhagia Patient with history of anovulation now bleeding heavily? Check for symptomatic anaemia.

Diagnostic Approach

1

History & Physical

  • History: Last LMP, contraceptives, stress, exercise, weight changes, galactorrhoea, headaches, medications (antipsychotics, opioids).
  • Exam: BMI (low?), Tanner staging, hirsutism (Ferriman-Gallwey), acne, acanthosis nigricans (PCOS), visual fields (pituitary).
2

1st Line Labs (After Negative Pregnancy Test)

FSH Prolactin TSH
  • FSH: Differentiates Ovarian Failure (High) vs Hypothalamic/Pituitary (Low/Normal).
  • Prolactin: Elevated? Check for meds, hypothyroidism, or prolactinoma (>100 ng/mL).
  • TSH: Screen for thyroid dysfunction (can cause amenorrhoea).
3

Secondary Labs / Imaging

  • Estradiol: Low in POI or Hypothalamic suppression.
  • Androgens (Testosterone/DHEA-S): If hirsutism/virilisation. High levels -> PCOS vs Tumor.
  • Pelvic Ultrasound: Anatomy, endometrial thickness, ovarian morphology (PCOS).
  • Progestogen Challenge: Assess estrogen status/outflow tract (Optional, largely replaced by labs/US).

Normal → Other causes
TSH

Androgens
Markedly High → Tumor (Ovarian/Adrenal)
Moderate High → PCOS, NCAH

Key Differentials at a Glance

Condition Key History / Exam Classic Lab Findings Action
PCOS Obesity, Hirsutism, Acne, Irregular cycles Normal FSH, Elevated LH (Ratio >3), High Androgens Lifestyle mod, OCPs, Metformin
Functional Hypothalamic Low BMI, Stress, Exercise, Eating disorder Low/Normal FSH, Low Estradiol Weight gain, Stress reduction, Diet
Premature Ovarian Insufficiency Age <40, Hot flashes, Vaginal dryness High FSH (>40), Low Estradiol HRT, Bone health, Fertility counseling
Hyperprolactinaemia Galactorrhoea, Headache, Visual defects High Prolactin, Low/Normal FSH MRI if >100ng/mL, Dopamine agonists
Hypothyroidism Fatigue, Cold intolerance, Weight gain High TSH, Prolactin may be mildly elevated Levothyroxine
Asherman's Syndrome Prior D&C, Infection, Scarring Normal Hormones, No bleed on challenge Hysteroscopy (Gold standard)
Androgen Tumor Rapid Virilisation, Clitoromegaly Very High Testosterone Urgent imaging (US/CT/MRI), Surgery
