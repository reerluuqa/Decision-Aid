Genital Herpes – Quick-Reference Algorithm

1. Is it Genital Herpes?

First Episode
Painful clustered vesicles → ulcers, bilateral, systemic flu-like, ≤ 3 weeks.
Recurrent
Prodrome (tingle/burn 48 h), unilateral, milder, 6–12 days.

Confirm with PCR swab (sexual-health clinic preferred). Diff Dx: syphilis, shingles, candida, trauma, Behçet.

2. Immediate Actions

  • Red flags: systemically unwell, urinary retention, pregnancy, immunocompromise → Emergency referral
  • Counsel: abstain while lesions/prodrome; condoms reduce but do not abolish risk; screen partners
  • Self-care: saline baths, paracetamol/ibuprofen, lidocaine 5 % gel, dilute urine, loose clothing

3. Treatment Pathways

First Episode
Aciclovir 400 mg TDS or Valaciclovir 500 mg BD × 5 days (extend to 10 if new lesions)
Recurrent – Episodic
Aciclovir 800 mg TDS × 2 days or Famciclovir 1 g BD × 1 day or Valaciclovir 500 mg BD × 3 days
Recurrent – Suppressive
Aciclovir 400 mg BD or Valaciclovir 500 mg OD for 1 year, then reassess
Immunocompromise / HIV
Double standard dose; 5–10 days; seek specialist if breakthrough lesions

Never topical antivirals.

4. When to Refer

  • Uncertain diagnosis
  • Pregnancy (any gestation)
  • Immunocompromise with severe / non-responding lesions
  • ≥ 6 episodes/yr despite suppressive therapy

5. Pregnancy Pearls

  • Primary episode in 3rd trimester → highest neonatal risk → urgent obstetric & sexual-health liaison
  • Recurrent lesions at delivery → low risk; still inform obstetrics
