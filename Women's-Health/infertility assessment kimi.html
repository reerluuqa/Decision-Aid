Infertility Assessment & Management

Clinical decision support algorithm based on Patient.info guidelines (June 2023) & NICE recommendations

Assessment & Initial Investigation

Definition & Prevalence

  • Failure to achieve pregnancy after 12 months regular unprotected intercourse WHO
  • 84% conceive within 1 year, 92% within 2 years with regular intercourse
  • Affects ~1 in 7 UK couples

When to Investigate

  • Start investigations after 1 year of trying Standard
  • Offer earlier assessment if risk factors present Earlier
  • Over 80% conceive within 1 year if woman <40 years

Female Investigations

  • Mid-luteal progesterone: Confirm ovulation (day 21 of 28-day cycle)
  • STI screening: Chlamydia serology
  • Thyroid function: If symptomatic
  • Prolactin: If galactorrhoea, PCOS symptoms, or pituitary concerns
  • Gonadotrophins (FSH/LH): For irregular cycles to identify ovulation disorders

Male Investigations

  • Semen analysis: Refer if 2 abnormal results Key test
  • STI screening: Chlamydia serology

General Care & Advice

  • Folic acid: 0.4mg daily (5mg if high risk) Essential
  • Intercourse frequency: Every 2-3 days optimizes conception
  • Alcohol: Men within safe limits; women avoid when pregnant
  • Smoking: Adversely affects fertility in both Quit support
  • Weight: BMI 19-30 optimal range
  • Alternative therapies: No proven benefit, may be harmful

Referral Criteria

  • Woman <36 years: Refer after 1 year if investigations normal
  • Woman ≥36 years: Refer after 6 months Earlier referral
  • Women with: Amenorrhoea/oligomenorrhoea, pelvic surgery history, PID, STI, abnormal examination Do not delay
  • Men with: Genital surgery, urogenital issues, varicocele, abnormal examination Do not delay

Ensure counseling offered before, during, after investigation

Diagnosis & Etiology

Causes of Infertility

  • Unexplained infertility: No identified cause (most common)
  • Ovulatory disorders: 21% of cases
  • Tubal damage: 14% of cases
  • Male factors: 26% of cases
  • Uterine/peritoneal disorders: 14% of cases
  • Mixed factors: ~40% have disorders in both partners
  • Endometriosis: Significant contributing factor

WHO Ovulation Disorder Classification

WHO Group I Hypothalamic-pituitary failure

  • Hypothalamic amenorrhoea or hypogonadotrophic hypogonadism
  • Low gonadotrophins, low estrogen
  • Treat with weight gain if BMI <19, moderate exercise

WHO Group II PCOS-related

  • Hypothalamic-pituitary-ovarian dysfunction
  • Normal gonadotrophins, normal estrogen
  • Predominantly PCOS

WHO Group III Ovarian failure

  • Ovarian failure or hypergonadotrophic hypogonadism
  • High gonadotrophins, low estrogen
  • Premature ovarian insufficiency

Important Diagnostic Notes

  • 40% of cases involve both partners Dual evaluation
  • Lifestyle factors (smoking, obesity) adversely affect fertility
  • Consider genetic testing if severe male factor
  • Antisperm antibodies significance unclear
Treatment Options

Medical Treatment

  • Clomifene citrate: Anti-oestrogen, first-line for WHO Group II (PCOS) First line
  • Metformin: Consider with clomifene for PCOS
  • Gonadotrophins: For clomifene-resistant cases, WHO Group I Second line
  • Dopamine agonists: For hyperprolactinaemia
  • Pulsatile GnRH: For WHO Group I disorders

Monitor with ultrasound, max 6 cycles clomifene

Surgical Treatment

  • Tubal microsurgery: For mild tubal disease, proximal blockage
  • Laparoscopic ovarian drilling: For clomifene-resistant PCOS Second line
  • Endometriosis surgery: Ablation/resection + adhesiolysis
  • Epididymal surgery: For obstructive azoospermia
  • Ovarian tissue: Removal/preservation/reimplantation after gonadotoxic treatment NICE approved

Varicocele surgery NOT recommended for fertility

Assisted Conception

Intrauterine Insemination (IUI) Less invasive

  • Prepared sperm into uterus at ovulation
  • Consider for: physical disability, HIV (after sperm washing), same-sex couples
  • NOT routinely offered for unexplained/mild endometriosis/mild male factor Consider IVF instead

In Vitro Fertilisation (IVF) Standard ART

  • Ovarian stimulation → egg retrieval → fertilisation → embryo transfer
  • Single embryo transfer recommended if top-quality blastocyst available Reduces multiples
  • Success rates: 32% live birth per embryo <35 years, <5% >43 years

ICSI Severe male factor

  • Single sperm injected directly into oocyte
  • For: severe semen deficits, obstructive/non-obstructive azoospermia, failed IVF
  • Check karyotype if severe male factor

Donor Options Third party

  • Donor insemination: Azoospermia, severe deficits, genetic/infectious risk
  • Oocyte donation: Premature ovarian failure, post-chemotherapy, IVF failures
  • Embryo donation: From successful IVF/ICSI couples

Male Factor Treatments

  • Gonadotrophins: For hypogonadotrophic hypogonadism Effective
  • Orchidopexy: For cryptorchidism (uncertain age effect)
  • Testicular biopsy: For non-obstructive azoospermia
  • NO antioestrogens/androgens: No proven benefit Not recommended
  • NO antibiotics: Unless identified infection
Follow-up & Complications

Complications of Assisted Conception

Ovarian Hyperstimulation Syndrome (OHSS) Potentially serious

  • Mild: Lower abdominal discomfort, nausea, bloating
  • Severe: Ascites, rapid weight gain, tachycardia, hypotension, oliguria Hospitalize
  • Incidence reduced by careful tailoring of drugs/techniques
  • Cabergoline may be beneficial for prevention

Multiple Pregnancy Common risk

  • Elective single embryo transfer reduces risk
  • Multiple birth rate: 6% in 2019 (down from 28% in 1990s)
  • Single embryo transfer: 75% of cycles in 2019

Safety & Long-term Outcomes

  • No direct association: Between ovulation induction and invasive cancer
  • No short/medium-term risks: Adverse outcomes in children
  • Small increased risk: Borderline ovarian tumours with IVF
  • Lowest effective dose: Keep stimulation agents minimal
  • Long-term data: Still being collected

Psychological Support

  • Fertility problems cause significant psychological stress Recognize impact
  • Offer counseling before, during, after treatment
  • Recommend fertility support groups
  • Stress can adversely affect relationships and fertility

Key Statistics

  • Single embryo transfer: 75% of cycles (2019)
  • Live birth rates <35 years: 32% per embryo
  • Live birth rates >43 years: <5% per embryo
  • Donor eggs: >30% success across all ages
  • Frozen embryo transfers: Increased 86% (2014-2019)
