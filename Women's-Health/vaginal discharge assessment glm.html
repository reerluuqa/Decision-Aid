Bacterial Vaginosis Common
  • Thin, Grayish-white discharge
  • Fishy Odor (Whiff test +)
  • Rarely itchy/painful
pH Level > 4.5
Wet Mount Clue Cells
Aetiology & Notes

Reduction of lactobacilli; overgrowth of Gardnerella/anaerobes. Do NOT routinely treat asymptomatic patients unless pre-op or pregnant with high risk.

Trichomoniasis STI
  • Purulent, Malodorous
  • Can be Green/Yellow, Frothy
  • Strawberry Cervix (rare)
pH Level > 4.5
Wet Mount Motile Trichomonads
Important Actions

Concurrent treatment of sex partners is MANDATORY. Screen for HIV.

Vulvovaginal Candidiasis (VVC) Common
  • Intense Pruritus (Itch)
  • White, Curd-like ("Cottage Cheese")
  • Minimal odor
pH Level 4.0 - 4.5 (Normal)
Wet Mount Hyphae / Yeast
Risk Factors

Diabetes, antibiotics, high estrogen, immunosuppression. Not an STI.

Paediatric Consideration

In pre-pubertal patients: Discharge is often NOT infectious.
Common causes: Foreign body (45%), Poor hygiene, Non-specific vaginitis.

pH Decoder

Normal Vaginal pH is acidic.

3.5 (Normal) 4.5 6.0 (High)
  • ≤ 4.5: Normal, VVC (Candida)
  • > 4.5: BV, Trichomoniasis, GSM
First-Line Tests
  • History & Speculum Exam
    Visualize lesions, source of discharge.
  • pH Testing
    Litmus paper essential first step.
  • Wet Mount
    Saline (Clue cells/Trich) + KOH (Whiff/Yeast).
Tip: If wet mount is negative but pH > 4.5, consider NAATs for Trichomoniasis or BV.
Is it Normal?

If discharge is transparent/white, odorless, and pH is normal:

REASSURE

Physiological discharge increases with ovulation, pregnancy, or estrogen therapy.
