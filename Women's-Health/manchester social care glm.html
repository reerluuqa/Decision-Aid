Urgent & Emergency Pathways

Same-day support to prevent hospital admission (2hr response standard)

Out-of-Hours Emergency (EDS)

When: Evenings (5pm-9am), Weekends, Bank Holidays.

For: Carer breakdown, sudden loss of care, urgent safeguarding, homelessness, respite needed overnight.

0161 234 5001 Call EDS

Community Crisis Response (UCR)

Goal: 2-hour response to avoid A&E. Frailty, falls, infections, palliative crises.

Team: ACPs, Nurses, OTs, Social Workers. Can do IVs, bloods, equipment, temporary care packages.

Hours: 8:30am ‚Äì 10:00pm, 7 days a week.

Emergency Residential Placement (Step-Up)

If home is unsafe even with carers (e.g. carer hospitalized), Social Services can arrange a temporary care home bed via approved providers.

Action: Call 0161 234 5001 (Daytime). Explain need for "24hr urgent placement". EDS can authorize out of hours.

Routine Social Care Process

Manchester Local Care Organisation (MLCO) - Standard Pathway
1

Referral

Contact Centre (SPOA). Include NHS #, address, decline details.

2

Assessment

Social Worker visits home. Checks Care Act 2014 eligibility.

3

Planning

Strengths-based plan. Personal budget calculated.

4

Service

Council arranges care OR Direct Payments given to patient.

How to Refer

Single Point of Access: Manchester Adult Social Care Contact Centre.

  • Phone: 0161 234 5001
  • Email: mcsreply@manchester.gov.uk
  • Online: Council Adult Social Care Form

*Do NOT use online form for emergencies.

Eligibility (Care Act 2014)

Needs must arise from physical/mental impairment and result in inability to achieve outcomes (dressing, bathing, nutrition, safety) impacting wellbeing.

If not eligible -> Advice/info given to voluntary services.

Funding & Financial Assessment

Threshold: Savings > ¬£23,250 = Self-funder.

Process: Financial assessment done unless patient refuses.

Direct Payments: Patient receives budget to arrange own care (help available).

Brokerage Service

If Council arranges care, they use a centralized Brokerage Team.

Network: 1,800+ people receiving homecare via contracted providers.

Timeline: Routine takes few weeks (assessment days/weeks).

System Overview & Commissioning

Statutory Responsibility

Manchester City Council holds the statutory duty for Care Act assessments.

Delivered via MLCO (Manchester Local Care Organisation) ‚Äì a partnership of Council + NHS providers.

Commissioning

NHS Greater Manchester ICB (Manchester Locality) holds NHS commissioning since July 2022.

MHCC (Manchester Health and Care Commissioning) vision continues under ICB.

Chief Exec of Council is Place-Based Lead for ICB.

Integration Framework ("Our Healthier Manchester")

Joint health & social care strategy. Integrated Neighbourhood Teams work proactively.

Key Concept: "Joined-up care" ‚Äì pooling budgets, co-commissioning, reducing duplication.
