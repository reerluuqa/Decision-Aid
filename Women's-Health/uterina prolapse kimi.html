Uterine Prolapse

Straight to the point of care • Updated Jan 10, 2025
Definition & Overview

Loss of anatomical support for the uterus, typically at the vaginal apex.

Includes cystocele, rectocele, and enterocele. Often associated with hysterectomy.

Prevalence (US):
11.1% risk of surgery by age 80.
41% of women (50-79 yrs) have POP.
Clinical Presentation
  • Specific Sign: Seeing/feeling cervix/vagina protruding.
  • Sensation: Bulging, heaviness, pelvic pressure.
  • Urinary: Frequency, incontinence, incomplete emptying.
  • Defecatory: Dysfunction, splinting needed.
  • Pain: Dyspareunia, pelvic pain (advanced stages).
* Symptoms worsen after prolonged standing/physical exertion.
Risk Factors
Vaginal Delivery Advancing Age Obesity Family History White Ancestry Connective Tissue Disorders Chronic Constipation COPD Heavy Lifting

* Only 10-20% of women seek help. Nulliparous women can also be affected (e.g., post-hysterectomy).

POP-Q Staging (Reference)
Stage Extent of Prolapse (relative to hymen)
0 No prolapse
I > 1 cm above hymen
II Within 1 cm above/below hymen
III > 1 cm below hymen, but no further than total vaginal length - 2cm
IV Complete eversion (Procidentia)
Diagnostics & Tests

Exam

  • Resting & Straining: Supine and standing.
  • Speculum: Use single blade to support walls.
  • Reduction: Assess occult stress incontinence with cough test.

1st Line Tests

Post-void Residual (PVR)
Check for retention (Stage 3/4).
Urinalysis
Rule out UTI exacerbating incontinence.
Treatment Algorithm
1

Assess Symptoms & Anatomy

Is the patient symptomatic? Assess bladder emptying and renal function.

Asymptomatic? Symptomatic?
2a

Asymptomatic / Minor Symptoms

Primary: Observation with education.

Option: Pelvic Floor Muscle (PFM) training (supervised is more effective).

2b

Symptomatic Prolapse

Primary: Pessary (Mechanical support). High satisfaction. Treat erosion with estrogen if needed.

If Conservative Fails: Surgical Intervention.

3

Surgical Options

Reconstructive: Sacrocolpopexy (abdominal/vaginal), Sacrohysteropexy, Native tissue repair.

Obliterative: Colpocleisis (High success, for older/not sexually active).

Consider concurrent incontinence surgery (Burch or Sling) if stress incontinence exists or risk of de novo incontinence is high.

Complications & Warnings

Surgical Risks

  • Presacral bleeding (Life-threatening)
  • Bladder / Bowel injury
  • Ureteral injury (especially uterosacral suspension)
  • De novo stress incontinence (~40%)
⚠️ VAGINAL MESH BAN
No FDA-approved transvaginal mesh products for POP (2019). Do not use.
