Definition & Normal Values

What is Normal? Safe

Shorthand: K = 7/21-35

  • 7 days duration
  • 21-35 day cycle length
  • Average loss: 30-40 ml
  • Heaviest in first 3 days

Menorrhagia Definition

Bleeding that interferes with physical, social, emotional, and material quality of life.

Note: Objective definition is >80 ml blood loss.

Immediate Red Flags

Signs of Malignancy

  • Post-menopausal bleeding
  • Persistent IMB (Intermenstrual Bleeding)
  • Postcoital bleeding (PCB)
  • Cervical pathology on exam
ACTION: Two-week wait referral

Systemic Red Flags

  • Signs of severe anaemia (syncope, chest pain)
  • Bleeding disorders (e.g., von Willebrand's)
  • Pregnancy related (Ectopic/Miscarriage)
ACTION: Urgent assessment

High Risk History

  • Unexplained weight loss
  • Family history of gynae cancer
  • Tamoxifen use
ACTION: Low threshold for biopsy
Diagnosis & Investigations

History Focus

  • Pattern: Cycle length, duration of bleed, clots?
  • Impact: Time off work, social limitations, "flooding".
  • Contraception: IUCD? Anticoagulants?
  • Symptoms of Anaemia: Fatigue, pallor, shortness of breath.

Examination

  • General: BMI, Hirsutism (PCOS), Bruising (clotting), Pallor/Koilonychia (Anaemia).
  • Abdominal: Masses? Check before pelvic exam.
  • Pelvic: Not always needed for adolescents. Indicated if pathology suspected or LNG-IUS planned.

Essential Investigations Primary Care

1. Full Blood Count (FBC)

Mandatory for all presentations. Check for Iron Deficiency Anaemia.

2. Ultrasound (TVS)

First-line for structural abnormalities (fibroids/polyps). Endometrial thickness <12mm normal (pre-menopausal).

Pharmacological Management
1st Line Recommended

Levonorgestrel-releasing Intrauterine System (LNG-IUS / MirenaÂ®)

Action: Long-term treatment. Leave in situ for at least 12 months.

Why: Most effective medical treatment. Improves QoL, reduces bleeding significantly.

2nd Line (If LNG-IUS declined or unsuitable) Effective

Tranexamic Acid

Reduces loss: Up to 50%

Mechanism: Plasminogen-activator inhibitor.

Usage: Take during bleeding. Best for IUCD/fibroids/bleeding disorders.

*Stop if visual disturbance

NSAIDs (e.g., Mefenamic Acid)

Reduces loss: ~25%

Mechanism: Inhibits prostaglandin synthesis.

Usage: Also helps with dysmenorrhoea.

Combined Oral Contraceptive (COC)

Reduces loss: Up to 50%

Benefits: Regulates cycle, improves PMS, protects against endometrial/ovarian cancer.

3rd Line / Others Consider

Oral Progestogens

Norethisterone 5mg tds (days 5-26) or Cyclical progestogens.

Evidence suggests less effective than other options. Short-term use often preferred.

Ulipristal Acetate (Esmya)

For premenopausal women with fibroids (>3cm). Intermittent use only.

WARNING: Risk of serious liver injury. LFTs required.

Surgical & Procedural Options

Uterus < 10 weeks size

Recommended: Endometrial Ablation

  • First-line surgical option if fertility not required.
  • Removal of endometrium + superficial myometrium.
  • Types: Impedance-controlled bipolar, Balloon thermal, Microwave, etc.

Uterus > 10 weeks size

Options:

  • Uterine Artery Embolisation (UAE)
  • Hysteroscopic Myomectomy (Lower re-intervention rate than UAE per 2024 meta-analysis).

Hysterectomy

Definitive treatment. Consider when:

  • Other treatments failed/declined
  • Desire for amenorrhoea
  • No desire to retain uterus/fertility
  • Patient fully informed and requests it
