Initial Assessment

Excluding Pregnancy

Reasonably certain if ANY ONE criterion is met:

  • No intercourse since last normal period/childbirth/abortion
  • Consistent & correct use of reliable contraception
  • Within first 5 days of normal menstrual period
  • <21 days postpartum (non-breastfeeding)
  • Fully breastfeeding, amenorrhoeic, <6 months postpartum
  • Within first 5 days after abortion/miscarriage
  • No intercourse >21 days + negative high-sensitivity pregnancy test

History & Discussion

  • Preferred contraceptive method
  • Future pregnancy plans
  • Personal beliefs/views about contraception
  • Partner/family attitudes toward contraception
  • Medical history, medications, allergies
  • STI risk factors

Contraceptive Method Selection

Methods by Effectiveness

Special Populations

Under 16 Years

Assess Fraser criteria:

  • Understands advice
  • Cannot be persuaded to inform parents
  • Likely to continue intercourse
  • Physical/mental health likely to suffer without contraception
  • Best interests require treatment without parental consent

Comorbidities

  • Menorrhagia: LNG-IUD (1st line), COC (2nd line)
  • Abnormal bleeding: Investigate first; avoid implant/injectable
  • Ectopic pregnancy: All methods acceptable
  • Uterine fibroids: Hormonal methods OK; IUD only if no cavity distortion
  • Migraine with aura: Avoid CHC; progestogen-only/IUD acceptable
  • Hypertension: Avoid CHC if BP â‰¥145/90 or controlled with meds
  • VTE history: Avoid CHC; progestogen-only/IUD acceptable

Postpartum

  • Breastfeeding: Progestogen-only methods, IUD, implant
  • Non-breastfeeding: All methods acceptable after 21 days

Drug Interactions

Liver enzyme-inducers:

  • Avoid COC
  • Increased dose POP or implant/IUD preferred

Menopause

Continue contraception until:

  • 1 year after last period if >50
  • 2 years after last period if <50

STI Risk Assessment

Consider Testing If:

  • Under 25 years
  • New or multiple sexual partners
  • History of STIs
  • Inconsistent condom use
  • Men who have sex with men
  • Sex workers
  • From areas with high HIV prevalence

Quick Starting Contraception

Guidelines

If pregnancy excluded but immediate contraception needed:

  • Can quick start: CHC (except co-cyprindiol), POP, implant
  • Less preferred: Injectable (cannot be removed if pregnant)

Quick starting is outside the product license for many contraceptive methods but supported by FSRH guidelines.
