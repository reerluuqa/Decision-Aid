Red Flags & Urgent Findings
  • Fixed, Irregular Mass Hard, immobile mass with irregular borders — suspicious for malignancy
  • Skin Changes — Peau d'Orange Dimpling, retraction, or edema indicating advanced disease
  • Nipple Inversion / Paget's Disease New nipple retraction, excoriation, or ulceration
  • Bloody Nipple Discharge Spontaneous, unilateral discharge — may indicate intraductal papilloma or carcinoma
  • Axillary Lymphadenopathy Palpable, firm, fixed lymph nodes — requires urgent evaluation
Diagnostic Algorithm
1

History & Physical Exam

Document mass characteristics, skin changes, nipple discharge, family history

2

Imaging Based on Age Age-Dependent

<30 yrs: Ultrasound first (dense breast tissue limits mammography sensitivity)
>30 yrs: Mammogram + Ultrasound

3

BI-RADS Classification

Assign category based on imaging findings to guide management

4

Tissue Diagnosis Biopsy Indicated

Core needle biopsy for BI-RADS 4-5; consider for high-suspicion BI-RADS 3

Differential Diagnosis

Common Benign

  • Fibroadenoma — Smooth, mobile, rubbery; most common in <30 yrs
  • Fibrocystic Change — Tender, cyclic, bilateral; common in pre/perimenopausal
  • Simple Cyst — Fluctuant, well-circumscribed; anechoic on US
  • Fat Necrosis — History of trauma/surgery; may mimic cancer
  • Breast Abscess — Painful, erythematous; usually lactational

Malignant / High Risk

  • Invasive Ductal Carcinoma — Most common; hard, irregular mass
  • Invasive Lobular Carcinoma — May lack imaging findings
  • DCIS — Non-invasive; often detected on mammography
  • LCIS — Risk marker, not direct precursor
  • Phyllodes Tumor — Rapidly growing; can be benign or malignant
Physical Exam Checklist
  • Inspection — symmetry, skin changes, nipple position
  • Palpation — mass location, size, shape, mobility, consistency
  • Axilla — lymphadenopathy (cervical, supraclavicular, infraclavicular)
  • Position — seated upright AND supine (masses more apparent in different positions)
  • Arm Maneuvers — arms overhead, hands on hips (reveals retraction)
Family History Red Flags

Consider BRCA Testing

  • Breast cancer ≤50 years
  • Male breast cancer
  • Ovarian cancer at any age
  • Pancreatic or prostate cancer
  • Ashkenazi Jewish ancestry
  • Multiple cancers on same family side
Action Required

Urgent Referral (≤28 days)

  • Any unexplained breast mass ≥30 yrs
  • Suspicious imaging findings (BI-RADS 4-5)
  • Any suspicious physical exam finding
  • Persistent bloody nipple discharge

Non-Urgent Referral

  • Unexplained mass <30 yrs with benign imaging
  • Probably benign findings (BI-RADS 3)
  • Simple cyst on ultrasound
