Acute Abdominal Pain – Quick Reference (NICE CKS Jan 2025)

1. Immediate Triage (≤60 s)

  • 999 Sepsis / shock / peritonitis
  • 999 Suspected appendicitis, ruptured AAA, ectopic pregnancy w/ instability, bowel obstruction, pancreatitis, testicular torsion
  • 999 Red-currant-jelly stool, bilious vomiting, irreducible hernia, severe trauma

2. Focused History & Exam (2-3 min)

KeyPointer
RLQ painAppendicitis
RUQ painCholecystitis / gallstones
LLQ painDiverticulitis
Radiation to backPancreatitis / AAA
Murphy signCholecystitis
Psoas / Rovsing / Obturator↑ likelihood of appendicitis

3. Bedside Tests

Everyone: FBC, U&E, CRP, LFT, glucose, urinalysis, β-hCG (♀ 12-55 y)
If indicated: Amylase/lipase, STI swab, FIT

4. Referral Pathway

UrgencyConditionNext step
999Sepsis, peritonitis, AAA ruptureED
Urgent Cancer suspicion, Crohn’s flareSuspected-cancer pathway
ReferCholecystitis, kidney stonesOP USS / surgical
Primary careUTI, constipation, gastroenteritisTreat + safety-net

5. Analgesia & Safety-net

  • Give analgesia early – does not mask surgical signs
  • Return if ↑ pain, fever, bilious vomiting, inability to pass flatus/stool, or systemic deterioration
