Chlamydia – Uncomplicated Genital

Quick Reference Guide | Last update: February 2025

Who to Test

  • < 25 y – screen annually
  • New/multiple partners in last 12 m
  • Partner of positive case
  • Termination of pregnancy request
  • Any STI concern

How to Test

  • Women: vulvo-vaginal swab (self OK)
  • Men: first-catch urine (≥ 1 h hold)
  • Extra-genital: rectal/pharyngeal if symptomatic or MSM/HIV+

First-line Treatment (≥ 13 y)

PatientDrug & Dose
Non-pregnantDoxycycline 100 mg bd × 7 d
PregnantAzithromycin 1 g day 1, 500 mg days 2–3
Doxy CIAzithromycin (as above)
Macrolide allergyErythromycin 500 mg qds × 7 d or Amoxicillin 500 mg tds × 7 d

Immediate Actions

  • Refer to GUM if possible; treat in primary care if declined.
  • Treat all sexual contacts within 4 weeks.
  • Abstain until 7 days after azithro or end of doxy course.

Follow-Up

  • Test of cure: pregnancy OR persistent symptoms → ≥ 3 weeks post-tx
  • Re-test 3–6 m: < 25 y or high-risk
  • No routine TOC in uncomplicated non-pregnant adults

Red Flags – Urgent GUM

  • Pelvic pain / suspected PID
  • Epididymo-orchitis
  • Treatment failure at 14 days
  • Pregnant patient with persistent symptoms
