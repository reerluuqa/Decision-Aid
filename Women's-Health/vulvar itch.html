Pruritus Vulvae

A Quick Reference Algorithm for Primary Care

1. Initial Patient Assessment

History

  • Symptoms: Onset, duration, location, triggers.
  • Associated: Discharge, pain, bleeding, urinary symptoms.
  • Practices: Hygiene, products used (soaps, etc.).
  • Medical History: Atopy, diabetes, skin conditions, menopause.

Examination

  • Anogenital: Colour, texture, lesions, architectural changes, discharge.
  • Extragenital: Check skin (elbows, scalp) and oral mucosa.

2. Investigation & Differential Diagnosis

Is a specific cause suspected based on the assessment?

YES

Probable cause identified. Proceed to Section 3: Targeted Management.

NO

Cause is unclear. Proceed to Section 4: Management of Unexplained Itch.

Consider Investigations: High Vaginal Swab (if discharge/infection suspected) or Blood Tests (for generalized itch/systemic cause).

3. Targeted Management (Known or Suspected Cause)

Condition Key Features Primary Care Action
Malignancy / VIN Unexplained lump, ulceration, bleeding, or persistent warty/pigmented lesion. URGENT 2-WEEK WAIT REFERRAL
Lichen Sclerosus / Planus LS: White, crinkled plaques, scarring. LP: Erosive red patches, purple papules. Refer to specialist for confirmation & management.
Infection / Infestation Candidiasis (discharge), BV (odour), Threadworm (nocturnal itch). Swab if needed. Treat specific cause.
Dermatitis / Eczema Erythema, lichenification, fissuring. History of atopy or irritant exposure. Remove irritants, advise emollients & topical steroids.
Atrophic Vaginitis Pale, thin, dry tissue in peri/post-menopausal women. Manage as per menopause guidelines (e.g., topical oestrogen).

4. Management of Unexplained Vulval Itch

  1. Universal Advice & Symptomatic Treatment: Provide self-care advice (see below) & prescribe emollients. For sleep disturbance, consider sedating antihistamine or low-dose TCA.
  2. Trial of Topical Treatment: Prescribe a short course (1-2 weeks) of a low-potency topical corticosteroid (e.g., hydrocortisone 1%).
  3. Review & Refer: If symptoms persist despite above measures, refer to Dermatology, Gynaecology, or specialist vulval clinic.

Universal Self-Care Advice (for ALL Patients)

Washing Routine

Use an emollient as a soap substitute, once daily at most. Pat dry gently.

Avoid Irritants

Avoid soaps, bubble baths, perfumes, wet wipes, and scented sanitary products.

Soothing Measures

Apply cool compresses or cool gel packs. Keep fingernails short to prevent scratching.

Clothing

Wear loose-fitting, cotton underwear. Avoid tight trousers and synthetic fabrics.
