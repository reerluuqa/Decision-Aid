üîç Clinical History & Risk Factors
‚ñº

Definition: Lines or creases of the skin appearing as a natural part of ageing, exacerbated by actinic damage.[20,22]

Essential History

  • Age: Moderate/severe wrinkles commonly evident after age 40.[33]
  • Habits: Smoking status (pack-years), alcohol consumption, nutrition.[41,47]
  • Sun Exposure: Occupation, recreational activities, use of sun protection.[147]

Risk Factors

  • Strong Age >40 years: Progressive loss of skin thickness and elasticity.[88,150]
  • Strong Chronic Sun Exposure: Major environmental factor. Affects face, neck, dorsum of hands.[47,212]
  • Strong Smoking: Associated with deep wrinkles and accelerated ageing.[48,227]
  • Variable Skin Type: White skin (Fitzpatrick I-III) is more susceptible than Black or Asian skin.[36,38]
üëÅÔ∏è Physical Examination
‚ñº

Visual Inspection

Observe the face at rest and during animation.[152]

Key Characteristics

  • Location: Forehead, glabellar, crow's feet, nasolabial folds, perioral lines.[185]
  • Depth: Fine lines vs. deep creases/folds.[151]
  • Dynamics:
    • Dynamic lines: Caused by muscle contraction (e.g., forehead, crow's feet).[52]
    • Gravitational lines: Sagging folds due to atrophy and gravity (cheek/neck).[95]

Associated Photodamage Signs

Look for dyschromia (liver spots), leathery texture, telangiectasia, and actinic keratoses.[251]

Investigation

Macrophotography: High-resolution digital photography is the 1st test to document baseline.[402]

Diagnosis is usually clinical.[143] Biopsy is rarely indicated unless ruling out differentials.[190]

  • Type I No wrinkles.
  • Type II Wrinkles in motion.
  • Type III Wrinkles at rest.
  • Type IV Only wrinkles (yellow/gray skin).

Classification by Aetiology[15]

  • Atrophic
  • Elastotic
  • Expressional (dynamic)

Treatment depends on severity, photoageing, and gravitational changes. Lifestyle modification (strict sun protection, smoking cessation) is recommended for ALL patients.[440,541]

Mild No obvious wrinkles (at rest or animation)

Goal: Prevention and early intervention.

  • 1st Line: Topical Retinoids (Tretinoin 0.02‚Äì0.05%).[492]
  • Adjunct: Antioxidants (Vit C, E, ferulic acid, CoQ10).[500]
  • Adjunct: Superficial Chemical Peel (glycolic acid 30‚Äì70%).[502]

Moderate Superficial wrinkles on animation only

Goal: Reduce hyperkinetic lines.

  • 1st Line: Topical Retinoids + Antioxidants.[507]
  • Procedure: Botulinum Toxin Type A (forehead, glabella, crow's feet).[449]
  • Procedure: Superficial Chemical Peel.[446]

Moderate/Severe Deep wrinkles on animation only

Goal: Aggressive line reduction.

  • 1st Line: Botulinum Toxin Type A.[520,528]
  • 2nd Line: Medium-depth Chemical Peel (TCA 35%).[484]
  • 3rd Line: Hyaluronic acid fillers.[452,456]
  • 4th Line: Dermabrasion.[484]
  • 5th Line: Ablative Laser (CO‚ÇÇ or Er:YAG).[470,484]

Goal: Resurfacing and collagen remodelling.
