Menopause Management Algorithm

Quick Reference Guide for Clinical Practice

Initial Assessment & Diagnosis

Age-Based Diagnostic Approach:

  • ≥45 years: Diagnose based on symptoms alone (no lab tests needed)
  • 40-45 years: Clinical diagnosis, consider FSH if unclear
  • <40 years: Measure FSH levels to assess for premature ovarian insufficiency (POI)

Common Symptoms:

  • Vasomotor: Hot flushes, night sweats (80% of women)
  • Genitourinary: Vaginal dryness, dyspareunia, urinary symptoms
  • Psychological: Mood changes, sleep disturbance, cognitive changes
  • Physical: Joint pains, palpitations, headaches

Management Options

First-line:

  • Lifestyle modifications:
    • Reduce caffeine, alcohol, spicy foods
    • Maintain healthy weight, regular exercise
    • Cognitive behavioral therapy for symptom management

Hormone Replacement Therapy (HRT):

  • Indications: Moderate-severe vasomotor symptoms, GSM, POI
  • Contraindications:
    • History of breast cancer, estrogen-dependent cancer
    • Unexplained vaginal bleeding
    • Active VTE, stroke, or acute liver disease
    • Pregnancy

HRT Regimens:

  • With uterus: Combined estrogen + progestogen (continuous or sequential)
  • Without uterus: Estrogen-only
  • Local for GSM: Low-dose vaginal estrogen (cream, tablet, ring)

Non-hormonal Options:

  • Vasomotor symptoms: SSRIs, SNRIs, gabapentin, clonidine
  • GSM: Moisturizers, lubricants, ospemifene
  • Bone health: Calcium, vitamin D, bisphosphonates if indicated

Follow-up & Monitoring

  • Initial review: 3 months after starting treatment
  • Routine reviews: At least annually
  • Monitoring: Blood pressure, weight, symptom assessment
  • Bleeding on HRT: Investigate unscheduled bleeding promptly
Note: Women, trans men and non-binary people registered female at birth having treatment for menopause-associated symptoms should have a review 3 months after starting each treatment and then at least annually.

Referral Criteria

Refer to specialist menopause service when:

  • Suspected POI (<40 years)
  • Inadequate response to first-line treatments
  • Persistent unexplained bleeding on HRT
  • Contraindications to HRT with severe symptoms
  • Complex medical history (e.g., history of hormone-sensitive cancer)
  • Transgender men and non-binary people who previously had gender-affirming hormone therapy

Special Considerations

Premature Ovarian Insufficiency (POI):

  • Offer HRT or combined hormonal contraceptive until natural menopause age
  • Refer for specialist investigation and management

Cardiovascular risk:

  • HRT may reduce risk if started <60 years or within 10 years of menopause
  • May increase risk if started >60 years or >10 years after menopause

Bone health:

  • Consider bone density assessment in early menopause/POI
  • Ensure adequate calcium and vitamin D intake
Note: This algorithm is based on NICE CKS guidance (April 2025). For complete guidance, refer to the full document.
