Progestogen-Only Contraception

A Quick Reference Clinical Algorithm

1. Method Selection & Key Considerations

Method Types Available Key Advantages Key Disadvantages & Cautions
Progestogen-Only Pill (POP) Traditional: Norethisterone, Levonorgestrel
Desogestrel (12hr window)
Drospirenone (24hr window)		 Suitable when oestrogen is contraindicated. Good for high BMI, smokers >35, migraine with aura. Daily adherence required. Unscheduled bleeding common. Efficacy reduced by enzyme-inducing drugs.
Progestogen-Only Implant Etonogestrel 68 mg (Nexplanon®) Highly effective (>99%). "Fit and forget" for 3 years. Requires trained professional for insertion/removal. Unscheduled bleeding common. Efficacy reduced by enzyme-inducing drugs.
Progestogen-Only Injectable Depot medroxyprogesterone acetate (DMPA) - Depo-Provera® / Sayana Press® Given every 13 weeks. Not affected by enzyme-inducing drugs. Delayed return to fertility (up to 1 year). Potential weight gain. Small, reversible loss of bone mineral density. Review use every 2 years.

2. Initial Assessment & Starting Contraception

Patient Situation When to Start Additional Precautions Needed?
During Menstrual Cycle (Days 1-5) Start immediately No
During Menstrual Cycle (After Day 5) Start immediately (if pregnancy excluded) Yes. Use condoms for:
• 2 days (Traditional/Desogestrel POP)
• 7 days (Drospirenone POP / Implant / Injectable)
Postpartum (<21 days) Start any time No
Postpartum (>21 days) or Amenorrhoeic Start any time (if pregnancy excluded) Yes. Use condoms for the duration listed above.
After EC (Levonorgestrel) Start immediately Yes. Use condoms and advise pregnancy test in 3 weeks.
After EC (Ulipristal Acetate) Wait 5 days before starting Yes. Use condoms and advise pregnancy test in 3 weeks.

3. Switching Between Methods

Switching FROM... Instructions
Combined Hormonal Contraception (CHC) Start on Day 1-2 of hormone-free interval. No extra precautions needed. If starting later, use condoms for 7 days.
Another POP Start immediately. No pill-free gap. Use condoms for 7 days if switching TO Drospirenone.
Implant / IUS / Injectable Start on the day of removal or when next dose is due. No extra precautions needed.
Copper IUD If Day 1-5 of cycle: Remove IUD and start. No extra precautions.
If after Day 5: Start method, use condoms for 7 days, and remove IUD 7 days later.

4. Managing Common Problems

Missed Progestogen-Only Pill (POP)

  1. Identify Time Window:
    • Traditional POP: > 3 hours late
    • Desogestrel POP: > 12 hours late
    • Drospirenone POP: > 24 hours late
  2. Take pill ASAP (even if two in one day).
  3. Continue subsequent pills at usual time.
  4. Use condoms for 2 days (Trad/Desogestrel) or 7 days (Drospirenone).
  5. Consider EC if unprotected sex occurred.

Unscheduled Bleeding

  1. Exclude Pathology: Screen for STIs, perform pregnancy test, consider speculum exam if indicated.
  2. Reassure: Explain this is common, especially in the first 3 months, and often settles.
  3. Treat (if unacceptable):
    • Mefenamic Acid 500mg TDS for 5 days.
    • Trial a 3-month course of a COC.
    • Consider switching methods if persistent.

Late Injectable (DMPA)

  1. If < 14 weeks since last injection: Give injection. No further action needed.
  2. If > 14 weeks since last injection:
    • No UPSI: Give injection. Advise condoms for 7 days.
    • Yes, UPSI: Assess need for EC. Give injection. Advise condoms for 7 days and pregnancy test in 3 weeks.

5. Key Drug Interactions

Enzyme-Inducing Drugs (e.g., Carbamazepine, Phenytoin, Rifampicin, St. John's Wort) significantly reduce the efficacy of POPs and the Implant. Advise switching to an unaffected method (DMPA injectable, IUD/IUS) or using consistent barrier protection.
