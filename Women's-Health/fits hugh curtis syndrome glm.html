Fitz-Hugh Curtis Syndrome

Acute Perihepatitis • PID Association • Clinical Reference
Snapshot Definition Epidemiology: 4-27% of PID

Acute perihepatitis with characteristic "violin-string" adhesions between the liver and abdominal wall/diaphragm.

Associated with Pelvic Inflammatory Disease (PID). Primarily caused by Chlamydia trachomatis or Neisseria gonorrhoeae.

Typical Patient
  • Women of reproductive age
  • Often younger women
  • Rarely reported in men
Differential Diagnosis (Rule Out)
Emergency Priority: Must rule out Ectopic Pregnancy and Acute Appendicitis initially.
  • Cholecystitis
  • Viral Hepatitis
  • Pyelonephritis
  • Renal Colic
  • Pulmonary Embolism
  • Pleurisy
Symptoms & Presentation
Pain Location Right Upper Quadrant (RUQ) & Gallbladder area. May refer to Right Shoulder.
Pain Character Acute, sharp, pleuritic. Aggravated by cough, sneeze, movement.
Associated Fever, Nausea, Vomiting, Hiccups, Malaise, Night sweats.

Examination Signs

  • Features of PID (Lower abd tenderness, cervical excitation, tender adnexa) may be present but not invariable.
  • "Violin String" Friction Rub: Auscultation over anterior cost margin may sound like "walking in new snow" (similar to pericarditis).
Investigations
  • Swabs: Endocervical swabs for Gonorrhoea & Chlamydia.
  • FBC/ESR: Likely elevated WCC and ESR.
  • Urine: Microscopy & culture.
  • CXR: Exclude pneumonia/PE.
Clinical Pearl: LFTs are typically NORMAL because liver parenchyma is not involved. This helps differentiate from hepatitis.

Imaging

Ultrasound Exclude renal/biliary stones. May show ascites/thickening.
CT (Enhanced) Shows perihepatic enhancement.
Laparoscopy Gold Standard. Shows "violin-string" adhesions.
Management Plan

Treat the underlying PID. Hospital admission may be required for severe pain or complications.

  • Antibiotics: Full regimen for PID (cover Chlamydia & Gonorrhoeae).
  • Partner Notification: Treat sexual partner(s) immediately.
  • Analgesia: Manage RUQ pain.
  • Surgical: Laparoscopy can divide adhesions if pain is chronic/severe.
Prognosis & Complications

Generally prognosis is as for PID.

Note: Incidental findings of FHC during infertility workups often indicate tubal damage.

Potential Complications

  • Chronic pelvic pain
  • Infertility
  • Ectopic pregnancy
  • Small intestinal obstruction (due to adhesions)
Mechanism

Spread from pelvis to liver capsule via:

  • Paracolic gutter circulation
  • Lymphatic drainage
  • Bloodstream
