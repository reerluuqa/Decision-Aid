Amenorrhoea Management Algorithm

Quick reference guide for busy clinicians

Based on NICE CKS Guidance (February 2024)

I. Definitions

Primary Amenorrhoea

No menstruation by:

  • Age 15 with normal secondary sexual characteristics, OR
  • Age 13 with no secondary sexual characteristics, OR
  • Within 3 years of breast development (5 years if before age 10)
Secondary Amenorrhoea

Cessation of menstruation for:

  • 3 consecutive cycles (3 months if regular monthly cycle), OR
  • 6 months in previously menstruating woman

II. Initial Assessment

A. History
  • Menstrual history (age at menarche, regularity, last period)
  • Sexual activity and contraception
  • Pregnancy history
  • Weight changes, diet, exercise patterns
  • Stress levels, psychological factors
  • Symptoms of systemic disease
  • Medication history
  • Family history
  • Headaches, visual changes, galactorrhoea
B. Examination
  • Height, weight, BMI calculation
  • Secondary sexual characteristics (Tanner staging)
  • Signs of endocrine disorders:
    • Hirsutism, acne (PCOS)
    • Thyroid enlargement
    • Features of Cushing's syndrome
  • Signs of virilization
  • Breast examination (galactorrhoea)
  • Abdominal and pelvic examination (if appropriate)
  • Visual field testing (if pituitary lesion suspected)

III. Initial Investigations

A. All patients
  • Pregnancy test (urine or serum β-hCG)
  • Hormonal profile:
    • FSH
    • LH
    • Prolactin
    • TSH
    • Oestradiol
  • Pelvic ultrasound (if indicated)
B. Additional tests based on clinical suspicion
  • Androgens (testosterone, DHEAS)
  • 17-OH progesterone
  • Anti-Müllerian hormone (AMH)
  • Karyotype
  • Pituitary MRI (if prolactin elevated or neurological symptoms)
  • Bone density scan (if prolonged hypoestrogenism)

IV. Management Pathways

Normal secondary sexual characteristics?
YES
Check for outflow tract obstruction:
- Imperforate hymen
- Vaginal septum
Consider Müllerian anomalies
NO
Measure FSH/LH:
- High FSH → Premature ovarian insufficiency
- Low/normal FSH/LH → Hypothalamic-pituitary dysfunction

Exclude pregnancy
Normal prolactin?
YES
High FSH/LH → Premature ovarian insufficiency
Low FSH/LH →
- Normal BMI → Functional hypothalamic amenorrhoea
- High BMI → PCOS (check androgens)
- Low BMI → Eating disorder/excessive exercise
ELEVATED
Medication-related? → Review medications
No → Consider pituitary MRI for prolactinoma
C. Management Principles
  • Treat underlying cause:
    • PCOS: Lifestyle modification, metformin, cyclical progestogens
    • Hyperprolactinaemia: Dopamine agonists
    • Thyroid dysfunction: Thyroid hormone replacement
    • Premature ovarian insufficiency: HRT until average menopausal age
  • Oestrogen deficiency management:
    • HRT or combined oral contraceptive
    • Calcium and vitamin D supplementation
    • Weight-bearing exercise
    • Bone density monitoring
  • Fertility considerations:
    • Referral to specialist if pregnancy desired
    • Specific treatments based on underlying cause
  • Psychological support:
    • Address body image concerns
    • Provide counseling regarding fertility implications

V. Referral Indications

Urgent Referral (within 2 weeks)
  • Suspected pituitary tumor (visual field defects, severe headaches)
  • Androgen-secreting tumors (rapid virilization)
Routine Referral
  • Primary amenorrhoea requiring specialist assessment
  • Premature ovarian insufficiency
  • Suspected Müllerian anomalies
  • Persistent hyperprolactinaemia
  • Infertility investigation and management
VI. Follow-up
  • Monitor treatment response
  • Assess bone health in prolonged cases
  • Regular review of hormonal status
  • Psychological support as needed
