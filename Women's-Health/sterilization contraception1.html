Contraception - Sterilization

Quick Reference Algorithm for Clinical Decision Making

Definition & Scope

  • Male sterilization (vasectomy): Interruption of vas deferens
  • Female sterilization (tubal occlusion): Occlusion of fallopian tubes
  • Target audience: Ages 18-60 years
  • Important: Permanent contraception method
  • Does NOT protect against: Sexually transmitted infections (STIs)

Effectiveness

Vasectomy
  • Lifetime failure rate: ~1 in 2000 (0.05%)
  • After confirmed azoospermia
Tubal Occlusion
  • Lifetime failure rate: ~1 in 200 (0.5%)
  • Filshie clips: ~1 in 333-500

Pre-Procedure Assessment

  • Apply WHO Medical Eligibility Criteria for Contraceptive Use
  • Assess understanding and mental capacity
  • Evaluate risk for future regret
  • Assess partner's suitability as well
  • Discuss alternative long-acting reversible contraception (LARC) methods

Key Patient Information

Provide details on:

  • Procedure specifics
  • Effectiveness compared to other methods
  • Advantages: Highly effective, permanent, one-time procedure
  • Disadvantages: Surgical requirement, not easily reversible
Important: NHS does not routinely offer reversal procedures

Risks & Complications

Vasectomy
  • Hematoma
  • Infection
  • Chronic post-vasectomy pain (1-14%)
Tubal Occlusion
  • Ectopic pregnancy if fails (2.4-7.3 per 1000)
  • Surgical/anaesthesia risks
  • Bowel/bladder/vessel injury risk

Post-Procedure Care

Vasectomy:
  • Continue contraception until azoospermia confirmed (PVSA at 12 weeks)
  • NSAIDs for pain, rest, avoid sex 2-7 days
  • Seek help for: bleeding, pain, infection, scrotal hematoma
Tubal Occlusion:
  • Laparoscopic: Continue contraception 7 days post-procedure
  • Hysteroscopic: Use additional contraception â‰¥3 months until confirmed
  • Seek help for: abdominal pain with fever, missed period, DVT signs

Special Considerations

  • Reversal: Not routinely available on NHS
    • Vasectomy success: 40-97%
    • Tubal success: 31-92%
  • Regret risk: Assessment process identifies at-risk patients
  • Cancer considerations:
    • Vasectomy: Possible prostate cancer link (likely not causal)
    • Tubal occlusion: Possible protective effect against ovarian cancer
