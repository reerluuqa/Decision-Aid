üö® Initial Assessment

Life-Threatening Reactions (Immediate Emergency Referral)
üö® RED FLAG - Emergency Care Required
  • Anaphylaxis: Airway compromise, hypotension, bronchospasm
  • Stevens-Johnson Syndrome/Toxic Epidermal Necrolysis: Mucocutaneous blistering, skin detachment
  • Neuroleptic Malignant Syndrome: Hyperthermia, rigidity, autonomic instability
  • Blood Dyscrasias: Unexplained bleeding, severe anemia, infection
  • Acute Dystonias: Severe muscle spasms, airway compromise

üìã Comprehensive History Taking

Evaluate the nature and severity of the reaction to determine management setting:

  • Timing: When did symptoms start relative to drug administration?
  • Dose Relationship: Is the reaction dose-dependent? Does it resolve with dose reduction?
  • Previous Exposure: Any similar reactions with this or related drugs?
  • Drug History: Complete list including OTC, herbal, supplements
  • Concurrent Medications: Check for drug-drug interactions
  • Medical History: Atopy, renal/hepatic impairment, pregnancy

  • Liver Function: For hepatotoxicity suspicion
  • Renal Function: For nephrotoxic drugs

üè∑Ô∏è ADR Classification

  • Type A Pharmacological/Augmented: Dose-dependent, predictable (e.g., respiratory depression with opioids)
  • Type B Idiosyncratic/Bizarre: Unpredictable, not dose-related (e.g., anaphylaxis with penicillin)
  • Type C Continuing: Persistent reactions (e.g., osteonecrosis with bisphosphonates)
  • Type D Delayed: Appear months/years later (e.g., chemotherapy-induced leukopenia)
  • Type E End-of-Use: Withdrawal reactions (e.g., benzodiazepine cessation)

üíä Treatment
‚ñº

üöë Emergency Management

For life-threatening reactions:
  • CALL 999 Immediate emergency referral
  • Stop all suspected drugs immediately
  • Anaphylaxis: IM adrenaline, airway management
  • SJS/TEN: Burn unit referral, stop triggering drug
  • Malayanant Hyperthermia: Active cooling, dantrolene

üè• Primary Care Management

For non-life-threatening ADRs:

  • Stop Suspected Drug: If serious or at patient's request
  • Dose Reduction: For dose-dependent Type A reactions
  • Temporary Discontinuation: If benefit-harm balance favorable
  • Alternative Therapy: Consider safer alternatives
  • Symptomatic Treatment: Manage ADR symptoms appropriately

‚ö†Ô∏è Drug Withdrawal Considerations

  • Benzodiazepines: Taper over weeks to avoid withdrawal syndrome
  • Antidepressants: Gradual taper to prevent discontinuation symptoms
  • Beta-blockers: Taper to avoid rebound tachycardia/hypertension
  • Corticosteroids: Gradual reduction after prolonged use

üìã Information to Include

üë• Patient Communication

Patients can also report directly! Encourage them to use the Yellow Card Scheme.
  • Explain the suspected ADR clearly

  • Warn about cross-reactivity within drug classes
  • Provide written information about the reaction
  • Issue medical alert jewellery if appropriate
