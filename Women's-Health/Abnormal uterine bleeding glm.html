EMERGENCY PROTOCOL: ACUTE BLEEDING

Acute AUB Definition URGENT

Non-gestational bleeding of sufficient quantity to require immediate intervention to prevent further blood loss.

Key Signs:
  • Hypovolaemia / Haemodynamic collapse
  • Soaking pad every 1-2 hours
  • Signs of anaemia (pallor, dyspnoea)

Immediate Actions

1st Line IV Conjugated Oestrogens (25mg) or Combined OCP
Adjunct Tranexamic Acid (Specialist dose)
Support Blood products, Fluid resuscitation
Surgical Dilation & Curettage (if refractory)

Diagnostics & Aetiology

Investigations Flow

1st Pregnancy Test (Urine/beta-hCG)
All Full Blood Count (Check anaemia)
If sympt. TSH (Thyroid)
Img TVUS (Structural abnormalities)
Inv. Hysteroscopy (If persistent IMB/Polyp)

Management Protocol

1st Line: Progestogens Preferred

Indication: Anovulatory AUB & HMB.

Gold Standard: Levonorgestrel IUS (LNG-IUD)
Best for HMB without pathology or fibroids <3cm.
  • Oral Norethisterone: 5mg TDS days 5-25
  • Medroxyprogesterone: 5-10mg days 16-21
  • Benefit: Endometrial protection.

2nd Line: Combined Hormone

Indication: Inadequate response to progestogens.

  • Combined Oestrogen & Progestogen (COC).
  • Restores regular withdrawal bleeding.
  • Continuous use up to 3 months to reduce bleeding frequency.
Avoid in thromboembolic history, smokers >35 yrs.

When Hormones are Contraindicated

NSAIDs

Mefenamic acid, Ibuprofen, Naproxen.

Mechanism: Reduces prostaglandins.
Tranexamic Acid

Antifibrinolytic.

Reduces loss by ~50%. Low thrombosis risk.
  • Useful if dysmenorrhoea is present.
  • Contraindicated in peptic ulcer disease/asthma (NSAIDs).

Endometrial Ablation

Criteria: Failed medical tx, no fertility desire.

  • Minimally invasive.
  • High satisfaction rates.
  • Contraception required afterwards.

Hysterectomy

Criteria: Failed other procedures, definitive cure needed.

  • Definitive permanent cure.
  • Higher morbidity than ablation.
  • Preferred laparoscopic approach (especially in obese).
NICE advises against D&C alone for treatment of HMB.
