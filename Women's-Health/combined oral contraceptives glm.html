COC Pill Reference

Follow-Up Protocol

Schedule

First Review: 3 months.

Ongoing: 6-12 monthly intervals if stable.

Supplies of up to 1 year may be issued.

Essential Questions

  • Easy to use/remember?
  • Adverse effects?
  • Missed pills management understood?
  • Breakthrough bleeding (BTB)?
  • New meds or social changes (smoking)?

Examination

  • Check Blood Pressure
  • Measure BMI
  • Check cervical screening status

Common Problems

Breakthrough Bleeding (BTB)

Common in first few months. If no missed pills/vomiting, efficacy likely maintained.

Exclude: Pregnancy, STIs, missed pills, malabsorption.

Amenorrhoea (Post-Pill)

Withdrawal bleeds mask underlying issues, not a direct effect of the pill.

Investigate if: Lasts > 6 months after stopping.

Vomiting / Diarrhoea

< 2 hours after pill: Absorption affected. Take another pill ASAP.

> 24 hours duration: Follow missed pill rules.

Surgery (Elective)

STOP COC: 4 weeks before major elective surgery.

RESTART: Day 1 of next period (at least 2 weeks post-mobilisation).

Offer progestogen-only alternatives during this time.

Benefits & Other Uses

  • Menorrhagia: Reduces bleeding.
  • Dysmenorrhoea: Reduces pain.
  • PCOS: Protects endometrium.
  • Acne: Improvement shown.
  • Cancer Risk: Reduces Ovarian/Endometrial cancer risk significantly (benefit lasts 30yrs).
