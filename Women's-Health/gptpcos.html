PCOS – Primary Care Quick Reference Algorithm

Fast decision aide for consultations • Adapted from NICE CKS (last revised March 2025). For clinical use alongside full guidance.

1

Suspect PCOS

Adults
  • Hyperandrogenism: hirsutism, acne, female pattern hair loss.
  • Ovulatory dysfunction: oligo-/amenorrhoea, infertility.
  • Consider: family history, central obesity/insulin resistance, acanthosis nigricans.
Adolescents
  • Require BOTH clinical hyperandrogenism and irregular cycles (definitions below).
  • Those with features but not meeting criteria: label “increased risk”; reassess ≤ 8 yrs post‑menarche.
Irregular cycles (post‑menarche definitions): 1–<3 yrs: >45 d or <21 d; ≥3 yrs: >35 d or <21 d or <8/yr; any cycle >90 d ≥1 yr post‑menarche; primary amenorrhoea by 15 yrs or >3 yrs post‑thelarche.
2

Investigations

Hormonal
  • Total testosterone ± FAI (↑/normal); SHBG (normal/↓).
  • If T/FAI not elevated → consider androstenedione & DHEAS.
On hormonal contraception? Reliable biochemical hyperandrogenism assessment isn’t possible. If essential, stop COC for ≥3 months and ensure alternative contraception.
Exclude differentials
  • TSH, prolactin, FSH/LH; consider non‑classic CAH, Cushing’s, androgen‑secreting tumour (rapid virilisation, T >5 nmol/L).
  • Ultrasound (adults): polycystic morphology = ≥20 follicles in ≥1 ovary. Not for adolescents
Baseline metabolic/CVD screen at diagnosis: OGTT or HbA1c (± fasting glucose), lipids, BP, BMI & waist.
3

Diagnosis

  • Adults: Any 2 of 3: (1) clinical/biochemical hyperandrogenism; (2) ovulatory dysfunction; (3) polycystic ovarian morphology on USS.
  • Adolescents: Both hyperandrogenism + irregular cycles required (no USS).
  • Note ethnic variation in presentation/thresholds; use clinical judgement.
  • PCOS is enduring; hyperandrogenism may persist post‑menopause. Consider alternate causes if late/severe onset.
4

Management

Lifestyle – first line
  • Weight optimisation, healthy diet, regular physical activity.
  • Benefits: improved cycles/fertility, ↓ insulin resistance, ↓ CVD risk, QoL ↑.
Cycle / Endometrium
  • COC first line if no contraindications. Avoid EE 35 µg + cyproterone as 1st line
  • Prolonged amenorrhoea (>3 mo) or AUB: induce bleed with cyclical progestogen (e.g., medroxyprogesterone 10 mg × 14 d) → USS endometrium.
  • If endometrium >10 mm or atypical → refer for sampling.
  • Prevention options: cyclical progestogen q1–3 mo, low‑dose COC, or LNG‑IUD.
  • Declines hormones/IUD → seek specialist advice; consider 6–12 mo USS surveillance.
Hyperandrogenism
  • Acne: COC ± dermatology agents per acne severity.
  • Hirsutism: COC; discuss hair‑removal methods (will remain important).
Metformin off‑label
  • Consider if BMI ≥25, diabetes risk factors, insulin resistance, high‑risk ethnicity, or metabolic goals unmet with COC/lifestyle.
  • Counsel on GI effects & possible ↓B12; review ongoing need.
Pregnancy: If pregnant/planning, offer OGTT pre‑conception or <20 weeks; all with PCOS: OGTT at 24–28 weeks. Review/stop hormonal treatments when pregnant.
5

Screening & Risk Reduction

  • Glycaemic status: baseline for all; repeat q1–3 yrs. In high‑risk (BMI ≥25; Asians ≥23; HTN; age >40; GDM/IFG/IGT; non‑Caucasian): prefer OGTT; annual OGTT if IFG/IGT.
  • Lipids: fasting profile at diagnosis; repeat per CVD risk/treatment.
  • BP: at least annually (more often per global CVD risk).
  • Smoking: assess & support cessation.
  • CVD risk: consider QRISK3 (use cautiously; not validated specifically for PCOS).
  • OSAS: ask about snoring, daytime sleepiness; screen if symptomatic (e.g., STOP‑Bang, Epworth; don’t use Epworth alone for referral).
  • Psychological: screen for depression, anxiety, eating disorders; address body‑image/psychosexual issues.
  • NAFLD: consider risk and manage per NAFLD guidance.
6

Refer / Seek Specialist Advice

  • Infertility management or pre‑conception optimisation.
  • Endometrial thickness >10 mm, atypical appearance, or abnormal bleeding.
  • Severe/rapid virilisation or total testosterone >5 nmol/L (or >2× ULN): rule out androgen‑secreting tumour.
  • Uncertain/complex adolescent presentation or when declining standard endometrial protection.
✔

Key Point

PCOS is lifelong. Goals: control symptoms (cycles, acne, hirsutism), prevent metabolic/CVD/psychological complications, and support fertility goals.

Handy Tools

  • OSAS screening: STOP‑Bang, Epworth (not alone for referral).
  • CVD: QRISK3 (interpret cautiously in PCOS).
  • Lifestyle: weight, BMI, waist at each visit; support cessation.
