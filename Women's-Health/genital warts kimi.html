Red Flags

  • Ulcerated or bleeding lesions
  • Fixed to underlying structures
  • Lesions >1cm diameter
  • Refractory to standard treatment
  • Immunocompromised patient
  • Urinary symptoms (terminal hematuria)

Safe to Discharge

  • Small, typical appearing warts
  • No complications or red flags
  • Patient competent for self-treatment
  • Follow-up arrangements made
  • Partner notification completed

Diagnosis

Clinical Features

  • 1-3mm papules, may coalesce
  • Flesh-colored to hyperpigmented
  • Asymptomatic usually
  • Located on genital/anal area

Investigations

  • Clinical diagnosis in most cases
  • Biopsy if atypical features
  • Anoscopy for recurrent perianal warts
  • Urethroscopy if urinary symptoms

Immediate Actions

  • Document lesion size, number, location
  • Screen for other STIs
  • Assess immunocompromised status
  • Discuss treatment options
  • Arrange follow-up in 3 months

Special Populations

Pregnancy

  • Avoid: Podophyllotoxin (teratogenic)
  • Safe: Cryotherapy, TCA, surgical
  • Not indication for C-section

Immunocompromised

  • Longer treatment course
  • Combination therapy
  • Lower clearance rates
  • Higher recurrence risk

Prevention

  • HPV vaccination (9-valent covers types 6,11)
  • Condom use reduces transmission
  • Partner notification and screening
  • Cervical screening for women
  • Abstinence during active treatment

Follow-up & Prognosis

Monitoring

  • Recurrence check at 3 months
  • Annual cervical screening
  • More frequent if immunocompromised

Key Points

  • 10-30% resolve spontaneously in 3 months
  • No cure for HPV infection
  • Treatment reduces but doesn't eliminate infectivity
