Critical Safeguarding

Immediate Action Required for: FGM, Forced Marriage, Honour Killings, Domestic Violence.

Women from minority communities may face extra barriers (cultural pressure, isolation) to seeking help.

  • FGM: Illegal in UK. Look for dermatological/gynaecological complications.
  • Domestic Violence: In some communities, abuse is perpetrated by extended family.
  • Approach: Ask sensitively. Ensure patient safety before involving community leaders.

Interpreter Best Practice

Standard: Use professional interpreters, not family (especially children).
  • Booking: Always book a double appointment (at least).
  • Resource: Use LanguageLine (24/7: 0845 3109900).
  • Technique: Speak to patient (eye contact), not interpreter. 1-2 sentences at a time.
  • Confidentiality: Emphasize it applies to interpreter too.

Disease Screening Actions

Targeted screening is crucial for equity.
South Asians Screen for Diabetes & Coronary Heart Disease (Lower access to care).
West Africans Sickle Cell Disease/Tract (Partial protection vs Malaria).
Afro-Caribbean Hypertension & Prostate Cancer (Higher prevalence).
Migrants/Travel TB & Malaria (Immunity lost over time; push prophylaxis).

Cultural Modifiers

  • Diet: Hindus/Buddhists = Vegetarian. Muslims/Jews = Halal/Kosher restrictions.
  • Gender: Strong taboos re: gender of health professional during examination.
  • Chaperones: Ideally speaks patient's language.
  • Modesty: Balti cooking + modest dress = Vitamin D deficiency risk.
  • Fasting (Ramadan): Ill/Pregnant/Elderly exempt. Diabetics may need med regime adaptation.

Inequalities & Social Factors

Poorer socio-economic position is the main driver of ethnic health inequalities.

  • Pakistani/Bangladeshi Women: Illness rates 10% higher than White women.
  • Gypsy/Travellers: 2x rate of limiting long-term illness.
  • Chinese Group: Consistently better health outcomes reported.
  • London: Most diverse, but severe ethnic inequalities exist here.

Legal & Admin Requirements

  • Race Relations Amendment Act 2000: Statutory duty to eliminate discrimination.
  • Data Recording: Must record ethnic origin of admitted patients (since 1996).
  • Asylum Seekers: Entitled to NHS treatment/GP registration. Do not place barriers.
Marmot Review Objective: Reduce health inequalities via social determinants (education, employment, standard of living).
