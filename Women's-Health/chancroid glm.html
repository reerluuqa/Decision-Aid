Snapshot & Epidemiology
CRITICAL: Chancroid is a major co-factor for HIV transmission. HIV testing is mandatory for all patients.

Pathogen

Haemophilus ducreyi (Gram-negative coccobacillus).

Highly infectious. Incubation: 3-7 days.

Transmission: Unprotected sex, auto-inoculation. Survival outside host is brief.

Risk Factors

  • Unprotected intercourse
  • Contact with sex workers
  • Cocaine use / Substance abuse
  • Uncircumcised males
  • Travel to: Caribbean, Africa, Asia, Latin America

Classic Presentation

Male: Painful genital ulcer (prepuce/frenulum) + tender inguinal lymphadenitis (Buboes).

Female: Often asymptomatic or subtle (dyspareunia, discharge). Painful if external; painless if vaginal/cervical.

Appearance: Ragged, undermined edges, friable base with grey/yellow exudate. NO induration.

Don't Miss

Co-infection is common. Always rule out Syphilis and HSV.

HIV Patients: Atypical lesions, slower healing, higher risk of treatment failure.

Diagnosis & Differentials

Rapid Differential Guide

Condition Ulcer Appearance Pain Lymph Nodes Key Test
Chancroid Ragged, undermined edges, grey base Painful Tender, often unilateral, may suppurate (Buboes) Culture / PCR
Syphilis Clean base, rolled edge Painless Non-tender, bilateral Serology (RPR/FTA)
Herpes (HSV) Vesicular -> multiple shallow ulcers Painful Tender, bilateral PCR / Culture
LGV Small, transient ulcer Painless Tender, suppurative (Buboes) w/o prominent ulcer PCR for C. trachomatis

First-Line Tests

PCR (Preferred where available) High Sensitivity

Sample from ulcer base. Sensitivity ~98%.

Culture (Standard) Diagnostic Standard

Requires specialized media (MH-HB/GC-HgS). Sensitivity 53-92%.

Rule Out (Mandatory)

Syphilis Serology: Treponemal + Non-treponemal tests.

HSV PCR: Viral culture or PCR.

HIV Test: Essential due to transmission risk.

Management & Regimens

CDC Recommended Regimens (Effective)

Azithromycin 1st Line

1 g orally
Single dose.

Ceftriaxone 1st Line

250 mg IM
Single dose. Safe in pregnancy.

Ciprofloxacin Alternative

500 mg orally
Twice daily for 3 days.
WARNING: Do not use in pregnancy/breastfeeding.

Erythromycin Alternative

500 mg orally
Three times daily for 7 days. Safe in pregnancy.

Pregnancy Considerations

SAFE: Azithro, Ceftriaxone, Erythromycin

AVOID: Ciprofloxacin

Macrolides use with caution in 1st trimester (theoretical risk of malformations).

HIV Co-infection

Higher risk of treatment failure and slower healing.

Consider multiple-dose regimens (Cipro/Erythro) if follow-up is uncertain.

Monitor closely: Re-evaluate 3-7 days post-treatment.

Follow Up & Management

  • Re-evaluate: 3-7 days after completion.
  • Healing: Ulcers usually heal in 1-2 weeks (larger >2 weeks).
  • Buboes: Repeated needle aspiration if fluctuant (avoid incision/drainage if possible to prevent secondary infection).
  • Sexual Contacts: Treat all contacts within last 10 days, even if asymptomatic.
No response to treatment?
Consider: Co-infection (Syphilis/HSV), Non-compliance, or Antibiotic resistance.
