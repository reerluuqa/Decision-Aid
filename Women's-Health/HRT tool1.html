Menopause Symptoms Recognition

Vasomotor Symptoms

  • Hot flushes
  • Night sweats

Sleep & General

  • Sleep disturbance
  • Joint and muscle pains
  • Fatigue

Genitourinary

  • Vaginal dryness
  • Urinary problems
  • Sexual difficulties (low libido)

Psychological

  • Mood changes
  • Cognitive disturbance
  • Memory issues
Key Point

Diagnosis is clinical — based on typical symptoms in a woman ≥45 years. Consider differentials if symptoms are atypical (alcohol, drugs, thyroid disease, anxiety, tumours).

Diagnosis & Investigations Assessment

⚠️ Investigate First

  • Abnormal bleeding — always investigate
  • Rule out other pathology

When to Check FSH

  • Diagnosis uncertain
  • Age <45 years
  • Women <40 (premature ovarian insufficiency)

Lifestyle Modifications First Line

Beneficial Changes

  • Regular exercise
  • Healthy diet and maintain BMI
  • Stop smoking
  • Reduce alcohol
  • Stress reduction techniques
  • Sleep hygiene

Avoid Triggers

  • Spicy foods
  • Caffeine (may worsen flushes)
  • Hot environments
  • Stressful situations

Hormone Replacement Therapy Treatment

First-Line Indications
  • ✓ Vasomotor symptoms (hot flushes, night sweats)
  • ✓ Depressive symptoms with other menopausal symptoms
  • ✓ Beneficial for sexual function, urogenital symptoms, bone density

Oestrogen-Only

For women without a uterus

  • Main symptom-relieving component
  • Use with Mirena IUS if uterus present
  • Available: oral, patch, gel, spray

Sequential Combined

For perimenopausal women

  • Daily oestrogen + progestogen 12-14 days/month
  • Triggers withdrawal bleed
  • Use until post-menopausal, then switch

Continuous Combined

For postmenopausal women (>12 months amenorrhoea)

  • Daily oestrogen + progestogen
  • No withdrawal bleed (atrophic endometrium)
  • Switch from sequential within 5 years or by age 54

Alternatives to HRT Options

CBT (± HRT)

  • Vasomotor symptoms
  • Low mood
  • Insomnia
  • Anxiety

Herbal Remedies

  • Isoflavones (soy)
  • Black cohosh
  • ⚠️ Vary in content/safety

SSRIs/SNRIs

  • Consider for flushes if HRT unsuitable
  • ⚠️ Counsel regarding side-effects

Neurokinin Receptor Antagonists

Newer class of medications for vasomotor symptoms. Consult latest guidelines for availability.

Contraindications to HRT Safety

ABSOLUTE CONTRAINDICATIONS — Refer to Menopause Specialist
  • History of breast cancer
  • Oestrogen-dependent tumour
  • Untreated endometrial hyperplasia
  • Current or past VTE (unless anticoagulated)
  • Arterial thromboembolic disease
  • Undiagnosed vaginal bleeding
  • Thrombophilic disorder or strong FH of VTE
  • Uncontrolled hypertension
  • Liver disease with abnormal LFTs

Counseling Points Risks

Breast Cancer Risk

  • Individual risk depends on modifiable/non-modifiable factors
  • Lifestyle factors (obesity, excess alcohol) likely greater impact than HRT
  • HRT unlikely to increase risk in women <51 years
  • Risk increases with duration of use

VTE Risk

  • HRT increases VTE risk
  • Individual risk depends on other factors
  • Risk generally low in early post-menopause
  • Transdermal may be safer (lower VTE/CVA risk)

Stroke Risk

  • Oral HRT increases stroke risk
  • Transdermal preparations appear safer (observational evidence)
  • Consider transdermal in women >60 or with CVD risk factors

Overall Summary

HRT use may be associated with small risks, but these are balanced by benefits in a symptomatic menopausal woman.

Overall, HRT is unlikely to affect life expectancy when appropriately prescribed.

Which HRT to Choose? Decision Guide

❓ Does she have a uterus?
→
✓ YES — Needs progestogen protection
✓ NO — Offer oestrogen-only HRT
→
❓ Requires contraception?

Perimenopausal (needs contraception)

  • Oestrogen-only pill/patch/gel/spray + 52mg IUS
  • Combined hormonal contraception (if eligible)
  • Sequential combined HRT + progestogen-only contraception

Postmenopausal (>1 year amenorrhoea)

  • Continuous combined HRT
  • Oestrogen + 52mg IUS
  • Oestrogen + oral progestogen
Special Circumstances

Women with sub-total hysterectomy or hysterectomy for severe endometriosis should be started on combined HRT regardless of uterine status.

Dosing Guidance
  • ✓ Start with low-dose preparation
  • ✓ Match progestogen dose if using separate formulations
  • ✓ If urogenital symptoms predominate: consider vaginal oestrogens
  • ✓ Transdermal preferred if: age >60, overweight, migraines, or VTE/CVA risk factors

Troubleshooting Management

Persistent Symptoms

  1. Increase to maximum licensed dose
  2. Consider switch to alternative preparation

Unscheduled Bleeding

  • Common in first 3-6 months
  • May indicate pathology — examine and investigate
  • Once pathology excluded: reduce oestrogen, change progestogen, or increase dose

Progestogenic Side-Effects

  • Mood changes, bloating, breast tenderness
  • Management: Switch to alternative progestogen or consider IUS
Review Schedule

Initial Review (3 months)

  • Discuss symptom control
  • Review side-effects
  • Check ongoing indication

Annual Review

  • Check BP, BMI
  • Ensure screening up to date
  • Reinforce lifestyle optimisation
When to Stop HRT
  • ❌ When risks outweigh benefits
  • ❌ No arbitrary limits on age or duration
  • ✓ Stop when patient happy to stop
