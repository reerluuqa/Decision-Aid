üìñ ICD-11 Criteria

Sexual dysfunctions are syndromes comprising various ways in which adults may have difficulty experiencing personally satisfying, non-coercive sexual activities. To meet diagnostic criteria:

  • Occur frequently (although may be absent on some occasions)
  • Have been present for at least several months
  • Associated with clinically significant distress
Persistence & Distress Required

üë´ Epidemiological Context

  • Prevalence: 40-45% of adult women, 20-30% of adult men
  • Lifetime prevalence: 68% for women, 54% for men
  • Increases with age
  • Erectile dysfunction: Up to 52% in men aged 40-70

Treatment
‚ñº

üí¨ Communication Guidelines

  • Involve both partners whenever possible
  • Use gender-neutral language if orientation unknown
  • Explain and justify questions asked
  • Reassure throughout the consultation
  • Be sensitive to emotionally charged words (e.g., rape, trauma)
  • Respond to non-verbal cues signaling discomfort
Primary Care Manageable

üéØ Intervention Levels

  • Intervene at level qualified and comfortable with
  • Primary care management for stable cases
  • Early referral for complex or distressing cases
  • Address both medical and psychological components

üîÑ Reassessment Criteria
