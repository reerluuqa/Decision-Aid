Genitourinary Syndrome of Menopause (GSM)

Updated: 22 Jan 2025

Peer Review: Dr Philippa Vincent

When NOT to Prescribe Critical
  • Active Breast Cancer: Do not use.
  • Undiagnosed Vaginal/Uterine Bleeding: Investigate first.
  • Aromatase Inhibitors: Discuss with specialist (systemic absorption concerns).
  • ER Negative: Safe (unlikely to increase recurrence).
  • ER Positive: Risk unknown; Tamoxifen may reduce impact.
  • Non-hormonal: Use moisturisers/lubricants first-line.
General Principles Standard
  • First-line: Low-dose vaginal oestrogen.
  • Duration: Continue as long as needed (Long-term OK).
  • Progestogen: NOT needed for endometrial protection (minimal absorption).
  • Review: Annually.
Timing: Relief usually ~3 weeks. Max benefit 1-3 months (can take up to a year).
Dosing Regimens Quick Reference
Method Initial Maintenance
Tablet Daily (2 weeks) Twice weekly
Cream / Gel Daily (3-4 weeks) Twice weekly (Bedtime)
Pessary Daily (3 weeks) Twice weekly
Ring Insert Upper 1/3 Replace q3 months (Max 2yrs)
Indications & Symptoms
Vaginal Dryness Dyspareunia Overactive Bladder Recurrent UTIs Frequency/Nocturia

Other Uses: Pre-prolapse repair surgery (if atrophy present) & prior to cervical screening (if prior speculum exam uncomfortable).

If Topical HRT Unsuitable Second Line
  • Ospemifene (Oral SERM):
    Agonist in vagina, antagonist in breast/endometrium. OK for history of breast/endometrial cancer (post-treatment), NOT during active treatment.
  • Prasterone (DHEA Pessary):
    Converted to androgens/oestrogens locally. Contraindicated in history of breast cancer.
Safety & Side-Effects
Common/Rare:
  • Local irritation (rare).
  • Report any vaginal bleeding.
Safety Notes:
  • Minimal systemic absorption.
  • NOT associated with increased breast cancer risk.
