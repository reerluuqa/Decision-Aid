Female Pattern Hair Loss (FPHL)

Quick Reference • Last updated November 2021

1. Is this FPHL?

Typical presentation
  • Diffuse crown/frontal thinning
  • Widened central parting
  • Frontal hairline preserved
  • Onset ≥18 y, often peri-menopausal
Red flags
  • Sudden shedding → telogen effluvium
  • Temporal recession → traction / FFA / thyroid
  • Patchy / scarring → biopsy / referral

2. 30-Second Work-up

  • Hair-pull test: grasp 50-100 hairs, positive if >3 hairs removed
  • Look for hirsutism, acne, virilisation (androgen excess)

3. When to test?

  • Typical FPHL No labs
  • Atypical TSH, ferritin, vit D, FBC
  • Androgen signs Free-androgen index, prolactin → endocrine referral

4. Management Decision Tree

1
Reassure & explain prognosis
(rarely total baldness)
2
Patient prefers no drugs → cosmetic options (wigs, hair fibres, styling)
3
Drug therapy → Minoxidil 5 % foam once-daily (or 2 % BID OTC)
4
Review at 6 mo – continue indefinitely if responding; if not, refer

5. Minoxidil Fast Facts

  • Age limits: 18–65 y
  • Common AE: transient shedding (wk 2-6), facial hypertrichosis, scalp irritation
  • Stop if: pregnancy, chest pain, dizziness, persistent rash

6. Referral triggers

  • Atypical or extensive disease
  • Evidence of androgen-secreting tumour
  • No response after 12 mo minoxidil
  • Severe psychosocial impact → psychology / Alopecia UK
