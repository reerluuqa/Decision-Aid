Female Genital Candidiasis

Management Algorithm
Updated: October 2023

Assessment & Diagnosis

Symptoms

  • Vulval/vaginal itching (often defining symptom)
  • Soreness and irritation
  • White, "cheese-like" discharge (non-offensive)
  • Superficial dyspareunia
  • Dysuria (pain during urination)

Risk Factors

  • Recent antibiotic use (within 3 months)
  • Pregnancy and hormonal contraceptives
  • Uncontrolled diabetes
  • Immunosuppression (HIV, corticosteroids)
  • Local irritants (soaps, douching, tight clothing)

Diagnostic Tests

  • Not routinely needed for acute cases
  • High vaginal swab if recurrent/treatment failure
  • Vaginal pH testing (optional)
  • HbA1c if recurrent/severe infection
  • STI screening if risk factors present

Management Pathways

Acute Infection

First-line: Fluconazole 150mg oral single dose

Alternative: Clotrimazole 500mg intravaginal pessary (single dose)

  • Add topical clotrimazole cream for vulval symptoms
  • Advise on self-care measures (emollients, avoid irritants)

Severe Infection

Treatment: Fluconazole 150mg oral on day 1 AND day 4

  • Consider adding topical clotrimazole cream
  • Follow up if symptoms persist beyond 7-14 days

Recurrent Infection (≥4 episodes/year)

Induction: Fluconazole 150mg every 72 hours for 3 doses

Maintenance: Fluconazole 150mg weekly for 6 months

  • Confirm diagnosis with culture
  • Consider specialist referral if resistant or non-albicans species

During Pregnancy

First-line: Clotrimazole 500mg intravaginal pessary (single dose)

  • Avoid oral fluconazole (especially first trimester)
  • If using oral fluconazole, advise 1-week washout before pregnancy

Important Considerations

Key Points

  • Topical imidazoles may damage latex condoms and diaphragms
  • Treat male partners only if symptomatic
  • Follow up if symptoms persist beyond 7-14 days or recurrent
  • Consider STI screening if risk factors or atypical presentation
  • Treatment failure: Reassess diagnosis, check compliance, culture with sensitivity

Differential Diagnosis

Alternative Conditions

Infections

  • Bacterial vaginosis (malodorous discharge)
  • Trichomoniasis (frothy, green discharge)
  • Chlamydia (discharge, dysuria, no itch)
  • Gonorrhea (purulent discharge)
  • Genital herpes (ulceration, pain)

Non-infective

  • Vulval skin conditions (eczema, lichen sclerosus)
  • Atrophic vaginitis (postmenopausal)
  • Normal physiological discharge
  • Vulvodynia (vulval discomfort)
  • Contact dermatitis (irritants, allergens)
