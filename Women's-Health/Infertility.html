Infertility Assessment & Management Algorithm

A quick reference guide for primary care physicians based on NICE CKS guidance (July 2023)

Definition & When to Investigate

  • Primary infertility: Couple who have never conceived
  • Secondary infertility: Couple who have conceived at least once before
  • Investigate after:
    • 12 months of regular unprotected intercourse (women <35 years)
    • 6 months of regular unprotected intercourse (women ≥35 years)
    • Earlier if known risk factors present

Initial Assessment

History

  • Duration trying to conceive
  • Obstetric/gynecological history
  • Menstrual history (regularity)
  • Sexual history (frequency, difficulties)
  • Medical history (STIs, PID, endocrine disorders)
  • Lifestyle factors (smoking, alcohol, weight)
  • Medication history

Examination

  • BMI calculation (target 19-30)
  • Signs of endocrine disorders (hirsutism, acne)
  • Pelvic examination (if indicated)
  • Blood pressure measurement
  • Thyroid palpation

Initial Investigations

For Women

  • Mid-luteal progesterone (day 21)
  • TSH and prolactin levels
  • Chlamydia test
  • Pelvic ultrasound (if indicated)
  • FSH/LH (if irregular cycles)

For Men

  • Semen analysis (repeat if abnormal)
  • Testosterone (if indicated)
  • FSH/LH (if low testosterone)
  • Consider genetic testing if azoospermia

Common Causes

40%
Female Factors: Ovulatory disorders (25%), Tubal damage (20%), Uterine/peritoneal disorders (10%)
30%
Male Factors: Testicular failure, Obstructive azoospermia, Ejaculatory dysfunction, Abnormal sperm
25%
Unexplained: No identifiable cause despite normal investigations
40%
Combined: Disorders in both partners (overlap with above)

When to Refer

Immediate Referral

  • Amenorrhoea
  • Previous sterilization
  • Known genetic disorders affecting fertility
  • Single women/same-sex couples seeking assisted conception

Routine Referral

  • After 12 months of trying (women <35 years)
  • After 6 months of trying (women ≥35 years)
  • Abnormal semen analysis
  • Anovulation not responding to initial treatment
  • Tubal factor infertility

Basic Management in Primary Care

Lifestyle Advice

  • Weight optimization (BMI 19-30)
  • Smoking cessation
  • Reduce alcohol intake
  • Regular intercourse (every 2-3 days)
  • Reduce stress where possible

Treat Underlying Conditions

  • Thyroid dysfunction
  • STIs (screen and treat)
  • Consider ovulation induction for anovulatory women (after referral confirmation)

Red Flags

Amenorrhoea

Requires urgent investigation for underlying endocrine disorders

Severe Pelvic Pain

May indicate endometriosis, PID, or other serious pathology

History of PID

High risk of tubal damage and ectopic pregnancy

Previous Chemotherapy/Radiotherapy

May cause permanent gonadal damage

Known Genetic Conditions

Requires specialist genetic counseling

Based on NICE CKS guidance last revised July 2023. Always refer to full guidance for comprehensive management.
