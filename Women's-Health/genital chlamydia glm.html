Genital Chlamydia

BMJ Best Practice Ref
Immediate Action
Suspected PID / Ascending Infection?

If pelvic pain, fever, or cervical motion tenderness is present, treat empirically for PID immediately. Do not wait for NAAT results.

  • Fever/Chills High Risk
  • Pelvic/Abdominal Pain PID Risk
  • Right Upper Quadrant Pain Fitz-Hugh Curtis
  • Testicular Pain/Tenderness Epididymitis
Key Presentation
  • Women Often Asymptomatic
  • Mucopurulent Discharge Yellow/Cloudy
  • Post-coital Bleeding Common sign
  • Dysuria Common in Men
  • Men Penile Discharge
Diagnosis
1st Line: NAAT

Nucleic Acid Amplification Test (Sensitivity >90%)

Sample Type (Women) Vaginal swab (Self/Collected)
Cervical swab
Urine
Sample Type (Men) Urine (First-pass)
Urethral swab
Rectal/Pharyngeal Swab at exposure site
Do not test for cure routinely unless pregnant or rectal infection.
Management: Adult Updated May 2025
1st Line: Doxycycline
100 mg orally twice daily for 7 days

Preferred by CDC & UK guidelines. Excellent cure rate.

Alternatives (if doxycycline unsuitable):
Azithromycin
1 g orally single dose
Levofloxacin
500 mg orally once daily for 7 days

⚠️ Black Box Warning: Tendon rupture, neuropathy. Use only if alternatives inappropriate.

Pregnancy Alert: Doxycycline and Fluoroquinolones are contraindicated.
1st Line: Azithromycin
1 g orally single dose

Safe in pregnancy. May reduce risk of preterm delivery.

Alternative: Amoxicillin
500 mg orally three times daily for 7 days
Follow-up:
  • Test of cure 4 weeks after treatment.
  • Retest again within 3 months.
Partner Notification

All sexual contacts within the past 60 days must be notified.

Expedited Partner Therapy (EPT)

Delivering prescription or medication to the partner without clinical exam is permissible in many jurisdictions.

Follow Up
7
Abstain from sex for 7 days after single-dose therapy or until 7-day regimen is complete.
3m
Retest for re-infection approx. 3 months post-treatment.
Complications & Risks
Women
  • Pelvic Inflammatory Disease (PID)
  • Infertility (Tubal factor)
  • Ectopic Pregnancy
  • Fitz-Hugh-Curtis Syndrome (Perihepatitis)
Men
  • Epididymitis
  • Prostatitis
  • Urethral Strictures
  • Reactive Arthritis (3-8% risk)
Neonates
  • Conjunctivitis (Ophthalmia neonatorum)
  • Pneumonia
