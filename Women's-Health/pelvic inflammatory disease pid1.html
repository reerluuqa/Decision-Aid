Pelvic Inflammatory Disease (PID) – Quick Reference

1. Suspect PID

  • New pelvic / lower-abdominal pain
  • Deep dyspareunia
  • Abnormal bleeding or discharge

Red flags: fever, adnexal mass, RUQ pain, pregnancy, IUD <3 wks

2. Immediate Actions

  • Pregnancy test
  • Start empiric antibiotics NOW
  • Swabs: Chlamydia, Gonorrhoea, M. genitalium
  • HIV / syphilis / hepatitis screen
  • Trace partners (last 6 mo)

3. Empiric Antibiotics (14 days)

Risk of GC First-line Alternative
High Ceftriaxone 1 g IM + Doxy 100 mg bid + Metro 400 mg bid Micro consult
Low Same OR Moxifloxacin 400 mg od Levo 500 mg od + Metro 400 mg bid
Fluoroquinolones only if no other options (MHRA)

4. Refer / Admit

  • Pregnant, systemically unwell, TOA, uncertain dx
  • HIV, IUD + ALOs, perihepatitis

5. Follow-up & Patient Advice

  • Review 48–72 h – admit if not improving
  • IUD: remove if severe or no response
  • Barrier contraception + abstinence until both partners treated & cured
  • Test-of-cure: gonorrhoea 2–4 wks; chlamydia 3–5 wks; Mgen 4 wks
  • Analgesia: paracetamol ± ibuprofen

6. 15-Second Phone Script

Rule-out pregnancy → Ceftriaxone 1 g IM + Doxy 100 mg bid + Metro 400 mg bid 14 d → partner trace → review 48–72 h.
