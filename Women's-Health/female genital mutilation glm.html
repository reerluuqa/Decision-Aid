MANDATORY REPORTING DUTY CRITICAL

As of 31st October 2015, UK healthcare professionals must report to the police if they discover FGM in girls under 18. Failure to report is a breach of duty.

Definition & Overview

Female Genital Mutilation (FGM) involves partial or total removal of external female genitalia for non-medical reasons.

WHO Classification (Quick Reference)
Type Name Description
Type I Clitoridectomy Partial or total removal of the clitoris and/or prepuce.
Type II Excision Partial or total removal of clitoris and labia minora, with or without excision of labia majora.
Type III Infibulation High Risk: Narrowing of vaginal opening by cutting/repositioning labia. Seal covers introitus.
Type IV Other Pricking, piercing, incising, scraping, and cauterizing.
Immediate Risks
  • Sepsis
  • Death (Est. 2.3% mortality)
  • Hemorrhage
  • Shock
Note: Procedures often use knives, scissors, glass, or razor blades without anesthetic.
Long-Term Complications
Physical:
  • Uterine, vaginal, pelvic infections
  • Cysts and neuromas
  • Vesico-vaginal fistula
  • Difficulties in menstruation
  • Extensive damage to reproductive system
Obstetric & Sexual:
  • Complications in pregnancy/childbirth
  • Sexual dysfunction
  • Psychological damage
  • Need for Deinfibulation (Type III)
Antenatal Management
RCOG Guideline: Every pregnant woman should be asked if she has been cut.
  • Assessment: Identify grade (requires genital assessment). May be intrusive; refer to specialist FGM clinic if unsure.
  • Deinfibulation (Type III): If the introitus is too narrow for childbirth, tissues must be separated. Offer this antenatally before birth.
Legal & NHS Actions

Female Genital Mutilation Act 2003

Offence: UK nationals/residents performing FGM abroad (even where legal).
Penalty: Up to 14 years prison.

  • Record Data: All acute NHS hospitals must record FGM and report centrally (Maternity, Gynae, Urology, FP).
  • Protect: Ensure daughters do not suffer. Support parents to understand it is a crime.
Ongoing Management & Care
Key Clinical Actions:
  • Multidisciplinary Care: Psychological, educational, and medical input.
  • Patient Education: Ensure they realize the practice is a crime in the UK.
  • Surgery: Reconstructive surgery for sexual consequences is feasible and available.
