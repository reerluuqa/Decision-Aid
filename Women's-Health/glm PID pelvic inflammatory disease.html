Pelvic Inflammatory Disease (PID)

Clinical Reference Algorithm for Fast Decision Making

Based on NICE CKS & BASHH Guidelines (June 2024)

Diagnosis

Key Symptoms

  • Pelvic/lower abdominal pain (usually bilateral)
  • Deep dyspareunia (recent onset)
  • Abnormal vaginal bleeding
  • Abnormal vaginal discharge
  • Systemic symptoms: fever, nausea, vomiting
  • Right upper quadrant pain (perihepatitis)

Risk Factors

  • Age <25 years
  • Multiple sexual partners
  • New sexual partner (within 3 months)
  • No barrier contraception
  • History of STI or PID
  • Recent uterine instrumentation
  • IUD insertion (within past 3 weeks)

Examination

  • Lower abdominal tenderness
  • Cervical motion tenderness
  • Adnexal tenderness/mass
  • Uterine tenderness
  • Fever or signs of systemic illness
  • Mucopurulent discharge
  • Cervical friability

Investigations

  • Pregnancy test (essential)
  • Vaginal swabs: chlamydia, gonorrhoea
  • Consider Mycoplasma genitalium testing
  • Wet mount microscopy for pus cells
  • FBC, CRP/ESR if indicated
  • HIV/syphilis/hepatitis testing

Management

Refer to Hospital

  • Pregnancy or suspected ectopic pregnancy
  • Severe systemic symptoms
  • Adnexal mass (possible TOA)
  • Systemically unwell
  • Unable to tolerate oral treatment

Primary Care

  • Start empirical antibiotics immediately
  • Refer to sexual health service
  • Symptom relief: paracetamol/ibuprofen
  • Manage sexual partners (within last 6 months)
  • Follow-up within 72 hours

Antibiotic Regimens

First-line
Ceftriaxone 1g IM stat + Doxycycline 100mg BD for 14 days + Metronidazole 400mg BD for 14 days
Alternative
Ofloxacin 400mg BD for 14 days + Metronidazole 400mg BD for 14 days
If Mycoplasma genitalium
Moxifloxacin 400mg OD for 14 days

Follow-up

  • Review within 72 hours to assess response
  • If no improvement: reconsider diagnosis, consider admission, change antibiotics
  • Test of cure not routinely needed if symptoms resolve
  • Discuss long-term risks: infertility, ectopic pregnancy, chronic pelvic pain
