Female Infertility Management Algorithm

Evidence-based clinical decision support tool | BMJ Best Practice Guidelines

Initial Assessment & History

Comprehensive History
Key pointers for female infertility: age >35, irregular menses, STD history, pelvic pain, BMI extremes, smoking
  • Menstrual history (cycle length, flow, moliminal symptoms)
  • Sexual history (frequency, timing, dyspareunia)
  • Prior pregnancies and outcomes
  • Past medical history (autoimmune disease, cancer, chemotherapy)
  • Surgical history (pelvic surgery, appendectomy)
  • Social history (smoking, alcohol, caffeine, stress)
  • Occupational exposures
High Priority
Physical Examination
Look for signs of hyperandrogenism, galactorrhea, and pelvic abnormalities
  • BMI assessment
  • Signs of hyperandrogenism (hirsutism, acne)
  • Galactorrhea check
  • Pelvic exam (uterine size, mobility, tenderness)
  • Adnexal masses or tenderness
  • Cul-de-sac nodularity
Timing of Evaluation
Guideline recommendations for when to start evaluation

Women <35 years: Evaluate after 1 year of unsuccessful attempts

Women ≥35 years: Evaluate after 6 months

Earlier evaluation: If known cause (ovarian failure, azoospermia) or medical history suggests impaired fertility

Age >35 = Urgent
Male Partner Evaluation
Male factors detected in 27.8% of ART cycles

Mandatory: Semen analysis

Consider: Hormonal evaluation, genetic testing if abnormal

Important: Often present even if female factor identified

Standard Procedure

Diagnostic Investigations

Ovulation Assessment
Regular cycles with moliminal symptoms highly correlate with ovulation

Tests to Confirm Ovulation:

  • Luteal-phase progesterone: >9.5 nmol/L (>3 ng/mL) indicates ovulation
  • Urinary LH kits: Predicts ovulation ~24 hours in advance
  • Basal body temperature: No longer recommended (not reliable)
  • Serial ultrasound: Confirms follicular growth and ovulation
Routine
Anatomical Assessment

Imaging Options:

All patients need structural assessment of reproductive tract
  • Transvaginal ultrasound (TVUS/S): First-line imaging
  • 3D Ultrasound: Superior for uterine anomalies
  • MRI: Historical gold standard for Müllerian anomalies
  • HSG: For tubal patency (sensitivity 53%, specificity 87%)
  • HyCoSy: No radiation, better sensitivity (93%)
  • Saline infusion sonography: For intracavitary lesions
Minimize Radiation
Ovarian Reserve Testing
Age is the best predictor of fertility

Common Tests:

  • Basal FSH (day 2-5): >10 IU/L suggests diminished reserve
  • Antral follicle count (day 3): <5-7 oocytes suggests diminished reserve
  • AMH (any day): <7.1 pmol/L (<1 ng/mL) suggests diminished reserve
  • Estradiol: Provides context for FSH interpretation

Note: Tests predict response to stimulation, not necessarily pregnancy

Age >35 = Consider Testing
Surgical Evaluation
Decision based on clinical suspicion

Indications for Laparoscopy:

  • Cyclic pelvic pain suggestive of endometriosis
  • History of pelvic infection or surgery
  • Abnormal HSG/HyCoSy results
  • Endometriomas on ultrasound

Benefits: Diagnostic + therapeutic (adhesiolysis, endometriosis ablation)

Invasive - Use Selectively

Treatment Options

Ovulatory Dysfunction

First-line Options:

Goal: Monofollicular ovulation to reduce multiple pregnancy risk
  • Clomifene: 50-100mg days 2-6, max 6 months
  • Letrozole: 2.5-7.5mg days 3-7 (first-line for PCOS)
  • Gonadotrophins: For hypothalamic amenorrhea or failed oral agents

PCOS-Specific:

  • Weight loss if BMI elevated
  • Letrozole preferred over clomifene
  • Metformin adjunct (1500-2000mg)
  • Laparoscopic ovarian drilling if clomifene-resistant
Effective
Tubal Infertility
IVF is the best option for abnormal fallopian tubes

Management:

  • IVF: First-line treatment
  • Hydrosalpinx: Remove/clip before IVF
  • Tubal surgery: Rarely first-line (except sterilization reversal)
  • Success rates: ~30% pregnancy, but 14% ectopic risk

Consider surgery: Younger age, unilateral disease, mild adhesions

IVF Urgent if Age >35
Endometriosis-Related Infertility

Treatment Approach:

Decision based on staging, duration, symptoms, and age
  • Mild disease: Expectant management or IUI + ovarian stimulation
  • Moderate-Severe: IVF has highest success rate
  • Surgery: Laparoscopic excision/ablation before IVF if pain present
  • Adenomyosis: GnRH agonist pre-treatment may improve IVF outcomes
Individualize Treatment
Unexplained Infertility
~25% of couples; expectant management often effective

UK NICE Recommendations:

  • Expectant management: 2 years before intervention (if age <38)
  • IVF: First-line after failed expectant management
  • IUI + ovarian stimulation: Consider but less cost-effective
  • Avoid: Oral agents alone (no superiority over expectant)

Success rates: 50.7% live birth per egg retrieval for women <35

Patience Required
Age-Related Infertility
Fecundity decreases significantly after age 35

Options:

  • Ovarian stimulation: May confer little benefit
  • Oocyte donation: Successful option for diminished reserve
  • Expect realistic success rates:
    • <35 years: 50.7% live birth per retrieval
    • 35-37 years: 36.3%
    • 38-40 years: 23.3%
    • >40 years: 7.9%
Time-Critical
Uterine Pathology

Management:

  • Endometrial polyps: Hysteroscopic removal recommended
  • Uterine septum: Metroplasty controversial for infertility alone
  • Leiomyomas: Consider removal if submucosal/intramural
  • Asherman's syndrome: Hysteroscopic adhesiolysis
Effect on fertility outcomes varies by pathology type
Surgical Indication

Follow-up & Monitoring

Treatment Monitoring

During Ovarian Stimulation:

  • Serial transvaginal ultrasound (follicle count/size)
  • Hormone levels (estradiol, LH, progesterone)
  • Review responsiveness every 3 cycles minimum
  • Adjust doses to avoid hyperstimulation
Close monitoring essential for safety
Specialist Supervision
Complications

Key Risks:

  • Multiple gestation: 10% clomifene, 30% gonadotrophin/IVF cycles
  • OHSS: ~20% of IVF, <5% severe
  • Ectopic pregnancy: 1.8-8.6% after IVF (vs 2-4% natural)
  • Congenital anomalies: RR 1.33 vs natural conception
  • Ovarian cancer: Possible association (weak evidence)
Monitor Closely
Prognosis
Highly dependent on age, diagnosis, and treatment plan

Success Rates:

  • Clomifene: 73% ovulation, 36% pregnancy, 10% twins
  • Gonadotrophins: 20-22% per cycle, 30% multiple gestation
  • IVF: Most successful treatment
  • Expectant management: Effective in well-selected couples

Key factor: Female age is strongest predictor

Favorable Outcomes
Psychological Support
Infertility treatment is highly stressful
  • Depression/anxiety scores similar to cancer/CVD/HIV patients
  • Stress associated with treatment failure
  • Psychological intervention may improve pregnancy rates
  • Focus on lowering anxiety and communication

Resources: Resolve, ASRM, local support groups
