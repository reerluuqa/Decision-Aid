Infertility – Primary Care Quick Reference

Based on NICE CKS, last revised July 2023

First Contact Red Flags Investigations Lifestyle Referral Post-Referral Complications Quick Numbers

1. First Contact – Both Partners

History

  • Time trying to conceive
  • Cycle regularity / coital frequency
  • STI / PID history
  • Medications, lifestyle factors

Examination

  • BMI
  • Secondary sexual characteristics
  • Pelvic / genital exam

2. Immediate Referral Red Flags (< 1 year)

WomenMen
≥ 36 y & 6 m trying Azoospermia / severe oligospermia
Amenorrhoea / oligomenorrhoea Previous urogenital surgery / cancer
Known endometriosis, PID, tubal surgery Varicocele
Abnormal pelvic exam Two abnormal semen results

3. Day-1 Investigations

Woman

  • Mid-luteal progesterone (Day 21 ± 7)
  • Chlamydia NAAT
  • TFT / prolactin if oligo- / amenorrhoea

Man

  • Semen analysis (WHO 2021)
  • Repeat in 3 m if abnormal
  • Chlamydia NAAT

5. Referral Thresholds

  • Women < 36 y1 year normal tests + no conception
  • Women ≥ 36 y6 months
  • Any red flagimmediate

6. Post-Referral Snapshot

Women

  • Tubal patency: HSG or lap & dye
  • Endometriosis surgery if needed

Men

  • Endocrine tests ± imaging
  • Surgical correction (varicocele, obstruction)

Treatment Options

  • Medical – clomifene, gonadotrophins
  • Assisted – IUI, IVF, ICSI, donor

7. Post-IVF Complications to Watch

  • OHSS: bloating, ascites, ↓ urine → urgent specialist call
  • Ectopic pregnancy – low abdominal pain, PV bleeding
  • Pelvic infection – pain & fever weeks after oocyte retrieval
  • Multiple pregnancy – refer to NICE twin guideline
