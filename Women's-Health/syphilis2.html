Quick-Reference: Syphilis Care Pathway

1. When to Suspect

Primary: painless chancre ± nodes Secondary: rash on palms/soles, condylomata lata Latent: asymptomatic positive serology Tertiary: neuro, cardio, ocular, gummatous

High-risk: MSM, multiple partners, prior STI, HIV-positive, transactional sex.

2. Immediate Actions (10-second rule)

  1. Refer ALL suspected cases → GUM / sexual health clinic.
    Specialist tests, staging, contact tracing.
  2. Advise abstinence or condoms until diagnosis excluded or treatment complete.
  3. Screen simultaneously: HIV, Hep B/C, Chlamydia, Gonorrhoea.

3. If Patient Refuses Referral

  • Phone GUM consultant → may approve primary-care serology.
  • Swab active lesion: PCR ± dark-field microscopy.
  • Blood: Treponemal EIA + RPR (repeat 3 months if high-risk exposure).
  • Any positive → re-refer urgently.

4. Serology Cheat-Sheet

Test Use Limit

5. Red Flags → Urgent Neuro / Cardio Review

Neurological or visual symptoms Aortic regurgitation / aneurysm Pregnancy (congenital risk)

6. Patient Info & Prevention

  • Leaflets: NHS, BASHH, FPA.
  • Condoms, risk-reduction counselling.
  • MSM at high risk → screen every 3 months.
See a chancre or syphilitic rash? → Refer to GUM same day.
