Endometriosis Clinical Reference

Red Flags & Immediate Actions

Deep Disease Symptoms Haematuria, flank pain, haematochezia, or dyschezia (cyclic).
Action: Suspect bladder/bowel/ureter involvement. Refer to specialist endometriosis service. Consider MRI.
Complex Adnexal Mass Mass >10cm or solid components on ultrasound.
Action: Urgent Gynaecology referral to rule out malignancy (CA-125 not definitive). MRI for mapping.
Severe Anatomical Distortion "Frozen pelvis", fixed retroverted uterus, obliterated cul-de-sac.
Action: Advanced surgical planning required. Do not attempt simple laparoscopy without expertise.
Refractory Pain (Adolescents) Chronic pelvic pain unresponsive to NSAIDs/OCPs for 3-6 months.
Action: Refer to Paediatric/Adolescent Gynaecology service. Laparoscopy may be diagnostic.

Definition & Theory

Endometrial glands and stroma outside the uterine cavity.

  • Common Sites: Pelvic peritoneum, ovaries, uterosacral ligaments.
  • Demographics: Up to 10% of reproductive-aged women. Peak 18-29 years.
  • Risk: First-degree relative = 7-10x increased risk.
  • Theories: Retrograde menstruation (most common), Mullerian rests (adolescents), lymphatic spread.

Presentation

PROGRESSIVE SIGNS
Acyclic Pain Dyschezia School/Work Absenteeism
Exam Findings:
  • Uterosacral nodularity & tenderness (Rectovaginal)
  • Fixed, retroverted uterus (Late stage)
  • Pelvic mass (Endometrioma)
  • Tender pelvic musculature

Diagnostics

Transvaginal US (1st Line) Gold Std (Imaging)
Detect Endometrioma Sens 93% / Spec 96%
Detect Deep Disease (Rectosigmoid) Sens 89% / Spec 97%
Laparoscopy Definitive
Histopathology Visual + Biopsy
MRI Pre-op planning / Deep disease

Note: Normal US/MRI does NOT exclude endometriosis.

Management Approach

Medical (First Line)
Analgesia
NSAIDs (Ibuprofen) or Paracetamol. Target prostaglandin-mediated pain.
Hormonal Contraceptives
Combined OCP (Cyclical or Continuous). Progestogens (Medroxyprogesterone, Dienogest). Induce amenorrhoea.
Levonorgestrel IUD
Effective for localized pain and reducing dysmenorrhoea.
GnRH Agonists/Antagonists
For severe symptoms or pre-surgical shrinkage. Add-back therapy recommended.
Surgical & Specialist
Diagnostic Laparoscopy
Indicated if empirical therapy fails or symptoms are highly suspicious.
Excision / Ablation
Laparoscopic destruction of lesions. Excision preferred for deep disease.
Infertility Management
Controlled ovarian hyper-stimulation & IVF post-surgery if natural conception fails.
Multidisciplinary Care
Involve pain specialists, physiotherapy (pelvic floor), and psych support for chronic pain.
Condition Key Differentiators
Adenomyosis Symptoms similar to endometriosis. MRI shows junctional zone widening. Uterus often globular/enlarged.
PID (Pelvic Inflammatory Disease) Acute: Fever, malodorous discharge, CMT. Chronic: Indistinguishable without history/labs.
Interstitial Cystitis Urinary frequency/urgency, pain relieved by voiding. Positive Potassium sensitivity test.
Irritable Bowel Syndrome Alternating constipation/diarrhoea, bloating. Dyschezia typical of endo.
Ovarian Cancer Weight gain, increased abdominal girth, complex solid/cystic mass with ascites on US.
Uterine Myoma Heavy/irregular bleeding. Enlarged nodular uterus. Concentric hypoechoic mass on US.
