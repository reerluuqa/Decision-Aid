Progestogen-Only Contraception

Clinical Quick Reference Algorithm
Based on NICE CKS (Nov 2024) | For Fast Decision-Making in Primary Care

1. Patient Eligibility

Age: 13–60 years (female)

Key Rule: Use UKMEC to assess safety.

  • Active breast cancer → UKMEC 4 (contraindicated)
  • Most conditions (e.g., hypertension, migraine) → UKMEC 1–2 (safe)

2. Methods Overview

Method Examples Duration Efficacy
POP (Mini-pill) Norethisterone (Noriday®)
Desogestrel (Zelleta®)		 Daily ~99% / 91%
Implant Etonogestrel (Nexplanon®) 3 years >99%
Injectable DMPA (Depo-Provera®)
Sayana Press®		 12–13 weeks >99%
Note: Desogestrel POPs (e.g., Zelleta®) have a 12-hour window vs. 3-hour for others.

3. Starting Contraception

General Rule: Start on days 1–5 of cycle → no backup needed.

  • After day 5? Use condoms/abstinence for 7 days (POP) or 7–14 days (implant/injectable).
  • Postpartum (non-breastfeeding): Start POP/injectable at day 21; implant at day 21–28.
  • Post-abortion/miscarriage: Start immediately if <24 weeks.

4. Switching Methods

Direct switch between progestogen-only methods → no gap, no backup.

From combined hormonal contraception → switch on day 1 of pill-free interval or after last active pill.

5. Key Safety & Advice

  • ✅ Advantages: No estrogen (safe in thrombotic risk, migraine), breastfeeding-safe, high adherence (LARC)
  • ⚠️ Adverse Effects: Irregular bleeding, weight gain (esp. injectable), BMD ↓ with long-term DMPA

6. Drug Interactions

⛔ Enzyme-inducing drugs (e.g., carbamazepine, rifampicin, St John’s wort):
Reduce efficacy of POPs/injectables.
Implant remains effective (UKMEC 1).
For others: Use alternative (e.g., copper IUD) or backup.

7. Managing Common Issues

  • Unscheduled Bleeding: Reassure; rule out pregnancy/STIs if persistent.
  • Missed POP:
    • Desogestrel: >12h late → backup for 7 days
    • Other POPs: >3h late → backup for 2 days
  • Injectable timing: Repeat every 12 weeks; up to 13 weeks acceptable. >13 weeks? → restart with 7-day backup.
  • Pregnancy with implant? Remove if possible (risk of ectopic).

8. Follow-up & Monitoring

  • Injectables: Monitor weight, BMD with long-term use (>2 years)
  • All methods: Annual review for side effects, adherence, risk changes

9. NICE Quality Standard

Women must be offered information and choice of all methods, including LARC.
