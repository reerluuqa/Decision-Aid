Menorrhagia Quick Reference

Heavy Menstrual Bleeding Management Algorithm

Definition

Excessive menstrual blood loss which interferes with a woman's physical, social, emotional, and/or material quality of life. Highly subjective - no specific blood loss volume required for diagnosis.

Assessment

History

  • Bleeding pattern: Onset, frequency, duration, temporal association
  • Menstrual history: Menarche age, gravidity/parity, cycle regularity, LMP
  • Medical history: Endometriosis, coagulation disorders (e.g., von Willebrand disease)
  • Drug history: Current medications, previous treatments for menorrhagia
  • Contraceptive history: Current use, future plans
  • Cervical screening: Confirm up-to-date with normal results
  • Symptoms of anemia: Dyspnea, fatigue, headache, sore tongue
  • Quality of life impact: Social, emotional, occupational effects

Physical Examination

  • Consider if no history of menorrhagia alone (not required before pharmacological treatment except for LNG-IUS)
  • Abdominal exam: Assess for fibroids/masses
  • Pelvic exam: Bimanual and speculum exam (except in non-sexually active young women)
  • Signs of systemic disease: Goitre (hypothyroidism), bruising (coagulation disorders), acne/hirsutism (PCOS)

Investigations

Essential

  • Full blood count: All women (to detect iron deficiency anemia)

Selective (based on history/exam)

  • Pregnancy test: Women of reproductive age with abnormal bleeding
  • Swabs: If infection suspected
  • Thyroid function tests: If features of hypothyroidism
  • Coagulation studies: If heavy bleeding since menarche + personal/family history

Not Recommended

  • Routine serum ferritin
  • Female hormone testing
  • Measurement of menstrual blood loss

Management Principles

Treatment choice based on:

  • Underlying cause (if identified)
  • Woman's preferences and priorities
  • Contraceptive needs
  • Future fertility plans
  • Impact on quality of life

Referral Criteria

  • Suspected gynecological cancer
  • Diagnostic uncertainty
  • Surgical treatment indicated
  • Failed pharmacological management
  • Suspected structural abnormalities (fibroids, polyps)

Red Flags

  • Postcoital bleeding
  • Postmenopausal bleeding
  • Intermenstrual bleeding
  • Pelvic pain/pressure symptoms
  • Signs of systemic disease

Note: This algorithm is based on NICE CKS guidance. For detailed management options, prescribing information, and supporting evidence, please refer to the complete guidance document.
