Patient Assessment

1
Confirm not pregnant - Reasonably certain using clinical criteria
2
Review medical history - Check for:
VTE risk factors Migraine with aura Cardiovascular disease Smoking >35 years Breast cancer history
3
Check medications - Identify potential interactions
4
Discuss preferences - Lifestyle factors and method preferences

First-Line Options

First-line: Monophasic preparations with 30mcg oestrogen + norethisterone or levonorgestrel
Note: Consider patient preference - any COC can be offered first-line

Prescription Duration

Up to 12 months supply for women initiating or continuing CHC

Tailored Regimens

Extended/continuous use to reduce hormone-free interval symptoms

When to Start CHC

Situation When to Start Additional Contraception
Regular menstrual cycles Day 1-5 of cycle None (if day 1-5)
Amenorrhoea Any time (if not pregnant) 7 days (9 days for Qlaira®)
Postpartum (not breastfeeding) Day 21 postpartum 7 days
Postpartum (breastfeeding) After 6 weeks 7 days
Post-termination/miscarriage Within 5 days None

Managing Missed Pills

Important: Qlaira® and Zoely® have different regimens - follow specific guidelines

Standard COCs (not Qlaira® or Zoely®)

Missed 1 pill

Take immediately, continue as normal

Missed 2+ pills (Week 1)

Take immediately, use barrier contraception for 7 days, consider emergency contraception if UPSI in pill-free week

Missed 2+ pills (Week 2)

Take immediately, continue as normal

Missed 2+ pills (Week 3)

Finish active pills, start new pack immediately (skip pill-free interval)

Drug Interactions

Reduced Efficacy

Rifampicin Antiepileptics St John's wort Some antiretrovirals Tirzepatide

Antibiotics

Only additional precautions needed if causing vomiting/diarrhea

Lamotrigine

CHC may reduce lamotrigine levels - monitor seizures

Follow-up & Monitoring

1
First review: 3 months after initiation
2
Subsequent reviews: Annually if no problems
3
Monitor: Blood pressure, significant changes in bleeding pattern
4
Adverse Effects:
Nausea Breast tenderness Headache Mood changes VTE risk
