Immediate Care & Forensics

Core Principle: Listen, Believe, Don't Judge. Give back control.

Forensic Windows:
  • Mouth evidence: Lost within 24 hours. Collect Early-evidence kit.
  • Urine/Drugs: Store for facilitated assault.

SARC (Sexual Assault Referral Centre)

Encourage prompt reporting for best evidence.

  • Encourage police reporting (if desired)
  • Refer to SARC (Anonymous option available)
  • DNA contamination avoided
  • Early-evidence kit used (Mouth swab)

Medical Management

Pregnancy Risk

Assess and manage need for emergency contraception.

Risk Action
Chlamydia / Gonorrhoea Prophylaxis if in doubt
Hepatitis B Vaccination
HIV PEPSE (See Below)

PEPSE (HIV Prophylaxis)

Guidelines: BASHH (2021) / UK Guideline (2021).

Note: Risk quantification is difficult. If in doubt, prophylaxis is the standard strategy.

Context & Definitions

Legal Definitions (UK):
  • Rape: Penetration of vagina/anus/mouth by penis without consent.
  • Assault by penetration: Vagina/Anus penetration with object/part without consent.
  • Sexual Assault: Includes attempts. 'Serious' (rape/penetration) vs 'Less serious' (touching).

High Risk Groups

  • 16-24 yr women (4x risk)
  • Survivors of childhood abuse
  • People with disabilities
  • Substance abuse / Homeless
  • Sex workers / Prisoners

Long-term Effects & Sequelae

PTSD

Most common long-term consequence. Higher risk if: threat to life, violent force, negative reaction to disclosure, or ethnic minority.

Warning: Psychological debriefing may harm. Early intervention for distress is indicated.

Physical Health

Chronic Pain: Pelvic pain, fibromyalgia, chronic headaches.

Cancer Risk: Higher cervical cancer risk (confounders accounted), but less likely to access screening.

Key Learning Point

"While others saw me as unable to think for myself, the staff encouraged me to make my own informed decisions. Trusting in myself was crucial in restoring my sense of self worth."
