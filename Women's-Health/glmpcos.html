When to Suspect PCOS

Adults

  • Hyperandrogenism: acne, hirsutism, female pattern hair loss
  • Ovulation disorders: oligomenorrhoea, amenorrhoea, infertility

Adolescents

  • Clinical hyperandrogenism (severe acne, hirsutism)
  • Irregular menstrual cycles:
    • >1 year to <3 years post-menarche: cycles >45 or <21 days
    • >3 years post-menarche: cycles >35 or <21 days, or <8 cycles/year
    • Any cycle >90 days
    • Primary amenorrhoea by age 15

Other Indicators

  • Family history of PCOS
  • Insulin resistance (central obesity, acanthosis nigricans)
Note

Adolescents with features but not meeting criteria should be considered at 'increased risk' and reassessed at 8 years post-menarche.

Diagnostic Criteria

Adults

2 of 3 criteria required:

  • Ovulatory dysfunction
  • Clinical/biochemical hyperandrogenism
  • Polycystic ovarian morphology on ultrasound (≥20 follicles in at least one ovary)

Adolescents

Both required:

  • Hyperandrogenism
  • Irregular menstrual cycles
Note

Ultrasound not recommended for diagnosis in adolescents.

Postmenopausal

  • Consider if past diagnosis or long-term history of oligo-amenorrhoea with hyperandrogenism and/or polycystic ovary morphology
  • Further investigations needed for new-onset hyperandrogenism to rule out tumors

Key Considerations

  • Polycystic ovaries not required for diagnosis
  • Women of non-Caucasian ethnicity may need different criteria
  • Diagnosis considered enduring/lifelong

Essential Investigations

Initial Tests

  • Total testosterone
  • SHBG (calculate free androgen index)
  • LH, FSH, prolactin, TSH (to rule out other causes)
Important

Stop hormonal contraception 3 months before testing if androgen assessment needed.

Ultrasound

  • For adults only (not adolescents)
  • Not required if diagnosis obvious clinically/biochemically
  • Polycystic ovarian morphology: ≥20 follicles in at least one ovary

Additional Tests

Consider if:

  • Signs of virilization
  • Rapidly progressing hirsutism (<1 year)
  • Testosterone >5 nmol/L or >2× upper limit of normal

Tests to consider:

  • Androstenedione
  • DHEAS

Red Flags

  • Deep voice
  • Reduced breast size
  • Increased muscle bulk
  • Clitoral hypertrophy

Screening for Complications

All Women with PCOS

Metabolic Screening

  • Lipid profile
  • Oral glucose tolerance test (regardless of BMI)
  • Blood pressure monitoring
  • Weight/BMI assessment

Mental Health

  • Screen for depression
  • Screen for anxiety
  • Screen for eating disorders

Other Considerations

  • Sleep apnoea screening if symptomatic
  • Endometrial cancer risk assessment
  • Cardiovascular risk assessment

Complications to Monitor

  • Type 2 diabetes (3-5× increased risk)
  • Cardiovascular disease
  • Endometrial hyperplasia/cancer
  • Obstructive sleep apnoea
  • Non-alcoholic fatty liver disease
  • Pregnancy complications

Management Principles

Lifestyle Modifications

  • Weight management (if overweight/obese)
  • Regular exercise program
  • Dietary advice (low glycemic index)
  • Smoking cessation

Pharmacological

  • Menstrual irregularities: combined hormonal contraception
  • Hyperandrogenism: anti-androgens, cosmetic treatments
  • Metabolic features: metformin if insulin resistance
  • Infertility: ovulation induction agents

Referral Indications

  • Infertility concerns
  • Inadequate response to treatment
  • Suspected androgen-secreting tumor
  • Severe psychological comorbidity
  • Endometrial hyperplasia

Follow-up

  • Annual review of symptoms
  • Regular metabolic parameter monitoring
  • Diabetes screening every 1-3 years
  • CVD risk assessment
