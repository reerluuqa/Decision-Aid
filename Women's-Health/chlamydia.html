Chlamydia Clinical Algorithm

One-Page Reference for Fast Clinical Decision Making
Adapted from NICE CKS Guidance (February 2025)

When to Suspect

Women

  • Increased vaginal discharge
  • Post-coital/intermenstrual bleeding
  • Dysuria, pelvic pain, dyspareunia
  • Mucopurulent cervical discharge

Men

  • Dysuria, urethral discharge
  • Urethral discomfort
  • Epididymo-orchitis
Remember

70% of women and 50% of men are asymptomatic

Screening

Screen asymptomatic individuals who:

  • Are sexual partners of infected persons
  • Are sexually active women <25 years (annually)
  • Have concerns about sexual exposure
  • Are <25 years and treated for chlamydia in past 3 months
  • Had ≥2 sexual partners in previous 12 months
  • Are seeking termination of pregnancy

Testing

Method of choice: Nucleic acid amplification test (NAAT)

Women

  • Vulvo-vaginal swab (preferred)
  • Endocervical swab
  • First-catch urine

Men

  • First-catch urine (preferred)
  • Urethral swab

Extra-genital: Consider rectal swabs if symptomatic or MSM with HIV

Management

  1. Strongly recommend referral to GUM clinic
  2. If declined/unable to attend, manage in primary care
  3. Provide written information on chlamydia
  4. Advise:
    • Treat current partners
    • Avoid sexual intercourse until treatment completed (7 days after azithromycin)

Treatment

Non-pregnant adults & children >13 years

  • First-line: Doxycycline 100mg twice daily for 7 days
  • Alternatives:
    • Azithromycin 1g single dose, then 500mg daily for 2 days
    • Erythromycin 500mg twice daily for 10-14 days
    • Ofloxacin 200mg twice daily for 7 days (only if other options inappropriate)

Pregnancy

  • Azithromycin 1g single dose, then 500mg daily for 2 days
  • Erythromycin 500mg four times daily for 7 days
  • Amoxicillin 500mg three times daily for 7 days

Partner Notification

  • All sexual partners within last 6 months (or most recent if longer)
  • Encourage attendance at GUM clinic for screening and treatment
  • If unwilling, provide details to GUM for partner notification (with consent)

Follow-up

Test of cure: Not routinely needed, but indicated in:

  • Pregnancy (defer for ≥3 weeks after treatment)
  • Poor compliance suspected
  • Persistent symptoms

Repeat testing:

  • Offer to all <25 years at 3-6 months post-treatment
  • Consider for those >25 years at high risk of reinfection

Urgent Referral

Refer urgently if:
  • Suspected pelvic inflammatory disease
  • No response to first-line treatment
  • Children/young people (follow child safeguarding procedures)
