Trichomoniasis Clinical Algorithm

A concise reference guide for diagnosis and management of trichomoniasis in primary care

Assessment & Diagnosis

Women
  • Vaginal discharge (70%)
  • Vulval itching
  • Dysuria
  • Offensive odor
  • 50% asymptomatic
Men
  • Urethral discharge (20-60%)
  • Dysuria
  • 15-50% asymptomatic
Risk Factors
Multiple partners Inconsistent condom use Current gonorrhea/chlamydia Bacterial vaginosis
Testing
  • Ideal: Refer to GUM clinic
  • Primary care: NAAT testing
  • Also test for: Chlamydia, gonorrhea, HIV, syphilis

Treatment Guidelines

Non-Pregnant
  • Metronidazole 400-500mg twice daily x7 days
  • OR Metronidazole 2g single dose
Pregnant/Breastfeeding
  • Metronidazole 400mg twice daily x7 days
  • Avoid single dose
  • Inform midwife/obstetrician
HIV Positive
  • Metronidazole 500mg twice daily x7 days
Special Considerations
  • Metronidazole allergy: Refer to GUM specialist
  • Asymptomatic pregnant women: Seek specialist advice

Treatment Algorithm

Follow this step-by-step approach for managing trichomoniasis

If Symptoms Persist: Check Adherence, Exclude Reinfection, Repeat 7-Day Course, Refer to GUM

Partner Management

  • Treat current partners
  • Treat partners from past 4 weeks
  • Screen all partners for STIs
  • Advise abstinence until all complete treatment

Follow-Up Protocol

  • Only test for cure if symptoms persist/recur
  • Wait 48 hours after treatment before retesting
  • Treatment failure: Check adherence, exclude reinfection
  • Repeat 7-day metronidazole course
  • Refer to GUM if still symptomatic
