Tamoxifen Advisor

Last Revised: Feb 2024 • Target: Primary Care

Immediate Red Flags

⚠
Symptoms of Thromboembolism Pain/swelling in calf, sudden breathlessness. Use DVT/PE Wells Score immediately.
⚠
Gynaecological Cancer Signs Abnormal vaginal bleeding or discharge. Refer to Gynaecological cancers pathway.
⚠
Pregnancy Teratogenic risk. Contraindicated. Do not use unless treating infertility under specialist care.

Contra-indications & Cautions

  • STOP
    Pregnancy
    Risk of malformations
  • STOP
    History of DVT/PE
    Especially for primary prevention
  • STOP
    Active Thrombosis
  • CAUTION
    Premenopausal Women
    Risk of reduced bone density.

Key Drug Interactions

AVOID: CYP2D6 Inhibitors Paroxetine, Fluoxetine, Quinidine, Bupropion. Reduces efficacy.
AVOID: Hormonal Contraceptives Increases thrombosis risk. Use barrier methods.
AVOID: HRT Increases thrombosis risk & may reduce tamoxifen efficacy.
CAUTION: Warfarin Tamoxifen enhances anticoagulant effect. Monitor INR closely.
ALTERNATIVE Antidepressants Citalopram, Escitalopram, Sertraline, Venlafaxine (weak CYP2D6).

Common Adverse Effects

Hot Flushes Very Common (~50%)
Nausea Common
Vaginal Bleeding/Discharge Common
Fatigue Common
Fluid Retention Common
Leg Cramps / Muscle Pain Common

Management Actions

Nausea
Take with food, milk, or at night. Usually improves after a few weeks.
Menopausal Symptoms
Try non-hormonal options: CBT, relaxation, vaginal moisturisers.
Elective Surgery
Discuss with consultant. For primary prevention/infertility: Stop 6 weeks prior. For cancer treatment: Continue unless risk outweighs benefit.

Patient Information & Safety

Contraception

Use Barrier methods only. Do not use hormonal contraceptives. Avoid pregnancy for 2 months after stopping.

Reporting

Report abnormal vaginal bleeding, calf pain/swelling, or sudden breathlessness immediately.

Duration

Standard dose is 20mg daily. Do not stop without specialist advice. Course typically 5 years for prevention.
