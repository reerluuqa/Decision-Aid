Key Considerations & When to Recommend

Male Condoms

Best for: STI protection, dual protection with other methods, men who can maintain erection

Key points:

  • Check expiration date and safety markings (BSI, CE)
  • Use new condom for each act
  • Water-based lubricants only for latex condoms
  • Non-latex alternatives available for allergies
  • Dispose properly after use

Female Condoms

Best for: Women wanting control over protection, latex allergy, oil-based lubricant use

Key points:

  • Can insert up to 8 hours before intercourse
  • Only one size available
  • Polyurethane material (oil-based lubricants OK)
  • Less likely to tear than male condoms

Diaphragms & Cervical Caps

Best for: Women wanting non-hormonal method, can plan ahead

Key points:

  • Require fitting by healthcare professional
  • Must use with spermicide (Gygel®)
  • Leave in place 6 hours after intercourse
  • Recheck fit after 3kg weight change or childbirth
  • Not for use until 6 weeks postpartum

Important Counseling Points

STI Protection

Only condoms provide reliable STI protection. Diaphragms and cervical caps offer minimal protection against STIs.

Spermicides

Required with diaphragms/caps. Not recommended with condoms (increases HIV risk). N-9 may cause irritation/allergy.

Emergency Contraception

Counsel patients on access to emergency contraception if method failure occurs.

Allergies

Latex allergy requires non-latex alternatives. Spermicides may cause allergic reactions in some patients.

Contraindications & Precautions

Absolute

  • Latex allergy (for latex products)
  • History of toxic shock syndrome (diaphragms/caps)

Relative

  • Poor vaginal muscle tone (diaphragms/caps)
  • History of UTIs (diaphragms)
  • High STI risk (avoid spermicides)
