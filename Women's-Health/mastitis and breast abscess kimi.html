Mastitis & Breast Abscess

Quick Reference Guide for Clinical Management

Emergency Signs Treatment Drug Doses Follow-up

RED FLAGS - Immediate Action Required

Sepsis Signs: Fever >38.5°C, tachycardia, hypotension, confusion
Rapid Progression: Symptoms worsening within 24 hours despite treatment
Immunocompromised: Diabetes, HIV, immunosuppressive therapy
Abscess >5cm: Large or multiloculated abscess

Immediate Actions

Milk Removal: Continue breastfeeding/pumping 8-12x daily
Supportive Care: Warm compresses, adequate fluids, rest
Analgesia: Paracetamol 1g Q6H or Ibuprofen 400mg Q6H
Culture: Milk or abscess aspirate before antibiotics

Antibiotic Therapy

First Line (MSSA):
Flucloxacillin 500mg QID × 10-14 days
OR Cloxacillin 500mg QID × 10-14 days
MRSA Coverage:
Clindamycin 300-450mg QID
OR Trimethoprim/Sulfamethoxazole DS BID
⚠️ Contraindicated if breastfeeding jaundiced infant <30 days
Severe/IV:
Vancomycin 15mg/kg IV Q12H
OR Clindamycin 600mg IV Q8H

Abscess Management

Size Management Follow-up
<5cm Needle aspiration ± USG Daily aspiration 5-7 days
>5cm Incision & drainage USG at 14 days
Multiloculated Surgical drainage Consider fistula excision

Diagnostic Criteria - Milk Leukocyte Counts

Milk Stasis
Leukocytes <10⁶/mL
Bacteria <10³/mL
Non-infectious Mastitis
Leukocytes >10⁶/mL
Bacteria <10³/mL
Infectious Mastitis
Leukocytes >10⁶/mL
Bacteria >10³/mL

Follow-up & Monitoring

Response Time: Should improve within 48 hours
No Response: Re-culture, consider resistance/abscess
Recurrent >2-3x: Investigate underlying lesion
Breastfeeding: Continue unless contraindicated

Complications

Complication Risk Prevention
Breastfeeding cessation Medium Support, early treatment
Abscess formation Low Prompt antibiotic therapy
Sepsis Low Early recognition, IV antibiotics
Recurrence Variable Smoking cessation, correct technique
