Definition & Classification

AUB = Any variance from normal menstruation in regularity, frequency, volume, or duration

ACUTE Episode requiring immediate intervention
CHRONIC Present >6 months
IMB Inter-menstrual bleeding

Red Flags - Urgent Action Required

  • ❌ Hemodynamic instability (tachycardia, hypotension)
  • ❌ Severe anemia (Hb <8 g/dL)
  • ❌ Age >45 with persistent bleeding
  • ❌ Failure of medical management
  • ❌ Postmenopausal bleeding
  • ❌ Risk factors: obesity, PCOS, diabetes, hypertension
  • ❌ Family history of endometrial/colon cancer

Emergency Management Protocol

1 Stabilize Patient
  • IV access + fluids
  • Blood products if Hb <8 or symptomatic
  • Monitor vital signs
2 Hormone Therapy (First Line)
  • Conjugated Estrogens: 25mg IV q6-12h
  • OR Combined Oral Contraceptives: Per product
  • OR Medroxyprogesterone: 20mg PO TID x7 days
3 Adjunct Therapy
  • Tranexamic Acid: 1.3g PO TID during menses (max 5 days)
  • Or 10mg/kg IV q8h during menses
  • Manage underlying cause
4 Surgical Options (If medical fails)
  • D&C (with hysteroscopy)
  • Uterine artery embolization
  • Endometrial ablation
  • Hysterectomy (definitive)

Chronic AUB - First Line Treatment

★ 1st Line: Levonorgestrel IUS Best evidence for HMB reduction
↓
★ 2nd Line: Combined OCP If progestogen alone inadequate
↓
★ Alternative: NSAIDs Good for dysmenorrhea + AUB
↓
★ Alternative: Tranexamic Acid Reduces blood loss ~50%

Contraindications to Estrogen:

  • History of thromboembolism
  • Smoking >35 years
  • Uncontrolled hypertension
