Special Considerations for Drospirenone

Contraindicated in severe renal insufficiency, acute renal failure. Avoid in hyperkalaemia, untreated hypoaldosteronism, potassium-sparing diuretics.

Starting Method

Days 1-5 of menstrual cycle: Start immediately, no additional precautions needed.
After day 5: Start if certain not pregnant, use barrier contraception for:
  • POP: 7 days for drospirenone, 2 days for others
  • Implant/Injectables: 7 days

Special Considerations

Drospirenone POP

  • Contraindications: Severe renal insufficiency, acute renal failure
  • Avoid in: Hyperkalaemia, untreated hypoaldosteronism, potassium-sparing diuretics, potassium supplements
  • Monitor: Urea/electrolytes and BP in mild/moderate renal insufficiency
  • Consider monitoring: Before prescribing in patients with risk factors for chronic kidney disease, especially >50 years

Liver Enzyme-Inducing Drugs

  • Reduced efficacy: POP and implant (additional contraception needed)
  • No effect: Injectable efficacy
  • Examples: Rifampicin, certain antiepileptics, St. John's Wort

Common Problems Management

Problem Management
Unscheduled Bleeding
  • Reassurance (common in first 3 months)
  • Exclude pregnancy/pathology if persistent
  • Consider mefenamic acid or tranexamic acid
Missed POP
  • Traditional POPs: If >3 hours late, take ASAP, use barrier for 2 days
  • Desogestrel/Drospirenone: If >12 hours late, take ASAP, use barrier for 2 days
Weight Considerations
  • POP/Implant: Efficacy not reduced by higher BMI
  • Injectables: Can use with BMI >35, but may cause weight gain

Follow-up Schedule

POP

First visit at 3 months, then annually

Implant

Check after insertion if problems; replace after 3 years

Injectables

Review every 3 months (DMPA) or 8 weeks (NET-EN); consider bone health with long-term use

Key Advantages & Disadvantages

Method Advantages Disadvantages
POP
  • Can use while breastfeeding
  • No fertility delay
  • Suitable for COC contraindications
  • Daily dosing
  • Unscheduled bleeding
  • Reduced efficacy with enzyme-inducers
Implant
  • Long-acting (3 years)
  • Immediate fertility return
  • No user action required
  • Insertion/removal procedure
  • Unscheduled bleeding
  • Reduced efficacy with enzyme-inducers
Injectables
  • Long-acting
  • Not affected by enzyme-inducers
  • May reduce dysmenorrhoea
  • Delayed fertility return
  • Menstrual disturbances
  • Possible weight gain/bone density loss
