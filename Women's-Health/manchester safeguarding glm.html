Decision Support & Thresholds

Significant Harm

Action: Refer to Children's Social Care (Section 47 Enquiry).

  • Suspected abuse/neglect likely to cause significant harm.
  • Clear disclosure of serious maltreatment.
  • Urgent medical attention needed for non-accidental injury.
Doc: Right Intervention – Right Time (Level 4)

Child in Need / Early Help

Action: Consider Early Help Assessment or Section 17 Referral.

  • Complex needs not meeting significant harm threshold.
  • Disability impacting development.
  • Parental capacity issues requiring support.
Doc: Right Intervention – Right Time (Levels 1-3)

Referral Checklist (Children)

Demographics:
Full name, DOB, Address, NHS No.
Family:
Parents/Carers, PR, household composition.
Concern:
Factual description of event/disclosure. State "Possible significant harm" clearly.

Section 42 Enquiry Criteria (Care Act)

All 3 criteria MUST be met for statutory safeguarding enquiry:

1. Needs
Has care and support needs (inc. physical/mental health, disability, frailty).
2. Abuse
Experiencing or at risk of abuse/neglect.
3. Inability
Unable to protect themselves because of those needs.

Referral Checklist (Adults)

Capacity:
Mental capacity regarding the decision. Consent status?
Risk:
Nature of abuse, perpetrator details, injuries/evidence.
Wishes:
What does the adult want? Why is referral in best interest?

What Happens Next?

Children Pathway

1. MASH Screening Information shared across agencies (Police/Health/Edu). Decision within 1 working day.
2. Strategy Discussion If Section 47 required. Police & Social Care plan investigation immediately.
3. CP Conference Within 15 days. Multi-agency meeting. If risk substantiated -> Child Protection Plan.
4. GP Role Attend conference or send report. Contribute to medical oversight.

Adults Pathway

1. Contact Centre Initial screen & Risk Rating (Red/Amber/Green).
2. MASH Enquiry Information gathering (Multi-agency "Huddle" for high risk).
3. Section 42 Enquiry Social worker led investigation. "Making Safeguarding Personal" approach.
4. Safeguarding Plan If abuse substantiated. Ongoing risk management with GP input.
