GMC Guidance on Conscientious Objection

Doctors with conscientious objections must:

  • Inform patients in advance without judgment
  • Ensure access to alternative providers without delay
  • Not obstruct patient access or leave patients with nowhere to turn

Abortion Act 1967 Requirements

  • Two medical practitioners must certify in good faith (Form HSA1)
  • Gestational limit: Up to 24 weeks for most cases
  • No upper limit for: risk to life, grave permanent injury, or serious fetal handicap
  • Must be conducted in NHS hospital for post-24 week cases

  • Confirm pregnancy and gestational age (LMP, ultrasound if needed)
  • Confirm voluntary request (screen for coercion)
  • Assess medical history and social circumstances
  • Screen for sexual/domestic abuse concerns
  • Discuss options: medical vs surgical TOP
Conscientious Objection: If objecting, ensure prompt referral to alternative provider without judgment.

üëß Under 16s Assessment Special Consideration

Can proceed without parental consent if:

  • Has capacity to understand advice and implications
  • Cannot be persuaded to involve parents
  • Health likely to suffer without treatment
  • In best interests to proceed without parental knowledge
Key Point: Young person's consent overrides parental refusal. Consider sexual abuse assessment if lack of capacity.

üíä Method Selection

Medical TOP

Gestation: Up to 24 weeks

Success Rate: 98-99%

Advantages: Non-invasive, home option available

Common: 86% of all TOPs

Surgical TOP

Gestation: Up to 24 weeks

Success Rate: 99.9%

Advantages: Quick, one-day procedure

Anesthesia: Local or conscious sedation preferred

üíâ Medical TOP Protocol

  • First Trimester: Mifepristone + misoprostol (most effective)
  • Second Trimester: Multiple misoprostol doses
  • Home Use: Available up to 9-10 weeks (teleconsultation)
  • Completion: 7% need further intervention <14 weeks, 13% >14 weeks

üî™ Surgical TOP Options

  • Manual Vacuum Aspiration: ‚â§12 weeks
  • Electric Vacuum Aspiration: ‚â§14 weeks
  • Dilatation & Evacuation: ‚â•15 weeks
  • Completion: 3.5% need further intervention <14 weeks, 3% >14 weeks

Anesthesia Recommendations

Local anesthesia or conscious sedation preferred. General anesthesia associated with higher complication rates and longer admissions.

‚ö†Ô∏è Complications Monitor

Major Complications: Rare but serious. Early recognition is critical.
  • Continuing Pregnancy: 1-2% medical, 0.1% surgical Requires Repeat
  • Unplanned Intervention: 7% medical (<14wks), 13% medical (>14wks)
  • Infection: <1% (medical or surgical)
  • Cervical Trauma: <1% (surgical only)
  • Uterine Perforation: 0.1-0.4% (surgical only)
  • Uterine Rupture: <0.1% (second trimester medical)
  • Bleeding Requiring Transfusion: <0.1% (<20wks), 0.4% (>20wks)

üß† Psychological Support

  • Early Distress: Common, usually continuation of pre-TOP symptoms
  • Long-term Sequelae: Only small proportion affected
  • No Evidence: Link between TOP and subsequent mental health problems
  • Counselling: Arrange for those experiencing long-term distress
Important: Pre-existing mental health issues are the strongest predictor of post-TOP psychological outcomes.
