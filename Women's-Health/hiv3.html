HIV Quick-Reference Algorithm

Last updated: March 2025 – NICE CKS

1. When to Test

  • Symptomatic: unexplained fever, weight-loss >10 kg, lymphadenopathy, oral candida, shingles, seborrhoeic dermatitis.
  • AIDS-defining illnesses: PCP, TB, KS, lymphoma, CMV retinitis.
  • Asymptomatic: risk factors (MSM, trans women, partner HIV+, high-prevalence origin, IDU, sex work, STI, indicator conditions*).
  • Screening: annual if high-prevalence area (>2/1000) or pregnancy.
*Indicator conditions: any STI, lymphoma, cervical dysplasia, shingles, hepatitis B/C, unexplained cytopenias, weight-loss, oral candida, etc.

2. Test & Result

  • Test: 4th-generation blood test (window 4–6 wks).
  • Pre-test: consent, confidentiality, window period, risk-reduction.
  • Negative: repeat at 12 wks if recent exposure; give PrEP/PEP advice.
  • Positive: urgent (<48 h) referral to HIV clinic; provide support leaflets (THT, NAM).

3. Red-Flag Admission / Referral

  • Admit suspected PCP, TB, CMV retinitis, cryptococcal meningitis, KS lung/gut, severe ART adverse effects.
  • Refer same day oesophageal candida, persistent diarrhoea, KS lesions, cervical dysplasia, neuro-cognitive decline.

4. Established HIV – Primary Care

  • Monitoring: CD4 & viral load in specialist clinic.
  • ART adherence: missed doses → resistance; check interactions (hiv-druginteractions.org).
  • CV prevention: offer statin ≥40 yrs (avoid simvastatin).
  • Vaccinations: inactivated OK; avoid live vaccines.
  • Cervical screening: colposcopy at diagnosis, annual cytology.
  • Mental health: screen depression/anxiety; watch drug interactions.
  • Contraception: Cu-IUD or DMPA unaffected; check ART interactions with OCP.
  • Travel: check entry restrictions, malaria prophylaxis interactions.

5. Exposure Prophylaxis

  • Occupational / Needlestick: wash wound, report → A&E for PEP within 72 h.
  • Sexual exposure (PEPSE): direct to GUM/A&E within 72 h; omit if source on ART with undetectable VL >6 m.
  • Community needlestick / bite: PEP generally not required; seek specialist advice if high risk.

6. End-Stage / Palliative

  • Early advance-care planning; involve HIV specialist + palliative MDT.
  • Death certificate – state HIV if related; maintain confidentiality post-mortem.
