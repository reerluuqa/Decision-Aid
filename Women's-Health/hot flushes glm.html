Hot Flushes | Clinical Reference

Last Updated: Jan 2025 • Est. reading: 2 mins

Prevalence

~80%

of menopausal women

Duration

>7 yrs

in >50% of cases

1st Line

HRT

Discuss benefits/risks

⚠ Differential Diagnosis: Do Not Miss

High Priority

Consider these pathologies if presentation is atypical or severe. Hot flushes are vasomotor instability usually linked to menopause, but exclusion is key.

Condition Key Clue/Association Action
Hyperthyroidism Weight loss, tachycardia, tremor Check TSH/T4
Carcinoid Tumour Diarrhoea, valvular heart disease 5-HIAA urine
Phaeochromocytoma Paroxysmal hypertension, headaches 24h Urine Metanephrines
Panic Disorder Psychiatric history, anxiety Psychiatric eval
Medications SSRIs, Nitrates, Tamoxifen, Raloxifene Review Med List

Typical Presentation

Sensation: Intense heat, face/body flushing.
Duration: Seconds to 10 mins (Avg: 4 mins).
Area: Face, head, neck, chest.
Associated: Night sweats, poor sleep, poor memory.
Men (Prostate Cancer Tx): Anti-androgen therapy can cause identical symptoms due to testosterone suppression.

Investigations

Labs are not required for women >45 with typical symptoms.

When to check FSH:
  • Suspected premature ovarian insufficiency (POI).
  • If symptoms are atypical.

Treatment & Management

1
HRT (Hormone Replacement Therapy)

Most effective treatment. Offer after discussing risks/benefits.

2
Cognitive Behavioural Therapy (CBT)

Menopause-specific CBT shown effective for some women.

NICE: Do not routinely offer SSRIs/SNRIs or clonidine as 1st line for vasomotor symptoms alone.

  • SSRIs / Venlafaxine: Effective but often short-acting. Unlicensed use.
  • Clonidine: Widens thermoneutral zone. Trial 2-4 weeks. Watch for dry mouth/tiredness.
  • Gabapentin: Anticonvulsant. Unlicensed.
⚠ HERBAL/COMPLEMENTARY WARNING

Avoid in oestrogen-dependent cancer (e.g., breast cancer). No safety data for VTE risk. Products lack standardization. Look for MHRA logo if patient insists.

Recommended Actions
  • Regular exercise (Low certainty evidence, but beneficial).
  • Weight loss if overweight/obese.
  • Wear lighter clothing; cooler bedroom.
Identify & Avoid Triggers
  • Spicy foods
  • Caffeine
  • Alcohol
  • Smoking
  • Stress

Risk Factors & Prognosis

Higher Risk
  • Low body weight
  • Smoking
  • Surgical/Radiation menopause
  • African-American/Hispanic ethnicity
Lower Risk
  • Japanese/Chinese ethnicity
  • Onset after menstruation cessation
Outlook

Symptoms last 5-7 years typically. Persistent symptoms linked to smoking, weight gain, and younger age at onset.
