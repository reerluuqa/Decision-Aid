Cervical Cancer Screening Clinical Protocol

Based on BMJ Best Practice (Last Updated Mar 2025)

Immediate Referral Needed

  • Visible Lesions: Suspected carcinoma on speculum exam (refer to Gyn-Onc).
  • Adenocarcinoma in Situ (AIS) on cytology. Do not treat without biopsy.
  • Post-Coital Bleeding: Visible mass, needs urgent biopsy.

High-Risk Findings

Requires colposcopic evaluation, not routine screening interval.

  • ASC-H (Cannot exclude HSIL)
  • HSIL on cytology
  • Atypical Glandular Cells (AGC)
  • HPV 16/18 Positive (with normal cytology in some protocols)

Key Pathology Insight

Cervical sampling targets the transformation zone (junction of ectocervix and endocervix).

Liquid-based cytology (LBC) has largely replaced conventional smears due to lower unsatisfactory specimen rates (2.6% vs 4.1%) and ability to run ancillary tests (HPV, Chlamydia, Gonorrhoea).

High-Risk HPV Subtypes

HPV-16 HPV-18 HPV-31 HPV-33 HPV-45 HPV-52 HPV-58

HPV-16/18 account for ~70% of cervical cancers.

Stop Screening Criteria

  • Age 65+ with adequate prior screening (3 neg cytology or 2 neg co-test within 10 yrs, most recent <5 yrs).
  • Total hysterectomy (cervix removed) NO history of CIN2+.
  • Limited life expectancy.

Unsatisfactory Test

Repeat in 2-4 months. Do not wait 3 years.

ASC-US

Atypical Squamous Cells of Undetermined Significance

Action: Reflex HPV

If HPV+, Colposcopy. If HPV-, Repeat 3 yrs.

ASC-H

Cannot exclude HSIL

Action: Colposcopy

Immediate referral regardless of HPV status.

LSIL

~90% regress spontaneously. Reflex HPV recommended for triage.

HPV Testing Strategies

1

Primary hrHPV Testing

Co-testing

Cytology and hrHPV done simultaneously. Recommended for average-risk women 30+.

Risk Stratification Note

Women who are Cytology-negative but HPV-16 or HPV-18 positive have an elevated 10-year risk of CIN 3+ (21%). Reflex testing strategies help identify these missed high-risk cases.

HPV Vaccination (9-Valent)

Women who have received the HPV vaccine should still follow population screening guidelines.
