HRT Bleeding Management

Unscheduled uterine bleeding is a common side-effect of HRT. Urgent gynaecological referrals increased by 43% between 2021 and 2024, but endometrial cancer diagnoses did not rise proportionally.

Goal: Stratify risk effectively to avoid missing cancer while reducing unnecessary anxiety and referrals.

Red Flags: Arrange Investigation

  • Persistent intermenstrual bleeding (>3 months)
  • Persistent irregular bleeding
  • Infrequent heavy bleeding (with obesity/PCOS)
  • Postcoital bleeding
  • Postmenopausal bleeding (>1y after last period)

üíä Management & HRT Optimisation

  • Address Modifiables: Weight loss (reduces bleeding and cancer risk).
  • IUS Option: Offer IUS as progestogen component, especially if high risk.
  • Dose Adjustment: Reduce oestrogen dose or switch to a different progestogen.
  • Route: Transdermal generally has less bleeding risk than oral (consider switch).
  • Additional Progestogen: Add oral progestogen (e.g., 100mg micronised progesterone) for 3-month trial.
  • Atrophy: If exam suggests atrophy, offer vaginal oestrogen.

If Age < 50

Consider switching to Combined Hormonal Contraception (CHC)* if no contraindications.

*Not licensed ‚â•50y

If Age > 45

Swap to continuous combined regimen:

  • No later than 5 years after start of sequential HRT.
  • OR by age 54 (whichever comes first).

If on Micronised Progesterone:

  • Increase to 300mg/day sequentially OR increase days (e.g., to 14d).
  • Change to synthetic progestogen (Norethisterone/MPA).
  • Offer Desogestrel (75mcg add-on or 150mcg instead) - Off-license.
  • If recently postmenopausal: Switch to sequential regimen for 6 months (stabilizes endometrium).
  • If on Micronised Progesterone:
    • Recommend daily 100mg (licensed is 25d/cycle) - Off-license.
    • Consider increasing to 200mg/day - Off-license.
  • Off-license Note: Discuss fully. Review at 3 months. Counsel on risks if continued long-term.

Mirena is the only IUS licensed for endometrial protection with HRT (4 years).

Clinical Tip: Evidence shows all 52mg IUS brands provide protection for up to 5 years.

New-onset bleeding after 4 years of use:

  1. Exclude pathology (Exam + TVS, or refer if symptoms/risk factors).
  2. Change IUS for new device once pathology excluded.

Malpositioned IUS

If USS shows IUS >2cm from fundus or in cervical canal, BMS suggests replacing the device (may cause erratic suppression/BTB).
