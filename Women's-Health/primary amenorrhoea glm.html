Primary Amenorrhoea: Lack of menses by age 15 years in a patient with appropriate development of secondary sexual characteristics, OR absent menses by age 13 years and no other pubertal maturation.

Prevalence in US: < 0.1%

Urgent Considerations

Immediate assessment required

Pregnancy Assessment

Patients with secondary sexual development should be assessed for pregnancy immediately.

Action: Order Œ≤-hCG

Neuroimaging Required

Hyperprolactinaemia or hypogonadotrophic hypogonadism with neurological symptoms.

Action: MRI Brain

Rapid Virilisation

Suggests adrenal or ovarian androgen-producing tumours.

Action: Androgen workup, Imaging

Gonadal Dysgenesis (Y chromosome)

Risk of dysgerminoma or gonadoblastoma.

Action: Counsel re: gonadectomy

Urinary Retention Risk

Obstructive anomalies (e.g., haematometrocolpos) compressing urethra.

Action: Surgical drainage/repair
