📌 Definition

Osteoporosis = low bone mass + structural deterioration → fragility fractures.

Diagnosis: BMD T-score ≤ −2.5 at femoral neck (2.5 SD below healthy young woman mean).

Prevalence in women: 2% at age 50 → 25% at age 80.

⚡ Important Caveat

T-score is a surrogate marker. Most people who sustain a fragility fracture do NOT have an osteoporotic T-score. Use clinical risk assessment alongside DEXA.

🚨 Fragility Fractures – Impact

  • Commonly affect spine, hip, wrist
  • Hip fractures: hospitalisation in nearly all, 20% fatal, 50% permanent disability
  • UK cost: £5.4bn (2.4% healthcare spending, 2019)

✅ Key Takeaways

  • Postmenopausal women at ↑ risk due to age + oestrogen loss
  • Don't routinely assess fracture risk <65y unless risk factors present
  • Encourage healthy lifestyle for bone health
  • HRT can prevent/treat bone loss if benefits outweigh risks
  • HRT licensed for prophylaxis only, not treatment of osteoporosis

⚠️ Key Point

Some HRT preparations are licensed for osteoporosis prophylaxis, but none are licensed for treatment of established osteoporosis (NICE NG23, 2015).

✅ HRT: Mode of Action

HRT reduces bone turnover rate → antiresorptive effect. Good evidence for significant protection against osteoporosis and fragility fractures (hip + vertebral).

📊 Evidence Summary ▼
  • Cochrane review (CD004143, 2017): ↓ risk of hip, vertebral & any fracture with oestrogen-only AND combined HRT
  • NICE NG23: RCT + epidemiological evidence → significantly ↓ fragility fracture risk
  • Meta-analysis 28 RCTs (Menopause 2016): Total fracture RR 0.74 (CI 0.69–0.80)

📝 Duration Notes

Bone protection is dose & duration related. Benefit appears to decline after stopping, though some studies suggest longer-term persistence.

When to prescribe HRT for bone health

🎯 Bottom Line

Offer HRT to prevent or treat bone loss associated with menopause if benefits outweigh risks for the individual woman.

📋 Guideline-by-Guideline Indications ▼
Guideline HRT Indication for Bone Health
NICE NG23
2015
  • Management of premature ovarian insufficiency (menopause <40y)
  • To treat menopausal symptoms – inform of ↓ fracture risk as secondary benefit
SIGN 142
2021
  • HRT/tibolone may be considered for fracture prevention in younger postmenopausal women
  • Only after individual risk-benefit assessment
  • Lowest effective dose, shortest time
  • Presumably if bisphosphonate threshold not met
NOGG
2024
  • First-line in postmenopausal women ≤60y who are low risk for malignancy + VTE
  • Consider stopping at 60y → reassess
  • On stopping: reassess fracture risk → offer alternative if needed
BMS
2023
  • First-line for prevention AND treatment in POI and early menopause (40–45y)
  • Consider for prevention in menopausal/postmenopausal women (including >60y) who already benefit from HRT for symptoms

🔑 Remember

  • HRT licensed for prophylaxis, NOT treatment of established osteoporosis
  • When stopping HRT: reassess fracture risk and offer alternative Rx if appropriate
  • Bone-protective effect declines after discontinuation
