Breast Mass Assessment

Interactive Clinical Decision Support Algorithm

Initial Assessment

Patient History

  • Age and menstrual status
  • Family history of breast/ovarian cancer
  • Previous breast biopsies or surgeries
  • Hormone replacement therapy use
  • Duration and changes in mass
Key Information

Physical Examination

Mass Characteristics:
  • Size, location, and mobility
  • Skin changes (peau d'orange, dimpling)
  • Nipple changes (inversion, discharge)
  • Axillary lymphadenopathy
Complete Bilateral Exam Required

Age-Specific Considerations

< 30 years: Ultrasound first
≥ 30 years: Mammography + Ultrasound
Suspicious findings → Biopsy
Diagnostic Workup

Imaging Protocol

Mammography (≥30 years):
  • Standard views + spot compression
  • Digital breast tomosynthesis preferred
  • BI-RADS classification
BI-RADS 4-5: Immediate Biopsy
Ultrasound:
  • Characterize cystic vs solid
  • Simple cysts: Benign if classic features
  • Complex cysts: Consider biopsy

Biopsy Decision Tree

Suspicious Imaging
Core Needle Biopsy Preferred
FNA if Cystic
Excision if High Risk

Red Flags - Urgent Referral

  • BI-RADS 5 (≥95% malignant)
  • Skin involvement or ulceration
  • Fixed to chest wall
  • Inflammatory changes
Refer within 2 weeks
Treatment Planning

Benign Masses

Simple Cyst: Aspiration if symptomatic Routine follow-up
Fibroadenoma: Observation if <3cm and classic features Excision if growing or patient preference
High-Risk Lesions: ADH, ALH, LCIS → Surgical excision Complete excision required

Malignant Masses

Core Biopsy → Diagnosis
Staging Workup
Multidisciplinary Team
Treatment Plan
Options:
  • Breast conserving surgery + radiation
  • Mastectomy ± reconstruction
  • Systemic therapy (chemo, hormonal, targeted)
  • Neoadjuvant vs adjuvant approach
MDT Discussion Mandatory
Follow-up Protocol

Benign Masses

Simple Cyst: No follow-up if completely aspirated Discharge
Fibroadenoma: Clinical exam in 6 months, then annually Patient education on self-exam
High-Risk Lesions: Enhanced screening protocol MRI consideration

Post-Treatment Surveillance

Years 1-2: Every 3-6 months
Years 3-5: Every 6-12 months
Year 5+: Annually
Components:
  • History and physical examination
  • Annual mammography (contralateral breast)
  • Patient-reported symptoms
  • No routine metastatic workup without symptoms
Survivorship care planning

When to Contact

  • New masses or changes in treated breast
  • Persistent pain or skin changes
  • Systemic symptoms (bone pain, dyspnea)
  • Psychological distress or concerns
24/7 contact available
