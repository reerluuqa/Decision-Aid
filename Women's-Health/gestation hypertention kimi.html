Gestational Hypertension

Quick Reference & Protocols

Updated: Dec 06, 2024

SEVERE HYPERTENSION EMERGENCY

BP ≥160/110 mmHg (persistent >15 mins)

ADMIT IMMEDIATELY. Treat with Labetalol IV, Nifedipine Oral, or Hydralazine IV.

Definition

  • BP ≥140/90 mmHg on two occasions.
  • Readings at least 4 hours apart.
  • Onset after 20 weeks' gestation.
  • Previously normotensive woman.
  • Excludes proteinuria & pre-eclampsia features.

Key Diagnostics

Perform immediately upon elevated BP:

  • Urinalysis: Check for protein (Pre-eclampsia screen).
  • Bloods: FBC, LFTs, Urea/Creatinine, Uric acid.
  • Fetal: Ultrasound (growth/fluid) + Umbilical Doppler.
  • PlGF testing if available to rule out pre-eclampsia.

Risk Factors & Flags

Nulliparous Age >35 Black/Hispanic Obesity Type 1 Diabetes Previous HDP

Management Protocol

Outpatient Management

Usually manageable on an outpatient basis with close monitoring.

  • Lifestyle modifications (Dietitian consult).
  • Avoid salt restriction solely for GH prevention.
  • Monitor BP & Urine frequently.
  • Target BP: ≤135/85 mmHg.

Pharmacological Treatment

If lifestyle fails and BP remains >140/90:

  • 1st Line: Labetalol (Oral).
  • 2nd Line: Nifedipine (Extended-release).
  • Alternative: Methyldopa (Avoid postpartum).
  • Avoid in asthma.

< 37 Weeks' Gestation

Do NOT offer planned early delivery unless:

  • Preterm labour / PPROM.
  • Vaginal bleeding.
  • Abnormal fetal testing / IUGR.
  • Development of pre-eclampsia.
  • End-organ damage.
Note: If delivery <34 wks is necessary, offer Antenatal Corticosteroids + MgSO4.

≥ 37 Weeks' Gestation

INDUCE WITHIN 24-48 HOURS

After maternal stabilization.

  • Expectant management is generally not recommended.
  • Agree timing between mother & senior obstetrician.
  • Caesarean if induction fails.

Immediate Actions

  • ADMIT to Hospital.
  • Measure BP every 15-30 mins until <160/110.
  • Target BP: ≤135/85.
  • Consider MgSO4 if seizure risk (pre-eclampsia features).

First-Line Acute Treatment

Choose one of the following:

  • Labetalol IV: 10-20mg, then 20-80mg q10-30min (Max 300mg).
  • Nifedipine Oral: 10-20mg, repeat in 20min if needed.
  • Hydralazine IV: 5-10mg q20-40min (Max 20mg).

Note: Hydralazine can cause acute BP fall; use with plasma expansion.

Medication Reference

Drug Dose (Oral) Dose (IV/Severe) Notes
Labetalol 100mg BD (Max 2400mg) 10-20mg bolus, then 20-80mg q10-30min 1st Choice Avoid in asthma.
Nifedipine 30-60mg ER daily (Max 120mg) 10-20mg IR, repeat 20min Effective alternative.
Hydralazine - 5-10mg IV q20-40min (Max 20mg) Watch for hypotension.
Methyldopa 250mg TDS (Max 3000mg) - Use if others unsuitable. Stop postpartum.

Patient Counseling

Instruct patient to report:

  • Severe headache
  • Visual changes
  • Epigastric/Upper abdominal pain
  • Decreased fetal movement
  • Sudden swelling

Postpartum Follow-up

  • Monitor BP and adjust meds.
  • If pre-delivery meds used: Reduce if BP <130/80.
  • If NO pre-delivery meds: Start if BP ≥150/100.
  • Medical Review: 6-8 weeks after birth.
