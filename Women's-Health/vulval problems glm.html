Immediate Referral Required (2-Week Wait)

Refer urgently if presenting with:

  • Unexplained vulval lump
  • Unexplained vulval ulceration
  • Unexplained vulval bleeding
Genital Herpes
Infection
  • Painful, fluid-filled vesicles
  • Tender ulcers (heals 2-4 weeks)
  • Dysuria
Management
Salt baths, analgesics, antiviral therapy.

Caused by HSV-1 or HSV-2. Confirm via viral culture. Advise abstinence until ulcers healed. Recurrent outbreaks usually milder.

Candidiasis (Thrush)
Infection
  • Pruritus and red rash
  • White, curdy discharge
  • Vulvitis & Vaginitis
Management
Topical antifungal creams. Treat vaginal infection if present.

Can occur at all ages. If suspected dermatitis co-exists, consider combination hydrocortisone + antifungal.

Genital Warts
STI
  • HPV types 6 & 11
  • Appear 1-8 months post-infection
  • Pain, bleeding, pruritus possible
Action
Refer to GUM clinic. Contact tracing required.

Treatments: Podophyllin paint (not in pregnancy), Imiquimod, Cryotherapy, Electrocautery.

Lichen Sclerosus
Pre-Malignant
  • 'Cigarette paper' skin (thin, white, crinkly)
  • Pruritus vulvae, vulvodynia
  • Fusion of labia minora
Management
Potent topical corticosteroids. Risk of invasive disease ~4%.

Mostly postmenopausal. Associated with autoimmune thyroid disorders. Follow up with Gynaecologist.

Erosive Lichen Planus
Dermatological
  • Intense erythema, oedema, superficial ulceration
  • Women > 45 years
  • Scarring & introital narrowing
Management
Biopsy required to distinguish. Systemic steroids often needed.

Examine mouth (may be involved). Distinguish from pemphigus, pemphigoid, erythema multiforme.

Vulval Cancer
Malignancy
  • 85% are squamous cell
  • Lump, ulceration, bleeding
  • Pruritus or pain
CRITICAL ACTION
2-week wait referral for unexplained lump/ulcer/bleeding.

Consider Paget's disease (eczematous/velvet-like surface, often weeping/bleeding) and Melanoma (blue-black, jagged border).

Vulvovaginitis (Child)
Pediatric
  • Redness, soreness, itching
  • Dysuria, discharge
  • NOT usually thrush
Management
Show Details

Common in pre-pubescent girls due to hypo-oestrogenisation. Threadworms frequently cause vulval itching in children.

Nappy Rash
Pediatric
  • Visible erythematous lesions
  • Skin contact with wetness/faeces
  • Patches away from main area = Candida
Management
Show Details

Irritant contact dermatitis. Note child's general care. Use barrier prep at every change.

Sexual Abuse
Safeguarding
  • STIs in children (e.g. Warts, HSV)
  • Trauma with spurious explanation
  • Behavioural disorders
Action
Consider safeguarding. See "Safeguarding children" article.

Can occur at any age including elderly. If child presents with usually sexually transmitted condition, consider sexual activity but it is not the only cause.

Swelling & Ulcers
General
  • Swelling: Venous/Lymphatic obstruction, pregnancy, haematoma.
  • Ulcers: Exclude STI, Malignancy, BehÃ§et's, SLE.
Investigations
Swabs, Biopsy + Colposcopy for persistent ulcers.

Ulcer causes: HSV, Chancroid, Granuloma inguinale, Lymphogranuloma venereum, Syphilis.

VIN (Intraepithelial Neoplasia)
Pre-Malignant
  • Pre-malignant condition
  • Pruritus is common
  • Red, white or raised areas
Management
Can arise in existing disorders or independently. Biopsy required.

Treatment usually local excision. Imiquimod can be effective alternative/adjunct.

Genitourinary Menopause
Hormonal
  • Lack of oestrogen
  • Pruritus, irritation, recurrent UTIs
  • Dyspareunia, soreness
Management
Exclude ulceration/mass. Consider hormonal treatments.
