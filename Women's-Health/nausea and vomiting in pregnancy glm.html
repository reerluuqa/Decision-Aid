Definition

NVP (Morning Sickness): Nausea/vomiting between 4th & 8th week, resolves in 2nd trimester.

Hyperemesis Gravidarum: Persistent vomiting + Volume Depletion + Ketosis + >5% Weight Loss.

Key Risk Factors
  • Multiple gestation / Hydrops / Trophoblastic disease
  • Previous NVP or family history
  • Female fetus
Physical Exam Checks
Vitals: Orthostatic HR/BP changes.
Abdomen: Check for tenderness (not typical for NVP).
Neuro: Papilledema, nystagmus (exclude CNS causes).
Neck: Thyroid enlargement (check if TSH abnormal).
Management Algorithm
Based on Severity

1. Conservative (No Volume Depletion)

First-line for mild NVP.

  • Diet: Small, frequent meals. Bland, low fat, high carb. Salty foods AM.
  • Liquids: Tart/lemonade better than water.
  • Supplements: Stop iron; swap for Folic Acid only.
  • Ginger: 250mg orally 4x daily (Safe).
  • Acupressure: P6 (Neiguan) point wristbands.

2. Failed Conservative? Add Pharmacology

If diet & ginger fail.

First Line: Pyridoxine (B6) +/- Doxylamine

B6: 10-25mg PO TDS. Doxylamine: 12.5mg PO q6-8h prn.

Second Line: Antihistamines / Anti-emetics
  • Antihistamines: Meclizine, Dimenhydrinate, Diphenhydramine.
  • Phenothiazines: Promethazine, Prochlorperazine.
  • Dopamine Antagonist: Metoclopramide (Max 5 days only).

3. Hyperemesis / Volume Depletion

Hospitalization usually required.

  • IV Fluids: Ringer's Lactate or Saline. Correct electrolytes.
  • Thiamine (B1): Give BEFORE glucose to prevent Wernicke's.
  • Second Line IV: Metoclopramide (5 days max) OR Ondansetron.
  • ⚠ Ondansetron Warning:
  • Small risk of cleft palate in 1st trimester. Reserve for severe cases.
  • Corticosteroids: Methylprednisolone (Consider after 1st trimester only).
  • Nutrition: Enteral (NG) or TPN if extreme.
