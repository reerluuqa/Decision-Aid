All aspects of abortion care should be delivered in a respectful, empathetic, and sensitive manner that recognizes the pregnant person as the decision maker.

  • Confirm pregnancy and pregnancy duration
  • Detailed health assessment
  • Test for sexually transmitted infections if indicated
  • Assess contraception needs and preferences
  • Ensure understanding of confidentiality and consent
  • Provide aftercare information

Check for Coercion
  • Ensure decision is voluntary
  • Screen for sexual and domestic abuse (physical and emotional)

Age & Capacity Considerations
  • Under-16s: Can consent if competent (Fraser guidelines)
  • 13-15 years: Safeguarding referral may be needed
  • Under 13: Mandatory safeguarding response
  • Lacks capacity: Decision in best interests

Pregnant person can:

  • Self-refer directly to abortion care provider
  • Be referred by GP, sexual health clinic, or specialist clinic (Brook)

Diagnosis

Determine gestational age and appropriate method

‚ñº

üìÖ Gestational Age Determination

Accurate dating is critical for method selection and legal compliance.

  • Last menstrual period (LMP)
  • Early pregnancy ultrasound if uncertain
  • Legal limit: 24 weeks in England, Scotland, Wales
  • After 24 weeks: Only in specific circumstances (risk to life, severe fetal abnormality)
Important: Legal Limit

‚öñÔ∏è Legal Framework (UK)

Abortion Act 1967 Criteria

Two registered medical practitioners must certify in good faith that:

  • A: Pregnancy ‚â§24 weeks and continuation poses greater risk to physical/mental health than termination
  • B: Termination necessary to prevent grave permanent injury
  • C: Continuation poses risk to life greater than termination
  • D: Substantial risk of serious handicap if child born
Legal Requirement

ü§î Patient Preferences & Circumstances

Discuss and document:

  • Personal preferences for medical vs surgical method
  • Home vs clinic setting availability
  • Support person involvement
  • Contraceptive plans post-procedure
  • Any cultural, religious, or personal considerations

‚ùå Conscientious Objection

‚ö†Ô∏è Professional Obligation

üíä Medical Abortion

Regimen

Mifepristone 200mg orally, followed 24-48 hours later by misoprostol (buccal, sublingual, or vaginal)

  • Most common method (87% of abortions)
  • Up to 9+6 weeks: Can self-administer at home (England/Wales)
  • After 9+6 weeks: Usually in healthcare facility
  • Process mimics miscarriage with cramping and bleeding
Preferred ‚â§10 weeks

üî™ Surgical Abortion

  • Vacuum aspiration:
    • Manual (MVA) or electric (EVA)
    • Local anaesthesia ¬± sedation
    • Up to 14-16 weeks
  • Dilatation & Evacuation (D&E):
    • Cervical dilation required
    • General/deep sedation anaesthesia
    • Second trimester procedures

üíâ Contraception Provision

All methods safe and effective after abortion:

  • After medical: All methods except IUD until pregnancy expelled
  • After surgical: All methods including IUD immediately
  • Offer before discharge or as soon as possible after
  • Provide chosen method at time of abortion
Immediate Provision

‚ö†Ô∏è Important Principles

  • NO compulsory counselling or reflection period required
  • NO mandatory waiting period
  • Counselling is optional and available if requested
  • Partner has NO legal right to demand/refuse termination
  • Confidentiality must be maintained unless exceptional circumstances

üíö Emotional Support

The most common emotion after abortion is relief. However, it's normal to experience a range of emotions.

  • Provide information on counselling services
  • Offer follow-up emotional support
  • Screen for pre-existing mental health conditions
  • Abortion is NOT associated with increased risk of mental health issues
