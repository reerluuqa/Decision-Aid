Hand, Foot, and Mouth Disease

Clinical Reference Tool (CKS May 2025)
Last Revised: May 2025
Target: Primary Care
RED FLAGS: Arrange Urgent Admission

Signs of CNS Involvement

  • Persistent or severe headache or fever
  • Myoclonus with sleep disturbances
  • Confusion, weakness, lethargy, drowsiness
  • Generalized seizures, coma (Encephalitis)
  • Stiff neck or back pain

Signs of Complications

  • Dehydration: Reduced urine output, lethargy, cold peripheries, reduced skin turgor.
  • Severe respiratory distress (rare, mainly EV71).

Diagnosis (Typical Presentation)

Prodrome (12-36 hrs)

  • Low-grade fever (38–39°C)
  • Malaise, loss of appetite, myalgia
  • Sore throat, cough, abdominal pain

Oral Enanthem

  • Starts as erythematous macules (2–8mm).
  • Progress to vesicles → shallow yellow-grey ulcers surrounded by red halo.
  • Location: Hard palate, tongue, buccal mucosa, lips.

Skin Exanthem

  • Follows oral lesions by 1-2 days.
  • Hands & Feet: Sparse erythematous macules/papules with central greyish vesicle.
  • Shape: Often elliptical, parallel to skin lines.
  • Sides of fingers, dorsum of hands, heels are common.
  • Buttocks/Groin: Macular or papular (rarely vesicles).
Note: Coxsackie A6 (Atypical)
May cause larger bullae, severe fever, extensive rash (eczema areas), nail shedding, or perioral rash.

Management Plan

✓ Reassurance & Advice

Usually mild, self-limiting (7–10 days).

Patient Info: NHS or CDC leaflets.

✓ Hydration & Diet

  • Ensure adequate fluid intake.
  • Use soft diet if mouth ulcers painful.
  • Avoid hot, spicy, salty, or acidic foods.

✓ Analgesia

  • Paracetamol or Ibuprofen for fever/pain.

✓ Symptomatic Relief (Oral)

  • Consider topical oromucosal antiseptics.
  • Consider corticosteroid lozenges or anaesthetic gels.

✗ Do NOT Prescribe

  • Antibiotics (unless secondary bacterial infection suspected).
  • Antivirals.

✗ Blister Care

Let blisters dry naturally. Do not pierce them (fluid is infectious).

Prevention & Exclusion

Hygiene Measures

  • Wash hands thoroughly (toilet, eating).
  • Cover cough/sneeze (dispose tissues).
  • Don't share cups/utensils/towels.
  • Wash soiled clothes/bedding on hot cycle.

Exclusion Rules

  • No exclusion from nursery/school if well enough.
  • Do not isolate household contacts.
  • Notification not required (UK).
  • Pregnant women: avoid close contact.

Differential Diagnosis

Condition Key Differentiator
Herpangina High fever, oral lesions, no rash.
Chickenpox Very itchy, mainly trunk/extremities, different stages of spots.
Herpes Simplex Gingivostomatitis (painful gums), no hand/foot rash.
Erythema Multiforme Target lesions, may involve mucous membranes severely.
Kawasaki Disease Fever >5 days, strawberry tongue, peeling, cervical lymphadenopathy.
