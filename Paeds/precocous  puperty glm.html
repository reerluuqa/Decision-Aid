Precocious Puberty - Quick Reference

Last updated: May 23, 2023
URGENT: Consider precocious puberty when secondary sexual characteristics appear before 8 years in girls and 9 years in boys. Early diagnosis and treatment are critical to prevent compromised adult height and psychosocial issues.

Definition & Thresholds

  • Girls: Secondary sexual characteristics before 8 years
  • Boys: Secondary sexual characteristics before 9 years
  • Key signs: Breast development in girls, testicular enlargement in boys
  • Consequences: Accelerated bone development, reduced adult height, psychosocial impact

Epidemiology

  • Prevalence: 1 in 500-5000 children
  • CPP affects girls 5-10× more than boys
  • CPP prevalence: 0.2% in girls, 0.05% in boys
  • Earlier age of presentation = higher likelihood of organic cause
  • Factors affecting onset: familial pattern, nutrition, ethnicity, chronic disease

Types of Precocious Puberty

Central (CPP)

Premature activation of hypothalamo-pituitary-gonadal axis

  • Consonant pattern (follows normal puberty sequence)
  • Most common in girls (97% idiopathic in ages 6-8)
  • Less common in boys (60% idiopathic)
  • Causes: Idiopathic, CNS tumors, cranial radiation, genetic mutations

Gonadotrophin-Independent (GIPP)

Autonomous secretion of sex steroids independent of GnRH

  • Disconsonant pattern (doesn't follow normal puberty sequence)
  • Causes: Ovarian/testicular tumors, CAH, McCune-Albright syndrome
  • Exogenous hormone exposure
  • Can accelerate onset of CPP with prolonged exposure

Red Flags & Urgent Findings

Critical Signs

  • Rapid progression of pubertal signs
  • Neurological symptoms (headaches, visual changes)
  • Disconsonant puberty pattern
  • Very early onset (<6 years in girls, <8 years in boys)
  • Severe virilization in girls

High-Risk Conditions

  • Brain tumors (optic gliomas, craniopharyngiomas, hamartomas)
  • McCune-Albright syndrome
  • Congenital adrenal hyperplasia
  • Gonadal tumors
  • History of cranial radiation

Initial Assessment Approach

History

  • Document onset and progression of secondary sexual characteristics
  • Record growth velocity and clothing size changes
  • Obtain parental heights and puberty timing
  • Ask about history of CNS disease, trauma, radiation
  • Screen for exposure to exogenous hormones
  • Consider social history (adoption, abuse)

Examination

  • Accurate height/weight measurements and plotting
  • Tanner staging of puberty
  • Testicular volume assessment in boys (Prader orchidometer)
  • Skin examination for café-au-lait spots
  • Neurological examination including visual fields
  • Look for dysmorphic features or asymmetry

Diagnostic Investigations

  • Bone age: Left hand/wrist X-ray (typically advanced by 1-3 years)
  • Biochemical: Basal LH (≥0.3 IU/L suggests CPP)
  • GnRH stimulation test: LH peak ≥5 IU/L confirms CPP
  • Sex steroids: Elevated estrogen (girls) or testosterone (boys)
  • Pelvic ultrasound (girls): Uterine size, endometrial thickness, ovarian follicles
  • Brain MRI: All boys and girls <6 years; consider for girls 6-8 years
  • Pituitary hormone evaluation: If history of CNS disease, radiation, or structural abnormalities
  • Genetic testing: Consider for familial CPP (MKRN3, DLK1, KISS1R mutations)
  • 17-OHP and androgens: To diagnose CAH
  • Adrenal imaging: CT/MRI if adrenal tumor suspected
  • Thyroid function tests: To rule out hypothyroidism
  • Genetic testing: For McCune-Albright syndrome, familial testotoxicosis
  • Additional endocrine workup: Based on clinical suspicion of MAS

Management Approach

  • Depends on underlying cause and treatment
    • Type Treatment Monitoring
    CPP GnRH agonists (monthly or 3-monthly depot formulations) Growth velocity, bone age, pubertal progression every 6-12 months
    GIPP Treatment varies by cause (aromatase inhibitors, anti-androgens, ketoconazole)

    Treatment Goals

    • Preserve adult height potential
    • Delay puberty until appropriate psychosocial age
    • Treat underlying cause when identifiable
    • Stop treatment when acceptable age of puberty is reached
