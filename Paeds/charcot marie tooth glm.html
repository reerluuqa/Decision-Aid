CMT Quick Scan

Last Updated: May 2024

Definition

A group of hereditary motor and sensory neuropathies (HMSN). Both motor and sensory nerves are affected sym symetrically.

üîë Key Point: Absence of family history does NOT rule out CMT.
  • Genetically heterogeneous (90+ genes).
  • Demyelinating or Axonal pathology.
  • Length-dependent progression.

Epidemiology

1:2500 Prevalence
50% Cases are CMT1A

Most common inherited neurological disorder.

Typical Clinical Presentation

History

  • Clumsiness in childhood: Poor runner, unable to ice skate.
  • Foot deformities: High arches (Pes cavus), difficulty finding shoes.
  • Gait issues: Steppage gait, frequent tripping, ankle twisting.
  • Hand symptoms: Difficulty with buttons, fine motor tasks (later onset).

Physical Exam

  • Distal Weakness: Foot dorsiflexion (foot drop), intrinsic hand muscles.
  • Atrophy: "Stork legs" (peroneal muscular atrophy), claw hands.
  • Reflexes: Diffuse areflexia or hyporeflexia (Achilles first).
  • Sensation: Reduced vibration/proprioception (large fibers).

Investigation Pathway

1. Nerve Conduction Studies (NCS)
Demyelinating (CMT1):
Velocity < 38 m/s (Ulnar). Prolonged distal latencies.
Axonal (CMT2):
Reduced amplitudes, Velocity > 38 m/s (Normal).
2. Genetic Testing
  • CMT1A (50%): PMP22 Duplication.
  • CMT1X (10%): GJB1 Mutation (Connexin 32).
  • CMT2A: MFN2 Mutation (Axonal).
  • HNPP: PMP22 Deletion (Transient palsies).

    Primary Management

    üéØ Goal: Maintain function & independence.
    • Rehabilitation: Strength, stretching, balance training.
    • Orthotics: Ankle-foot orthoses (AFOs) for foot drop.
    • Assistive Devices: Canes, walkers if needed.

    Orthopedic Interventions

    • Foot: Tendon transfers, triple arthrodesis, hammer toe correction.
    • Spine: Spinal fusion for severe scoliosis (>40-50 deg).
    • Heel Cord: Lengthening for tight cords/toe-walking.

    Patient Discussion & Prevention

    Education Points
    • Counsel regarding disease progression (generally slow).
    • Discuss genetics and recurrence risk.
    • Avoid neurotoxic drugs (e.g., specific chemotherapeutics like vincristine) if possible.
    Genetic Counseling
    • AD: 50% risk to offspring.
    • X-linked: 50% risk (daughters) or 100% (daughters) / 0% (sons) depending on parent affected.
    • AR: 25% risk if both parents carriers.
