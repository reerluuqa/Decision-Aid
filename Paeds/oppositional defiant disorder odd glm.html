ODD Clinical Reference

Updated: Jan 14, 2025 • BMJ Best Practice Source

Overview

Oppositional Defiant Disorder (ODD)

Definition: Persistent and substantial antagonism/defiance + chronic anger/irritability.
Onset: Usually < Age 8. Persists through adolescence into adulthood.

Red Flags & Dangers

  • Rule out Conduct Disorder (CD): Aggression, physical harm to people/animals, destruction of property, theft, or serious rule-breaking is not ODD.
  • Suicidality Risk: Elevations of the chronic irritability dimension are predictive of depression, anxiety, and suicidality.
  • Substance Exclusion: Symptoms must not occur exclusively during substance abuse.
  • Mood/Psychotic Exclusion: Ensure symptoms are not solely part of a psychotic episode or depressive/bipolar disorder.

Immediate Clinical Action

1. Referral Protocol

Refer ALL patients with suspected ODD to a mental health professional (specializing in ODD if possible) for comprehensive assessment.

2. Children/Adolescents

Refer for evidence-based behavioral interventions & Parent Training. Parents MUST be involved.

3. Adults

Evidence is scarce. Refer to specialist for consideration of psychotherapy.

Dimension 1: Chronic Irritability

Predictive of depression, anxiety, suicidality.

  • Often loses temper
  • Often touchy or easily annoyed
  • Often angry and resentful

Dimension 2: Oppositional Behavior

Predictive of future Conduct Disorder (CD).

  • Often argues with authority figures
  • Often actively defies/refuses to comply with requests
  • Often deliberately annoys people
  • Often blames others for mistakes
  • Often spiteful or vindictive (≥2 times in 6mo)

Diagnostic Thresholds (DSM-5-TR)

Parameter Criteria for Diagnosis
Symptom Count 4 or more symptoms (from either or both dimensions)
Context Exhibited with at least one person who is NOT a sibling
Duration Persisting for at least 6 months
Frequency (Age < 5) Occurs on most days
Frequency (Age ≥ 5) Occurs at least once per week
ADHD

Very common comorbidity (13.6% - 29.5%). Usually precedes ODD. Treat separately.

Conduct Disorder (CD)

Higher oppositional behavior dimension increases CD risk. Aggression suggests CD, not ODD.

Anxiety/Depression

Strong link with chronic irritability dimension. 92.4% of adults with ODD have another lifetime disorder.

Condition Why it differs from ODD
Conduct Disorder Involves aggression to people/animals, destruction of property, theft, or serious rule-breaking. ODD does not.
DMDD Severe temper outbursts + chronic irritability. Overlap is high, but DMDD implies higher intensity outbursts.
PDA (PDA) Not in DSM-5/ICD-11. Obsessive avoidance of everyday demands. Often linked to autism profiles (UK focus).

Biological/Genetic

  • Heritability: 40-60%
  • Low basal cortisol
  • Low resting heart rate
  • History of ADHD

Environmental

  • Low socioeconomic status
  • Maternal smoking/stress in pregnancy
  • Child exposure to abuse/violence
  • Maladaptive parenting (timid discipline, low warmth)
