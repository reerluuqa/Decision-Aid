Infantile Spasms Quick-Scan

Red Flags / Emergencies
  • Clusters of spasms >50/day or status pattern
  • Developmental regression or plateau
  • Hypsarrhythmia on EEG
  • Onset 3-12 months (peak 3-5 m)
  • Asymmetrical / focal spasms → suspect lesion

Action: Admit, EEG within 24 h, start 1-line therapy immediately

1-Line Treatment (start today)
Non-Tuberous Sclerosis
DrugDose (typical)
Prednisolone4-6 wk taper
+ Vigabatrin100-150 mg/kg/d
Tuberous Sclerosis
DrugDose (typical)
Vigabatrin mono100-150 mg/kg/d

Target: spasm cessation 14 days + EEG resolution

Key Investigations (do early)
  • Sleep / awake EEG
  • MRI brain
  • Wood-lamp skin exam
  • Baseline vision check
  • Micro-array CGH
  • Next-gen epilepsy panel
  • Metabolic screen
  • CMV PCR & toxo serology

Prognosis at a Glance
  • Good: unknown aetiology, normal dev at onset, lead-time <3 wk
  • Poor: brain malformation, post-infectious, TSC, hypsarrhythmia >3 wk
  • 60 % develop other epilepsy
  • 80-90 % developmental delay
  • 5-30 % mortality by 3 y
For licensed clinicians only • Confirm local dosing • Refer to tertiary neurology early
