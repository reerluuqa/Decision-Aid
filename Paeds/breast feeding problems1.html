üìù HISTORY - Mother ‚ñº

Breastfeeding History

  • Concerns about baby's feeding
  • Pattern: frequency, duration, night feeds
  • One or both breasts offered
  • Milk supply issues (engorgement, low/over)
  • Expressing: hand or pump, frequency
  • Supplementary feeds given?
  • Use of nipple shields/breast shells
  • Previous breastfeeding experience

Pain History (if applicable)

  • Onset: when post-partum?
  • Timing: with attachment, during/between feeds
  • Location: unilateral/bilateral, nipple/breast
  • Character: burning, sharp, dull, itching
  • Nipple trauma: abrasions, fissures, bleeding
  • Associated: fever, skin changes, malaise
  • Colour changes to nipple?

Medical History

  • Pregnancy/labour/postnatal complications
  • Thyroid disorders, diabetes
  • Raynaud's phenomenon
  • Eczema/psoriasis
  • Recent Candida or bacterial infection
  • Previous breast surgery
  • Medications & allergies
  • Alcohol & smoking
  • Depression/anxiety/stress
üìù HISTORY - Infant ‚ñº
  • Gestation at birth, birth trauma
  • Known conditions/congenital abnormalities
  • Birth weight, current weight, weight gain
  • General health & behaviour
  • Number of wet & dirty nappies
  • Stool colour
  • Behaviour at breast: rhythmic sucking, audible swallowing
  • Pulling, biting, coughing, sleepiness at breast
  • GI symptoms (reflux, colic)
  • Known tongue-tie or cleft
  • Dummy use
üëÅ EXAMINATION - Mother

General

  • Signs of infection or systemic illness
  • Fever, tachycardia, hypotension

Breast Inspection

  • Flat or inverted nipples
  • Skin damage, discharge, crusting
  • Fissuring, discolouration
  • Peau d'orange
  • Individual lesions or rashes

Breast Palpation

  • Lumps (localised or diffuse)
  • Firmness or tenderness
  • Fluctuant mass (abscess?)
üëÅ EXAMINATION - Infant
  • Signs of dehydration
  • Jaundice
  • Lethargy / inconsolability
  • Head & neck symmetry
  • Oral anatomy: tongue-tie, palate, jaw, lips
  • Muscle tone
  • Signs of oral thrush
üëÄ OBSERVE A BREASTFEED

Ensure trained person (HV/lactation specialist) observes:

  • Maternal & infant positioning
  • Attachment (mouth wide, lips everted)
  • Behaviour at the breast
  • Sucking pattern
  • Shape & colour of nipples AFTER feed

üìâ Low Milk Supply
‚ö†Ô∏è True low supply is unusual - often perceived
Insufficient breast access Short/infrequent feeds, no night feeds, dummy use
Suboptimal attachment Nipple pain, >2hrly feeds, <5min or >40min feeds
Prolactin deficiency Drugs, thyroid, retained placenta, pituitary
Maternal factors Depression/anxiety, hypoplastic breasts, surgery
üìà Milk Oversupply
‚ÑπÔ∏è Common in first weeks - usually physiological
Physiological First few weeks as supply adjusts
Suboptimal attachment Inefficient removal ‚Üí more suckling ‚Üí more milk
Feeding pattern Switching sides too early, excessive expressing

Infant signs:

  • Choking/spluttering at let-down
  • Pulling off breast, colic, explosive stools

Condition-Specific Management

üíß ENGORGEMENT
  • Feed frequently - no restrictions on frequency/length
  • Simple analgesia (paracetamol) for pain
  • Express MINIMALLY to relieve fullness (avoid overstimulation)
  • Hand express to soften areola before latch if struggling
  • Cold packs AFTER feeding to reduce pain/oedema
  • Well-fitting bra; loose clothing
üîí COMPRESSED/BLOCKED DUCTS
  • Feed frequently from affected breast
  • Well-fitting bra; avoid tight clothing
  • Warn about mastitis signs - seek advice if fever, redness, flu-like
‚ö†Ô∏è Persistent breast mass: Consider alternative diagnosis (cancer) ‚Üí referral
‚ö° NIPPLE DAMAGE
  • Review positioning/attachment (most common cause)
  • Refer infant for tongue-tie division if present
  • Stop nipple shields/breast shells if contributing
  • Apply expressed breast milk if cracked/fissured
ü¶† NIPPLE INFECTION

Bacterial:

  • Topical fusidic acid OR mupirocin (see Prescribing)
  • If cellulitis/mastitis ‚Üí see Mastitis CKS

If red/inflamed:

  • Consider hydrocortisone 1% cream + antimicrobial
  • Remove from nipple with warm water before feed
üö® Vesicular rash (HSV/VZV): Discuss with specialist urgently - neonatal transmission risk
üî¥ SKIN CONDITIONS

Eczema/Contact Dermatitis:

  • Avoid triggers: lanolin, beeswax, chamomile, soaps
  • Regular emollients
  • Topical corticosteroid after feeds (potency by severity)

Psoriasis:

  • Emollients + topical treatment after feeds
‚ö†Ô∏è Unilateral, persistent, unresponsive eczema ‚Üí 2WW for Paget's
‚ùÑÔ∏è VASOSPASM / RAYNAUD'S
  • Avoid cold exposure; warm clothing
  • Breastfeed in warm environment
  • Heat packs / warm shower after feeds
  • Avoid caffeine
  • Stop smoking (see Smoking cessation CKS)
If persistent: Consider specialist referral for trial of oral nifedipine (off-label)
üìâ LOW MILK SUPPLY
‚ö†Ô∏è Urgent paeds referral if infant dehydration/faltering growth
  • Manage underlying cause in mother or infant
  • Lactation specialist assessment
  • Optimise positioning, attachment, skin-to-skin
  • Increase feeding frequency
  • Effective breast drainage
  • Expressing between feeds if needed
If prolactin deficiency suspected: Discuss with/refer to endocrinologist
üìà MILK OVERSUPPLY
  • Recognise early hunger cues - initiate feeds appropriately
  • If latch difficult due to fullness: hand express small amount until flow slows
  • Try leaning back if forceful let-down continues
  • Ensure optimal positioning/attachment
  • Feed from ONE breast per feed to reduce supply
  • Avoid expressing between feeds where possible

üíä PARACETAMOL

üíä Pain relief for engorgement/breast pain

Paracetamol
500mg-1g every 4-6 hours PRN
Max 4g in 24 hours
Safe in breastfeeding. See Analgesia CKS for full details.
üß¥ TOPICAL FUSIDIC ACID

üíä Localised bacterial nipple infection (impetigo, secondary infection)

Fusidic acid 2% cream
Apply up to 3 times daily for 5 days
Manufacturer advises avoid breastfeeding from affected nipple during treatment
Cautions:
  • Avoid use >10 days (resistance risk)
  • Avoid contact with eyes/mucous membranes
üß¥ TOPICAL MUPIROCIN

üíä Alternative if fusidic acid resistance suspected/confirmed

Mupirocin 2% cream/ointment
Apply 3 times daily for 5 days
Wash area thoroughly before breastfeeding if applied to nipple
Cautions:
  • Avoid contact with eyes
  • Avoid ointment in mod-severe renal impairment (polyethylene glycol)
  • Prolonged use ‚Üí overgrowth of non-susceptible organisms
üß¥ TOPICAL MICONAZOLE

üíä Suspected nipple candidiasis

Miconazole 2% cream
Apply twice daily, continuing for 10 days after lesions healed
Apply sparingly after breastfeed. Manufacturer advises caution in breastfeeding.
Cautions:
  • Avoid contact with eyes/mucous membranes
  • May enhance anticoagulant effect of warfarin - monitor
Note: Candida as cause of nipple pain remains controversial. Consider potential for over-diagnosis.
üß¥ TOPICAL CORTICOSTEROIDS

üíä Inflamed nipples (with infection) / Eczema / Psoriasis

Hydrocortisone 1% cream (MILD)
Apply after feeds to nipple/areola
Preferred for nipple/areola. Remove with warm water before feeding.
Clobetasone butyrate 0.05% (MODERATE)
Apply after feeds if hydrocortisone insufficient
Preferred over potent steroids for nipple/areola area
‚ö†Ô∏è Important:
  • Use cream (not ointment) - easier to remove before feeds
  • Least potent for shortest duration
  • AVOID high potency on nipple/areola
  • Remove from ALL breast areas before feeding
