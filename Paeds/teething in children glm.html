Teething Management

NICE CKS Birth to 36 Months Last Revised: Oct 2024

Immediate Exclusion Required

Do NOT attribute symptoms to teething if the child is systemically unwell, severely distressed, or has a temperature > 38°C. Investigate infection or sepsis first.

Recognising Teething

Consider teething only after excluding other causes. Symptoms usually start 3–5 days before eruption.

Typical Presentation:
Mild Pain Drooling Gum Rubbing Biting/Chewing Irritability Temp < 38°C Facial Rash Ear Rubbing
Clinical Signs: Gums may appear swollen, red, and tender on palpation. Facial flushing may be present.

Differential Diagnosis

Rule out these conditions first:

ConditionClue
Acute Otitis MediaEar pain, fever >38°C
Herpetic GingivostomatitisUlcers, high fever, refusing to drink
GastroenteritisVomiting, diarrhea
SepsisSystemically unwell, lethargic
Eruption HematomaLocalized swelling over tooth

Self-Care (1st Line)

  • Reassure: Teething is a normal, self-limiting process.
  • Comfort: Cuddling and extra reassurance.
  • Massage: Gentle rubbing of gums with a clean finger.
  • Cooling: Bite on a clean, cool object (chilled teething ring or cold wet flannel).
  • Food (Weaned only): Supervised chilled fruit/veg (banana/cucumber). Avoid hard pieces (choking risk).
  • Hygiene: Wipe excess saliva to prevent facial rash.
Oral Hygiene:

Brush teeth twice daily with fluoride toothpaste as soon as they erupt. Dentist visit by 1st birthday.

Pharmacological Options

Simple Analgesia:
If self-care fails, consider sugar-free Paracetamol or Ibuprofen (licensed doses for age/weight).
⚠️ Lidocaine-containing Gels (e.g., Anbesol, Calgel, Dentinox)
  • Only available under pharmacy supervision.
  • Not licensed for children < 5 months.
  • Avoid if possible (risk of methaemoglobinaemia). Use only if distress is significant and other measures failed.
⛔ Do NOT Recommend
  • Homeopathic teething tablets/gels (MHRA warning).
  • Herbal teething powders.
  • Lidocaine gels for children under 5 months.
  • Salicylate gels (e.g., Bonjela original - contraindicated <16yrs).
Note: Bonjela Junior Gel is not licensed for teething.
