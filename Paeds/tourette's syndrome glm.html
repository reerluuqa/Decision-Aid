Risk Factors

Strong: Male, Age 3-8, Family history of TS/OCD/ADHD.
Weak: Maternal smoking, Stress/NAUSEA (1st tri), Low birth weight.

Exams & Tests

Neuro Exam: Typically normal. No biomarker exists.

Rule out: Allergies (sniffing), Seizures (EEG), Wilson's (Copper/Ceruloplasmin), Sydenham's (ASO titers).

Management Algorithm

Tics (General)

1

Education & Behavioral

CBIT (Comprehensive Behavioral Intervention for Tics) is first-line. Habit Reversal Therapy.

2

Alpha-2 Agonists (Mild/Mod)

Guanfacine Clonidine

Favorable side effect profile.

Dosing Guide

Guanfacine: Start 0.25mg, max 4mg/day.
Clonidine: Start 0.1mg, max 0.4mg/day.

3

Antipsychotics (Severe)

Warning: EPS, weight gain, tardive syndromes.

Aripiprazole Risperidone
Secondary/Tertiary Options

Secondary: Haloperidol, Pimozide.
Tertiary: Tetrabenazine, Topiramate.

Comorbidities

ADHD

Stimulants Safe

First-line: CNS Stimulants (Methylphenidate, Dexamfetamine).
Meta-analysis shows no worsening of tics at therapeutic doses.

Adjunct: Alpha-2 agonists.

OCD

First-line: CBT (Exposure and Response Prevention).

Refractory: SSRIs (Fluoxetine) or Clomipramine.

Refractory Focal Tics

Consider Botulinum Toxin Type A. Reduces motor component and premonitory sensations.
