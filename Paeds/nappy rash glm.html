ü©∫ Diagnosis & Confirmation

Physical Examination

  • Gowers' Sign: Characteristic of DMD (ages 4-7). Uses hands to "climb up" legs to stand.
  • Gait: Waddling or toe walking (symmetrical).
  • Contractures: Heel cord and increased lumbar lordosis (due to strength imbalance).
  • Reflexes: Diminished/absent deep tendon reflexes. Sensation is normal.
  • Cognitive: Potential delay (mean IQ 85-90). Assess for ADHD/ASD.

Investigation Flow

1st Line: Genetic Testing
Test for DMD gene deletion/duplication (MLPH or array CGH).
Essential Confirmatory
2nd Line (If Genetics -):
  • Muscle Biopsy (Immunohistochemistry for dystrophin)
  • EMG (Myopathic pattern: short duration, polyphasic)
üöÄ Management Protocols

Pharmacology

Goal: Preserve muscle strength & delay loss of ambulation.

  • Steroids: Daily Prednisolone or Deflazacort. Weekend-only regimens may reduce side effects.
  • Supplements: Vitamin D & Calcium (to counter steroid-induced osteoporosis).

Physical Therapy

Goal: Prevent contractures.

  • Night Splints: Moulded ankle-foot orthoses (AFOs).
  • Exercise: Sub-maximal aerobic (swimming, cycling). Avoid high-resistance strength training.
  • Stretching: Daily home program for ankles, knees, hips.
üí° Clinical Note: Steroid-treated patients have milder scoliosis and delayed need for ventilation.

Mobility & Scoliosis

  • Wheelchair: Provide powered wheelchair to maintain independence.
  • Standing: Use standing frame or standing wheelchair to maintain bone density.
  • Scoliosis: Monitor curve progression. Spinal orthoses are generally not recommended. Posterior spinal fusion may be needed if curve is severe/progressive.

Activities of Daily Living

  • Assistive Tech: Environmental control units, robotic arms, adapted eating utensils.
  • Upper Limb: Continue steroids to preserve function longer.

Respiratory Support

  • Lung Volume Recruitment: When FVC ‚â§ 60% (Air stacking/bagging).
  • Assisted Cough: When FVC < 50% or Peak Cough Flow < 270 L/min.
  • Nocturnal NIV: Indicated for hypoventilation symptoms or FVC < 50%.
  • 24-hr NIV: Daytime mouthpiece ventilation if SpO2 < 95% or pCO2 > 45 mmHg.

Cardiac Management

  • Prophylaxis: Start ACEi/ARB by age 10.
  • Heart Failure: Add Beta-blocker. Consider Eplerenone.
  • Arrhythmias: Standard anti-arrhythmics. Consider ICD if LVEF < 35%.
  • Monitoring: Annual ECG and Echo (or MRI).

üîç Key Differentials
  • Becker MD: Milder, later onset (teens).
  • Spinal Muscular Atrophy: Genetic (SMN1), low CK. Hypotonia prominent.
  • Polymyositis: Inflammation on biopsy, usually acquired.
  • Cerebral Palsy: Spasticity (increased tone), asymmetric MRI findings.
