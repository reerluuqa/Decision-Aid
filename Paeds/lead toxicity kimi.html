Treatment

üö® Medical Emergency

Acute lead encephalopathy requires aggressive chelation therapy in ICU. Presents with altered sensorium, seizures, coma.

Treatment Algorithm Overview
1 Remove Source of Exposure - Primary intervention for ALL patients
2 GI Decontamination - If solid lead objects ingested
3 Chelation Therapy - Based on blood lead levels & symptoms
4 ICU Support - If encephalopathy present

First-Line Agents

üß† Neurological Not Reversible

  • Neurodevelopmental delay in children - NO improvement with chelation
  • Cognitive impairment persists into adulthood
  • Lower IQ, psychopathology, low socioeconomic status

‚ù§Ô∏è Cardiovascular Not Reversed by Chelation

  • Hypertension - key toxicity in adults
  • Coronary artery disease
  • Increased stroke mortality
  • Peripheral vascular disease

ü©∏ Hematological

  • Anemia - resolves with decreased exposure
  • Iron supplementation treats co-existing deficiency

ü´ò Renal

  • Fanconi's syndrome (phosphaturia, glucosuria, aminoaciduria)
  • Responds to decreased exposure and chelation
  • Chronic renal failure may persist in some patients
üîÆ Prognosis
‚ñº

üí° Key Principle

Much of lead toxicity is NOT reversible by medical therapy. Lead resides in bone for decades after exposure ceases. Lead poisoning is a chronic illness requiring long-term management.

‚úÖ Readily Reversed

  • Acute symptoms (abdominal pain)
  • Lead colic responds to chelation
  • Anemia with appropriate treatment

‚ùå Poorly Reversible

  • Neurological impairments (CNS & PNS)
  • Cardiovascular toxicity
  • Neurodevelopmental delay in children

üßí Encephalopathy

  • Survival is good with ICU care
  • Most survivors have residual neurological deficits

üìä Long-term Outlook

  • Lifetime lead exposure expressed later in life
  • May accelerate cognitive decline in older adults
  • Requires lifelong monitoring
