Type I Glycogen Storage Disease

BMJ Best Practice Interactive Algorithm (Jan 2024)

Assessment

History, Examination & Risk Factors

Critical

Key Diagnostic History

Frequent Feedings

Infants unable to sleep through night without feeding

Irritability/Lethargy

Episodes resolving after feeding

Early Presentation

Typically within first year of life

Bleeding Tendency

Epistaxis, ecchymoses, prolonged bleeding

Physical Examination

Hepatomegaly

Distinguishing feature

Hyperpnoea

Lactic acidosis

Growth Delay

Faltering growth, delayed puberty

Risk Factors

Strong Family History

Autosomal recessive inheritance - 1 in 4 chance if both parents carriers

Diagnosis

Investigations & Differentials

Essential

First-Line Investigations

Test Result Interpretation
Serum Glucose Low <40 mg/dL within 3-4 hours after feed
Serum Lactic Acid Elevated Essential for characterization
Serum Uric Acid Elevated Hyperuricaemia characteristic
Serum Triglycerides Elevated May cause milky serum appearance

Confirmatory Testing

Molecular Genetic Testing

Recommended to confirm diagnosis - G6PC1 and SLC37A4 gene analysis

Gold Standard

Key Differentials

GSD Type III

Higher AST/ALT, muscle weakness, intact gluconeogenesis

Moderate Distinction

Fructose-1,6-bisphosphatase Deficiency

Similar labs but brisk glycaemic response to glucagon in fed state

Very Rare

Treatment

Management Algorithm & Protocols

Actionable

Hypoglycaemic Emergency

Acute hypoglycaemia correction protocol

URGENT
  • Give glucose IV or enterally
  • Continue 10% dextrose infusion at maintenance rate
  • Do NOT use glucagon (unlike diabetic hypoglycaemia)

Ongoing Management - Cornstarch Therapy

Uncooked Cornstarch

Mainstay therapy from early childhood through adulthood

CORE THERAPY

Dosage Guidelines

  • • Infants: ~0.5 g/kg/hour
  • • Children: 1-1.5 g/kg every 3-4 hours
  • • Adults: 1 g/kg every 4-6 hours

Administration

  • • Mix with water/sugar-free fluid
  • • Day: every 3-5 hours
  • • Night: every 4-6 hours

Hyperuricaemia

CAUTION

Allopurinol 10 mg/kg/day (children) or 300-800 mg/day (adults) if persistent

Severe Hyperlipidaemia

URGENT

Fenofibrate when TG >5.7 mmol/L despite optimal therapy

GSD Type Ib Specific

SPECIFIC

Vitamin E supplementation: 600 mg/day (pre-pubertal) or 900 mg/day (adults) to improve neutrophil function

Follow-up

Monitoring & Complications

Long-term

Monitoring Schedule

Metabolic: Glucose, lactate, uric acid, lipids
Renal: Creatinine, urine albumin
Growth: Height, weight, puberty status
CGM: Continuous glucose monitoring
Liver US: Annual from age 5 years

Key Complications

Hepatic Adenomas

High likelihood by age 30 - risk of hemorrhage or malignant transformation

HIGH RISK

Nephropathy

Proximal tubular dysfunction → FSGS → ESRD

MODERATE RISK

Osteopenia/Osteoporosis

Decreased bone mineral content

PREVENTABLE

Neutropenia (GSD Ib)

Recurrent infections, IBD-like disease

TYPE-SPECIFIC

Prognosis

OPTIMISTIC

With appropriate nutritional therapy, recurrent hypoglycaemia can be avoided, allowing survival into adulthood. Optimal metabolic control can delay or prevent complications.
