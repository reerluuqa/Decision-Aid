Definition & Pathophysiology Core

ALL is a malignant clonal disease of lymphoid progenitor cells (B or T lineage) resulting in uncontrolled proliferation.


  • Infiltration: Bone marrow replacement + organ infiltration (CNS, testes, spleen).
  • B-ALL: ~75% of adult cases.
  • T-ALL: ~25% of adult cases; often presents with mediastinal mass.

Common Clinical Presentation Cytopenias
Anemia (80%)

Fatigue, dyspnoea, palpitations, pallor, dizziness.

Thrombocytopenia (75%)

Easy bruising, epistaxis, petechiae, menorrhagia.

Neutropenia

Recurrent infections, fever. (High risk for sepsis)

Constitutional

Fevers, night sweats, weight loss.

Organ Involvement
  • Lymphadenopathy: Generalised, painless, freely movable.
  • Hepatosplenomegaly: Diffuse enlargement; abdominal pain/distension.
  • Testicular: Painless unilateral enlargement (Sanctuary site). Common in children/adolescents T-ALL.
  • Bone Pain: Medullary cavity/periosteum pressure.
Specific Red Flags by Subtype
T-ALL

Anterior mediastinal mass (widened mediastinum on CXR), SVC obstruction, stridor.

Mature B-ALL (Burkitt)

Rapidly proliferating abdominal mass, often extra-medullary.

Definitive Diagnosis (BM)
  • Aspirate & Trephine: Hypercellularity, lymphoblast infiltration. Generally ‚â•20% blasts required.
  • Cytomorphology: MPO negative (rules out AML).
  • Immunophenotyping:
    - B-ALL: CD19, CD22, CD79a, TdT+.
    - T-ALL: CD3, CD7.
  • Cytogenetics/FISH: Detect translocations (t(9;22), KMT2A).
  • Molecular (RT-PCR/NGS): Mutations (e.g., Ph-like JAK-STAT).
CNS Staging (Mandatory)

CNS involvement occurs in 5-7% at diagnosis (Higher in T-ALL/Mature B). LP must be done after intracranial pressure is ruled out.

Grading (Children's Oncology Group):
Based on Lymphoblast count, WBC count, and RBC count (traumatic tap) in CSF.
Required Imaging:
  • CXR (Mediastinal mass)
  • CT Thorax (if widened mediastinum)
  • CT Neck/Thorax/Abd/Pelvis (Extramedullary disease)
  • Scrotal US (if abnormal exam)
  • Echo/MUGA (Baseline cardiac function for Anthracyclines)

Targeted & Immunotherapy
  • Ph+ ALL: Add Tyrosine Kinase Inhibitors (Imatinib, Dasatinib).
  • Immunotherapy: Blinatumomab (BiTE), Inotuzumab (ADC), CAR T-cell therapy.
  • SCT: Allogeneic Stem Cell Transplant for high-risk/relapsed patients.
Prognosis (Adults)
40-60%
Cure Rate

Poor Prognosis Factors: Age >35, High WBC at dx, Ph+ status, Time to CR >4 weeks, MRD+.

Long-term Adverse Effects: Heart failure, sterility, secondary malignancies, neurotoxicity.
