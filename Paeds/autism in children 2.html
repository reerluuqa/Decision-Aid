Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD)

Last updated: Jun 13, 2023

Quick-scan clinical guide for Paediatricians, Psychiatrists, and GPs.

üö® Red Flags & Immediate Actions

Regression (Language or Motor) Critical

Child younger than 3 years?

Loss of previously acquired language skills is a red flag.

ACTION: Refer immediately to Specialist Team

Regression > 3 years or Motor Loss Urgent

Language loss > 3 years OR Motor skill regression at ANY age.

Think of other diagnoses (e.g., neurodegenerative).

ACTION: Refer to Paediatrician/Neurologist

Absence of Key Milestones Screen

At 12-18 months: No pointing, no eye contact, no babbling.

  • No to-and-fro social interaction.
  • No proto-declarative pointing.
ACTION: Specialist Assessment
üìä Overview & Epidemiology

Definition Core

Lifelong neurodevelopmental condition characterized by:

  • Persistent impairments in social communication.
  • Restricted, repetitive patterns of behavior, interests, or activities.

Onset: Early childhood (may manifest later).

Key Stats US/Global

  • Prevalence: ~1-2% (1 in 44 children in US).
  • Sex: 4.2x more prevalent in boys (Girls often under-diagnosed).
  • Intellectual Disability: 40-80% of cases.
  • Epilepsy: 20-30% develop epilepsy.

Risk Factors Genetic

Heritability: 50-80%. (Most have no relative with ASD).

Sibling Recurrence: ~20%.

Maternal Health: Sodium Valproate during pregnancy (Causal link).

Common Co-morbidities Context

  • ADHD (Common co-occurrence).
  • Anxiety (~50% of children/adults).
  • Depression (~10% children, 25-50% adults).
  • Sleep difficulties (~50-80% children).
üîç Diagnostic Approach

Step 1: History & Exam

Requires info from more than one environment (home + school/work).

  • Exclude hearing loss (Audiology).
  • Genetic testing (targeted if ID/dysmorphism; universal in US).
  • Wood Lamp: Check for hypopigmented macules (Tuberous Sclerosis).
  • Measure head circumference (Macrocephaly association).

Step 2: Tools & Observation

Children: Observation in school/nursery is valuable.

Adults: Needs informant for developmental history if possible.

ADOS-G ADI-R AQ-10 (Screening)

Note: AQ-10 has reasonable sensitivity but lower specificity. Use with caution.

üë• Adults & Females

Female Presentation Key Insight

Often under-diagnosed due to different presentation and masking.

  • Masking: Imitate peers to hide social deficits.
  • Interests: May be socially typical (e.g., literature, animals) vs. mechanical.
  • Diagnosis: Often shy, perfectionistic, or "bossy" rather than classically autistic.
  • Risk: Higher likelihood of eating disorders as a comorbidity.

Adult Diagnosis Late ID

Often presents during times of stress, transition, or after child's diagnosis.

  • Screen adults in mental health services for ASD (Anxiety/Depression/ADHD common).
  • Ask about: History of neurodevelopmental issues, social relationship difficulties, employment sustainability.
  • Masking: Adults may be very skilled at hiding symptoms in short interviews.
‚úÖ Management & Goals

Treatment Aims Goals

  • Maximize functional independence.
  • Maximize quality of life.
  • Minimize distress.
  • Support learning & cognitive skills.
  • Manage co-occurring physical/mental health problems.

General Approach Strategy

Early Intervention: Crucial for better outcomes.

Multidisciplinary: Paediatrics, Psychiatry, Psychology, Speech Therapy, Occupational Therapy.

Environment: Structured, predictable environments are beneficial.

Prognosis Long Term

Variable outcomes in adulthood:

  • ~15%: Live independent lives.
  • ~20%: 24-hour care or community support.
  • Others: Varying degrees of support needed.
