Clinical Reference: Down's Syndrome

Evidence-Based Quick Scan (BMJ Best Practice)
CRITICAL: Cardiac Evaluation

~50% of newborns have CHD.

Most common: Atrioventricular septal defect (AVSD).

  • Action Echocardiogram (Even if NO murmur)
  • Specialist Paediatric Cardiologist
Gastrointestinal Obstruction

5% to 12% risk.

Suspect if: Vomiting, abdominal distention, delay in stool passage.

  • Condition Duodenal/Anal stenosis or atresia
  • Action Abdominal X-Ray
  • Action Surgical Consult
Physical Diagnostic Checklist (Common Features)

Hypotonia (80%), Flat facial profile (90%), Slanted palpebral fissures (80%)

Mandatory Investigations (Day 0-3)
  • Chromosomal Karyotype Confirm Diagnosis
  • Echocardiogram All newborns
  • FBC (with diff) Rule out TAM/Polycythemia
  • Thyroid (TSH + T4) Congenital hypothyroidism
  • Hearing Screen OAE / ABR
  • Vision Exam Red reflex (Cataracts)
Genetics & Recurrence Risk
Standard Trisomy 21 95% (Non-disjunction)
Robertsonian Translocation 4-5% (Test parents!)
Mosaicism ~1% (Milder phenotype)
Recurrence (Standard): 1% or maternal age risk.
Translocation Carrier: Father (3-5%), Mother (10-15%), 21q21q (100%).
Developmental Delay & Milestones

Global developmental delay is common. Hypotonia and ligamentous laxity affect motor skills.

Motor Milestones
  • Roll: 5.0 - 6.4 mo
  • Sit: ~14.4 mo
  • Crawl: 12.2 - 17.3 mo
  • Walk: ~54 mo
Cognitive/Behavior
  • IQ: 40 - 72 (Mild-Mod ID)
  • Expressive language > Receptive delay
  • Age-related behaviors (ADHD in young, Anxiety in adults)
Ongoing Monitoring
6 months
Hearing evaluation, Ophthalmology exam
6 - 12 months
Sleep Study (if snoring/apnea suspected)
Annually
Thyroid function (TSH), Celiac screening (if symptomatic)
Adolescence
Atlantoaxial instability screening (pre-sports), Mental health screening
Common Associated Conditions
  • ENT Chronic Otitis Media, Hearing loss
  • Vision Refractive errors, Cataracts, Strabismus
  • Hematology Transient abnormal myelopoiesis (TAM), Leukemia risk
  • Endocrine Thyroid dysfunction, Diabetes risk
  • Neuro Seizures, Alzheimer's (early onset >40yo)
Essential Interventions
Early Intervention Physical Therapy (PT), Occupational Therapy (OT), Speech Therapy (ST) are essential to support development.
Education Individualized Education Programme (IEP) / resources. Inclusive schooling recommended.
Support Groups Connect families with Down's Syndrome Associations for social support and advocacy.
