Clinical Diagnosis

Typical Presentation

Age: Usually < 10 years.

Incubation: 4-7 days.

Prodrome: Low-grade fever (<38.5Â°C), malaise, anorexia, sore throat.

Key Features (The "Hand-Foot-Mouth" Triad)

  • Oral: Painful ulcers (yellowish) on tongue, buccal mucosa, palate. Heals in ~1 week.
  • Hands/Feet: Oval/linear vesicopustules with erythematous halo. May crusted.
  • Buttocks: Maculopapular rash (less common).

Investigations

Diagnosis is primarily Clinical. Labs are not usually required.

Consider if: Exposure to EV71 is possible (Travel to Asia) or presentation is atypical.

  • Viral Culture: Stool or vesicle fluid (preferred).
  • PCR: Rapidly distinguishes EV71 from Coxsackievirus A16.
  • FBC: Elevated WBC or atypical lymphocytes if done.

Management Protocol

1. Primary Treatment (All Patients)

Analgesia & Antipyretics

Paracetamol (15mg/kg q4-6h)
OR
Ibuprofen (5-10mg/kg q6-8h)

â›” NO Aspirin (Risk of Reye's syndrome)

Oral Care

Topical anaesthetics (e.g., Lidocaine 2% viscous) for painful ulcers.

"Magic Mouthwash" (Diphenhydramine + Maalox + Lidocaine +/- Sucralfate) - consult pharmacist.

Hydration & Nutrition

Cold fluids, soft foods. Avoid hot/spicy/acidic drinks.

Monitor for dehydration.

2. Escalation (Red Flags)

Hospitalization required if:

  • Enterovirus 71 (EV71) infection suspected or confirmed.
  • Immunocompromised host.
  • Severe complications develop:
  • Aseptic meningitis / Encephalitis
  • Acute flaccid paralysis (Polio-like)
  • Myocarditis
  • Pulmonary oedema / haemorrhage

Patient Education

  • Hygiene: Hand washing is critical. Virus persists in stool for up to 1 month.
  • Isolation: Exclude from school/daycare during first week of acute illness.
  • Cleaning: Disinfect surfaces with diluted bleach.

Aetiology

Most Common: Coxsackievirus A16.

Severe (Asia): Enterovirus 71 (EV71).

Other: A4, A5, A6, A7, A9, A10.

Epidemiology

Age: < 10 years.

Transmission: Fecal-oral, respiratory droplets, vesicle fluid.

Outbreaks: Common worldwide. EV71 epidemics in East/South-east Asia.

Prognosis

Typical: Self-limiting. Resolution in 10-14 days.

Severe (EV71): Mortality approx 8% in some outbreaks; 4% long-term sequelae.
