Diagnosis & Classification

Key Diagnostic Factors

History: Age 12-24 (peak), Genetic predisposition, Drugs (Androgens, Corticosteroids).

Lesion Types:
Comedones (Open/Closed) Papules Pustules Nodules/Cysts

Note: Routine lab testing is NOT indicated unless signs of hyperandrogenism (PCOS) are present.

Severity Classification (Simplified)

Grade Clinical Picture
Mild Comedones main. Small papules/pustules <10.
Moderate 10-40 papules/pustules. 10-40 comedones.
Mod. Severe 40-100 lesions. Few large nodules (<5). Widespread.
Very Severe Nodulocystic / Acne Conglobata. Scarring.

Treatment Algorithm

üßº General Care (All Patients): Non-comedogenic products, gentle cleanser (pH neutral), avoid picking, limit sun exposure.
MILD ACNE (Grade 1-2)

First Line: Topical Combination

Choose a fixed-dose combination:

  • Topical Retinoid + Benzoyl Peroxide
    e.g., Adapalene/BPO
  • Topical Retinoid + Antibiotic PLUS BPO
    Always add BPO to prevent resistance.
  • Alternative: Azelaic Acid or Salicylic Acid

Contraindicated

Do not use oral antibiotics for mild acne.

MODERATE to SEVERE (Grade 3-4)

Option A: Oral Antibiotics

Use ONLY with topical Benzoyl Peroxide (limit resistance).

  • Doxycycline (Preferred)
  • Minocycline or Sarecycline
  • Azithromycin (Conditional rec.)

Option B: Hormonal (Females)

  • Combined Oral Contraceptives (Anti-androgenic)
  • Spironolactone (Anti-androgen)

Option C: Isotretinoin

Severe Nodulocystic or treatment failure.

View Safety & Monitoring
  • Teratogenic: Pregnancy test required (iPledge program in US). 2 forms of contraception.
  • Labs: LFTs & Fasting Lipids at baseline and periodically.
  • Mental Health: Monitor for depression/suicidal ideation.
  • Duration: 15-20 weeks, until clear + 2-3 months.

Key Differentials

Rosacea No comedones. Central face erythema, telangiectasias. Older adults (30-50y).
Folliculitis Follicular pustules, trunk/extremities. No comedones.
Gram-Neg Folliculitis Occurs after long-term antibiotic use. Pustules/nodules on anterior nares.
Peri-oral Dermatitis Papules/pustules around mouth, clear zone near lip. Topical steroid link.
