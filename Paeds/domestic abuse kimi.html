Domestic Abuse

Quick Clinical Reference Guide
IMMEDIATE RISK INDICATORS
Strangulation/assault with weapon
Threats to kill
Controlling/jealous behaviour
Pregnancy assault
Substance misuse
Mental health issues
IMMEDIATE ACTIONS
  • Ensure patient safety & privacy
  • Call 999 if immediate danger
  • Refer to safeguarding teams
  • Contact IDVA service
  • Document clearly & safely
RECOGNITION INDICATORS
Type Signs
Physical Repeated injuries, vague explanations, injuries on covered areas
Behavioural Missed appointments, non-concordance, 'accident prone'
Psychological Depression, anxiety, PTSD, self-harm, substance misuse
Red Flags Partner always present, patient afraid to speak
KEY QUESTIONS TO ASK
  • "How are things at home?"
  • "Do you feel safe in your relationship?"
  • "Is there anyone hurting you?"
  • "Who are you frightened of?"
  • "Where is your partner now?"
  • "What would you like to happen?"

RISK FACTORS
Female gender
Age 16-24 years
Pregnancy/recent birth
Separation process
Disability/mental health

DOCUMENTATION
  • Use patient's own words
  • Record injuries accurately
  • Hide from online records
  • Limit access to care team
  • Include photos/maps if needed
