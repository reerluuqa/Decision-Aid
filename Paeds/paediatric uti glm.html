Definition

INFO
  • Lower UTI (Cystitis): Dysuria, frequency, urgency, suprapubic pain. Usually non-severe.
  • Upper UTI (Pyelo): Fever ‚â•38¬∞C, flank pain, costovertebral tenderness, vomiting.

Pathogens

  • E. Coli (85-90%): Most common.
  • Proteus mirabilis: Uncircumcised males.
  • Klebsiella / Enterococcus: Common in malformations.
  • Pseudomonas: Congenital anomalies.
  • S. saprophyticus: Female adolescents.

Initial Diagnosis

WORKUP

Urinalysis (Dipstick) is first line for age >2 months.

Imaging Guide

  • Ultrasound: All <2mo (1st UTI) or 2-24mo (Febrile UTI). Atypical UTI.
  • VCUG: Abnormal US, Atypical UTI, Recurrent UTI. Detects VUR.
  • DMSA Scan: Recurrent/Atypical UTI (4-6mo post-infection). Detects scarring.
