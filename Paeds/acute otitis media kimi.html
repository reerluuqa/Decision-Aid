Acute Otitis Media

NICE CKS Based • Last Revised Aug 2024
Age 1 month+

Immediate Admission Required

  • Severe systemic infection or signs of sepsis.
  • Red Flags: Meningitis, Mastoiditis, Intracranial abscess, Facial nerve paralysis, Swelling/erythema behind ear.
  • Infants < 3 months with temp ≥ 38°C.
  • Consider admission: Children < 3 months; Children 3–6 months with temp ≥ 39°C.
Diagnosis Criteria

Symptoms (Acute Onset)

Older children/Adults: Earache.
Younger children: Tugging/rubbing ear, fever, crying, irritability.

Otoscopy Signs

Distinctly red, yellow, or cloudy tympanic membrane.
Moderate to severe bulging with loss of landmarks.
Air-fluid level behind TM.
Perforation / Discharge in canal.
Not suggestive of AOM: TM not bulging (even if red), or air-fluid level without bulging.
Management Scenario

All Patients

Analgesia: Paracetamol or Ibuprofen (Regular doses).

No decongestants or antihistamines.

Immediate Antibiotics

Offer if systemically unwell OR high risk of complications.

Advise return if worsening.

No Rx OR Back-up Rx

Consider for those more likely to benefit (e.g., otorrhoea, or <2yrs with bilateral AOM) or routine cases.

Safety Netting: Return if no improvement in 3 days, or rapid worsening.

Definition: Returning with same episode, symptoms persisting or worsening after initial management.

Switch Antibiotics

Offer immediate Co-amoxiclav.

(If already on Amoxicillin).

Definition: ≥3 episodes in 6 months, or ≥4 in 12 months.

Management

Provide immediate or back-up antibiotics for acute episodes.

Consider Referral:

  • For hearing assessment / ventilation tubes (grommets).
  • For underlying causes (e.g., immunodeficiency, cleft palate).
Prescribing Info
Drug Note
1st Line Amoxicillin High dose if systemically unwell (e.g. 80-90mg/kg/day in 2 div doses - see local guidance).
Penicillin Allergy Clarithromycin or Erythromycin Check interactions (statins, QT prolongation).
2nd Line Co-amoxiclav For treatment failure or systemically unwell.
Advice: Children may return to school/daycare once afebrile and pain resolves.
Background & DDx

Differential Diagnosis

Otitis Media with Effusion (Glue Ear)
Chronic Suppurative OM
Otitis Externa
Foreign body / Wax

Prognosis

60% improve in 24h without antibiotics. Most recover within 3 days. Complications (mastoiditis) are rare.
