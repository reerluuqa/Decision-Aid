Meningitis Quick Ref

Based on NICE CKS (Aug 2025)
Overview

Definition: Inflammation of the meninges (brain/spinal cord membranes). Life-threatening.

Meningococcal Disease: Infection with Neisseria meningitidis (includes meningitis + septicaemia).

Key Facts:
• Most common in babies & children.
• Transmission: Aerosol/Droplets.
• Incubation: 3–5 days.

Common Causes
  • Neisseria meningitidis (Meningococcus)
  • Streptococcus pneumoniae (Pneumococcus)
  • Group B Streptococcus (Neonates)
  • Haemophilus influenzae (Rare due to vaccine)
Risk Factors
  • Young age (Highest < 2 years)
  • Winter season
  • Adolescents/Early adulthood
  • Immunocompromised (e.g., HIV, Chemo)
  • Asplenia / Sickle cell
  • Cochlear implants / CSF leak
  • Smoking / Student halls
Suspect Bacterial Meningitis

Be aware: Presentation can be non-specific, especially in babies/elderly.

Red Flag Combination

Fever
Headache
Neck Stiffness
Altered Consciousness
REFER IMMEDIATELY Do not wait for full symptom evolution. Send to hospital.
Suspect Meningococcal Disease

Look for signs of sepsis and shock alongside rash.

Key Red Flag

Haemorrhagic Non-Blanching Rash

(Check conjunctivae, difficult to see on dark skin)

  • Pale / Mottled skin
  • Lethargy / Irritability
  • Seizures
  • Cold extremities
GIVE PARENTERAL ANTIBIOTICS If immediate transfer to hospital is not possible (e.g. Benzylpenicillin or Ceftriaxone).
Clinical Diagnosis Guide
Category Babies / Children Adults
Appearance Bulging fontanelle, Ill appearance Ill appearance (Fever less common in >65s)
Behaviour Irritability, Lethargy, Weak/high-pitched cry, Reduced feeding Lethargy, Confusion (Delirium), Agitation
Rash Non-blanching petechial/purpuric (Meningococcal) Non-blanching petechial/purpuric (Meningococcal)
Cardio/Resp Tachypnoea, Apnoea, Grunting, Shock signs Shock signs, Tachycardia
Neuro Seizures, Focal neuro deficits Seizures, Focal neuro deficits
Other Unexplained body pain, Vomiting Photophobia, Neck stiffness (harder in dementia/arthritis)

* Always consider antipyretic use masking fever. * Neck stiffness less common in babies.

Complications
  • Hearing Loss (Common)
  • Cognitive Impairment
  • Motor Deficits
  • Seizures
  • Amputations (Septicaemia)
  • Hydrocephalus
Prognosis

Excellent with prompt antimicrobial treatment.

Poor Prognosis Factors:

  • Extremes of age
  • Delay in treatment
  • Low GCS on admission
  • Coma / Shock
  • Pneumococcal cause

Source: NICE CKS [Meningitis - bacterial meningitis and meningococcal disease]. Last revised Aug 2025.
