Turner Syndrome

Quick-Scan Reference Tool
Updated 2025
Critical Alerts & Immediate Actions
NEONATAL URGENT
Congenital Heart Failure & Lymphoedema
  • Signs: Generalised oedema, webbed neck, severe upper body hypertension, Left Ventricular failure.
  • Risk: Hypoplastic left heart or critical coarctation.
  • Action: Urgent Cardiology consult & Echocardiogram.
LIFE THREATENING
Aortic Dissection / Coarctation
  • Signs: Upper extremity hypertension, weak femoral pulses, systolic murmur/click.
  • Risk Factor: Bicuspid Aortic Valve (30% of pts) & Hemizygous TIMP1/TIMP3 genes.
  • Action: BP in all 4 limbs. STAT MRI Angiography if BP discrepancy >20mmHg.
Recurrent Otitis Media
Severe or frequent bouts in childhood. May indicate anatomical Eustachian tube issues.

Ref: ENT / Audiology.
Diagnosis & Screening
Core Definition

Phenotypic female with complete or partial absence of the second sex chromosome.

Key Features: Short stature, Premature ovarian failure.
Gold Standard: Karyotype
Peripheral blood WBC karyotype (min 30 cells).
45,X~60% (Non-mosaic)
Mosaic~15% (45,X/46,XX)
StructuralisoXq, delXp, Ring Y
*Confirm with postnatal karyotype even if antenatal screen positive.
Mandatory Screening
  • Renal: Ultrasound (Horseshoe/agenesis ~25%).
  • Cardiac: Echo & MRI (Aortic root, BAV).
  • Thyroid: TFTs & Antibodies (Autoimmune risk).
  • Hearing: Formal audiologic assessment.
  • Eyes: Strabismus, refractive errors.
Key Differentials
Condition Differentiating Signs Key Test Result
Noonan Syndrome Hypertrophic cardiomyopathy, pulmonic stenosis, triangular facies. Normal Karyotype (46,XX or 46,XY).
Constitutional Delay No dysmorphic features, no congenital defects. Normal FSH/AMH, delayed bone age.
46,XX Gonadal Dysgenesis No short stature, no heart defects. Normal Karyotype 46,XX.
Management Plan
Short Stature Rx
  • Growth Hormone (GH): Start early (often <6yrs) to maximize height potential.
  • Monitoring: Track height velocity vs Turner-specific growth charts.
  • Oxandrolone: Consider in older children if GH suboptimal (specialist decision).
Puberty Induction
  • Estrogen: Start around 11-12 years (or older if diagnosed late). Low dose titration.
  • Progesterone: Add after 1-2 years of estrogen or after breakthrough bleeding.
  • Fertility: Spontaneous puberty in ~15%. Most require ART (Egg donation).
Lifelong Cardio
  • Hypertension: Monitor & treat aggressively.
  • Dissection: Pregnancy is high-risk. Pre-conception cardiac MRI mandatory.
  • Valves: Endocarditis prophylaxis if indicated.
Clinical Features Checklist
Common (>30%)
  • Short Stature (Primary)
  • Bicuspid Aortic Valve (30%)
  • Delayed Puberty
  • Primary Amenorrhoea
  • Renal Anomalies
Dysmorphic (Variable)
  • Webbed Neck (Cystic Hygroma sequelae)
  • Low-set ears / Posterior hairline
  • High-arched palate
  • Multiple melanocytic naevi
  • Down-sloping eyes / Ptosis
Skeletal
  • Cubitus Valgus (wide carrying angle)
  • Short 4th Metacarpal
  • Scoliosis
  • Madelung deformity (~5%)
Neurocognitive
  • Normal intelligence
  • Verbal > Visual-spatial
  • Social difficulties
  • Non-verbal learning disorder
