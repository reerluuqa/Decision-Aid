Child Maltreatment

Recognition & Management
Revised: May 2023

Recognition: Red Flags

Physical Abuse

Critical
  • Bruises: In shape of hand/ligature; on non-mobile infants; on ankles/wrists/neck/eyes/ears; or multiple clustered bruises.
  • Burns: Shape of implement (cigarette/iron); glove/stocking distribution; clear boundaries (forced immersion).
  • Fractures: In absence of medical condition (e.g. osteogenesis imperfecta); fractures of different ages; rib fractures in infants.
  • Head/Spine: Intracranial injury in <3yr without major trauma; retinal hemorrhages; spinal injury without accident history.

Sexual Abuse

Critical
  • Pregnancy: In any female under 16 years of age (or under 13 is automatic crime).
  • STIs: Presence of sexually transmitted infections.
  • Behavior: Sexualized behavior inappropriate for age/development.
  • Anogenital: Injury, bleeding, or discharge without adequate medical explanation.

Neglect

High Risk
  • Physical: Persistent failure to meet basic needs (food, clothing, shelter, hygiene).
  • Medical: Non-attendance at hospital appointments; failure to administer essential prescribed meds.
  • Growth: Faltering growth or severe malnutrition not explained by medical condition.
  • Education: Poor school attendance with no health justification.

Emotional & FII

Complex
  • FII (Fabricated Illness): Symptoms reported/induced by parent; unexplained discrepancy between signs/history; child becoming "expert patient".
  • Emotional: Marked changes in behavior/emotional state not explained by medical condition; self-harm; persistent harmful interaction from parent.

Management & Actions

1

Suspect Abuse

If you suspect abuse or neglect, refer to children's social care immediately. Do not delay.

If immediate danger → Police / Emergency.

2

Listen & Record

Listen to the child without judgment. Do not promise confidentiality. Document accurately using their own words.

3

Medical Care

Treat medical needs. If urgent forensic evidence is needed, consider referral to a SARC (Sexual Assault Referral Centre) or Pediatrician.

Risk Factors Context

Child Factors

  • Age under 4 years or adolescence.
  • Physical and/or mental impairment (disability).
  • Living in the care system.
  • Being a twin or multiple.
  • LGBTQ+ identity.

Parent/Carer Factors

  • Substance misuse.
  • History of domestic abuse/violence.
  • Mental or neurological disorders.
  • Emotional volatility / Anger issues.
  • History of being abused themselves.

Family/Environment

  • Poverty and financial pressures.
  • Family/relationship breakdown.
  • Violence between family members.
  • Unemployment / Poor housing.

Definitions

Abuse of children under 18. Includes inflicting harm or failing to act to prevent harm. For this topic: Child = <13 years, Young Person = 13–17 years.

Hitting, shaking, throwing, poisoning, burning, scalding, drowning, suffocating, or causing physical harm. Also includes Fabricated or Induced Illness.

Persistently treating a child in a way that causes severe adverse effects on emotional development. Includes conveying worthlessness, silencing, making fun of them, or inappropriate expectations.

Child harmed due to parent/carer behavior to convince doctors of illness. Results in emotional/physical abuse and iatrogenic harm.

Based on NICE Clinical Knowledge Summaries (CKS). Last revised May 2023.
