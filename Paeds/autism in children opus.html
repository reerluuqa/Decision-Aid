DIFFERENTIAL DIAGNOSIS ‚Äî Conditions with overlapping features
üß† Neurodevelopmental Disorders
Specific Language Delay
Normal non-verbal communication, imagination, play; relative strength in social interaction
Learning Disability
Delay across ALL areas; imitation present; social interest consistent with developmental age
DCD (Dyspraxia)
Motor planning difficulties; normal play and language
Stereotypic Movement
Repetitive motor behaviour; not explained by another disorder
üìâ Regression Disorders
Rett Syndrome
Mainly girls; motor regression, ataxia, hand-wringing, oro-motor problems
Epileptic Encephalopathy
Onset 2-7y over days; loss of words and language understanding; social interest preserved
üß© Mental Health & Behavioural
Social Communication Disorder
Social language deficits BUT no restricted/repetitive behaviours
ADHD
Can show social reciprocity; understands social norms; behaviour driven by impulsivity
Depression
Episodic; "normal" social behaviour when not depressed
Anxiety
Repetitive but not stereotyped; cares about others' opinions
OCD
Onset >4y; behaviours cause distress; harm-avoidance theme
ODD
Understands behaviour unacceptable but persists; competent in social interaction
Conduct Disorder
Social competence present; sophisticated avoidance strategies
Psychosis
Later onset after normal development; hallucinations/delusions

üìñ Definition

Autism describes a pattern of qualitative differences and impairments in:

  • Reciprocal social interaction
  • Social communication
  • Restricted interests
  • Rigid and repetitive behaviours

May be associated with cognitive, learning, language, medical, emotional, and behavioural problems.

ICD-11 DSM-5 criteria apply

üìà Prognosis
  • ‚Ä¢Lifelong condition; cannot be cured
  • ‚Ä¢Trajectory unpredictable at diagnosis
  • ‚Ä¢Core symptoms often decrease with age
  • ‚Ä¢Early diagnosis improves outcomes
Positive predictors: Language development, IQ in/around average range
