MEDICAL EMERGENCY

Immediate IV Resuscitation Required. Do not attempt Oral Rehydration initially.

Red Flags & Bacterial Suggestors

  • High Fever >39°C (102.2°F)
    Suggests bacterial etiology.
  • Bloody Diarrhoea
    Bacterial cause likely (e.g., Salmonella, Shigella).
  • Significant Abdominal Tenderness
    Consider surgical abdomen (Appendicitis) or invasive bacteria.
  • Tenemus / Toxicity
    Signs of severe systemic infection.

Typical Viral Features

  • Watery, non-bloody stools
    Rotavirus / Norovirus common.
  • Low-grade or No fever
    Vomiting often precedes diarrhoea.
  • Age 6-24 Months
    Peak incidence for Rotavirus.
  • Daycare/History of Exposure
    Faecal-oral transmission common.

Management Protocol

1. Early Re-feeding

Continue age-appropriate diet immediately. Breastfeeding should continue. Do NOT dilute formula. Complex carbs (rice, bread) preferred.

2. Ongoing Losses

Weight <10kg: Add 60-120mL ORS per stool/vomit.
Weight >10kg: Add 120-240mL ORS per stool/vomit.

3. Antiemetics (Ondansetron)

Consider if vomiting interferes with ORT. Oral single dose preferred. Use with caution if diarrhoea is predominant.

4. IV Fluids (If ORT Contraindicated)

Shock/Severe Dehydration: 20mL/kg 0.9% Saline/Ringer's over 1 hour.
Mild/Mod: Deficit replaced over 4 hours (IV or NG).

Clinical Reference

Aetiology & Causes

Rotavirus: Most severe in 6-24 months. Characteristic "wheel" shape. Prior to vaccine, leading cause.

Norovirus: Currently most common cause of viral GE. Outbreaks common (food/water).

Adenovirus: Can cause outbreaks. Asymptomatic excretion possible.

Differential Diagnosis
  • Bacterial GE: Fever >39°C, bloody stool.
  • Intussusception: Colicky pain, redcurrant jelly stool, palpable mass.
  • Appendicitis: Pain at McBurney's, tenderness, rebound.
  • Antibiotic-associated: History of recent antibiotics (C. diff).
Pathophysiology

Virus destroys mature enterocytes (villous tips) sparing crypts. Leads to decreased absorption, increased secretion (osmotic/secretory diarrhoea), and transient disaccharidase deficiency.
