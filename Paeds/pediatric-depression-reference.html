IMMEDIATE ACTION REQUIRED - Assess Safety at Every Encounter

ü©∏ Active Suicidal Ideation

Ask about thoughts, plans, intent, and access to lethal means. Screen privately without carers present. If imminent risk ‚Üí Emergency Department referral.

üî™ Self-Harm with Plan

Any self-harm with plan or intent requires urgent psychiatric evaluation. Initiate safety planning immediately.

üß† Psychotic Features

Hallucinations or delusions (mood-congruent or incongruent) require urgent specialist referral and possible hospitalization.

üìµ Unable to Maintain Safety

If patient cannot guarantee safety or has made recent attempt ‚Üí Immediate ED referral for urgent mental health assessment.

Requires: 5+ symptoms, 2 weeks duration

Must have at least one: Depressed OR Irritable mood OR Anhedonia (loss of interest/pleasure)

    Most of the day, nearly every day

  • 2

  • 3

  • 4

  • 5
    Psychomotor changes

    Agitation OR Retardation

  • 6

    Nearly every day

  • 7
    Feelings of worthlessness/guilt

    Excessive or inappropriate guilt

  • 8
    Diminished concentration

    Indecisiveness

  • 9

    Suicidal ideation, plan, or attempt

Significant functional impairment in school, social, or family settings

üíä Treatment Algorithm

1

Active Monitoring (Mild Depression)

6 weeks of watchful waiting with supportive care and psychoeducation for child and parents. Often resolves spontaneously. If no improvement or worsening ‚Üí escalate.

2

Specific Psychotherapy (Mild-Moderate)

First-line: CBT, IPT (Interpersonal Therapy), or ABFT (Attachment-based Family Therapy - UK). Consider digital CBT. CBT has long-term prevention effects.

3

SSRI ¬± Psychotherapy (Moderate-Severe)

Combination therapy preferred over either alone. US guidelines allow SSRI monotherapy; UK recommends combination. Fluoxetine is first-line SSRI.

First-Line Medications ‚ñº

üíä Fluoxetine (FIRST CHOICE)

Best evidence base in children and adolescents. Longest half-life (4-6 days). Potent CYP2D6/2C19 inhibitor.

Dose: Start 10mg daily, may increase to 20mg after 1-2 weeks if inadequate response. Max 60mg daily.

üíä Escitalopram (Adolescents Only)

Positive trial results in adolescents (not children). Active S-enantiomer of citalopram. Half-life 27-32 hours.

Dose: Start 5-10mg daily. Max 20mg daily.

üíä Sertraline

Stronger evidence for anxiety/OCD than depression. Moderate P450 enzyme inhibitor.

Dose: Start 25mg daily. Usual range 50-200mg daily.
NOT Recommended ‚ñº
  • ‚úï
    Paroxetine

    Lack of efficacy and poor tolerability in children. Not recommended.

  • ‚úï
    Tricyclic Antidepressants

    Limited evidence, significant side effect profile. Not recommended as first-line.

  • ‚ö†
    Venlafaxine

    Not well studied in children. SNRIs may have higher risk than SSRIs.

‚ö†Ô∏è Black Box Warning

FDA warning: Antidepressants increase suicidal ideation in children/adolescents. Monitor closely, especially in first 4-8 weeks.

üîÑ Differential Diagnosis

Key Differentials & Distinguishing Features ‚ñº

üü¢ Bipolar Disorder Rule Out

Key Features: Elevated mood, decreased need for sleep, grandiosity, pressured speech, reckless behavior. Low threshold for suspicion - bipolar often presents initially as depression.

Action: Screen for manic/hypomanic symptoms. Family history of bipolar disorder increases risk.

üü° Anxiety Disorder Common Comorbidity

Key Features: Anxiety symptoms precede and occur independently of mood symptoms. Dysphoria dissipates when anxiety-provoking situation ends.

Prevalence: 73.8% of depressed children also have anxiety. Both should be assessed and treated.

üîµ ADHD Common Comorbidity

Key Features: Poor concentration is chronic and precedes mood symptoms. May be side effect of stimulants or demoralization from difficulties.

Action: ADHD diagnosed when full criteria met before age 12. Poor concentration must represent CHANGE from baseline for depression diagnosis.

‚ö™ Substance Use Disorder Rule Out

Key Features: Substance use may precede OR result from depression. MDD diagnosis should NOT be made if symptoms are direct effect of substance.

Action: Urine drug screen if indicated. Screen for substance use in all adolescents with depression.

‚ö™ Bereavement/Adjustment Disorder Rule Out

Key Features: Bereavement - loss ALWAYS precedes symptoms. Adjustment disorder - stressor ALWAYS precedes symptoms. Symptoms do NOT meet full MDD criteria.

Note: Symptoms may increase risk of self-medication with substances.

‚ö™ Medical Conditions Rule Out

Consider: Thyroid dysfunction, anemia, vitamin deficiencies (B12, folate, D), infectious mononucleosis, chronic illnesses

Recommended Labs: CBC with differential, TSH, vitamin B12, folate, vitamin D

üìÖ Follow-up & Monitoring

Acute Phase (0-12 weeks)

  • Weekly or biweekly follow-up
  • Monitor for treatment response
  • Watch for emergence of suicidality
  • Assess medication side effects
  • Safety assessment each visit

Continuation Phase (6-12 months)

  • Monthly monitoring
  • Continue effective treatment
  • Relapse rates ~50% without continuation
  • Assess functional recovery
  • Family/School involvement

Maintenance Phase (1-2 years)

  • Consider for high-risk patients
  • Genetic loading, chronic/severe episodes
  • Gradual taper if stable
  • Ongoing relapse prevention
  • Lifestyle optimization

Key Monitoring Points

‚úì Treatment Response: Depression rating scales (PHQ-9, MFQ, CDI) to measure change over time

‚úì Suicidality: Assess at EVERY encounter - this is a SYMPTOM of the disorder

‚úì Side Effects: Monitor for headaches, nausea, insomnia, agitation, sexual effects

‚úì Functional Recovery: School performance, social relationships, family functioning
