Key History Points

  • Age of onset & toilet training history
  • Frequency of soiling (Home vs School)
  • Stool withholding behaviors (buttock tightening)
  • Painful bowel movements
  • Associated bladder dysfunction / enuresis
  • Dietary fiber/fluid intake

Physical Exam Focus

  • Abdomen: Palpate for fecal mass
  • Perianal: Inspect for soiling, fissures, fistulae
  • Rectal: Tone, impacted stool (distinguish retentive vs non-retentive)
  • Spine: Inspect for sacral dimple, hair tuft (dysraphism)
  • Neuro: Lower extremity reflexes

Laboratory & Radiology

Test Indication
Abdominal X-Ray Baseline evidence of retention if rectal exam refused/inconclusive
TSH / Free T4 If signs of hypothyroidism
Sweat Chloride If cystic fibrosis suspected (esp. with rectal prolapse)
tTG IgA If celiac disease suspected
Barium Enema Suspected Hirschsprung's (transition zone)
