Scarlet Fever

Quick Clinical Reference & Decision Support

Last revised: September 2024 (NICE CKS)
⚠️ Red Flags Diagnosis Management Prescribing

Admit Urgently

Arrange immediate hospital admission if:

  • Severe symptoms unable to be managed in primary care.
  • Suspected serious or life-threatening complication (e.g., iGAS, necrotizing fasciitis, pneumonia, sepsis).
  • High risk of developing complications based on clinical judgement.
High Risk iGAS Factors

Be alert for complications in:

  • Age ≥75 years or Neonates (≤28 days).
  • Immunocompromised / Immunosuppressed.
  • Chickenpox (active lesions within 7 days prior).
  • Pregnant / Postpartum (within 28 days).
  • Comorbidities: skin breakdown, diabetes, cardiovascular disease, malignancy.

Diagnosis & Clinical Features

Typical Presentation
  • Sore throat & Fever (>38.3°C).
  • Headache, fatigue, nausea/vomiting.
  • Pharyngitis & Petechiae on soft palate.
  • Cervical lymphadenopathy.
Rash Characteristics
  • Onset: Usually within 48h, starts on chest/stomach.
  • Texture: Sandpaper-like, pinpoint (punctate).
  • Pastia's lines: Deep red linear appearance in skin folds (neck, axillae, groin).
  • Palms/soles typically spared.
  • Peeling of skin (fingers/toes) after rash resolves.
Key Signs
  • Strawberry Tongue: White coating (early) -> Red/Swollen (peels later).
  • Flushed Face: Marked circumoral pallor.
  • Note: On brown/black skin, the redness may be harder to see; rely on texture (sandpaper) and Pastia's lines.

Management Protocol

Antibiotic Treatment

Prescribe appropriate antibiotics promptly.

First-Line (No Allergy):
Phenoxymethylpenicillin (Penicillin V) for 10 days.
Penicillin Allergy:
  • 0-6 months: Clarithromycin (10 days).
  • 6mo - 17y (Non-pregnant): Azithromycin (5 days) OR Clarithromycin (10 days).
  • Pregnant / Postpartum: Erythromycin (10 days).
Public Health & Advice
  • Notify: Notify local Health Protection Team promptly (within 3 days) of suspected diagnosis.
  • Exclusion: Exclude from nursery/school/work for 24 hours after starting appropriate antibiotics.
  • Hygiene: Advise hand hygiene, no sharing utensils/towels, dispose of tissues.
Safety Netting:
  • Seek help if symptoms worsen or not improved after 7 days.
  • Seek immediate advice for suspected complications (e.g., persistent fever, dehydration, breathing difficulty).

Prescribing Information

Dosage (10 days)

Adults500 mg four times daily OR 1000 mg twice daily
1-11 months62.5 mg 4x daily OR 125 mg twice daily
1-5 years125 mg 4x daily OR 250 mg twice daily
6-11 years250 mg 4x daily OR 500 mg twice daily
12-17 years500 mg 4x daily OR 1000 mg twice daily

Cautions & Interactions

Do NOT prescribe: History of anaphylaxis/urticaria/immediate rash to penicillin.

Caution: Atopic allergy (asthma, eczema), cephalosporin sensitivity, markedly impaired renal function.

Interactions: Take on empty stomach (1 hour before food). Monitor INR if on warfarin.

Pregnancy/Breastfeeding: Safe to use.

Dosage (5 days)

Adults500 mg once daily
Child 6mo-11y12 mg/kg once daily (max 500 mg)
Child 12-17y500 mg once daily

Cautions & Interactions

Caution: QT prolongation risk, hepatic impairment, severe renal impairment, myasthenia gravis.

Key Interactions:

  • Avoid antacids (take 1h before or 2h after).
  • Warfarin: Monitor INR.
  • Statins: Increased risk of myopathy.
  • Avoid with drugs prolonging QT (e.g., domperidone, hydroxyzine).

Pregnancy: Use only if clinically needed/benefit outweighs risk.

Dosage (10 days)

Adults250–500 mg twice daily
Child 1mo-11yDose by weight (e.g., 30-40kg: 250mg twice daily)
Child 12-17y250–500 mg twice daily

See BNF for specific pediatric weight banding.

Cautions & Interactions

Do NOT prescribe: QT prolongation history, severe hepatic + renal impairment.

Key Interactions:

  • Statins: Stop Simvastatin/Lovastatin. Reduce Atorvastatin.
  • Warfarin/DOACs: Monitor for bleeding.
  • Contraindicated: Ivabradine, Eletriptan, Ergot alkaloids.
  • Avoid colchicine.

Pregnancy: Avoid (especially 1st trimester) unless benefits outweigh risks.

Dosage (10 days)

Pregnant/Postpartum250–500 mg four times daily

Cautions & Interactions

Do NOT prescribe: Acute porphyrias, QT prolongation, electrolyte disturbances.

Key Interactions:

  • Theophylline: Monitor levels/toxicity.
  • Statins: Stop Simvastatin/Lovastatin.
  • Warfarin: Monitor INR.
  • Avoid with drugs prolonging QT (Domperidone, etc.).

Pregnancy: Use only if clinically needed/benefit outweighs risk.

Differential Diagnosis

Viral Exanthems

  • Measles: Conjunctivitis, coryza, cough, rash starts head/neck.
  • Rubella: Transient rash, lymphadenopathy (suboccipital/posterior).
  • Parvovirus B19: "Slapped cheek" appearance.
  • Roseola Infantum: High fever drops as rash appears (trunk).

Other Conditions

  • Kawasaki Disease: Fever >5 days, cracked lips, strawberry tongue, extremity changes.
  • Glandular Fever: Fatigue, malaise, negative strep swab.
  • Staphylococcal TSS: Diffuse erythroderma, hypotension, multi-organ involvement.
  • Drug reaction: Recent medication changes.
