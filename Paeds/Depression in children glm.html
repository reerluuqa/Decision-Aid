Depression in Children & Young People

Quick Reference Algorithm (Ages 5-18 Years)

ASSESSMENT

History & Symptoms

  • Take history from child/young person AND parent/carer
  • Consider seeing child/young person alone
  • Ask about key symptoms: Persistent sadness/irritability OR loss of interest/pleasure
  • Ask about associated symptoms: Reduced concentration, low self-esteem, guilt, hopelessness, suicidal thoughts, sleep disturbance, appetite changes, fatigue
  • Duration: Symptoms present ≥2 weeks
  • Functioning: Impact on home, school, social settings
  • Risk factors: Family history, recent negative events, trauma, bullying, chronic illness

Examination

  • Look for signs of self-neglect, self-harm, neglect, or abuse
  • Rule out other conditions (anemia, hypothyroidism, infections)
  • Mental state examination

DIAGNOSIS & SEVERITY

Diagnosis Criteria

  • ≥5 symptoms present for ≥2 weeks
  • Must include depressed mood/irritability OR loss of interest/pleasure
  • Significant impairment in functioning
  • Not better explained by other conditions

Severity Classification

Mild

No intense symptoms, some difficulty functioning

Moderate

Several marked symptoms, considerable difficulty functioning

Severe

Many marked/intense symptoms, serious difficulty functioning (may include psychotic features)

MANAGEMENT

Mild Depression

Watchful waiting if appropriate
  • Follow up in 2 weeks
  • Contact if non-attendance
Continuing symptoms: Offer psychological intervention
  • Delivered by trained therapists in schools, primary care, etc.
  • Discuss options based on individual needs/preferences

Moderate to Severe Depression

Refer to CAMHS (Child and Adolescent Mental Health Services)
  • Severe depression with suicide risk: Assess within 24 hours
  • Severe depression without suicide risk: Assess within 2 weeks
Treatment (usually coordinated by CAMHS)
  • Psychological therapy as first-line
  • Consider antidepressants (fluoxetine first-line) + psychological therapy
  • Monitor closely for suicidality, especially when starting medication

URGENT REFERRAL

Refer immediately if:

  • High risk of suicide
  • Severe self-neglect
  • Psychotic symptoms

INFORMATION & ADVICE

  • Provide age-appropriate information about depression and treatment
  • Involve family/carers in treatment decisions
  • Consider confidentiality, consent, and safeguarding issues
  • Build supportive relationship with child/young person and family

Based on NICE CKS Depression in children (March 2025)
