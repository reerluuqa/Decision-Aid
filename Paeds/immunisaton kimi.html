Childhood Immunizations Algorithm

Interactive clinical decision support tool based on NICE CKS guidelines (June 2025) for managing childhood immunizations from age 2 months to 25 years.

Assessment Core Evaluation

Age Determination

Identify the child's current age and immunization status to determine the appropriate schedule.

Infants (< 1 year)

Primary immunization series - most critical period for vaccine administration

Toddlers (1-2 years)

Booster doses and missed dose catch-up

Children (2-10 years)

Annual influenza and scheduled boosters

Adolescents (10-25 years)

HPV, MenACWY, and Td/IPV vaccinations

Immunization History Review

Gather complete vaccination history from:

  • Personal Child Health Record (Red Book)
  • GP records and Child Health Information System (CHIS)
  • Parent/carer recall and previous vaccination cards
  • UK and international schedules comparison tool for children born abroad
Red Flags & Contraindications Critical
Absolute Contraindications:
  • Severe allergic reaction (anaphylaxis) to previous vaccine dose
  • Significant immunosuppression (live vaccines)
  • SCID screening must be checked before BCG or rotavirus vaccines
  • Infants exposed to infliximab during pregnancy (live vaccines contraindicated up to 12 months)
Precautions:
  • Acute severe illness (postpone vaccination)
  • Premature infants <32 weeks gestation and <4kg (adjust paracetamol dose)
  • Bleeding disorders (use subcutaneous route)
  • Phenylketonuria (caution with Revaxis®)
Unknown History Protocol

If immunization history cannot be verified:

  • Assume unvaccinated and plan catch-up schedule
  • Do NOT repeat doses if documentation confirms previous administration
  • Check maternal hepatitis B status for infants
  • Verify SCID screening result before live vaccines

Missed Doses Protocol

Key Principle: Continue schedule, do NOT restart. Space catch-up doses at minimum intervals:

Under 1 Year

  • DTaP/IPV/Hib/HepB: Give missed doses, 4 weeks apart
  • PCV: Single dose ≥1 month after any previous dose
  • MenB: If <2 doses in first year, give 2 doses 4 weeks apart in second year
  • Rotavirus: Do not give if child >24 weeks old
  • MMR: Give first dose, then second at 18 months or 3y4m

1-2 Years

Complete primary series, then continue with boosters

2-10 Years

  • DTaP/IPV/Hib/HepB: Complete 3-dose primary series 4 weeks apart
  • MMR: Ensure 2 doses, 4 weeks apart minimum
  • DTaP/IPV booster: 1 year after completing primary series

Over 10 Years

  • Td/IPV: 3 doses 4 weeks apart, then boosters at 5 and 10 years
  • MMR: 2 doses 4 weeks apart if no history
  • MenACWY: Single dose if aged 10-25 with incomplete MenC
  • HPV: Single dose if under 25 (3 doses if immunosuppressed)
High-Risk Children Additional Vaccines

Children requiring extra immunizations beyond routine schedule:

Chronic Respiratory/Heart Disease: PCV13/PPV23 + Annual flu
Asplenia/Splenic Dysfunction: MenACWY, MenB, PCV13/PPV23, Annual flu
Chronic Kidney/Liver Disease: Hepatitis A & B, PCV13/PPV23, Annual flu
Travel Vaccines: Consider hepatitis A, typhoid, yellow fever for endemic areas

Treatment Administration

Pre-Vaccination Checklist
  • Obtain verbal consent from person with parental responsibility
  • Check for contraindications (immunosuppression, severe allergy)
  • Verify SCID screening result before live vaccines
  • Confirm vaccine correct, stored properly, not expired
  • Provide UKHSA leaflets: "What to expect after vaccinations"
  • Discuss possible adverse effects and management
Administration Guidelines

Infants & Toddlers (< 2 years)

  • Route: Intramuscular (IM) anterolateral thigh
  • Needle: 25mm 23-gauge (blue) or 25-gauge (orange)
  • MenB: Left thigh, ideally separate from other vaccines
  • Multiple vaccines: Separate limbs or sites ≥2.5cm apart
  • Rotavirus: Oral administration towards inner cheek
  • Do NOT: Inject into buttocks

Older Children (> 2 years)

  • Route: IM deltoid or anterolateral thigh
  • BCG: Intradermal at designated centres
  • Varicella: Deep subcutaneous or IM
  • Bleeding disorders: Use subcutaneous route
Paracetamol Prophylaxis MenB Only

Recommended for infants receiving MenB vaccine to reduce fever risk:

Dosing (Infant paracetamol suspension 120mg/5mL):
• Dose 1: 2.5mL (60mg) immediately after vaccination
• Dose 2: 2.5mL (60mg) 4-6 hours later
• Dose 3: 2.5mL (60mg) 4-6 hours after second dose
For infants <32 weeks gestation & <4kg: dose by weight

Do NOT routinely give paracetamol for other vaccines unless fever/pain problematic after vaccination

Egg Allergy Considerations
  • MMR: Can be given safely to egg-allergic children
  • Influenza: Egg allergy may increase reaction risk - check vaccine-specific guidance
  • Observation: Consider 60-minute post-vaccination observation for severe egg allergy
Documentation Requirements

Record: Date, vaccine name, product, batch number, expiry date, dose, route, site, and administration method

Follow-up Ongoing Care

Immediate Post-Vaccination Observation
  • Observe for immediate adverse reactions (especially anaphylaxis)
  • Ensure bleeding has stopped from injection site
  • Check for signs of anaphylaxis before leaving premises
  • Reassure parents/carers about common mild reactions
  • Provide contact information for urgent concerns
Anaphylaxis: Usually occurs within minutes. Ensure adrenaline is available and staff trained in emergency management.
Future Dose Scheduling

Ensure robust recall system for upcoming vaccinations:

  • Schedule next appointment before family leaves
  • Send reminders via preferred method (SMS, email, phone)
  • Flag incomplete schedules in patient record
  • Review immunization status at every clinical encounter
  • Follow up non-attenders promptly
Monitoring & Reporting
Common Expected Reactions:
• Pain, swelling, redness at injection site (1-2 days)
• Mild fever, irritability, tiredness
• Loss of appetite
• Temporary behavior changes
Report Immediately:
• Severe allergic reaction or anaphylaxis
• Fever >39°C lasting >48 hours
• Persistent inconsolable crying >3 hours
• Seizures or neurological symptoms
• Signs of intussusception after rotavirus (severe abdominal pain, vomiting, blood in stool)
Public Health Responsibilities
  • Report adverse reactions via MHRA Yellow Card scheme
  • Update CHIS and GP records within 24 hours
  • Contribute to COVER (Coverage of Vaccination Evaluated Rapidly) statistics
  • Participate in outbreak response if vaccine-preventable disease identified
  • Support vaccination catch-up campaigns for under-immunized populations
Long-term Protection

Benefits of completed immunization schedule:

  • Diphtheria: Virtual eradication in UK since 1940s vaccination program
  • Measles: 95% coverage needed to maintain herd immunity and prevent outbreaks
  • Meningococcal B: 75% reduction in incidence among vaccinated cohorts
  • Pertussis: Maternal vaccination provides 90% protection for infants <3 months
  • Polio: Near-global eradication maintained through continued vaccination
Parent/Carer Education

Provide written and verbal information about:

  • Benefits and risks of vaccination
  • What to expect after vaccination
  • How to manage common side effects at home
  • When and how to seek medical help
  • Importance of completing vaccine schedule
