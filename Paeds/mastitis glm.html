Mastitis & Breast Abscess

Clinical Reference Tool

Last Revised: March 2025

IMMEDIATE RED FLAGS

  • Sepsis Signs: Tachycardia, fever, chills, haemodynamic instability.
  • Rapid Progression: Infection spreading quickly.
  • Suspected Abscess: Fluctuant lump, skin discoloration, persistent hardness.
  • Suspected Malignancy: Underlying mass or features of inflammatory breast cancer.
Actions: Arrange hospital admission if septic/immunocompromised. Arrange Urgent 2-week wait referral if cancer suspected.

Suspect Mastitis

  • Painful breast + Fever/Malaise.
  • Tender, red, swollen, wedge-shaped area.
  • Usually unilateral.
Note: Most cases are non-infectious initially. Treat with conservative measures first unless signs of infection are clear.

Signs of Infection

Consider antibiotics if:

  • No improvement after 12-24 hrs of effective milk removal/rest.
  • Infected nipple fissure.
  • Purulent discharge.
  • Spreading cellulitis.

First-Line Actions

  • Continue Breastfeeding: From affected breast if possible. It helps drainage.
  • If too painful: Hand express to match infant's needs (avoid pumps if worsening oedema).
  • Analgesia: Paracetamol or Ibuprofen.
  • Cold Compresses: 10 mins hourly if swollen/inflamed.
  • Rest & Supportive Bra: Reduce pressure on breast.

Antibiotics?

Only if no improvement in 12-24hrs or clear signs of infection:

  • 1st Line: Flucloxacillin 500mg qds (10-14 days).
  • Penicillin Allergy: Erythromycin 250-500mg qds OR Clarithromycin 500mg bd.
View Treatment Failure Protocol

If no improvement after 48 hours:

  • Send breast milk culture if not done.
  • Switch to Co-amoxiclav 500/125mg tds (10-14 days).
  • Check for abscess or alternative diagnosis.

Preventing Recurrence

Technique
  • Ensure correct infant attachment.
  • Feed on demand.
  • Avoid missed feeds.
  • Finish first breast before offering second.
Hygiene
  • Frequent hand washing.
  • Wash pump parts thoroughly.
  • Discard contaminated creams.

General Measures

  • Analgesia: Paracetamol or Ibuprofen.
  • Temperature: Cold compresses or warm shower/bath for pain relief.
  • Identify Cause: Check for nipple damage (eczema, psoriasis, Raynaud's).

Antibiotics (For ALL)

Unlike lactating mastitis, treat all cases:

  • 1st Line: Co-amoxiclav 500/125mg tds (10-14 days).
  • Penicillin Allergy: Erythromycin/Clarithromycin PLUS Metronidazole 400mg tds (10-14 days).

Key Prevention: Smoking Cessation

Smoking is a major risk factor for periductal mastitis. Nicotine accumulation damages ducts.

Advise stopping smoking. Refer to smoking cessation services.

Other measures: Frequent hand washing, remove nipple rings (potential bacterial contamination).

URGENT REFERRAL REQUIRED

Refer urgently to General Surgeon for:

  • Ultrasound confirmation.
  • Drainage (Ultrasound-guided needle aspiration or surgical).
  • Culture of fluid.
Do not manage abscesses solely with antibiotics in primary care.

During Referral

For Lactating Women:

  • Continue breastfeeding if possible.
  • If too painful, express milk (hand or pump) to maintain supply and prevent stasis.

Contraception Advice

Additional contraceptive precautions are NOT required during or after courses of these broad-spectrum antibiotics.

However, advise women on the importance of correct practice if they experience vomiting or diarrhoea.
