üö® Immediate Evaluation Urgent

    • Previous seizures or neurological conditions
    • Developmental milestones
    • Family history of epilepsy

Diagnostic Evaluation

‚ñº

‚ö° EEG Assessment Priority

  • Timing: Perform as soon as possible after seizure
  • Protocol:
    • Awake and sleep recordings
    • Hyperventilation and photic stimulation
    • Video-EEG if available
  • Key Findings:
    • Generalized spike-wave discharges
    • 3Hz spike-wave (absence epilepsy)
    • Hypsarrhythmia (infantile spasms)

üß† Neuroimaging

  • Indications for MRI:
    • Focal neurological findings
    • Developmental delay
    • Atypical seizure semiology
  • Protocol:
    • High-resolution epilepsy protocol
    • Sedation if required
    • Contrast if structural lesion suspected

üß¨ Genetic Testing

  • Consider when:
    • Developmental and epileptic encephalopathy
    • Dravet syndrome suspected

Treatment Protocol

‚ñº

üÜò Status Epilepticus Emergency

  • First Line (0-5 minutes):
    • Buccal/IV midazolam 0.2mg/kg (max 10mg)
    • OR rectal diazepam 0.5mg/kg (max 20mg)
    • Protect airway, position, monitor
  • Second Line (5-20 minutes):
    • IV lorazepam 0.1mg/kg (max 4mg)
    • Repeat if necessary
    • Establish IV access
  • Third Line (>20 minutes):
    • IV phenytoin 20mg/kg
    • OR IV levetiracetam 60mg/kg
    • Consider ICU admission

üíä Anticonvulsant Selection

  • Childhood Absence Epilepsy:
    • First line: Ethosuximide
    • Second line: Valproate or lamotrigine
    • Avoid: Carbamazepine
  • Juvenile Myoclonic Epilepsy:
    • First line: Valproate (avoid in females)
    • Alternatives: Levetiracetam, lamotrigine
    • Lifelong treatment required
  • Dravet Syndrome:
    • Valproate + clobazam
    • Add: Stiripentol or fenfluramine
    • Avoid: Sodium channel blockers
