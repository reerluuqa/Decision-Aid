Impetigo Quick Scan Updated Feb 2024

Immediate Referral / Emergency Indicators

  • Neonate (0-28 days)
  • Systemic Signs (Fever/Lymphadenopathy)
  • Deep Tissue Infection (Cellulitis/Abscess)
  • Atypical Lesions (Extensive/No Response)
Overview

Highly contagious bacterial skin infection. Common in children.

Causative: Staphylococcus aureus & Streptococcus pyogenes.

Forms:
  • Non-bullous (70%): Crusts, vesicles.
  • Bullous: Large fluid-filled blisters (>0.5cm).
Diagnosis (Clinical)
Classic Presentation
Yellowish/Golden "Honey-colored" Crust
Perioral/Perinasal Distribution
Often asymptomatic, sometimes pruritic. Rarely painful.
Bullous Specifics
  • Thin-walled bullae (2cm+) containing clear fluid.
  • Breaks easily leaving rim of scale.
  • Intact skin affected (S. aureus toxin).
Key Differentials
Condition Distinguishing Sign
Herpes Simplex Monomorphic grouped vesicles
Varicella Widespread different stages
Tinea Peripheral scale, central clearing
Eczema/Dermatitis Pruritus invariably present
Scabies Dermoscopy shows burrows
Neonate (0-28 days) CRITICAL

High risk of sepsis/pneumonia. Collaboration with Specialist/Paediatric Dermatologist required.

  • Non-bullous: Oral Antibiotics (Erythromycin).
  • Bullous: Parenteral Antibiotics (Nafcillin/Oxacillin/Clindamycin).
  • MRSA Suspected: Vancomycin (IV).
Limited / Superficial ROUTINE

No systemic signs, no deep tissue involvement.

  • 1st Line (UK NICE): Hydrogen Peroxide 1% cream.
  • Topical Antibiotics: Mupirocin, Fusidic acid, Retapamulin, or Ozenoxacin.
  • Hygiene: Soap/Water wash twice daily.
Widespread Cutaneous CAUTION

Numerous lesions, but no deep tissue/fever.

  • Oral Antibiotics: Dicloxacillin/Flucloxacillin, Cephalexin (1st Gen), or Erythromycin.
  • Adjunct: Intranasal Mupirocin if recurrent or family spread.
MRSA Risk: Use Clindamycin or Trimethoprim/Sulfamethoxazole.
Deep / Systemic URGENT

Cellulitis, abscess, fever, or constitutional symptoms.

  • Treatment: Parenteral Antibiotics (IV/IM).
  • Options: Nafcillin, Oxacillin, or Clindamycin.
  • MRSA: Vancomycin (primary).
General Prevention & Hygiene
Washing: Soap and water twice daily. Use Chlorhexidine for older children/adults with widespread involvement (Avoid <4yrs & Neonates).
School: Exclude until lesions are crusted/healed or 24h after systemic treatment starts.
