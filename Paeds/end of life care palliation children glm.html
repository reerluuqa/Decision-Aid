Overview

Scope & Definitions

Target: Children & Young People (0-17) with Life-Limiting Conditions.

Life-Limiting: No reasonable hope of cure, premature death (e.g., Duchenne MD).

Life-Threatening: Curative treatment possible but may fail (e.g., Cancer).

Note: Access to palliative care should not depend on diagnosis or prognosis.

The Team
MDT
  • Named medical specialist leads care.
  • Specialist Paediatric Palliative Care Team.
  • Social Care, Education, Chaplains.
  • Allied Health (OT, Physio, Psychology).
Prevalence
Stats

Marked rise in prevalence (66.4 per 10,000 children in 2017/2018).

Highest risk groups: Under 1 year, Pakistani ethnicity, most deprived groups.
Communication Essentials
Do:
  • Be sensitive, honest, and realistic.
  • Consider age/understanding (use play, art, digital media).
  • Provide info verbally AND in writing.
Discuss:
  • Confidentiality & information sharing.
  • Uncertainties about condition/treatment.
  • Practical arrangements after death (funeral, post-mortem).
NICE Quality Standards Checklist

Ensure the following criteria are met for optimal care.

Status Standard Requirement
Child/YP & parents/carers involved in developing Advance Care Plan.
Child has a named medical specialist leading care.
Info provided on emotional/psychological support & access.
Care provided by an MDT including specialist paediatric palliative care.
Parents/carers offered support for grief and loss near end of life & after death.
24-hour access to children's nursing care AND consultant advice (if at home).
Advance Care Plan (ACP)
A core element of care. Start at diagnosis/recognition.
Essential Components
  • Demographics & key contacts.
  • Statement of consent responsibility.
  • Summary of condition & agreed communication approach.
  • Preferred Place of Care & Death.
  • Organ & tissue donation wishes.
  • Plans for resuscitation/life support.
  • Education plans (if relevant).
ACP Actions

Development: Involve MDT, child (if appropriate), and parents/carers.

Parallel Planning: Make multiple plans for different outcomes (unpredictable course).

Review: Regularly and on change of circumstance (admission, discharge, moving home).

Preferred Place of Care/Death

Discuss wishes, religious values, safety, and practicality.

Home Hospice Hospital

Transfer: If not in preferred place near death, consider rapid transfer.

APPROACHING END OF LIFE
Likely to die within hours or days.
Recognition Signs
Red Flags

Monitor non-invasively. Context is key.

  • Clinical judgement: Overall progression & baseline.
  • Family input: If parents/carers feel death is imminent, they are often correct.
  • Physiological changes: (Document specific signs in notes).
Immediate Actions
Urgent
  • Resuscitation: Attempt unless 'Do Not Attempt Resuscitation' order in place.
  • ACP Review: Update plan immediately (place of death, funeral, organ donation).
  • Support: Discuss care expected when child dies.
Discuss: Care of body, Coroner involvement, Death registration, Funeral arrangements, Post-mortem, Memory making (photos/handprints).
Emotional & Practical Support
Child/YP: Listen to fears. Be aware they may not express feelings openly (fear, hopelessness, anger).
Parents: Explain rituals, memory making, and social media plans. Give time for discussion.
Managing Distressing Symptoms

Rapid response required. Use anticipatory prescribing.

Agitation

Cause: Anxiety, pain, hypoxia, drug side effects.

Treat underlying cause. Sedation may be required if distress is severe despite other measures. Reassure family.
Pain

Action: Assess regularly. Analgesia ladder.

WHO analgesic ladder principles. Use non-pharmacological methods (massage, music) alongside pharmacology.
Respiratory Distress

Signs: Tachypnoea, recession, grunt, cyanosis.

Position child upright (if comfortable). Oxygen if hypoxic. Opioids/Midazolam to reduce breathlessness sensation. Reassure.
Seizures

Risk: Common in neurological conditions at end of life.

Rescue medication (buccal midazolam/rectal diazepam) should be prescribed in advance. Ensure parents know administration.
Hydration & Nutrition

Assess needs individually. Discuss risks/benefits of artificial hydration and nutrition with the family.

For some, IV fluids can increase secretions and edema. Mouth care is essential.
