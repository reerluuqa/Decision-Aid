DermHIV Quick-Scan

Immediate Triage: TEN / SJS

Life-threatening mucocutaneous reaction. Look for diffuse redness, purpuric centers, blisters, sloughing starting on trunk/face.

Stop Suspect Drugs Sulfa / Nevirapine / Anticonvulsants

Clinical Approach

History

Physical Exam Focus

Tip: Skin disease often reflects immune status. Low CD4 = consider OIs.
HIV-Specific Dermatoses

Kaposi's Sarcoma (KS)

Malignancy

Common HIV-associated malignancy. Vascular lesions. Look in mouth (palate) and skin.

Associated with HHV-8.
Visuals:Purple/red macules, plaques, nodules. May be fungating in endemic cases.
Location:Lower extremities, face, oral cavity (palate).
Next: Assess extent & CD4 count

Eosinophilic Folliculitis

Inflammatory

Intensely itchy, follicular papules/pustules. Consider Immune Reconstitution (IRIS).

Key Symptom: Pruritus (Itch).
Visuals:Urticarial follicular papules on face, upper trunk, shoulders.
Cause:Immune disturbance; Demodex mite possible trigger.
Next: Rule out Staph folliculitis

Oral Hairy Leukoplakia

Viral (EBV)

White, corrugated plaques on tongue lateral border. Marker of immunosuppression.

Visuals:White, vertical corrugated ("hairy") plaques. Cannot be scraped off (vs Candida).
Pathogen:Epstein-Barr Virus (EBV).

Papular Pruritic Eruption

Inflammatory

Generalized itchy papules. Strong association with immune dysregulation.

Visuals:Papules with excoriations. Often symmetrical.
DDx:Scabies (rule out).

Prurigo Nodularis

Chronic

Hyperkeratotic nodules on extensor surfaces. Intensely itchy.

Visuals:Firm, dome-shaped nodules, often hyperpigmented.
Note:Check for Hepatitis B/C co-infection.

HIV Lipodystrophy

Metabolic

Fat redistribution (lipoatrophy/lipohypertrophy) linked to ART.

Visuals:Facial atrophy, dorsocervical fat pad ("buffalo hump"), breast enlargement.
Drugs:NRTIs (mitochondrial toxicity), Protease Inhibitors.
Altered Presentations (Common Diseases)

Seborrhoeic Dermatitis

Inflammatory

Much more common and severe in HIV. Affects "T-zone" and chest.

Visuals:Yellow greasy scales on scalp, eyebrows, nasolabial folds.
Tip:Severity often correlates with falling CD4 counts.

Psoriasis

Inflammatory

May be the first sign of HIV. Can present explosively or as Reiter's syndrome.

Visuals:Silvery scales on plaques. Inverse (flexural) and palmoplantar patterns common.
Note:Risk of psoriatic arthritis. Associated with HLA-B27.

Herpes (HSV/VZV)

Viral

More frequent, severe, or atypical. Zoster can be multidermatomal.

HSV:Chronic ulcers > 1 month are AIDS-defining. May look like pyoderma.
VZV:Painful vesicular rash in dermatomal distribution.

Molluscum Contagiosum

Viral

Extensive disease indicates low CD4 (<200). Look for giant lesions.

Visuals:Pearly flesh-colored papules with central umbilication.
DDx:Disseminated Histoplasmosis/Cryptococcosis if immunocompromised.

Syphilis

STI

Increased prevalence. Presentation can be atypical (aggressive lues).

Visuals:Classic: Chancre, rash (palms/soles). Atypical: Lues maligna (ulcerative nodules).
Action:Always exclude Neurosyphilis in HIV+ patients.

Scabies

Infestation

Norwegian (Crusted) scabies occurs with high viral load/low CD4. Highly contagious.

Visuals:Hyperkeratotic crusted plaques. Millions of mites. Minimal itch usually.
Distinction:May involve head/neck (unlike classic scabies).

Drug Eruptions

Adverse Event

High prevalence of morbilliform reactions (e.g., to Bactrim).

Type Drugs
Morbilliform Abacavir, Sulfa, NNRTIs
SJS/TEN Nevirapine, Allopurinol
Warning: If rash + flu-like symptoms + blisters -> STOP DRUGS.

Pruritic Follicular Papules Eosinophilic Folliculitis, Staph, PPE Biopsy / Skin scrape
White Oral Lesions Candida (scrapes off), OHL (doesn't scrape) KOH prep, Swab
Purple Vascular Lesions Kaposi Sarcoma, Bacillary Angiomatosis Biopsy (urgent if systemic sx)
Photodistributed Rash Photodermatitis, Porphyria Cutanea Tarda Check liver/Uroporphyrins
