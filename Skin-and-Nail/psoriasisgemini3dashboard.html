Psoriasis: Diagnosis & Management

Last Revised: December 2024 | [cite_start]Target: Adults (18+) in Primary Care [cite: 14, 24]

‚ö†Ô∏è Medical Emergencies

Immediate Action

Arrange immediate same-day specialist dermatology assessment if suspected:

  • Erythrodermic Psoriasis: >90% body surface area affected. Lesions feel warm. [cite_start]Risk of heart failure, hypothermia, dehydration[cite: 296, 299].
  • Generalized Pustular Psoriasis: Rapid widespread erythema followed by sterile pustules. [cite_start]Systemic signs: fever, malaise, tachycardia[cite: 290, 291].

üíä Management: Trunk & Limbs

Stepwise Approach
[cite_start]

Advise: Not infectious, controls symptoms but not cure, lifestyle advice (smoking/alcohol/weight)[cite: 392, 396].

[cite_start]Core Treatment: Prescribe generous emollients (500g/week) to reduce scale and itch[cite: 425, 763].
[cite_start]

Step 1: Initial Treatment (4 Weeks) [cite: 427, 432]

  • Potent Corticosteroid (AM) + Vitamin D (PM) applied separately.
    • [cite_start]
  • OR Combined product (e.g., calcipotriol/betamethasone) applied OD[cite: 443].
  • Review at 4 weeks. If good response, continue until clear.
  • Safety: Max 8 weeks continuous potent steroid at one site. [cite_start]Require 4-week break before restarting steroid[cite: 433, 434].
[cite_start]Step 2: Poor Response (After 4 Weeks) [cite: 438]

If poor response to Step 1:

  • Stop the potent corticosteroid.
  • Apply Vitamin D preparation twice daily for up to 12 weeks.
[cite_start]Step 3: Resistant Disease [cite: 440]

If poor response to Vitamin D alone (after 8-12 weeks total treatment):

  • Potent Corticosteroid applied twice daily for up to 4 weeks.
  • OR Coal Tar preparation once or twice daily.
[cite_start]Short-Contact Dithranol [cite: 445]

Can be offered if educational support for self-use is available. Useful for treatment-resistant plaques.

üíÜ Management: Scalp

[cite_start]

Use lotions, solutions, or gels for hair-bearing areas[cite: 505].

[cite_start]

Step 1: Initial Therapy [cite: 520, 523]

  • Potent Corticosteroid (OD) for up to 4 weeks.
  • OR Vitamin D preparation (OD) if steroids not tolerated/mild disease.
    • [cite_start]
  • OR Coal tar shampoo (adjunct, not monotherapy for severe disease)[cite: 525].
[cite_start]Thick Scale? Use a salicylic acid, olive oil, or coconut oil preparation to remove scale before applying potent steroid[cite: 533].
[cite_start]Step 2: Poor Response (After 4 Weeks) [cite: 535]
  • Switch to Combined Potent Steroid + Vitamin D (OD) for up to 4 weeks.
  • OR Vitamin D alone (OD) if not already tried.

üõ°Ô∏è Face, Flexures & Genitals

Caution Required
[cite_start]Warning: Do NOT prescribe potent/very potent corticosteroids for face, flexures, or genitals due to risk of atrophy[cite: 591].

Treatment Protocol

    [cite_start]
  • Mild to Moderate Corticosteroid (OD or BD) for short term (up to 2 weeks)[cite: 587].
    • [cite_start]
  • Review after 4 weeks[cite: 593].
  • Maintenance: Only use steroids for 1-2 weeks per month. [cite_start]Requires "treatment break"[cite: 594].
    • [cite_start]
  • Off-label option: Topical calcineurin inhibitors (tacrolimus/pimecrolimus) for difficult sites[cite: 460].

üíß Guttate Psoriasis

[cite_start]

Usually self-limiting (resolves 3-4 months)[cite: 612].

    [cite_start]
  • Antibiotics: Do NOT use for guttate psoriasis even if triggered by strep (unless active infection persists)[cite: 621].
    • [cite_start]
  • Topical: Emollients, or weak steroid/coal tar if needed[cite: 619, 620].
    • [cite_start]
  • Phototherapy: Referral for UVB if widespread/extensive[cite: 618].

üìã Quick Prescribing Reference

Emollients

Dose: 250-500g per week. [cite_start]Apply liberally and frequently[cite: 745].

Cautions: Fire risk (paraffin). [cite_start]Avoid aqueous cream (skin reactions)[cite: 703, 741].

Vitamin D Preparations
[cite_start]

Max Dose: Calcipotriol max 100g/week[cite: 788].

[cite_start]

Contraindications: Calcium metabolism disorders, severe liver/kidney disease[cite: 775].

[cite_start]

Pregnancy: Avoid unless essential[cite: 778].

[cite_start]

Adverse Effects: Local irritation, hypercalcemia (rare)[cite: 783].

Coal Tar
[cite_start]

Contraindications: Sore/acute/pustular psoriasis, infected skin[cite: 843]. [cite_start]First trimester pregnancy[cite: 844].

[cite_start]

Adverse Effects: Staining, photosensitivity, folliculitis[cite: 849].
