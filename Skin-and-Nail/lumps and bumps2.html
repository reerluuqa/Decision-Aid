Critical Red Flags
  • Neurological: GCS < 8, new focal deficit, seizure > 5min
  • Respiratory: SpO2 < 90%, accessory muscle use, tracheal deviation
  • Cardiac: HR < 40 or > 140, SBP < 90, chest pain with diaphoresis
  • Infectious: Temp > 39.5°C, petechial rash, immunocompromised with fever
  • Lab: pH < 7.2, K+ > 6.5, glucose < 3.0 mmol/L

Common Differentials

Chest Pain

  • Cardiac: MI, angina, pericarditis
  • Pulmonary: PE, pneumonia, pneumothorax
  • GI: GERD, esophageal spasm

Headache

  • Urgent: SAH, meningitis, tumor
  • Common: Migraine, tension, cluster
  • Benign: Sinus, medication-overuse
