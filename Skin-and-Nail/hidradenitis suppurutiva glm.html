Hidradenitis Suppurativa Clinical Reference

Emergency Actions

Overview

Chronic inflammatory skin disease affecting intertriginous areas (axilla, groin, perineum, infra-mammary).

  • Pathogenesis: Hair follicle involvement (not sweat glands)

Hurley Classification & Treatment

Stage I (Mild)
Abscesses and inflammatory nodules without scarring
  • Topical clindamycin (1%)
  • Topical metronidazole (0.75-1%)
  • Tetracycline antibiotics (12 weeks)
  • Analgesia (NSAIDs)
  • Lifestyle modifications
Stage II (Moderate)
Abscesses, nodules with scarring, separated by normal skin
  • Tetracycline antibiotics (12 weeks)
  • Clindamycin + Rifampicin (10-12 weeks)
  • Triple antibiotics (second-line)
  • Dapsone (if failed antibiotics)
  • Spironolactone (premenstrual flare)
  • Oral retinoids (with acne)
  • Surgical repair (with scarring)
Stage III (Severe)
Multiple abscesses, interconnected sinus tracts and scars
  • IV Ertapenem (6 weeks) + consolidation therapy
  • Adalimumab (first-line biologic)
  • Infliximab (second-line biologic)
  • Wide excision surgery
  • Adjunctive therapies (as above)

Differential Diagnosis

Condition Differentiating Features Diagnostic Tests
Acne vulgaris Face, chest, back; rarely draining sinus tracts Clinical examination
Crohn's disease Perianal distribution; GI symptoms Endoscopy, biopsy
Inverse psoriasis Macerated red patches, not abscesses Skin biopsy
Epidermal inclusion cyst Solitary lesion with visible punctum Excisional biopsy
Furunculosis Solitary abscess, often after abrasion Bacterial culture
Squamous cell carcinoma Ulcerated; complication of long-standing HS Skin biopsy

Management Approach

Acute Abscess Management

First-line Treatment

  • Tetracycline: 500 mg orally twice daily
  • Doxycycline: 100 mg orally twice daily
  • Minocycline: 100 mg orally twice daily

12-week course recommended

Severe Flare Treatment

  • IV Ertapenem: 1 g daily (6 weeks)
  • Followed by consolidation therapy:
  • Moxifloxacin: 400 mg daily
  • Metronidazole: 500 mg TID
  • Rifampicin: 300 mg BID

Additional Measures

Note: Incision and drainage is supplemental only; recurrence is common

Ongoing Management by Severity

Lifestyle Modifications

  • Smoking cessation
  • Weight loss if obese
  • Cardiovascular disease evaluation
  • Antimicrobial wash (chlorhexidine, benzoyl peroxide)

Special Considerations

  • Premenstrual flare: Consider spironolactone
  • With acne vulgaris: Consider oral retinoids
  • With scarring: Consider surgical repair
  • Early specialist referral if not responding

Referral Indications

  • No response to 1-2 courses of long-term (3 months) antibiotics
  • Hurley Stage II or III disease
  • Significant impact on quality of life
  • Need for surgical intervention

Targeted Investigations

Bacterial Cultures

  • Exclude MRSA furunculosis
  • Document change in flora with long-term antibiotics

Skin Biopsy

  • Exclude squamous cell carcinoma
  • Differentiate from Crohn's disease

Other Tests

  • Zinc levels (if acrodermatitis enteropathica suspected)
  • Screen for comorbidities (diabetes, metabolic syndrome)
