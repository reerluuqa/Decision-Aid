Warts and Verrucae

Management of cutaneous warts in primary care (Age 12 months+).

Have I got the right topic?

Covers:
  • Cutaneous warts (Common, Plantar/Verrucae, Plane, Filiform)
  • Adults and children > 12 months
Excludes (See other topics):
  • Anogenital warts
  • Mucosal warts
  • Molluscum contagiosum
  • Genital Herpes

Diagnosis

Diagnosis is usually clinical. Further investigation (dermatoscope/paring) only if doubtful.

Typical Appearances

  • Common: Cauliflower-like papules (knuckles/knees/fingers).
  • Plane: Round, flat-topped, skin-coloured (face/backs of hands/shins).
  • Filiform: Finger-like, stalk (face/neck).
  • Plantar (Verrucae): Soles of feet, central dark dots (thrombosed capillaries). Painful.
  • Mosaic: Coalesced plaques.

Diagnostic Aids

  • Paring: Reveals black dots (thrombosed capillaries) in warts. Calluses preserve skin lines; warts interrupt them.
  • Caution: Paring may cause bleeding and spread the virus.

Differential Diagnosis (What else might it be?)
  • Molluscum contagiosum: Umbilicated pink/pearly papules.
  • Corns/Calluses: Areas of friction, intact skin markings, no black dots.
  • Actinic/Seborrheic Keratosis: Sun-exposed or "stuck on" appearance.
  • Squamous Cell Carcinoma: Non-healing, enlarging, tender.
  • Amelanotic Melanoma: Irregular outline, history of change.

Step 1: Decision to Treat

Strong case for NOT treating: Warts often resolve spontaneously (esp. in children). Treatment is painful, prolonged, and may cause scarring.

Only consider treatment if:
  • Painful (e.g., soles of feet, near nails).
  • Cosmetically unsightly (hands/face).
  • Persisting and patient requests it.

Step 2: Active Treatment Options

DO NOT TREAT FACIAL WARTS IN PRIMARY CARE

Topical Salicylic Acid (1st Line)

Regimen: Daily application for up to 12 weeks.

  • Soak 2-5 mins, dry, pare gently.
  • Apply directly to wart (protect surrounding skin).
  • Advise OTC purchase (NHS policy).

Recommended for children and adults.

Cryotherapy (Liquid Nitrogen)

Regimen: Every 2-4 weeks. Max 6 treatments.

  • 2 freeze-thaw cycles per session.
  • Spray until 2mm halo appears (5-30 secs depending on site).
  • Painful – usually not for young children.

Better for hand warts than plantar.

Difficult Cases (Mosaic/Plantar):
  • Cryotherapy less effective for plantar warts.
  • Consider combination therapy (Salicylic acid between Cryo sessions).
  • Paring dead skin helps reduce pain.

When to Refer (Dermatology)

  • Facial warts: Do not treat in primary care.
  • Diagnostic uncertainty: Possible malignancy.
  • Immunocompromised patients: Large/resistant lesions.
  • Extensive affected areas: e.g., large mosaic warts.
  • Persistent symptoms: Unresponsive to Salicylic Acid AND Cryotherapy.

Safety & Prescribing Details

Salicylic Acid: Contraindications & Cautions
DO NOT USE ON:
  • Face
  • Anogenital regions
  • Mucous membranes
  • Broken/infected skin
  • Children < 2 years
  • Moles or birthmarks
Caution:
  • Diabetic patients / Poor circulation (Neuropathic feet).
  • Pregnancy: Safety not established (though absorption low).
Cryotherapy: Contraindications & Cautions
AVOID IF:
  • Raynaud's syndrome
  • Peripheral vascular disease
  • Cryoglobulinemia / Autoimmune disease
  • Darker skin (Risk of permanent depigmentation)
  • Periungual sites (Risk of nail deformity)
  • Young children (Pain/Fear)
  • Over tendons/superficial nerves

Adverse Effects: Pain, blistering, scarring, hypopigmentation, nerve damage, nail dystrophy.

Background & Advice

Prognosis

  • Children: 50% clear in 1 year, 90% in 5 years.
  • Adults: More persistent (5-10 years).
  • Immunosuppressed: Resistant to treatment.

Transmission Advice

  • Cover with waterproof plaster for swimming (No socks needed).
  • Wear flip-flops in communal showers.
  • Do not share towels/socks.
  • Avoid biting nails (Autoinoculation).

Prevalence & Definition

Definition: Infection of keratinocytes with HPV. Common on hands/feet.

Prevalence: 5-30% of children/young adults. Peak in adolescence.

Incubation: Few weeks to >1 year.
