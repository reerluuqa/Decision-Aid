Risk Assessment

Assessment Protocol

Assess pressure ulcer risk for:

  • All people admitted to secondary care or care homes with NHS care
  • People receiving NHS care in primary/community settings with risk factors
  • Consider for all people in their own homes or social/nursing care

Validated Assessment Tools

  • Adults: Braden, Waterlow, Norton, or PURPOSE-T
  • Children: Braden Q Scale

High Risk Indicators: Limited mobility, nutritional deficiency, inability to reposition, cognitive impairment, history of pressure ulcers

Diagnosis & Assessment

Diagnostic Criteria

Diagnose a pressure ulcer if a person with risk factors develops:

  • Non-blanchable erythema (may present as color changes in darker skin)
  • Marked localized skin changes
  • Wound on anatomical site exposed to significant unrelieved pressure

NPUAP-EPUAP Classification System

  • Category I: Non-blanchable erythema
  • Category II: Partial thickness skin loss
  • Category III: Full-thickness skin loss
  • Category IV: Full-thickness tissue loss
  • Unstageable: Depth unknown
  • Suspected deep tissue injury: Depth unknown

Prevention Strategies

Prevention Plan

For individuals at risk of pressure ulcers:

  • Reposition every 6 hours (every 4 hours if high risk)
  • High-specification foam mattress for high-risk individuals
  • Pressure redistributing cushion for prolonged sitting
  • Heel offloading strategies for high-risk individuals
  • Barrier preparations for moisture lesions

Heel Pressure Ulcer Prevention

For Category I/II: Elevate heels using suspension device or pillow, offload completely

For Category III+: Use specifically designed heel suspension device

Consider: Prophylactic dressings as adjunct to offloading

Avoid: Skin massage, unnecessary IV fluids, nutritional supplements if intake adequate

Management

Wound Management
  • Nutritional assessment and support for deficiencies
  • High-specification foam mattress (dynamic if needed)
  • Consider debridement (autolytic first, then sharp if needed)
  • Antibiotics only for systemic infection or spreading cellulitis
  • Moist wound healing dressings for Categories II-IV

What NOT to Do

  • Do not use gauze dressings
  • Do not offer electrotherapy or hyperbaric oxygen
  • Do not routinely use negative pressure wound therapy
  • Do not use topical antiseptics routinely
  • Avoid skin massage for prevention

Complications

Potential Complications
  • Pain and distress
  • Infection (cellulitis, osteomyelitis, sepsis)
  • Longer hospital stays (5-8 days per ulcer)
  • Increased mortality and morbidity

Red Flag: Always consider abuse or neglect as a potential cause, especially in vulnerable populations.

NICE Quality Standards

Key Quality Indicators
  • Risk assessment within 6 hours of admission to hospital/care home
  • Skin assessment for high-risk individuals
  • Repositioning advice for at-risk individuals
  • Pressure redistribution devices for high-risk individuals
  • Information provision to patients and carers
