Head Lice – One‑Page Clinical Algorithm
Pediculosis capitis • Primary care quick reference (from 1 month+)

At‑a‑glance Algorithm

Clinic quick view

1) Confirm diagnosis

1
Detection combing (fine‑toothed 0.2–0.3 mm). Wet with conditioner is most sensitive; dry faster but less accurate.
2
Diagnosis = live louse. Nits/itching alone ≠ active infestation. Check household & close contacts.

2) Choose treatment

1
Wet combing (Bug Buster® or equivalent): Days 1, 5, 9, 13; Day 17 check.
2
Physical insecticide (preferred): Dimeticone 4% (Hedrin® Lotion/Once/Spray Gel); Dimeticone 92–95% (NYDA®/Linicin®); Isopropyl myristate + cyclomethicone (Full Marks®); Isopropyl myristate + alcohol (Vamousse®).
3
Reserve chemical: Malathion 0.5% aqueous (Derbac‑M®) – resistance reported.

3) Aftercare & verification

1
Treat all affected household contacts same day. Ensure sufficient product/coverage & contact time.
2
Re‑examine: Day 10 after insecticide course; Day 17 after wet combing. Success = no live lice (nits may persist).

Decision Support & Instructions

Confirming active infestation
  • Use a plastic detection comb (0.2–0.3 mm teeth spacing). Metal combs may pull hair.
  • Wet method (preferred for sensitivity): shampoo → plenty of conditioner → de‑tangle → comb root‑to‑tip in sections, checking/wiping comb each stroke; repeat after rinsing conditioner. Takes 10–30 min depending on hair.
  • Dry method: de‑tangle dry hair → comb crown‑to‑tips, each section 3–4 times. Takes ~3–5 min (long/curly hair longer).
  • Only a live louse confirms diagnosis. Nits (hatched shells) may adhere up to 8 months.
First‑line options
Wet combing
Days 1, 5, 9, 13 (two comb‑throughs each session) → Day 17 detection check.
Physical insecticides
  • Dimeticone 4% – Hedrin® Lotion/Once/Spray Gel; also Chemists' Own® Spray. Often 2 applications 7 days apart; some require 3.
  • Dimeticone 92–95% – NYDA®/Linicin®; generally good ovicidal activity.
  • Isopropyl myristate + cyclomethicone – Full Marks®.
  • Isopropyl myristate + alcohol – Vamousse®.
Chemical (reserve)
Malathion 0.5% aqueous (Derbac‑M®); consider resistance patterns and odour/skin irritation risk.
Special groups & preferences
  • Pregnant/breastfeeding, age 6 mo–2 yr, and asthma/eczema: prefer wet combing or dimeticone 4% lotion. Avoid alcohol‑containing products in asthma.
  • Shampoos generally not recommended (excess dilution/insufficient contact time).
  • Supply adequate volume for hair length/thickness; lotions/liquids often require 8–12 h (overnight) contact; some gels/sprays: ~15 min. Follow specific product IFU.
Post‑treatment verification
  • Insecticides: if single‑application product → re‑examine Day 1 and Day 10. If two‑application course → re‑examine the day after last treatment; then again ~Day 10.
  • Wet combing: detection combing on Day 17. Success = no live lice.
  • Itching alone or presence of eggs/nits does not equal failure.
If treatment appears to fail
  • Audit technique: full course completed, correct application, sufficient volume & contact time, whole‑hair coverage.
  • Reassess household/close contacts for reinfestation; treat simultaneously.
  • Repeat same regimen or switch class; if malathion used, consider resistance.
Education & transmission
  • Children on treatment may attend school.
  • No link with hygiene; pets do not transmit; lice do not jump/fly/swim; off‑host survival ~1–2 days.
  • No need to launder/fumigate bedding or clothing beyond normal hygiene.
  • Prevention: not reliably possible – advise regular (e.g., weekly) home detection combing during outbreaks.
  • Not recommended for prevention: repellents, prophylactic treatments, repeated treatment without live lice.
Background quick facts
  • Species: Pediculus humanus capitis. Lifecycle: egg (7–10 d) → nymph (7–10 d) → adult; typical burden ~30 lice.
  • Transmission requires head‑to‑head contact; sharing hats/combs is an unlikely route.
  • Complications: pruritic rash (nape/post‑auricular), sleep disturbance; rarely excoriation/secondary infection.

Safety & Practical Notes

Dimeticone & heat: keep hair away from flames/ignition sources while product is on hair.
  • Prefer physical agents (lower resistance risk).
  • Metal nit combs are not detection combs; use plastic 0.2–0.3 mm spacing for detection.
  • Supply adequate volume: short/shoulder hair ≈ small bottle; long/thick hair may need larger or two bottles.
  • Itching may persist days–weeks post‑cure; do not re‑treat unless live lice found.
  • Consider skin sensitivity; alcohol‑containing sprays not ideal in asthma.
