Definition & Overview

Disease Type Idiopathic inflammatory dermatosis with keratinocyte apoptosis
Affected Sites Skin, mucous membranes, genitalia, scalp (lichen planopilaris), nails
Course Usually self-limiting, but can be chronic (1-2 years typical)
Prevalence <1% worldwide; peak incidence 30-60 years

Diagnostic Features

‚óè Classic Cutaneous Signs

  • Pruritus: Present in 80% of patients
  • Violaceous flat-topped papules
  • Polygonal shape lesions
  • Wickham's Striae: White lacy network
  • Flexor wrists and ankles common sites
  • Usually no excoriations

‚óè Mucosal & Other Features

  • Oral: Lacy white reticulation, painful erosions
  • Genital: Glazed red patches, white borders
  • Nails: Longitudinal ridging, dorsal pterygium
  • Scalp: Follicular plugs ‚Üí scarring alopecia
  • May follow Blaschko's lines
  • Can be post-vaccination (COVID, Hep B, Flu)

Investigations & Histology

‚óè Histopathology

  • Lichenoid lymphocytic infiltrate
  • Civatte bodies: Necrotic keratinocytes
  • Hypergranulosis and hyperkeratosis
  • "Saw-tooth" rete ridges
  • Basement membrane changes

‚óè Laboratory / Other

  • Hepatitis C serology (if indicated)
  • LFTs if Hep C positive
  • Direct immunofluorescence (rarely needed)
  • Biopsy: DIF shows IgM + fibrinogen
  • 75% sensitivity for DIF
