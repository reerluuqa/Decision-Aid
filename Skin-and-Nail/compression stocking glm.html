A concise guide for healthcare professionals on compression therapy for venous disease

Definition

Compression stockings provide graduated pressure from distal to proximal to improve venous return and reduce venous hypotension.

Used for:

  • Chronic venous insufficiency
  • Varicose veins
  • Venous ulcers
  • Venous eczema
  • Post-thrombotic syndrome
  • Lipodermatosclerosis

Patient Assessment

History: Previous DVT, trauma, medications, family history

Leg Examination: Varicose veins, dermatitis, ulcers, edema

ABPI Measurement:

  • <0.8: Contraindicated (refer)
  • 0.8-1.3: Safe to wear
  • >1.3: Avoid compression (refer)

Skin Condition: Check for fragility, ulcers, infections

Patient Ability: Assess mobility, dexterity, need for aids

Contraindications

  • Suspected/proven peripheral arterial disease
  • Peripheral arterial bypass grafting
  • Peripheral neuropathy/sensory impairment
  • Local conditions causing damage (fragile skin, dermatitis, gangrene)
  • Allergy to stocking material
  • Severe leg edema
  • Major limb deformity
  • Acute infection of leg/foot
  • Suspected acute DVT
  • Suspected skin cancer

Compression Strength

  • Varicose veins: Class 1 (14-17 mmHg) or Class 2 (18-24 mmHg)
  • Venous eczema/lipodermatosclerosis/healed ulcers: Class 2
    • Try Class 3 (25-35 mmHg) if inadequate response
    • Try Class 1 if Class 2 not tolerated
  • After DVT: Do NOT prescribe for post-thrombotic syndrome prevention
  • Superficial vein thrombosis: Class 2 (or Class 1 if not tolerated)

Length Selection

  • Below knee (preferred):
    • Ulcer prevention
    • Swelling limited to lower leg
    • When thigh length not possible/desirable
  • Thigh length:
    • Severe varicose veins above knee
    • Swelling extending above knee
Therapy may be lifelong - prioritize patient preference for compliance

Toe Type Selection

Open Toe (Preferred for):

  • Arthritic or clawed toes
  • Fungal infections
  • Wearing socks over stocking
  • Long foot size relative to calf
  • Regular podiatry visits

Closed Toe:

  • Manages toe edema
  • Standard option for most patients

Fitting Guide

Measurements (taken seated, feet flat):

  • Ankle circumference (narrowest above malleoli)
  • Calf circumference (widest part)
  • Foot length (for closed-toe)
  • Thigh circumference (for thigh-length)

Key Points:

  • Measure directly against skin
  • Measure swollen legs in morning
  • Consider made-to-measure for deformities
  • Severe edema may require compression bandaging first

Usage Instructions

  • Wear schedule: Remove at bedtime, put on first thing in morning
  • Continuous wear: Max 7 days if necessary
  • Care: Hand wash at ~40°C, dry away from direct heat
  • Replacement: Every 3-6 months
  • Quantity: Provide at least two stockings
Replace immediately if damaged or loses elasticity

Review Schedule

Frequency: Every 3-6 months

Review Components:

  • Reassess condition
  • Check compliance
  • Re-measure legs
  • Evaluate fit
  • Consider repeat ABPI every 6-12 months

Fit Check:

  • Stockings should not feel loose
  • Fabric evenly distributed
  • Knee-length: 2 fingers below knee crease
  • Thigh-length: 4 fingers below gluteal fold

Compliance Tips

  • Explain benefits clearly
  • Demonstrate application technique
  • Consider application aids (e.g., ActiGlide®)
  • Check color preference
  • Ensure proper fit
  • Try lower compression if not tolerated
  • Recommend emollient when stockings off
  • Emphasize regular replacement schedule
