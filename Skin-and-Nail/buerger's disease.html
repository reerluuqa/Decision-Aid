üîç Imaging Modalities

  • Echocardiogram: Exclude cardiac embolic source

  • Infrapopliteal arterial occlusive lesions

  • Absence of atherosclerotic risk factors (except smoking)

Differential Diagnosis

Smoking Cessation Impact

  • 94% of patients who stopped smoking avoided amputation (n=43)
  • 19% major amputation rate in continued smokers
  • Even 1-2 cigarettes/day or nicotine replacement keeps disease active
  • Return to smoking = disease flare-up

Advanced Therapies

üî¨ Intravenous Iloprost

  • Prostacyclin analogue
  • 62% ulcer healing at 4 weeks (24-day course)
  • Reduces rest pain
  • Limited availability

‚ö° Spinal Cord Stimulation

  • Implantable stimulator
  • Reduces ischaemic pain
  • Improves ulcer healing
  • Used after medical therapy fails

üíâ Intravenous Guanethidine

  • Regional sympathetic blockade
  • Reduces catecholamine release
  • May improve ulcer healing
  • Limited data/availability

üî™ Sympathectomy

  • Chemical or surgical (thoracoscopic/lumbar)
  • Reduces peripheral resistance
  • Enables necrotic lesion healing
    • Mixed results; invasive

Surgical Options

ü©∫ Revascularisation

  • Distal bypass if suitable target vessel
  • Primary patency: 41% at 1 year, 30% at 10 years
  • Secondary patency: 54% at 1 year, 39% at 20 years
  • Often not possible due to lack of distal vessels

ü¶µ Amputation

  • 34% require amputation within 15 years
  • Indicated for non-viable tissue
  • Level determined by healing potential
  • Repeated amputations often needed

Long-term Outcomes

üìä Key Statistics

  • Amputation-free survival: 85% at 5 years, 74% at 10 years
