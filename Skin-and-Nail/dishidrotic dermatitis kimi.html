Dyshidrotic Dermatitis

Interactive Clinical Management Algorithm

üìã

Assessment

‚ñº

Clinical Presentation

  • Pruritic vesicles on palms/soles
  • 1-2mm vesicles on non-inflammatory base
  • Recurrent crops lasting weeks
  • May have prodrome of itching

Risk Factors

Hyperhidrosis Irritant exposure Nickel allergy Emotional stress

History Taking

  • Occupational exposure history
  • Atopic background
  • Trigger identification
  • Previous treatments
üîç

Diagnosis

‚ñº

Clinical Diagnosis

Primary Criteria:

  • Characteristic vesicular eruption
  • Palms/soles involvement
  • Pruritus
  • Recurrent pattern

Differential Diagnosis

Tinea manuum/pedis Contact dermatitis Palmoplantar psoriasis Scabies

Investigations (if needed)

  • KOH prep for fungal infection
  • Patch testing for allergies
  • Skin biopsy for atypical cases
  • Microscopic examination

Severity Assessment

Use DASI (Dyshidrotic Eczema Area and Severity Index):

Mild: 0-15 Moderate: 16-30 Severe: 31-60

Treatment

‚ñº

First-Line: Lifestyle Measures

  • Avoid identified triggers
  • Frequent emollient use
  • Protective gloves for wet work
  • Mild cleansers only

Topical Corticosteroids

High-potency for 2 weeks, then taper to medium potency

Clobetasol 0.05% Fluocinonide 0.05%

Topical Immunomodulators

For maintenance or steroid-sparing

Tacrolimus 0.1% Pimecrolimus 1%

Adjunct Therapies

  • Oral antihistamines for pruritus
  • Hyperhidrosis treatment if indicated
  • Nickel avoidance for allergic patients

Severe/Resistant Cases

Short oral prednisolone Phototherapy (PUVA/NB-UVB) Systemic immunosuppressants

Follow-up

‚ñº

Monitoring

  • Response to treatment
  • Adverse effects of medications
  • Need for treatment adjustment
  • Patient adherence

Complications

Secondary infection Skin atrophy Treatment resistance

Prognosis

Chronic relapsing-remitting course. Most achieve remission with treatment, but recurrence is common. Emphasize:

  • Trigger avoidance
  • Continued skin care
  • Early treatment of flares
  • Regular follow-up

Patient Education

  • Excellent hand/foot care routine
  • Importance of moisturization
  • When to seek medical attention
  • Stress management techniques
