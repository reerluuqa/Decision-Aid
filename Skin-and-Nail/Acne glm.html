Diagnosis & Assessment

Diagnostic Features

  • Comedones must be present (open/blackheads or closed/whiteheads)
  • Inflammatory lesions: papules, pustules, nodules, cysts
  • Distribution: face (99%), back (60%), chest (15%)

Assessment

  • History: duration, previous treatments, exacerbating factors, psychosocial impact
  • Examination: type, distribution, severity of lesions; scarring

Severity Classification

  • Mild: Predominantly non-inflammatory lesions with few inflammatory lesions
  • Moderate: More widespread with increased inflammatory papules and pustules
  • Severe: Widespread inflammatory papules, pustules, nodules/cysts; scarring may be present

Treatment

Treatment Algorithm

1
Assess Severity - Classify as mild, moderate, or severe acne
2
Provide Patient Education - Treatment expectations, skin care, adherence
3
Initiate First-line Treatment - Based on severity classification
4
Re-evaluate at 12 Weeks - Assess response and adjust treatment

Mild to Moderate Acne

First-line (12-week course, apply once daily in evening):

Option 1
Fixed combination adapalene + benzoyl peroxide
Option 2
Fixed combination tretinoin + clindamycin
Option 3
Fixed combination benzoyl peroxide + clindamycin

Alternative: Topical benzoyl peroxide monotherapy

Moderate to Severe Acne

First-line (12-week course):

Option 1
Fixed combination adapalene + benzoyl peroxide
Option 2
Fixed combination tretinoin + clindamycin
Option 3
Fixed combination adapalene + benzoyl peroxide PLUS oral lymecycline 408mg or doxycycline 100mg daily
Option 4
Topical azelaic acid (15-20%) twice daily PLUS oral lymecycline 408mg or doxycycline 100mg daily

Alternatives:

  • If can't tolerate lymecycline/doxycycline: trimethoprim or oral macrolide (e.g., erythromycin)
  • For women: combined oral contraceptive (3rd/4th generation preferred) + topical agents (alternative to systemic antibiotics)

Referral Criteria

Consider Referral For:

  • Severe acne not responding to primary care treatment
  • Scarring acne
  • Suspected underlying endocrine disorders
  • Significant psychological distress
  • Possible need for isotretinoin

Follow-up

  • Review after 12 weeks to assess response
  • If inadequate response, consider alternative treatment or referral

Special Considerations

Pregnancy: Topical retinoids and oral tetracyclines contraindicated
Contraception: Required when using topical retinoids or oral tetracyclines
Skin irritation: Common at start - begin with alternate-day application
Skin care: Non-alkaline cleansers; avoid oil-based comedogenic products
Counseling: Effects may take 6-8 weeks to become noticeable
