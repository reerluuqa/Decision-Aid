Clinical Assessment

Identify characteristic features and risk factors

Key Clinical Features

Classic Triad: Crater-like pits + malodour + hyperhidrosis on pressure-bearing areas of soles
  • Pits on stratum corneum: 1–3mm diameter, may coalesce into irregular erosions/sulci
  • Appearance: Brown pits (dirty feet appearance), amplified when wet
  • Texture: Slimy skin surface
  • Odor: Characteristic malodour from sulfur compounds (thiols, sulphides, thioesters)
  • Symptoms: Often asymptomatic; may have pruritus or pain on walking when symptomatic

Treatment Management

Evidence-based therapeutic approach

Comprehensive Treatment Strategy

Success depends on combination of foot hygiene + antibiotics/antiseptics + moisture control

General Measures (Foundation of Treatment)

Essential First Step: Hygiene measures alone may resolve mild cases and prevent recurrence

Footwear Modifications

  • Avoid prolonged occlusive footwear (vinyl, rubber)
  • Wear properly fitted shoes to reduce friction
  • Rotate dry insoles daily
  • Never share footwear

Sock Management

  • Wear absorbent cotton socks
  • Change socks frequently (at least daily)
  • Wash socks at 60°C with soap to eliminate bacteria
  • Ensure feet are dry before wearing socks

Foot Hygiene

  • Wash feet twice daily with antiseptic cleanser
  • Dry thoroughly between toes
  • Apply antiperspirant to feet regularly
  • Consider antifungal powder for moisture control

Hyperhidrosis Management

  • Address underlying excessive sweating
  • Consider aluminum chloride antiperspirants
  • Botulinum toxin for severe cases (rarely needed)
  • Weight management if obesity-related

Specific Treatment - Topical Antibiotics

Duration: Apply twice daily for 2–4 weeks. Continue for at least 10 days after clearance.

First-Line Options

  • Clindamycin 1% solution Twice daily
  • Erythromycin 2% solution Twice daily
  • Mupirocin 2% ointment Twice daily
  • Fusidic acid 2% cream Twice daily

Adjunctive Agents

  • Benzoyl peroxide 5-10%: Antimicrobial + keratolytic
  • Can be used alone or combined with topical antibiotics
  • May cause irritation - start with lower concentration
  • Stains fabrics - caution with clothing

Oral Antibiotics (Refractory Cases)

Reserved for: Severe, extensive, or treatment-resistant cases despite optimal topical therapy and hygiene measures

First-Line Oral Options

  • Clindamycin 150-300mg Q6H
  • Erythromycin 250-500mg Q6H

Alternative Options

  • Tetracyclines (doxycycline) 100mg BID
  • Duration: 10-14 days minimum Extended if needed

Treatment Failure Considerations

If no improvement after 2 weeks: Review diagnosis, check compliance with hygiene measures, consider resistance, evaluate for differential diagnoses

Compliance Check

Verify hygiene measures are being followed consistently

Footwear Review

Assess if occlusive shoes still being worn

Resistance Pattern

Consider culture and sensitivity testing

Re-evaluate Diagnosis

Rule out tinea pedis, psoriasis

Prevention Strategies

Hygiene Maintenance

  • Continue foot hygiene measures indefinitely
  • Regular antiperspirant use
  • Daily sock changes
  • Weekly shoe rotation and drying

Moisture Control

  • Manage hyperhidrosis proactively
  • Use moisture-wicking socks
  • Foot powder application
  • Shoe ventilation

Regular Monitoring

  • Self-examination weekly
  • Early treatment of recurrence
  • Annual foot health check if high-risk
  • Prompt treatment of intercurrent infections

Occupational Measures

  • Minimize occlusive footwear duration
  • Use breathable work boots
  • Change socks during long shifts
  • Foot care education programs

Patient Education Points

Key Messages: Benign condition, excellent prognosis, preventable with proper hygiene, not contagious under normal circumstances
  • Reassurance: Condition is treatable and not serious
  • Hygiene importance: Prevention is better than cure
  • Patience: Takes 2-4 weeks to clear completely
  • Recurrence risk: Continue preventive measures long-term
  • When to return: If no improvement after 2 weeks, or early signs of recurrence
