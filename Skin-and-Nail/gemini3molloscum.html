Molluscum Dashboard

Diagnosis

Clinical Features

Standard Care
  • Appearance: Smooth, firm, dome-shaped papules.
  • Color: Flesh-coloured or pearly white.
  • Key Sign: Central umbilication (dimple).
  • Size: Usually 2–5 mm (Giant >1cm).
  • Distribution: Clusters. Children (trunk/flexures), Adults (genitalia/thighs).

Transmission & Risk

Transmission
Direct contact, Fomites (towels), Autoinoculation.
Incubation
2–12 weeks (up to 26).

Risk Factors:

Atopic Dermatitis Immunocompromise Swimming Warm Climate

Referral Criteria & Red Flags

Urgent & Specialist Referrals

Ophthalmology

Urgent referral if eyelid-margin or ocular lesions are associated with red eye.

HIV Specialist

Urgent referral for people with HIV who have extensive lesions.

Dermatology

Refer if: Diagnostic uncertainty, immunocompromised, lesions are extensive/painful.

Genito-Urinary

Adults with Anogenital Lesions

Refer to GUM for STI screening (including HIV).

Children Caution

Lesions common in anogenital area.
Do NOT refer for suspected sexual abuse unless other evidence suggests it.

Management

First Line

Immunocompetent People

Treatment is usually not required. The condition is self-limiting.

General / Children

  • • Reassure regarding self-limiting nature.
  • • No single treatment is convincingly effective.
  • • Physical/Topical treatments (cryotherapy, imiquimod) generally not recommended for routine cases.

Anogenital / Adults

  • • Reassurance (self-limiting).
  • • Avoid shaving/waxing (prevents spread).
  • • Use condoms (partial protection only).
  • • Screen for other STIs.

Prognosis & Patient Advice

Prognosis

18
Months
average resolution

Most lesions resolve spontaneously. Occasionally persist >3-4 years.

Note: Lesions may last longer in people with atopic dermatitis or HIV.

Key Advice

  • School/Swimming: Exclusion is NOT necessary. Cover lesions with waterproof bandages/clothing for swimming.
  • Hygiene: Do not scratch or squeeze (prevents spreading/superinfection).
  • Sharing: Avoid sharing towels, clothing, bedding.

Complications

Molluscum Dermatitis

Eczema/inflammation around lesions (halo eczema). Occurs in ~10% of cases.

Tip: Inflamed lesions often indicate resolution is imminent. Treat the eczema if symptomatic.

Secondary Infection

Bacterial superinfection (impetiginization) due to scratching.

Other

Scarring (~7%), Conjunctivitis (if near eye).
