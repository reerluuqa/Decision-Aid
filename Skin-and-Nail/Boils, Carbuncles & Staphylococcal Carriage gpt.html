Boils, Carbuncles & Staphylococcal Carriage
Quick Reference (NICE CKS • Last revised: Feb 2025)
From age 1 month • Primary care use

Fast, clinic‑ready algorithm for diagnosis and management

Indications: boils (furuncles), carbuncles, and staph carriage Antibiotic course: typically 5–7 days when indicated

1) Initial Assessment

Site/size Pain Systemic features Recurrence MRSA/PVL risk Comorbidities
  • Examine for fluctuance, discharge, surrounding cellulitis, vitals if indicated.
Admit / urgent same‑day action if:
  • Systemically unwell or spreading cellulitis
  • Face involvement
  • Immunocompromised
  • Large or fluctuant lesions requiring I&D

2) Acute Boil / Carbuncle

  • Small boil: warm compresses 3–4×/day; when drains, cover with sterile dressing.
  • Large/fluctuant boil or any carbuncle: perform/arrange I&D (refer if not feasible); cover with sterile dressing.
Give oral antibiotics if: fever/systemic features, cellulitis, face lesion, severe pain, or significant comorbidity (e.g., diabetes, immunosuppression).
First line: Flucloxacillin 5–7 days. Alternatives for true penicillin allergy: clarithromycin; erythromycin (preferred in pregnancy/breastfeeding).

Swab if not responding, recurrent, multiple lesions, immunocompromised, MRSA risk, or suspected PVL‑SA (state PVL on form).

3) Recurrent Boils / Carbuncles

  • Exclude/manage: diabetes, anaemia/blood dyscrasias, obesity, eczema, steroid/immunosuppressant use, local friction/pressure.
  • Swab lesion → treat to sensitivities (~7 days).
  • PVL suspected → flag on request; discuss with microbiology.

4) Staphylococcal Carriage (post‑acute)

  • Swab sites: lesion & nose; consider perineum, groin, axillae, umbilicus if widespread/frequent recurrences.
  • Decolonize after acute resolves:
    • Nasal: Naseptin® QID ×10d (avoid peanut/soya allergy) or mupirocin TDS ×5d.
    • Skin: Chlorhexidine 4% or Triclosan 1–2% daily ×5d (also as shampoo days 1, 3, 5). Apply to wet skin ~1 min → rinse → dry.
  • Hygiene advice (during 5 days): daily linen/towel changes (>55 °C), personal towels/face cloths, liquid soap, regular cleaning of bathrooms, vacuum/dust bedrooms.

5) Persistent Recurrence After Decolonization

  • Re‑swab other sites; request PVL testing.
  • Consider screening/treating willing household contacts.
  • Seek microbiology/dermatology input for MRSA/PVL or refractory cases.

Self‑Care & Prevention (patient advice)

Daily shower/bath Hand hygiene No sharing towels/razors Wash sportswear each use Gym/sauna: sit on clean towel
  • Pain relief: paracetamol or ibuprofen if appropriate.
  • Keep wounds covered; change dressings when soiled; dispose safely.
  • Avoid contact sports, pools, gyms until cleared.

Prescribing Quick Notes

  • Flucloxacillin adults 250–500 mg QID × 5–7d; paeds per BNF. Avoid in true penicillin allergy; caution hepatic/renal impairment.
  • Clarithromycin adults 250–500 mg BD × 5–7d (halve dose if eGFR <30). QT‑risk interactions.
  • Erythromycin alternative in pregnancy/breastfeeding; check interactions.
  • Consider pseudomembranous colitis with severe diarrhoea during/after antibiotics.

Clinical Notes

  • Common organisms: S. aureus ± MRSA or PVL‑SA; occasionally S. pyogenes; anaerobes/enterics in anogenital sites.
  • Complications: scarring; cellulitis; rare severe invasive infection; cavernous sinus thrombosis with facial lesions.
