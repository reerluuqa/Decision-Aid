Head Lice Management

Clinical Reference Algorithm for Fast Decision Making

Diagnosis

Detection combing is essential to confirm active infestation

  • Must find live louse to confirm active infestation
  • Itching alone ≠ active infestation
  • Nits (empty egg shells) alone ≠ active infestation
  • Wet combing method (most accurate):
    • Wash hair with ordinary shampoo
    • Apply conditioner, untangle with wide-tooth comb
    • Use detection comb (0.2-0.3mm teeth) from roots to tips
    • Check comb after each stroke, wipe/rinse if lice found
    • Repeat entire process
  • Check all household members and close contacts

Treatment Options

1. Wet Combing (Bug Buster® kit)

  • First-line for: Pregnancy, breastfeeding, children 6m-2y, asthma/eczema
  • Regimen: Days 1, 5, 9, 13 (four sessions over 2 weeks)
  • Advantages: No insecticides, no resistance, inexpensive, reusable
  • Disadvantages: Time-consuming (10-30 min/session), lower cure rates (38-52%)
  • Follow-up: Check on day 17

2. Physical Insecticides (Dimeticone-based)

  • Examples: Hedrin® (4%), NYDA® (92%), Full Marks Solution®
  • Application: Apply to all scalp/hair, leave for recommended time (15min-8hr)
  • Regimen: Usually two applications 7 days apart
  • Advantages: Good safety profile, resistance unlikely
  • Disadvantages: Some products poor ovicidal activity, minor skin irritation possible
  • Follow-up: Re-examine one day after last treatment
  • Caution: Keep hair away from flames during treatment

3. Chemical Insecticides (Malathion 0.5%)

  • Use when: First-line treatments fail
  • Application: Two applications 7 days apart
  • Caution: Resistance reported, not first-line
  • Follow-up: Re-examine one day after last treatment

Treatment Failure

  • If live lice found after completing treatment course:
    • Confirm correct technique was used
    • Switch to alternative treatment class
    • Consider three applications instead of two

Prevention Advice

  • Transmission requires head-to-head contact
  • Not transmitted via pets or shared objects (hats, combs, pillows)
  • Treat all affected household members on same day
  • No evidence lice prefer clean or dirty hair

Key Points

  • Treatment success = no living lice found (nits may remain)
  • Presence of eggs alone or itching ≠ treatment failure
  • Shampoos generally not recommended (insufficient contact time)
  • Supply adequate treatment: longer/thicker hair requires more product
Important: Always follow manufacturer instructions for any treatment product. Advise patients to read instructions carefully before use.

Based on NICE Clinical Knowledge Summaries (CKS) - Head Lice (Last revised November 2021)

This reference is for healthcare professionals working within the NHS in the UK
