Clinical Presentation
Tender, erythematous nodules typically on shins. May be associated with fever, arthralgia, and malaise.
Risk Factors Caution
Female gender (20-40 years), recent infections, medications, autoimmune diseases, pregnancy.
Physical Examination
Red, tender, non-ulcerated, immobile nodules. Check for joint involvement and systemic symptoms.
Red Flags Urgent Referral
Ulcerated lesions, atypical distribution, treatment-resistant cases, constitutional symptoms.

Diagnosis
‚ñº
Initial Tests Stable
FBC, anti-streptolysin-O titres, chest X-ray, tuberculin testing. ESR typically elevated.
Clinical Diagnosis
Primarily clinical based on characteristic lesions. Biopsy reserved for atypical cases.
Investigate Underlying Cause
Based on clinical suspicion: sarcoidosis, tuberculosis, infections, inflammatory bowel disease.
Differential Diagnosis Caution
Weber-Christian disease, nodular vasculitis, alpha-1-anti-trypsin deficiency, cutaneous polyarteritis nodosa.

Treatment
‚ñº
Mild to Moderate Symptoms Stable
Bed rest, leg elevation, NSAIDs (ibuprofen 400-800mg, naproxen 250-500mg BID). Most cases resolve spontaneously in 1-2 months.
Severe/Refractory Cases
Potassium iodide 300mg TDS, intralesional corticosteroids, or short course systemic corticosteroids (prednisolone 20mg daily).
Treat Underlying Cause
Address primary cause: antibiotics for infections, manage autoimmune conditions, discontinue offending medications.
Emerging Therapies Caution
Colchicine, dapsone, TNF-alpha inhibitors (etanercept, infliximab) for refractory cases.

Follow-up
‚ñº
Monitoring Stable
Self-limiting condition. Monitor for resolution within 1-2 months. No specific follow-up required for idiopathic cases.
Complications
Extremely rare. Ulceration may occur with trauma. Consider alternative diagnoses if ulcers develop.
Prognosis
Excellent. Usually resolves without scarring. Older patients with venous insufficiency may have persistent erythematous swelling.
Prevention of Recurrence
Identify and treat underlying cause. Avoid known triggers when possible.
