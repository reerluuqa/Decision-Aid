Definition & Scope

  • Venous leg ulcer: Break in skin below knee not healed within 2 weeks
  • Location: Typically in gaiter area (ankle to mid-calf)
  • Most common type: 60-80% of all leg ulcers
  • Covers: Assessment, management in primary care, prevention of recurrence
  • Does NOT cover: Arterial ulcers, mixed ulcers, specific dressing recommendations, compression bandaging details

Assessment

History

  • Duration of ulcer
  • Venous symptoms (pain, heaviness, swelling - worse at end of day)
  • Risk factors (immobility, DVT history, varicose veins)
  • Comorbidities (diabetes, rheumatoid arthritis)
  • Medications (nicorandil, corticosteroids, NSAIDs)
  • Impact on quality of life
  • Allergies (latex, lanolin)

Examination

  • Ulcer characteristics: Site, edge (gently sloping/irregular), size, depth, wound bed, exudate
  • Signs of infection: Cellulitis, fever, increased pain, malodour
  • Venous signs: Pitting oedema, skin changes (hyperpigmentation, eczema, lipodermatosclerosis), varicose veins
  • Ankle mobility (calf muscle pump)
  • Arterial signs: Hair loss, pallor, coldness, capillary refill >4 seconds

Investigations

  • Essential: Doppler assessment for ABPI in both legs
  • Consider if indicated: FBC, ESR/CRP, Urea & creatinine, Albumin, HbA1c
  • Bacteriological swabs: Only if clinical evidence of infection

Differential Diagnosis

  • Peripheral arterial disease - "punched out" ulcers, signs of arterial compromise
  • Rheumatoid arthritis - vasculitic ulcers
  • Systemic vasculitis - multiple necrotic ulcers, atypical distribution
  • Diabetes mellitus - neuropathic ulcers under calluses/pressure points
  • Malignancy - rolled/everted edges, no response to treatment
  • Pressure ulcers - over bony prominences
  • Drug-induced - nicorandil, hydroxycarbamide, etc.

Management Principles

  1. Compression therapy (mainstay of treatment)
  2. Wound management:
    • Appropriate dressing selection
    • Infection management if present
  3. Skin care:
    • Emollients for dry skin
    • Topical steroids for eczema
  4. Pain management
  5. Lifestyle modifications:
    • Leg elevation when resting
    • Regular exercise
    • Weight management
  6. Prevention of recurrence:
    • Compression stockings after healing
    • Regular follow-up

Red Flags for Referral

Refer immediately if:

  • ABPI <0.8
  • Suspected arterial component
  • No improvement after 2-3 weeks of appropriate therapy
  • Atypical appearance suggesting malignancy
  • Severe or recurrent infection
  • Large ulcer (>5cm)
  • Ulcer duration >1 year

Prognosis

  • Healing rates: 45% at 6 months (community), 70% (specialist clinics)
  • Recurrence rates: 26-69% at 12 months
  • Poor prognostic factors:
    • Wound duration >1 year
    • Large size
    • History of varicose vein surgery
    • ABPI <0.8
    • Impaired calf muscle pump
    • Fibrin in >50% of wound surface
    • Lower socioeconomic status
    • Poor compliance with treatment
