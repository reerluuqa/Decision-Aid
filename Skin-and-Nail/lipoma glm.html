Lipoma Reference

Clinical Decision Support Tool

RED FLAG: Liposarcoma Suspected

Do NOT treat as simple lipoma if any of the following are present. Refer urgently to Oncology/Surgery.

  • Size > 5cm (Strong predictor if >10cm)
  • Rapid recent growth
  • Deep to fascia / Fixed / Immobile
  • Firm or hard texture (not doughy)
  • Retroperitoneal location
Clinical Differentiation

Likely Benign Lipoma

LOW RISK
  • Superficial subcutaneous location
  • Soft, doughy consistency
  • Mobile and slippery
  • Size typically < 5 cm
  • Painless (unless Dercum's/Angiolipoma)
  • Slow growing or static

Suspect Liposarcoma

URGENT
  • Deep seated (intra-muscular/retroperitoneal)
  • Firm or hard rubbery consistency
  • Fixed to underlying tissue
  • Size > 5 cm (esp. > 10 cm)
  • Rapid enlargement
  • Pain (if deep/infiltrative)

Diagnostic Modality Comparison
Modality Best Use Case Key Findings (Lipoma)
Ultrasound Superficial lesions (extremities, head, neck). First-line. Discrete, encapsulated, homogeneous mass. High sensitivity.
MRI Deep lesions, complex masses, planning surgery. Contents identical to subcutaneous fat on all sequences. No/faint enhancement.
CT Scan Trunk lesions, GI tract, deep tissue, retroperitoneum. Hounsfield units identical to fat (-50 to -150).
Core Needle Biopsy Suspicious lesions (>3cm, deep, growing). Mature adipocytes, thin fibrous capsule (Subcutaneous). No cellular atypia.
Lipoma Variants & Syndromes

Angiolipoma

Painful, young adults, capillaries with fibrin thrombi.

Spindle Cell

Posterior neck/shoulder, men 45-65. Collagen spindle cells.

Intramuscular

Deep, thigh/trunk. Infiltrative. Must rule out Sarcoma.

Hibernoma

Brown fat. Tends to bleed/recuer if not fully excised.

Dercum's Disease

Multiple painful lipomas, obesity, weakness (Middle-aged women).

Madelung's Disease

Symmetric lipomatosis (Neck/Shoulders). Associated with alcoholism.

Clinical Pearls

  • Gardner's Syndrome: Association with familial adenomatous polyposis and colon cancer.
  • FNA Limitation: Fine Needle Aspiration is generally not helpful as it provides cells but not tissue architecture.
  • GI Lipomas: Can cause obstruction or bleeding. Check for "pillow sign" on endoscopy.
  • Excision: Ensure complete removal to prevent recurrence. Positive margins increase recurrence risk.
