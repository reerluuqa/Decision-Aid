Definition

What is Hirsutism?

Excess terminal hair in androgen-dependent areas in women:

  • Face
  • Chest
  • Abdomen
  • Lower back
  • Upper arms
  • Thighs

Assessment

History & Examination
  • Evaluate pattern and severity of hair growth
  • Assess impact on quality of life
  • Look for features of underlying causes:
    • PCOS: oligomenorrhoea/amenorrhoea, infertility, acne, central obesity, acanthosis nigricans
    • Androgen-secreting tumor: sudden onset, rapid progression, virilization (voice deepening, clitoromegaly), mass
    • Endocrine disorders: Cushing's, thyroid dysfunction, hyperprolactinaemia, acromegaly
  • Medication history (danazol, sodium valproate, anabolic steroids)
  • Family history
Investigations
  • Ferriman-Gallwey score (‚â•8 indicates hirsutism in UK white/black women)
  • For all women with abnormal score or clinical evidence of hyperandrogenism:
    • Serum total testosterone
  • If normal but moderate/severe hair growth or clinical signs of hyperandrogenism:
    • Early morning serum total and free testosterone
  • Consider 17-hydroxyprogesterone in high-risk women (family history, Ashkenazi Jewish, Hispanic, Eastern European)

Management

Referral Criteria

  • URGENT (within 2 weeks) if suspected adrenal/ovarian neoplasm:
    • Sudden onset/rapid progression
    • Signs of virilization
    • Pelvic/abdominal mass
    • Testosterone >6-7 nmol/L
  • Refer if:
    • Testosterone >4 nmol/L
    • Elevated 17-hydroxyprogesterone
    • Suspected endocrine condition requiring specialist care
    • COCs contraindicated or ineffective after 6 months
    • Postmenopausal women requiring treatment beyond topical eflornithine
Treatment Options

For all patients:

  • Weight loss if overweight/obese
  • Discuss hair removal methods:
    • Temporary: shaving, waxing, chemical depilatories, bleaching
    • Permanent: electrolysis, laser (most effective for light skin/dark hair)

Premenopausal Women

  • Mild with minimal QoL impact: reassurance
  • Facial hirsutism: topical eflornithine
  • Other cases: COC with ethinylestradiol (consider lowest dose if high VTE risk)

    • Avoid cyproterone in women with meningioma history

Postmenopausal Women

  • Mild with minimal QoL impact: reassurance
  • Facial hirsutism: topical eflornithine
  • Other cases: refer for specialist treatment

Contraindications

  • Pregnancy
  • Breastfeeding
  • Age <19

Adverse Effects

  • Skin irritation, acne, stinging/burning

Counseling Points

  • Effects seen in 6-8 weeks
  • Discontinue if no benefit at 4 months
