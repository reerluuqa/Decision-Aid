Initial Assessment

1

ABCs (Airway, Breathing, Circulation)

Assess and stabilize airway, breathing, and circulation first

If ABCs compromised

EMERGENCY REFERRAL
2

History & Examination

Document cause, timing, tetanus status, comorbidities, allergies

3

Burn Assessment

Location, size (TBSA*), depth, signs of inhalation/infection

4

Safeguarding Consideration

Assess for non-accidental injury (see Red Flags section)

*TBSA (Total Body Surface Area) estimation: Lund and Browder chart (most accurate), Wallace's Rule of Nines (adults), Rule of Palm (patient's palm ≈ 1% TBSA)

Burn Depth Classification

Depth Appearance Sensation Capillary Refill Healing Time Scarring
Superficial epidermal Red, no blisters Painful Blanch, rapid refill ~7 days None
Superficial dermal Red/pink, blisters Painful Blanch, slow refill ~14 days None
Deep dermal Dry, blotchy, red Painful No blanch May need surgery Possible
Full-thickness White/brown/black, leathery Painless No blanch Needs surgery Significant

Note: Burn depth may increase with time - reassess after 24-72 hours. Most burns are a mixture of different depths.

First Aid Measures

1

Stop burning process

Remove from heat source, extinguish flames

2

Cool the burn

Run cool water (15-25°C) for 20 minutes

3

Remove constrictive items

Jewelry, clothing before area swells

4

Cover wound

Sterile, non-adherent dressing (cling film)

5

Analgesia

Paracetamol or ibuprofen as needed

Referral Criteria

Complex Burns (Always refer):

  • All electrical and chemical burns
  • Burns to critical areas (face, hands, feet, perineum, joints)
  • Circumferential burns
  • Full-thickness burns
  • Burns >10% TBSA in adults (>5% in children, >2% in <1 year)
  • Inhalation injury
  • Significant comorbidities

Non-Complex Burns (Consider referral):

  • Deep dermal burns >1% TBSA
  • Superficial dermal burns >5% TBSA
  • Burns not healing after 2 weeks
  • Signs of infection
  • If safeguarding concerns

Management of Non-Complex Burns

1

Cleanse wound

With saline or tap water

Superficial epidermal burns

Moisturizer + analgesia

Superficial dermal burns

Blisters: Leave intact if small, de-roof if large
Dressing: Non-adherent (e.g., silicone dressing)
Review in 3-5 days
2

Tetanus prophylaxis

Administer if indicated based on immunization history

3

Follow-up

Reassess depth after 24-72 hours, monitor for infection, document thoroughly

Red Flags for Non-Accidental Injury

History Red Flags

  • Absent or inconsistent explanation
  • Delayed presentation
  • Unrelated adult bringing child
  • Evasive or changing history
  • Trigger event mentioned
  • History inconsistent with development
  • Lack of parental/carer concern

Examination Red Flags

  • Injury to protected areas (buttocks, back of hands, soles)
  • Pattern injuries (implement marks, glove/stocking distribution)
  • Symmetrical burns, sharp borders
  • No splash marks in scalds
  • Signs of restraint on upper limbs
  • Sparing of flexion creases
  • "Doughnut sign" (central sparing of buttocks)
  • Associated unrelated injuries of different ages
