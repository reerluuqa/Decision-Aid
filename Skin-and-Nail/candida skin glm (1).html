Candida - Skin

Quick Reference Algorithm for Healthcare Professionals

Diagnosis

Clinical Features

  • Soreness, itching, thin-walled pustules with red base
  • White-yellow, curd-like scales over infected area
  • Flexural areas: red, moist skin with fringed irregular edge and satellite lesions
  • Web spaces: marked maceration with thick crusty layer

Differential Diagnoses

  • Atopic eczema
  • Bacterial skin infections (impetigo, cellulitis)
  • Dermatophyte infections (more dry/scaly with prominent border)
  • Erythrasma (red-brown patches in axillae/groin)
  • Flexural psoriasis (sharply demarcated, "glazed" appearance)

Management Algorithm

1

Suspected systemic candidiasis?

YES → ADMIT TO HOSPITAL
NO → Proceed to step 2
2

Localized infection, non-immunocompromised

Adults: Topical imidazole (clotrimazole, econazole, miconazole, ketoconazole) OR terbinafine

Children: Topical clotrimazole, econazole, or miconazole

With significant inflammation/itch: Add hydrocortisone 1% for 7 days, then review

3

Treatment failure, widespread infection, or immunocompromised

Adults/children ≥16 years: Oral fluconazole 50mg/day for 2 weeks

Resolved? → Stop treatment
Not resolved? → Extend 2 more weeks OR swab OR specialist advice OR refer

Children <16 years or fluconazole contraindicated: SEEK SPECIALIST ADVICE

4

Patient Advice

  • Avoid skin occlusion (tight clothing, non-breathable fabrics)
  • Change dressings/pads/nappies before saturated
  • Wash with soap substitute, dry thoroughly (especially skin folds)
  • Weight loss if obesity is a factor
5

Refer to dermatologist if:

  • Widespread/recurrent infection with unidentified cause
  • Uncertain diagnosis

Prescribing Information

Topical Antifungals

Medication Dosage Duration Special Notes
Clotrimazole 1% 2-3 times/day ≥2 weeks May damage latex contraceptives
Miconazole 2% Twice/day 10 days after lesions heal Enhances anticoagulant effect of warfarin
Econazole 1% Twice/day Until lesions heal May damage latex contraceptives
Ketoconazole 2% (≥18y) 1-2 times/day Few days after lesions heal Effect on latex contraceptives unknown
Terbinafine 1% (adults) 1-2 times/day 14 days No known drug interactions

Oral Fluconazole Cautions

Contraindications: Acute porphyria, pregnancy, certain drugs

Cautions: QT prolongation risk, hepatic/renal impairment

Monitor for: Abdominal discomfort, headache, rash

Check for: Drug interactions (especially with warfarin)
