1. Assessment

Initial Evaluation

History, wound characteristics, and impact on daily life

Wound Characteristics

  • Type Ulcerative (crater-like) vs Proliferative (nodular).
  • Site Affects dressing choice and securing method.
  • Tissue Amount of necrotic/sloughy tissue (determines cleansing need).

Patient Discussion

  • Identify treatment priorities.
  • Assess impact on daily functioning.
  • Key Point: Explain healing is unlikely; goal is symptom control.

2. Diagnosis

Identifying Infection

Distinguishing colonization from infection

Clinical Signs of Infection

  • Systemic Fever, Rigors, Tachycardia
  • Local Cellulitis, Abscess, Pocketing
  • Wound Change Increased pain, exudate, odour

Microbiology Swabs

Only take swabs if there is clinical evidence of infection.

1 Cleanse wound first (sterile water/saline).
2 Zig-zag motion across wound bed while rotating swab.
Caution: Presence of bacteria does not always indicate infection.

3. Treatment & Management

Click sections to expand management strategies.

Cleansing & Debridement

Cleansing

  • Warm tap/shower water (clean technique).
  • Sterile saline if bone involved/immunocompromised.
  • Gentle irrigation. No scrubbing.
  • Avoid cotton wool/gauze (sheds fibres).

Debridement

  • Preferred: Autolytic (Hydrogels, hydrocolloids).
  • Avoid: Sharp/Mechanical debridement (risk of bleeding).
  • Refer to Tissue Viability if sharp debridement needed.

Pain Management

Dressing Changes:

Use non-adherent dressings. Soak with saline before removal. Administer breakthrough opioid 30 mins prior.

Topical Opioids (Specialist Advice):

Mixture of Diamorphine (or Morphine) + Hydrogel (0.1% w/w). Apply to wound bed daily.

Bleeding Control Urgent

Mild Bleeding

Gentle pressure (10-15m) with moist non-adherent dressing. Adrenaline 1:1000 gauze or sucralfate.

Heavy Bleeding

Apply pressure. Seek urgent oncology/palliative advice. Consider oral Tranexamic acid (specialist).

End of Life (Catastrophic)

Use dark towels to mask blood. Consider sedation (Benzodiazepine) to minimize distress.

* Review anticoagulants. Alginates are haemostatic but avoid on very fragile tissue (bleeding on removal).

Odour Management

Caused by anaerobes and necrosis. Can induce nausea and isolation.

Intervention Detail
Topical Metronidazole 0.75% Gel. Apply liberally daily for 7-14 days. Good for necrotic tissue.
Oral Metronidazole 400mg TDS for 5-7 days. Better for deep tissue infection.
Dressings Activated Charcoal (keep dry). Silver/Iodine/Honey dressings.
  • Topical Metronidazole: 0.75% Gel daily (7-14 days).
  • Oral Metronidazole: 400mg TDS (5-7 days).
  • Dressings: Activated Charcoal, Silver, Honey.

Exudate

  • Use Alginate, Hydrofibre, or Foams.
  • Protect surrounding skin with barrier cream/film (prevent maceration).
  • For copious exudate: Stoma appliances (pouches).

Itch

  • Exclude infection or contact dermatitis.
  • Mild corticosteroid: 1% Hydrocortisone cream.
  • Specialist: TENS machine.

4. Referral & Follow-up

Specialist Referral

Urgent

Airway obstruction or vessel compression.

Urgent

Uncontrolled severe bleeding.

Consider

Significant necrotic tissue (larval therapy).

Routine

Potential for cancer treatment (Radiotherapy/Chemo/Surgery).

End of Life Considerations

  • Recognize signs of dying to adjust care plan.
  • Prioritize symptom control over healing.
  • Anticipatory prescribing for pain/distress.
  • Minimize dressing changes to reduce distress.
