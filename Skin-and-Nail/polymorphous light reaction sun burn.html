Diagnosis & Differential

Diagnostic Criteria

  • Characteristic morphology: papules, plaques, or vesicles
  • Photo-distribution pattern
  • Recurrent annual pattern
  • Spontaneous resolution within days
  • Normal minimal erythema dose testing

Key Differential Diagnoses

  • Photo-allergic contact dermatitis
  • Phototoxic reactions
  • Chronic actinic dermatitis
  • Solar urticaria
  • Systemic lupus erythematosus

Treatment Approach

Stepwise management based on severity

‚ñº

Mild Disease (Papular Type)

First-line: Topical Therapy

Low-potency topical corticosteroids (1-2.5% hydrocortisone)

Apply sparingly twice daily to affected areas

Combine with emollients for moisturization

STABLE

Moderate Disease (Plaque Type)

Second-line: Potent Topicals

Triamcinolone 0.025-0.1% or fluocinonide 0.05%

Apply twice daily for 7 days

Consider NSAIDs for anti-inflammatory effect

CAUTION

Severe Disease (Widespread/Bullous)

Third-line: Systemic Therapy

Prednisolone 0.5-1mg/kg/day for 8 days with taper

Reserve for extensive involvement or severe symptoms

Monitor for corticosteroid side effects

URGENT

Follow-up & Prevention

Long-term management strategies

‚ñº

Prevention Strategies

  • Strict photoprotection with SPF 25+ broad-spectrum sunscreen
  • Protective clothing and shade-seeking behavior
  • Gradual sun exposure for natural hardening (if tolerated)
  • Vitamin D supplementation if avoiding sun exposure
  • Consider prophylactic Polypodium leucotomos extract
üìÖ Follow-up Schedule: Annual review for severe cases. Monitor for treatment complications. STABLE

Patient Education Points

  • Condition is benign and self-limiting
  • Annual recurrence pattern is typical
  • No increased skin cancer risk (may be protective)
  • Importance of year-round photoprotection
