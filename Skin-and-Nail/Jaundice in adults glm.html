Definition & Assessment

  • Jaundice: Yellow pigmentation of skin/sclera due to elevated bilirubin

Initial Assessment

History

  • Duration and progression
  • Alcohol intake
  • Medications (prescribed, OTC, herbal)
  • Travel history
  • Risk factors (IV drug use, blood transfusions)
  • Associated symptoms (pain, fever, weight loss, pruritus)

Examination

  • Degree of jaundice
  • Abdominal examination (hepatomegaly, splenomegaly, ascites)
  • Signs of chronic liver disease
  • Stool/urine color (pale stools/dark urine suggests obstruction)

Classification of Causes

Pre-hepatic

  • Hemolysis (sickle cell, G6PD deficiency)
  • Drugs (methyldopa, sulfasalazine)
  • Gilbert's syndrome
  • Crigler-Najjar syndrome

Intra-hepatic

  • Viral hepatitis (A, B, C, D, E)
  • Alcohol-related liver disease
  • MASLD (formerly NAFLD)
  • Autoimmune disorders (PBC, PSC, AIH)
  • Drugs (paracetamol, antibiotics)
  • Hereditary metabolic causes

Post-hepatic

  • Gallstones
  • Malignancy (pancreatic, cholangiocarcinoma)
  • Surgical strictures
  • Pancreatitis
  • Parasitic infections

Red Flags (Urgent Referral Required)

üö® Immediate Action Required

Jaundice + any of the following:

  • Fever + abdominal pain (suspected cholangitis)
  • Altered mental state (suspected encephalopathy)
  • Abdominal pain + vomiting (suspected pancreatitis)
  • Unexplained weight loss
  • Palpable abdominal mass
  • Age >40 with jaundice (consider cancer pathway)

Initial Investigations

Urgent Blood Tests

  • FBC, LFTs, coagulation studies
  • Bilirubin (conjugated vs unconjugated)
  • CRP, amylase if indicated

Other Tests

  • Urinalysis (urobilinogen and bilirubin)
  • Abdominal ultrasound (first-line imaging)

When to Admit/Refer

Admit Immediately

  • Signs of acute liver failure (coagulopathy, encephalopathy)
  • Suspected cholangitis (Charcot's triad: fever, pain, jaundice)
  • Severe abdominal pain suggesting pancreatitis
  • Significant deterioration

Urgent Referral (48 hours)

  • Unexplained jaundice with systemic symptoms
  • Known liver disease with decompensation
  • Suspected malignancy

Routine Referral

  • Gilbert's syndrome (mild unconjugated hyperbilirubinemia)
  • Stable chronic liver disease
  • Mild drug-induced jaundice after stopping causative agent
