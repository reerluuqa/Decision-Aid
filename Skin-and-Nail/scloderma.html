Systemic Sclerosis (SSc) Quick-Scan

RED FLAG: Scleroderma Renal Crisis

Abrupt onset hypertension + Acute Renal Failure + Micro-angiopathic haemolytic anaemia.
Most common in early diffuse disease. Click for details.

Action: Immediate BP control. Associated with Anti-RNA polymerase III antibody.

Symptoms: Headache, blurred vision (papilloedema), confusion, oliguria.

Disease Subtypes LeRoy Classification

Limited Cutaneous

Skin thickening distal to elbows/knees. Face may be involved.

Better Prognosis

Diffuse Cutaneous

Proximal extremities + Trunk skin thickening.

Aggressive
Sine Scleroderma: Internal organ involvement without skin thickening (<5% of cases).

ACR/EULAR Classification Score Score ≥ 9
Skin Findings
  • 9 pts: Skin thickening of fingers of both hands extending proximal to MCP joints.
  • 4 pts: Skin thickening of fingers (only distal to MCP).
  • 2 pts: Puffy fingers.
Other Findings
  • 3 pts: Raynaud's phenomenon.
  • 3 pts: Scleroderma-related antibodies.
  • 2 pts: Abnormal nail-fold capillaries.
  • 2 pts: Telangiectasia.
  • 2 pts: PAH / ILD on imaging.
Key Clinical Features History & Exam
  • Raynaud's Phenomenon
    Secondary if onset >30 yrs, asymmetric, painful ulcerations.
  • Sclerodactyly
    Thickening/tightening of finger skin, loss of grasp, shiny skin.
  • Digital Ulcers / Pits
    Ischaemic origin, painful, heal slowly. High morbidity.
  • GI Involvement
    Heartburn, Dysphagia (GORD), Bloating, Constipation.
  • Pulmonary Signs
    Dry crackles (basilar - ILD), Dyspnoea (PAH/ILD), Decreased tolerance.

Management Overview Therapy

Vasculopathy

Treat peripheral vasculopathy and Raynaud's.

Calcium-channel blockers
Other vasodilators

Fibrosis (Lung)

Decrease progression of pulmonary fibrosis.

Cyclophosphamide
(Demonstrated for early SSc)

Renal Crisis

Target reversible ischemic activation.

ACE Inhibitors*
*Standard care inferred

Monitoring: PFTs and Echo annually. Refer for right-heart catheterization if RVSP elevated or clinical suspicion of PAH.
