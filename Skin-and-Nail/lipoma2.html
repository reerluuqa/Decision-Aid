Clinical Evaluation Required
History Taking
When was the lump first noticed? Any changes in size, symptoms, or related weight loss?
Physical Examination
Assess size, location, texture (soft/doughy), mobility, and tenderness
Risk Factors Assessment
Family history, genetic syndromes, alcohol consumption, trauma history

Key Assessment Points:

Size: Most lipomas <5cm. Lesions >5cm require careful evaluation
Texture: Should be soft and doughy. Firm lesions need further investigation
Location: Superficial vs deep. Deep lesions may require imaging

Diagnosis
‚ñº
Clinical Diagnosis Possible Imaging May Be Required Biopsy if Suspicious
Clinical Diagnosis
Superficial lesions with classic features may be diagnosed clinically
Imaging Studies
Ultrasound (87% sensitivity, 96% specificity) or MRI for deep/intramusular lesions
Biopsy Indications
Lesions >3cm, symptomatic, enlarging, or with solid components

Differential Diagnosis:

Liposarcoma: Usually >5cm, firmer texture, may be fixed to underlying structures
Epidermoid Cyst: Firm, rounded, may have central punctum
Abscess: Tender, warm, with surrounding erythema

Treatment
‚ñº
Observation Option Surgical Excision Available
Observation
Asymptomatic, small lipomas can be monitored without intervention
Surgical Excision
Indicated for symptomatic, enlarging, or cosmetic concerns. Usually under local anesthesia
Special Considerations
GI lipomas may require resection if causing obstruction/bleeding. Atypical locations need individual assessment

Treatment Decision Tree:

Asymptomatic <5cm: Observation with periodic follow-up
Symptomatic or enlarging: Surgical excision recommended
Deep or atypical location: Consider referral to specialist

Follow-up
‚ñº
Excellent Prognosis 1-2% Recurrence Rate
Post-Excision Care
Wound care, suture removal in 5-14 days depending on location
Complications
Low risk of infection, seroma, hematoma, or nerve injury
Long-term Monitoring
Recurrence uncommon (1-2%). Patient education about new lesions

Follow-up Schedule:

Post-operative: Wound check in 5-10 days, suture removal as appropriate
Recurrence monitoring: Patient self-examination and return if new masses develop
Multiple lipomas: Consider genetic counseling and family screening
