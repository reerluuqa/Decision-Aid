Key Assessment Points

  • History of sun exposure and sunburns
  • Immunosuppression status
  • Previous skin cancers
  • Family history of skin cancer
  • Occupational exposure to carcinogens

Clinical Examination

Perform thorough skin examination including:

  • Total body skin check
  • Dermoscopy for suspicious lesions
  • Documentation with photography
  • Measurement of lesion dimensions

Follow-up Schedule

Low Risk: 6-12 monthly for 2 years, then annually

High Risk: 3-6 monthly for 2 years, then 6-12 monthly

What to Monitor

  • Local recurrence at surgical site
  • New primary lesions
  • Regional lymph node involvement
  • Signs of metastatic disease
