URGENT: Malignancy Screening Required

Adult-onset dermatomyositis has strong association with occult malignancy. All adult patients require age-appropriate cancer screening at diagnosis and regular surveillance.

‚ö†Ô∏è
RED FLAGS - Urgent Actions
Immediate attention required
  • Interstitial Lung Disease - Can progress rapidly, especially with anti-MDA5 antibody. Requires HRCT and early aggressive treatment.
  • Cardiac Involvement - Arrhythmias, myocarditis, or heart failure. Poor prognostic indicator. ECG and echo mandatory.
  • Severe Dysphagia - Risk of aspiration. Evaluate with barium swallow. Oesophageal involvement = poor prognosis.
  • Respiratory Muscle Weakness - Can cause respiratory failure. Monitor respiratory rate and oxygen saturation.
  • Rapidly Progressive Weakness - Consider anti-SRP antibodies or necrotizing myopathy. May require IVIG or rituximab.

Clinical subsets & prognostic implications

Strongest malignancy association. Intensive cancer screening required. Often treatment-resistant rash.
Anti-MDA5 (CADM-140)
Clinically amyopathic DM. Rapidly progressive ILD with poor prognosis. Tender palm papules.
Anti-SRP
Acute severe necrotizing myositis. Treatment-resistant. Often requires early IVIG/rituximab.
Anti-Mi-2
Classic DM presentation. Best steroid response. Low malignancy risk. ~10% of patients.
Anti-Jo-1
Anti-synthetase syndrome. ILD + myositis + fever + mechanic's hands + Raynaud's. Steroid-resistant.
Anti-NXP2
Juvenile DM. High risk of cutaneous calcinosis (dystrophic calcification).

Initial Workup - First Tests
Essential investigations at presentation
Test Finding in DM
Serum CK Elevated (up to 50x). Most sensitive muscle enzyme. May be normal in active disease.
Aldolase Elevated. Useful when CK normal. Less specific than CK.
ANA Positive in ~80%. Non-specific - also positive in other CTDs.
Muscle Biopsy Perifascicular atrophy diagnostic. Perivascular inflammation.
EMG Fibrillation, positive sharp waves. Not essential if CK + biopsy typical.

Organ Involvement Screening
Mandatory at diagnosis
  • Lungs: CXR + HRCT chest, PFTs (FVC, DLCO). Ground glass = better prognosis than honeycombing.
  • Heart: ECG (30% abnormal), Echo, cardiac troponin I for silent myocardial damage.
  • Swallow: Barium swallow if dysphagia, nasal regurgitation, or dysphonia.
  • Skin: Biopsy if CADM or diagnosis uncertain. Classic DM may not need skin biopsy.

Treatment Dosages
First-line and steroid-sparing agents

üíâ Corticosteroids - Initial

Prednisone: 1mg/kg/day (max 80mg/day) oral. Taper slowly over 6-12 months based on response.

IV Methylprednisolone: 500-1000mg/day √ó 3 days for severe disease or acute flare.

üíâ Methotrexate

Dose: Start 15mg/week, increase to 25mg/week as tolerated. Oral or subcutaneous. Faster onset than azathioprine.

Use: Both muscle AND skin disease. Folic acid 1mg daily supplementation required.

üíâ Azathioprine

Dose: 2-3mg/kg/day (max 150mg/day). TPMT testing prior to starting.

Use: Steroid-sparing. Slower onset (3-6 months) but long-term efficacy.

Skin-Directed Treatment
Cutaneous manifestations

Topical Therapy

  • High-potency corticosteroids
  • Topical tacrolimus 0.1%
  • Antipruritics for symptom control
  • Photoprotection - essential!

Systemic for Skin

  • Hydroxychloroquine 200-400mg/day
  • Methotrexate (also helps muscle)
  • IVIG for resistant disease
  • Mycophenolate mofetil

Key Points - At a Glance
Critical reminders
  • Best Response: DM is most responsive to therapy among IIMs
  • Poor Prognosis: Cardiac involvement, malignancy, severe ILD, anti-MDA5, anti-SRP
  • Skin May Persist: Cutaneous disease can continue despite muscle control
  • Juvenile DM: Different epidemiology. More calcinosis, less malignancy association
  • Screen All Adults: Cancer screening mandatory at diagnosis and follow-up
