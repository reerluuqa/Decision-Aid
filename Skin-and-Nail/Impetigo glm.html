Impetigo Clinical Algorithm

A concise guide for diagnosis and management

Definition & Types

Common superficial bacterial skin infection

Non-bullous (70%) Thin-walled vesicles/pustules → golden-brown crusts

Bullous Large, fragile bullae → rupture → scaley rim (collarette)

Highly contagious; incubation 4-10 days

Causes

Primary pathogens: Staphylococcus aureus or Streptococcus pyogenes

Risk factors:

  • Skin breaks, warm/humid weather
  • Poor hygiene, crowded environments
  • Immunosuppression

Diagnosis

Clinical diagnosis (usually no investigations needed)

Consider swabs if:

  • Treatment failure
  • Recurrent/widespread cases
  • Diagnostic uncertainty
  • MRSA suspected

Management Algorithm

Follow this step-by-step approach for impetigo treatment

PATIENT WITH IMPETIGO
SYSTEMICALLY UNWELL or HIGH RISK OF COMPLICATIONS?
YES → ORAL ANTIBIOTICS (5-7 days)
NO → DETERMINE TYPE & EXTENT
BULLOUS IMPETIGO?
YES → ORAL ANTIBIOTICS (5-7 days)
NO → NON-BULLOUS IMPETIGO
NON-BULLOUS: LOCALIZED OR WIDESPREAD?
LOCALIZED → HYDROGEN PEROXIDE 1% CREAM (2-3 times daily × 5 days)
WIDESPREAD → TOPICAL ANTIBIOTIC OR ORAL ANTIBIOTIC

Antibiotic Choices

Antibiotic Adult Dose Pediatric Dose
Flucloxacillin (First-line) 500 mg four times daily Age-based: 62.5-500 mg four times daily
Clarithromycin (Penicillin allergy) 250-500 mg twice daily 7.5 mg/kg twice daily
Erythromycin (Pregnant) 250-500 mg four times daily 250-500 mg four times daily (8-17 years)

MRSA suspected: Consult microbiologist

Follow-up & Referral

Follow-up:

  • Reassess if symptoms worsen or no improvement
  • Poor response: Consider swabs, switch antibiotics
  • Recurrent cases: Swab for microbiology, consider decolonization

When to Refer:

  • Suspected serious complications (cellulitis, sepsis)
  • Immunocompromised with widespread infection
  • Systemically unwell or frequent recurrences
  • High risk of complications or diagnostic uncertainty

Complications

Rare but possible complications:

Cellulitis
Ecthyma
Lymphangitis
Staphylococcal scalded skin syndrome
Osteomyelitis
Septic arthritis
Sepsis
Acute post-streptococcal glomerulonephritis
