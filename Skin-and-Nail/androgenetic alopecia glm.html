Androgenetic Alopecia

Clinical Quick Reference
Red Flags & Urgent Considerations

Early Vertex Baldness (Men) Male

Early onset hair loss in the vertex area is a marker for early onset coronary heart disease in patients with hypertension and/or dyslipidaemia.

Action: Conduct cardiovascular questioning and examination. Check BP, Lipids.

Signs of Hyperandrogenism Female

Only ~40% of female cases have hyperandrogenism. Look for Hirsutism, Acne, Irregular menses.

Differential: PCOS (67% prevalence in studies), Adrenal tumors, Hyperprolactinaemia.

Diagnosis & Pattern Recognition

Male Pattern Hair Loss Male

  • Pattern: Temporal recession (M-shape) advancing to vertex (crown).
  • Classification: Norwood-Hamilton (Types I-VII).
  • Hair-Pull Test: Usually Negative (≤3 hairs).
  • Onset: Usually 20-25 years.

Female Pattern Hair Loss Female

  • Pattern: Diffuse thinning on crown. Preservation of frontal hairline ("Christmas Tree" pattern).
  • Classification: Ludwig (Types I-III - mild, mod, severe).
  • Hair-Pull Test: Usually Negative.
  • Onset: Usually before 40, spikes at menopause.

1st Line Investigations

All Patients

  • TSH: Exclude thyroid dysfunction.
  • Serum Ferritin: Exclude iron deficiency.
  • Hair-Pull Test: Measure telogen density.

Women + Signs of Hyperandrogenism

  • Total & Free Testosterone
  • DHEA-Sulfate (Rule out adrenal source)
  • Sex Hormone-Binding Globulin
  • Pelvic Ultrasound (for PCOS)

Specialist Tests (If indicated)

  • Dermatoscopy: Examine follicular ostia.
  • Biopsy: Ratio of terminal to vellus hairs (rarely needed).
Management Protocols

Treatment: Male Primary

Note: Treatments slow loss; complete reversal is rare. Must be used indefinitely.

Topical Minoxidil 5%

1 mL twice daily. Foam causes less irritation than solution.

Oral Finasteride 1mg

Inhibits Type II 5-alpha-reductase. Monitor for sexual side effects (usually reversible).

Adjunct: Combination Therapy

Minoxidil + Finasteride often superior to monotherapy.

Failed Medical Treatment?

Hair Transplant (Follicular unit extraction/strip). Ideal >25yo with good donor density.

Treatment: Female Primary

Note: Minoxidil contraindicated in pregnancy/lactation.

Topical Minoxidil

2% solution twice daily OR 5% foam once daily.

With Hyperandrogenism

Contraception required (Risk of feminizing male fetus)

  • Spironolactone: 200mg daily (12+ months).
  • Oral Contraceptives: Use low-androgenic progestins (e.g., Norgestimate, Drospirenone).
  • Cyproterone / Flutamide / Bicalutamide: Specialist guidance needed.
Key Differentials
Condition Differentiating Signs Test/Action
Diffuse Alopecia Areata Exclamation-point hairs, pitted nails, circumscribed asymmetrical baldness. Biopsy: Lymphocytic infiltrate in peribulbar region.
Acute Telogen Effluvium Diffuse shedding 3-5 months post-trigger (stress, surgery, fever). Density normal/minimally decreased. Positive Pull Test (usually). Biopsy: Normal appearance, increased telogen follicles.
Chronic Telogen Effluvium Idiopathic abrupt onset. Fluctuating shedding for years. NO frontoparietal loss. Biopsy: Normal-appearing scalp skin.
Anagen Effluvium Associated with chemotherapy/mitotic inhibitors. Immediate shedding. Biopsy: Predominantly anagen hairs.
