Boils, Carbuncles & Staphylococcal Carriage

Clinical Reference Algorithm for Primary Care

Source: NICE CKS (Last revised February 2025)

Diagnosis

Feature Boil (Furuncle) Carbuncle
Appearance Firm, tender, red nodule → fluctuant abscess Large, hard, red dome; multiple draining points
Size Pea- to golf ball-sized Larger (>3 cm)
Location Hair-bearing areas (face, neck, axilla, buttocks) Nape of neck, back, thighs
Systemic Symptoms Rare (mild fever) Common (fever, malaise, lymphadenopathy)
Healing Days-weeks; may scar Slow; often scars

Key Differentials

Folliculitis: Superficial pustules (no deep fluctuance)
Hidradenitis suppurativa: Chronic, recurrent in axilla/groin
Cystic acne: Comedones + nodules on face/trunk
Cellulitis: Diffuse redness/swelling (no central abscess)
Dental abscess: Causing swelling of the face
Epidermoid cyst (ruptured): Round, unilocular cyst

Antibiotic Indications

  • Multiple/recurrent lesions
  • Systemic symptoms (fever, lymphadenopathy)
  • Facial/ear/nose lesions (risk of cavernous sinus thrombosis)
  • Immunocompromised/diabetic patients
  • Failed drainage

First-line Antibiotics (5-7 days)

Flucloxacillin 500mg QDS

(if no penicillin allergy)

Clarithromycin 500mg BD

(if penicillin-allergic)

Consider MRSA coverage

if risk factors (healthcare worker, recent hospitalization)

Staphylococcal Carriage Management

When to Screen

  • Recurrent boils/carbuncles (≥2 episodes/year)
  • Household contacts with infection

Decolonization Protocol (5-day course)

  1. Nasal: Mupirocin 2% ointment BD
  2. Skin: Chlorhexidine 4% body wash daily
  3. Hygiene: Daily towel/bedding changes at 60°C
Follow-up: No routine repeat swabs (eradication assumed if symptoms resolve)

Red Flags: Refer Urgently

Systemic sepsis (hypotension, tachycardia)
Facial lesions near eyes/nose/ears
Rapid progression or extensive cellulitis
Immunocompromised with severe infection
Suspected PVL-SA (necrotizing lesions, severe pneumonia)
Recurrent despite treatment

Key Updates (February 2025)

Antibiotic duration updated to 5–7 days (reduced from 7-14 days)
