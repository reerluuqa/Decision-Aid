Classification & Severity Grading

Simplified Severity Classification

  • Mild: comedones dominant, <10 papules/pustules
  • Moderate: 10-40 papules/pustules, 10-40 comedones
  • Moderately Severe: 40-100 lesions, occasional nodules (‚â§5)
  • Very Severe: nodulocystic, acne conglobata, many large painful lesions

Lesion Types

  • Non-inflammatory: whiteheads (closed comedones), blackheads (open comedones)
  • Inflammatory: papules, pustules, nodules, cysts
  • Common sites: face, chest, back, upper arms (sebaceous gland-rich areas)
  • Complications: post-inflammatory hyperpigmentation, atrophic/hypertrophic scarring

Diagnosis - Key Factors

Key Diagnostic Factors

  • Risk factors present: age 12-24, family history, oily skin
  • Characteristic lesions: comedones, papules, pustules, nodules, cysts
  • Typical distribution: face, chest, back
  • Onset typically at puberty
  • Clinical diagnosis - usually no testing required

Risk Factors

  • Strong: adolescence/young adulthood, genetic predisposition, oily skin
  • Drugs: androgens, corticosteroids, anticonvulsants, lithium, JAK inhibitors
  • Endocrine: PCOS, hyperandrogenism, precocious puberty
  • Other: gender-affirming testosterone therapy, obesity/insulin resistance
  • Diet: dairy, high GI foods (controversial evidence)

When to Consider Lab Testing

  • Routine testing NOT indicated for most patients
  • If hyperandrogenism suspected: free testosterone, DHEAS, LH, FSH, 17-OHP
  • If gram-negative folliculitis suspected: bacterial culture
  • For isotretinoin monitoring: LFTs, fasting lipids (monthly)
  • Pregnancy test before isotretinoin (monthly during treatment)

Treatment Algorithm

Treatment Principles

  • Combination therapy is mainstay - use multiple mechanisms of action
  • NEVER use antibiotics as monotherapy - risk of resistance
  • Topical retinoids should be included in most treatment regimens
  • Benzoyl peroxide helps prevent antibiotic resistance

Before Starting

  • Pregnancy test (monthly thereafter)
  • Liver function tests
  • Fasting lipid panel
  • Mental health assessment

During Treatment

  • Monthly pregnancy tests
  • Monthly LFTs + lipids
  • Monitor for depression/suicidal ideation
  • Report severe headaches, night vision changes

üå∏ Pregnancy Considerations

Safe in Pregnancy

  • Clindamycin topical
  • Erythromycin topical
  • Azelaic acid 20%
  • Benzoyl peroxide (limited use)

Contraindicated

  • Oral tetracyclines (teeth/bone)
  • Oral erythromycin (malformation risk)
  • Oral isotretinoin (teratogenic)
  • Topical retinoids (theoretical risk)

Referral Guidelines

Referral Checklist - Click to Track

  • Severe nodulocystic acne unresponsive to standard treatment
  • Acne fulminans or suspected systemic involvement
  • Significant scarring or high scarring risk
  • Severe psychological distress/impact on quality of life
  • Failure of at least 3 standard treatment regimens
  • Suspicion of underlying endocrine disorder (e.g., PCOS)
  • Consider isotretinoin - requires specialist oversight
  • Gram-negative folliculitis requiring specialist management

Patient Self-Care Advice

  • Use non-comedogenic products only - including makeup and skincare
  • Cleanse twice daily with non-alkaline (pH neutral or slightly acidic) synthetic detergent
  • Avoid: scrubs, astringents, fragranced products, other irritants
  • DO NOT pick, squeeze, or scratch - increases scarring risk
  • Avoid hair products on face - can trigger breakouts
  • Treatment takes 6-8 weeks to show improvement - set expectations
  • Continue treatment even after clearing - maintenance therapy prevents relapse
