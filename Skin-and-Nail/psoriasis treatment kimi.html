üíß Moisturisers & Emollients

Foundation of ALL psoriasis treatment ‚Äì use even when on UV/systemic/biologic therapy

Why Moisturise?

  • Reduces dryness, scaling, cracking, soreness, itching
  • Improves absorption of active treatments (Vitamin D, steroids, tar)
  • Makes skin look and feel better

Types & Consistency

Form Characteristics Best Use Examples
Lotions More water, less oil; quick absorption Hairy areas, daytime E45 lotion
Creams Oil + water mix, less greasy Daytime, weeping skin Diprobase
Ointments Very greasy, excellent barrier Nighttime, dry plaques Epaderm, 50/50 white/liquid paraffin
Gels/Sprays Light, non-greasy, fast On-the-go Various spray emollients

Added Ingredients

  • Anti-pruritics (itch): Lauromacrogols ‚Äì check ingredients list
  • Keratolytics: Salicylic acid, allantoin ‚Äì for thick plaques
  • Anti-microbials: Only if infection present
‚úÖ Key Application Rules:
  • Apply gently following hair line, avoid aggressive rubbing
  • Use soap substitutes in bath/shower (not regular soap)
  • Apply immediately after bathing while skin is warm
  • Wait 30 min after moisturiser before active treatment
  • Prescribe large quantities ‚Äì small pot lasts ~1 month for full body

‚òÄÔ∏è Vitamin D Based Topicals

Slows skin cell production & anti-inflammatory

When to Use

  • First-line topical for many patients
  • Can be used alone or combined with topical steroids
  • Review at 4 weeks

Available Types

Calcipotriol (Dovonex) ‚Äì Ointment, gel, lotion, foam, scalp solution
Calcitriol (Silkis) ‚Äì Ointment

üí° Note: Dovonex ointment can be bought OTC at pharmacies (costly), others prescription-only

How to Use

  • Apply once daily (nighttime recommended)
  • Moisturise 30 min before application
  • Limit sun exposure ‚Äì increases UV sensitivity
  • Overuse can affect calcium absorption

üíä Topical Steroids

Anti-inflammatory corticosteroids (NOT anabolic steroids)

When to Use

  • Small-area psoriasis, first-line option
  • Can combine with Vitamin D treatments
  • Review at 4 weeks (2 weeks children)
  • Sensitive areas: use mild/moderate potency for 2 weeks only

üõ¢Ô∏è Coal Tar Preparations

Anti-inflammatory & anti-scaling for plaque psoriasis

When to Use

  • Scalp psoriasis (most common use)
  • Plaque psoriasis on body
  • Can buy OTC or prescribe
  • Try Vitamin D/steroids first (more evidence)
  • Review at 4 weeks

Available Forms

Shampoos
Lotions
Creams & Ointments
Bath Additives

How to Use

  • Apply to plaques (safe on non-psoriatic skin)
  • Avoid inflamed/broken skin, eyes, genitals
  • May stain clothes & increase sun sensitivity
üí° Modern formulations: More cosmetically acceptable than traditional versions

üü£ Dithranol Preparations

Anti-inflammatory, slows skin cell production

‚ö†Ô∏è Availability: No longer manufactured ‚Äì special-order prescription only (ointment)

When to Use

  • Well-defined plaques on limbs/torso only
  • NOT suitable for: small/widespread plaques, guttate, pustular psoriasis
  • Avoid face, genitals, skin folds

How to Use

  • Start with lowest strength, increase gradually
  • Apply once daily for 10-60 minutes, then wash off
  • Use daily until skin clear (up to 6 weeks)
  • Apply petroleum jelly border to protect normal skin
  • Moisturise before use
Side Effects: Skin irritation, staining (purple/brown ‚Äì harmless)
üëï Patient Advice: Stains skin, fabrics, surfaces. Use gloves, old clothes, bleach for tiles

‚òÄÔ∏è Ultraviolet Light Therapy

Reduces skin inflammation ‚Äì careful dosing essential

Two Types

Narrowband UVB

  • Guttate & plaque psoriasis
  • Widespread or topical-resistant
  • 2-3x/week, up to 10 weeks
  • TL-01 lamp standard

PUVA

  • UVA + Psoralen (P)
  • Moderate-severe plaque psoriasis
  • 2x/week, 5-8 weeks
  • Hand/foot psoriasis

When to Use

  • NICE: Offer UVB when topicals fail
  • PUVA for palmoplantar pustulosis
  • Can combine with topicals for stubborn areas
‚ö†Ô∏è Risks: Skin damage, premature aging, skin cancer (lifetime limit on treatments). May cause redness, tanning, burning
üö´ Sunbeds ‚â† Medical UV: Broader spectrum, uncontrolled dosing, mostly UVA (ineffective without psoralen)

üß¥ Prescribing Tips

  • Quantities: Adult using emollient all over needs ~500g/week
  • Finger-tip unit: 1 FTU = enough for 2 hand areas
  • Combination therapy: Vitamin D (AM) + Steroid (PM) can improve efficacy
  • Scalp: Part hair, apply to skin, not hair shafts
  • Compliance: Patient preference crucial ‚Äì let them choose formulation
