üß¥ Treatments from a GP (Topical Focus)

Moisturisers & Emollients ‚ñ∂

**Action:** Decreases dryness, scaling, cracking, soreness, and itching. May improve absorption of other active treatments.

**Application:** At least **twice a day**. Must be applied **before** active topical treatments (allow 10-60 mins to sink in). Gently stroking motion (follow hair line), avoid aggressive rubbing.

**Consistency/Examples:**

  • **Lotions:** (e.g., E45 lotion) - More water, less oil, cool, spreads easily (good for hairy areas).
  • **Creams:** (e.g., Diprobase) - Oil/water mix, less greasy (good for daytime/weeping skin).
  • **Ointments:** (e.g., Epaderm, 50/50 white soft paraffin/liquid paraffin) - Greasy, very effective water retention (good for night-time, fewer preservatives).

**Special Ingredients:**

  • **Anti-Pruritics:** Contain lauromacrogols (for itching).
  • **Keratolytics:** Contain salicylic acid or allantoin (for thick/built-up plaques).
Vitamin D Based Topicals (e.g., Calcipotriol) ‚ñ∂

**Action:** Slows down skin cell production and has an anti-inflammatory effect.

**Brands/Ingredients:**

  • Calcipotriol (Dovonex - OTC in some pharmacies, expensive).
  • Calcitriol (Silkis).
  • **Combination:** Calcipotriol + Steroid (Dovobet, Enstilar).

**Caution/Use:** Apply **after** emollient (30 mins later). Do not overuse (risk of calcium absorption). May increase sensitivity to UV light/sun. Review after **4 weeks** (2 weeks for children).

Topical Steroids (Corticosteroids) ‚ñ∂

**Action:** Reduces skin inflammation and irritation.

**Use Case:** Small amount of body coverage (not widespread). Sensitive areas (face, genitals, folds) need **less potent** steroids for shorter duration (e.g., 2 weeks).

**Potency Examples (Selected):**

  • **Very Potent:** Dermovate, Etrivex.
  • **Potent:** Betnovate, Elocon, Dovobet (Combination).
  • **Moderately Potent:** Eumovate.
  • **Mild:** Hydrocortisone 0.1-2.5%.

**Side Effects (Local):** Burning, stinging, thinning of the skin, hair growth. **Rare systemic effects** if used correctly.

Coal Tar Preparations ‚ñ∂

**Action:** Anti-inflammatory and anti-scaling properties. Good for scalp psoriasis.

**Formulations:** Lotions, creams, ointments, shampoos, bath additives (some OTC).

**Caution:** May cause skin irritation. **Do not use on inflamed/broken skin, near eyes, or genital areas.** May stain clothes/items. May increase sun sensitivity.

**Note:** Generally considered less effective than Vitamin D or Topical Steroids, but suitable for longer-term use.

Dithranol Preparations (Special Order Ointment) ‚ñ∂

**Action:** Slows down cell over-production, anti-inflammatory. No significant long-term side effects.

**Use Case:** Most appropriate for **well-defined plaques** on the limbs or torso. **NOT** for sensitive areas (face, genitals, folds), small/widespread plaques, or pustular forms.

**Application:** Start at **lowest strength**. Applied once a day for **10 min to 1 hour**, then washed off. May take up to **6 weeks** for clearance.

**Major Caution:** Significantly stains skin, fabrics, and surfaces (use gloves/old clothes). Irritates non-psoriatic skin (use Vaseline border).

üè• Treatments from a Dermatologist (Referral Required)

Ultraviolet (UV) Light Therapy ‚ñ∂

**Action:** UV light reduces inflammation. Effects are temporary.

**Type 1: Narrowband UVB (TL-01):** For widespread plaque or guttate psoriasis not controlled by topicals. 2-3 times a week, up to 10 weeks.

**Type 2: PUVA (Psoralen + UVA):** For moderate/severe plaque psoriasis, or hand/foot psoriasis (palmoplantar pustulosis). Psoralen (tablet/topical) makes skin sensitive to UVA. Twice a week, 5-8 weeks.

**Major Risk:** Skin damage, premature ageing, increased skin cancer risk (lifetime limits apply). PUVA requires **UVA-protective glasses** for 24 hours.

Systemic & Biologic Treatments ‚ñ∂

**Systemic Treatments:** Oral medications that work throughout the body. Used for severe psoriasis or when topical/UV treatments fail.

**Biologic Treatments:** Injections/infusions that target specific parts of the immune system. Reserved for severe cases that haven't responded to other treatments due to cost and potential side effects.

**Note:** Your role is typically referral; the Dermatologist manages these treatments.

This information is for rapid clinical reference only. Always consult patient information leaflets and discuss concerns with a pharmacist or specialist.
