Treatment
‚ñº

First-line Options

Small/Single Keloids

Intralesional corticosteroids (triamcinolone 10-40mg/ml)

Protocol:

  • Inject every 4-6 weeks
  • Continue until flattening occurs
  • Combine with 5-FU for resistant cases
  • Consider laser therapy (PDL or Nd:YAG)
Large/Multiple Keloids

Surgical excision + adjuvant therapy

Must use adjuvant therapy

Post-surgical options:

  • Radiotherapy (8-20 Gy in 1-3 fractions)
  • Corticosteroid injections
  • Pressure therapy
  • Silicone sheeting

Combination Therapies

Evidence-based
  • Triamcinolone + 5-FU (improved efficacy)
  • Laser + corticosteroids (synergistic effect)
  • Surgery + radiotherapy (lowest recurrence)

Emerging Therapies

  • Botulinum toxin (tension reduction)
  • Platelet-rich plasma
  • Microneedling
  • Cryotherapy (for small lesions)

Follow-up
‚ñº

Monitoring Schedule

Post-treatment monitoring

Every 4-6 weeks initially, then every 3-6 months

  • Assess for recurrence
  • Monitor treatment side effects
  • Patient-reported outcomes
  • Photographic documentation

Warning Signs

Return immediately
  • Rapid regrowth after treatment
  • New satellite lesions
  • Severe pain or infection
  • Functional limitation

Patient Education

Prevention key
  • Avoid unnecessary skin trauma
  • No piercings or tattoos
  • Early treatment of acne/inflammation
  • Sun protection for treated areas

Prognosis Counseling

Key points to discuss:

  • Chronic condition with recurrence risk
  • Multiple treatments often needed
  • Individual response varies
  • Psychological impact recognition
