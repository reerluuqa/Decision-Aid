üìà At a Glance

What is it?

Acute, self-limiting skin rash mainly affecting young adults (10-35 years). Characterized by distinctive scaly, erythematous lesions with a "herald patch" in up to 90% of cases.

Key Feature

Herald patch appears days to 3 weeks before generalized eruption. Typically 2-5cm, round/oval with peripheral "collarette" scaling.

Prognosis

  • Resolves in 2-6 weeks (up to 3-5 months max)
  • Mean duration: 45 days
  • Recurrence rate: 3-4%
  • No scarring; temporary pigment changes possible

üîç Diagnosis: When to Suspect

üî¥ Lesion Appearance

  • Pink-red, "salmon-colored"
  • Hypopigmented in dark skin
  • 0.5-1cm, circular/oval
  • Peripheral "collarette" scaling
  • Not vesicular, no palm/sole involvement

üìç Distribution

  • Symmetrical trunk, neck base, proximal limbs
  • "Christmas tree" pattern on chest/back
  • Face/scalp common in children
  • Oral lesions ~25% of cases

üéØ The Herald Patch

Appears in up to 90% of cases. Larger (2-5cm), sharply defined, with elevated collarette scale. Appears days to 3 weeks before general eruption.

üí°

Atypical Forms (20%)

  • Inverse: Face, axillae, groin predominant
  • Gigantean: Larger lesions
  • Vidal: Few large patches in axillae/inguinal region
  • Other: Pustular, purpuric, vesicular variants
