General Information

Eczema (Atopic Dermatitis)

Key Features
Itchy rash, family atopy, flexural involvement
Common Age
Childhood onset, rarely in adults
Clinical Pearls
  • If not itchy, unlikely to be eczema
  • Weepy eczema ≠ infected eczema (avoid unnecessary antibiotics)
  • Steroid phobia common - requires education
  • Pigmented skin: redness hard to appreciate, look for scale/papules
First-line Management
  • Emollients: 1000g/month in kids, soap substitutes, apply on damp skin
  • Topical steroids: Daily up to 14 days, taper to 2x/week for maintenance
  • Consider: Topical calcineurin inhibitors for face/steroid phobia

Eczema Herpeticum

URGENT: HSV infection on eczema background
Presentation
Monomorphic punched lesions, unwell, fever
Risk
Kids with background eczema + HFMD outbreak
  • Immediate action: Urgent referral if systemically unwell
  • Treatment: Antivirals (aciclovir) + treat underlying eczema
Differential
Impetigo Bullous impetigo Varicella

Scabies

Key Features
Intractable itch, worse at night, burrows
Distribution
Hands, ankles, waist, genitals (kids: can involve face)
Red Flag: Genital nodules = scabies until proven otherwise
  • First-line: Permethrin 5% cream - apply neck down, wash off after 8-12 hrs, repeat in 7 days
  • Hygiene: Hot wash clothing/bedding, vacuum, treat all contacts
  • Note: Itch can persist weeks after treatment

Impetigo

Key Features
Honey-colored crusts (cornflake-like), Staph/Strep
Risk Factors
Crowded environment, poor hygiene, warm climate
Assessment Tip
Ask: "Does it look like cornflakes?" If yes → impetigo
  • Mild: Fusidic acid topical
  • Severe/Widespread: Oral flucloxacillin
  • Bullous impetigo: Rare, consider referral to exclude other bullous diseases

Napkin Dermatitis

Contact Irritant
Spares skin folds, from urine/feces irritation
Candida
Satellite lesions, involves skin folds
Differential Diagnosis
Contact irritant Candida Psoriasis Seborrheic dermatitis
  • General: Keep dry, disposable nappies, avoid bubble baths/fragranced wipes
  • Candida: Topical antifungal (clotrimazole/miconazole)
  • If unsure: Swab and treat empirically

Infantile Hemangioma

URGENT REFERRAL IF: Obstructing vision/airway/feeding
Timeline
Appears weeks after birth, grows 4-6 months, regresses by age 4
Management
Usually observation, propranolol if treatment needed
High-Risk Locations
  • Face (eyes, lips) - risk obstruction
  • Chin - risk laryngeal extension
  • Scalp - risk skull extension
  • Lumbosacral - risk spinal extension
  • Refer urgently if: Obstructing, ulcerating, bleeding, or high-risk location
  • Otherwise: Simple reassurance, 80% regress by age 4

Tinea Capitis

URGENT: Can cause scarring alopecia if untreated
vs Alopecia Areata
Scaling, annular plaques, itchy (AA: no scale, not itchy)
Kerion
Boggy swelling with pustules, needs longer treatment
  • Do NOT: Incise and drain kerion - makes it worse
  • Treatment: Oral terbinafine/griseofulvin + ketoconazole shampoo 2x/week
  • Family: Treat all household contacts with shampoo

Molluscum Contagiosum

SELF-LIMITING: Usually resolves spontaneously
Appearance
Umbilicated skin-colored papules, multiple
Course
Red/angry appearance = herald of resolution (not infection)
Key Point
Treatment can cause scarring/post-inflammatory pigmentation, especially in darker skin
  • First-line: Reassurance, self-resolves in months
  • If treating: Molludan topical daily up to 14 days

Steroid Phobia Management

Common Concern
Parents afraid of steroids, use only 3 days max
Education Needed
Safe use guidelines, duration, potency selection
Key Talking Points
  • Robust evidence for long-term safety when used appropriately
  • Can use up to 14 days depending on area/potency
  • Better to treat early flare hard than let it become severe
  • Consider topical calcineurin inhibitors for face/steroid-phobic
Topical Steroid Withdrawal: Burning (not itch) after stopping, flushing, redness
  • Education: Provide written materials, direct to Eczema Society
  • Strategy: "Hit hard and taper" - treat flare immediately, then reduce

Pityriasis Rosea

SELF-LIMITING: Resolves in weeks-months
Key Feature
Herald patch → widespread rash
Distribution
Christmas tree pattern on trunk
Differential: Guttate Psoriasis
Guttate psoriasis: follows strep throat, teardrop scaly papules
  • Management: Reassurance, moisturizers, resolves in 6-12 weeks
  • If persistent: Refer for phototherapy consideration

Alopecia Areata

Key Features
Non-scarring, exclamation mark hairs, yellow dots on dermoscopy
Prognosis
High likelihood of regrowth (non-scarring)
Treatment Options
  • Topical steroids (clobetasol) daily → twice weekly
  • Intralesional steroid injections
  • Protopic for eyebrows
  • JAK inhibitors for >50% hair loss (age >12)
  • Primary care: Start topical steroids, refer if not improving
  • Important: Rule out tinea capitis (scaling, itchy)
