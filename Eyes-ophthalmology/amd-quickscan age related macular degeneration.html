üî¨ Diagnostic Approach

1

History & Amsler Grid

Ask about distortion, scotoma, recent vision changes. Amsler grid reveals metamorphopsia or blind spots.

2

Dilated Fundus Examination

Look for drusen size/quantity, pigmentary changes, subretinal hemorrhage, fluid.

3

OCT (First-Line Imaging)

Detects subretinal/intraretinal fluid, retinal thickening, MNV. Essential for monitoring treatment response.

4

OCT-Angiography (If Available)

Non-invasive MNV detection without dye. Cannot show active leakage - correlates with OCT findings.

5

Fluorescein Angiography (If Needed)

Gold standard for MNV confirmation and active leakage. Order if OCT suggests neovascular AMD.

üíä Treatment Algorithm

Prevention (All Stages)

  • Smoking cessation counseling
  • Blood pressure control
  • UV protection
  • Diet rich in leafy greens, fish

AREDS2 Supplements

  • Category 3-4 only:
  • Vitamin C 500mg
  • Vitamin E 400IU
  • Zinc 80mg + Copper 2mg
  • Lutein 10mg + Zeaxanthin 2mg

Neovascular AMD (Wet) - URGENT

Anti-VEGF intravitreal injections: Aflibercept, Ranibizumab, or Bevacizumab. Loading phase (3 monthly) then PRN or treat-and-extend regimen. Requires retinal specialist.

Geographic Atrophy (Dry)

No proven treatment to reverse atrophy. New therapies (Syfovre, Izervay) available in some regions to slow progression. Monitor for conversion to wet AMD.

üì± Patient Education & Self-Monitoring

Amsler Grid Instructions

  • Test each eye separately (with reading glasses)
  • Use in good lighting, hold at normal reading distance
  • Report any: distortion, waviness, dark spots, missing areas
  • Frequency: Daily for wet AMD risk, weekly for intermediate
