Meibomian Cyst (Chalazion) – Quick-Scan

Red Flags ‑ Emergency / Urgent

  • Severe pain, ↓ vision, diplopia, limited eye movement, proptosis, fever
  • Hard, warm, tender, erythematous lid → orbital / periorbital cellulitis
  • Atypical features: lid distortion, lash loss, ulceration, bleeding
  • Recurrent lesion same site (esp. >50 y) → sebaceous Ca
Same-day ophthalmology/A&E

First-Contact Actions

  • Reassure: usually self-limiting weeks–months
  • Warm compress 10–15 min, ≤5×/day
  • Lid massage (toward lashes) after heat
  • Treat blepharitis / rosacea
  • NO routine antibiotics
Routine + self-care

Differential Diagnosis

  • Stye (hordeolum) – acute, painful pustule (lid margin or tarsal)
  • Dacryocystitis – medial canthus pain/swelling, discharge
  • Sebaceous cyst – non-ocular sites, implantation history
  • Sebaceous gland carcinoma – chronic, recurrent, older age

Complications

  • Mechanical ptosis, astigmatism, amblyopia (kids)
  • Chronic skin changes
  • Rare secondary infection → cellulitis

Risk Factors

  • Chronic blepharitis, seborrhoeic dermatitis, rosacea
  • Diabetes, elevated cholesterol, pregnancy
  • Prior stye, contact lens, cosmetics
