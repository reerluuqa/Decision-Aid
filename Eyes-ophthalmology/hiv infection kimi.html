HIV Clinical Reference

Quick-scan guide for primary care clinicians
EMERGENCY REFERRAL: Suspected advanced HIV with CD4 <200 or AIDS-defining illness → Immediate specialist referral
Red Flags - Immediate Action
Primary Infection (2-4 weeks post-exposure)
Fever + rash + lymphadenopathy → Test immediately
AIDS-defining Conditions
Pneumocystis pneumonia, CMV retinitis, Kaposi's sarcoma, TB
CD4 Count <200 cells/μL
Immediate specialist referral required
Green Actions - Routine Care
Annual HIV Testing
MSM, Black Africans, PWID, Sex workers, Trans women
Test When:
New STI diagnosis, unexplained fever, lymphadenopathy, weight loss
U=U Message
Undetectable = Untransmittable with ART adherence
Testing Window Periods
Test Type Window Period Retest If Negative
4th Generation Lab 45 days After 45 days
3rd Generation POCT 60 days After 60 days
Self-test Kits 90 days After 90 days
ART Monitoring
CD4 Count:
Normal: >500 cells/μL
High risk: <200 cells/μL
Viral Load:
Target: <20-50 copies/mL
Test every 3-6 months
HIV Indicator Conditions
Category 1 - AIDS-defining (0.1% prevalence)
TB, Kaposi's sarcoma, Pneumocystis pneumonia, CMV retinitis
Category 2a - >0.1% prevalence
STIs
Herpes zoster
Hepatitis B/C
Unexplained fever
Category 2b - Likely >0.1%
Oral candidiasis
Chronic diarrhea
Weight loss
PrEP & PEP
PrEP Indications:
MSM, trans women, PWID, partner with HIV not suppressed
PEP Window:
Start within 24 hours, maximum 72 hours post-exposure
Not needed if: Partner on ART >6 months with undetectable VL
Drug Interactions & Complications
Critical Interactions:
Statins (especially simvastatin), anticoagulants, contraceptives
Monitor for:
Lactic acidosis, hepatotoxicity, bone marrow suppression
Check before prescribing:
www.hiv-druginteractions.org
Routine Monitoring:
Lipids, glucose, renal function, bone density
