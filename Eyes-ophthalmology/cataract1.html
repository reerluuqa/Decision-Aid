Assessment

Initial patient evaluation and risk assessment

‚ñº
Visual Acuity Testing
Stable
Test best-corrected visual acuity at standardized distance and illumination. Document near and far vision capabilities.
  • Use Snellen chart for distance vision
  • Test near vision with reading cards
  • Document current spectacle prescription
Slit Lamp Examination
Caution
Evaluate lens opacification following pupillary dilation. Assess cataract type and density.
  • Nuclear sclerotic changes
  • Cortical opacities
  • Posterior subcapsular involvement
Risk Factor Assessment
Caution
Identify contributing factors and comorbidities that may affect management decisions.
  • Age >60 years
  • Diabetes mellitus
  • Previous eye trauma
  • Long-term corticosteroid use

Diagnosis

Confirm diagnosis and determine severity

‚ñº
Visual Function Assessment
Stable
Evaluate impact on daily activities and quality of life. Determine if vision loss affects functional capacity.
  • Difficulty with reading or driving
  • Problems with glare or halos
  • Impact on work or hobbies
Cataract Classification
Caution
Classify cataract type and grade severity to guide treatment planning.
  • Nuclear Sclerotic: Yellowing of lens nucleus
  • Cortical: Wedge-shaped opacities in cortex
  • Posterior Subcapsular: Opacity at posterior pole
Differential Diagnosis
Urgent Referral
Rule out other causes of vision loss that may require different management.
  • Refractive error
  • Glaucoma
  • Macular degeneration
  • Diabetic retinopathy

Treatment

Management options and surgical planning

‚ñº
Conservative Management
Stable
For early cataracts without significant functional impairment.
  • Optimize refractive correction
  • Increase illumination for reading
  • Use tinted lenses for glare reduction
  • Regular monitoring every 6-12 months
Surgical Planning
Caution
Pre-operative assessment and IOL selection for cataract surgery.
  • Biometry and IOL power calculation
  • Corneal topography for astigmatism
  • IOL type selection (monofocal, toric, multifocal)
  • Patient expectation counseling
Surgical Procedure
Urgent Referral
Phacoemulsification with IOL implantation - the gold standard treatment.
  • Topical or local anesthesia
  • 2.2-2.8mm clear corneal incision
  • Phacoemulsification of cataract
  • Posterior chamber IOL implantation
  • Same-day discharge typically

Follow-up

Post-operative care and monitoring

‚ñº
Immediate Post-op (24-48 hours)
Urgent Referral
Early post-operative assessment to identify complications.
  • Visual acuity check
  • Slit lamp examination for inflammation
  • Intraocular pressure measurement
  • Wound integrity assessment
Medication Regimen
Caution
Post-operative medication to prevent infection and inflammation.
  • Topical antibiotics (1 week)
  • Topical corticosteroids (4-6 weeks)
  • NSAID eye drops (4-6 weeks)
  • Follow tapering schedule carefully
Long-term Monitoring
Stable
Regular follow-up to monitor for late complications and visual outcomes.
  • 1 week: Visual acuity and inflammation check
  • 1 month: Refraction for glasses prescription
  • 3 months: Final visual assessment
  • Annual: Monitor for posterior capsule opacification
