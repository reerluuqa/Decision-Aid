üî¨ Key Investigations

  • CT Orbits + Sinuses with contrast

    Gold standard. Look for subperiosteal abscess, sinus disease, orbital involvement

  • MRI Head/Orbits with contrast

    If neurological signs or cavernous sinus thrombosis suspected

  • WBC Count

    Usually elevated. Does not differentiate types

  • Blood Cultures

    Before antibiotics. Positive in 0-33% of cases

  • LP if meningeal signs

    For drowsiness, vomiting, headache, neck stiffness

‚ö° Life-Threatening Complications

  • Cavernous Sinus Thrombosis

    Bilateral signs, cranial nerve palsies, V1/V2 involvement

  • Subperiosteal Abscess

    Most common orbital complication. Often requires surgical drainage

  • Intracranial Abscess

    Frontal sinusitis increases risk. Can be fatal

  • Permanent Vision Loss

    From increased orbital pressure or optic nerve compromise

  • Orbital Compartment Syndrome

    Requires immediate lateral canthotomy to save vision

üíä Special Populations

Immunocompromised / Diabetic

Consider fungal infections (Mucor, Aspergillus). Look for black eschar, necrotic tissue. Early Amphotericin B is critical.

Children Specific:

  • Higher orbital cellulitis risk

    Children 2x more likely than adults. May progress rapidly

  • Hib vaccination protective

    Routine vaccination has dramatically reduced Hib-related cases

  • IV therapy preferred in children

    Oral therapy only with reliable daily follow-up
