üìùKey Symptoms

Symptoms are usually bilateral and vary between individuals. Clinical features do not necessarily correlate with disease severity:

üî• Irritation & Discomfort

Burning, stinging, or 'gritty' foreign body sensation

üëÅÔ∏è Dryness

üå°Ô∏è Sensitivity

üíß Discharge

üîçAssessment Questions

  • Any red flag symptoms

‚ö†Ô∏èRed Flag Symptoms

  • Sudden-onset pain or visual loss
  • Persistent or severe visual loss
  • Diplopia (double vision)
  • Unilateral symptoms
  • Systemic symptoms: weight loss or fever

üéØExacerbating Factors

  • Environmental conditions (AC, heating, wind)
  • Allergies or low blink rate
  • Digital device use or reading

  • Ever eyelids: check for blepharitis/Meibomian gland dysfunction

üè∑Ô∏èClassification

üí®Evaporative Dry Eye

  • More common type
  • Increased tear film evaporation
  • Deficient lipid layer (Meibomian gland dysfunction)

üíßAqueous Deficient Dry Eye

  • Reduced aqueous secretion from lacrimal glands
  • Reduced tear volume
  • Often overlaps with evaporative type

üí°Important Note

Both types often overlap and co-exist in the same patient. Management should address both components.

üíäTear Substitutes (Artificial Tears)

üíßFirst-line Treatment

Advise when lifestyle measures insufficient for symptom relief

üß¥Formulations Available

  • Eye drops (low viscosity)
  • Gels (medium viscosity)
  • Ointments (high viscosity)
  • Sprays (liposomal)

‚òÄÔ∏èDaytime Use

  • Low-viscosity drops recommended
  • Reduced risk of blurred vision
  • Less eyelid debris
  • Examples: hypromellose, carbomers

üåôNight-time Use

  • Increased viscosity ointments/gels
  • Longer-lasting lubrication
  • May cause temporary blurred vision

üåøPreservative-Free

  • If intolerant to preservatives
  • Moderate-to-severe disease (>4 applications/day)
  • Multiple topical preparations
  • Soft/hybrid contact lens users
üéØPreferred Option

üí°Product Selection

For complete list of UK products, see BNF or electronic Medicines Compendium (eMC). Options include hypromellose (temporary relief, frequent application) and carbomers (more viscous, longer-lasting).

üíäAlternative Preparations

If symptoms not relieved with initial topical preparation:

üíßSodium Hyaluronate

  • Better retention on ocular surface
  • May provide longer relief

üßàLipid-based Preparations

  • Especially if Meibomian gland dysfunction present
  • Stabilizes tear film lipid layer

üí®Liposomal Spray

  • Alternative delivery method
  • May be easier for some patients

üìûSeek Specialist Advice

If uncertain which topical preparation to recommend or prescribe, seek specialist advice before proceeding.
