Pterygium Clinical Reference

Based on BMJ Best Practice (Last updated: Nov 28, 2024)

Overview & Immediate Recognition
Definition Wing-shaped fibrovascular overgrowth from conjunctiva onto cornea.
Triangular shape (Body, Head, Cap). Occurs interpalpebrally, usually nasal side, often bilateral.
‚ö° Critical Findings (Red Flags)
  • Double Vision: Tethering of medial rectus (Rare but significant).
  • Visual Axis Encroachment: Growth to centre of cornea causing reduced vision.
  • Irregular Astigmatism: Significant blurring.

Surgical Techniques Comparison
Technique Selection
Conjunctival Autografting
Bare sclera covered with free graft from superior bulbar conjunctiva. Often includes limbal tissue.
RECOMMENDED
Amniotic Membrane Transplantation
Used for extensive pterygium or glaucoma patients (preserve superior conjunctiva).
Alternative
Simple Excision (Bare Sclera)
Shearing off head/body. Quick but high failure rate.
High Recurrence (>33%)
Adjunctive Therapies (Mitomycin C, 5-FU, Beta-radiation)
Used to reduce recurrence in recurrent cases.
