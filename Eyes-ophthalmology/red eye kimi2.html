Red Eye Assessment

Quick Reference Guide for Primary Care
⚠️ IMMEDIATE SAME-DAY REFERRAL REQUIRED FOR ACUTE GLAUCOMA, CORNEAL ULCER, OR TRAUMA
RED FLAGS - Sight Threatening
Reduced visual acuity
Moderate to severe pain
Photophobia
Ciliary injection
Corneal staining
Abnormal pupils
High-velocity injury
Contact lens use
Neonatal conjunctivitis
IMMEDIATE ACTIONS
Chemical Injury IRRIGATE IMMEDIATELY with water/saline
Acute Glaucoma Same-day ophthalmology referral
Corneal Ulcer Same-day ophthalmology referral
Trauma Same-day ophthalmology referral
Key Assessment Points
History Examination
Onset & duration Visual acuity (Snellen)
Unilateral/bilateral Pattern of redness
Contact lens use Fluorescein staining
Trauma history Pupil assessment
Associated symptoms Eye pressure (if indicated)
Differential Diagnosis
Condition Key Features Urgency
Conjunctivitis Discharge, irritation Routine
Episcleritis Segmental redness, painless Routine
Subconjunctival hemorrhage Localized redness, no pain Routine
Anterior uveitis Pain, photophobia 24h referral
