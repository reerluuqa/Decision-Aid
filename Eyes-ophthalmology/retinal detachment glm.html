Retinal Detachment - Quick Clinical Reference

Last revised: August 2024
For NHS Primary Care Professionals

IMMEDIATE REFERRAL REQUIRED

Same-day referral to ophthalmologist with retinal surgery expertise for any visual field loss, changes in visual acuity, or fundoscopic signs of retinal detachment/vitreous haemorrhage

Red Flag Symptoms

  • New onset floaters (dots, lines, or haze)
  • New onset flashes (recurrent, brief)
  • Sudden painless progressive visual field loss (dark curtain/shadow)
  • Reduced visual acuity or blurred/distorted vision
  • Loss of central vision

Definition & Types

Retinal Detachment

Separation of the inner neurosensory retina from the underlying retinal pigment epithelium, allowing fluid to accumulate in the subretinal space

Rhegmatogenous (Most Common)

Vitreous shrinks and separates from retina, causing a tear. Fluid enters subretinal space through the tear.

Exudative

Caused by leakage of fluid into subretinal space, often due to inflammatory or malignant conditions.

Tractional

Most common in proliferative diabetic retinopathy. Vitreous contracts and pulls on retina without a break.

Key Risk Factors

  • Myopia (especially >3 dioptres)
  • Family history of retinal break/detachment
  • Previous retinal break/detachment in either eye
  • Eye trauma (especially blunt)
  • Previous cataract surgery
  • Proliferative diabetic retinopathy
  • Inflammatory conditions (uveitis, scleritis)
  • Increasing age

Assessment Approach

History

Characteristics, duration, and laterality of flashes/floaters

Visual Changes

Onset, nature, and duration of any change in vision

Personal/Family History

Previous retinal break/detachment in either eye

Visual Acuity

Test using Snellen chart (may be reduced to finger counting if macula detached)

Visual Field

Perform confrontational field testing

Fundoscopic Exam

Look for asymmetric red reflex, vitreous opacities, or detached retinal folds

Patient Advice

Driving
Contact DVLA if visual field defect and/or had retinal treatment in both eyes
Warning Signs
Provide information on early warning signs of possible future retinal tear/detachment
Eye Protection
Advise wearing eye protection when doing at-risk sports to reduce risk of future eye injury
Fellow Eye
Approximately 1 in 8 people develop detachment in the contralateral eye
