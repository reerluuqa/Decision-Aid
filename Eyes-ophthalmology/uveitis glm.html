Uveitis Quick Reference

Last revised: March 2025
URGENT: Same-day ophthalmology referral required for all suspected uveitis cases

Definition & Types

Uveitis: Inflammatory condition affecting the uveal tract (iris, ciliary body, choroid)

Anterior (75%)
Intermediate (15%)
Posterior (15-30%)
Panuveitis
Posterior uveitis has poorer prognosis with higher morbidity

Causes & Risk Factors

Common Causes:

  • Idiopathic (>50%)
  • Autoimmune disorders
  • Infections
  • Trauma/surgery

Risk Factors:

  • Previous uveitis
  • Autoimmune disease
  • Age 20-50 years
  • HLA-B27 positivity
Ask about autoimmune disease history during assessment

Symptoms by Type

Type Key Symptoms Typical Presentation
Anterior Pain, redness, photophobia, blurred vision Unilateral, acute onset
Intermediate Blurred vision, floaters Painless, no prominent redness
Posterior Blurred vision, floaters Painless, no prominent redness
Panuveitis Combination of above Variable presentation

Assessment

1
Take history: symptoms, onset, duration, laterality
2
Examine: lids, conjunctiva, discharge, redness pattern
3
Check: visual acuity, pupil reactions, photophobia
4
Look for: 360Â° perilimbal injection, pupillary miosis
If globe perforation suspected, do not palpate the eye
Diagnosis must be confirmed by slit-lamp examination and dilated fundus examination

Management

Do NOT initiate treatment in primary care unless directed by ophthalmologist
Arrange same-day ophthalmology referral for suspected uveitis
Continue and monitor treatment prescribed by ophthalmologist
Provide verbal and written information on uveitis and treatment
Advise patient to seek urgent medical advice if symptoms worsen

Differential Diagnosis

Acute glaucoma - severe eye pain, nausea/vomiting, halos around lights
Endophthalmitis - severe pain, vision loss, red eye, history of recent surgery/trauma
Keratitis - pain, redness, photophobia, blurred vision
Scleritis - severe pain worse with eye movement, often associated with headache
Ocular trauma - penetrating injury, foreign body, chemical burns
Dry eye syndrome - gritty sensation, burning, redness
Episcleritis - redness, mild discomfort, no vision loss
Infective conjunctivitis - redness, discharge, gritty sensation
Allergic conjunctivitis - itching, redness, watery discharge
Superficial corneal injury - pain, redness, tearing, photophobia
Photokeratitis - pain, redness, photophobia after UV exposure
Subconjunctival hemorrhage - bright red patch, no pain
Lymphoma
Leukemia
Ocular melanoma
Metastases
