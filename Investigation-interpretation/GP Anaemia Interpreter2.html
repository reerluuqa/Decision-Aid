Microcytic Anaemia MCV <80

Check Ferritin

Iron Deficiency Management

Prescribe: Oral iron OD (empty stomach)
• Ferrous sulfate 200mg, OR
• Ferrous fumarate 210mg, OR
• Ferrous gluconate 600mg
Avoid: Tea/coffee (↓90% absorption), antacids, food if possible
Cancer Investigation (IDA)
  • FIT test (all adults ≥10 µg Hb/g = refer)
  • Age ≥60: Routine FIT + OGD/colonoscopy consideration
  • Men ≥40 + post-menopausal women: GI investigation
  • Coeliac serology (duodenal biopsy if positive)
Microcytosis without Anaemia

Red flag for malignancy if age ≥40 (men) or ≥70 (all)
Associated with: Colorectal, lung, kidney, stomach, lymphoma

Anaemia of Chronic Disease

Ferritin normal/high + Low serum iron + Low TIBC

Consider: CKD, Heart failure, RA/SLE, TB, Malignancy, Liver disease

Trial of IV iron if: CKD (dialysis), IBD, Heart failure, Late pregnancy, or oral intolerance

Normocytic Anaemia MCV 80-100

Consider:
  • Anaemia of chronic disease (most common)
  • Early iron deficiency (check ferritin)
  • Mixed deficiency
  • Myeloma (if age appropriate + symptoms)
  • CKD (check eGFR)

Macrocytic Anaemia MCV >100

URGENT
⚠️ Neurological symptoms present
Start B12 IMMEDIATELY (do not wait for results)
Other Causes to Consider
Drugs: Methotrexate, Anticonvulsants, PPIs, Metformin, Nitrous oxide
Other: Alcohol, Liver disease, Hypothyroidism, Pregnancy, MDS/Myeloma

B12 Replacement

Definite deficiency (<180): IM Hydroxocobalamin 1mg
• Alternate days × 1 week, then weekly × 4 weeks, then 3-monthly for life
If neurological symptoms: continue 2-weekly until no further improvement
Possible deficiency (180-350): Check MMA/homocysteine or trial of therapy if symptomatic

Folate Replacement

Folic acid 5mg daily × 4 months
⚠️ NEVER give folate alone if B12 deficient (risk of neurological damage)
