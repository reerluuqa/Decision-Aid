💡 Worked Example — Raj's HbA1c

Raj's HbA1c was 49 mmol/mol (6.6%) and six months later is 45 mmol/mol (6.3%).

The change is 8.2% — but HbA1c falls in the 11–20% variation band.
This means the change is likely due to normal variation and is not clinically significant.

⚠️ Don't attribute this to lifestyle changes or treatment effect — you'd need a change of at least 11% to be confident it's a real change.

⚡ Key Points for Practice

  • All test results vary naturally due to analytical and biological variation
  • Two different results for the same test on the same patient may not be significant
  • Low-variability tests (e.g. sodium): even small changes are clinically meaningful
  • High-variability tests (e.g. iron, triglycerides): large changes may still be normal variation
  • Avoid over-interpreting normal variation as a treatment effect
  • Avoid missing true changes by dismissing them as noise
  • Particularly relevant at diagnostic thresholds (e.g. HbA1c for diabetes diagnosis)
