🩸 "Check I Am Well" – Clinical Navigator

Asymptomatic blood test requests | Primary Care Decision Support

⚠️ Core Risks of Saying "Yes" ▼
  • Missed undisclosed symptoms
  • Over-investigation in low-risk individuals
  • False positives → cascade of harm & anxiety
  • Reinforcement of health anxiety
  • Tests often fail to provide durable reassurance
Key Principle: Investigations answer a specific clinical query, not "just in case."
🧭 Clinical Flow Navigator
Step 1

Any Symptoms? (Including subtle/red flags)

Undisclosed symptoms may prompt further attendance

Step 2

Any Risk Factors?

e.g., alcohol excess, HIV/hepatitis risk, strong family history of cancer

Step 3

Any Particular Worries?

e.g., "Dad had cancer," specific feared diagnoses

Step 4

NHS Health Check / Screening Eligible?

Asymptomatic & eligible for NHS screening programme

Step 5

Will Testing Actually Relieve Concern?

Consider: Is this health anxiety? Will normal result reassure long-term?

✓ Action: Focused history & examination → Targeted tests (not screening)
✓ Action: Targeted evaluation for specific risk factor
✓ Action: Address concern specifically ± targeted testing. Use communication scripts.
✓ Action: Advise attendance for NHS Health Check or Screening Programme as appropriate.
⚠️ Caution: If proceeding, set explicit expectations: "I expect this to be normal. We will not repeat if negative."
🗣️ Action: Avoid testing. Focus on communication, lifestyle optimisation, and uncertainty management.

🎣 The "Fishing Trip" Problem ▼

Inflammatory Markers (BJGP 2019)

15% Disease incidence if raised
85% False positives (no illness)
<50% Sensitivity
4% False negative rate

Per 1000 tests: 236 false positives → 710 GP appointments + 229 phlebotomy + 24 referrals

FBC Screening (Can Fam Physician 2017)

  • 11% abnormal results
  • <1% changed management
  • No mortality benefit

General Limitations

  • Reference ranges: 5% of healthy people fall outside "normal"
  • Significant biological/analytical variation on repeat
  • Incidental findings → insurance/health anxiety issues
💬 High-Yield Communication Scripts
Validate & Empathise
"It sounds like you are quite concerned about this. Being worried about your health can have a big impact on your life."
Explain Limitations
"A negative result doesn't necessarily rule out a health problem, and many people only feel reassured for a short time. Around 1 in 20 results come back just outside the normal range but are not necessarily a sign of illness."
Set Expectations
"I'm expecting the result to be normal, and we won't need to repeat the test if it's negative."
Avoid Over-investigation
"It's important to only carry out tests when we really need them, otherwise they are less helpful and can make people unnecessarily anxious. Living with uncertainty is a real challenge—it's not always possible to be totally 'sure' that you are well, no matter how many tests we do."
Twin-Track Approach
"I take your health concerns very seriously and I'm always willing to review the physical aspects of your health. You seem very worried about your health. We can help with this, and this will not affect any other treatment. I'm wondering if worries about your health are also having an impact on your wellbeing?"
Screen for Anxiety
"How anxious are you about this? How much time do you spend thinking about it? Do you often find yourself checking your health, looking up symptoms on the internet, or seeking reassurance?"
Redirect to Lifestyle
"It is often more helpful to focus on changing lifestyle to maximise health rather than carrying out tests without a clear indication. Could we discuss some ways to improve your quality of life that may also benefit your physical health?"
✅ What Does Success Look Like? ▼

Success depends on patient context:

  • ✓ Appropriate testing performed based on symptoms/risk
  • ✓ Psychological driver identified (health anxiety/depression addressed)
  • ✓ Patient reassured without testing (shift toward lifestyle optimisation)
Bottom Line: Routine blood testing in asymptomatic individuals is low-value, high-noise medicine. The consultation is fundamentally about uncertainty management, not diagnostics. The highest-yield intervention is skilled communication, not investigation.
