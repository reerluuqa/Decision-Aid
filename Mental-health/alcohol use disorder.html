AUD Clinical Quick-Scan

Updated: Sep 25, 2025

Immediate Action Required

Assess for withdrawal and acute physiological complications immediately.

Withdrawal Risk

CIWA-Ar Score
≥ 8-10
Treat with pharmacological agents immediately.

Look for:

  • Autonomic hyperactivity (Tachycardia, Hypertension)
  • Agitation, Nervousness
  • Generalized seizures
  • Delirium Tremens (Confusion, Hallucinations)

Intoxication Levels

Severe BAC > 200 mg/dL with minimal impairment indicates high tolerance.
Lethal BAC of 400 mg/dL is lethal for 50% of adults.

Note: Withdrawal can occur even if alcohol is still present in serum if levels drop for that individual.

Red Flags on Exam

  • • Jaundice / Ascites (Cirrhosis)
  • • Tremors / Anxiety
  • • Broad-based gait (Cerebellar degeneration)
  • • Peripheral neuropathy (Lower extremities)
  • • Underweight status / Anorexia

SCREENING & DEFINITIONS

"At-Risk" Drinking (NIAAA)

Unhealthy alcohol use limits. Exceeding these warrants intervention.

Gender Daily Limit Weekly Limit
Men > 4 drinks > 14 drinks
Women > 3 drinks > 7 drinks
Binge Drinking: BAC ≥ 0.08% (approx. 5 drinks men / 4 drinks women in 2 hours).

Quick Screeners

SASQ (Single Item):
"How many times in the past year have you had X+ drinks in a day?"
Any answer other than 0 = Positive.
AUDIT-C (3 Questions):
  • Frequency of consumption
  • Typical drinks per occasion
  • Frequency of heavy episodes
Use full AUDIT (10 qns) if secondary screen needed. Score ≥ 8 indicates at-risk use.

MANAGEMENT & TREATMENT

Treatment Goals

Driven by patient informed decision-making. Options include abstinence, reduction, or harm reduction.

Pharmacotherapy Options

Drug Mechanism / Use
Naltrexone Opioid antagonist. Reduces heavy drinking & craving.
Acamprosate Stabilizes glutamate. Maintains abstinence (post-withdrawal).
Disulfiram Aldehyde dehydrogenase inhibitor. Causes aversive reaction to alcohol.
Gabapentin Adjunct for insomnia/anxiety; improves abstinence.
Topiramate Reduces craving; useful if other agents fail.

*Treat nicotine-use disorder concurrently (70% co-occurrence).

Monitoring & Labs

  • Gamma-GT: Correlates with consumption. Normalises 2-6 weeks abstinence.
  • MCV: Macrocytosis. Normalises 3-4 months abstinence.
  • PEth (Phosphatidylethanol): Detects recent use. Normalises 2-5 weeks.
  • CDT (Carbohydrate-deficient transferrin): Long-term use marker. High specificity, low sensitivity.
Action: If elevated transaminases (AST/ALT), encourage 1-2 months reduction/stop to see improvement.
