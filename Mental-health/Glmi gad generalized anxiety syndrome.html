GAD Clinical Algorithm

Generalized Anxiety Disorder Assessment & Management Guide for Primary Care

Identification

Consider GAD in patients presenting with:

  • Chronic excessive worry disproportionate to circumstances
  • Physical symptoms: headaches, muscle tension, GI symptoms, back pain, insomnia
  • Frequent attenders with unexplained somatic symptoms
  • Patients with chronic physical health problems
  • Repeated worrying about multiple issues

Assessment

GAD-7 Questionnaire (Score 0-21)

Minimal anxiety 0-4
Mild anxiety 5-9
Moderate anxiety 10-14
Severe anxiety 15-21

History & Examination

  • Duration, severity, and nature of symptoms
  • Current stressors, trauma history
  • Mental health and family history
  • Comorbid physical conditions
  • Substance use history
  • Medication review (some medications can cause anxiety)
  • Physical exam: May show increased HR, shortness of breath, tremor, exaggerated startle
  • Assess suicide risk (especially with comorbid depression)

Diagnostic Criteria

DSM-5-TR

  • Excessive anxiety/worry ≥6 months about multiple events
  • Difficulty controlling worry
  • ≥3 symptoms: restlessness, fatigue, poor concentration, irritability, muscle tension, sleep disturbance
  • Significant distress/impairment
  • Not due to substances or another mental disorder

ICD-11

  • Marked anxiety with general apprehensiveness or excessive worry
  • Additional symptoms: muscle tension, autonomic overactivity, nervousness, poor concentration, irritability, sleep disturbance
  • Symptoms persist several months, cause significant distress/impairment

Differential Diagnosis

  • Situational anxiety (related to specific life event)
  • Adjustment disorder (temporary, ≤6 months after stressor)
  • Depression (anhedonia, hopelessness)
  • Panic disorder (sudden episodes with ≥4 physical symptoms)
  • Social phobia (limited to social situations)
  • Obsessive-compulsive disorder
  • Post-traumatic stress disorder
  • Substance-induced anxiety disorder

Management

Goals

  • Identify GAD accurately
  • Treat effectively to relieve symptoms and restore function
  • Minimize adverse effects
  • Refer for specialized treatment when necessary

Quality Standards

  • Comprehensive assessment identifying specific disorder, severity, and functional impairment
  • Offer evidence-based psychological interventions
  • Avoid benzodiazepines/antipsychotics unless specifically indicated
  • Document treatment response at each session

When to Refer

  • Severe symptoms or significant functional impairment
  • Comorbid depression or high suicide risk
  • Inadequate response to initial treatment
  • Diagnostic uncertainty

Complications & Prognosis

  • Chronic condition with fluctuating severity
  • High comorbidity with depression (≥50%)
  • Increased risk of substance misuse
  • Appropriate treatment improves symptoms, functioning, and quality of life
  • Prognosis worse with comorbid depression
