Clinical Quick Ref: Social Anxiety Disorder

BMJ Best Practice Summary

URGENT: Screen for Suicide & Comorbidity

70-80% have comorbid anxiety, mood, or substance-use disorders. Always assess for suicidality and substance misuse.

Quick Navigation
Red Flags & Urgent Actions
Suicide Risk Assessment
Depression present in >45% of cases. Always conduct formal suicide risk assessment. Consider PHQ-9 for depression screening.
Substance Misuse Screening
40% have history of substance misuse/dependence. Screen using urine/blood toxicology. Avoid benzodiazepines in this population.
Bipolar Disorder Warning
If bipolar suspected, initiate mood stabilizer first. Antidepressants may precipitate mania. Refer to psychiatrist.
3-Question Quick Screen
  • "Do you feel anxious or uncomfortable being around other people?"
  • "Are you worried about being rejected, embarrassed, or criticized by others?"
  • "Is this anxiety interfering with your life or causing you to avoid situations?"

If affirmative to multiple questions → Administer standardized screening tool (Mini-SPIN, LSAS)

DSM-5 Diagnostic Criteria
  • 1Marked fear/anxiety in social situations where exposed to possible scrutiny by others
  • 2Fear of acting in a way (or showing anxiety) that will be humiliating, embarrassing, or lead to rejection
  • 3Exposure almost invariably provokes anxiety or panic attack
  • 4Fear is out of proportion to actual threat and sociocultural context
  • 5Situations are avoided or endured with intense anxiety/distress
  • 6Fear/avoidance interferes significantly with routine, relationships, or social activities
  • 7Duration must be at least 6 months (children and adults)
  • 8Not due to substance/medical condition; not better explained by another mental disorder
Screening Tool Scores
Mini-SPIN (3 items) Positive: ≥6
  • 0-5: Unlikely social anxiety disorder
  • 6+: Likely social anxiety disorder – further assessment indicated
GAD-7 (7 items, 0-21) Moderate: ≥10
  • 0-4: Minimal/no anxiety – re-monitor
  • 5-9: Mild – continue monitoring
  • 10-14: Moderate – possible anxiety disorder
  • 15-21: Severe – anxiety disorder likely
PHQ-9 (9 items, 0-27) Moderate: ≥10
  • 0-4: Minimal depression
  • 5-9: Mild – monitor
  • 10-14: Moderate – consider treatment
  • 15-19: Moderately severe
  • 20+: Severe – urgent intervention
Treatment Pathway
✓ First Line
  • CBT – Individual or group; 6-12 sessions, 60-120 min each
  • SSRI/SNRI – Sertraline, Paroxetine, Escitalopram, or Venlafaxine
  • Psychoeducation – Lifestyle factors, substance use, caffeine reduction
→ Second Line
  • Combination therapy – CBT + SSRI/SNRI
  • Benzodiazepines – Short-term only; avoid with substance misuse history
First-Line Medications
Drug Starting Dose Titration Maximum
Sertraline 25 mg daily +25-50 mg weekly 200 mg/day
Paroxetine 5-10 mg daily +10 mg weekly 60 mg/day
Escitalopram 10 mg daily +10 mg every 4 weeks 20 mg/day
Venlafaxine XR 37.5 mg daily +37.5-75 mg weekly 225 mg/day

Allow 12-week trial to assess efficacy. Most adverse effects are time-limited during titration.

Differential Diagnosis
Condition Key Differentiator
Panic Disorder Recurrent unexpected panic attacks; fear of panic sensations themselves
Agoraphobia Fear of escape/difficulty leaving safe place; not fear of evaluation
GAD Pervasive worry across multiple domains; not social-situations specific
Specific Phobia Fear of specific objects/situations, not fear of negative evaluation
AVPD Pervasive social inhibition; 36% comorbidity with social anxiety
Selective Mutism Failure to speak in specific situations; speaks normally otherwise
