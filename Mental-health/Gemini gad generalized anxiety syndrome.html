Generalized Anxiety Disorder (GAD)

A Quick-Reference Management Algorithm for Primary Care

🔍 1. Initial Assessment & Diagnosis

Suspect GAD if: Patient presents with chronic, excessive worry for at least 6 months, plus 3 or more of the following symptoms:

  • Restlessness or feeling "on edge"
  • Easily fatigued
  • Difficulty concentrating
  • Irritability
  • Muscle tension
  • Sleep disturbance

Assessment Steps

  • Take full history (stressors, comorbidities, suicide risk).
  • Perform physical exam to rule out organic causes.
  • Rule out key differentials (Depression, Panic Disorder, Hyperthyroidism, etc.).

Quantify Severity (GAD-7)

  • 5-9: Mild Anxiety
  • 10-14: Moderate Anxiety
  • 15-21: Severe Anxiety

🚶 2. Stepped-Care Management Pathway

1

Step 1: All Diagnosed GAD

Focus on foundational care and monitoring.

  • Communicate diagnosis and provide patient information.
  • Initiate active monitoring of symptoms.
  • Advise on self-care: Sleep Hygiene & Regular Exercise.
  • If comorbid disorder is more severe, treat that first.
2

Step 2: GAD Not Improved from Step 1

Offer low-intensity psychological interventions based on patient preference.

  • Individual non-facilitated self-help (CBT-based).
  • Individual guided self-help (practitioner-supported).
  • Psychoeducational groups (CBT-based).
3

Step 3: Marked Impairment OR No Improvement After Step 2

Offer a choice between high-intensity therapy OR drug treatment.

High-Intensity Psychological Intervention

  • Cognitive Behavioural Therapy (CBT)
  • Applied Relaxation

Drug Treatment Pathway

  1. 1st Line: SSRI (e.g., Sertraline - *off-label*).
  2. 2nd Line: Alternative SSRI or SNRI.
  3. 3rd Line: Consider Pregabalin.
Partial Response? Consider combining psychological and drug treatments.
4

Step 4: Complex, Treatment-Refractory GAD

Refer for specialist mental health assessment for patients with:

  • Severe needs and marked functional impairment.
  • No response to Step 3 interventions.
  • High risk of self-harm.

💊 3. Key Prescribing & Monitoring Considerations

General Monitoring

  • Review effectiveness & side effects every 2-4 weeks for first 3 months.
  • If effective, continue drug for at least 1 year.
  • Patients < 30 on SSRI/SNRI: Review within 1 week, then weekly for the first month due to suicide risk.

Pregnancy

  • Offer high-intensity psychological intervention first.
  • If medication is required, discuss risks/benefits and seek specialist advice.

Medications to AVOID in Primary Care

Do NOT offer Benzodiazepines (e.g., Diazepam) except for short-term use (2-4 weeks) during a crisis.

Do NOT offer Antipsychotics for GAD.
