Paracetamol Overdose: Clinical Companion
Updated: Oct 03, 2023

IMMEDIATE ACTION REQUIRED

Assess ABCDE immediately. Start Acetylcysteine (NAC) WITHOUT DELAY if:

  • Ingestion time unknown OR dose is potentially life-threatening.
  • Patient presents >8 hours post-ingestion with detectable levels.
  • Any signs of liver injury (Jaundice, RUQ pain, ALT > ULN, INR > 1.3).
Ingestion Classification
History
Acute Overdose

All doses taken within < 1 hour (usually self-harm).

Staggered Overdose

Excessive amounts taken over > 1 hour (self-harm intent).

Therapeutic Excess

Intent to treat pain/fever. Dose > licensed daily limit (4g) AND ≥ 75mg/kg.

Risk Assessment Tool
Critical

High-Risk Factors (Red Flags):

Alcohol Use Disorder Malnutrition / Anorexia HIV / Cystic Fibrosis Weight < 50kg CYP450 Inducers

Drugs: Carbamazepine, Phenytoin, Rifampicin, St John's Wort.

Diagnostics & Labs

Serum Paracetamol Level
Gold Standard
  • Do NOT interpret if drawn < 4 hours post-ingestion.
  • < 8 hrs post-ingestion: Wait until 4h mark to draw blood (unless delay >8h is certain).
  • > 8 hrs post-ingestion: Draw blood immediately.
  • Repeat level at 6h if level < threshold but patient took opioids (delayed gastric emptying).
> 700 mg/L + Coma = Massive Overdose (Renal Replacement may be needed)
Liver Function & Injury
Monitor
ALT Rapid rise in severe poisoning. Above ULN = Injury.
INR > 1.3 (absence of warfarin) = Injury. > 30s requires senior review.
Bilirubin Elevated levels suggest established necrosis.
Lactate > 3.5 mmol/L (adm) or > 3.0 (post-fluid) = Transplant criteria.
Glucose Hypoglycaemia is a sign of liver failure.

Management: Acetylcysteine (NAC)

Start Treatment Indications
Treat Now

Acute (< 8 hrs)

  • Dose ≥ 150 mg/kg
  • Nomogram indicates risk
  • Time of ingestion uncertain
  • ALT > Upper Limit Normal

Acute (8 - 24 hrs)

  • Dose ≥ 150 mg/kg (or unknown)
  • Clinical features of injury
  • Nomogram indicates risk
  • Detectable Paracetamol level

> 24 hrs / Staggered / Therapeutic

  • Clinical features of injury
  • INR > 1.3
  • Detectable level
  • Susceptibility uncertain
Note: Hepatotoxicity is extremely rare if NAC is administered within 8 hours of ingestion.

Transplant Criteria (King's College Criteria)

Urgent Referral to Hepatology Required If:
Criterion Details
Arterial pH < 7.3 (after fluid resuscitation)
Prothrombin Time > 100 seconds
Encephalopathy Grade 3 or 4 (Established)
Serum Creatinine > 300 micromoles/L
Serum Lactate > 3.5 mmol/L on admission OR > 3.0 mmol/L (24 hrs post-ingestion/post-fluid)
