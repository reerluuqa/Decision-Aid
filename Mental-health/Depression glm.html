Depression Management Algorithm

A concise clinical decision support tool for primary care management of adult depression (18+ years)

1. Initial Screening

Ask the two depression identification questions:

  • "During the last month, have you often been bothered by feeling down, depressed, or hopeless?"
  • "During the last month, have you often been bothered by having little interest or pleasure in doing things?"

If "yes" to either question and symptoms present ≥2 weeks, proceed to assessment.

2. Comprehensive Assessment

Evaluate:

  • Symptom onset, duration, pattern, severity
  • Impact on daily functioning
  • Lifestyle factors (sleep, diet, activity, substance use)
  • Past history of depression, self-harm, mental health conditions
  • Risk factors (family history, chronic conditions, trauma)
  • Current medications and previous treatments

Always assess suicide/self-harm risk: thoughts, plans, intent, protective factors

Consider validated tools: PHQ-9 (preferred), HADS, BDI-II

3. Severity Classification

Based on PHQ-9 score:

Less Severe

Score < 16

(Subthreshold & Mild)
More Severe

Score ≥ 16

(Moderate & Severe)

4. Management Pathway

Less Severe (PHQ-9 <16)
  • Active monitoring with follow-up
  • Psychological interventions (CBT, IPT, BA)
  • Consider antidepressants if:
    • Patient preference
    • Previous positive response
    • Persistent symptoms
More Severe (PHQ-9 ≥16)
  • Combination approach:
    • Antidepressant medication
    • Psychological intervention
  • Consider referral if:
    • High suicide risk
    • Psychotic symptoms
    • Poor treatment response
    • Significant comorbidities

5. Treatment Considerations

  • Antidepressant choice: SSRIs first-line (consider individual factors)
  • Duration: Continue for at least 6 months after remission
  • Stopping: Reduce dose gradually; monitor for withdrawal symptoms
  • Special populations:
    • Does NOT cover pregnant/breastfeeding women
    • Does NOT cover bipolar disorder, psychosis, or personality disorders

6. Follow-up

  • Regular review based on clinical judgment
  • Monitor treatment response and side effects
  • Assess suicide risk at each visit
  • Evaluate functional improvement (not just symptom reduction)

Red Flags Requiring Urgent Attention

  • Active suicidal ideation with plan/intent
  • Psychotic features
  • Severe functional impairment
  • Significant self-neglect

Based on NICE CKS guidance (last revised April 2025) for adults 18+ years in primary care settings

Designed for quick reference during patient consultations
