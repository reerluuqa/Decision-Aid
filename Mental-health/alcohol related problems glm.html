Bleeding Oesophageal Varices

Signs: Haematemesis, melaena, shock (cirrhosis complication).

  • ABC: Intubate to protect airway.
  • Resuscitate: Large bore cannulas, Blood transfusion.
  • Correct: Coagulopathy (Vitamin K, FFP).
  • Treat: Vasoactive drugs (Terlipressin/Ocreotide), Endoscopic band ligation, Balloon tamponade.

Acute Pancreatitis

Signs: Epigastric pain radiating to back, vomiting.

  • Diagnosis: Check Serum Amylase (may be normal in chronic).
  • Risk: Spirits > Beer/Wine.
  • Monitor: Shock, sepsis, abscess formation.
  • Supportive: Fluid resuscitation, analgesia.

Wernicke-Korsakoff Syndrome

Acute (Wernicke's): Confusion, visual impairment (diplopia), ataxia.

  • Cause: Thiamine (B1) deficiency.
  • Action: URGENT PARENTERAL THIAMINE before glucose.
  • Chronic (Korsakoff's): Memory deficits, confabulation (often irreversible).

Delirium Tremens (DTs)

Signs: Severe withdrawal, hallucinations, circulatory collapse, ketoacidosis.

  • Timeline: Usually 48-72 hours after last drink.
  • Setting: Hospital admission required.
  • Rx: Benzodiazepines (Chlordiazepoxide), Fluids, Electrolytes.

üè• Long-Term Systemic Harm

Fatty Liver (Steatosis)

Reversible with cessation. Early stage of disease.

Alcoholic Hepatitis

Can be acute or chronic. Inflammation of the liver.

Cirrhosis

Risk: 57% risk increase at 2 units/day; 207% increase at 5 units/day.

Irreversible scarring. Leads to portal hypertension.

Hepatocellular Carcinoma

Increased risk of liver cancer.

Hypertension

Excessive use is strongly associated. Leads to stroke, MI, CKD.

Arrhythmias

Risk: +13% risk at 2 units/day; +34% risk at 5 units/day.

Includes Atrial Fibrillation ("Holiday Heart").

Cardiomyopathy

Dilated cardiomyopathy leading to heart failure.

High Risk

Upper Aerodigestive

Oral cavity, Laryngeal, Oesophageal.

Risk multiplies with tobacco use.

Gastric & Liver

Adenocarcinoma of stomach; Hepatocellular carcinoma.

CNS Impairment

Blackouts, head injuries, subdural haemorrhage. Young people especially vulnerable to brain impairment.

Peripheral Neuropathy

Damage to peripheral nerves.

Cerebellar Degeneration

Affects balance and coordination.

Psychiatric

Depression, Anxiety, Suicide (History in 47% of suicides), Alcohol dependence syndrome.

Pregnancy

FASD: Fetal Alcohol Spectrum Disorders (Miscarriage, stillbirth, growth restriction). Heavy drinking (>5 units/day) causes FASD.

Sexual Function

Erectile dysfunction, premature/delayed ejaculation, loss of libido.

Social Impact

Domestic violence, child abuse (involved in 25-33% of cases), unemployment.
