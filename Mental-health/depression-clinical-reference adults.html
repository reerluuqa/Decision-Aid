Assessment & Diagnosis

üìã DSM-5-TR Diagnostic Criteria
‚ñº

‚â•5 symptoms present during same 2-week period, representing change from previous functioning. At least one is: (1) Depressed mood OR (2) Loss of interest/pleasure.

  • 1. Depressed mood Most of the day, nearly every day
  • 2. Anhedonia Markedly diminished interest or pleasure
  • 3. Weight/appetite change Significant weight loss/gain or appetite change
  • 4. Sleep disturbance Insomnia or hypersomnia nearly every day
  • 5. Psychomotor changes Agitation or retardation observable by others
  • 6. Fatigue Loss of energy nearly every day
  • 7. Worthlessness/guilt Excessive or inappropriate guilt
  • 8. Concentration problems Diminished ability to think/concentrate
  • 9. Suicidal thoughts Recurrent thoughts of death or suicide
Functional impairment required - symptoms cause distress or impairment in social/occupational functioning.

üîÑ Differential Diagnosis
‚ñº
Bipolar Disorder
Screen for prior manic/hypomanic episodes before starting antidepressants. Depression may precede first manic episode.
Grief/Bereavement
Normal grief usually doesn't include inappropriate guilt, marked psychomotor retardation, or prolonged impairment. ICD-11 now includes prolonged grief disorder.
Medical Conditions
Hypothyroidism, Cushing's disease, vitamin B12/folate deficiency, neurological disorders. Check TSH, B12, folate, cortisol.
Substance/Medication Effects
Corticosteroids, interferon, beta-blockers, oral contraceptives, alcohol, cannabis. Review medications and toxicology if indicated.
Anxiety Disorders
Highly comorbid (45.7% of MDD patients have anxiety disorder). "Anxious depression" common - assess for GAD, panic, PTSD, OCD.
Dementia
Depression may be prodromal or comorbid. MMSE or neuropsych testing if cognitive concerns. Consider Geriatric Depression Scale.
Adjustment Disorder
Subsyndromal depression with clear precipitant. Usually resolves with stressor resolution. Often doesn't require medication.
Sleep Disorders
OSA can cause depressive symptoms that reverse with CPAP treatment. Consider sleep study if indicated.

Treatment Guidelines

‚úÖ First-Line Treatment Options
‚ñº
All Patients
Psychoeducation + lifestyle advice: sleep hygiene, exercise, diet, smoking cessation, reduce alcohol/substances.

Evidence-Based Options

‚úì
Psychological Therapy

CBT, IPT effective. Comparable efficacy to antidepressants in moderate depression.

‚úì
Pharmacotherapy

SSRIs, SNRIs, or other second-generation antidepressants first-line.

‚úì
Combination Therapy

Psychotherapy + pharmacotherapy superior for moderate-severe depression.

Watchful Waiting
Appropriate for subthreshold or mild depression with good prognosis. Reassess in 2 weeks.
üîÑ Treatment-Resistant Depression
‚ñº

Definition: Inadequate response to ‚â•2 adequate antidepressant trials.

Escalation Options

  • Switch to different antidepressant class
  • Augmentation (add antipsychotic, lithium, or T3)
  • Combination of 2 antidepressants
  • Esketamine nasal spray (consultant-led)
  • Electroconvulsive Therapy (ECT)
ECT Indications
  • Severe, treatment-resistant depression
  • Psychotic depression
  • Catatonia
  • High suicide risk requiring rapid response
  • Pregnancy with severe depression
  • Previous good response to ECT
üë• Special Populations
‚ñº

ü§∞ Perinatal

Screen with EPDS or PHQ-9. Score ‚â•10 suggests depression. ACOG recommends screening at multiple timepoints. SSRIs generally considered safe. Weigh risks/benefits.

üë¥ Older Adults

Use Geriatric Depression Scale (GDS) or Cornell Scale (if dementia). ECT effective and avoids drug interactions. Watch for medication sensitivity.

üîû Youth <25

Increased suicide risk warning with antidepressants in weeks 3-6. Close monitoring essential. FDA black box warning for <25 years.

‚ö†Ô∏è Common Side Effects & Warnings
‚ñº

‚ö° Common Initial Side Effects

  • Nausea, GI upset (usually transient)
  • Headache
  • Insomnia or somnolence
  • Agitation/anxiety initially
  • Sexual dysfunction (all SSRIs)

üö® Serious Warnings

  • Serotonin syndrome (with other serotonergic drugs)
  • Hyponatremia (elderly, especially with diuretics)
  • Bleeding risk (with NSAIDs, antiplatelets)
  • Discontinuation syndrome (especially paroxetine, venlafaxine)
  • Activation of suicidal ideation (under 25)
Drug Selection Considerations
Choose based on: patient preference, past response, side effect profile, comorbidities, drug interactions, safety in overdose, and cost. SSRIs are generally first-line. No consistent efficacy differences between antidepressants.

Follow-up & Monitoring

üìÜ Follow-up Timeline
‚ñº
Week 1-2
Initial Assessment & Safety Check
Baseline PHQ-9, review side effects, assess suicidal ideation, establish safety plan, provide crisis numbers.
Week 2-4
Early Response Assessment
Check for side effects, assess early response. Watch for increased suicidality weeks 3-6. Consider dose increase if partial response.
Week 4-8
Treatment Response
Reassess PHQ-9. Full response typically by 6-8 weeks. If minimal response, consider dose adjustment or medication change.
Week 12+
Maintenance & Relapse Prevention
Continue effective dose for ‚â•6 months after remission. Higher risk of relapse if stopped early. Consider maintenance for recurrent depression.
üõ°Ô∏è Safety Plan Template
‚ñº
Patient Safety Plan Components
  • 1 Warning signs: What thoughts, feelings, or situations indicate I'm becoming suicidal?
  • 2 Coping strategies: Things I can do on my own to distract or keep safe.
  • 3 Social contacts: People who can help during a crisis (name and contact).
  • 4 Professional contacts: GP, crisis team, helpline numbers (Samaritans 116 123).
  • 5 Reduce access: Remove or restrict access to means (medications, weapons).
  • 6 Reasons for living: What makes life worth living? Who depends on me?
üìä Monitoring Parameters
‚ñº

Clinical Monitoring

  • PHQ-9 score - track treatment response
  • Suicidal ideation - at every visit
  • Side effects - especially early treatment
  • Functional improvement - work, relationships
  • Adherence - ask about missed doses

Investigations (Baseline)

  • FBC, U&E, LFTs - baseline for some drugs
  • Thyroid function - rule out hypothyroidism
  • Vitamin B12/folate - deficiency linked to depression
  • LFTs - before agomelatine, regularly during
  • BP monitoring - with SNRIs (venlafaxine)
