Restless Legs Syndrome Clinical Reference

Last Updated: Sep 10, 2025
URGENT: Consider iron deficiency in all RLS patients - check ferritin levels

Key Diagnostic Criteria

  • Urge to move legs, usually with uncomfortable sensations
  • Symptoms begin or worsen during rest/inactivity
  • Symptoms partially/totally relieved by movement
  • Symptoms occur/worsen in evening/night
  • Not solely accounted for by another condition

IRLSSG 10-Question Rating Scale:

  • Mild: 1-10 points
  • Moderate: 11-20 points
  • Severe: 21-30 points
  • Very severe: 31-40 points

Johns Hopkins RLS Severity Scale:

  • 0 = no symptoms
  • 1 = bedtime only symptoms
  • 2 = evening and bedtime symptoms
  • 3 = day and night symptoms

Treatment Algorithm

Intermittent RLS
Chronic RLS
Refractory RLS
For symptoms occurring less than twice weekly
First Line Lifestyle modifications
Second Line Carbidopa/levodopa (on-demand)
Third Line Low-potency opioid or benzodiazepine
For symptoms occurring at least twice weekly
First Line Gabapentinoids (pregabalin, gabapentin)
Second Line Dopamine agonist (pramipexole, ropinirole)
Third Line Opioid
Unresponsive to monotherapy with tolerable doses
First Line Combination therapy (different classes)
Second Line Low-dose opioid monotherapy

Key Risk Factors

Strong

  • Iron deficiency
  • Pregnancy
  • Family history
  • Certain medications
  • Female sex

Weak

  • Increased age
  • Obesity
  • Diabetes
  • Parkinson's disease
  • Multiple sclerosis
  • Antidepressants (except bupropion)
  • Sedating antihistamines
  • Dopamine-blocking antiemetics (metoclopramide)
  • Antipsychotics

Emergency Actions

Check iron status in ALL patients with RLS symptoms
Screen for sleep apnea in patients with excessive daytime sleepiness
Consider iron replacement if ferritin ≤169 pmol/L or TSAT <20%
Order Iron Studies
Screen for Sleep Apnea
Review Medications
Refer to Specialist
