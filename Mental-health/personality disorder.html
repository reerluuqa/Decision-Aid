Red Flags & Emergency Situations

‚ö†Ô∏è Elevated Self-Harm & Suicide Risk
  • Borderline Personality Disorder (highest risk)
  • Any PD with co-occurring substance use
  • Co-occurring major depression
  • Recurrent suicidal ideation/attempts
  • Self-mutilation behaviors (cutting, burning)
  • Access to lethal means
  • Recent discharge from psychiatric hospitalization
‚ö° Aggression Risk to Others
  • Antisocial PD traits + substance use
  • History of violence/assault
  • Current homicidal ideation or intent
  • Identifying specific targets
  • Impulsive aggression episodes
  • History of domestic violence
  • Threats in clinical setting
üî¥ Acute Clinical Presentations
  • Acute stress with disproportionate anger
  • Inappropriate demands on staff
  • Rapid mood changes
  • Scars/markings from self-mutilation
  • Frequent arguments/altercations
  • Turbulent/volatile relationships
  • Clinician strong emotional reactions
üíâ Substance Use Emergencies
  • Acute intoxication with self-harm history
  • Complicated withdrawal syndromes
  • Overdose with impulsivity history
  • Co-occurring medical conditions
  • Highly impulsive behavior patterns
  • Failed outpatient detoxification

  • Poor response to evidence-based treatments
  • Multiple medically unexplained symptoms
  • Chronic pattern of complaints
  • Multiple failed medication trials
üë• Interpersonal Patterns

  • Heavy reliance on physician
  • Clingy/anxious attachment behaviors

  • Odd or strange thinking patterns
  • Ideas of reference / magical thinking

üìà Affective & Impulse Dyscontrol
  • Marked mood and anxiety symptoms

  • Reckless disregard for safety

Cluster A: Odd/Eccentric Disorders

Schizotypal PD Cluster A
  • Ideas of reference
  • Odd thinking/speech
  • Unusual perceptions
  • Social anxiety
  • Suspiciousness
  • Inappropriate affect
  • First-degree relative with schizophrenia
Schizoid PD Cluster A
  • Detachment from social relationships
  • Restricted emotional expression
  • Solitary activities
  • Indifferent to others
  • Little interest in sex
  • Lacks close friends
  • Appears cold/indifferent
Paranoid PD Cluster A
  • Distrust/suspiciousness of others
  • Motives viewed as malevolent
  • Bears grudges
  • Perceives threats in neutral events
  • Unjustified doubts about loyalty
  • Reluctant to confide
  • Recalls attacks on character

Cluster B: Dramatic/Emotional Disorders

Borderline PD Cluster B
  • Instability in relationships
  • Frantic efforts to avoid abandonment
  • Identity disturbance
  • Impulsive self-damaging behaviors
  • Recurrent suicidal behavior/gestures
  • Affective instability
  • Chronic feelings of emptiness
  • Inappropriate intense anger
  • Transient dissociative/psychotic symptoms
Histrionic PD Cluster B
  • Excessive emotionality
  • Uncomfortable when not center of attention
  • Shifting/shallow emotions
  • Draws attention via appearance
  • Speech impressionistic/lacks detail
  • Views relationships as more intimate
  • Easily influenced by others
Antisocial PD Cluster B
  • Disregard for rights of others
  • Since age 15
  • Impulsivity
  • Deceitfulness
  • Lack of remorse
  • Illegal acts/disrespect for norms
  • Irritability/aggressiveness
  • Reckless disregard for safety
  • Fails to sustain consistent work
Narcissistic PD Cluster B
  • Grandiosity (fantasy/behavior)
  • Need for admiration
  • Lack of empathy
  • Views self as "special"
  • Unwilling to recognize others' feelings
  • Arrogant behavior
  • Interpersonally exploitative
  • Often envious of others

Cluster C: Anxious/Fearful Disorders

Avoidant PD Cluster C
  • Social inhibition
  • Feelings of inadequacy
  • Hypersensitivity to negative evaluation
  • Interpersonally inhibited
  • Reluctant to take risks due to rejection fear
  • Restraint in intimate relationships
  • Views self as socially inept
  • Preoccupied with criticism/rejection
Dependent PD Cluster C
  • Excessive need to be cared for
  • Submissive/clinging behavior
  • Difficulty decision-making alone
  • Fears solitude
  • Difficulty expressing disagreement
  • Difficulty initiating projects
  • Seeks new relationships for support
  • Uncomfortable when alone
Obsessive-Compulsive PD Cluster C
  • Preoccupation with orderliness
  • Perfectionism
  • Mental/interpersonal control
  • Over-conscientious
  • Stubborn
  • Excessively devoted to work
  • Inflexible about morality/ethics
  • Reluctant to delegate
  • Miserly spending style

Treatment Approaches

Psychotherapy (Standard of Care)
A

Cluster A: Straightforward communication

Unintrusive style, display interest in shared concerns

B

Cluster B: Clear boundaries, calm demeanor

Prepare for changes in care, coordinate among providers

C

Cluster C: Avoid criticism, reinforce help-seeking

Regular scheduled visits, support self-efficacy

Evidence-Based Psychotherapy by Cluster
  • Borderline: DBT, MBT, TFP
  • Antisocial: Contingency management, CBT
  • Avoidant: CBT, psychodynamic, social skills training
  • Mixed PD: Short-term dynamic, DBT
  • All PDs: Primary care support important
Pharmacotherapy Guidelines
  • Used selectively for target symptoms
  • Exercise caution due to limited evidence
  • Drug treatment should be time-limited
  • Aimed at specific measurable target symptoms
  • Adjunctive to psychotherapy, not standalone
  • Consider polypharmacy history in BPD
Symptom-Targeted Medications
  • Cognitive-perceptual: Low-dose antipsychotics
  • Impulsivity/aggression: Mood stabilizers (lithium)
  • Affective dysregulation: Anticonvulsants
  • Avoidant traits: SSRIs, venlafaxine
  • NICE Guidelines: Avoid routine meds for antisocial PD
