Quick Overview

üìã Definition

Cocaine toxicity refers to adverse events from cocaine use, including hyperthermia, rhabdomyolysis, dysrhythmia, ischemia, intracranial hemorrhage, agitation, psychosis, and seizures.

‚ö† Key Warning

Some patients may die suddenly before treatment is given. Presentation includes tachycardia, hypertension, hyperthermia, diaphoresis, mydriasis, and agitation.

Critical Points

  • ‚Ä¢ Urine cocaine screen confirms recent use but do not delay treatment pending results
  • ‚Ä¢ Benzodiazepines are first-line treatment for most symptoms
  • ‚Ä¢ AVOID beta-blockers - risk of coronary vasoconstriction
  • ‚Ä¢ Hyperthermia requires rapid cooling to prevent death

Essential Investigations

Test Finding in Cocaine Toxicity
ECG Sinus tachycardia, prolonged QRS/QT, ischemia, dysrhythmias
Cardiac Troponin Elevated if myocardial injury present (hs-cTn preferred)
Serum CPK Elevated in rhabdomyolysis (often in thousands)
Serum Creatinine May indicate renal injury from rhabdomyolysis
Bedside Glucose Exclude hypoglycemia immediately
Chest X-Ray If chest pain: pneumothorax, pneumomediastinum, hemorrhage
CT Head If headache, seizure, or obtunded: look for ICH
Urine Cocaine Confirms exposure (positive 48-72hrs) - DO NOT delay treatment

Differential Diagnosis

Condition Key Differentiators
Anticholinergic Toxicity Dry skin, decreased bowel sounds, urinary retention
Sympathomimetics (Amphetamines) Clinically similar - management is the same
Thyroid Storm Insidious onset, longer duration, history of hyperthyroidism
Pheochromocytoma Extremely rare, recurrent attacks, headache prominent
Serotonin Syndrome Clonus, hyperreflexia, recent serotonergic drug use
Infection No cocaine history, responds to antibiotics

Risk Factors for Severe Toxicity

High ambient temperature Male sex Plasma cholinesterase deficiency Age 18-25 years Concomitant sympathomimetics Route: IV injection highest risk High doses Concomitant opioids (speedball) Body packing/stuffing
