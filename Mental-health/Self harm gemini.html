Management of Self-Harm in Primary Care

Scenario 1: After an Act of Self-Harm

1. IMMEDIATE ACTIONS & TRIAGE

Treat with compassion. Assess physical injury/poisoning.

Universal Principles & Long-Term Management

✅ ALWAYS

  • Maintain a compassionate, non-judgmental approach.
  • Assess for Safeguarding concerns (patient & dependents).
  • Consider Mental Capacity (assume in ages 16+).
  • Work collaboratively with the patient and, with consent, family/carers.
  • Maintain continuity of care.
  • Share information with the multidisciplinary team.

❌ DO NOT

  • Use risk assessment tools to decide who gets treatment.
  • Offer drug treatment as a specific intervention to reduce self-harm.
  • Use punitive or criminal justice approaches.

⭐ LONG-TERM MANAGEMENT

  • Refer for tailored psychological interventions (CBT, DBT-A).
  • Ensure patient has a clear Care & Crisis Plan.
  • Discuss harm minimization strategies if needed.
  • Conduct regular medication reviews for toxicity and risk.
  • Provide ongoing support for family and carers.

This is a quick reference guide based on NICE CKS on Self-harm (revised February 2025). Always use clinical judgment and refer to the full, up-to-date guidance for comprehensive information.
