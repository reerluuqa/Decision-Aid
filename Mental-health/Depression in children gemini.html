Depression in Children & Young People

A Quick Reference Clinical Algorithm (Ages 5-18)

1. Assessment & Diagnosis

Suspect Depression If Core Symptoms Present:

  • Persistent sadness, low mood, or irritability.
  • Marked loss of interest or pleasure (anhedonia).

Diagnostic Assessment Steps:

  1. Take History: Inquire about symptoms, duration, and functional impact.
  2. Identify Risk Factors: Family history, life events, trauma, etc.
  3. Screen for Comorbidities: Anxiety, neurodiversity, substance use.
  4. Rule Out Organic Causes: Consider tests if indicated (e.g., anaemia).

Associated Symptoms:

  • Reduced concentration/indecisiveness
  • Low self-esteem/guilt
  • Hopelessness / Suicidal thoughts
  • Disturbed sleep & appetite
  • Fatigue / Psychomotor changes

2. Severity Assessment & Management Pathway

Severity Guidance Management Pathway
MILD None of the symptoms are intense. Some difficulty with functioning.
  • Step 1: Watchful Waiting & Support
    Provide lifestyle advice (sleep, diet, exercise). Arrange follow-up in 2 weeks.
  • Step 2 (If No Improvement): Psychological Intervention
    Offer digital CBT, group CBT, or non-directive supportive therapy.
  • Step 3 (If No Response after 2-3 months):
    Refer to CAMHS.

    Offer high-intensity therapy (e.g., individual CBT, family therapy).
  • Step 3 (If No Response after 4-6 Sessions): Multidisciplinary Review
    Re-evaluate and consider coexisting factors.
  • Step 4 (Post-Review): Consider Combination Treatment
    Add Fluoxetine to ongoing psychological therapy.
SEVERE Most symptoms are marked/intense. Serious difficulty functioning in most domains.

3. Key Prescribing & Referral Information

Fluoxetine Prescribing Principles

  • Prerequisites: Must be prescribed by a child/adolescent psychiatrist & combined with psychological therapy.
  • Starting Dose: 10 mg daily (may increase to 20 mg after 1 week).
  • Critical Monitoring: Review weekly for the first 4 weeks. Monitor for increased suicidal ideation, self-harm, or hostility.
  • Duration: Continue for at least 6 months after remission.
  • Discontinuation: Taper dose gradually over 6-12 weeks.
  • Medications to AVOID: Paroxetine, Venlafaxine, TCAs, St. John's Wort.

Refer urgently to CAMHS if the child or young person has:

  • Active suicidal ideas or a plan.
  • Psychotic symptoms (delusions or hallucinations).
  • Moderate to severe depression.
  • High and recurrent risk of self-harm.
  • Significant self-neglect harmful to physical health.
  • Mild depression unresponsive to primary care interventions.
