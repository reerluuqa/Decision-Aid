CRITICAL: Acute Limb Ischaemia (ALI) - MEDICAL EMERGENCY

Immediate vascular referral required. Time is limb. Target revascularization within 6 hours.

! The 6 Ps of Acute Limb Ischaemia

Pain - Sudden onset, severe, out of proportion
Paralysis - Motor weakness or loss
Paraesthesia - Sensory loss or numbness
Pulselessness - Absent distal pulses
Pallor - Pale, mottled extremity
Poikilothermia - Cold to touch

‚ö° Critical Limb-Threatening Ischaemia (CLTI)

Ischaemic rest pain - Worse when supine, better when dependent
Gangrene - Necrosis of toes, foot, or heel
Non-healing ulcer - >2 weeks duration, below knee
Dependent rubor - Reddish discoloration when leg dependent
Pallor on elevation - Foot turns pale when raised
Muscle atrophy - Reduced calf circumference

‚úì Immediate Actions for ALI

Urgent vascular surgery consult - Same hour
IV heparin infusion - Unless contraindicated
Analgesia - Paracetamol + opioid as needed
Baseline ABI/TBI - Document before heparin
Nil by mouth - Prepare for possible intervention
Keep warm - No active warming devices

Bilateral EIA occlusions

SFA/popliteal stenosis <3cm

Femoropopliteal (Type D - Surgery preferred)

Complete SFA or popliteal occlusions
Stenosis/occlusion >5cm

üíä Medical Therapy - All PAD Patients

Antiplatelet Therapy

Aspirin 75-100mg daily - First line
Clopidogrel 75mg daily - Aspirin alternative
Rivaroxaban 2.5mg BD + aspirin - Selected patients (lower bleeding risk)

Lipid Management

High-intensity statin - All patients
Target LDL <1.8mmol/L (<70mg/dL) US
Target LDL <1.4mmol/L (<55mg/dL) ESC

Blood Pressure

Target <130/80mmHg US
Target 120-129/<80mmHg ESC
ACE inhibitor preferred - Ramipril proven benefit

Glycaemic Control (Diabetes)

GLP-1 RA or SGLT2 inhibitor preferred - CV benefit
Avoid thiazolidinediones (fluid retention)

Approach Indications Key Points

‚úÖ Lifestyle Modifications Checklist

  • Smoking Cessation
    Most powerful intervention. Offer NRT, varenicline, bupropion
  • Supervised Exercise Program
    30-45 min walking, 3x/week, 12 weeks minimum
  • Diet & Weight Control
    Mediterranean diet, BMI <25kg/m¬≤
  • Foot Care Daily
    Inspect feet, avoid barefoot, proper footwear
  • Regular Walking
    Walk through pain, rest, then continue (claudication)
