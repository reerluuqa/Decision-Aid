CKD Quick Reference

Immediate Referral Required

Rapidly declining eGFR (>5 mL/min/1.73m² within 1 year) or eGFR <30 with persistent proteinuria

Diagnosis Criteria

eGFR <60 mL/min/1.73m² for ≥3 months OR markers of kidney damage

Key Tests

eGFR, ACR, blood pressure, urine sediment

BP Target

<140/90 mmHg (or <130/80 if ACR ≥30 mg/mmol)

First-line

ACE inhibitor/ARB titrated to max tolerated dose

CKD Diagnosis Criteria
Definition

Abnormalities in kidney function or structure present for >3 months with health implications.

Diagnostic Criteria (≥3 months)
eGFR <60 mL/min/1.73m² on ≥2 occasions (90+ days apart)
Markers of kidney damage: ACR >3 mg/mmol, urine sediment abnormalities, electrolyte abnormalities, structural abnormalities, or history of kidney transplantation
CKD Classification
Stage eGFR (mL/min/1.73m²) Description
G1 ≥90 Normal or high
G2 60-89 Mildly decreased
G3a 45-59 Mild to moderately decreased

G4 15-29 Severely decreased
G5 <15 Kidney failure
When to Suspect CKD
Risk Factors
Age >65 years
Hypertension
Diabetes mellitus
Family history of kidney disease
Nephrotoxic medications
Incidental Findings
Raised serum creatinine
Proteinuria (ACR ≥3 mg/mmol)
Persistent haematuria (2/3 positive tests)
Urine sediment abnormalities
Management Goals
Blood Pressure Control
Target: <140/90 mmHg for most patients
Target: <130/80 mmHg if ACR ≥30 mg/mmol
ACE inhibitor/ARB titrated to max tolerated dose
Cardiovascular Risk Management
Offer atorvastatin 20 mg to all adults with CKD
Consider antiplatelet therapy if not contraindicated
Lifestyle modifications: diet, exercise, smoking cessation
Medication Considerations
Medications to Use with Caution
NSAIDs - avoid if possible
ACE inhibitors/ARBs - monitor eGFR and potassium
Diuretics - monitor for volume depletion
Metformin - avoid if eGFR <30
Newer Treatment Options
SGLT2 inhibitors (dapagliflozin, empagliflozin) for CKD with or without diabetes
GLP-1 receptor agonists for CKD with type 2 diabetes
Vadadustat for symptomatic anaemia in adults on dialysis

Referral Criteria
Urgent Referral
Rapidly declining eGFR (>5 mL/min/1.73m² within 1 year)
eGFR <30 with persistent proteinuria
5-year risk of needing renal replacement therapy >5%
Routine Referral
eGFR <30 (G4-G5)
ACR ≥70 mg/mmol (A3) unless known diabetic kidney disease
Uncontrolled hypertension despite optimal therapy
Suspected renal artery stenosis
Specialist Advice Consideration
eGFR 30-59 with persistent proteinuria
Hereditary kidney disease
Difficulty achieving BP targets
CKD Complications
Common Complications
Cardiovascular disease (5-10x higher risk of death than progression to ESRD)
Hypertension
Anemia (due to reduced erythropoietin production)
Mineral and bone disorder
Pulmonary edema
Metabolic Complications
Hyperkalaemia
Metabolic acidosis
Malnutrition
Peripheral neuropathy and myopathy
Increased stroke risk
Prognosis
Disease Progression

CKD is generally progressive, but the rate varies:

  • ~50% remain stable
  • 10-15% progress
  • 25-30% improve

Less than 2% progress to need renal replacement therapy over 5 years.

Poor Prognostic Factors
Decreasing eGFR
Increasing proteinuria
Cardiovascular disease
Diabetes mellitus
