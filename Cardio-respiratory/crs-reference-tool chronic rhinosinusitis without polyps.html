Stepwise Treatment Algorithm

1

Nasal Saline Irrigation + Intranasal Corticosteroids

FIRST LINE Initial treatment for ALL patients. Irrigate 2-3x daily, volume >60mL.

2

Add Adjunct Therapies (if indicated)

SELECTIVE Antibiotics for purulence, decongestants for congestion, antihistamines for allergy.

3

Reassess After 3 Months

If persistent symptoms despite Step 1-2 therapy, proceed to CT scan for surgical evaluation.

4

Endoscopic Sinus Surgery (ESS)

HIGHLY EFFECTIVE For patients failing medical therapy. Major complications <1%.

First-Line Medical Therapy

üíß

Nasal Saline Irrigation

All patients: 2-3x daily, high-volume (>60mL) device. Duration >8 weeks recommended. Improves mucus clearance, reduces congestion.

Intranasal Corticosteroid Dose
Budesonide 32-64 mcg/spray in each nostril once daily
Fluticasone propionate 100 mcg (2 sprays) per nostril daily OR 50 mcg twice daily
Mometasone 100 mcg (2 sprays) in each nostril once daily
Triamcinolone 110 mcg (2 sprays) in each nostril once daily
Flunisolide 50 mcg (2 sprays) in each nostril twice daily

Key points: Safe for long-term use. Minimal systemic absorption. Common side effect: epistaxis (improves with proper technique - spray laterally away from septum).
