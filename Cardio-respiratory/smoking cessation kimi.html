Medication Review
Critical
Drugs Affected by Smoking Cessation

Smoking cessation may require dose adjustment of certain medications due to altered metabolism.

Theophylline
High Risk
Monitor levels - reduce dose by 25-33% after 1 week
Toxicity risk: Nausea, palpitations, seizures
Clozapine
High Risk
Monitor plasma levels - reduce dose to 75% gradually
Toxicity risk: Sedation, hypotension, seizures
Warfarin
Moderate Risk
Monitor INR more frequently - may need dose reduction
Risk: Increased bleeding if INR rises
Olanzapine
Moderate Risk
Monitor for adverse effects - reduce dose if needed
Side effects: Dizziness, sedation, hypotension

Pharmacotherapy Options
Individualized
Nicotine Replacement Therapy (NRT)
First Line
Available as patches, gum, lozenges, inhalators, nasal/oral sprays
  • Choice: Patient preference & previous experience
  • Combination: Patch + fast-acting form most effective
  • Duration: 8-12 weeks
  • Success rate: 150% more effective than unaided
Varenicline (Champix)
Second Line
Start 7-14 days before quit date
  • Days 1-3: 500mcg once daily
  • Days 4-7: 500mcg twice daily
  • Day 8+: 1mg twice daily
  • Course: 12 weeks
  • Success rate: 200% more effective than unaided
Contraindications: Pregnancy, breastfeeding, end-stage renal disease
Bupropion SR (Zyban)
Alternative
Start 7-14 days before quit date
  • Days 1-6: 150mg once daily
  • Day 7+: 150mg twice daily (≥8h apart)
  • Duration: 7-9 weeks
  • Success rate: Comparable to NRT
Contraindications: Seizure disorders, eating disorders, MAOI use
Cytisinicline
New Option
25-day course with tapering schedule
  • Days 1-3: 1 tablet every 2 hours (max 6/day)
  • Days 4-12: 1 tablet every 2.5 hours (max 5/day)
  • Days 13-16: 1 tablet every 3 hours (max 4/day)
  • Days 17-20: 1 tablet every 5 hours (max 3/day)
  • Days 21-25: 1-2 tablets per day
Contraindications: Age <18 or >65, cardiovascular disease, pregnancy
E-cigarettes

While e-cigarettes are less harmful than smoking, they are not licensed medications. Recommend licensed NRT first, but support patients who choose to use e-cigarettes.

Pregnancy & Breastfeeding
Specialist
Pregnancy Treatment Protocol

Preferred: Behavioral support without medication

If medication needed: NRT only (varenicline & bupropion contraindicated)

NRT in Pregnancy
Use with care
  • Preferred: Intermittent forms (gum, lozenges)
  • Patches if nausea/vomiting severe
  • Remove patches: At night
  • Benefits: Outweigh risks of continuing smoking
Prescribe 2-week supply only
NRT in Breastfeeding
Preferred
  • Timing: Use immediately after feeds
  • Form: Intermittent preferred
  • Benefit: Safer than secondhand smoke exposure
  • Monitoring: Watch for infant irritability
Young People (12-17 years)
Specialist
Youth Treatment Protocol

First-line: Behavioral support through specialist services

Second-line: NRT (if evidence of nicotine dependence)

Contraindicated: Varenicline & bupropion (under 18)

Assess: Level of nicotine dependence
Behavioral Support: Specialist youth services
Consider NRT: If high dependence AND failed behavioral attempt
Harm Reduction Approach
Alternative

For patients not ready to quit completely:

Cutting Down
  • Reduce cigarettes per day
  • Use NRT to manage cravings
  • Set gradual reduction targets
  • Aim for complete cessation within 6 weeks
Temporary Abstinence
  • Identify triggers/situations
  • Use NRT during abstinent periods
  • Build confidence for permanent quit
  • Practice coping strategies
Substitute & Reduce
  • Replace some cigarettes with NRT
  • Gradually increase substitution
  • Reduce compensatory smoking
  • Progress to complete substitution
Important Note

Harm reduction is not a substitute for complete cessation. Continue to encourage full quit and provide ongoing support.

Assessment Questions
Standard
Since your last visit:
  • How many days have you been smoke-free?
  • Have you had any cigarettes at all?
  • How strong are your cravings?
  • Are you using your medication as prescribed?
  • What withdrawal symptoms are you experiencing?
Action: Document responses and adjust treatment plan
Withdrawal Management
Important
Cravings
  • Increase NRT dose if needed
  • Add fast-acting NRT product
  • Behavioral strategies: delay, deep breathing, drink water
  • Identify and avoid triggers
Sleep Disturbance
  • Reduce caffeine intake
  • Practice good sleep hygiene
  • Consider 16-hour patch instead of 24-hour
  • Avoid NRT close to bedtime
Low Mood
  • Reassure it's temporary
  • Encourage physical activity
  • Screen for depression
  • Consider bupropion if not contraindicated
Weight Gain
  • Provide dietary advice
  • Encourage regular exercise
  • Consider NRT to delay weight gain
  • Reassure: health benefits outweigh risks
Relapse Prevention
Critical
Identify high-risk situations:
  • Stressful life events
  • Social situations with smokers
  • Alcohol consumption
  • Negative mood states
Develop coping strategies:
  • Practice refusal skills
  • Plan alternative activities
  • Use NRT proactively
  • Contact support services
If Relapse Occurs

Don't give up! Most smokers need 3-5 attempts before successful quit. Review what went wrong and try again.
