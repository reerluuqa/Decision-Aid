NAFLD Clinical Decision Algorithm

A one-page reference guide for fast clinical decision-making in non-alcoholic fatty liver disease management

When to Suspect NAFLD

  • Risk factors present: Obesity, type 2 diabetes, hypertension, dyslipidemia, metabolic syndrome
  • Persistent elevated liver enzymes (ALT > AST, typically <3x ULN) for ≥3 months
  • Alcohol intake below threshold: <20g/day (women) or <30g/day (men)
  • Ultrasound findings: Increased hepatic echogenicity

Initial Assessment

  • History: Symptoms, risk factors, alcohol consumption, comorbidities, medications
  • Examination: BMI, waist circumference, BP, signs of advanced liver disease
  • Investigations: LFTs, FBC, clotting, hepatitis serology, autoantibodies, ferritin, HbA1c, lipids, renal function, thyroid function

Fibrosis Risk Assessment

  • FIB-4 Score: >2.67 indicates advanced fibrosis
  • NAFLD Fibrosis Score: >-1.455 indicates advanced fibrosis
  • ELF Test: ≥10.51 indicates advanced fibrosis
  • Low risk: Manage in primary care
  • High risk: Refer to hepatology

Management Pathway

Fibrosis Risk Assessment (FIB-4, NFS, or ELF)
Low Risk
Manage in Primary Care
  • Lifestyle Modifications
  • Optimize Comorbidities
  • Annual Follow-up
High Risk
REFER to Hepatology
  • Specialist investigations
  • Advanced monitoring
  • Specialist management

When to Refer

  • High risk of advanced liver fibrosis (FIB-4 >2.67, NFS >-1.455, ELF ≥10.51)
  • Signs of advanced liver disease on examination
  • Uncertainty about diagnosis
  • Persistently abnormal LFTs despite lifestyle intervention

Lifestyle Modifications

  • Weight loss: 5-10% over 6 months (600kcal/day deficit)
  • Diet: Mediterranean diet, water instead of sugary drinks
  • Exercise: 150-200 min/week moderate intensity
  • Alcohol: Limit to recommended levels
  • Comorbidities: Optimize hypertension, diabetes, lipids
