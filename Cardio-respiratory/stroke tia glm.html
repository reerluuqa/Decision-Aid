Stroke & TIA Quick Reference

Last revised: November 2025

EMERGENCY ACTION REQUIRED

Arrange immediate transfer of people with suspected stroke to a specialist stroke service in hospital for rapid investigation and treatment. Refer people suspected of having a TIA urgently for specialist assessment.

Definitions

  • Stroke: Clinical syndrome of presumed vascular origin characterized by rapidly developing signs of focal or global disturbance of cerebral functions lasting >24 hours or leading to death.
  • Ischaemic Stroke: Neurological dysfunction caused by focal cerebral, spinal, or retinal cell death due to infarction following vascular occlusion or stenosis (85% of strokes).
  • Haemorrhagic Stroke: Neurological dysfunction due to focal collection of blood within brain parenchyma or ventricular system (15% of strokes).
  • TIA: Transient (<24 hours) neurological dysfunction caused by focal brain, spinal cord, or retinal ischemia, without evidence of acute infarction.

Management Goals

Prompt Recognition

Recognize symptoms and signs of stroke and TIA immediately.

Urgent Referral

Arrange immediate transfer to specialist stroke service for suspected stroke.

Specialist Assessment

Refer suspected TIA urgently for specialist assessment and investigation.

Secondary Prevention

Ensure appropriate measures for secondary prevention are started and reviewed.

Complication Management

Recognize and manage consequences and complications of stroke.
