CVD Risk Assessment & Management

NICE Clinical Knowledge Summary | Last revised July 2025
Remember: This CKS topic covers primary prevention only and does not cover secondary prevention of further cardiovascular events in people who already have CVD.
Who to Assess
From age 18 years onwards
  • Use a systematic strategy to identify people likely to be at high risk
  • Prioritize people with estimated 10-year CVD risk of 10% or more
  • People aged over 40 years should have CVD risk reviewed on an ongoing basis
  • In England, all people aged 40-74 years are offered an NHS Health Check every 5 years
Risk Assessment Tools
Use QRISK3 assessment tool (not Framingham)

Adjust calculated risk to account for:

  • Ethnic background (adjust for men of South Asian origin)
  • Family history of premature CVD (multiply by 1.5-2)
  • Severe obesity (BMI > 40 kg/m²)
  • Comorbidities (diabetes, CKD, RA, etc.)
  • Age 75+ years (regard as high risk)
Risk Factors
Non-modifiable:
Age (primarily >50 years)
Gender (men higher risk)
Family history of premature CVD
Ethnic background
Modifiable:
Smoking
Overweight/obesity
Physical inactivity
Unhealthy diet
Excessive alcohol
Dyslipidaemia
Comorbidities:
Hypertension
Diabetes
Chronic kidney disease
Rheumatoid arthritis
Risk-Based Management
Risk Level Management
10% or more (High Risk)
Prescribe statin
Lifestyle advice
Manage risk factors

Offer atorvastatin 20mg daily after informed discussion
Less than 10% (Low Risk)
Lifestyle advice
Reassess periodically

Statins not routinely recommended
Pharmacological Interventions
Aspirin is not recommended for primary prevention of CVD
Statin Therapy:
  • Atorvastatin 20mg daily is recommended first-line
  • Consider higher intensity if needed
  • Check lipid profile at 2-3 months
  • Target: LDL ≤2.0mmol/L or non-HDL ≤2.6mmol/L
Lifestyle Interventions
Recommendations:
  • Smoking cessation support
  • Healthy diet (Mediterranean-style)
  • Regular physical activity
  • Maintain healthy weight
  • Limit alcohol consumption
  • Stress management
