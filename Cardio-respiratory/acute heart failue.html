Overview & Key Facts

Definition

Heart failure is a clinical syndrome with symptoms and signs resulting from cardiac structure/function abnormality.

Acute heart failure refers to rapid onset or worsening of symptoms/signs requiring urgent evaluation and treatment.

üìä

Epidemiology

  • ~900,000 people in UK have heart failure
  • 5% of adult emergency hospital admissions in UK
  • Prevalence ‚â•10% in people ‚â•70 years
  • Mean age at presentation: 77.5 years

‚óè Suspect Acute Heart Failure In:

  • Breathlessness - acute, orthopnoea, PND
  • Ankle swelling - bilateral, pitting
  • Reduced exercise tolerance
  • Fatigue/tiredness - prolonged recovery
  • Nocturnal cough - may have frothy sputum
  • Risk factors - CVD history, age, diabetes
!

Key Point

Heart failure is unusual in patients with no relevant medical history. Always assess for underlying causes and precipitants that require immediate treatment.

Clinical Presentation

Common Symptoms

  • Dyspnoea - acute, on exertion, at rest
  • Orthopnoea - breathlessness lying flat
  • PND - paroxysmal nocturnal dyspnoea
  • Ankle swelling - bilateral, pitting
  • Reduced exercise tolerance
  • Fatigue/tiredness
üîç

Uncommon Symptoms

  • Nocturnal cough - frothy sputum suggests alveolar origin
  • Wheezing - "cardiac asthma"
  • Dizziness - sign of poor perfusion
  • Confusion - especially in elderly
  • Loss of appetite
  • Nocturnal ischaemic pain

Diagnosis & Investigations

üìã

ALWAYS Order These Tests

For any patient with new suspected acute heart failure:

üìà

ECG

Record and interpret 12-lead ECG for all patients. Monitor continuously.

  • Check rhythm, rate, QRS morphology
  • Look for: arrhythmias, block, MI evidence
  • LVH, previous infarction (Q waves)

Prognostic

Wide QRS or bundle branch block = worse prognosis

ü©ª

Chest X-Ray

CXR for all patients with suspected heart failure

  • Pulmonary congestion
  • Pleural effusion
  • Interstitial/alveolar oedema
  • Cardiomegaly

Remember

Significant LV dysfunction may be present WITHOUT cardiomegaly

Natriuretic Peptide Thresholds

Age (years) NT-proBNP (ng/L or pg/mL) Interpretation
<50 >450 Acute HF likely
50-75 >900 Acute HF likely
>75 >1800 Acute HF likely
Any age <300 (NT-proBNP) / <100 (BNP) Acute HF unlikely (normal)
üí°

Practical Tips

Most older patients with acute breathlessness have elevated NT-proBNP. Natriuretic peptides may be raised due to: ACS, myocarditis, PE, older age, renal/liver impairment. Low levels can occur in flash pulmonary oedema or end-stage HF.

Blood Tests

ü©∏

Troponin

Measure in ALL patients with suspected AHF

  • Most AHF patients have elevated troponin
  • Useful for prognosis - elevated = poorer outcome
  • Type 2 MI and myocardial injury common
üß™

Routine Bloods

  • FBC - anaemia worsens HF
  • U&E - baseline for diuretics/ACEi
  • Glucose/HbA1c - screen for diabetes
  • LFTs - elevated in congestion, prognostic
  • TSH - both hypo/hyperthyroid cause HF
‚úÖ

Echocardiography

Haemodynamically unstable: Arrange immediate bedside echo (specialist required)
Stable: Arrange within 48 hours

Assesses: LV/RV function, valvular function, shunts, LVEF (key for management)

For SBP >90-100 mmHg

Classification of Heart Failure

By Left Ventricular Ejection Fraction (LVEF)

üìâ

HFrEF

Heart Failure with reduced EF

LVEF ‚â§40%

  • Associated with LV dysfunction
  • Characterised by cardiomegaly
  • S3 gallop, volume overload
  • Pulmonary congestion common
Benefits from standard HF therapies
üìä

HFmrEF

Heart Failure with mildly reduced EF

LVEF 41-49%

  • Emerging group
  • Evidence of increased LV filling pressures
  • SGLT2 inhibitors recommended
Benefits of standard HF therapies less certain
üìà

HFpEF

Heart Failure with preserved EF

LVEF ‚â•50%

  • Typically normal cardiac size
  • Associated with hypertension
  • S4 gallop common
  • Evidence of diastolic dysfunction
Requires evidence of elevated filling pressures

  • Thyrotoxic crisis
