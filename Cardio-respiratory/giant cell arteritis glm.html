Investigations Lab & Imaging

1st Line Tests

  • ESR / CRP: Elevated in most active vasculitis (non-specific).
  • ANCA: Strongly correlated with ANCA-associated vasculitis (GPA, MPA, EGPA).
    • c-ANCA (PR3) ‚Üí GPA
    • p-ANCA (MPO) ‚Üí MPA / EGPA
  • Urinalysis + Microscopy: Essential to rule out glomerulonephritis.

Biopsy (Gold Standard)

Choose accessible, affected site. Include subcutaneous fat for skin lesions to catch medium vessels.

Pathognomonic Finding: Fibrinoid necrosis, karyorrhexis, RBC extravasation.

Imaging

  • Angiography (CT/MR): Medium/Large vessels (Beading, stenosis, aneurysm).
  • PET Scan: Large vessel inflammation (Takayasu/GCA).

Vasculitis Classification (CHCC 2012)

Large Vessel Aorta & Major Branches

Affects aorta and main branches. Often presents with claudication or pulse deficits.

  • Takayasu Arteritis:
    Usually <40 yrs. Female predominance.
  • Giant Cell Arteritis:
    >50 yrs. Headache, visual loss.
Medium Vessel Main Visceral Arteries

Predominantly affects visceral arteries. Can cause aneurysms.

  • Polyarteritis Nodosa (PAN):
    Skin ulcers, neuropathy, mesenteric ischemia. ANCA negative.
  • Kawasaki Disease:
    Children <5 yrs. Coronary aneurysms.
Small Vessel Capillaries, Venules, Arterioles

Affects organs with dense capillary networks (Kidney, Lung, Skin).

ANCA-Associated:
  • GPA (Granulomatosis)
  • MPA (Microscopic Polyangiitis)
  • EGPA (Eosinophilic)
Immune Complex:
  • IgA Vasculitis (HSP)
  • Cryoglobulinemic
Variable Vessel Any Size
  • Beh√ßet's Disease: Oral/genital ulcers, uveitis, skin lesions.
  • Cogan's Syndrome: Audiovestibular and ocular inflammation.
Secondary / Associated
  • Infection: Hepatitis B/C, HIV, Syphilis.
  • Connective Tissue Disease: SLE, Rheumatoid Arthritis, Sarcoidosis.
  • Drug-Induced: Propylthiouracil, Cocaine (Levamisole).

Life-Threatening or Organ-Threatening

e.g., Pulmonary capillaritis, rapidly progressive glomerulonephritis, mononeuritis multiplex.

Induction (3-6 mo)
Option A (Preferred): Rituximab + Steroids
Option B: Cyclophosphamide + Steroids
Steroid Regimen
IV Pulse: Methylpred 0.5-1g/day x 3-5 days
OR
Oral High Dose: Pred 1mg/kg (Max 80mg)
Note: Reduced-dose regimens are becoming common to lower infection risk.

Non-Life-Threatening / Limited

No major organ involvement. Aim to reduce steroid exposure.

Induction
Methotrexate + Steroids (First line per ACR)
OR Azathioprine / Mycophenolate
OR Rituximab (if contraindications to MTX)
Adjunctive
Avacopan: C5a receptor inhibitor. Can be used as steroid-sparing agent with Rituximab/Cyclophosphamide.

Taper steroids slowly based on clinical response. Continue immunosuppressant for a duration specific to disease type and organ involvement.
