Obstructive Sleep Apnoea in Adults

Interactive Clinical Decision Support Algorithm | BMJ Best Practice (Dec 2025)
Assessment & History
Key Symptoms & Signs
Loud Chronic Snoring Witnessed Apnoeas Daytime Sleepiness Morning Headaches
  • Excessive daytime sleepiness (despite adequate sleep)
  • Witnessed apnoeas or gasping episodes during sleep
  • Chronic loud snoring (often audible beyond bedroom)
  • Unrefreshing sleep, multiple awakenings
  • Morning headaches, dry mouth, nocturia
  • Neurocognitive dysfunction (memory, attention issues)
  • Mood disorders (depression, irritability)
  • History of motor vehicle accidents due to sleepiness
Physical Examination
  • Obesity or large neck circumference (≥40 cm)
  • Maxillomandibular anomalies (retrognathia, narrow palate)
  • Macroglossia with lateral crenations (scalloping)
  • Enlarged tonsils/adenoids, elongated soft palate
  • Cardiopulmonary examination (HTN, arrhythmias)
Risk Factors (STOP-BANG)
S - Snoring
Loud snoring heard through closed doors
T - Tiredness
Daytime fatigue, sleepiness, dozing off
O - Observed Apnoea
Someone witnessed breathing pauses
P - Pressure
Hypertension or on treatment
B - BMI >35 kg/m²
Obesity significantly increases risk
A - Age >50 years
Prevalence increases with age
N - Neck >40cm
Large neck circumference
G - Gender Male
Higher risk in males (2:1 ratio)
STOP-BANG Score Interpretation:
0-2: Low Risk 3-4: Intermediate Risk 5-8: High Risk
Diagnostic Testing
Diagnostic Tests
Polysomnography (PSG)
Gold standard attended sleep study. Measures EEG, airflow, effort, EMG, ECG, and oximetry
Home Sleep Apnoea Test (HSAT)
Portable unattended testing for high pre-test probability patients without major comorbidities
Peripheral Arterial Tonometry (PAT)
Wearable device detecting sympathetic arousals during sleep
Diagnostic Criteria
OSA is diagnosed if:
AHI ≥15 events/hour OR AHI ≥5 with symptoms/comorbidities
Mild: AHI 5-14.9/hr Moderate: AHI 15-29.9/hr Severe: AHI ≥30/hr
Differential Diagnosis
  • Central Sleep Apnoea (absent respiratory effort on PSG)
  • Narcolepsy (cataplexy, sleep paralysis, MSLT testing)
  • Insufficient sleep syndrome (sleep diary/actigraphy)
  • Periodic Limb Movement Disorder
  • Hypothyroidism (check TSH/free T4)
Treatment Algorithm
Severity-Based Treatment

Severe OSA (AHI ≥30)

1st Line: CPAP
Continuous Positive Airway Pressure is first-line therapy. Auto-titrating devices may improve adherence
2nd Line: Oral Appliance
For CPAP-intolerant patients. Less effective but better tolerated
Alternative: Hypoglossal Nerve Stimulation
Implantable device for CPAP-intolerant patients, BMI <32, no complete concentric collapse on DISE

Mild-Moderate OSA (AHI 5-29.9)

1st Line: Oral Appliance
Preferred for mild-moderate OSA or patient preference. Titratable devices preferred
Alternative: CPAP
Evidence inconsistent but recommended for symptomatic patients
Positional Therapy
For patients with positional OSA (low AHI in non-supine position)

Discrete Anatomical Lesions

1st Line: Surgery
Tonsillectomy, UPPP, or MMA for discrete lesions. Can avoid lifelong therapy
Concurrent Obesity Management
Weight Loss Program
Intensive lifestyle interventions effective for AHI reduction
Bariatric Surgery
BMI >40 kg/m² or lower BMI with comorbidities. Marked AHI improvement
Important: OSA may recur despite weight loss. Continued monitoring required.
Pharmacotherapy for Residual Sleepiness
Modafinil/Armodafinil
200-400mg daily. Partially effective for residual sleepiness in CPAP-adherent patients
Solriamfetol
37.5-150mg daily. Dopamine-noradrenaline reuptake inhibitor
Pitolisant
Selective H3-receptor antagonist. Approved in EU for OSA-related sleepiness
NICE UK: Does not recommend pitolisant or solriamfetol due to insufficient evidence
Follow-up & Monitoring
Monitoring Schedule
  • Assess CPAP adherence within 12 weeks (Medicare requires ≥4 hours/night, 5/7 nights)
  • Annual clinical assessments (1-2 times/year)
  • Repeat sleep testing for persistent/recurrent symptoms
  • Repeat testing with ≥10% weight change and symptom change
  • Review CPAP device data for leak rates and residual obstructions
Complications & Comorbidities
Cardiovascular Disease Cognitive Dysfunction Motor Vehicle Accidents Depression
  • Hypertension, arrhythmias, MI, heart failure, stroke
  • Impaired glucose metabolism and type 2 diabetes
  • Increased all-cause mortality if untreated
  • Neurocognitive impairment and dementia risk
Prognosis & Outcomes
Effective Treatment Improves:
  • Daytime alertness and quality of life
  • Cardiovascular morbidity and mortality
  • Cognitive function and mood
  • Motor vehicle accident risk
Patient Counseling
  • Avoid driving when drowsy
  • Avoid alcohol and sedatives before bedtime
  • Maintain healthy weight
  • Consider non-supine sleeping position
  • Airline travel: use CPAP on flights, avoid sedatives/alcohol
