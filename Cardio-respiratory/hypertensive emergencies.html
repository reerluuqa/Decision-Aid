Immediate Definition & Action

Definition: Severely elevated BP (usually >180/120) PLUS new or progressive target organ dysfunction.

BP Reduction Goals

1
Immediate (Mins to 1 Hr)

Reduce Mean Arterial Pressure (MAP) by no more than 25%.

Do not precipitate ischemia
2
Short Term (2-6 Hrs)

If stable, reduce to:

SBP: 160 mmHg DBP: 100-110 mmHg
3
Next 24-48 Hrs

Gradual reduction towards Normal BP.

Transition to oral meds

Exceptions to BP Goals

Aortic Dissection

Target SBP <120 mmHg within first 20 mins.

Pre-eclampsia / Eclampsia

Target SBP <140 mmHg in the first hour.

Phaeochromocytoma

Target SBP <140 mmHg in the first hour. Use Alpha-blocker first.

Management by Organ Involvement

NEURO

Intracranial Haemorrhage

If SBP 150-220: Lower to 140 (Range 130-150).

If SBP >220: Consider intensive lowering, but watch for renal adverse events.

1st Line Labetalol
2nd Line Nicardipine
NEURO

Ischemic Stroke

If Thrombolysis:
Must maintain SBP <185 / DBP <110.

If SBP >220 / DBP >120: Reduce by ~15% in first 24 hrs.

Avoid rapid drops if not treating comorbidities.

CARDIAC

MI / Ischaemia

Goal: Reduce myocardial oxygen demand & improve coronary flow.

1st Line Esmolol (Beta-blocker)
+ Glyceryl Trinitrate (Vasodilator)
2nd Line Labetalol
+ Glyceryl Trinitrate

‚ö†Ô∏è Contraindication: Mod-Severe LV Failure / Pulmonary Edema

CARDIAC

Acute Heart Failure / PE

Goal: Preload and Afterload reduction.

1st Line Glyceryl Trinitrate (IV) or Nitroprusside
Adjunct Loop Diuretic (e.g., Furosemide)
OB/GYN

Pre-eclampsia

Target SBP <140 mmHg in the first hour.
1st Line Labetalol (IV) OR Hydralazine (IV)
Alternative Nifedipine (Oral)

‚ö†Ô∏è Avoid ACEi/ARBs.

NEURO

Hypertensive Encephalopathy

Symptoms: Headache, seizure, vision changes, confusion. Reverses with BP lowering.

1st Line Labetalol
2nd Line Nicardipine
