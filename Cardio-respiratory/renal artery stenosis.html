üîç Diagnostic Approach & Aetiology

Epidemiology & Causes

Atherosclerotic 90% of cases.

Age >55, Male, Diabetes, Dyslipidaemia, PVD/CAD history.

Fibromuscular Dysplasia 10% of cases.

Age <30, Female (2-10x risk). Nonsmoker typically. "String of beads" appearance.

Exam Findings

  • Abdominal Bruit: Highly suggestive. Lateral to midline.
  • Other Bruits: Carotid/Femoral (suggests systemic atherosclerosis).
  • Hypokalaemia: Suggests high renin activation.
  • Retinopathy: May indicate severe hypertension duration.

üíä Management Strategy

First-Line: Medical Therapy

‚úì PRIMARY: Antihypertensives + Statin + Aspirin
  • BP Control: ACEi/ARB (Monitor K+ and Creatinine).
  • Statin: Target LDL < 1.8-2.6 mmol/L (70-100 mg/dL).
  • Lifestyle: Smoking cessation, Low salt, Glycaemic control.

Intervention (Stenting)

Routine stenting is not recommended (CORAL trial). Reserve for specific high-risk cases:

  • Recurrent flash pulmonary oedema.
  • Refractory hypertension on >3 drugs.
  • Stenosis in a single functioning kidney.
  • Unstable angina + uncontrolled HTN.

First-Line: Angioplasty (PTA)

‚úì PROCEDURE: Percutaneous Transluminal Angioplasty

Often curative. Technical success & 10-year patency ~90%.

Stenting is not indicated unless complications (dissection) occur.

Adjunctive Therapy

  • Antihypertensives: Required if BP remains elevated post-procedure.
  • Smoking Cessation: Critical factor in etiology.
