Initial Assessment
Identify Severity
Massive: >600mL/24h or >150mL/h
Moderate: 30-600mL/24h
Mild: <30mL/24h
Confirm True Haemoptysis

Differentiate from haematemesis and pseudohaemoptysis:

  • Bright red, frothy, alkaline (pH >7.0) - True haemoptysis
  • Dark blood, acidic (pH <7.0), food particles - Haematemesis
  • Check oral cavity, nasopharynx for bleeding sources
Immediate Actions
ABC Assessment - Secure airway, breathing, circulation
Position patient with bleeding lung dependent
Protect non-bleeding lung
Diagnostic Workup
Detailed History

Key historical factors:

  • Smoking history, occupational exposures
  • Previous TB, lung cancer, bronchiectasis
  • Anticoagulation use, bleeding disorders
  • Travel history to endemic areas
  • Associated symptoms: fever, weight loss, dyspnea

Treatment Approach
Massive Haemoptysis - Emergency
ICU admission, airway protection
Immediate bronchoscopy

Medical Management
Tranexamic acid (oral/inhaled)
Correct coagulopathy
Treat underlying infection
Surgical Options

Consider surgery for:

  • Failed embolization
  • Aspergilloma
  • Traumatic injury
  • Localized bronchiectasis
Follow-up Care
Monitoring
Resolution of bleeding
Investigate recurrent episodes
Regular respiratory review
Prevention
  • Smoking cessation counseling
  • Optimize treatment of underlying lung disease
  • Vaccination (influenza, pneumococcal)
  • Pulmonary rehabilitation if indicated
When to Refer

Suspected malignancy
