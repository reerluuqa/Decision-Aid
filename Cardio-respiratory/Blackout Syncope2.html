Blackouts & Syncope – One‑Page Primary Care Algorithm

For adults (≥16 yrs). Built for fast memory aide & decision support at point‑of‑care.
Safety Assessment Classify Management Lifestyle Other

1 Initial Safety Check

Immediate hospital admission / specialist referral if any of:

  • Ongoing impaired consciousness
  • Suspected life‑threatening cause (MI/arrhythmia/PE/major haemorrhage/SAH, etc.)
  • Serious injury from collapse

2 Core Assessment

Vitals → pulse, BP (lying + ≥1 min standing), RR, SpO₂, temp
  • Capillary glucose
  • Cardiac exam (murmur, HF signs)
  • Neurological exam (focal deficits, parkinsonism, neuropathy)
Assess OHSBP ↓ ≥20 or DBP ↓ ≥10 or SBP <90 + symptoms
Assess PoTSHR ↑ >30 bpm (or >120) within 10 min standing, no OH
12‑lead ECG for all

Targeted history / witness account:

  • Context + posture (standing/sitting/supine); injury details
  • Prodrome (sweating, warmth), déjà vu/jamais vu (→ seizure)
  • Appearance (pallor; eyes closed → PPS/PNES)
  • Movements, tongue bite (lateral → seizure; tip → vasovagal), incontinence
  • Duration: <30s syncope; >1 min epilepsy; >5 min PPS/PNES
  • Associated Sx: new breathlessness, palpitations, chest pain, headache, neuro Sx
  • Triggers: exertion/supine, post‑exercise, prolonged standing, meals, pain, micturition/cough/swallow; neck rotation/tight collar
  • PMH: structural/CAD; meds predisposing to reflex/OH; FH sudden death <40; alcohol

3 Classify Likely Cause

Reflex syncope (vasovagal, situational, carotid sinus)

  • 3 Ps: Posture (standing), Provoking (fear, pain, heat), Prodrome (pallor, sweat, N/V)
  • Situational: micturition, defaecation, cough, swallow, post‑exercise, laughter
  • Carotid sinus: head turn / neck pressure; age ≥40

Orthostatic hypotension / PoTS

  • Worse on standing; post‑prandial; mornings/heat; after exertion; prolonged bed rest
  • Symptoms: light‑headedness, visual blurring, fatigue; relieved by sitting/lying
  • Consider neurogenic vs non‑neurogenic; PoTS = excessive HR rise without BP fall

Cardiac syncope

  • During exertion or supine; sudden palpitations → syncope; new breathlessness
  • Age ≥65 with no prodrome; murmur/HF; FH sudden death <40
  • ECG: brady/tachyarrhythmia, AV block, long/short QT, Brugada, WPW, etc.

Non‑syncope d/dx

  • Epilepsy (tonic/clonic/atonic), PNES/PPS (often prolonged, eyes closed)
  • Cataplexy; stroke/TIA; hypoglycaemia/hypoxia/hyperventilation
  • Cardiac arrest (no spontaneous recovery); migraine‑associated episodes

4 Management Pathways

Red flags → Urgent specialist assessment (often within 24h)

  • Exertional or supine syncope
  • No prodrome in older adults; recurrent unexplained syncope; high trauma risk
  • Palpitations immediately before event
  • Abnormal ECG; murmur/known heart disease; FH sudden death <40

Directed referrals

  • Cardiac suspected → urgent cardiology (avoid exercise until assessed if exertional)
  • Carotid sinus (≥40 yrs) → falls & syncope service (consider CSM)
  • Epilepsy / PNES / PPS → neurology
  • Unexplained syncope → consider ambulatory ECG monitoring

Primary care management

  • Uncomplicated reflex syncope → reassure; education; trigger avoidance
  • Ask patients to record future episodes if triggers unclear
  • Review meds that worsen reflex/OH; adjust if possible

5 Lifestyle / Self‑Management

Reflex syncope

  • Avoid triggers; recognize prodrome → sit/lie promptly
  • Hydration ≈ 2 L/day
  • Counter‑pressure manoeuvres (leg/knee cross, squat, handgrip, arm tensing)

Orthostatic hypotension / PoTS

  • Rise slowly; sit before standing; prefer sitting tasks
  • Small frequent meals; limit alcohol; morning caution; heat avoidance
  • Hydration 2–3 L/day; consider salt if no contraindication; optional 500 mL water bolus for immediate BP lift
  • Compression garments to waist / abdominal binders; head‑up sleeping
  • Regular physical activity to prevent deconditioning

If persistent OH despite optimisation → consider specialist input ± fludrocortisone/midodrine.

6 Other Considerations

  • Driving: advise stop driving and follow DVLA guidance while awaiting assessment/diagnosis.
  • Occupational risk: consider Occupational Health for safety‑critical roles.
  • Patient resources: STARS (Syncope Trust) and PoTS UK.
