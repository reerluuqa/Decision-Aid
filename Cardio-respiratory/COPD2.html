COPD-GEMS

Suspect COPD

Target: Smokers/Ex-smokers >35y

  • Exertional breathlessness or wheeze
  • Chronic cough
  • Regular sputum production
  • Frequent ‘winter bronchitis’

Investigations

  • Spirometry: FEV1/FVC <0.7 (Post-bronchodilator). Reversibility not routinely needed.
  • FBC: Eosinophils, anaemia, polycythaemia. Red FlagThrombocytosis (?cancer)
  • CXR: Exclude other diagnoses.
  • BMI: Check for obesity/cachexia.

RED FLAGS Features Suggesting Other Disease

  • Weight loss
  • Fatigue
  • Ankle swelling
  • Paroxysmal nocturnal dyspnoea
  • Occupational risk (e.g. asbestos)
  • Haemoptysis (≥40y: Refer 2ww pathway)
  • Chest pain (Urgent CXR)
Act Immediately

Subjective Severity (MRC)

Note: Used by NICE (not mMRC)

1 Breathless only with strenuous exercise
2 Breathless hurrying/walking up hills
3 Can’t keep up with peers on level ground
4 Stops for breath after walking 100m
5 Breathless when dressing/housebound

Acute Management Priorities

Triage

  • Admission needed? (Assess ABCs, sats, confusion)
  • Bloods/CXR/Sputum: NOT routinely needed if admission not required.

Self-Management

  • Home rescue therapy reduces admissions (NNT 12-17).
  • Patient MUST know to book review & call help if deteriorating.

Steroids

30mg Prednisolone
for FIVE days

Evidence shows no benefit from longer courses.

Start if: Breathlessness interferes with activities of daily living.

Antibiotics (All 5 day courses)

Start if: Sputum changes colour, increases volume, or increases thickness.

Special Considerations

Prophylactic Azithromycin?

If on prophylactic azithromycin, continue it but treat exacerbation with a non-macrolide.

High Risk of Failure?

Repeated antibiotic courses or high risk of complications? Base choice on sputum culture.

Health Promotion & Maintenance

  • Smoking Cessation: Document pack years.
  • Pulmonary Rehab: If admission with exacerbation or MRC ≥3.
  • Vaccinations: Pneumococcal & Flu.
  • Mental Health: Screen for anxiety/depression.

Management Strategy: NICE vs GOLD 2025

Initial Approach: Short-acting agent (SABA preferred) prn.

Few Symptoms/Exacerbations

(MRC <3, CAT <10, <2 exacs/y)

GOLD A: LABA or LAMA (Monotherapy)
NICE: SABA prn

More Symptoms (Majority)

(Ongoing symptoms/breathlessness)

GOLD B & NICE:
LABA + LAMA

When to add ICS (Inhaled Corticosteroids)

Who needs ICS? Only if benefits outweigh pneumonia risk.

NICE Approach

ICS + LABA if:

  • PMH Asthma/Atopy
  • Symptom variability
  • Higher blood eosinophils

GOLD Approach (Triple Therapy)

Remember the "Es":

  • Exacerbations: ≥2/year OR admission in past year.
  • Eosinophils: Blood eosinophils ≥0.3.

*Also consider if Asthma present.

Step-Up Therapy

If initial therapy insufficient...

  • Check adherence & inhaler technique.
  • Consider comorbidities.
  • If on monotherapy → swap to LABA+LAMA.
  • For everyone else: Try different device or molecule.
Ongoing Exacerbations (≥1/y):
Recalculate the "Es":
• Eosinophils ≥0.1 (Lower threshold here!)
• Just 1 exacerbation counts.

Referral Criteria

  • Diagnostic uncertainty / symptoms disproportionate to spirometry.
  • Haemoptysis (Cancer pathway).
  • Rapid decline in FEV1 (≥500ml in <5y).
  • Signs of cor pulmonale.

Long-Term Oxygen

Refer if used ≥15h/day AND:

  • FEV1 <30% (or 30-49% if clinical need)
  • Sats ≤92%
  • Cyanosis/Polycythaemia

Inhaler Device Choice

Dry-powder devices and soft mists have lower carbon footprints.
