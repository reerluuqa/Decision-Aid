Type 2 Diabetes Clinical Reference

Last revised: July 2025

Diabetic Emergency Alert

Immediate action required for hypoglycaemia (<4.0 mmol/L) or hyperglycaemic emergencies

Hypoglycaemia (<4.0 mmol/L)

Symptoms: Sweating, tremor, palpitations, anxiety, hunger, confusion, seizures, coma

Conscious: 15-20g fast-acting glucose
Unconscious: IM/SC glucagon or IV glucose
Recheck in 15 mins

Hypersomolar Hyperglycaemic State (HHS)

Symptoms: Extreme hyperglycaemia (>30 mmol/L), dehydration, altered mental status

Urgent hospital admission
IV fluids
IV insulin (cautiously)

Red Flags Requiring Immediate Referral

  • Severe persistent hyperglycaemia (>30 mmol/L)
  • Ketosis >1.5 mmol/L with symptoms
  • Uncontrolled diabetes with complications
  • Rapid deterioration in vision
  • Pregnant women with poor glycaemic control
  • Severe foot infection (cellulitis, ulcer, gangrene)
  • eGFR <30 ml/min/1.73m²
  • New/worsening renal impairment

Who to Test

  • Adults >40 years (earlier in high-risk groups)
  • BMI ≥25 (≥23 in South Asian, Chinese, African-Caribbean)
  • First-degree relative with diabetes
  • History of CVD, hypertension, or dyslipidaemia
  • Women with previous gestational diabetes
  • Women with PCOS
  • High-risk ethnic groups

Special Considerations

  • Children & young people: Refer to specialist team
  • Black people with sickle cell trait: HbA1c may underestimate glycaemia
  • HIV patients: Consider alternative testing methods
  • Pregnant women: Use pregnancy-specific criteria
  • Elderly/frail: Consider individualized targets

Annual Review Components

  • HbA1c measurement
  • Blood pressure measurement
  • Lipid profile
  • Renal function (eGFR, UACR)
  • Foot examination and risk assessment
  • Retinal screening
  • Weight/BMI measurement
  • Lifestyle advice
  • Medication review
  • Psychological wellbeing assessment

First-line Medications

Metformin
First-line for most patients. Dose: 500mg-1g BD with food (max 2g/day)
GI upset Vitamin B12 deficiency Lactic acidosis
Sulfonylureas (Gliclazide, Glimepiride)
Consider if metformin contraindicated or not tolerated
Hypoglycaemia Weight gain DRESS

Second-line Options

SGLT2 inhibitors (Dapagliflozin, Empagliflozin)
Consider if established CVD, heart failure, or CKD
UTIs Genital infections Euglycaemic DKA
GLP-1 receptor agonists (Liraglutide, Semaglutide)
Consider if BMI ≥27 with weight-related comorbidity or BMI ≥30
GI upset Injection site reactions Pancreatitis

Medication Safety Alerts

SGLT2 inhibitors: Risk of euglycaemic DKA
GLP-1 RAs: Potential risk of pancreatitis
Metformin: Contraindicated if eGFR <30
Pioglitazone: Increased risk of bladder cancer
Dapagliflozin: New interaction with lithium
Tirzepatide: May reduce oral contraceptive efficacy
