Hypertension — Quick Algorithm

One‑Page Reference Algorithm

Based on NICE CKS: Hypertension (last revised May 2025). Designed for rapid decision‑making at point of care.

Adults ≥18 years Primary care UK practice Print‑friendly

1) Confirm Diagnosis Key thresholds

  • Clinic ≥140/90 → repeat ×2; record lower of last two.
  • Confirm with ABPM/HBPM average ≥135/85.
  • Stages (clinic / ABPM–HBPM):
    • Stage 1: 140/90–159/99 / 135/85–149/94
    • Stage 2: ≥160/100 / ≥150/95
    • Severe: ≥180/120
  • White‑coat: clinic exceeds ABPM/HBPM by >20/10. Masked: normal clinic but raised out‑of‑clinic.
  • Consider secondary HTN if <40 y, resistant, accelerated, or atypical history/exam.

Same‑day specialist

  • BP ≥180/120 with retinal haemorrhage/papilloedema or life‑threatening symptoms (new confusion, chest pain, heart failure, AKI).
  • Suspected phaeochromocytoma (labile/postural BP, headache, palpitations, pallor, diaphoresis, abdo pain).

2) Baseline Investigations

  • Target organ damage screen: urine ACR, haematuria; fundoscopy; 12‑lead ECG.
  • Bloods: HbA1c, electrolytes, creatinine, eGFR, lipids (total & HDL).
  • QRISK 10‑yr CVD risk (use clinic BP).
  • Specialist tests if signs of secondary causes or organ damage.

Monitoring Considerations

  • Postural symptoms/diabetes/≥80 y: measure standing and seated BP; if standing drop ≥20 mmHg, base target on standing and review therapy.
  • For self‑monitoring: train on HBPM; use average of waking‑hours readings.

3) Lifestyle (all patients)

  • Weight loss, regular exercise; heart‑healthy diet.
  • Salt reduction; avoid KCl substitutes in older adults, CKD, diabetes, or on ACEi/ARB.
  • Limit alcohol; stop smoking; moderate caffeine.
  • Provide patient information and local support; annual CVD risk review.

4) When to Start Drug Treatment

  • Stage 2: offer drug therapy regardless of age.
  • Stage 1: if <80 y with TOD/CVD/renal disease/diabetes or QRISK ≥10%.
  • Consider if <60 y even with QRISK <10% (lifetime risk).
  • ≥80 y: treat if clinic ≥150/90.
  • Use clinical judgement in frailty/multimorbidity.

5) Drug Algorithm

General

  • Titrate to optimal/tolerated dose at each step; combinations are often required.
  • Isolated systolic (SBP ≥160): treat as per combined HTN.
  • Do not combine ACEi + ARB.
  • Pregnancy/planning/breastfeeding: avoid ACEi/ARB unless essential—discuss risks.

Step 1

  • <55 y (non‑Black): ACEi or ARB.
  • ≥55 y or Black African/Caribbean (any age): CCB.
  • T2DM (any age): ACEi/ARB (prefer ARB if Black African/Caribbean).
  • CCB intolerance or heart failure → thiazide‑like diuretic (e.g., indapamide).

Step 2

  • If on ACEi/ARB → add CCB or thiazide‑like diuretic.
  • If on CCB → add ACEi/ARB or thiazide‑like diuretic.

Step 3

  • Triple therapy: ACEi/ARB + CCB + thiazide‑like.

Step 4 — Resistant HTN

  • Confirm with ABPM/HBPM; assess for postural drop; support adherence.
  • K⁺ ≤4.5: add low‑dose spironolactone (check eGFR).
  • K⁺ >4.5 or unsuitable: add α‑blocker or β‑blocker.
  • Uncontrolled on 4 drugs → seek specialist advice.

6) Treatment Targets

  • <80 y: clinic <140/90 (ABPM/HBPM <135/85).
  • ≥80 y: clinic <150/90 (ABPM/HBPM <145/85).
  • Frailty/multimorbidity: individualise targets.

7) Follow‑up & Safety

  • Check U&Es/eGFR ~1 month after starting ACEi/ARB/diuretic; repeat as needed.
  • Review adherence and adverse effects at each step.
  • Annual review: BP, risk factors, target organ damage, patient education.

Notes

  • Prefer thiazide‑like over conventional thiazides when initiating.
  • Use generics where appropriate.

Scope & Exclusions

  • Adults ≥18 years. Primary HTN management in primary care.
  • Not covered here: CKD‑specific targets, type 1 diabetes, pregnancy, children, confirmed secondary HTN (see relevant guidance).
