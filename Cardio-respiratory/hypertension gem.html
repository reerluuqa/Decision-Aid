Hypertension Quick Ref

SAME-DAY REFERRAL REQUIRED

BP ≥180/120 = Severe Hypertension

  • Accelerated HTN: Papilloedema, retinal haemorrhages
  • Phaeochromocytoma: Labile BP, headache, palpitations, pallor, sweating
  • Life-threatening: Confusion, chest pain, heart failure, AKI

ASYMPTOMATIC SEVERE BP (≥180/120)

1
Look for target organ damage ASAP
ECG, fundoscopy, U&Es, urine ACR
2
If damage present
TREAT IMMEDIATELY without ABPM/HBPM
3
No damage
Repeat clinic BP within 7 days

BP CLASSIFICATION

Category Clinic BP Action
Normal <140/90 Recheck every 5y
Elevated 140–179/90–119 Offer ABPM
Severe ≥180/120 Same-day action

BP MEASUREMENT

  • Use appropriately-sized cuff
  • If ≥140/90: repeat. If 2nd differs, do 3rd → record lowest of last 2
  • Irregular pulse: auscultate manually
  • First diagnosis: measure both arms
  • If difference >15mmHg persists → use higher arm

Adapt for age/frailty. Use standing BP if postural symptoms.

POSTURAL BP DROP

Significant drop:

Systolic ≥20 mmHg
Diastolic ≥10 mmHg

Check if:

  • ≥80 years old
  • Type 2 diabetes
  • Postural symptoms (falls/dizziness)

Action: Use STANDING BP for targets

LIFESTYLE FOR ALL

  • Smoking cessation
  • Weight loss if overweight (1kg = 1mmHg SBP drop)
  • Regular exercise
  • Healthy diet

Lifestyle advice remains important even on medication

BEFORE ADDING 4TH DRUG

  • Check compliance
  • Confirm BP elevated with ABPM or home average
  • Check for postural drop

Irish study: almost half didn't have resistant HTN when compliance and ABPM checked

SPECIAL POPULATIONS

  • African/Caribbean: Prefer ARB over ACEi (less angioedema risk)
  • Pregnancy/breastfeeding: Follow NICE pregnancy guidance
  • Heart failure: Use indapamide + follow HF guidance
  • CVD: Follow NICE guidance for that disease first
