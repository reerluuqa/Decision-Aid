NSTEMI vs. Unstable Angina

Feature Unstable Angina (UA) NSTEMI
Biomarkers (Troponin) Normal / Absent Elevated (>99th %)
Pathology Ischemia without necrosis Ischemia causing necrosis

Definition

An acute coronary syndrome defined by the absence of biochemical evidence of myocardial damage.

Clinical Findings

Look for these specific patterns:

  • Prolonged angina at rest (>20 mins)
  • New onset severe angina
  • Increasing frequency/duration
  • Accelerating pattern of angina
  • Post-MI angina

ECG & Labs

ECG Possibilities:
Normal ST Depression Transient ST Elevation T-wave Inversion
Lab Action:

Measure high-sensitivity troponins on presentation. Serial measurements may be needed to rule out MI.

Early Management Focus

Initial interventions and triage according to the presumptive diagnosis. Since troponins are negative, the focus is on symptoms and ECG changes to prevent progression to infarction.

Classification Logic

Classically, ACS is divided into three clinical categories based on:

  • Presence/absence of ST-segment elevation on initial ECG.
  • Measurement of myocardial biomarkers (hs-troponins).
Note: Unstable Angina and NSTEMI are often grouped as 'non-ST-elevation acute coronary syndromes' because they represent a continuum of pathology, differing mainly by the presence of markers of myocardial damage.
