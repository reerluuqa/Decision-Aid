Drugs to AVOID in Heart Failure

  • ‚úó

    NSAIDs (e.g., Ibuprofen, Naproxen)

    Worsen fluid retention and can impair renal function. Strongly contraindicated.

  • ‚úó

    Glitazones (Thiazolidinediones)

    Pioglitazone, rosiglitazone ‚Äî cause fluid retention and worsen heart failure.

  • ‚úó

    Doxazosin

    Alpha-blocker associated with fluid retention and reflex tachycardia.

  • ‚úó

    Non-Dihydropyridine Calcium Channel Blockers

    Verapamil, diltiazem ‚Äî impair myocardial contraction in HFrEF.

  • ‚úó

    Class I Antiarrhythmics

    Flecainide, quinidine ‚Äî negative inotropes, increase mortality risk.

NYHA Functional Classification

NYHA I No limitation of physical activity. Ordinary activity doesn't cause symptoms.
NYHA II Slight limitation. Comfortable at rest, but ordinary activity causes symptoms.
NYHA III Marked limitation. Comfortable at rest, but minimal activity causes symptoms.
NYHA IV Symptoms at rest. Unable to carry out any physical activity without discomfort.
