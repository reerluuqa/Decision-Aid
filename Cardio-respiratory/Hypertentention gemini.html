Hypertension Management Algorithm

A Quick Reference Guide for Primary Care (Based on NICE CKS)

This is a summary aid. Refer to the full NICE CKS guidance for complete details.

Diagnosis & Initial Assessment

Suspicion

Measure BP in both arms. If difference >15 mmHg, use arm with the higher reading. Suspect hypertension if:

Clinic BP ≥ 140/90 mmHg

Confirmation

If clinic BP is 140/90-180/120 mmHg, offer ABPM or HBPM. Confirm diagnosis if:

  • ABPM (daytime avg): ≥ 135/85 mmHg
  • HBPM (average): ≥ 135/85 mmHg

Initial Workup

While awaiting confirmation, assess for target organ damage:

  • Urine: ACR, haematuria dipstick
  • Blood: HbA1c, eGFR, electrolytes, lipids
  • Other: 12-lead ECG, Fundoscopy
  • Calculate 10-year CVD risk (e.g., QRISK®)

Urgent Action: Clinic BP ≥ 180/120 mmHg

Immediate Same-Day Specialist Referral if:

  • Signs of retinal haemorrhage/papilloedema
  • Life-threatening symptoms (chest pain, confusion, heart failure, AKI)

If no such signs, investigate urgently and consider immediate treatment.

Staging & When to Start Treatment

Stage Clinic BP (mmHg) Action: Offer Drug Treatment IF...
Stage 1 140/90 - 159/99 Age < 80 AND any of: Target organ damage, CVD, renal disease, diabetes, or 10-year CVD risk ≥ 10%.
Stage 2 ≥ 160/100 Offer treatment to all adults, regardless of age.

Always provide lifestyle advice: healthy diet, low salt, exercise, weight control, smoking cessation, and limited alcohol.

Monitoring & Treatment Targets

Patient Group Clinic BP Target ABPM/HBPM Target
Adults < 80 years < 140/90 mmHg < 135/85 mmHg
Adults ≥ 80 years < 150/90 mmHg < 145/85 mmHg

* Use clinical judgement for frailty/multimorbidity. Base targets on standing BP for postural hypotension.

Pharmacological Treatment Pathway

Step 1: A or C

  • Age < 55 OR have Type 2 Diabetes:
    Start A (ACEi or ARB*)
  • Age ≥ 55 OR person of Black African/Caribbean family origin (no T2DM):
    Start C (CCB)

*Prefer ARB over ACEi for people of Black African/Caribbean family origin.

Step 2: A + C or A + D

If not controlled, combine treatments:

A (ACEi/ARB) + C (CCB)

If CCB not tolerated, use A + D (Thiazide-Like Diuretic).

Step 3: A + C + D

If still not controlled, offer triple therapy:

A + C + D

Step 4: Resistant Hypertension (Refer)

If uncontrolled on 3 drugs, confirm with ABPM/HBPM, assess adherence & seek specialist advice.

  • If Blood K+ ≤ 4.5 mmol/L: Consider adding low-dose Spironolactone.
  • If Blood K+ > 4.5 mmol/L: Consider an Alpha- or Beta-blocker.
