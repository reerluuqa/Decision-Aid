Familial Hypercholesterolaemia - Clinical Reference

Last revised: May 2025
URGENT: Untreated FH patients aged 20-39 have a 100-fold increased risk of death from heart disease. Early detection and treatment are critical.
Critical Diagnostic Values
Condition LDL Cholesterol Level
Adult heterozygous FH > 7.5 mmol/L
Adult homozygous FH > 13 mmol/L
Child homozygous FH > 11 mmol/L
Refer to Specialist
Quick Facts
  • Prevalence: 1 in 250-500 (heterozygous)
  • Inheritance: Autosomal dominant
  • Underdiagnosed: Only ~4-17% identified
  • Present from birth
  • 50% risk for first-degree relatives
Identification Criteria

Suspect FH in adults with:

  • Total cholesterol > 7.5 mmol/L
  • Personal or family history of premature CHD (before 60 years)

Clinical Signs
  • Tendon xanthomata (highly specific)
  • Most commonly on Achilles tendons and hands
  • Eyelid xanthelasma
  • Premature corneal arcus

Note: Absence of clinical signs does not exclude FH

Complications & Prognosis
  • Ischaemic heart disease
  • Peripheral vascular disease
  • Acute coronary syndrome
  • Stroke
  • Erectile dysfunction

Without treatment:

  • >50% risk of CHD in men by age 50
  • >30% risk of CHD in women by age 60
Genetic Causes
  • LDL receptor mutations (80-95%)
  • Apolipoprotein B-100 (APOB) mutations
  • PCSK9 gain-of-function mutations
  • Rare mutations in APOE and STAP1
Management Priorities
  1. Refer all confirmed FH to specialist
  2. Initiate cascade testing for relatives
  3. Start lipid-modifying treatment
  4. Address modifiable risk factors
  5. Annual structured review

Risk Factors
Unhealthy diet (high in saturated fats)
Sedentary lifestyle
Overweight or obesity
Smoking
Excessive alcohol consumption
Special Considerations for Children

Diagnosis

  • Children at risk: offer DNA test by age 10
  • Homozygous FH: measure LDL before age 5
  • Manage in secondary care by specialist

Treatment

  • Assess for lipid-modifying treatment by age 10
  • Specialist with expertise in FH in children
  • Child-focused setting recommended
