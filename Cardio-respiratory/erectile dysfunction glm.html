Erectile Dysfunction Quick Reference

Last revised: September 2025
RED FLAG: Erectile dysfunction may be an early marker of cardiovascular disease. Assess cardiac risk before initiating treatment.
Definition: Persistent inability to attain and maintain an erection sufficient to permit satisfactory sexual performance.
Assessment
Investigations
  • Assess with partner if possible
  • Onset: Gradual (organic) vs Sudden (psychogenic)
  • Erection quality: Morning, spontaneous, self-stimulated, partner-related
  • Sexual desire, arousal, ejaculation, orgasm issues
  • Consider using IIEF or SHIM questionnaires
  • For all patients:
    • HbA1c or fasting glucose
    • Lipid profile
    • Fasting total testosterone (9-11am)
  • If testosterone low/borderline:
    • Repeat testosterone + FSH, LH, SHBG, prolactin
  • Consider: PSA, thyroid function, LFTs, renal function

    Management in Primary Care

    Initial Management

    • Address lifestyle factors (weight, exercise, smoking, alcohol)
    • Manage comorbidities (diabetes, hypertension, lipids)
    • Review and adjust medications if possible
    • Provide psychological support if indicated

    Pharmacological Treatment

    • First-line: PDE-5 inhibitors (sildenafil, tadalafil, vardenafil, avanafil)
    • Consider dose escalation if initial response unsatisfactory
    • Contraindicated with: Nitrates, guanylate cyclase stimulators (riociguat)
    • Use with caution: Alpha-blockers (risk of hypotension)
    Prescribing Information
    When to Refer

    Emergency Actions

    • High cardiac risk: Urgent cardiology referral before initiating ED treatment
    • Sudden onset of severe ED: Consider urgent referral to exclude serious underlying pathology
    • Priapism (erection >4 hours): URGENT urology referral - medical emergency
    • Severe psychological distress: Urgent mental health referral if suicidal ideation

    PDE-5 Inhibitor Prescribing

    Drug Dose Onset Duration Key Points
    Sildenafil 50mg 1h before activity (max 100mg) 30-60min 4-5h Avoid high-fat meal, may cause visual disturbances
    Tadalafil 10mg 30min before (max 20mg) 30-45min 24-36h Less affected by food, daily low-dose option available
    Vardenafil 10mg 25-60min before (max 20mg) 30min 4-5h Orodispersible tablet available
    Avanafil 100mg 15-30min before (max 200mg) 15-30min 6h Fastest onset, watch for CSCR adverse effect

    Referral Criteria

    Urgent Referral

    • Priapism (erection >4 hours)
    • Penile curvature or pain (Peyronie's disease)
    • Penile abnormalities or masses
    • Testicular lumps or pain

    Specialist Referral

    • Failed trial of PDE-5 inhibitors
    • Low testosterone levels
    • Complex psychogenic factors
    • Post-prostatectomy ED
    • Consideration of vacuum devices, injections, or surgery
