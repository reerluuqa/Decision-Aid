Hypertension GEMS

Quick-Scan Clinical Reference (Based on NICE NG136 2019)

RED FLAGS: SAME-DAY ACTION

BP ≥ 180/120 + Symptoms

Confusion, chest pain, heart failure, AKI, papilloedema, retinal haemorrhages.

Accelerated Hypertension

Papilloedema or retinal haemorrhages (regardless of BP level).

Suspected Phaeochromocytoma

Labile BP (hypertension/postural hypotension), headache, palpitations, sweating.

NORMOTENSIVE
<140/90

Recheck in 5y (or sooner if close to 140/90).

STAGE 1
140-179/90-119

Requires ABPM/HBPM confirmation. Check target organ damage.

SEVERE
≥180/120

If asymptomatic & no target organ damage: repeat clinic within 7 days.

Diagnosis Protocol

  • ABPM (Gold Standard): ≥14 readings avg (daytime only). Target: ≥135/85.
  • HBPM: 2 readings twice daily, ≥4 days (discard day 1). Target: ≥135/85.
  • Clinic BP: Use for screening/targets. If 1st & 2nd differ, take 3rd: record LOWEST of last 2.
  • Irregular Pulse? Auscultate manually (automated fails).

↕ Postural Hypotension Check

Who to check:
  • Age ≥ 80
  • Type 2 Diabetes
  • History of falls / postural dizziness
Significant Drop:

Systolic ≥20 mmHg OR Diastolic ≥10 mmHg upon standing (after 1 min).

Action:

Review meds. If symptomatic & persistent: consider referral. Use STANDING BP for future targets.

Blood Pressure Targets

Standard (<80y) Clinic <140/90
Standard (<80y) Home/ABPM <135/85
Older (≥80y) Clinic <150/90
Older (≥80y) Home/ABPM <145/85
CKD Exception: If ACR ≥70 mg/mmol AND <80y, target Clinic <130/80.

Referral Criteria

Routine Referral Consider
  • Under 40s: Need detailed assessment (secondary causes, lifetime risk).
  • Suspected Secondary Hypertension: e.g. unusual presentation.
  • Unresponsive on Step 4 therapy.
Same-Day Referral
  • Accelerated hypertension (see Red Flags above).
  • Suspected phaeochromocytoma.
