When to Suspect

üö© High-Yield Presentations

  • Incidental bilateral hilar lymphadenopathy on CXR
  • Persistent cough + dyspnoea (no obvious cause)
  • Hypercalcaemia + respiratory symptoms
  • Erythema nodosum + arthralgia
  • Lupus pernio (bluish-red facial nodules)
  • Unexplained arrhythmia or heart failure

Investigations

‚úÖ First-Line Tests in Primary Care

  • Chest X-ray (if not done)
  • FBC (lymphopenia in 50%)
  • LFTs (‚Üë ALP common)
  • U&Es + serum calcium
  • 12-lead ECG
  • Spirometry + gas transfer (if available)

Referral & Management

‚úÖ Primary Care Actions

  • Refer ALL suspected cases to secondary care (respiratory/appropriate specialist)
  • Urgent referral if cardiac/neuro symptoms or arrhythmia
  • Baseline eye assessment if ocular symptoms
  • Manage fatigue/mood (affects 80%)
  • Simple analgesia for chest pain
  • Smoking cessation advice
  • Signpost to Sarcoidosis UK support
üè• Secondary Care Assessment ‚ñº

Diagnostic workup includes:

  • CT chest (high-resolution cuts)
  • Bronchoscopy ¬± biopsy
  • Tissue biopsy confirmation
  • Echo if cardiac involvement suspected
  • PET/MRI for staging extent

Treatment indications: Dangerous disease OR unacceptable quality of life loss

First-line: Oral corticosteroids
Second-line: Methotrexate, hydroxychloroquine, azathioprine
Third-line: Anti-TNF (infliximab, adalimumab)

üîÑ Long-term Primary Care Role ‚ñº

For patients discharged from secondary care:

  • Monitor for new/worsening symptoms ‚Üí re-refer
  • Assess fatigue & mood at every contact
  • Shared-care prescribing if arranged
  • Signpost to benefits advice
  • Refer for occupational therapy/pulmonary rehab
  • Palliative care liaison if progressive disease
