Critical Recognition & Emergency Actions

Hypertensive Emergency

Systolic BP

≥180 mmHg

Diastolic BP

≥110 mmHg

  • End-organ damage present
  • Acute stroke/encephalopathy
  • Acute coronary syndrome
  • Acute heart failure/pulmonary edema
  • Aortic dissection
  • Acute kidney injury
Red Flag Symptoms
  • Severe headache (sudden onset)
  • Visual changes / papilloedema
  • Chest pain
  • Shortness of breath
  • Sensory or motor deficit
  • Altered consciousness

Treatment Actions

Lifestyle Modifications
  • Sodium: ≤1.5 g/day optimal
  • DASH Diet: 8-10 servings fruit/veg daily
  • Exercise: 150 min/week moderate aerobic
  • Alcohol: ≤2 drinks/day (men), ≤1 (women)
  • Weight: Target BMI ~25 kg/m²
  • Waist: <102cm (M), <88cm (F)

Risk Factors & Screening

Strong Risk Factors
  • Obesity – 60% higher risk with 5kg+ weight gain
  • Age >60 years
  • Black ancestry – highest incidence
  • Diabetes mellitus
  • Family history of HTN or CAD
  • Metabolic syndrome
  • Sedentary lifestyle – <3x/week exercise
  • Obstructive sleep apnoea
Secondary Causes to Exclude
  • Drug-induced (NSAIDs, OCP, steroids)
  • Chronic kidney disease
  • Renal artery stenosis
  • Primary aldosteronism
  • Phaeochromocytoma
  • Cushing syndrome
  • Thyroid dysfunction
  • Coarctation of aorta

Proper BP Measurement
  • Seated quietly for ≥5 minutes
  • Feet flat on floor, arm at heart level
  • No caffeine/smoking 30 min prior
  • Correct cuff size (bladder ≥80% arm circumference)
  • Check both arms at first visit
  • Average of ≥2 readings on ≥2 occasions
  • Confirm with ABPM or HBPM
