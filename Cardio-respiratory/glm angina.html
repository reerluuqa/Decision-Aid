Definition & Assessment

Stable Angina

Chest pain/discomfort (constricting) in chest, neck, shoulders, jaw, or arms:

  • Precipitated by physical exertion/emotional stress
  • Relieved by rest or GTN within ~5 minutes
  • Lasts ≤10 minutes (usually less)

Assess Typicality

  • Typical: All 3 features above
  • Atypical: 2 features above (+/- GI discomfort, breathlessness, nausea)
  • Non-anginal: None/minimal features

Diagnostic Pathway

Diagnostic Algorithm

Follow these steps to confirm or exclude stable angina

1

ECG

Perform resting 12-lead ECG. Abnormal ECG increases likelihood but doesn't confirm diagnosis.

2

Refer

If typical/atypical symptoms → specialist chest pain service

3

Avoid

Do NOT use exercise ECG to diagnose/exclude stable angina without known CAD

Management

While Awaiting Diagnosis

  • GTN sublingual: For symptom relief
  • Aspirin 75mg daily: If pain likely to be stable angina
  • Safety netting: Instruct to call 999 if pain not relieved after 2 doses of GTN (5 mins apart)

Initial Treatment

  • Symptom relief: Short-acting nitrate (GTN sublingual)
  • First-line: Beta-blocker OR Calcium-channel blocker
  • Secondary prevention: Aspirin, Statin, ACE inhibitor if indicated

Ongoing Management

  • Routine review: Monitor symptom control, medication adherence, side effects
  • Annual review: Full cardiovascular risk assessment
  • Poor control: Optimize first-line, add second-line, consider combination therapy

When to Refer

Referral Indications

  • Diagnostic uncertainty
  • Poor symptom control despite optimal medical therapy
  • Recent deterioration in symptom frequency/severity
  • For consideration of revascularization

Patient Advice

GTN Use

Before activities that trigger angina

Action Plan

What to do if symptoms worsen (call 999 if not relieved by GTN)

Lifestyle

Smoking cessation, healthy diet, regular physical activity

Driving

Notify DVLA if symptoms affect driving ability

Work

Most can continue; may need modifications

Sexual Activity

Usually safe; may use GTN prophylactically

Red Flags

  • Pain at rest
  • Increasing frequency/severity
  • Pain not relieved by GTN
  • Associated syncope, sweating, or nausea
