Initial Assessment

Rapid evaluation of aspiration risk

üö® High Risk Indicators: Urgent Referral
  1. ABC Assessment
    • Airway: Check for obstruction, secretions
    • Breathing: Respiratory rate, oxygen saturation
    • Circulation: Blood pressure, heart rate
  2. Conscious Level GCS < 9
    • Glasgow Coma Scale assessment
    • Protective airway reflexes
  3. Risk Factor Identification
    • Recent anesthesia/sedation
    • Neurological conditions (stroke, Parkinson's)
    • Gastrointestinal disorders
    • Advanced age (>70 years)
  4. Clinical Signs Caution
    • Coughing/choking episodes
    • Wheezing or crackles
    • Tachypnea or dyspnea
    • Fever or hypoxia

Diagnostic Workup

Confirm aspiration and assess severity

‚ñº
üí° Key Point: Maintain high index of suspicion in at-risk patients
  1. Imaging Studies Chest X-ray within 1 hour
    • Chest X-ray: Look for bilateral infiltrates
    • Right lung more commonly affected
    • CT chest if symptoms persist
  2. Laboratory Tests
    • Complete blood count
    • Arterial blood gas
    • Procalcitonin/CRP for infection
  3. Bronchoscopy If severe or barium aspiration
    • Remove particulate matter
    • Bronchoalveolar lavage for cultures
  4. Differential Diagnosis
    • Community-acquired pneumonia
    • ARDS
    • Heart failure
    • Pulmonary embolism

    • Nasogastric tube insertion

    • Positive pressure ventilation if needed
  3. Antibiotic Considerations
    Gastric contents are typically sterile - avoid routine antibiotics
    • Consider if: mechanical ventilation, gastroparesis, small bowel obstruction
    • Start if no improvement after 48 hours
    • Culture before starting antibiotics
  4. Special Considerations
    • Barium aspiration: Early bronchoscopy
    • GLP-1 agonists: Delayed gastric emptying risk
    • Pregnancy: Higher aspiration risk

Follow-up & Prevention

Monitoring and preventive strategies

‚ñº
üìä Monitoring: Observe for 48 hours minimum
  1. Monitoring Parameters Daily assessment
    • Respiratory status
    • Temperature trends
    • Oxygen requirements
    • Chest X-ray evolution
  2. Complication Watch High vigilance
    • Aspiration pneumonia (25% risk)
    • ARDS (12% mortality if develops)
    • Lung abscess/empyema
    • Barium fibrosis (rare)
  3. Prevention Strategies
    • Dysphagia screening in high-risk patients
    • Speech therapy assessment
    • Feeding modifications
    • Positioning during meals
  4. Patient Education
    • Recognition of aspiration signs
    • Feeding technique training
    • When to seek medical attention
    • Family/caregiver education
