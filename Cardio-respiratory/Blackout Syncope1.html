Blackouts & Syncope – Quick-Aide

Last updated: November 2023 | NHS CKS condensed guide

Immediate hospital referral if any red flag present

1. Rapid Room Assessment (2–3 min)

ActionCut-off / Target
VitalsPulse, BP lying + ≥1 min standing (OH: ↓≥20/10 mmHg)
ECGAll patients – arrhythmia, QT, Brugada, HCM
GlucoseHypoglycaemia screen
History3 Ps (Posture, Provoking factors, Prodrome)
ExamMurmur, focal neuro, bleeding signs

2. Likely Cause – Decision Tree

├─ Vasovagal / Situational → reassurance, trigger avoidance, 2 L fluids, counter-pressure ├─ Orthostatic Hypotension → add salt 6–10 g, compression stockings, review meds ├─ Suspected Cardiac → URGENT cardiology ├─ Carotid Sinus Syndrome → falls/syncope service ├─ Epileptic features → URGENT neurology └─ Uncertain / recurrent → cardiology ± tilt / ambulatory ECG

3. Instant Patient Advice

  • DVLA: cease driving until specialist review
  • Occupational: refer to OH if high-risk job
  • Info: STARS PoTS UK
