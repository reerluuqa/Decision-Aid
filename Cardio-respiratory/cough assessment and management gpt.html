Diagnosis: duration-based differential + “must-not-miss” features

All coughs start acute; recurrent “acute” can be undiagnosed chronic disease.
Pathway
Acute (<3 weeks)
Common first
URTI (most common)
Cough ± sputum, malaise/fever; nasal/ear/throat/sinus symptoms; typically viral.
Consider other acute causes
COVID-19; acute bronchitis; pneumonia; exacerbation of asthma/COPD/bronchiectasis; PE; pneumothorax.
Pneumothorax / tension pneumothorax features
Sudden pleuritic pain, breathlessness, reduced movement/breath sounds; tension: tracheal deviation, hypotension, collapse.
Pulmonary embolism features
Acute breathlessness, pleuritic pain, haemoptysis, crackles, sinus tachycardia.
Sub-acute (3–8 weeks)
Often post-infectious
Post-infectious cough (commonest)
Persistent dry cough after obvious RTI; systemically well; normal respiratory exam.

Management: acute → sub-acute → chronic (targeted, sequential trials)

Emphasis: self-care + safety-net for uncomplicated acute cough; targeted therapy for likely cause in persistent cough.
Actionable
Acute cough: URTI phenotype (common pathway)
Default
Expected course + self-care
Advise acute cough often persists up to ~3–4 weeks. Symptom control: paracetamol/ibuprofen for pain; some may trial honey, pelargonium, OTC expectorant (guaifenesin) or cough suppressants.
Safety-net
Seek review if rapid/significant worsening, no improvement by 3–4 weeks, or systemically very unwell.
Avoid bronchodilator/ICS unless underlying airways disease
Do not offer oral/inhaled bronchodilator (e.g., salbutamol) or oral/inhaled corticosteroid solely for uncomplicated acute cough.
Avoid mucolytics
Do not offer acetylcysteine/carbocisteine for uncomplicated acute cough.
Antibiotics: reserve for systemically very unwell or higher-risk
No routine antibiotics if not very unwell/not higher risk; give rationale. If very unwell: consider immediate antibiotic (doxycycline 200 mg day 1 then 100 mg daily ×4 days; alternatives: amoxicillin 500 mg TDS ×5 days; clarithromycin 250–500 mg BD ×5 days; erythromycin ×5 days).
Higher-risk: consider backup prescription
Explain: not needed immediately; use if worsening rapidly/significantly at any time.
Reassess triggers (acute cough)
Re-check
If worsening rapidly/significantly
Reconsider differential; look for serious illness/cardiorespiratory failure/sepsis; consider resistant pathogens if prior antibiotics.
Sub-acute or chronic cough: targeted therapy
Sequential trials
Post-infectious (3–8w): usually self-limiting
Advise often resolves by ≤8w; re-attend if not improving by ~2 months. Consider trial: inhaled ipratropium; if QoL affected and persists: consider inhaled corticosteroid; severe paroxysms (after ruling out other causes): short finite oral prednisolone 30–40 mg daily. Centrally acting antitussive (codeine/dextromethorphan) if other options fail.
ACE inhibitor cough
Stop ACE inhibitor and switch to alternative; cough usually resolves within ~1 month (can persist longer).
Eosinophilic bronchitis
Inhaled corticosteroid first-line; if no improvement: increase ICS dose; consider leukotriene inhibitor (e.g., montelukast) after reconsidering diagnosis.
Upper airway cough syndrome
Antihistamine (e.g., chlorphenamine) + decongestant (e.g., pseudoephedrine) + avoid triggers; improvement typically within 1–2 weeks, but resolution can take weeks–months. Treat coexisting sinusitis/rhinitis appropriately (incl intranasal steroids/antibiotic where indicated).
Cough-variant asthma
Start ICS; if no improvement: reconsider; then increase ICS dose and consider leukotriene inhibitor or beta-agonist in combination.
Smoking-related cough
Advise cessation; warn transient increase in cough can occur.
Non-response / partial response / uncertain diagnosis
Refer to respiratory physician for assessment. Refractory chronic cough options may include low-dose morphine (5–10 mg BD), gabapentin, and speech & language intervention (specialist pathways).
Antibiotic counselling Time saver
When prescribing: counsel adverse effects (esp diarrhoea/nausea) and clear “when to use” instructions if backup prescription.
