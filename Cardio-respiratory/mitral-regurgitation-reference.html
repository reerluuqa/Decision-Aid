Acute MR - Key Features
  • Sudden onset of severe symptoms
  • Marked increase in congestive HF symptoms
  • Weakness, fatigue, severe dyspnea
  • May progress to respiratory failure & shock
  • Peripheral vasoconstriction, pallor, diaphoresis
  • ‚ö†Ô∏è May have NO murmur
Chronic MR - Symptoms
  • Dyspnea on exertion (most common)
  • Decreased exercise tolerance
  • Fatigue and weakness
  • Palpitations (often AFib)
  • Orthopnea & PND (if decompensated)
  • Lower extremity edema
Physical Exam Findings
  • Holosystolic blowing murmur at apex
  • Radiates to axilla - key diagnostic
  • Laterally displaced apical impulse (LV dilation)
  • Diminished S1 heart sound
  • Palpable P2 (if pulmonary hypertension)
  • S3 may be present (LV dysfunction)

NYHA Functional Classification

I
No symptoms. No limitation in ordinary physical activity.
II
Mild symptoms. Slight limitation during ordinary activity.
III
Marked limitation. Comfortable only at rest. Less than ordinary activity causes symptoms.
IV
Severe limitations. Symptoms at rest. Mostly bed-bound patients.

Diagnostic Approach

First-Line: Transthoracic Echo (TTE)
  • Definitive test for MR presence & severity
  • Assesses mechanism (flail, prolapse, restriction)
  • Evaluates LV size & function
  • Measures LA size & other valvular abnormalities
  • Calculates right ventricular systolic pressure
  • LV global longitudinal strain: earlier LV dysfunction detection

Additional Tests
  • TEE: When TTE inconclusive or surgical planning
  • 3D TEE: Surgical view for heart team discussion
  • Stress Echo: Assess exercise hemodynamics
  • ECG: Screen for arrhythmia, AFib, prior MI
  • Cardiac Cath: If non-invasive inconclusive or CAD assessment
  • CMR: Poor LV/RV function, myocardial fibrosis assessment

Risk Factors & Etiology

Primary MR (Degenerative - Valve Issue)
Mitral Valve Prolapse
Myxomatous degeneration, redundant tissue
Most Common
Rheumatic Heart Disease
Leaflet thickening, fibrosis, restriction
Infective Endocarditis
Leaflet/chordal disruption, flail, perforations
Congenital Abnormalities
Anterior mitral valve cleft, etc.
Cardiac Trauma
Sharp or blunt trauma to valve apparatus
Drug-Induced
Ergotamine, pergolide, cabergoline
Secondary MR (Functional - Ventricular Issue)
Ischemic Heart Disease
Post-MI remodeling, papillary muscle displacement
Most Common
Non-Ischemic Cardiomyopathy
LV dilation, annular dilatation
Hypertrophic Cardiomyopathy
Systolic anterior motion (SAM)
Persistent Hypertension
Long-standing elevated SBP
