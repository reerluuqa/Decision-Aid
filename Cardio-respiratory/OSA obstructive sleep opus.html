RED FLAGS - Urgent Referral

Refer to sleep clinic within 4 WEEKS if:

  • Professional driver or safety-critical worker with excessive sleepiness
  • COPD, poorly controlled arrhythmia, nocturnal angina
  • Heart failure or treatment-resistant hypertension
  • Pulmonary hypertension or respiratory failure
  • Pregnant patient
  • Pre-operative assessment for major surgery
  • Non-arteritic anterior ischaemic optic neuropathy

Routine Referral Criteria

Adults - Sleep Clinic:

  • Suspected moderate/severe OSAS
  • Suspected mild OSAS affecting quality of life

Children - Refer to:

  • ENT: Adenotonsillar hypertrophy with regular snoring
  • Paediatrician: Developmental disorder, obesity, or associated conditions

When to Suspect OSAS

SUSPECT in Adults with:

  • Excessive daytime sleepiness + snoring + fatigue
  • Witnessed apnoeas, gasping, or choking at night
  • Unrefreshing sleep, impaired concentration

SUSPECT in Children with:

  • Snoring + witnessed apnoeas
  • Restless sleep, unusual sleep posture
  • Behavioural problems, reduced concentration
  • Nocturnal enuresis

üè• Specialist Treatments (Overview) ‚ñº

Adults:

CPAP Therapy (1st line):
For all symptomatic OSAS
First-line for moderate-severe OSAS
Improves sleepiness, QoL, may lower BP
Long-term treatment required
Mandibular Advancement Devices:
For mild OSAS with normal alertness
Alternative if CPAP not tolerated
Custom-made titratable devices most effective
Surgery:
Rarely considered
If nasopharyngeal obstruction evident
After other treatments failed

Children:

Adenotonsillectomy:
First-line for adenotonsillar hypertrophy
Usually curative in children
Resolves symptoms in most cases
CPAP Therapy:
If surgery contraindicated
If surgery unsuccessful
