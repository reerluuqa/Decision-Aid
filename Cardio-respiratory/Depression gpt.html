Primary Care Depression – One‑Page Algorithm

Adults ≥18y · NICE CKS aligned · Fast decision support for consultations

Step 1
Identify & Diagnose
Screen → Assess → Risk
Screen (2Q): In the last month, felt down/depressed/hopeless? · Little interest/pleasure?
Confirm episode: ≥2 weeks, most days; assess functional impact & symptom cluster (sleep, appetite/weight, fatigue, psychomotor change, concentration, guilt/worthlessness, suicidality).
Use tools: PHQ‑9 (less severe <16 · more severe ≥16), HADS, BDI‑II. Track baseline.
Consider differentials/co‑morbidities: anxiety, bipolar, psychosis, substance misuse, bereavement, neurological/medical (e.g., anaemia, hypothyroid, OSA).
Risk & safeguarding: ask about thoughts/plans/intent for self‑harm/suicide; evaluate supports; act per local procedures.
Step 2
Initial Management
Psycho‑ed · Lifestyle · Choices
  • Education & support: explain condition, course, recovery; share reputable resources (RCPsych, MIND, Samaritans, NHS Gambling support).
  • Lifestyle: outdoor activity, sleep hygiene, healthy diet, alcohol moderation.
  • Less severe & declines treatment: Active monitoring + review in 2–4 weeks; provide help‑seeking/crisis info.
  • Less severe & wants treatment: offer guided self‑help first‑line; SSRIs not routine first‑line but may be offered if preferred.
  • More severe: any first‑line option per preference & access (individual CBT, behavioural activation, antidepressant, counselling, guided self‑help, group exercise).
  • Urgent referral now: high suicide/self‑harm risk, psychosis, severe self‑neglect, or risk to others (consider CRHT; MHA if required).
Step 3
If Starting Antidepressants
SSRI first‑line

Choice

SSRIs preferred (e.g., sertraline/citalopram). In chronic physical illness, favour low‑interaction SSRIs.

Safety

Avoid TCAs (except lofepramine) & venlafaxine if high overdose risk. Limit supply if necessary.

Counsel

Early anxiety/suicidality can transiently ↑; onset ≈4 wks; continue ≥6 mo post‑remission; withdrawal possible; check driving ability.

Review

1 wk if 18–25y or suicide risk; else 2 wks; again ≤4 wks. Track PHQ‑9; ensure crisis plan.

Step 4
Ongoing Management & Relapse Prevention
Review → Adjust → Prevent
  • At each review: symptoms, function (work/relationships/caring), adherence, adverse effects, risks, comorbidities, psychosocial factors; support carers.
  • No/limited improvement at 4–6 wks: re‑check adherence/AE/diagnosis; address modifiable risks; consider switch, dose change, add psych intervention, or combine therapy.
  • Relapse prevention: continue effective treatment ≥6–12 months (longer if recurrent/high risk). Consider CBT/MBCT in remission.
Step 5
Stopping & Switching
Taper smart
  • Do not stop abruptly (except urgent medical need). Taper over weeks–months; longer for higher doses/long‑term use.
  • Higher withdrawal risk: paroxetine, venlafaxine. Consider micro‑tapers; reinstate previous dose if severe withdrawal, then taper slower.
  • Switching: several SSRI↔SNRI switches allow direct switch; many other pairs require cautious cross‑taper. Beware fluoxetine interactions up to 5 wks after stopping.
  • Faster taper only if serious AEs (e.g., hyponatraemia, arrhythmia, GI bleed) or switching is urgent.
Step 6
Referral
Know the triggers

Urgent

Suicide/self‑harm risk, psychosis, severe self‑neglect, risk to others (CRHT/MHA considerations).

Routine

Poor response to adequate trials, chronic depression with impairment, complex comorbidity, diagnostic uncertainty.

Scope: adults ≥18; aligns with NICE CKS Depression (Last revised Apr 2025). Excludes perinatal care, psychotic depression management with antipsychotics/ECT, and other primary psychotic disorders.

PHQ‑9 guide

Less severe: <16 · More severe: ≥16. Track change from baseline.

Co‑prescribing notes
  • NSAIDs/aspirin: avoid SSRIs/SNRIs if possible (↑GI bleed). Consider mirtazapine/trazodone ± gastroprotection.
  • Anticoagulants (warfarin/heparin): avoid SSRIs/SNRIs (↑bleed). Consider mirtazapine/trazodone.
  • Anti‑epileptics: all ↓seizure threshold—seek advice; avoid TCAs where possible.
  • Triptans/MAO‑B inhibitors: avoid SSRIs; consider mirtazapine/trazodone/reboxetine.
  • Flecainide/propafenone: prefer sertraline; avoid citalopram/escitalopram.
