Blackouts & Syncope

Clinical Algorithm for Fast Decision Making
Updated: Nov 2023

Initial Assessment

  • ABCs first - ensure stability
  • Vitals: HR, BP (lying & standing after 1 min), RR, O₂ sat, temp
  • Check glucose - rule out hypo/hyperglycemia
  • 12-lead ECG - for all patients

Red Flags (Immediate Referral)

  • No full recovery of consciousness
  • Syncope during exertion or supine position
  • Palpitations immediately before syncope
  • New/unexplained breathlessness
  • Heart murmur on examination
  • Family history of sudden unexplained death <40 years
  • ECG abnormalities: Bradycardia, AV block, VT/SVT, Long/short QT, Brugada, WPW

Key History Questions

  • Circumstances: Posture before event, triggers (prolonged standing, pain, head movement)
  • Prodrome: Sweating, nausea, warmth (vasovagal); déjà vu (epilepsy)
  • Event details: Eye position (open = epilepsy; closed = psychogenic), Movements (brief myoclonus = vasovagal; prolonged = epilepsy)
  • Tongue biting: Lateral (epilepsy); Tip (vasovagal)
  • Incontinence: Suggests epilepsy
  • Duration: <30 sec (syncope); >1 min (epilepsy); >5 min (psychogenic)
  • Recovery: Confusion/amnesia (epilepsy); One-sided weakness (TIA)

Differential Diagnosis

Syncope Causes

  • Vasovagal: 3 P's - Posture (standing), Provoking factors (pain, fear), Progressive prodrome
  • Situational: During/after micturition, defecation, coughing, swallowing
  • Carotid sinus: Head rotation/pressure triggers; age >40
  • Orthostatic hypotension: BP drop ≥20 mmHg systolic or ≥10 mmHg diastolic
  • Cardiac: Exertional, sudden onset, structural heart disease

Non-Syncope Causes

  • Epilepsy: Tongue biting, prolonged movements, post-ictal confusion
  • Psychogenic: Prolonged episodes, eye closure, no injury, frequent recurrence
  • Falls: No loss of consciousness/amnesia
  • Metabolic: Hypoglycemia, hypoxia
  • TIA/Stroke: Focal neurological signs

Management

  • Vasovagal/Situational: Reassurance, trigger avoidance, physical counterpressure maneuvers
  • Orthostatic hypotension: Review medications, increase fluid/salt intake, compression stockings, consider fludrocortisone
  • Carotid sinus: Avoid tight collars; consider pacemaker if recurrent
  • Cardiac: Urgent cardiology referral

Driving Advice

  • Do not drive until specialist assessment completed
  • Inform DVLA if required (depending on cause)

When to Refer

  • Urgent (within 24h): Any red flag signs/symptoms
  • Routine: Recurrent syncope without clear cause, suspected orthostatic hypotension not responding to initial measures
