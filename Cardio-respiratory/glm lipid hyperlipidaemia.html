Lipid Modification for CVD Prevention

Quick Reference Algorithm for Clinical Decision Making

Primary Prevention

Who to treat:

  • QRISK3 ≥10% (including type 2 diabetes)
  • Type 1 diabetes if age >40 years, diabetes >10 years, nephropathy, or other CVD risk factors
  • CKD (eGFR <60 ml/min/1.73m² and/or albuminuria)
  • Familial hypercholesterolemia
  • Consider for: Age ≥85 years OR age 18-40 with type 1 diabetes

First-line treatment:

Atorvastatin 20mg

(high-intensity statin)

Target:

>40% reduction in non-HDL cholesterol

Secondary Prevention

Who to treat:

  • All patients with established CVD

First-line treatment:

Atorvastatin 80mg

(high-intensity statin)

Lower dose if: adverse effects, increased risk, or patient preference

Target:

LDL cholesterol ≤2.0 mmol/L
OR non-HDL cholesterol ≤2.6 mmol/L

Monitoring

Baseline Tests

  • Full lipid profile (non-fasting)
  • Liver transaminases (ALT/AST)
  • Renal function
  • Diabetes status

Follow-up

  • Repeat lipid profile and liver transaminases at 2-3 months
  • Ongoing monitoring as needed

Special Considerations

HIV patients

All patients with HIV aged ≥40 years should be offered statin treatment regardless of lipid profile or CVD risk

Women of childbearing potential

  • Contraception required during treatment
  • Stop statins 3 months before conception and during pregnancy
  • Do not restart until breastfeeding ends

Important interactions

  • Grapefruit juice
  • Certain medications (check patient information leaflet)

When to refer

  • Total cholesterol >9.0 mmol/L or non-HDL >7.5 mmol/L
  • Triglycerides >20 mmol/L (urgent)
  • Triglycerides 10-20 mmol/L (repeat fasting, refer if still >10 mmol/L)
  • Familial hypercholesterolemia suspected

Note: Always discuss benefits and risks with patient before initiating treatment. Consider lifestyle modifications alongside pharmacological therapy.
