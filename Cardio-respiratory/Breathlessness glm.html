Red Flags - Consider Emergency Admission
Severe respiratory distress

Unable to complete sentences, respiratory rate >25/min

Hemodynamic instability

Hypotension (SBP <90 mmHg), tachycardia >130/min

Hypoxaemia

Oxygen saturation <92% on room air

Altered consciousness

Confusion, drowsiness, or agitation

Silent chest

Absent breath sounds in severe asthma

Chest pain

Suggestive of acute coronary syndrome or PE

Immediate Actions
Emergency Services

Call 999 if life-threatening features present

Oxygen Therapy

Administer if SpO2 <92% (target 94-98%)

Medications

Consider nebulised bronchodilators, nitrates, diuretics

Positioning

Sit upright if possible; may relieve symptoms

Cardiac Causes
  • Acute pulmonary oedema

    Risk factors: Chronic heart failure, ischaemic heart disease

    Symptoms: Severe breathlessness, orthopnoea, cough with frothy sputum

    Signs: Elevated JVP, crackles, peripheral oedema

  • Cardiac arrhythmia

    Risk factors: Heart failure, valvular disease, coronary artery disease

    Symptoms: Palpitations, breathlessness, chest pain, syncope

    Signs: Bradycardia or tachycardia

  • Chronic heart failure

    Risk factors: Advanced age, hypertension, coronary heart disease

    Symptoms: Fatigue, breathlessness, orthopnoea, reduced exercise tolerance

    Signs: Oedema, basal crepitations, displaced apical pulse

  • Myocardial infarction

    Risk factors: Coronary artery disease, smoking, diabetes

    Symptoms: Breathlessness, chest discomfort, malaise

    Signs: Abnormal pulse, sweating, hypotension

Pulmonary Causes
  • Asthma

    Risk factors: Personal/family history of atopy

    Symptoms: Wheeze, chest tightness, cough, variable symptoms

    Signs: Increased respiratory rate, wheeze, reduced PEFR

  • COPD

    Risk factors: Age >35, smoking history

    Symptoms: Progressive exertional breathlessness, productive cough

    Signs: Wheeze, hyperinflated chest, purse lip breathing

  • Pneumonia

    Risk factors: Advanced age, comorbidities

    Symptoms: Cough, fever, pleuritic pain

    Signs: Focal chest signs, systemic features

  • Pulmonary embolism

    Risk factors: Immobilization, surgery, cancer, DVT

    Symptoms: Acute-onset breathlessness, pleuritic chest pain

    Signs: Tachycardia, tachypnoea, hypoxaemia

Other Causes
  • Anaemia

    Symptoms: Fatigue, light-headedness, exertional breathlessness

    Signs: Pallor, tachycardia, signs of cardiac failure

  • Anxiety-related

    History: Anxiety, panic, phobia

    Symptoms: Paraesthesia, dizziness, chest discomfort

    Signs: Hyperventilation, tachycardia, no physical cause

  • Diaphragmatic splinting

    Causes: Ascites, obesity, pregnancy

    Symptoms: Chronic breathlessness with increasing abdominal size

    Signs: Ascites, obesity, signs of liver disease

  • Metabolic causes

    Examples: Diabetic ketoacidosis, renal failure, thyroid disease

    Symptoms: Vary by underlying condition

    Signs: Specific to underlying metabolic disorder

Recommended Investigations
Chest X-ray
Look for heart failure, pulmonary pathology, pneumothorax
Urgent if lung cancer suspected (within 2 weeks)
ECG
Identify arrhythmias, ischaemia, signs of heart failure
BNP/NT-proBNP
Assess for heart failure (may be elevated in other conditions)
Full Blood Count
Check for anaemia, evidence of infection
PEFR/Spirometry
Assess for asthma or obstructive airway disease
Pulse Oximetry
Detect hypoxaemia in various respiratory conditions
C-reactive Protein
Check for infection and inflammatory conditions
Kidney/Liver Function
Exclude renal impairment, liver failure
COVID-19 Considerations

Key Symptoms

  • New continuous cough
  • Fever or high temperature
  • Loss of smell or taste
  • Shortness of breath
  • Fatigue

Management Actions

  • Use appropriate PPE during assessment
  • Consider remote assessment where possible
  • Arrange testing if COVID-19 suspected
  • Advise self-isolation if indicated
  • Refer for emergency care if severe symptoms
