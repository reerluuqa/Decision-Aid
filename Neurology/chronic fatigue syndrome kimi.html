ME/CFS Quick-Scan

Red Flags – Immediate Action

  • Significant unintentional weight loss ≥5 % in 6–12 mo
  • Fever, night sweats, persistent ↑ inflammatory markers
  • Focal neurological signs or rapidly progressive symptoms
  • New-onset syncope or severe orthostatic hypotension
  • Unable to swallow / severe dysphagia
Emergency referral / exclude malignancy, infection, neurological cause

Suspect ME/CFS – Refer

  • 6 weeks debilitating fatigue + post-exertional malaise and
  • Unrefreshing sleep & cognitive difficulty ("brain fog")
  • Function ↓ compared to pre-illness
  • No other explanation
Direct referral to ME/CFS specialist team

Diagnostic Work-up

Baseline labs Purpose
FBC, ferritin Anaemia / malignancy
ESR/CRP Inflammation / infection
Thyroid, HbA1c Endocrine / metabolic
Renal & liver profile Organ dysfunction
Coeliac screen Mucosal disease

First-Line Advice (Start Today)

  • Energy envelope: do not "push through"
  • Planned rest periods before symptoms spike
  • Small, varied activity blocks; avoid fixed increments
  • Normal sleep hygiene; balance night sleep vs day rest
  • Balanced diet + adequate fluids
Provide written pacing sheet / activity diary

Annual Primary-Care Review

  • Symptom pattern, triggers, severity
  • Energy plan adherence & changes
  • Pain, sleep, mood, nutrition, mobility
  • Social supports, work/education needs
Update care plan; liaise with specialist team

Severe / Very Severe – Special Considerations

  • Low-stimulus environment (dark, quiet)
  • House-/bed-bound; ADL assistance required
  • Risk: pressure ulcers, DVT, malnutrition
  • Drug sensitivities – start low, go slow
Involve physio/OT, dietitian, CBT (adapted pace)

Address Comorbidities

Issue Action
Orthostatic intolerance / PoTS ↑ fluids, compression, refer cardiology
Pain (migratory, neuropathic) Analgesia ladder, pain team
Sleep disorder Sleep hygiene, exclude apnoea
Depression / anxiety CBT, liaison psychiatry
Vitamin D deficiency Replace if house-/bed-bound

Flare-Up vs Relapse

  • Flare: transient worsening (days); ↓ activity to baseline
  • Relapse: sustained escalation (>days-weeks); re-establish new lower baseline
Re-assess energy limit; contact specialist team if prolonged
