Erythema Migrans = Clinical Diagnosis

NO LAB TESTING REQUIRED ‚Äî Treat immediately based on clinical findings
  • Expanding erythema with defined edge
  • Usually >5cm diameter (typically ~15cm)
  • Appears 1-36 days post-bite
  • May have central clearing ("bulls-eye")
  • NOT usually itchy, hot, or painful
  • Lasts weeks untreated
?

Consider Lyme Disease If Multiple Of:

  • Fatigue
  • Fever and sweats
  • Headache / Neck pain or stiffness
  • Migratory joint or muscle aches
  • Cognitive impairment (memory, concentration)
  • Paraesthesia
  • Swollen glands
  • Malaise

Jarisch-Herxheimer Reaction (~15% of patients)

What is it? Worsening of fever, chills, muscle pain, headache within 1-24 hours of starting antibiotics

Cause: Cytokine release from bacterial death

Action:

  • Self-limiting (24-48 hours)
  • Continue antibiotics if no true allergy
  • Symptomatic treatment as needed

Tick Removal - Do It Right

1
Fine-tipped tweezers or tick tool
2
Grasp close to skin
3
Pull up slowly & firmly - no twisting
4
Clean with soap & water
Advise: Monitor site for several weeks. Seek help if rash or flu-like symptoms develop.

DO NOT

  • Give prophylactic antibiotics routinely
  • Diagnose Lyme in asymptomatic people (even after tick bite)
  • Rule out Lyme just because no tick exposure recalled
üõ°Ô∏è

Prevention Advice

  • Stick to paths, avoid long grass
  • Cover skin - long sleeves, tuck trousers into socks
  • Light-colored clothing (easier to spot ticks)
  • Use DEET-containing repellent
  • Check skin frequently, especially:
    • Skin folds, waistband, under breasts
    • Children: hairline, scalp, neck
  • Check pets for ticks

What is Lyme Disease?

  • Caused by Borrelia burgdorferi sensu lato spirochetes
  • Transmitted by Ixodes tick bite
  • Transmission usually requires ‚â•36-48 hours attachment
  • Europe: B. afzelii, B. garinii predominant
  • North America: B. burgdorferi sensu stricto
üìä

UK Epidemiology

  • Estimated 2,000-3,000 cases/year (England & Wales)
  • ~4% of UK ticks infected
  • Peak: late spring to early summer
  • All ages affected; peaks 45-64 years
  • ~15% acquired abroad
üìà

Prognosis

Most people recover completely with appropriate treatment
  • >90% complete response for erythema migrans
  • Reinfection possible (no lifelong immunity)
  • Relapse after appropriate treatment not reported

Early disseminated		 Weeks-months: multiple EM, arthritis, carditis, neuro

‚ö° Complications

With Appropriate Treatment

Long-term complications are uncommon. Some may have residual symptoms:

  • Incomplete recovery from severe neuro symptoms (if treated late)
  • Residual paraesthesias, facial paresis
  • Tissue damage from ACA

Post-Treatment Lyme Disease Syndrome

  • Persisting fatigue, pain, cognitive difficulties
  • Investigation usually negative
  • No evidence prolonged antibiotics help
  • Symptoms usually improve over time
