Headache Assessment

Clinical Reference Algorithm for Fast Decision Making

Scope

  • Assessment of adults (≥16 years) with undiagnosed headache
  • Diagnostic criteria for primary headache disorders & medication overuse headache
  • Identifies red flags suggesting serious secondary headache requiring referral

Assessment Approach

History

  • Onset, duration, frequency, pattern
  • Pain: location, severity, quality
  • Associated symptoms:
    • Aura (≤60 min) → Migraine
    • Nausea/vomiting, photophobia → Migraine
    • Autonomic features → Cluster headache
  • Systemic symptoms (fever, neurological deficit)
  • Triggers: trauma, posture, Valsalva, stress
  • Medication use (days/month)
  • Impact on daily activities

Examination

  • Vital signs (BP, pulse, temperature)
  • General appearance (rash, consciousness)
  • Extracranial structures (temporal arteries, TMJ, sinuses)
  • Neck examination (meningeal signs, movement)
  • Fundoscopy (papilloedema, pupils)
  • Neurological examination

Red Flags - Urgent Referral Needed

Red Flag Potential Cause
Sudden-onset severe ("thunderclap") headache Subarachnoid haemorrhage, intracerebral haemorrhage
New-onset headache in person >50 years Temporal arteritis, space-occupying lesion
Progressive/worsening headache Space-occupying lesion, subdural haematoma
Fever + neck stiffness + photophobia Meningitis/encephalitis
Papilloedema Space-occupying lesion, venous sinus thrombosis
New focal neurological deficit Stroke/TIA, malignancy
Atypical aura (>60 min, motor weakness) Stroke/TIA
Headache triggered by Valsalva Chiari malformation, posterior fossa lesion
Headache worsening on lying down Space-occupying lesion
Immunocompromised patient CNS infection, malignancy
History of malignancy Cerebral metastases
Pregnancy-related Pre-eclampsia

Diagnostic Criteria for Common Headaches

Migraine without Aura

  • ≥5 attacks lasting 4-72 hours
  • ≥2 of: unilateral, pulsating, moderate/severe, aggravated by activity
  • ≥1 of: nausea/vomiting, photophobia, phonophobia

Migraine with Aura

  • ≥2 attacks with reversible aura symptoms
  • Aura develops over ≥5 min, lasts 5-60 min
  • Headache follows aura within 60 min

Tension-Type Headache

  • Episodes lasting 30 min-7 days
  • No nausea/vomiting
  • ≤1 of photophobia or phonophobia
  • ≥2 of: bilateral, pressing/tightening, mild/moderate, not aggravated by activity

Cluster Headache

  • ≥5 attacks of severe unilateral pain lasting 15-180 min
  • Associated with restlessness/agitation
  • ≥1 ipsilateral autonomic symptom
  • Frequency: 1-8 attacks/day

Medication Overuse Headache

  • Headache ≥15 days/month for ≥3 months
  • Regular overuse for >3 months of:
    • Ergotamines, triptans, opioids, combination analgesics (≥10 days/month)
    • Simple analgesics (≥15 days/month)

When to Refer

  • Any red flags present (urgency depends on clinical judgement)
  • Uncertain diagnosis after initial assessment
  • Atypical headache features
  • Suspected medication overuse headache
  • Refractory to initial management

Investigations

  • Generally not needed for primary headache
  • Consider headache diary if diagnosis uncertain
  • Neuroimaging only if red flags present or atypical features
