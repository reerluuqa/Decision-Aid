Epilepsy Reference

NICE CKS • Revised April 2025

Topiramate Safety (2024)

Contraindicated in pregnancy and women of childbearing potential unless Pregnancy Prevention Programme conditions are fulfilled.

Valproate Warning

High risk of malformations & neurodevelopmental impairments. Do not use in women/girls of childbearing age unless other options failed.

Reproductive Advice: Men (2024)

Men taking antiseizure meds (specifically valproate) and their partners should use effective contraception as a precaution.

Emergency Action

Convulsive Status Epilepticus:
> 5 minutes

Intervention needed after 5 mins. Long-term consequences possible after 30 mins.

  • Acute management of prolonged seizures required.
  • Do NOT cover acute management of provoked seizures (alcohol withdrawal, hypoglycaemia) in this tool.
  • Refer urgently if status epilepticus suspected.

Definition & Types

Epilepsy: A disorder of the brain characterized by an enduring predisposition to generate epileptic seizures.

Type Characteristics
Focal-onset Originate in one hemisphere/localized network. May retain or impair awareness.
Generalized-onset Bilaterally distributed networks. Includes motor and non-motor (absence) seizures.
Unknown Features do not allow classification.

Causes & Aetiology

Structural Genetic Infectious Metabolic Autoimmune

Cause identified in only ~30% of people. Includes stroke (up to 50% of identified cases in older adults), tumours, malformations, and complex genetic inheritance.

Risk Factors (SUDEP)

Potentially Modifiable Risks for Sudden Unexpected Death:

  • Non-adherence to meds
  • Alcohol/drug misuse
  • Focal to bilateral tonic-clonic seizures
  • Uncontrolled seizures
  • Frequent nocturnal seizures
  • Living alone
  • Sleeping alone without supervision

Referral & Diagnosis

  • First Seizure: Refer for confirmation and classification.
  • Urgency: Seen by specialist within 2 weeks of presentation.
  • Tertiary Referral: Seen within 4 weeks (or 2 weeks if urgent).
  • Women: Urgently refer for specialist advice if planning pregnancy or on antiseizure meds.

Management Goals Primary Care

  • Refer: Appropriately after first seizure.
  • Prescribe: Antiseizure meds to stop/reduce frequency (following specialist advice).
  • Monitor: Regular review in primary care.
  • Inform: Provide info on first aid, driving, safety, contraception.

Complications

Mental Health
Depression, anxiety, psychosis. Suicide risk increased.
Cognitive
Learning, memory, executive function issues.
Physical
Injuries (falls, drowning, burns), fractures.

Lifetime Risk of SUDEP

7–12%

Risk reduced with optimized seizure control.

NICE Quality Standards (Summary)

1. Rapid Access

First suspected seizure: Seen by epilepsy expert within 2 weeks.

2. Nursing Support

Access to an epilepsy specialist nurse.

3. Care Plan

Up-to-date, agreed comprehensive epilepsy care plan.

4. Holistic Review

Regular checks on memory, mental health, and social wellbeing.
