‚õî Critical Red Flags & Emergencies High Priority

Neuroleptic Malignant Syndrome

Trigger: Abrupt stop of dopaminergic drugs.

Signs: High fever, altered mental state, muscle rigidity, autonomic dysfunction. Life-threatening.

Severe Dysphagia & Aspiration

Risk: Silent aspiration -> Pneumonia (leading cause of death in late stage).

Signs: Coughing when drinking, weight loss, chest infections.

Impulse Control Disorders (ICDs)

Hidden Risk: Patients often conceal symptoms.

Behaviors: Pathological gambling, hypersexuality, compulsive shopping, binge eating, dopamine dysregulation syndrome.

Early Falls (Atypical)

Falls at diagnosis may indicate alternative parkinsonism (e.g., Progressive Supranuclear Palsy) rather than idiopathic PD.

üìã Clinical Complications & Presentation Quick Reference
Motor Complications Medication Related
  • Wearing off: Benefit fades before next dose (end-of-dose).
  • On-off phenomenon: Unpredictable rapid fluctuations.
  • Dyskinesia: Involuntary choreiform or dystonic movements.
  • Freezing of Gait: Inability to lift foot/start walking.
  • Deteriorating function: Immobility, quiet voice (dysphonia).
Mental Health Non-Motor
  • Depression: Affects up to 50%. Underdiagnosed due to overlapping symptoms (reduced expression, psychomotor slowing).
  • Anxiety: Panic attacks, OCD, GAD.
  • Dementia: Visuo-spatial impairment, hallucinations, delusions.
  • Psychosis: Visual hallucinations (up to 40% on dopaminergic drugs).
  • Apathy: Can occur without depression.
Autonomic Dysfunction Non-Motor
  • Orthostatic Hypotension: Drop in SBP ‚â•20mmHg. Causes dizziness, falls, syncope.
  • Constipation: Colonic dysmotility & anorectal dysfunction. Lewy body degeneration.
  • Bladder: Nocturia, urgency, frequency (detrusor overactivity).
  • Sexual: Erectile dysfunction (60-70%), hypersexuality induced by drugs.
  • Thermoregulation: Excessive sweating (off or on state).
Other Complications General
  • Pain: Musculoskeletal, dystonic, radicular, or central neuropathic pain.
  • Salivation: Sialorrhea (drooling) - usually reduced swallow freq, not excess saliva.
  • Sleep: REM sleep behavior disorder, daytime sleepiness, nocturnal akinesia.
  • Weight Loss: Unintended, due to dyskinesia energy cost or dysphagia.
  • Pressure Ulcers: Risk due to immobility in later stages.
Drug Safety & Interactions Caution
  • Avoid: Drugs that worsen parkinsonism (e.g., typical antipsychotics, anti-emetics like metoclopramide).
  • Domperidone + Apomorphine: Risk of QT prolongation. Assess cardiac risk & ECG.
  • MAO-B Inhibitors: Selegiline/Rasagiline generally do not interact with tyramine-rich foods (cheese reaction), contrary to older MAOIs.
  • Serotonin Syndrome Risk: Care with venlafaxine/duloxetine if taking MAO-B inhibitors.
Recent Updates (2023-2025) New
  • Inbrija: Licensed for intermittent treatment of OFF episodes.
  • Foslevodopa: New HTA for advanced PD motor symptoms.
  • Gambling Guidance: Updated NICE guidance on identification/management of gambling harms (2025).
  • Remote Monitoring: NICE guidance on devices for remote monitoring available.
