AIRWAY & VENTILATION ALERT: 20-30% of patients develop respiratory failure. Monitor spirometry every 6 hours initially.

ICU Admission Indications

  • Vital Capacity (VC): <20 mL/kg
  • Max Inspiratory Pressure (MIP): Worse than -30 cm H‚ÇÇO
  • Max Expiratory Pressure (MEP): <40 cm H‚ÇÇO
  • Rapid Progression: Quick deterioration of muscle strength

High Risk Clinical Signs

  • Bulbar Dysfunction: Facial weakness, dysphagia, slurred speech. (OR 17.5 for ventilation)
  • Bilateral Facial Weakness: Significant predictor of respiratory failure
  • Dysautonomia: Labile BP, sinus tachycardia, arrhythmias.
  • Inability to lift head: OR 5.0 for mechanical ventilation.

Monitoring Autonomic Dysfunction (Common)

Occurs in approx. 2/3 of patients. Can be life-threatening.

  • Cardiac: Sinus tachycardia, bradycardia, asystole (esp. during suctioning).
  • Hemodynamic: Hypertension, postural hypotension.
Action:
  • Continuous cardiac monitoring.
  • Alert for vagal stimulation during intubation/suctioning.
  • Treat hypertension only if severe (>180/120) or symptomatic.

Classic Presentation

  • Progressive weakness: Ascending (Lower > Upper, Proximal > Distal).
  • Timing: Reaches nadir in 2 weeks (80%) or 4 weeks (97%).
  • Areflexia: Flaccid paralysis with absent reflexes.
  • Pain: Back/leg pain (often misdiagnosed as disc herniation).
  • Sensory: Paresthesia (tingling) common, objective sensory loss often mild.

Investigations

  • Lumbar Puncture: Elevated protein, normal cells (<5/mm¬≥). Albuminocytological dissociation. May be normal in first 2 weeks.
  • Nerve Conduction Studies: Abnormal in most pts early. Look for demyelination (prolonged latencies, slowed conduction).
  • Spirometry: VC, MIP, MEP every 6h.
  • Anti-ganglioside Ab: If clinical atypical (e.g., GQ1b for Miller-Fisher).

Common Triggers (Hx)

2/3 Have antecedent infection (6 wks prior)
30% C. jejuni (Gastroenteritis)
CMV EBV, Mycoplasma, Zika, COVID-19
Rare Post-vaccination

Classification & Variants

  • AIDP: Most common (Western). Demyelinating.
  • AMAN: Axonal motor. Common in Asia/Summer. C. jejuni link. Rapid progression.
  • AMSAN: Axonal sensory & motor. Severe, poor recovery.
  • Miller-Fisher (MFS): Ophthalmoplegia, Ataxia, Areflexia. Anti-GQ1b+. Benign.

Disease Modifying Therapy

Start as soon as possible after diagnosis (within 2 weeks ideally).

Option 1:
IV Immunoglobulin (IVIG)
Dose: 2 g/kg divided over 5 days.
Option 2:
Plasma Exchange (PLEX)
Regimen: 5 exchanges over 1-2 weeks.

Combination therapy is generally not better than either alone.

Supportive Care

  • Respiratory: Monitor VC. Intubate if VC <20 mL/kg or bulbar weakness with high aspiration risk.
  • DVT Prophylaxis: Heparin/LMWH essential due to paralysis.
  • Pain Control: Neuropathic pain (gabapentin, carbamazepine) or opioids for severe back pain.
  • Rehab: Early physiotherapy to prevent contractures.

Prognosis & Recovery

Recovery:

Starts after plateau phase (days to weeks). Most recover completely.

Poor Prognosis Indicators:
  • Age > 60
  • Preceding C. jejuni diarrhea
  • AMAN/AMSAN subtypes
  • Low distal CMAP amplitude
Mortality:

Approx 3-7%. Usually due to complications (pulmonary embolism, sepsis, cardiac arrest).

Key Differentials

Fatigability, ocular involvement early, preserved reflexes, no sensory loss.
