Acute Motor Deficit Assessment

Interactive clinical algorithm for rapid assessment and management of acute motor deficits

Initial Assessment

Immediate Triage

URGENT: Sudden onset motor deficit requires immediate evaluation for stroke or other neurovascular emergencies
Time Critical Neuroprotective Measures

Primary Assessment Checklist

  • Onset time and progression pattern
  • Associated symptoms (headache, seizure, sensory changes)
  • Medical history and risk factors
  • Medication history (anticoagulants, immunosuppressants)
  • Recent trauma or infections
  • Family history of neurological conditions

Physical Examination

  • Complete neurological examination
  • Motor strength assessment (MRC scale)
  • Sensory evaluation and reflex testing
  • Cranial nerve assessment
  • Gait and coordination testing
  • Vital signs and general examination

Diagnostic Workup

Immediate Investigations

Time-sensitive: Complete initial workup within 60 minutes of presentation
  • Non-contrast CT head (rule out hemorrhage)
  • Blood glucose measurement
  • Complete blood count and coagulation studies
  • Electrolytes, renal and liver function
  • ECG and cardiac biomarkers

Secondary Investigations

MRI Brain EEG CSF Analysis
  • MRI with DWI (if available and indicated)
  • Carotid Doppler ultrasound
  • Echocardiogram (if embolic source suspected)
  • EEG (if seizure activity suspected)
  • Lumbar puncture (if infection suspected)
  • Electrophysiological studies (if peripheral cause)

Key Differential Diagnoses

Consider stroke mimics: seizures, migraines, functional disorders, metabolic derangements
  • Ischemic stroke (most common)
  • Intracerebral hemorrhage
  • Subarachnoid hemorrhage
  • Guillain-Barré syndrome
  • Multiple sclerosis
  • Myasthenia gravis

Treatment Protocol

Acute Stroke Management

Time Window: IV thrombolysis within 4.5 hours, mechanical thrombectomy up to 24 hours
tPA Thrombectomy Neuroprotection
  • Activate stroke protocol immediately
  • Blood pressure management (maintain <185/110 for tPA)
  • IV thrombolysis if eligible
  • Mechanical thrombectomy for large vessel occlusion
  • Antiplatelet therapy (avoid in hemorrhage)
  • Neuroprotective measures

Supportive Care

  • Airway management and oxygenation
  • Fluid balance and electrolyte correction
  • Glycemic control (maintain 140-180 mg/dL)
  • Temperature management
  • Deep vein thrombosis prophylaxis
  • Pressure ulcer prevention

Condition-Specific Treatments

  • GBS: IV immunoglobulin or plasma exchange
  • Myasthenia: Acetylcholinesterase inhibitors
  • MS: High-dose corticosteroids
  • Infection: Appropriate antimicrobial therapy
  • Metabolic: Correct underlying abnormalities
  • Functional: Psychological support and rehabilitation

Follow-up & Monitoring

Neurological Monitoring

Neuro Checks Progress Tracking Complication Watch
  • Neurological assessments every 2-4 hours initially
  • NIHSS scoring for stroke patients
  • Monitor for signs of deterioration
  • Complication surveillance
  • Functional assessment scales

Rehabilitation Planning

  • Early rehabilitation assessment
  • Physical and occupational therapy
  • Speech and language therapy if needed
  • Swallowing assessment
  • Psychological support
  • Social work involvement

Long-term Management

Follow-up Schedule: Outpatient visits at 1 week, 1 month, 3 months, 6 months, then annually
  • Secondary stroke prevention
  • Risk factor modification
  • Medication optimization
  • Imaging surveillance if indicated
  • Quality of life assessment
  • Caregiver education and support
