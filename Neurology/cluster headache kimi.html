Cluster Headache

Rapid Clinical Reference Guide
EMERGENCY: Suicidal ideation reported in 55-64% of patients. Screen immediately!
RED FLAGS
• First-ever worst headache
• Sudden onset ("thunderclap")
• Neurological deficits
• Age >50 with new headache
• Immunocompromised
DIAGNOSTIC CRITERIA
Duration: 15-180 minutes
Frequency: Every other day to 8×/day
Pain: Severe, unilateral orbital/temporal
Autonomic: ≥1 ipsilateral symptom
ACUTE TREATMENT - FIRST LINE

✓ High-flow Oxygen

12-15 L/min via non-rebreather mask
Duration: 15+ minutes or until relief
Safe in: Pregnancy, cardiovascular disease

✓ Subcutaneous Sumatriptan

6mg subcutaneous injection
Onset: 10-15 minutes
Max: 2 injections/24h (separated by ≥1h)
CONTRAINDICATIONS
Triptans are CONTRAINDICATED in:
  • Coronary artery disease
  • Cerebrovascular disease
  • Uncontrolled hypertension
  • Peripheral vascular disease
PREVENTIVE TREATMENT
Verapamil - First Line
Start: 80mg TID → Max: 480mg/day
ECG required before and during treatment
Galcanzumab - Alternative
300mg monthly SC injection
FDA approved for episodic CH
DIFFERENTIAL DIAGNOSIS
Condition Key Features Differentiator
Migraine 4-72 hours, bilateral, nausea Worse with activity
Paroxysmal Hemicrania 2-30 min, 1-40×/day Responds to indomethacin
SUNCT 1-600 seconds, stabbing Very short duration
TRIGGERS TO AVOID
Alcohol
Strong smells
Sleep disruption
Weather changes
REFERRAL CRITERIA
  • All suspected cluster headache cases
  • Atypical presentations
  • Treatment failure after 2 weeks
  • Chronic cluster headache
  • Pregnancy considerations
