Diagnostic Red Flags

  • Symptoms inconsistent with neuroanatomy
  • Variability with attention/distraction
  • Give-way weakness pattern
  • Non-physiological sensory loss
  • Functional seizure signs

Positive Signs

  • Hoover's sign (leg weakness)
  • Tremor entrainment test
  • Fixed dystonia patterns
  • Preserved reflexes despite weakness
  • Normal imaging despite symptoms

Quick Assessment Checklist

History

  • Onset pattern (sudden/gradual)
  • Triggers and precipitants
  • Variability over time
  • Impact on daily function
  • Psychological comorbidities

Examination

  • Test for inconsistency
  • Check for distractibility
  • Observe gait patterns
  • Test Hoover's sign
  • Assess effort level

Immediate Actions

Rule IN diagnosis, not exclusion

  1. Document positive signs clearly
  2. Explain genuine nature of symptoms
  3. Avoid unnecessary investigations
  4. Refer to neurology for confirmation
  5. Provide patient information resources

Key Phrases for Patient Discussion

Core message: "This is a problem with how your brain and nervous system are working, not damage. It's like a software issue, not hardware damage."

For Weakness

"Your leg moved normally when I did that test, showing the pathways are intact but there's a signaling problem."

For Seizures

"These are like a trance state your brain enters. It's a learned response that can be unlearned."

Prognosis Factors

Good Prognosis

  • Recent onset (<6 months)
  • Good insight
  • Motivated for treatment
  • Supportive environment
  • Absence of litigation

Poor Prognosis

  • Symptoms >2 years
  • Fixed dystonia
  • Comorbid depression
  • Litigation pending
  • Strong alternative diagnosis belief
