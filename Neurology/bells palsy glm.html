ATYPICAL FEATURES (RED FLAGS)
CRITICAL: If present, refer to exclude alternative diagnosis (Stroke, Tumor, Infection).
  • !
    Stroke Signs: Sparing of brow function (UMN lesion), limb weakness.
  • !
    Insidious/Gradual: Slow progression over weeks.
  • !
    Severe Hearing Loss: Ipsilateral deafness/tinnitus not just mild otalgia.
  • !
    Rash: Vesicular rash (Ramsay Hunt) or Ulcerative lesions.
  • !
    Other Signs: Bilateral signs, Parotid mass, Vestibular abnormalities (dizziness), Recurrent episodes.
  • !
    Synkinesis: Abnormal muscle movement in acute phase.
Key Exclusions (Differential)
Condition Distinguishing Feature
Stroke Forehead sparing, extremity weakness, sudden onset.
Ramsay Hunt Syndrome Vesicular rash in ear/pinna, severe pain.
Otitis Media / Mastoiditis Conductive hearing loss, fever, discharge.
Lyme Disease Bilateral often possible, history of tick bite, rash, arthralgia.
Parotid Tumor Palpable mass, uneven weakness distribution.
Pharmacological Treatment
Window: Start Prednisolone within 72 hours of symptom onset.

Options (Choose one):

Option A: 50 mg once daily for 10 days.
Option B: 60 mg once daily for 5 days, then reduce by 10 mg daily (Total 10 days).

URGENT / EMERGENCY REFERRAL
Immediate action required. Do not treat as Bell's palsy.
A&E / 999
Suspected Stroke (UMN Lesion)
Limb paresis, facial paraesthesia, other cranial nerve involvement, forehead sparing.
2WW
Suspected Cancer
Gradual onset, persistent >6 months, parotid mass, hearing loss, head/neck lesion.
Urgent
Trauma or Severe Infection
Head/face trauma, acute systemic infection, mastoiditis signs.
Non-Urgent Specialist Review

Refer to ENT, Neurologist, or Ophthalmology as indicated.

ENT / Neuro
Atypical Features
Uncertain diagnosis, bilateral signs, recurrent episode.
ENT / Neuro
No Improvement
No improvement after 3 weeks of treatment.
ENT / Neuro
Incomplete Recovery
Incomplete recovery after 3 months from onset.
Neuro
Aberrant Reinnervation
Symptoms (e.g., gustatory sweating) developing after 5 months.
Ophthalmology
Ocular Symptoms
Eye pain, irritation, or itch developing at any time.
Do NOT Routinely Refer

Adults with an uncomplicated episode (unilateral LMN pattern affecting all parts of the face, including weak eye closure) and no evidence of other conditions (e.g., middle ear disease).
