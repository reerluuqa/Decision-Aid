üìã What is PHN?
Definition: Dermatomal pain persisting ‚â•3 months after shingles rash onset/healing
Cause: Herpes zoster-induced peripheral & central neuronal damage

Prevalence

  • 5-30% after shingles
  • Lifetime shingles risk: 23-30%
  • Age 80+: up to 50% risk

Prognosis

  • Up to 50% have pain >1 year
  • Duration highly variable
‚ö†Ô∏è Risk Factors for PHN
  • Increasing age (major risk)
  • Prodromal pain before shingles rash
  • Severe acute pain during shingles
  • Extensive rash (beyond single dermatome)
  • Ophthalmic involvement
  • Comorbidities: diabetes, respiratory disease, autoimmune conditions, immunosuppression
üíâ Vaccine efficacy: 66-75% effective in preventing PHN (UK data, ages 70-79)
üîç Diagnosis - What to Look For

Symptoms (Ask About)

  • Pain types: aching, burning, itching, lancinating, sharp
  • Distribution: unilateral, dermatomal
  • Pattern: intermittent OR constant
  • History of shingles (may not recall rash)

Examination Findings

  • Sensory deficits: thermal, tactile, pinprick, vibration
  • Anaesthesia in affected area
  • Hyperalgesia ‚ÑπÔ∏è Excessive pain to noxious stimuli
  • Allodynia ‚ÑπÔ∏è Pain from non-painful stimulus (touch/pressure)

ü©π Lidocaine Plasters - Key Info

Dosing

  • Max 3 plasters at once
  • 12 hours ON / 12 hours OFF
  • Can cut to size
  • Re-evaluate at 2-4 weeks

Contraindications

  • Amide anaesthetic allergy
  • Inflamed/injured skin
  • Active lesions/wounds
  • Caution: severe cardiac/renal/hepatic impairment

üí° Patient education: Takes WEEKS to titrate to effective dose. Treatment doesn't work immediately.
  • Titrate more slowly if side effects worsen with each increase
  • Switch to alternative neuropathic pain drug
  • Add second neuropathic pain drug
  • Refer to specialist
