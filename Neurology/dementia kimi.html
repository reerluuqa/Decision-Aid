  • Memory loss ‚Äì deferring to family, missing appointments

üìã Initial Assessment

History:

  • Onset and progression timeline
  • Impact on ADLs (shopping, finances, medication)
  • Cognitive, behavioural, psychological symptoms
  • Comorbidities (stroke, Parkinson's, depression)
  • Medication review (anticholinergic burden)
  • Family history and risk factors

Physical Examination:

  • Neurological exam (focal signs, coordination, gait)
  • Motor symptoms (hemiparesis, tremor, rigidity)
  • Cardiovascular examination

Blood Tests:

FBC, ESR, CRP, U&E, Calcium, HbA1c, LFT, TFT, B12 & Folate
Routine investigations

üßÆ Cognitive Assessment Tools

Do not rely solely on scores ‚Äì consider learning disabilities, sensory impairments, and baseline function

Recommended Tools:

  • 10-CS (10-point Cognitive Screener): Score 8+ normal, 6-7 possible impairment, 0-5 probable impairment
  • 6-CIT: Score 0-7 normal, 8+ significant
  • Mini-Cog: 3-item recall + clock drawing. Score 3-5 = lower likelihood
  • TYM (Test Your Memory): Self-administered, max 50. Score ‚â§42 detects 93% of mild Alzheimer's
Interpret with clinical context

ü§î Differential Diagnosis

Consider treatable causes: Delirium, depression, vitamin deficiency, hypothyroidism, medication effects
  • Normal ageing: Mild decline, no functional impact
  • Mild Cognitive Impairment: Objective impairment, minimal functional impact
  • Depression: Screen for low mood, anhedonia, self-neglect
  • Delirium: Acute fluctuating course, disturbed consciousness

üî¨ Specialist Investigations

Essential:

  • Structural imaging: MRI or CT scan

When indicated:

  • FDG-PET/Perfusion SPECT: Functional imaging
  • I-FP-CIT SPECT: If dementia with Lewy bodies suspected
  • CSF examination: Rapid decline, atypical presentation, age <55

Subtype Features:

  • Alzheimer's: Memory loss first, difficulty with executive function
  • Vascular: Stepwise progression, focal neurological signs
  • Dementia with Lewy bodies: Fluctuating cognition, visual hallucinations, parkinsonism
  • Frontotemporal: Personality change, behavioral disturbance, preserved memory

üíä Pharmacological Treatment

Initiated by specialist ‚Äì may be continued in primary care

Acetylcholinesterase (AChE) Inhibitors:

Donepezil: 5mg daily, increase to 10mg after 1 month if tolerated
Galantamine: 8mg daily for 4 weeks, then 16mg daily
Rivastigmine: 1.5mg twice daily, titrate up to 6mg twice daily
Mild-moderate Alzheimer's

Memantine:

Indications:
‚Ä¢ Moderate AD intolerant to AChE inhibitors
‚Ä¢ Severe Alzheimer's disease
‚Ä¢ Add-on to AChE in moderate-severe disease
Dose: 5mg daily, titrate to 20mg weekly
Specialist initiation

Other Dementias:

  • Dementia with Lewy bodies: Donepezil or rivastigmine first-line
  • Vascular dementia: AChE inhibitors if comorbid Alzheimer's suspected
  • Frontotemporal dementia: Do NOT offer AChE inhibitors or memantine

‚ö†Ô∏è Managing Non-Cognitive Symptoms

Behaviour that Challenges:

Structured assessment first ‚Äì explore reasons, check clinical/environmental causes
  • Psychosocial and environmental interventions
  • Personalized activities for engagement
  • Antipsychotics ONLY if: Risk of harm OR severe distress from hallucinations/delusions

Antipsychotic Guidance:

Risperidone or haloperidol ONLY ‚Äì specialist supervision required
Lowest effective dose, shortest duration ‚Äì review every 6 weeks
Increased risk: Cerebrovascular adverse events and mortality
  • Haloperidol: Start 0.5mg daily, max 5mg daily
  • Risperidone: Start 0.25mg twice daily, max 1mg twice daily
  • Avoid in DLB/Parkinson's: May worsen motor features
High risk medication Specialist initiation

Depression & Anxiety:

  • Consider psychological treatments for mild-moderate symptoms
  • Do NOT routinely offer antidepressants

Follow-up

‚ñº

üè• Primary Care Follow-up

At Diagnosis:

  • Provide information about subtype and progression
  • Discuss driving and DVLA notification
  • Legal rights, lasting power of attorney, advance care planning
  • Signpost to support groups and charities

Ongoing Care:

  • Single named practitioner to coordinate care
  • Annual care plan review (minimum)
  • Assess emerging needs and risks
  • Monitor physical and mental health (depression, anxiety)
  • Review medications ‚Äì minimize anticholinergics, polypharmacy
Annual review

üîÑ Treatment Monitoring

  • Monitor response and adverse effects of dementia drugs
  • Consider memantine add-on in moderate-severe disease
  • Review antipsychotic use regularly (every 6 weeks)
  • Stop unnecessary medicines cautiously
Medication review

üö® When to Refer/Seek Advice

Refer to specialist if:
  • BPSD not responding to initial management
  • Safeguarding concerns
  • Legal issues or Mental Health Act considerations
  • Dose adjustments of AChE inhibitors/memantine needed
  • Need for other services (dentistry, optometry, OT, SALT)
Specialist input

‚ù§Ô∏è End of Life Care

Plan ahead early: Advance decisions, lasting power of attorney, preferred place of care

Key Issues:

  • Eating/Drinking: Encourage oral intake, avoid routine enteral feeding
  • Distress: Assess for pain, delirium, inappropriate care
  • Withholding Treatment: Follow GMC guidance, consider advance decisions
  • Resuscitation: Discuss with person, family, and MDT. Record decisions
