Initial Clinical Assessment Urgent
Evaluate for signs of sepsis, orbital involvement, and neurological symptoms. Key indicators include fever, headache, periorbital edema, and ophthalmoplegia.
Risk Factor Identification Caution
Identify predisposing conditions: recent sinusitis, facial infections, dental procedures, trauma, or hypercoagulable states.
Neurological Examination Urgent
Assess cranial nerve function, particularly CN III, IV, and VI. Check for lateral gaze palsy, ptosis, and pupillary abnormalities.

‚ñº
Imaging Studies Immediate
Emergency contrast-enhanced CT or MRI to confirm diagnosis. CT venography may be useful for visualization of thrombus.
Laboratory Tests Essential
FBC (leukocytosis in septic cases), blood cultures, D-dimer, and evaluation for hypercoagulable states if aseptic.
Differential Diagnosis Consider
Rule out orbital cellulitis, superior orbital fissure syndrome, meningitis, and other causes of painful ophthalmoplegia.

‚ñº
Immediate Antibiotics Critical
High-dose IV antibiotics immediately upon suspicion. Cover Staphylococcus aureus, streptococci, and anaerobes. Consider vancomycin for MRSA coverage.
Anticoagulation Consider
IV unfractionated heparin after excluding hemorrhagic complications. Continue for 3-12 months based on response and underlying risk factors.
Surgical Intervention If Indicated
Prompt drainage of primary infection source (sinusitis, dental abscess) once patient is stabilized. Endoscopic sinus surgery for sinogenic cases.
Adjunctive Therapy Supportive
Corticosteroids for cranial nerve inflammation or pituitary insufficiency. Supportive care including eye protection and sepsis management.

‚ñº
Monitoring Regular
Monitor for relapses up to 6 weeks after apparent recovery. Watch for recurrence of ocular signs, meningismus, or new neurological deficits.
Complication Surveillance Vigilant
Screen for intracranial abscesses, meningitis, cranial nerve deficits, and pituitary insufficiency. Brain abscesses may present up to 8 months later.
Long-term Management Ongoing
Continue anticoagulation as indicated. Manage residual deficits including cranial nerve palsies, visual impairment, and pituitary dysfunction.
