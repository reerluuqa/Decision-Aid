Do NOT Combine

Do not prescribe more than one neuropathic pain drug at the same time (e.g., do not combine amitriptyline with duloxetine, gabapentin, or pregabalin).

When to Refer (Specialist)

Refer if: Severe pain, significantly limits daily activities/sleep, or underlying condition has deteriorated.

Initial Management Strategy Target: Adults 18+
Step 1: Assessment & Exclusion
  • Exclude Sciatica (see CKS Sciatica) and Trigeminal Neuralgia (see CKS Trigeminal Neuralgia).
  • Assess impact on lifestyle, sleep, mood.
  • Discuss coping strategies and non-pharmacological options.
  • For other conditions (including Diabetic Neuropathy), proceed to Step 2.
Step 2: First-Line Choice

Offer one of the following:

Amitriptyline Duloxetine Gabapentin Pregabalin
Action: Start low, titrate slowly. Arrange early follow-up (clinical judgement) to assess titration and tolerability.

Background Information (Definition, Prognosis) ‚ñº

Definition

Neuropathic pain is caused by damage or disease of the somatosensory system.

Typical descriptions: Shooting, stabbing, electric shock, burning, tingling.

Features: Allodynia (pain from non-painful stimulus), Hyperalgesia.

Prognosis

  • Difficult to treat; often persistent.
  • Only 40‚Äì60% obtain partial pain relief.
  • Significant impact on Quality of Life, sleep, and mood.
