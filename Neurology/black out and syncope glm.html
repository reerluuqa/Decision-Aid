CRITICAL RED FLAGS

Admit or Refer Immediately (Within 24h) if present

IMMEDIATE ACTION

Life-Threatening Causes

  • Acute MI / Ischaemia (Chest pain)
  • Cardiac Arrhythmia (Palpitations precede collapse)
  • Pulmonary Embolism
  • Occult Haemorrhage (Trauma, GI bleed, Aortic dissection)
  • Head Trauma / Subarachnoid haemorrhage
  • Not fully regained consciousness
URGENT SPECIALIST REFERRAL

Cardiac Syncope Flags

  • Syncope during exertion or when supine
  • Sudden onset palpitations immediately followed by syncope
  • New or unexplained breathlessness
  • Heart murmur on examination
  • Family history of sudden unexplained death < 40 yrs
  • Abnormal ECG (Brugada, Long QT, Heart block)

Key Questions

  • Witness account? (Crucial)
  • Prodrome? (Sweating/Nausea = Vasovagal vs None = Cardiac/Epilepsy)
  • Posture: Standing vs. Supine?
  • Tongue biting? (Lateral = Epilepsy, Tip = Vasovagal)
  • Duration of LOC: <30s (Syncope) vs >1m (Epilepsy)
  • Confusion/Amnesia after event?
  • Triggers: Pain, Emotion, Micturition, Coughing?

Clinical Definitions & Causes

Reflex Syncope (Neurally Mediated)

Mechanism: Cerebral hypoperfusion due to reflex bradycardia/hypotension.

Vasovagal (Simple Faint)

  • 3 'P's: Posture (standing), Provocation (pain/fear), Progressive Prodrome.
  • Typical history: Recurrent before age 40.

Situational & Carotid Sinus

  • Situational: Coughing, sneezing, swallowing, micturition.
  • Carotid Sinus: Triggered by head rotation/tight collars. Age >40yrs common.

Cardiac Syncope

Mechanism: Decreased cardiac output (Arrhythmia or Structural).

Arrhythmic

  • Bradyarrhythmias (Sinus node disease, AV block).
  • Tachyarrhythmias (VT, SVT, AF with rapid rate).
  • Channelopathies (Long QT, Brugada).

Structural

  • Valvular disease (Aortic stenosis).
  • Hypertrophic cardiomyopathy.
  • Acute MI/Ischaemia.
  • Pulmonary Hypertension.

Orthostatic Hypotension (OH)

Mechanism: Failure of vasoconstriction upon standing.

Common Causes

  • Volume depletion (Dehydration, Bleeding)
  • Medications (Diuretics, Alpha-blockers, TCAs)
  • Autonomic neuropathy (Diabetes, Parkinson's)
  • Age-related physical deconditioning

Epilepsy

  • Tonic-clonic movements (often prolonged)
  • Tongue biting (lateral)
  • Post-ictal confusion (>5 mins)

Psychogenic

  • Pseudosyncope: Eyes closed, long duration (mins), no injury.
  • PNES: Variable movements, asynchronous limb jerking.
  • Usually diagnosis of exclusion.

Management & Next Steps

PRIMARY CARE

If Uncomplicated Vasovagal / Orthostatic

  • Reassurance: Explain benign nature.
  • Lifestyle: Ensure adequate hydration/salt intake.
  • Triggers: Avoid known precipitants.
  • Manoeuvres: Leg crossing/counter-pressure if prodrome occurs.
  • Medication Review: Stop/Reduce hypotensive drugs if causing OH.
INVESTIGATIONS

When to Refer/Investigate

  • Abnormal ECG: Refer to Cardiology.
  • Heart Failure / Murmur: Echo required.
  • Recurrance without diagnosis: Ambulatory ECG.
  • Neurological signs: Neurology referral.
