  • Excessive daytime sleepiness (EDS) - daily episodes of irrepressible need to sleep
  • Cataplexy - sudden loss of muscle tone triggered by emotions
  • Sleep paralysis - inability to move during sleep-wake transitions
  • Hypnagogic/hypnopompic hallucinations - vivid perceptual experiences
  • Fragmented nocturnal sleep - frequent awakenings during night
üéØ Initial Evaluation Screening Required
  • Epworth Sleepiness Scale (ESS) - typically >10 in narcolepsy
  • Sleep history - duration, quality, timing of sleep episodes
  • Family history - 10x increased risk with first-degree relatives
  • Medical history - rule out secondary causes
  • Medication review - stimulants, antidepressants, sedatives
üö® Red Flags Immediate Attention
  • Sudden onset with neurological symptoms
  • Associated with head trauma or CNS infection
  • Rapid progression of symptoms
  • Safety concerns - driving accidents, work performance issues

  • Substance/medication-induced sleepiness

  • Antidepressants: Venlafaxine, clomipramine (second-line)
  • Avoid triggers: Emotional stress, fatigue, alcohol
