Hiccups – Quick-Scan Reference

Red Flags & Emergency Actions
  • Hiccups >48 h or continuing during sleep → investigate for organic cause
  • Associated weight loss, dyspnoea, fever, neurological signs → urgent referral
  • Intractable >1 month → consider phrenic-nerve block
Definitions
Category Duration
Acute <48 h
Persistent >48 h
Intractable >1 month
First-line / Self-help
  • Hold breath / Valsalva
  • Breathe into paper bag
  • Swallow tsp dry sugar
  • Sip iced water
  • Pull knees to chest
Pharmacotherapy (adult)
Drug Dose
Chlorpromazine 25 mg IV/PO
Metoclopramide 10 mg IV/PO
Baclofen 5–20 mg PO
Drug Dose
Gabapentin 300 mg PO
Nifedipine 5–20 mg PO
Haloperidol 0.5–1 mg PO
Palliative quick picks
Omeprazole 20 mg Domperidone 10 mg Dexamethasone 4–8 mg Haloperidol 0.5 mg Baclofen 5 mg
Refer / Admit
  • Intractable despite drugs
  • Phrenic-nerve lesion suspected
  • Signs of systemic infection, malignancy, CNS lesion
