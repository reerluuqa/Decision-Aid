ME/CFS Reference NICE CKS May 2025

RED FLAG: Progressive Symptoms

Stop: If symptoms are progressive or rapidly changing, suspect an underlying condition. Do not diagnose ME/CFS. Investigate and manage appropriately.

Suspect ME/CFS (Adults)

Symptoms present for a minimum of 6 weeks:

  • Debilitating Fatigue: Worsened by activity, not relieved by rest.
  • Post-Exertional Malaise (PEM):
    Worsening 12–48hrs after activity, disproportionate, prolonged recovery (hours to weeks).
  • Unrefreshing Sleep: Exhaustion/flu-like/stiff on waking, or hypersomnia.
  • Cognitive Difficulties: 'Brain fog', problems finding words, short-term memory, concentration.

Functional Impact

Diagnosis requires a significant reduction in the person's ability to engage in occupational, educational, social, or personal activities compared to pre-illness levels.

Key Exclusions

Symptoms must not be explained by another condition. Consider:

  • Anaemia / Thyroid disorders / Diabetes
  • Coeliac disease / Chronic Kidney Disease
  • Sleep Apnoea (primary cause)
  • Depression / Anxiety (primary cause)

Clinical History

Fatigue Nature Onset, severity, frequency, fluctuations. Not improved by rest.
PEM Pattern Delay in onset, disproportionate recovery. Use activity diary if needed.
Impact Mobility, ADLs, cognition, work/education, carer stress.
Context Social support, diet, alcohol, meds/supplements.

Physical Examination

  • General: Signs of frailty, self-neglect, BMI/nutritional status.
  • Cardiovascular: HR, BP. Assess for postural hypotension. Look for pale skin/cold peripheries.
  • Abdominal/Musculoskeletal/Neuro: Standard examination to exclude alternatives.
  • Mental State: Mood and cognitive functioning.
Note: Signs of increasing fatigue/cognitive slowing may occur during long consults. Consider multiple shorter sessions.

Consider Additional Tests

Dependent on clinical judgement/history:

  • Vitamin D: If at risk of deficiency.
  • Vitamin B12 & Folate: If deficiency suspected.
  • Viral Serology: If history of infection/risk (EBV, CMV, Hepatitis, HIV, Lyme, etc.).

Do Not Use for Diagnosis

ME/CFS is diagnosed on clinical grounds alone. There is no diagnostic test.

Do not use:
• Screening instruments for depression
• Physical functioning scales

Severity Classification

Based on impact of symptoms on daily functioning. Definitions are not clear-cut.

Mild
  • Care for self, light domestic tasks (sometimes needing support).
  • Difficulties with mobility.
  • Most still working/education, but stopped leisure/social activities.
  • Reduced hours, days off, use weekend to recover.
Moderate
  • Reduced mobility, restricted in all ADLs.
  • Peaks and troughs in symptoms/ability.
  • Usually stopped work/education.
  • Need rest periods (often 1-2 hrs in afternoon).
  • Poor quality/disturbed night sleep.
Severe
  • Unable to do any activity for self, or only minimal tasks (washing teeth).
  • Severe cognitive difficulties.
  • Depend on wheelchair for mobility.
  • Often unable to leave house (severe impact if they do).
  • Most time in bed, sensitive to light/sound.
Very Severe
  • In bed all day, dependent on care.
  • Help needed with personal hygiene and eating.
  • Very sensitive to sensory stimuli.
  • May be unable to swallow (need tube feeding).

Flare-ups vs Relapse

Flare-up: Worsening of symptoms > normal day-to-day variation. Transient. Resolves after few days (spontaneously or with energy management change). Part of PEM.
Relapse: Sustained and marked exacerbation. Lasts longer than flare-up. Needs substantial, sustained adjustment to energy management. Can lead to long-term reduction in energy limits.

Goals for Primary Care

  • Be aware of when to suspect diagnosis.
  • Arrange referral to ME/CFS specialist team if working diagnosis suspected.
  • Advise on info & support sources.
  • Advise on symptom management strategies (incl. flare-ups/relapse).
  • Annual review in primary care.
  • Refer to other professionals (Physio, OT, CBT) for support if appropriate.

Important: What NOT to do

  • Do not offer Graded Exercise Therapy (GET).
  • Do not advise people to push through symptoms (fixed increments in activity).
  • Do not offer general CBT as a cure. (CBT is offered to help manage/coping, not to imply illness is psychological).

Holistic Considerations

ME/CFS can cause severe long-term disability. Watch for:

  • Social Stigma: Lack of belief from professionals/family leads to loss of trust.
  • Mental Health: Increased risk of anxiety/depression (secondary to chronic illness).
  • Economic: Loss of employment/productivity.

Patient Support

Acknowledge the impact on their quality of life. Validate their experience.

"I believe you. This condition is real and we will work together to manage it."
