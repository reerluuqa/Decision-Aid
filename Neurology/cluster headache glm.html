Cluster Headache

Quick Clinical Reference
Last updated: Apr 02, 2025
Emergency Red Flags
Sudden onset "thunderclap" headache
Fever, neck stiffness
New neurological deficit
Altered consciousness
Papilledema
Headache with exertion
New headache >50 years
These may indicate secondary causes (SAH, meningitis, tumor, etc.) requiring urgent investigation.
Definition & Key Features
  • Severe unilateral orbital/supra-orbital/temporal pain
  • Duration: 15-180 minutes (untreated)
  • Frequency: 1 every other day to 8 per day
  • Ipsilateral autonomic symptoms
  • Restlessness/agitation during attacks
Episodic (80%) Cluster periods + remission
Chronic (20%) No remission or <3 months
Autonomic Symptoms
Lacrimation
Conjunctival injection
Nasal congestion
Rhinorrhoea
Ptosis
Miosis
Facial sweating
Eyelid/facial swelling
Acute Treatment
Treatment Dose/Route Notes
Sumatriptan 6mg SC 1st line Fastest relief
Oxygen 12-15 L/min, 15 min 1st line Non-rebreather mask
Zolmitriptan 5mg nasal Alternative to SC sumatriptan
nVNS 2 stimulations For episodic CH only
Avoid triptans in patients with cardiovascular disease, uncontrolled hypertension, or cerebrovascular disease.
Preventative Treatment
Verapamil
1st line
Start 80mg TID, titrate to 240-960mg/day
ECG before and during dose escalation
Galcanezumab
For episodic CH
300mg SC, then monthly
Lithium
300-900mg/day
Narrow therapeutic window
Topiramate
100-200mg/day
Teratogenic
Transitional (Bridge) Therapy
Prednisolone 60mg daily, taper over 2-3 weeks
Greater occipital nerve block with steroids
IV dihydroergotamine (if no CV risk)
Use while waiting for preventative therapy to take effect (usually 2+ weeks).
Key Differentials
Condition Distinguishing Features
Migraine Longer duration (>4h), photophobia/phonophobia, nausea, rest improves pain
Paroxysmal hemicrania Shorter attacks (2-30 min), more frequent (up to 40/day), indomethacin responsive
Trigeminal neuralgia Electric shock-like pain, V2/V3 distribution, mechanical triggers
SUNCT/SUNA Very short attacks (1-600 sec), conjunctival injection and tearing
Special Considerations
Pregnancy Avoid topiramate, lithium, valproate
Smoking cessation Associated but not causal
Sleep apnea Consider polysomnography
Head trauma Associated in some patients
For pregnant patients: high-flow oxygen is preferred for acute treatment; verapamil may be considered with specialist advice.
