Trigeminal Neuralgia

Primary Care / Adults 18+ Updated: Jan 2024
Exclusions: Pregnancy, breastfeeding, other facial neuropathic pain (refer to specific CKS topics).

Diagnosis Checklist

Classic Presentation

  • Severe facial pain: Electric shock-like, sharp, shooting.
  • Unilateral: Usually maxillary (cheek) or mandibular (jaw) branches. Ophthalmic rare.
  • Short duration: Seconds to 2 minutes. Sudden stop.
  • Triggers: Light touch, eating, talking, cold wind, brushing teeth.
  • Remission: Periods of no pain (weeks/months) initially.
Serious Red Flags (Refer/Urgent)
  • Age of onset under 40 years.
  • Bilateral pain or pain only in the ophthalmic division (eye/forehead).
  • Sensory changes (numbness/loss).
  • Optic neuritis or deafness.
  • History of Multiple Sclerosis or lesions.

Primary Management: Carbamazepine

1

Initial Dose

Start 100 mg once or twice daily.

2

Titration

Increase by steps of 100–200 mg every 2 weeks until pain is relieved.

3

Maintenance

Usually 200 mg three or four times a day. Modify-release: 600–800 mg in 2 divided doses.

MAX

Maximum Dose

1600 mg daily.

Follow-up: Arrange early review to assess titration and tolerability.
Once pain is in remission, gradually reduce to lowest maintenance level or stop.

When to Refer

  • Severe pain not controlled by Carbamazepine.
  • Carbamazepine is contraindicated or not tolerated.
  • Pain significantly limits daily activities (eating, sleeping).
  • Atypical features (burning background pain, sensory loss).
Do not initiate other drugs (e.g., Gabapentin) in primary care without specialist advice.

Carbamazepine Safety

DO NOT PRESCRIBE (Contraindications)

  • Hypersensitivity to carbamazepine or tricyclics.
  • Atrioventricular block.
  • History of bone marrow depression.
  • Hepatic porphyrias.
  • Taking MAOIs (or within 2 weeks of stopping).

Adverse Effects & Monitoring

Show Safety Warnings & Interactions

Hyponatraemia (20%)
Check if dizziness, confusion, or seizures occur. Reduce dose or stop.
Dermatological (SJS)
HLA-B*1502 Screening: Mandatory for Han Chinese, HK Chinese, Thai origin. Consider for Malaysia/Philippines.
Blood Disorders
Leucopenia (very common). Advise patient to report fever, sore throat, bruising immediately.
Suicidal Thoughts
Small risk. Monitor for mood changes.
Key Interactions:
  • Avoid Diuretics (risk of hyponatraemia).
  • Increased toxicity with: Acetazolamide, Fluoxetine/Fluvoxamine, Azole antifungals, HIV protease inhibitors.
