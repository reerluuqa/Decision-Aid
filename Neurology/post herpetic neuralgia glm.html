PHN Quick-Scan

Post-Herpetic Neuralgia Management • Last Revised Aug 2024
Suspect PHN?
  • Pain Characteristics Aching, burning, itching, lancinating, or sharp.
  • Timing Persists 3+ months after rash onset/healing.
  • Examination Signs Unilateral dermatomal distribution. Allodynia (pain from touch) or Hyperalgesia.
Note: Patient may not recall the rash. Check for sensory deficits.
Context & Risk
Prevalence 5–30% after Herpes Zoster (Shingles). Incidence reduced in vaccinated 70-79yo cohort.
Risk Factors Increasing age, severe prodromal pain, severe rash, ophthalmic involvement, comorbidities (diabetes, immunosuppression).
Prognosis Highly variable. Up to 50% experience pain >1 year. Satisfactory relief challenging.
Self-Management
  • Wear cotton or silk (least irritation).
  • Protect sensitive areas (cling film, Opsite, bandages).
  • Cold packs (unless allodynia is triggered).
  • Maintain pain diary to track progress.
Management Pathway
1
First Line Analgesia Routine

Paracetamol +/- Codeine.

For mild to moderate pain. Unlikely to be fully effective alone but contributes to relief. Consider comorbidities/drug interactions (elderly cohort).

2
Neuropathic Agents Titrate

Amitriptyline, Duloxetine, Gabapentin, or Pregabalin.

Choose based on local policy. Titrate slowly.
Controlled Drug Gabapentin & Pregabalin are Class C (since 2019). Check for abuse history.

3
Topical / Adjunct Targeted

Lidocaine Medicated Plasters.

Use if pain is mild, allodynia is prominent, or if concerned about CNS side effects of oral meds (elderly). Can be adjunct to oral therapy if severe.

4
Rescue / Specialist Specialist

Tramadol (Acute rescue only) OR Referral.

Tramadol: Do not prescribe long-term without specialist supervision.

Refer to Neurologist / Pain Clinic
  • Severe pain uncontrolled by primary care options.
  • Significant limitation of daily activities (sleep, self-care).
  • Signs of depression/anxiety.
  • Treatment poorly tolerated or ineffective at max dose.
Prescribing Details
Dose

Apply once daily for up to 12 hours.

Max 3 plasters at once. 12hr plaster-free interval required. Re-evaluate after 2–4 weeks.
Application Cut to size with scissors before removing liner. Cover painful area only.
Caution Do NOT apply to inflamed/injured skin (active zoster, wounds).
Caution in severe cardiac, renal, or hepatic impairment.
Adverse Effects Site reactions (burning, dermatitis, erythema, rash).

For detailed info on Amitriptyline, Duloxetine, Gabapentin, Pregabalin, and Tramadol, see CKS topic Neuropathic pain - drug treatment.

Warning (Class C Drugs):

Since April 2019, Gabapentin and Pregabalin are controlled substances. Evaluate for history of drug abuse before prescribing.

× Capsaicin Cream

Currently unavailable (likely until June 2026). Removed from recommendations.

× NSAIDs

Not recommended for PHN specifically; no evidence of benefit for this neuropathic pain type.
