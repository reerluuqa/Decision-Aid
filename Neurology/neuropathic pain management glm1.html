Initial Management Algorithm 1. Exclude sciatica & trigeminal neuralgia (see separate topics).
2. Assess pain severity, impact on sleep/activity, and mental health.
3. Offer one of the four first-line options.

Treatment Options

First Line
  • Amitriptyline (TCA)
  • Duloxetine (SNRI)
  • Gabapentin (Gabapentinoid)
  • Pregabalin (Gabapentinoid)
Rule: Do not prescribe more than one neuropathic pain drug concurrently.

When to Refer

Specialist
  • Severe pain unresponsive to initial treatment.
  • Pain significantly limiting daily activities (self-care, mobility, sleep).
  • Underlying health condition deteriorated.
Bridge to Referral: Consider short course of Tramadol (CD Schedule 3) if awaiting specialist input.

Review & Follow-Up

Timing: Arrange early follow-up to assess titration and tolerability.

Licensed? Yes (Neuropathic pain).

Withdrawal: Discontinue gradually over min 4 weeks to prevent discontinuation symptoms (dizziness, nausea, anxiety).

Pregnancy: Not recommended unless benefits outweigh risks. No robust evidence of malformation but cannot be ruled out.

Duloxetine

First Line
Starting Dose 30 mg once daily
Standard Dose 60 mg once daily
Note: Doses > 60 mg provide no additional benefit (Cochrane review).

Trial Period: Consider trialling for 6-8 weeks.

Contraindications: Uncontrolled narrow-angle glaucoma, use with MAOIs.

Gabapentin

Schedule 3 CD
Starting Dose 300 mg Day 1
Titration (Typical) 300 mg increments
Risk of Dependence: Assess history of drug abuse. Monitor for misuse. Dependence is an expected effect, not a reason to avoid, but requires management.

Renal Impairment: Dose adjustment required based on creatinine clearance.

Adverse Effects: Dizziness, drowsiness, peripheral oedema, toxic epidermal necrolysis (rare).

Withdrawal: Taper to avoid withdrawal reactions.

Pregabalin

Schedule 3 CD
Starting Dose 150 mg/day in 2-3 div doses
Max Dose 600 mg/day
Misuse Risk: High potential for addiction. Monitor for signs of misuse/abuse. Safe storage discussions required.

Renal Impairment: Dose adjustment required.

Safety: Monitor for suicidal ideation. Severe respiratory depression risk.

Pregnancy: Avoid unless clearly necessary.

Definition

Pain arising as a direct consequence of a lesion or disease affecting the somatosensory system.

Typical Descriptions: Shooting, stabbing, electric shock, burning, tingling, numb, prickling.

Associated Symptoms: Allodynia (pain from light touch), Hyperalgesia (increased pain response).

Prognosis

Neuropathic pain is complex and often difficult to treat completely.

  • Persistence: Often persistent due to irreversible nerve changes.
  • Response Rate: Only 40‚Äì60% of people obtain partial relief.
  • Impact: Quality of life, sleep, and mood are frequently impaired.
