NPH Clinical Reference

Immediate Red Flags / Emergencies

Sudden change in walking function: May indicate shunt malfunction, stroke, or subdural haematoma.
Signs of Infection: Fever, headache, inflammation at catheter site, or abdominal pain.

Definition General Info

Normal Pressure Hydrocephalus (NPH)

Characterised by hydrocephalus features without significantly raised CSF pressure.

  • Levodopa-unresponsive gait apraxia
  • With or without cognitive impairment/urinary symptoms
  • Responds to CSF diversion/shunting if treated early

Risk Factors Epidemiology

  • Age > 65 years
  • Vascular disease (CVA, CVS, Hypertension)
  • Diabetes mellitus (Weak association)

Estimated ~5x more common than idiopathic Parkinson's.

Diagnosis

Clinical Picture Key Diagnostic

Gait Apraxia (Hallmark)

Symptoms insidious, usually antedate others.

  • Slow, cautious gait / Shuffling
  • Gait initiation failure
  • Unsteadiness (widened base, retropulsion)
  • Freezing / Falls
  • Stuck in doorways

Other Symptoms

  • Cognitive: Mental slowing, memory loss.
  • Urinary: Frequency, urgency, urge incontinence.
  • Faecal incontinence: Rare (advanced disease).

Investigations Actionable

1st Line: MRI/CT Head (Non-contrast)

Rule out obstruction. Look for ventricular enlargement w/ preserved cortical sulci.

Diagnostic Challenge: Levodopa Trial

If responsive to levodopa, NPH is ruled out. If no response (unresponsive), NPH is likely.

Confirmatory: Large-Volume CSF Tap

Remove 30-60mL. Improvement in timed walk supports diagnosis & predicts shunt response.

Differential Diagnosis

Condition Key Differentiators Test Result
Parkinson's Disease Asymmetrical, predominant tremor (face/arms). Levodopa: Responsive
Parkinson-Plus Affects top half body more (speech, face). Early autonomic features. CSF Drainage: Unresponsive
Alzheimer's Cognitive impairment usually precedes gait impairment. CSF Drainage: Unresponsive
Cerebrovascular Parkinsonism Often indistinguishable clinically. May be end-stage untreated NPH. CSF Drainage: Unresponsive

Management & Treatment

Treatment Algorithm

Confirm Diagnosis: LP, Prolonged Lumbar Drainage, or CSF Infusion.
Risk Assessment: Is patient suitable for surgery? (Anaesthetic risk, vascular comorbidities).
If Suitable: Ventriculoperitoneal Shunting.
If Unsuitable: Control cardiovascular risk factors (BP, Cholesterol, Smoking).

Complications High Risk

Short Term (High Likelihood)
  • Subdural Haematoma: Common. Watch for deterioration/confusion.
  • Shunt Infection: Staphylococcus spp. (Headache, abdominal pain).
  • Stroke / Bleeding: Perioperative risk.
Long Term
  • Shunt malfunction (~50% require revision).
  • Vascular disease progression.
  • Prognosis depends on vascular health; gait improves most, cognition less so.

Typical Case History

75-year-old man presents with 2-year history of walking problems (slow gait, imbalance, short stride). Urinary frequency and memory loss reported. Symptoms symmetrical, lower body affected, hands/face spared. Previously diagnosed with Parkinson's but levodopa therapy has not helped.

Action: Consider NPH diagnosis immediately.
