URGENT ACTION
Adults (18+)

If patient presents with:

  • Progressive, sub-acute loss of central neurological function.
Required Action:
Consider Urgent Direct Access MRI Brain (or CT if MRI contraindicated).
⏱ To be performed within 2 weeks.
VERY URGENT
Children & Young People

If patient presents with:

  • Newly abnormal cerebellar or other central neurological function.
Required Action:
Consider a Very Urgent Referral.
⏱ Appointment within 48 hours.
Clinical Context

Prevalence

~9,000 cases/year (UK)

GP likely to see ~1 case every 3–5 years.

Prognosis (5-year survival)

~15%

Early detection is critical.

Safety Netting

If patient does not meet criteria but risk persists:

  • Active Monitoring: Review if symptoms change or worsen.
  • Explain: Tell patient which symptoms to watch for.
  • Reassure: Risk is low, but not zero. Provide safety netting advice in writing.
  • Action: Ensure investigation results are reviewed promptly.

Source: NICE Guideline [NG12] Suspected Cancer: Recognition and Referral (2025).
