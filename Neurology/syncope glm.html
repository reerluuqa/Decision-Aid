Syncope Assessment

Quick Clinical Reference Tool

IMMEDIATE CONCERNS

Rule out life-threatening causes: cardiac arrhythmias, MI, massive PE, aortic dissection, severe hypovolemia

Definition

Syncope is a sudden and transient loss of consciousness associated with loss of postural tone due to cerebral hypoperfusion. It resolves spontaneously without intervention.

Red Flags
  • Chest pain or palpitations preceding syncope
  • Syncope during exertion
  • Known structural heart disease
  • Family history of sudden cardiac death
  • Abnormal ECG findings
  • Evidence of heart failure
  • Severe anemia (Hct <30%)
Initial Assessment

History

  • Precipitating factors and prodrome
  • Position at time of event
  • Duration and recovery time
  • Witness account if available

Physical Exam

  • Vital signs (BP supine and standing)
  • Cardiovascular examination
  • Neurological examination

Initial Tests

  • 12-lead ECG for all patients
  • Cardiac enzymes if indicated
  • Hemoglobin and glucose
Benign Features
  • Clear precipitating factor (emotional distress, pain)
  • Prodromal symptoms (nausea, sweating)
  • Occurred while standing
  • Quick recovery without confusion
  • Normal ECG and examination
  • Age <40 years without heart disease
Risk Stratification

High Risk (Admit)

  • Structural heart disease or abnormal ECG
  • Heart failure or EF <35%
  • Symptoms during exertion
  • Family history of sudden death
  • Significant comorbidities

Low Risk (Discharge)

  • Age <40 years, no heart disease
  • Clear vasovagal trigger
  • Normal ECG and examination
  • No family history of sudden death
Differential Diagnosis
Cause Key Features Tests
Vasovagal Prodrome, situational triggers, standing position History, tilt table if needed
Orthostatic Hypotension Postural symptoms, medication use, autonomic dysfunction BP supine/standing, medication review
Arrhythmia Palpitations, exertional syncope, heart disease ECG, Holter, event monitor
Structural Heart Disease Exertional symptoms, murmurs, heart failure Echocardiogram, ECG
Cause Key Features Tests
Bradyarrhythmias AV block, sinus node dysfunction ECG, Holter, electrophysiology
Tachyarrhythmias VF, VT, SVT, WPW ECG, Holter, electrophysiology
Structural Aortic stenosis, HCM, valvular disease Echocardiogram, ECG
Ischemia Chest pain, risk factors Cardiac enzymes, ECG, angiography
Cause Key Features Tests
Neurological Seizure, SAH, TIA Neuro exam, CT/MRI, EEG
Pulmonary PE, pulmonary hypertension D-dimer, CT pulmonary angiogram
Psychiatric Anxiety, conversion disorder History, psychiatric evaluation
Metabolic Hypoglycemia, electrolyte abnormalities Blood glucose, electrolytes
Management Approach

Emergency Actions

  • ABCs - Secure airway, breathing, circulation
  • Cardiac monitoring for arrhythmias
  • IV access and fluids if hypovolemia suspected
  • Immediate ECG and cardiac enzymes if chest pain
  • CT head if neurological signs or trauma

Disposition

  • Admit High-risk cardiac causes, abnormal ECG, elderly with comorbidities
  • Observe Intermediate risk, unclear etiology
  • Discharge Clear vasovagal cause, normal evaluation, low risk
