Red Flag: Suspect Delirium?

Acute confusion, fluctuating consciousness, or sudden onset? Do NOT assume dementia. Action: Investigate infection, dehydration, or metabolic cause immediately. See separate CKS topic on Delirium.

Assessment & Causes

Alzheimer‚Äôs Disease (50-75%)
Atrophy, amyloid plaques, neurofibrillary tangles.

Features: Memory loss usually predominant. Often co-exists with vascular dementia.
Vascular Dementia (~20%)
Reduced blood supply to brain (strokes, infarcts).

Features: Stepwise decline, history of gait abnormalities, incontinence, emotional lability.
Dementia with Lewy Bodies (DLB)
Cortical/subcortical Lewy bodies.

Features: Fluctuating cognition, visual hallucinations, parkinsonism. Caution: High sensitivity to antipsychotics.
Frontotemporal Dementia (FTD)
Degeneration of frontal/temporal lobes.

Features: Personality/behaviour changes first, language problems. Common in younger people (<65).
Risk Factors
Non-Modifiable: Age, Genetics (ApoE, Down's syndrome), Family history.
Modifiable (Target for Intervention):
Smoking Hypertension Obesity Diabetes Hearing Loss Physical Inactivity Social Isolation Depression

Antipsychotic Prescribing

Avoid using antipsychotics for behaviour control if possible. If essential:
‚Ä¢ Start at lowest dose.
‚Ä¢ Review regularly (aim to stop).
‚Ä¢ Caution: Increased risk of stroke, CVAE in elderly.

Pharmacological Options
  • Acetylcholinesterase inhibitors: Donepezil, Galantamine, Rivastigmine (First-line for Alzheimer's, DLB, PD dementia).
  • Memantine: NMDA receptor antagonist. For moderate-severe Alzheimer's or if AChEIs not tolerated.
  • New Product: Voleze (rivastigmine) 4.6mg/9.5mg patches now available.
Non-Drug Interventions
  • Cognitive stimulation/rehabilitation.
  • Physical activity (exergaming shown to help).
  • Music therapy/Reminiscence therapy.
  • Tailored activities to promote wellbeing.
