🩸 Folate Deficiency

Interactive clinical reference for diagnosis, investigation and management of folate deficiency in primary care

BJH Guidelines 2014 NICE NG247 2025 Last Updated June 2025

⚠️ CRITICAL SAFETY RULE

If a patient is deficient in both folate AND B12, you must start B12 replacement at least 24 hours before initiating folic acid.

B BEFORE F

Replacing folate alone in combined deficiency can mask B12 deficiency, risking irreversible neurological damage including subacute combined degeneration of the cord.

Source: Derbyshire shared care pathology guidance, B12 (including folate), accessed June 2024

📋

Key Summary Points

▼

Core Principles

  • Isolated folate deficiency is rare in the Western world — always ask why?
  • Folate deficiency often co-exists with B12 deficiency — there is considerable symptom overlap.
  • Always check B12 before starting folic acid.
  • Replace B12 first if both are deficient: "B before F".
  • Standard treatment: 5 mg folic acid OD for 4 months.

Background Terminology

  • "Folate" = all biologically active forms of the vitamin.
  • "Folic acid" = the synthetic form used in supplements, fortified food and treatment.
  • Folate is required for DNA synthesis.
  • Folate and folic acid are absorbed from the proximal small intestine.
  • Almost half the body's folate is stored in the liver.

Useful Facts

  • Cooking reduces folate in food (e.g. raw spinach > cooked spinach).
  • The body absorbs twice as much folate from folic acid than from food with equivalent concentration.
  • Folate deficiency has become less common since the introduction of food fortification.

British Journal of Haematology 2014;166:496 — Guidelines for the diagnosis and treatment of cobalamin and folate disorders.

Investigations for Folate Deficiency

▼

First-line Test: Serum Folate

  • Reflects recent dietary intake — serum folate can drop several weeks after reduced ingestion.
  • Consumption of folate before the test can normalise the result even in true deficiency.
  • Normal ranges vary by lab (as do the units!) — always use local reference values.

⚠️ False Positives (Low serum folate but NOT truly deficient)

  • Anorexia
  • Acute alcohol consumption
  • Pregnancy
  • Anticonvulsant use

Second-line Test: Red Cell Folate

  • Assesses tissue folate status over ~3 months (lifetime of a red blood cell).
  • No benefit in the vast majority — serum folate remains the recommended first-line test.
  • Consider if strong clinical suspicion of deficiency + normal serum folate + B12 deficiency ruled out.
  • Red cell folate can be falsely low in B12 deficiency.

Specialist Test: Plasma Homocysteine

  • Can help confirm folate deficiency in special circumstances.
  • Not a routine first-line investigation.

⚠️ BEFORE YOU PRESCRIBE

Check B12 levels in ALL patients before starting folic acid for folate deficiency.

💊

Treatment Algorithm

▼

  1. Check B12 Levels

    Mandatory in ALL patients before initiating folic acid. If B12 is also deficient, replace B12 first (at least 24h before folic acid).

  2. Investigate the Cause

    Isolated folate deficiency is rare — consider dietary, GI, medication, alcohol and other causes (see Causes tab).

  3. Start Folic Acid 5 mg OD for 4 Months

    Standard oral replacement for folate-deficient megaloblastic anaemia. Set a recall or annotate the prescription to prompt review at 4 months.

  4. Consider Higher Dose in Malabsorption

    Up to 15 mg daily may be needed in malabsorptive states (e.g. coeliac disease, Crohn's).

  5. Dietary Advice

    Recommend folate-rich foods: spinach, kale, broccoli, cabbage, beans, legumes, nuts and seeds.

  6. Review at 4 Months

    Treatment should be for a defined duration. Reassess clinical appropriateness — do not leave patients on indefinite folic acid without review.

Indications for 5 mg Folic Acid in Pregnancy (NICE 2025, NG247)

  • Medication affecting folic acid metabolism (e.g. antiepileptics, HIV medicines)
  • Previous pregnancy affected by neural tube defect or other congenital malformation
  • Personal or family history (woman or partner) of neural tube defects or other congenital malformations
  • Type 1 or Type 2 diabetes
  • Sickle cell disease, thalassaemia

What About Obesity in Pregnancy?

  • NICE found no evidence to support high-dose folic acid for overweight/obese BMI alone.
  • Standard 400 μg is sufficient unless other risk factors are present.
  • This is a change from older RCOG guidance (BJOG 2019; 126:e62) which previously recommended higher doses in this group.

Bariatric Surgery

Women who have had bariatric surgery may require specialised advice on folic acid and other micronutrients before and during pregnancy. Consult the patient's bariatric surgery unit for guidance.

Treatment of Deficiency in Pregnancy

  • If treating confirmed folate deficiency during pregnancy, continue folic acid until term.
  • No need to routinely monitor folate in pregnancy unless deficiency clinically suspected or hyperemesis present.

Practice Audit Checklist — Folic Acid Prescribing

▼

Audit Prompt

Run a search for all patients on folic acid for treatment of folate deficiency. How long have they been taking it? Is it still clinically appropriate?

For Each Patient on Folic Acid, Confirm:

  • Indication for folic acid is documented
  • B12 was checked before folic acid was started
  • If B12 was also deficient, B12 replacement was started first (≥24h before folic acid)
  • Duration of treatment is defined (4 months for deficiency treatment)
  • Underlying cause has been investigated
  • If long-term, there is an ongoing clinical indication (e.g. chronic haemolysis, renal dialysis, prophylaxis in pregnancy)
  • Dose is appropriate for indication (5 mg OD for treatment; 400 μg or 5 mg for pregnancy prophylaxis)
  • If on malabsorption dose (≤15 mg/d), specialist guidance has been obtained
  • Dietary advice has been given and documented
  • Review date is set on the clinical system
