Patient History

Demographics & Risk Factors

Age: Median 70-75 years at diagnosis

Risk Factors: Prior chemotherapy, radiation, congenital disorders

Symptoms: Fatigue, infections, bleeding, autoimmune disorders

Key Questions:

  • Prior exposure to chemotherapy/radiotherapy?
  • Family history of hematological disorders?
  • Autoimmune symptoms (vasculitis, arthritis)?
  • Recent infections or bleeding episodes?

Physical Examination

Clinical Signs & Findings

Common Findings:

  • Pallor (common)
  • Petechiae/purpura (20% patients)
  • Bacterial infections (40-50%)
  • Splenomegaly (rare, consider CMML)

Approximately 25% of MDS patients have autoimmune disorders:

  • Vasculitis
  • Connective tissue disease
  • Inflammatory arthritis

Initial Laboratory Tests

Peripheral Blood Analysis

Cytopenias (≥1 required):

  • Anemia (90% of patients)
  • Neutropenia (40% of patients)
  • Thrombocytopenia (30% of patients)

Mandatory Tests:

  • FBC with differential
  • Peripheral blood smear
  • Reticulocyte count
  • Serum B12, RBC folate, iron studies
  • Viral serology (if risk factors)

Diagnosis & Classification

Bone marrow evaluation and risk stratification

Bone Marrow Evaluation

Definitive Diagnostic Tests

Required Procedures:

  • Bone marrow aspiration with iron stain
  • Core biopsy for cellularity & architecture
  • Cytogenetic analysis
  • Molecular mutation profiling

MDS diagnosis requires persistent cytopenia PLUS one of:

  • ≥10% dysplasia in ≥1 lineages
  • Blasts <20% in blood/bone marrow
  • Clonal cytogenetic abnormality

Blasts ≥20% suggests AML

Genetic Testing

Cytogenetic & Molecular Profile

Common Abnormalities:

  • del(5q), -7/del(7q), del(20q)
  • SF3B1, TP53, DNMT3A mutations
  • Complex karyotype (≥3 abnormalities)

Poor Prognosis: TP53, ASXL1, ETV6, EZH2, RUNX1

Favorable: SF3B1 mutations, isolated del(5q)

IDH1 mutations: May guide treatment (ivosidenib)

Treatment Algorithm

Risk-adapted management strategies

Supportive Care Drug Therapy Transplant

Lower-Risk Disease Management

1

Observation

Asymptomatic patients - monitor until symptoms or progression

2

Symptomatic Anemia

Stratify by erythropoietin level and genetic subtype

MDS-5q (del[5q])

Isolated 5q Deletion

Erythropoietin ≤500 IU/L:

  • Lenalidomide (preferred)
  • ESA (epoetin/darbepoetin)

Erythropoietin >500 IU/L:

  • Lenalidomide first-line
  • Hypomethylating agents (azacitidine)
  • Clinical trial
  • Ivosidenib (if IDH1 mutated)
  • Allogeneic SCT (select patients)

MDS-SF3B1

Ring Sideroblasts ≥15%

First-Line:

  • Luspatercept (preferred)
  • Imetelstat alternative

Erythropoietin ≤500 IU/L:

  • ESA + G-CSF if luspatercept fails

Subsequent Lines:

  • Hypomethylating agents
  • Ivosidenib (IDH1 mutated)
  • Allogeneic SCT

Non-del(5q) MDS

Other Lower-Risk MDS

Erythropoietin ≤500 IU/L:

  • ESA alone or luspatercept
  • G-CSF + ESA if no response

Erythropoietin >500 IU/L:

  • Immunosuppressive therapy (if eligible)

Higher-Risk Disease Management

Transplant Candidates

Allogeneic Stem Cell Transplant

Immediate SCT: If suitable donor available

Cytoreduction: Reduce blasts to <5% before transplant

Options: Chemotherapy, hypomethylating agents, ivosidenib (IDH1)

Non-Transplant Candidates

Medical Management

First-Line:

  • Azacitidine (preferred)
  • Decitabine or decitabine/cedazuridine

Second-Line:

  • Ivosidenib (if IDH1 mutated)
  • Clinical trial

Supportive Care (All Patients)

Transfusion Support

Blood Product Management

RBC Transfusion:

  • Hb <7-8 g/dL or symptomatic
  • Iron chelation if transfusion-dependent

Platelet Transfusion:

  • For bleeding or <10,000/μL

Infection Management

Antimicrobial Strategies

Prophylaxis:

  • Antibiotics during treatment
  • Vaccinations (non-live)

Fever ≥38.3°C: Urgent evaluation

Follow-up & Monitoring

Long-term disease surveillance

Monitoring Schedule

Regular Assessment Intervals

Full Blood Count:

  • Routine monitoring based on risk
  • Interval varies by patient status

Iron Studies: If transfusion-dependent

Iron Chelation Indications:

  • ≥15 RBC transfusions
  • Serum ferritin >1000 μg/L
  • Allogeneic SCT candidates

Improves event-free survival

Complication Monitoring

Early Detection & Management

Iron Overload:

  • Liver abnormalities
  • Organ dysfunction

Infections:

  • Neutropenia & neutrophil dysfunction
  • Leading cause of death

Progression to AML:

  • ≥20% blasts in bone marrow
  • Usually refractory to standard therapy
  • Consider allogeneic SCT
  • Clinical trial enrollment

Prognostic Updates

Survival & Transformation Risk

IPSS-R Reassessment:

  • Every 3-6 months
  • After major treatment changes

Prognostic Factors:

  • Cytogenetics & mutations
  • Transfusion dependence

Patient Education & Prevention

Vaccination

Infection Prevention

Recommended Vaccines:

  • Influenza (annual)
  • Streptococcus pneumoniae
  • COVID-19 (per local guidelines)
  • All non-live vaccines

Emergency Instructions

Fever & Bleeding Precautions

Fever ≥38.3°C (101°F):

  • Urgent evaluation required
  • Even without neutropenia
  • Neutrophil dysfunction may be present

Bleeding Precautions:

  • If platelet count low
  • Avoid trauma & NSAIDs
