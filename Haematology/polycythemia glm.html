Polycythaemia / Erythrocytosis

Primary Care Management & Decision Support

NICE CKS Updated: Sept 2024
URGENT REFERRAL REQUIRED
Suspected Polycythaemia Vera (PV) OR Symptoms of Hyperviscosity
Refer Haematology

Quick Diagnostic Triage

Suspect Primary (PV)

Red Flags: Splenomegaly, Thrombosis, Itching after warm water, Budd-Chiari.

Key Labs:

  • Hct >0.52 (M) / >0.48 (F)
  • JAK2 V617F Positive (Definitive)
  • High Platelets, High WBC
  • Low MCV
Action: Urgent Haematology Referral

Suspect Secondary

Possible Causes: Chronic hypoxia, smoking, COPD, Sleep apnoea, Renal disease.

Key Labs:

  • High Erythropoietin (EPO)
  • Saturation < 92%? (Hypoxic)
  • Abnormal LFTs/Renal Function
Action: Treat underlying cause (Resp/Renal)

Suspect Apparent

Cause: Reduced plasma volume (Normal Red Cell Mass).

Common Factors:

  • Obesity
  • Diuretics (Thiazides)
  • Alcohol excess
  • Smoking
Action: Lifestyle changes. No further investigations.

Primary Care Management

Initial Actions

  • Repeat FBC after 1 week if incidental.
  • Examine: Splenomegaly, Conjunctival plethora.
  • Check BP, Smoking status, Alcohol intake.
  • Urine dipstick (Renal pathology).

Ongoing Care

  • Manage CV risk factors (QRISK3).
  • Smoking cessation / Weight loss.
  • Switch thiazides if applicable.
  • Iron replacement: Use judiciously.

When to Refer

! Suspected PV / Hyperviscosity Urgent
→ High EPO without hypoxic cause Haematology (Malignancy)
→ Cyanotic heart disease Cardiology
→ Sleep Apnoea Sleep Clinic

Risk & Monitoring

Thrombosis Risk (PV)

High Risk: Age >65 OR History of Thrombosis.
  • Intermediate: Age <65 + CV risk factors (HTN, Diabetes, Smoking, Lipids).
  • Low: Age <65 + No CV risk factors.

Monitoring Secondary Erythrocytosis

Regular blood tests required. Target Hct usually 0.54. Refer for venesection if target exceeded.

Transformation Signs (PV)

Monitor annually for progression (2-8% Myelofibrosis, 1-3% Leukaemia).

  • Development of Anaemia
  • Loss of venesection requirement (untreated)
  • Increasing splenomegaly (>5cm growth)
  • Constitutional symptoms (Fever, Night sweats, >10% weight loss)
