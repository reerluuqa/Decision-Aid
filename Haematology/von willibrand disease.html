Initial Assessment
1. Bleeding History Assessment Caution
• Easy bruising or excessive bleeding from minor wounds
• Mucosal bleeding (epistaxis, gum bleeding)
• Heavy menstrual bleeding (HMB)
• Postpartum hemorrhage
• Post-operative bleeding complications
2. Family History
• Family history of VWD or bleeding symptoms
• Consanguineous relationships (Type 3 VWD)
3. Physical Examination
• Signs of anemia (pale conjunctiva, fatigue)
• Large bruises or unusual bruising patterns
• Joint swelling (severe cases)
4. Bleeding Assessment Tool (BAT) Screening
• MCMDM-1VWD questionnaire
• Self-BAT for self-assessment
• ISTH/SSC BAT for standardized evaluation
Diagnostic Workup
1. Initial Laboratory Tests Essential
• Full blood count (FBC) - check for anemia, thrombocytopenia
• Prothrombin time (PT) - usually normal in VWD
• Activated partial thromboplastin time (aPTT) - may be prolonged if FVIII low
• Fibrinogen - usually normal
2. Specific VWD Tests Critical
• VWF antigen (VWF:Ag) - quantitative VWF level
• VWF platelet-binding activity (VWF:GPIbM or VWF:GPIbR)
• Factor VIII coagulant activity (FVIII:C)
• VWF ristocetin cofactor activity (VWF:RCo) - alternative to GPIbM
3. Additional Tests for Subtyping
• VWF multimer analysis - identifies structural abnormalities
• VWF collagen binding assay (VWF:CB)
• Low-dose ristocetin-induced platelet agglutination (RIPA)
• VWF FVIII binding assay (VWF:FVIIIB) - for Type 2N
• Genetic testing - for Type 2B and 2N variants
4. Diagnostic Criteria
• Type 1: VWF:Ag <0.30 IU/mL, normal VWF activity/Ag ratio (>0.7)
• Type 2: VWF:Ag <0.30 IU/mL, abnormal VWF activity/Ag ratio (<0.7)
• Type 3: VWF:Ag undetectable (<0.01 IU/mL)
Treatment Approach
1. Type 1 VWD Treatment Mild
• Desmopressin (DDAVP) - first-line for responsive patients
• VWF-containing concentrate - for non-responders or severe bleeding
• Antifibrinolytic therapy (tranexamic acid) - adjunct for mucosal bleeding
2. Type 2 VWD Treatment Moderate
• Types 2A, 2M: May respond to desmopressin (test first)
• Type 2B: VWF-containing concentrate (desmopressin contraindicated)
• Type 2N: VWF-containing concentrate (desmopressin ineffective)
3. Type 3 VWD Treatment Severe
• VWF-containing concentrate - required for all bleeding/events
• Platelet transfusion - for refractory bleeding
• Prophylactic treatment - for recurrent bleeding
4. Special Considerations
• Pregnancy: Monitor VWF levels in 3rd trimester, plan delivery
• Heavy menstrual bleeding: Hormonal therapy ± antifibrinolytics
• Surgery: Pre-operative VWF level targeting >0.50 IU/mL
Follow-up & Monitoring
1. Regular Monitoring
• Annual review of bleeding history and symptoms
• Laboratory monitoring if on regular treatment
• Iron studies for patients with chronic bleeding
2. Complication Prevention Important
• Monitor for inhibitor development (Type 3 VWD)
• Avoid NSAIDs and antiplatelet agents
• Vaccination considerations (hepatitis A/B for plasma products)
3. Patient Education
• Genetic counseling for family planning
• Emergency contact information
• Medical alert identification
• Activity restrictions and safety measures
4. Special Populations
• Women: Gynecological follow-up for HMB
• Pregnancy: Multidisciplinary team management
• Children: Development and activity monitoring
