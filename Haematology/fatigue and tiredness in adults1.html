Adult Tiredness/Fatigue

Clinical Algorithm for Primary Care Assessment & Management

Initial Assessment

History:

  • Clarify symptoms: Sleepiness vs. weakness vs. fatigue
  • Timeline: Onset (sudden vs. gradual), duration, severity
  • Impact: Effect of sleep/rest/exercise, daily activities
  • Sleep: Quality, patterns, snoring, apnea, nocturia
  • Psychosocial: Stress, work, relationships, dependents
  • Mental health: Depression/anxiety symptoms
  • Medical history: Chronic conditions (asthma, COPD, CKD, HF)
  • Medications: Include OTC, herbal, internet-purchased

Physical Examination:

  • General appearance, vital signs
  • Mental state examination
  • Targeted exam based on symptoms
  • Look for red flags (see next section)

Red Flags

Urgent attention required for:

  • Significant unintentional weight loss (>5% body weight in 6-12 months)
  • Fever, night sweats, lymphadenopathy
  • Muscle/joint pain suggesting inflammatory/connective tissue disease
  • Focal neurological signs
  • History of travel, insect/tick bites, skin rash

These symptoms may indicate serious underlying conditions requiring immediate investigation or referral.

Suspect Chronic Fatigue Syndrome

Suspect CFS if fatigue ≥6 weeks with:

  • Significantly impaired daily activities
  • Not explained by other illness

All these features must be present:

  • Fatigue worsened by activity, not relieved by rest
  • Post-exertional malaise: Delayed onset, disproportionate to activity, prolonged recovery
  • Unrefreshing sleep/sleep disturbance
  • Cognitive difficulties: Word-finding, memory, concentration problems

First-Line Investigations

Order if fatigue ≥6 weeks or no obvious cause:

Full blood count and serum ferritin (anemia, malignancy)
ESR or CRP (infection, inflammation)
Liver function tests
Renal function tests
Thyroid function tests
HbA1c (diabetes)
IgA tissue transglutaminase (coeliac disease)
Urinalysis (protein, blood, glucose)
Creatine kinase (if muscle symptoms)
Bone biochemistry/myeloma screen (if ≥60 years)

Additional: Vitamin D level (if deficiency risk)

Management Approach

1
Identify and treat underlying causes using investigation results
2
For CFS:
  • Refer to specialist CFS service
  • Energy management/pacing strategies
  • Cognitive behavioral therapy
  • Graded exercise therapy (specialist-guided)
3
General advice:
  • Sleep hygiene
  • Stress management
  • Balanced nutrition
  • Gradual return to activities
4
Follow-up: Monitor progress, adjust management
5
Provide information sources and support contacts

When to Refer

  • Suspected CFS (confirmatory diagnosis and management)
  • Unexplained abnormal investigations
  • Red flag symptoms/signs
  • Significant functional impairment
  • Diagnostic uncertainty

Early referral for suspected CFS can improve outcomes and provide patients with appropriate support and management strategies.
