B12 & Folate Deficiency Anemia

Quick Clinical Reference Tool for Primary Healthcare Professionals

URGENT: Do not delay vitamin B12 replacement in patients with neurological symptoms, especially sub-acute combined degeneration of the spinal cord.
Definition

Deficiencies of vitamin B12 or folate are the most frequent causes of megaloblastic anaemia, characterized by:

  • Larger than normal red blood cells (macrocytosis, MCV > 100 fL)
  • Immature nuclei due to defective DNA synthesis
  • Anemia defined by Hb levels: <130 g/L (men), <120 g/L (women)
Prevalence
  • B12 deficiency affects at least 3% of those aged 20-39, 4% of those 40-59, and 6% of those 60+
  • 11% of people with vegan diets are deficient in vitamin B12
  • Pernicious anaemia prevalence: 0.1% in general population, 1.9% in those 60+
  • Folate deficiency affects no more than 5% of adults and children
Critical Symptoms
Neurological symptoms Ataxia Paraesthesia Memory lapses Progressive neuropathy Weakness Dyspnoea Glossitis Mild jaundice Palpitations

Note: Neurological disorders can occur independently of hematological manifestations.

Key Causes
B12 Deficiency
  • Pernicious anaemia (most common)
  • Vegan diet
  • Malabsorption
  • Gastric surgery
Folate Deficiency
  • Poor diet
  • Alcoholism
  • Malabsorption
  • Increased requirements
Initial Investigations
1
Full blood count to determine MCV, Hct and Hb levels
2
Blood film to identify megaloblastic anaemia
3
Measure total B12 or active B12 (holotranscobalamin)
4
Measure folate levels
Important Considerations
  • Normal MCV does not exclude B12 testing (neurological impairment occurs with normal MCV in 25% of cases)
  • Ask about over-the-counter B12 preparations before testing
  • Serum cobalamin levels fall in pregnancy and are less reliable
  • People of Black ethnicity may have higher reference ranges for vitamin B12
Diagnostic Thresholds
Test Confirmed Deficiency Possible Deficiency
Total B12 < 180 ng/L (133 pmol/L) 180-350 ng/L (133-258 pmol/L)
Active B12 < 25 pmol/L 25-70 pmol/L
Serum Folate < 7 nmol/L (3 µg/L) 7-10 nmol/L (3-4.5 µg/L)
Blood Film Findings
  • Hypersegmented neutrophils (>5% with 5+ lobes)
  • Oval macrocytes
  • Circulating megaloblasts
  • Low reticulocyte count (1-3%)
REMEMBER: Consider starting B12 replacement while waiting for test results if deficiency is suspected to be an adverse effect of a medicine.
Urgent Actions
!
Start B12 replacement immediately if patient has neurological symptoms, especially sub-acute combined degeneration of the spinal cord
!
Seek neurologist advice for management of neurological complications
Treatment Approach
1
B12 Deficiency: Hydroxocobalamin intramuscular injections
2
Folate Deficiency: Folic acid oral supplements
3
Investigate underlying cause (pernicious anaemia, malabsorption, etc.)
4
Maintenance therapy every 2-3 months for B12 deficiency
Management Actions
Urgent: Start B12 with neuro symptoms Routine: Investigate underlying cause Info: Refer to specialist if needed Routine: Monitor treatment response
When to Refer
  • Neurological symptoms not responding to treatment
  • Suspected pernicious anaemia for confirmation
  • Uncertain diagnosis after initial investigations
  • Pregnant patients with B12 or folate deficiency
  • Young patients with unexplained B12 deficiency
Critical Complications
Neurological Damage

Sub-acute combined degeneration of the spinal cord can become irreversible if not treated promptly.

Gastric Cancer

2-3% of pernicious anaemia cases develop gastric cancer. Increased vigilance required.

Cardiopulmonary Complications

Especially in elderly with severe anemia, risk of heart failure.

Pregnancy Complications
Neural Tube Defects

B12 or folate deficiency in pregnancy increases risk of neural tube defects (spina bifida, anencephaly).

Prematurity

Maternal folate deficiency associated with prematurity.

Other Complications
Sterility

Nutritional deficiency may cause sterility, reversible with appropriate supplementation.

Autoimmune Associations

Pernicious anaemia associated with other autoimmune diseases (thyroid disorders, vitiligo, Addison's).
