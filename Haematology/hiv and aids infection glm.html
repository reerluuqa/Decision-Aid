Clinical Goals & Overview

Identification & Action
  • Recognize features to enable early diagnosis.
  • Identify people requiring hospital admission, urgent referral, or signposting.
  • Refer for PrEP/PEP when appropriate.
  • Monitor for adverse effects of ART.
Prognosis & Prevention

Outcome: Timely diagnosis and ART adherence lead to near-normal life expectancy.

"Undetectable = Untransmittable" (U=U)

Disease Stages & Definitions

Primary HIV Infection

Timing: 2‚Äì4 weeks after infection.

Features: Flu-like symptoms (fever, sore throat, rash, lymphadenopathy, fatigue). Viral load is high; highly infectious.

Asymptomatic Phase

Duration: Several years.

Status: Person remains well but infectious. HIV replicates at low levels; CD4 cells decline gradually.

Advanced HIV Disease (AIDS)

Definition: CD4 count < 200 cells/ŒºL or presence of opportunistic infections/malignancies.

Key Risks: Pneumocystis pneumonia, Kaposi‚Äôs sarcoma, TB.

Testing & Diagnosis

When to Offer Testing
  • Universal: People who request testing.
  • High Risk Groups: MSM, Trans women, Trans men with risk factors, Black Africans, Sex workers, Prisoners, People who inject drugs.
  • Geographic: People from countries with high seroprevalence (>1%) or partners from these regions.
  • Indicator Conditions: See table below.
Prevention Strategies

PrEP (Pre-exposure): Refer for at-risk individuals.

PEP (Post-exposure): Refer urgently if exposure occurred (within 72 hours).

Note: Covers identification and primary care management; specialist management is secondary care.

Complications (Multisystem)

Respiratory
TB (Leading cause of death), Bacterial LRTI, COPD.
Neurological
Peripheral neuropathy, HIV-associated neurocognitive disorder (HAND), Dementia.
Cardiovascular
Increased risk of Atherosclerosis, MI, Stroke, Heart failure, Pulmonary hypertension.
Metabolic
Lipodystrophy, Insulin resistance, Diabetes, Dyslipidaemia.
Dermatological
Fungal (Tinea), Viral (Herpes, Warts, Shingles), Bacterial (Impetigo), Seborrhoeic dermatitis.
Psychological
Depression, Anxiety, PTSD, Insomnia, Addictions.

Background Information

Transmission

Transmitted by: Sexual activity (vaginal, anal, oral), Vertical (mother to child), Inoculation (needles, blood products, open wounds).

NOT transmitted by: Shaking hands, hugging, kissing, sharing utensils, insects (mosquitoes/lice).

Risk Factors
  • Unprotected sex / Multiple partners / Chemsex
  • History of STIs (Syphilis, Chlamydia, Gonorrhoea)
  • Occupational exposure (Sharps)
  • Blood transfusions in unscreened countries
Prevalence & Trends (UK)

Est. Living with HIV: ~113,500 (over 5,200 undiagnosed).

New Diagnoses (2023): 6,512 (46% increase from 2022). Trends show increases concentrated outside London.

Note: The UK is working toward a 2030 target of zero new transmissions, though current trends show challenges.
