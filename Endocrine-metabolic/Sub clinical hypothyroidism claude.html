Subclinical Hypothyroidism

Clinical Decision Support Tool

Clinical Guideline Overview

Definition

Subclinical hypothyroidism = Raised TSH with normal T4

Always repeat TSH test after 3 months: only act if subclinical hypothyroidism confirmed on second test.

Key Management Points

  • 2-6% of patients with subclinical disease progress to clinical hypothyroidism annually
  • Risk factors for progression: female, older age, positive thyroid peroxidase antibodies
  • Be more hesitant treating patients >65y due to risks of TSH over-suppression (e.g., AF)
  • Symptoms may not be different between euthyroid and subclinical patients, especially in older adults

Cardiovascular Risk Evidence

Generally no increased risk of coronary heart disease or mortality, except those with TSH >10 show increased coronary heart disease risk and mortality.

Treatment Evidence

Trials assessing treatment do not show significant improvements in symptoms/quality of life. Studies did not assess impact on cardiovascular risk factors.

Monitoring if Not Treating

  • Features of underlying thyroid disease (e.g., raised autoantibodies, previous surgery): Check TSH/T4 annually
  • No features of underlying thyroid disease: Check every 2-3 years
