What is it?

A complex skeletal disease characterized by low bone density and micro-architectural defects.

Key Clinical Feature: Asymptomatic until fracture occurs.

First Line Action

For Postmenopausal Women & Men:

  • Pharmacology: Bisphosphonates.
  • Lifestyle: Adequate Calcium + Vitamin D intake for ALL patients.
CRITICAL SAFETY WARNING

Sequential Therapy Required: All patients treated with Teriparatide, Abaloparatide, Denosumab, or Romosozumab MUST receive sequential therapy with antiresorptive osteoporosis treatment to prevent rapid bone loss.

Exclude secondary causes:

  • Calcium, Albumin, Phosphate
  • Vitamin D (25-OH) (Rule out deficiency)

üíä Management Strategy

Primary Prevention

For All Patients:

  • Adequate Calcium intake (Diet + Supplements).
  • Adequate Vitamin D intake.
  • Exercise & Weight bearing activity.
  • Fall prevention strategies.
Hormone Therapy

Estrogen:

Considered only for postmenopausal women at high risk for whom non-estrogen drugs are inappropriate.

Special Considerations
  • CKD Stages 1-3: Treat as osteoporosis if PTH 65-300 pg/mL.
  • Monitoring: Use biochemical markers (PINP, CTX) to monitor response, though DXA is preferred.

Case 1: Hip Fracture

Patient: 70yo Woman.

Event: Fall from bed -> Intertrochanteric hip fracture.

Red Flag: Chest X-ray revealed pre-existing asymptomatic vertebral fractures.

Action: Assess for underlying severe osteoporosis. Immediate fracture care.
Case 2: Severe Back Pain

Patient: 70yo Man.

History: Post-renal transplant, on corticosteroids.

Presentation: Severe back pain. X-ray: Multiple vertebral compression fractures.

Action: High index of suspicion for steroid-induced osteoporosis.
Differentials to Rule Out
Multiple Myeloma

Bone pain + anemia + renal failure. Monoclonal protein in urine/serum.

Osteomalacia

Vit D deficiency, elevated PTH, bone biopsy for poor mineralisation.

Metastatic Malignancy

History of cancer, pain in atypical locations. Hot spots on bone scan.
