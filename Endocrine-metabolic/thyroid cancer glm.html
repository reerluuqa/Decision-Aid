Classification & Genetics

Papillary Carcinoma 80%

Characteristics: Well-differentiated, indolent, multicentric, spreads via lymph nodes.

Cytology: Intranuclear holes/grooves, 'Orphan Annie' eyes, psammoma bodies.

Common Mutations: BRAF V600E RET/PTC

Follicular Carcinoma ~10%

Characteristics: Malignant follicular tumour with capsular/vascular invasion. Spreads haematogenously (blood).

Note: Cytology cannot distinguish Adenoma vs Carcinoma (requires surgical pathology).

Common Mutations: RAS PAX8::PPARG

Medullary Carcinoma 1-3%

Origin: Parafollicular C cells.

Markers: Calcitonin, CEA.

Hereditary: 25% (Associated with MEN2A/MEN2B or isolated familial).

Common Mutations: RET (Germline/Somatic) RAS (Sporadic)

Anaplastic Carcinoma (ATC) Aggressive

Characteristics: Undifferentiated, rapid local invasion, extremely poor prognosis.

Genetics: Heterogeneous. Often dedifferentiated from DTC.

Key Alterations: TP53 TERT PI3K

Oncocytic Carcinoma (Previously H√ºrthle Cell) 3-4%

Characterized by large cells with eosinophilic granular cytoplasm. Considered more aggressive than non-oncocytic follicular carcinoma. Requires surgical diagnosis for vascular invasion confirmation.

Bethesda System (Key Categories)

  • I. Non-diagnostic: Repeat FNA.
  • II. Benign: Clinical follow-up.
  • III. Atypia (AUS): Repeat FNA or molecular testing.
  • IV. Follicular Neoplasm: Lobectomy (Molecular testing optional).
  • V. Suspicious for Malignancy: Surgery (Lobectomy/Total).
  • VI. Malignant: Surgery/Active Surveillance.

Management Strategy

Differentiated Thyroid Cancer (Papillary/Follicular/Oncocytic)

Treatment depends on pre-treatment assessment of risk and surgical/pathological findings.

Low Risk

Criteria

  • Unifocal papillary microcarcinoma (‚â§1cm).
  • No extracapsular extension.
  • No lymph node mets.

Action

Option A: Active Surveillance (US follow-up every 6-12 months).

Option B: Lobectomy.

Intermediate Risk

Criteria

  • Aggressive histology.
  • Minor microscopic ETE.
  • Vascular invasion.
  • >5 involved lymph nodes.

Action

Total Thyroidectomy

Consider Radioactive Iodine (RAI) therapy.

High Risk

Criteria

  • Gross extrathyroidal extension.
  • Incomplete resection.
  • Distant metastases.
  • Lymph nodes >3.0cm.

Action

Total Thyroidectomy

Radioactive Iodine (RAI)

TSH Suppression

Medullary Thyroid Cancer (MTC)

Treatment: Total thyroidectomy + central neck dissection.

Screening: Genetic testing for RET proto-oncogene (mandatory if family history or MEN).

Labs: Serum Calcitonin (monitoring).

Anaplastic Thyroid Cancer (ATC)

Urgency: Extremely aggressive. Rapid airway compromise risk.

Treatment: Multimodal (Surgery if resectable, Chemo, Radiation).

Emerging: Targeted therapies (BRAF/MEK inhibitors) based on molecular profile.
