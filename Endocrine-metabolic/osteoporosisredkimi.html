Osteoporosis Quick Ref

Quick Decision Algorithm
Select patient characteristics to see recommended pathway

1. Identify Risk

Case-finding approach: Postmenopausal women & men ≥50y with risk factors. NO population screening

2. Quantify Risk

Use FRAX (NOGG/NICE) or QFracture (SIGN). Calculate 10-year probability of major osteoporotic fracture.

3. DXA Scan?

Click 'FRAX Tool' tab for specific thresholds. Generally: Intermediate/High risk → DXA. Very high risk → Treat without waiting for DXA.

4. Treatment Decision

Based on T-score + FRAX. Oral bisphosphonates first line (alendronate/risedronate).

Risk Factors (Tick in FRAX)
  • Age, Sex, BMI
  • Previous fracture
  • Parent with hip fracture
  • Smoking, Alcohol ≥3 units/day
  • Rheumatoid arthritis
  • Secondary osteoporosis
  • Steroids (any dose, oral, ≥3 months)
Tip: In FRAX, tick "RA" box if patient has T2 diabetes or Parkinson's (NOGG adjustment).
When to Skip FRAX → DXA
NICE Age <40y with:
  • Fragility fracture, OR
  • Prednisolone >7.5mg/day ≥3m, OR
  • Untreated premature menopause

    Glucocorticoid-Induced Osteoporosis
    Start bone protection immediately (don't wait for DXA) if:
    • Previous fragility fracture (any age/sex)
    • Woman ≥70y on ANY steroid dose
    • Postmenopausal women or men ≥50y on ≥7.5mg/day prednisolone ≥3m
    • FRAX in yellow zone but above intervention threshold
    FRAX adjustments for steroids:
    • Low dose (<2.5mg): May overestimate by 20%
    • High dose (≥7.5mg): May underestimate by 15% (add 15% to probability)
    • Always tick steroid box in FRAX
    Management if Steroids Stopped

    Reassess FRAX to decide if bone treatment can also be stopped. Risk returns to baseline ~12 months after stopping steroids.

    Intermittent Steroids

    Significant load = 30mg/day for total of 4 weeks within any 3-month period → Consider bone protection if recurrent courses.

    Men with Osteoporosis
    SIGN guidance:
    • Secondary prevention: Risedronate or Zoledronic acid (NOT alendronate - insufficient evidence)
    • Primary prevention (on high-dose steroids): Alendronate, Risedronate or Zoledronic acid OK
    >50% have underlying cause - consider referral for secondary cause workup if <50y or premenopausal woman.
    Vertebral Fractures
    When to X-ray spine (NOGG):
    • Acute back pain with risk factors
    • Height loss ≥4cm
    • Thoracic kyphosis
    • Long-term steroids
    • T-score ≤-2.5

    Management: Different pathway (often needs anabolic therapy). NOGG does NOT recommend vertebroplasty/kyphoplasty routinely.

    Age >80y (Oldest Old)

    • Time to benefit: ~12 months for oral bisphosphonates (shorter for IV/anabolic)

    • Consider treatment if life expectancy >6-12 months

    • Be careful with renal function (IV/SC drugs)

    • QFracture may be more accurate than FRAX in healthy elderly

    Cancer Therapy

    Aromatase inhibitors / Anti-androgens:

    • Baseline fracture risk assessment
    • High risk (red zone) → Start bisphosphonate/denosumab
    • Near threshold → Repeat FRAX/DXA at 12-18 months
    Baseline Investigations (NOGG)
    For postmenopausal women and men ≥50y with osteoporosis/fragility fracture:
    FBC, Creatinine, LFT, Bone profile, TFT, CRP/ESR, Vitamin D, Phosphate
    Consider: Coeliac screen, myeloma screen, PTH (if calcium abnormal), testosterone/LH/FSH (if hypogonadism suspected)
    ⚠️ CRITICAL: Denosumab Cessation
    Never stop denosumab without specialist review. Increased risk of multiple vertebral fractures within 18 months of stopping. Must step down to bisphosphonate if stopping.
    Bisphosphonate Safety
    GI: Take with full glass water, remain upright 30min. Do NOT use PPI for dyspepsia (reduces efficacy).

    Osteonecrosis of Jaw: Rare (1/10,000). Ensure dental check before starting. Report jaw pain/swelling.

    Atypical Femoral Fractures: Risk increases after 5 years. Thigh pain (bilateral in 50%) = X-ray femur and stop drug.

    Hypocalcaemia: Check vitamin D before IV bisphosphonates.
    Monitoring
    DXA monitoring: Minimum 2-year interval. Consider at end of treatment course.

    Success definition: Even if BMD falls up to 4%, may still have fracture benefit (check compliance).

    Bone turnover markers: Specialist use only (P1NP, CTX).

    Repeat FRAX: Only if risks change or 2-year interval.
    Calcium & Vitamin D
    Dietary calcium aim: 700mg/day
    Supplements: Only if dietary intake inadequate
    Vitamin D: 800 IU/day if deficient/at risk
    Note: Do NOT take calcium within 2h of oral bisphosphonates
    Lifestyle Advice
    • Stop smoking
    • Alcohol ≤2 units/day (NOGG)
    • Weight-bearing exercise
    • Balance training / Falls assessment
    • Maintain BMI 20-25
