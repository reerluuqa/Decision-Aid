Clinical Quick-Scan Reference Tool

Immediate Clinical Suspension Points:
Personal or Family history of CAD (Angina/MI) or Type 2 DM. Symptoms of Obstructive Sleep Apnoea (snoring, daytime sleepiness).
Clinical Snapshot

A cluster of abnormalities increasing risk for Cardiovascular Disease (CVD) and Type 2 Diabetes.

Insulin Resistance Abdominal Obesity Atherogenic Dyslipidaemia Hypertension

Note: Ethnic variations exist. Asians often develop conditions at lower BMI.

Harmonized Criteria (IDF/AHA)

Presence of ≥3 risk factors confirms diagnosis.

Essential 1st Line Tests
Test Clinical Action
Fasting Glucose < 5.5 mmol/L is normal. > 6.9 mmol/L = DM.
Lipid Profile TG, HDL, LDL (12hr fast required).
Blood Pressure Measure on both arms, average.
Waist Circ. Essential criteria. Midway between iliac crest & lowest rib.
Comorbidity Screening
  • HbA1c & OGTT (75g load) if fasting glucose 5.6-6.9 mmol/L.
  • LFTs (AST/ALT): Screen for MASLD (Fatty Liver). Refer if elevated.
  • Urine Albumin/Creatinine: Check for nephropathy.
  • TSH: Exclude hypothyroidism (causes dyslipidaemia).
  • Serum Uric Acid: Commonly elevated; independent predictor.
  • ECG: Look for LVH, previous MI, ischaemia.
Primary Goals

Lifestyle (First Line)

  • Diet: Low in saturated fats. Moderate-to-intense physical activity.
  • Weight Loss: Primary driver. Aim for 5-10% loss initially.

Pharmacological Targets

  • BP Control: Target based on overall CV risk.
  • Lipids: Target LDL-C / Non-HDL-C levels.
  • Glucose: Reduce fasting glucose / HbA1c.
Risk Factor Management
Risk Factor Intervention Impact
Obesity Diet, Exercise, Bariatric surgery (selected) Reduces insulin resistance, TG, BP.
Dyslipidaemia Statins, Fibrates Reduces CVD risk significantly.
Hypertension ACE inhibitors, ARBs, CCBs, Diuretics Reduces stroke & cardiac events.
Insulin Resistance Metformin (if pre-diabetic/DM), TZDs Delays onset of Type 2 DM.
Common Associated Conditions

Screen actively if clinical suspicion exists.

PCOS (Women)

Look for: Hirsutism, Acne, Oligo-amenorrhoea.

Test: Testosterone, DHEAS, Ultrasound.
MASLD (Fatty Liver)

Look for: Elevated ALT/AST, Hepatomegaly.

Test: Ultrasound, FibroScan (FAST score).
Other Key Comorbidities
Hypogonadism (Men)

Testosterone deficiency linked to insulin resistance.

Action: Check Testosterone/SHBG
Obstructive Sleep Apnoea (OSA)

Strongly linked to obesity. 'Syndrome Z' overlap.

Action: Sleep study if snoring/daytime sleepiness
Hyperuricaemia

Independent predictor of MetSyn. Not a diagnostic criterion.
