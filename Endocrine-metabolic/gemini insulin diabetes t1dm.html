Insulin Therapy in Type 1 Diabetes

Quick Reference Algorithm for Clinical Decision Making

Insulin Types & Characteristics

Type Onset Duration Examples
Rapid-acting ~15 min 2-5 hr Lispro, Aspart
Short-acting 30-60 min Up to 8 hr Actrapid, Humulin S
Intermediate-acting 1-2 hr 11-24 hr Humulin I, Insulatard
Long-acting 1-2 hr Up to 24 hr Glargine, Detemir

Recommended Regimens

Adults
Children
First-line: Multiple daily injection basal-bolus regimen
Basal: Twice-daily insulin detemir (or once-daily glargine/detemir if preferred)
Bolus: Rapid-acting insulin analogue before meals
Alternatives: Insulin pump therapy or hybrid closed loop systems for specific cases
First-line: Multiple daily injection basal-bolus regimen from diagnosis
Encourage dose adjustment after blood glucose measurement
Alternative: Insulin pump therapy if basal-bolus unsuitable
Consider personal/family circumstances and preferences

Administration Technique

1
Preparation: Bring insulin to room temperature (≥30 min), check expiry date
2
Mixing: For cloudy insulin, invert/rotate (don't shake) 10+ times
3
Injection sites: Rotate between abdomen (fastest), outer thigh, buttocks (slowest)
4
Technique: Use new needle, inject at 90° angle, leave needle in 5-10 seconds
5
Aftercare: Apply gentle pressure (don't rub), rotate sites to prevent lipohypertrophy

Monitoring Targets

Parameter Adults Children/Young People
Pre-prandial 4-7 mmol/L 4-8 mmol/L
Post-prandial <9 mmol/L <10 mmol/L
HbA1c ≤48 mmol/mol (≤6.5%) ≤48 mmol/mol (≤6.5%)

Managing Hypoglycemia

Mild/Moderate

15-20g fast-acting carbohydrate
Recheck after 15 minutes
Follow with complex carbohydrate if next meal >1 hour away

Severe

Glucagon 1mg injection (if unable to swallow)
Emergency services if no response within 10 minutes

Special Considerations

Driving: Check DVLA guidelines; must notify DVLA if using insulin
Sick-day rules: Never stop insulin; monitor glucose/ketones frequently; maintain hydration
Travel: Carry extra supplies; obtain travel letter; adjust for time zones
Pregnancy: Requires specialist management (not covered in this guidance)

Patient Education Essentials

Provide Insulin Passport and patient information booklet
Train on hypoglycemia recognition and management
Advise on injection site rotation
Recommend identification jewelry/card
Provide sharps disposal container

Note: This algorithm is a condensed reference. Always refer to full NICE guidelines for comprehensive management. Insulin therapy should only be initiated and managed by healthcare professionals with relevant expertise and training.
