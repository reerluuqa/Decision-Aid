General Information

Normal Physiology

TRH Hypothalamus
→
TSH Anterior Pituitary
→
T4 + T3 Thyroid Gland

Key Principle: Negative Feedback

When T3/T4 levels rise or fall, the hypothalamus and pituitary sense these changes via negative feedback pathways, adjusting TRH and TSH secretion to maintain thyroid hormone balance.

TSH Patterns & Interpretation

Elevated TSH → Hypothyroidism
HIGH TSH Hypothyroidism
NORMAL TSH Subclinical Hypothyroidism
Check T4 only
T4 falls before T3 - relying on T3 risks delayed/missed diagnosis
Low TSH → Hyperthyroidism
LOW TSH Hyperthyroidism
NORMAL TSH Subclinical Hyperthyroidism
Check BOTH T3 AND T4
T3-only elevation occurs - single test may miss diagnosis

Testing Strategy Decision Tree

1

Start with TSH

TSH is the first-line test for most patients. Simple, sensitive screening tool.

2

TSH Elevated?

If ↑ TSH - Check T4 only. T4 reduction precedes T3 fall.

3

TSH Low?

If ↓ TSH - Check BOTH T3 AND T4. T3-only elevation possible.

4

Pituitary Dysfunction Suspected?

If central hypo/hyperthyroidism suspected - Order TSH + T3 + T4 at outset.

Red Flags & Emergency Actions

Myxedema Coma / Thyroid Storm

Suspect in patients with known hypothyroidism presenting with altered mental status, hypothermia, or cardiovascular instability. Immediate endocrine consultation required.

Hyperthyroidism Red Flags

Atrial Fibrillation

New-onset AF in older patients - rule out thyrotoxicosis

Heart Failure

High-output failure or worsening existing CHF

Thyroid Storm ( Burch-Wartofsky Score )

Score ≥45: Immediate ICU transfer, beta-blockers, antithyroid drugs

Hypothyroidism Red Flags

Myxedema Coma

Altered mental status + hypothermia - mortality 30-40% without prompt treatment

Pericardial Effusion

Large effusions causing tamponade physiology

Hyponatremia

Severe SIADH from untreated hypothyroidism

Pituitary Dysfunction Patterns

TSH-Producing Pituitary Adenoma
↑ TSH Elevated TSH
↑ T3/T4 Elevated T3 and T4
Mechanism: Autonomous TSH production by pituitary adenoma → stimulates thyroid → excess T3/T4 despite elevated TSH (inappropriate)
Pituitary Compression (Non-TSH Adenoma)
↓ TSH Low/Undetectable TSH
↓ T3/T4 Low T3 and T4
Mechanism: Mass effect compresses pituitary → reduced TSH secretion → secondary hypothyroidism with inappropriately normal/low TSH

Clinical Tip

If pituitary dysfunction is suspected, order TSH, T3, AND T4 at the outset - do not rely on TSH alone.

Quick Reference Summary

Normal TSH

TSH within reference range (0.4-4.0 mIU/L typically)

No Action

Reassess

Normal thyroid function likely - clinical correlation needed only if symptoms present
High TSH

Suggestive of hypothyroidism

Check T4

Overt: ↑ TSH + ↓ T4
Subclinical: ↑ TSH + Normal T4
Low TSH

Suggestive of hyperthyroidism

Check T3 + T4

Overt: ↓ TSH + ↑ T3/T4
Subclinical: ↓ TSH + Normal T3/T4
