Type 2 Diabetes Clinical Reference

Fast decision-making algorithm for busy clinicians

Diagnosis & Initial Assessment

  • Diagnostic criteria: HbA1c ≥ 48 mmol/mol (6.5%) or fasting plasma glucose ≥ 7.0 mmol/L
  • Initial tests: HbA1c, fasting glucose, kidney/liver function, lipids, urine ACR
  • Risk factors: Obesity (central), inactivity, family history, ethnicity, age >40, previous GDM, hypertension, CVD

Management Targets

  • Glycaemic control: General population HbA1c ≤ 58 mmol/mol (7.5%)
  • Frail patients: HbA1c ≤ 75 mmol/mol (9.0%)
  • Blood pressure: ≤140/90 mmHg
  • Lipids: Consider statin for all patients >40 years (or with CVD)

Treatment Algorithm

1st LINE: Lifestyle intervention + METFORMIN
If HbA1c not at target after 3-6 months
2nd LINE: Add one of:
• SGLT-2 inhibitor (esp. if CVD, heart failure, or CKD)
• DPP-4 inhibitor
• Pioglitazone
• Sulfonylurea
• GLP-1 receptor agonist
If still not at target
3rd LINE: Combination of TWO drugs from above options
If still not at target
INSULIN therapy (refer to specialist if needed)

Annual Monitoring

HbA1c
3-6 monthly until stable, then 6 monthly
Weight
Every consultation
Blood pressure
Every 3-6 months
Foot examination
Annual risk classification
Retinal screening
Annual
Kidney function
Annual eGFR, urine ACR

Special Considerations

  • Children/young people: Refer to specialist paediatric diabetes team
  • Pregnancy/preconception: Specialist referral, avoid metformin, SGLT-2 inhibitors, ACE inhibitors/ARBs
  • Elderly/frail: Less stringent HbA1c targets, avoid hypoglycaemia
  • CVD/CKD: Consider SGLT-2 inhibitors regardless of HbA1c

Red Flags requiring urgent referral

  • HHS (hyperosmolar hyperglycaemic state)
  • Diabetic ketoacidosis (rare in T2DM but possible)
  • Rapidly deteriorating vision
  • Foot ulcer with signs of infection/ischaemia
  • Severe hypoglycaemia requiring assistance
