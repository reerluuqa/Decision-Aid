Diagnosis — Adults

Confirm if asymptomatic
  • Suspect T2DM with persistent hyperglycaemia + risk factors/symptoms (polyuria, polydipsia, weight loss, recurrent infections, tiredness, blurred vision; acanthosis nigricans).
  • Diagnostic thresholds: HbA1c ≥ 48 mmol/mol (6.5%); FPG ≥ 7.0 mmol/L; RPG ≥ 11.1 mmol/L with symptoms.
  • If asymptomatic, repeat the same test to confirm; note transient hyperglycaemia in acute illness.

Diagnosis — Children & Young People

Same‑day referral
  • Assume type 1 unless strong T2DM indicators (obesity, acanthosis, family/ethnic risk).
  • Thresholds: FPG ≥ 7.0 mmol/L; RPG ≥ 11.1 mmol/L with symptoms; HbA1c ≥ 48 mmol/mol.
  • Refer immediately the same day to a multidisciplinary paediatric diabetes team to confirm diagnosis & start care.

When not to use HbA1c for diagnosis / interpret with caution

Choose plasma glucose instead
  • Under 18 years; pregnancy or ≤2 months postpartum; symptoms < 2 months; acutely ill; on hyperglycaemia‑inducing meds (e.g., long‑term steroids); acute pancreatic damage; ESRD.
  • Caution with abnormal RBC turnover/haemoglobin (e.g., haemoglobinopathies incl. sickle trait), severe anaemia, altered RBC lifespan (post‑splenectomy), recent transfusion.

Self‑monitoring

Not routine
  • Do not routinely recommend SMBG.
  • Indications: insulin therapy; evidence of hypoglycaemia; starting steroids; suspected hypos; driving where SMBG is required by DVLA.

Stepwise Pharmacology (Adults)

Individualise by CV/renal status, weight, hypoglycaemia risk, preferences

1) First‑line

  • Metformin (standard‑release) and up‑titrate; switch to modified‑release if GI intolerance.
  • Assess CV status:
    • Established ASCVD / Heart failure: add SGLT2 inhibitor with CV benefit to metformin.
    • High CV risk: consider adding SGLT2 inhibitor.
  • If metformin contraindicated/intolerant:
    • ASCVD/HF/high risk → SGLT2 inhibitor.
    • Otherwise → choose DPP‑4 inhibitor, pioglitazone, or sulfonylurea.

2) If monotherapy inadequate

  • Add one of: DPP‑4 inhibitor, pioglitazone, sulfonylurea, or SGLT2 inhibitor (especially if SU contraindicated/not tolerated or high hypo risk).

3) If dual therapy inadequate

  • Triple therapy: add one of DPP‑4i / pioglitazone / SU / SGLT2i.
  • Specifics:
    • Canagliflozin or empagliflozin: with metformin + (DPP‑4i or SU or pioglitazone).
    • Dapagliflozin: with metformin + SU (not with pioglitazone).
    • Ertugliflozin: with metformin + DPP‑4i if SU/pioglitazone not appropriate.

4) GLP‑1 Receptor Agonist (switch one drug)

  • Consider if:
    • BMI ≥ 35 kg/m² (adjust for ethnicity) with obesity‑related issues, or
    • BMI < 35 and insulin has occupational implications, or weight loss would benefit obesity‑related comorbidities.
  • Continue only if ≥11 mmol/mol (1.0%) HbA1c reduction and ≥3% weight loss at 6 months.
  • If adding to insulin/SU, consider reducing insulin secretagogue dose to limit hypoglycaemia.
  • Stop DPP‑4 inhibitor when starting GLP‑1 RA (no added glycaemic benefit).

5) Insulin

  • Consider if symptomatic hyperglycaemia or failure of oral/GLP‑1 RA therapy; initiate with specialist support.
  • GLP‑1 RA + insulin only with consultant‑led team oversight.
Choose lowest acquisition‑cost options where clinically appropriate.
Match renal function & interactions (e.g., SGLT2 peri‑illness, metformin in AKI risk).

Lifestyle Advice (Adults)

Offer structured education (e.g., DESMOND)
  • Diet: high‑fibre, low‑GI carbs; oily fish; limit saturated/trans fat, salt, and sugary drinks; tailor carbs/alcohol & meal pattern; discourage “diabetic foods”.
  • Weight: initial goal 5–10% loss (smaller losses still beneficial).
  • Physical activity: regular exercise; minimise sedentary time; integrate with nutrition for weight/CV risk benefits.
  • Alcohol: stay within limits; carb snack before/after; delayed hypoglycaemia possible.
  • Smoking: cessation support; discuss complications risk.

Immunisation & Support

At/near diagnosis
  • Offer influenza & pneumococcal vaccination.
  • Provide contact route for diabetes team; signpost Diabetes UK resources.
  • Discuss contraception & pre‑pregnancy counselling where relevant.

Monitoring & Screening — Annual Care Processes

Coordinate at diagnosis and yearly
  • Eyes: diabetic retinopathy screening.
  • Feet: pulses, sensation, deformity, skin; risk classification & education.
  • Kidneys: UACR (first‑void AM preferred) and eGFR; confirm microalbuminuria with 2 further tests.
  • Neuropathy: ask for distal sensory symptoms; check for autonomic features if indicated.
  • BP: treat per HTN guideline; ACEi/ARB first‑line with CKD/albuminuria; manage resistant HTN per protocol.
  • Lipids: see CV risk section; aim >40% non‑HDL reduction.
  • Vaccines, psychosocial, education: refresh annually.

Lipid Modification

Statin first‑line
  • No established CVD: Atorvastatin 20 mg nocte if ≤84 yrs and QRISK10 ≥10%, or age ≥85 (consider comorbidity/frailty), or CKD.
  • Established CVD: Atorvastatin 80 mg nocte for secondary prevention.
  • Goal: >40% reduction in non‑HDL cholesterol; consider up‑titration if not achieved.

Antihypertensives & Antiplatelets

Safety first
  • Start/optimise per HTN guideline (ACEi/ARB pivotal with albuminuria/CKD). Escalate with CCB and thiazide‑like diuretic; confirm true resistance before 4th agent.
  • Do not offer routine aspirin/clopidogrel for primary prevention in T2DM.

Diabetic Ketoacidosis (DKA)

Emergency → admit
  • Consider with known diabetes or BG >11 mmol/L + polyuria/polydipsia, abdo pain, vomiting, Kussmaul breathing, dehydration, drowsiness.
  • Check ketones: blood > 3.0 mmol/L or urine ≥ 2+. Low ketones do not always exclude DKA.
  • Risk even with near‑normal glucose in insulin users and SGLT2 inhibitors (euglycaemic DKA).

Hyperosmolar Hyperglycaemic State (HHS)

Emergency → admit
  • Older, comorbid; prolonged severe hyperglycaemia (BG typically >30 mmol/L), profound dehydration, confusion/drowsiness.
  • Minimal/no ketosis on testing.
  • Precipitants: infection, missed insulin, acute illness, steroids/diuretics/atypical antipsychotics, physiological stress (surgery/trauma).

Sick‑day Rules (Adults)

Prevent AKI & DKA
  • Maintain hydration & carbohydrates; never stop insulin without specialist advice.
  • Pause metformin and SGLT2 inhibitors during acute illness, dehydration, or pre‑op; check blood ketones if unwell or eating/drinking poorly.
  • Increase glucose monitoring if on insulin or SU; seek urgent help for persistent vomiting, ketones, or reduced consciousness.

Children & Young People (12 months–17 years)

Specialist‑led care
  • Diagnosis: same thresholds as adults; assume T1DM unless strong T2DM features; same‑day referral to paediatric diabetes team.
  • Targets: HbA1c ≤48 mmol/mol (6.5%); measure every 3 months; individualise lowest safe target.
  • Therapy: paediatric team initiates; start metformin from diagnosis; insulin may be required.
  • Screening: annual retinopathy, foot assessment, kidney (UACR), BP (ABPM to confirm HTN), and lipids (TC, HDL, non‑HDL, TG; confirm on repeat).
  • Transition: anticipate HbA1c drift; address insulin resistance from puberty, erratic routines, and adherence; support education & lifestyle.

Resources & Notes

Use alongside local formulary
