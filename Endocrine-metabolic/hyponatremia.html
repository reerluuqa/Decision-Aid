Hyponatraemia Quick-Scan
Immediate Danger?

Symptoms of Cerebral Oedema:

  • Severe headache / Nausea / Vomiting
  • Altered mental status / Confusion
  • Seizures / Coma
  • Duration: Acute (<48 hours)
Stable or Chronic?

Patient Presentation:

  • Mild cognitive symptoms (confusion, gait issues)
  • Asymptomatic (incidental finding)
  • Duration: Chronic (≥48 hours) or Unknown

Definition

Serum sodium concentration <135 mmol/L.

Usually reflects excess total body water relative to sodium (Hypotonic Hyponatraemia).

Key Concept

Hyponatraemia is generally caused by an increase in renal water reabsorption due to Vasopressin (ADH) release combined with fluid intake.

Hypovolaemic

Loss of Volume

  • GI losses (Diarrhoea/Vomiting)
  • Third spacing (Pancreatitis)
  • Salt-wasting nephropathy
  • Diuretics (Thiazides)
Euvolaemic

Normal Volume

  • SIADH (Malignancy, CNS, Pulmonary)
  • Medications (SSRIs, Opioids)
  • Primary Polydipsia
  • Endocrine (Hypothyroid, Adrenal)
Hypervolaemic

Excess Volume

  • Heart Failure
  • Cirrhosis
  • Nephrotic Syndrome
  • Renal Failure

Urine Osmolality Guide
  • High (≥300) SIADH / Drug effects
    Inappropriate water retention.
  • Intermediate (150-300) Potomania / Partial SIADH
    Beer potomania, 'Tea and toast' diet, high fluid intake.
  • Low (≤100-150) Primary Polydipsia
    Excessive water intake, kidneys functioning correctly.
Acute Symptomatic Hyponatraemia

Target:

Raise serum sodium by 4-6 mmol/L in the first few hours to reverse cerebral oedema.


Regimen:

3% Hypertonic Saline (100ml bolus over 10 mins).

Repeat if symptoms persist.

WARNING: OVER-CORRECTION

Overly rapid correction can lead to Myelinolysis (OSM), a life-threatening complication.

  • Max correction limit: 8-10 mmol/L in 24h.
  • Monitor strictly.
  • If over-corrected: Give DDAVP (desmopressin) + Hypotonic fluids.
Chronic / Asymptomatic

Treat underlying cause.

Fluid Restriction

Usually <1L/day for SIADH.

Medication Review

Stop SSRIs, Thiazides, or other causative drugs.

Salt Tablets

Can be used with fluid restriction.

Hypovolaemic Treatment

Isotonic Saline (0.9% NaCl)

  • Restores intravascular volume.
  • Suppresses ADH release.
  • Allows kidneys to excrete excess water.

Hypervolaemic Treatment

  • Fluid restriction.
  • Loop diuretics (to promote water loss).
  • Salt tablets (with diuretics).
