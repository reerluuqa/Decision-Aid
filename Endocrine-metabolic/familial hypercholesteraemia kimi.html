üéØ When to Suspect FH

Suspect FH in adults with:
  • Total cholesterol > 7.5 mmol/L
  • Personal/family history of premature CHD (before 60 years)
Systematic screening for high-risk groups:
  • People < 30 years with total cholesterol > 7.5 mmol/L
  • People ‚â• 30 years with total cholesterol > 9.0 mmol/L
  • Family history of premature CHD with unknown cholesterol

üë∂ Children at Risk

Children (0-10 years) with one affected parent:
  • Offer DNA test at earliest opportunity
  • If not tested by age 10, offer additional opportunity
  • For homozygous FH risk: Measure LDL-C before age 5

üî¨ Clinical Assessment

Physical Examination:

  • Look for tendon xanthomata (hard, non-tender nodules on dorsum of hands, Achilles tendons)
  • Check for eyelid xanthelasma
  • Look for corneal arcus (before age 45 is significant)
  • Absence of signs does NOT exclude FH

Baseline Investigations:

  • Two measurements of LDL cholesterol
  • Homozygous FH suspected if:
    • Adults: LDL-C > 13 mmol/L
    • Children: LDL-C > 11 mmol/L

‚ö†Ô∏è Exclude Secondary Causes

Caution

Check the following before confirming FH diagnosis:

  • Conditions: Hypothyroidism, cholestatic liver disease, nephrotic syndrome, Cushing's syndrome
  • Drugs: Androgens, ciclosporin, thiazides, corticosteroids, beta-blockers
  • Lifestyle: Excess alcohol, uncontrolled diabetes

Recheck lipids after treating secondary causes

üöë Referral Requirements

URGENT REFERRAL

All patients with clinical diagnosis of FH (heterozygous or homozygous) must be referred to specialist for:

  • Confirmation of diagnosis
  • Initiation of cascade testing
  • Specialist management planning
Child & Adolescent Referral

Children <15 years should be referred to specialist with expertise in pediatric FH. If unavailable locally, refer to lipid/metabolic clinic. Diagnosis should be made by age 10.

Treatment & Management
‚ñº

üë®‚Äçü¶≥ Adults with Heterozygous FH (Age ‚â•15)

Very High Risk Referral

Refer to specialist if any of the following:

  • Established CHD
  • Family history of premature CHD (<60 years 1st degree, <50 years 2nd degree)
  • Two or more CVD risk factors (male, smoking, hypertension, diabetes)

Lipid-Modifying Treatment:

High-Intensity Statin (First-line)

Aim: >50% reduction in LDL-C from baseline

Options:

  • Atorvastatin 20 mg daily (preferred if predisposing factors for rhabdomyolysis)
  • Rosuvastatin 10 mg daily
Lifelong Treatment

Stepwise Approach:

Step 1: High-intensity statin
Step 2: If target not reached ‚Üí Add ezetimibe 10 mg
Step 3: If still not reached ‚Üí Refer for specialist options:
  • PCSK9 inhibitors (alirocumab/evolocumab)
  • Bempedoic acid + ezetimibe
  • Inclisiran (if established CVD and LDL-C ‚â•2.6 mmol/L)
  • LDL apheresis (exceptional cases)

Baseline Tests Before Treatment:

  • Creatine kinase (CK): Only if persistent muscle symptoms
  • Liver function tests: If abnormal, investigate cause
  • Renal function: eGFR for dose adjustment
  • HbA1c: Screen for diabetes
  • TSH: Exclude hypothyroidism

üß¨ Adults with Homozygous FH

Specialist Management Only

All patients must be managed in secondary care by FH specialist. Options include:

  • High-dose statins + ezetimibe
  • Bile acid sequestrants (resins)
  • Nicotinic acid/fibrates
  • LDL apheresis (regular removal of LDL)
  • Liver transplant (in severe cases)

üë∂ Children & Young People (Age 1 month - 15 years)

Pediatric Specialist Referral

All children with confirmed FH must be managed by specialist with pediatric FH expertise.

Management includes:
  • Lipid-modifying drugs started by age 10 (or earliest opportunity)
  • Routine monitoring of growth and pubertal development
  • Family counseling and cascade testing

ü•ó Lifestyle & Risk Factor Management

Essential for All Patients
  • Diet: Individualized nutritional advice from dietician
  • Stanols/Sterols: May be used consistently (not prescribable)
  • Omega-3: Not routinely recommended
  • Smoking cessation: Essential
  • Exercise: Regular physical activity
  • Weight management: Address obesity
  • Alcohol: Limit consumption
  • Blood pressure: Optimize control
  • Diabetes management: Optimize glycemic control

üíä Drug-Specific Information

Statins (Atorvastatin, Rosuvastatin, Simvastatin)

Contraindications: Active liver disease, transaminases >3x ULN, pregnancy/breastfeeding

Adverse Effects: Myalgia, elevated LFTs, rare myopathy/rhabdomyolysis

Key Interactions: Macrolides, azole antifungals, fusidic acid, grapefruit juice

Monitor LFTs at 3 months and 12 months
Ezetimibe

Indication: Add-on to statin or monotherapy if statin contraindicated

Adverse Effects: GI discomfort, headache, myalgia (rare)

Interactions: Ciclosporin, fibrates, warfarin

Bempedoic Acid

Indication: With ezetimibe if statins contraindicated/tolerated

Adverse Effects: Gout, hyperuricaemia, elevated LFTs

Inclisiran

Administration: Subcutaneous injection (284 mg at 0, 3 months, then every 6 months)

Indication: Established CVD with LDL-C ‚â•2.6 mmol/L despite max therapy

Adverse Effects: Injection site reactions (mild/moderate)

‚ñº

üìà Initial Follow-up (After Starting Treatment)

Timing: Measure lipids 2-3 months after starting treatment
Target: >50% reduction in baseline LDL-C

If target NOT reached:

  1. Discuss adherence and timing of dose
  2. Titrate statin to maximum licensed/tolerated dose
  3. Add ezetimibe 10 mg if on statin monotherapy
  4. Consider simvastatin 80 mg ONLY if:
    • Severe hypercholesterolaemia
    • High CVD risk
    • Benefits outweigh myopathy risk
  5. Refer to specialist if combined therapy fails

üóìÔ∏è Annual Structured Review

Minimum Annual Assessment

Components:

  • Medication adherence check
  • Adverse effects review (muscle symptoms, LFT changes)
  • Symptoms of CHD screening
  • Fasting lipids and blood pressure
  • Consider HbA1c and renal function
  • Lifestyle counseling (smoking, weight, diet)
  • Pregnancy/contraception advice (women of childbearing age)
ECG Consideration

Consider baseline ECG if not done at diagnosis. Low threshold for referral if CHD symptoms suspected.

üí™ Monitoring for Adverse Effects

Muscle Symptoms:

  • If muscle pain/tenderness/weakness: Check CK level
  • Stop statin immediately if:
    • CK >5x ULN
    • Muscle symptoms severe/intolerable
  • If previously tolerated >3 months: Look for other causes (vigorous exercise, hypothyroidism, infection, trauma)
  • Rechallenge: Can restart at lower dose once symptoms resolve

Liver Function:

  • Check LFTs at 3 months and 12 months
  • No further routine monitoring unless clinically indicated
  • Stop statin if transaminases persistently >3x ULN

üë©‚Äç‚öïÔ∏è Management of Adverse Effects

If Statin Not Tolerated:

Options:

  1. Stop and restart when symptoms resolve to confirm causality
  2. Switch to alternative high-intensity statin
  3. Try alternate day dosing
  4. Change to ezetimibe monotherapy
  5. Refer to specialist for consideration of:
    • Bile acid sequestrant (resin)
    • Fibrate

ü§∞ Pregnancy & Contraception

Teratogenic Risk

Women of childbearing age:

  • Adequate contraception required during treatment and for 1 month after
  • Stop lipid-lowering drugs 3 months before attempting conception
  • Some high-risk women may need continued treatment - specialist advice
  • Unplanned pregnancy: Stop treatment and refer urgently to obstetrician & cardiology
  • Breastfeeding: All lipid-lowering drugs contraindicated
