üë∂ Children & Young People (12 months - 17 years)

‚ö†Ô∏è Diabetic Ketoacidosis (DKA)

Suspect DKA if:
  • Hyperglycaemia: Blood glucose >11 mmol/L (may be normal in pump users)
  • Gastrointestinal: Nausea, vomiting, abdominal pain
  • Respiratory: Tachypnoea, deep sighing (Kussmaul) respiration
  • Dehydration: Dry mucous membranes, reduced skin turgor, oliguria
  • Neurological: Reduced consciousness, irritability, confusion
  • Precipitants: Infection, stress, insulin omission, drugs (steroids, antipsychotics)

ü©∏ Hypoglycaemia

Definition & Severity:
  • Alert value: 3.0-3.9 mmol/L
  • Clinically important: <3.0 mmol/L
  • Severe: Requires assistance for recovery
  • Nocturnal: Lethargy, altered mood, headache on waking
üéØ Key Assessment Points
  • Do not rule out type 1 diabetes based on BMI or age alone
  • Consider pancreatic cancer if age >60 with weight loss + new diabetes
  • Test ketones in ALL suspected cases
  • Same-day referral to specialist team if suspected

üë®‚Äç‚öïÔ∏è Adults: Diagnostic Process

1. Clinical Diagnosis
  • Based on: Hyperglycaemia + ketosis + typical features
  • Immediate same-day referral to specialist team
  • Diabetes specialist team confirms diagnosis & initiates care
2. Autoantibody Testing
  • Indication: Measure in all adults at diagnosis
  • Which antibodies: At minimum, test for 2 different types
  • Interpretation: At least one positive supports type 1 diagnosis
  • Timing: False negative rate lowest at diagnosis
3. C-peptide Testing
  • Do NOT routinely measure to confirm type 1 diabetes
  • Consider if: Clinical features consistent but autoantibodies negative
  • Type: Non-fasting serum C-peptide
  • Use: Resolve diagnostic uncertainty

üë∂ Children & Young People

Diagnostic Approach:
  • Assume type 1 diabetes unless strong indicators suggest otherwise
  • Same-day referral to paediatric diabetes specialist team
  • Specialist team confirms diagnosis & provides immediate care
Features Suggesting Type 2 Diabetes:
  • Strong family history of type 2 diabetes
  • Obesity
  • Black or Asian family origin
  • Evidence of insulin resistance (e.g., acanthosis nigricans)
Features Suggesting Other Types:
  • Age <12 months: Suggests neonatal diabetes
  • Autosomal dominant family history: Suggests monogenic diabetes
  • No ketonaemia during hyperglycaemia: Rare in type 1
  • Associated features: Optic atrophy, deafness, mitochondrial disease features
üéØ Diagnostic Priorities
  • Do not delay insulin therapy while awaiting test results
  • Autoantibody testing reduces misdiagnosis rate
  • Consider alternative diagnoses if presentation atypical
  • Specialist confirmation required for all cases

üö® Immediate Actions

At Diagnosis:
  • Immediate referral: Same-day to diabetes specialist team
  • Individual care plan: Developed by specialist team
  • Education: Basic survival skills (injection technique, glucose monitoring)
  • Specialist management: Insulin initiation & dose adjustment by trained professionals

üìö Structured Education

DAFNE Programme:
  • Timing: Offer 6-12 months after diagnosis (or when clinically appropriate)
  • Content: Carbohydrate counting, insulin dose adjustment
  • Format: Group sessions (alternative if unable/unwilling)
  • Goal: Enable self-management and normal eating

üçé Lifestyle Management

ü•ó Dietary Advice:
  • Healthy, balanced diet for optimal growth/weight
  • Carbohydrate counting education (essential for dose adjustment)
  • Structured education programme (DAFNE)
  • Refer to dietitian if overweight/obesity
üèÉ‚Äç‚ôÇÔ∏è Physical Activity:
  • Explain effects on blood glucose (may lower levels)
  • Monitor glucose before/after exercise
  • Carry carbohydrate for hypoglycaemia prevention
  • Adjust insulin/nutrition for prolonged activity
  • Avoid alcohol before/during exercise
üç∑ Alcohol Intake:
  • Safe limits education
  • Never drink on empty stomach
  • Eat carbohydrate before and after drinking
  • Regular glucose monitoring
  • Delayed hypoglycaemia risk (especially nocturnal)
  • Increased DKA risk with heavy consumption
  • Always carry diabetes identification
üö≠ Smoking & Substances:
  • Smoking cessation advice (risk factor for complications)
  • Reinforce annually for non-quitters
  • Advise young non-smokers never to start
  • Recreational drugs (cannabis, cocaine) increase DKA risk
ü¶∑ Oral Health:
  • Higher risk of periodontitis
  • Importance of good oral hygiene
  • Regular dental reviews
  • Periodontitis management improves glucose control

ü§í Intercurrent Illness ("Sick Day Rules")

üö® When to Seek Emergency Care
  • Moderate ketonuria (‚â•2+) or ketonaemia (1.5-2.9 mmol/L) with inability to eat/drink
  • Immediate DKA risk
  • No rapid improvement with insulin
  • Persistent vomiting >2 hours
  • Severe dehydration
If Managing at Home:
  • Never stop insulin: May need increased dose
  • Monitor frequently: Glucose every 1-2 hours (including overnight)
  • Check ketones: Every 3-4 hours (increase to 1-2 hours if elevated)
  • Urgent advice if: Urine ketones >2+ OR blood ketones >3 mmol/L
  • Maintain nutrition: Normal meals if possible; use carb drinks if poor appetite
  • Hydration: ‚â•3L fluid daily (water/carbs-free fluids if glucose high)
  • Hypoglycaemia treatment: Fast-acting carbs + carb drinks

üìà HbA1c Monitoring

Frequency:
  • Adults: Every 3-6 months (more often if control changing)
  • Children/Young People: Every 3 months (minimum 4x/year)
Targets:
  • Ideal target: ‚â§48 mmol/mol (minimizes complication risk)
  • Individualize: Based on age, activities, comorbidities, hypoglycaemia risk
  • Benefit: Any reduction reduces long-term complications

üëÅÔ∏è Diabetic Eye Disease

Adults:
  • At diagnosis: Immediate referral to diabetic eye screening service
  • Subsequent: Annual screening (12-24 month intervals if low risk)
  • Emergency ophthalmology review if: Sudden vision loss, rubeosis iridis, pre-retinal/vitreous haemorrhage, retinal detachment
Children/Young People:
  • Until age 12: Eye exam by optometrist every 2 years
  • From age 12: NHS diabetic eye screening programme annually

ü´ò Diabetic Kidney Disease

Screening Tests:
  • Urine ACR: Early morning sample annually
  • Serum creatinine & eGFR: Annually
  • Diagnose CKD if: eGFR <60 OR ACR >3 mg/mmol (persistent)
Management if Detected:
  • ACE inhibitor (or ARB) if hypertensive/albuminuric
  • Refer to renal specialist if indicated
  • Avoid high protein diet
  • Optimize glucose and BP control

ü¶∂ Diabetic Foot Problems

Risk Assessment (Annually):
  • Neuropathy: 10g monofilament testing
  • Ischaemia: Ankle brachial pressure index (ABPI)
  • Skin: Ulceration, callus, infection
  • Deformity: Including Charcot arthropathy
Risk Stratification:
  • Low Risk: No risk factors except callus alone
  • Moderate Risk: Neuropathy, ischaemia, or deformity alone
  • High Risk: Previous ulcer/amputation, neuropathy + ischaemia, on dialysis
  • Active Problem: Ulceration, infection, critical ischaemia, Charcot
Referral Pathways:
  • Moderate risk: Foot protection service (6-8 weeks)
  • High risk: Foot protection service (2-4 weeks)
  • Active problem: Multidisciplinary foot care service (1 working day)
  • Limb/life threatening: Immediate acute services

üß† Diabetic Neuropathy

Screening & Management:
  • Chronic painful neuropathy: Refer for treatment (see NICE neuropathic pain guideline)
  • Erectile dysfunction: Consider PDE-5 inhibitors
  • Autonomic neuropathy: Gastroparesis, bladder symptoms, orthostatic hypotension, excessive sweating
  • Refer: Gastroenterology/urology as appropriate

üßò Emotional & Psychological Wellbeing

Assessment at Each Visit:
  • Diabetes distress: Affects 20-40% (especially at diagnosis/complications)
  • Depression/anxiety: Twice as prevalent as general population
  • Eating disorders: Over-represented, especially young women ("diabulimia" - insulin omission for weight loss)
  • Children: Twice as likely to have psychiatric disorder
Management:
  • Screen for depression, anxiety, disordered eating
  • Offer referral to mental health services with diabetes expertise
  • Behavioural interventions: CBT, counselling
  • Support for carers/family members

  • Coeliac disease: Screen at diagnosis, repeat if symptoms
  • Addison's disease: If clinical features
