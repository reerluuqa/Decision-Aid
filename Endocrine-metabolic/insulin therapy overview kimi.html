EMERGENCY - Hypoglycaemia

Blood glucose <3.9 mmol/L

Immediate Action: Give 10-20g fast-acting carbohydrate
  • 3-6 glucose tablets
  • 90-180ml sugary drink
  • Recheck BG in 15 min

Red Flags - Refer Immediately

  • DKA suspected
  • Recurrent severe hypoglycaemia
  • Impaired hypoglycaemia awareness
  • Poor control despite optimal therapy
  • Injection site problems

Monitoring Requirements

Adults: Minimum 4x daily (before meals & bed)
Children: Minimum 5x daily

Increase frequency when:

  • Illness ('sick day rules')
  • Before driving
  • During exercise
  • Poor control

First-line Regimen

Adults: Multiple daily injections (basal-bolus)

Basal: Twice-daily detemir or once-daily glargine

Bolus: Rapid-acting analogue before meals

Key Prescribing Points

Do:

  • Prescribe by brand name
  • Provide insulin passport
  • Give patient information
  • Arrange specialist follow-up

Don't:

  • Change regimen without specialist input
  • Mix different insulin types
  • Use high-strength insulins in pumps
  • Ignore injection site problems
