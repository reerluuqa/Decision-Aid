Graves' Disease

Hyper
Red Flag Features
  • Orbitopathy (Eye signs)
  • Pretibial myxoedema
  • Acropachy
Diagnostic & Cause
Most common cause of hyperthyroidism in iodine-sufficient areas. Caused by TSH receptor antibodies.
Treatment Goal Normalise thyroid function parameters.

Toxic MNG & Adenoma

Hyper
Characteristics
Toxic MNG (Plummer's): Multiple autonomously functioning nodules.

Toxic Adenoma: Single benign monoclonal tumour.
Nodules function independently of TSH.
Risk Factors
Age >40, Iodine deficiency, Head/Neck irradiation.
Note: Non-functioning nodules in same goitre may be malignant.
Diagnosis
Radioisotope scan ("hot" area) + Biochemical hyperthyroidism.

Thyroiditis

Transient Hyper
Subacute (De Quervain's)
Key Feature: Thyroid PAIN.
Flu-like: Fever, myalgia, malaise.
High ESR/CRP. Low radioiodine uptake.
Painless (Lymphocytic)
Autoimmune inflammation. Transient hyperthyroidism → Hypothyroid phase → Recovery (usually). No pain.
Subacute Risks HLA-B*35 allele (70%), Viral infection.

Primary Hypothyroidism

Hypo
Diagnostic Path
TSH: Elevated (Most sensitive marker).
Cause: Autoimmune thyroiditis (Hashimoto's) is most common in iodine-sufficient areas.
Presentation
Weakness, lethargy, depression, mild weight gain. Non-specific symptoms.
Key Risk Factors
Female, Middle age, Family history, Autoimmune disorders, Down/Turner syndromes, Lithium/Amiodarone use.

Central Hypothyroidism

Rare
Diagnostic Trap
Do NOT rely on TSH being high.
Free T4 is LOW.
TSH can be Low, Normal, or Minimally Elevated.
Pathology
Anterior pituitary or hypothalamic hypofunction. <1% of cases.
Common cause: Pituitary adenomas.

Thyroid Nodules & Cancer

Oncology
Initial Action
Ultrasonography is the test of choice for structure/anatomy.
Goal: Differentiate benign vs malignant.
Context
Found in 50-60% of healthy people. Most are benign (colloid nodules).
Cancer risk in presenting patients: ~10-15%.
Warning Signs
Malignancy risk guided by clinical features (palpation, US findings) and genetic alterations (Papillary/Follicular/Hürthle cell).
