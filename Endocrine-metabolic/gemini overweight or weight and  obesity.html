Obesity Management Algorithm

A condensed guide for adults in primary care based on NICE CKS guidance, last revised May 2025.

1. Identification & Classification

At any appropriate clinical encounter (e.g., new registration, health check), follow these steps:

  1. Measure Height & Weight → Calculate Body Mass Index (BMI)
  2. Classify BMI (kg/m²)
Category Standard BMI Lower Thresholds*
Healthy Weight 18.5 - 24.9 n/a
Overweight 25.0 - 29.9 23.0 - 27.4
Obesity Class I 30.0 - 34.9 27.5 or more
Obesity Class II 35.0 - 39.9 Reduce thresholds by 2.5 kg/m²
Obesity Class III ≥ 40.0

*Use lower thresholds for individuals of South Asian, Chinese, other Asian, Middle Eastern, Black African, or African-Caribbean family backgrounds.

3. If BMI < 35, Measure Waist-to-Height Ratio (WHtR)

  • Healthy: 0.4 - 0.49 (Advise: "Keep your waist to less than half your height")
  • Increased Risk: 0.5 - 0.59
  • High Risk: ≥ 0.6

2. Initial Assessment & Goal Setting

For any patient classified as overweight or with increased/high-risk WHtR:

  • Ask permission to discuss weight in a sensitive, non-judgmental manner.
  • Assess for comorbidities (T2DM, hypertension, CVD, sleep apnoea, osteoarthritis, etc.) and underlying causes.
  • Explore the patient's views, cultural context, and barriers to weight management.
  • Agree on realistic, personalised goals, focusing on health and wellbeing, not just weight.

3. Management Interventions

3.1 Foundation for ALL Patients: Multicomponent Lifestyle Interventions

  • Behavioural Support: Encourage goal setting, self-monitoring, and problem-solving.
  • Dietary Advice: Advise a balanced, calorie-deficit diet tailored to preferences. Low-energy diets (800-1200 kcal/day) should only be part of a specialist multicomponent strategy.
  • Physical Activity: Advise building up to activity goals (UK Chief Medical Officer guidelines).
    • To prevent obesity: 45–60 minutes of moderate-intensity activity a day.
    • To prevent weight regain: 60–90 minutes of activity a day.

3.2 Consider Pharmacotherapy (as an adjunct to lifestyle changes)

Discuss with the patient if lifestyle changes are insufficient. All medicines for weight management should be used alongside a reduced-calorie diet and increased physical activity.

Drug Indication (Initial BMI) Prescribing Setting Key Stopping Rule
Orlistat ≥ 30 OR ≥ 28 + risk factors. Primary or Secondary Care Stop at 12 weeks if <5% weight loss.
Liraglutide (Saxenda®) ≥ 35 + pre-diabetes & high CVD risk. Specialist Service Only Re-evaluate if <5% weight loss at 6 months.
Semaglutide (Wegovy®) ≥ 35 + comorbidity OR 30-34.9 + meets specialist referral criteria. Specialist Service Only Re-evaluate if <5% weight loss at 6 months.
Tirzepatide (Mounjaro®) ≥ 35 + comorbidity. Primary or Specialist Care Re-evaluate if <5% weight loss at 6 months.

4. Bariatric Surgery Referral

Refer to a specialist multidisciplinary service for assessment.

Standard Referral

  • BMI ≥ 40
  • BMI 35 - 39.9 with a significant health condition (e.g., T2DM, hypertension) that could be improved by weight loss.

Expedited Assessment

  • BMI ≥ 35 with recent-onset T2DM (diagnosed within 10 years).
  • Consider for BMI 30 - 34.9 with recent-onset T2DM.
Important: For all surgery criteria, apply lower BMI thresholds (reduced by 2.5 kg/m²) for the at-risk ethnic groups mentioned in Step 1.

5. Monitoring & Follow-Up

  • Regularly review progress, health goals, and the effectiveness of interventions.
  • Reinforce lifestyle advice at every opportunity.
  • Provide support for long-term weight maintenance, especially after stopping medication or post-surgery.
  • Consider referral to Tier 2 (lifestyle weight management) or Tier 3 (specialist) services if complex needs arise or less intensive management is unsuccessful.

Prescribing Information

Orlistat

  • Dose: 120 mg with each of the three main meals per day.
  • Contraindications: Chronic malabsorption syndrome, cholestasis, breastfeeding.
  • Cautions: Chronic kidney disease (risk of oxalate nephropathy).
  • Adverse Effects: GI effects (oily spotting, flatulence, faecal urgency), headache. May impair absorption of fat-soluble vitamins (A, D, E, K).
  • Key Interactions: Ciclosporin (decreased levels), warfarin (monitor INR), antiepileptic drugs (risk of convulsions).

Tirzepatide (Mounjaro®)

  • Dose: Subcutaneous injection, starting at 2.5 mg once weekly and escalating every 4 weeks to a max of 15 mg.
  • Contraindications: Hypersensitivity to active substance or excipients.
  • Cautions: History of pancreatitis, severe GI disease, diabetic retinopathy.
  • Adverse Effects: Nausea, diarrhoea, vomiting, constipation (very common); dizziness, fatigue, injection site reactions.
  • Key Interactions: May affect absorption of other oral medications (e.g., paracetamol). Advise alternative/additional contraception for 4 weeks after starting and after each dose escalation.
