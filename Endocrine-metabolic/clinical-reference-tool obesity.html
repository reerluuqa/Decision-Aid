Drug Overview

Orlistat
Xenical, Alli (OTC)
Lipase Inhibitor

Pancreatic lipase inhibitor that reduces fat absorption by approximately 30%. Can be initiated in primary care.

Small benefits with high discontinuation rates due to GI side effects.

Dosing 120mg TID with meals
Weight Loss 3-5% vs placebo
Setting Primary Care
Liraglutide
Saxenda, Victoza (for diabetes)
GLP-1 Agonist

Daily injectable GLP-1 receptor agonist. Must be initiated in specialist weight management services.

Recommended for up to 2 years in specialist services. Stop after 12 weeks if <5% weight loss.

Dosing Daily injection
Weight Loss 6-8% vs placebo
Setting Specialist Only
Semaglutide
Wegovy, Ozempic, Rybelsus
GLP-1 Agonist

Weekly injectable GLP-1 agonist. Most effective GLP-1 for weight loss after tirzepatide. Reduces CV events in patients with established CVD.

NICE limit: 2 years maximum in specialist services only.

Dosing Weekly injection
Weight Loss 12% vs placebo
Setting Specialist Only
Tirzepatide
Mounjaro
GLP-1 + GIP Agonist

Dual GLP-1/GIP agonist. Most effective weight loss medication available (20% vs placebo). Can be prescribed in primary care per NICE 2024.

Phased NHS rollout starting June 2025 for highest need patients first.

Dosing Weekly injection
Weight Loss 20% vs placebo
Setting Primary/Specialist
Naltrexone-Bupropion
Contrave
NOT RECOMMENDED

Opioid antagonist + atypical antidepressant combination. Authorised in UK but NOT recommended by NICE due to poor cost-effectiveness.

Generally only available privately. Low prescribing rates.

Dosing Oral BID
Weight Loss 5-6% vs placebo
Setting Private Only

Eligibility Criteria (NICE NG246 2025)

General Requirements

  • BMI ≥30 (Obese)
    Or BMI ≥27 with ≥1 weight-related comorbidity
  • Failed to lose weight with lifestyle interventions alone
    Consider 4-9% weight loss from lifestyle as a benchmark
  • Committed to long-term treatment plan
    Including diet and lifestyle support alongside medication

Weight-Related Comorbidities

  • Type 2 Diabetes Mellitus
  • Hypertension
  • Established Cardiovascular Disease
  • Obstructive Sleep Apnoea
  • Dyslipidaemia

Red Flags & Contraindications

ABSOLUTE CONTRAINDICATIONS

  • Pregnancy: All weight management drugs are contraindicated. MHRA advises avoidance during pregnancy and breastfeeding due to lack of safety data.
  • Breastfeeding: Do not use any weight management medications while breastfeeding.
  • Trying to conceive: Discuss plans with all patients; recommend avoiding these medications.
  • Personal history of medullary thyroid carcinoma: GLP-1 agonists contraindicated
  • Multiple Endocrine Neoplasia syndrome type 2 (MEN2): Contraindication for GLP-1 based therapies

SERIOUS ADVERSE EFFECTS - MONITOR CLOSELY

  • Suicidal ideation: EMA reviewing ~150 reports. Monitor mood changes. Discontinue if suicidal ideation occurs.
  • Pancreatitis: Recognise signs. Can occur with rapid weight loss from any anti-obesity medication.
  • Gallstones: Increased risk with rapid weight loss. Educate patients on symptoms.
  • Thyroid cancer risk: Cohort studies show 31-58% increased incidence with GLP-1 use (3-4 years follow-up). Absolute risk remains low.
  • Aspiration during anaesthesia: MHRA 2025 warning - delayed gastric emptying persists despite fasting. Continue GLP-1s perioperatively per Association of Anaesthetists guidance.
  • Starvation ketoacidosis: Rare but serious, predominantly in female non-diabetic patients on tirzepatide.

Pregnancy Precautions

Stop Before Pregnancy

  • Semaglutide: Stop ≥2 months prior to conception
  • Tirzepatide: Stop ≥1 month prior to conception
  • Liraglutide: No washout required, but stop before pregnancy

MHRA alert June 2025: Avoid all GLP-1 medications during pregnancy and breastfeeding due to lack of safety data.

Pre-Conception Counseling

  • Discuss pregnancy plans at initial assessment and every 4-week review
  • Review need for additional contraceptive measures
  • For oral contraception: Tirzepatide may impact efficacy at initiation and dose increases - consider barrier methods for 4 weeks

All weight management drugs can cause harm to developing fetus based on animal studies.

Monitoring Timeline

● Baseline Assessment (Day 0)
Mandatory tests: Height, weight, BMI, waist circumference, blood pressure
Labs: FBC, renal function, liver function, lipids, HbA1c/fasting glucose
Review: Current medications (especially weight-gaining drugs), contraindications, pregnancy plans
● Every 4 Weeks During Titration
Assess: Injection technique, tolerability, GI adverse effects
Reinforce: Diet, physical activity, behavioural support
Record: Weight, BMI, waist-to-height ratio if BMI <35
Revisit: Pregnancy plans, contraceptive needs
● 12-Week Response Evaluation (Liraglutide Only)
STOP liraglutide if: <5% weight loss achieved after 12 weeks at 3mg/day
Document: Lack of response per licensed SPC requirements
● 6-Month Response Evaluation (Tirzepatide & Semaglutide)
Calculate: Percentage weight loss from baseline on highest tolerated dose
Discuss stopping if: <5% weight loss - risks now outweigh benefits
For T2DM: Check HbA1c every 3-6 months during adjustments
● Maintenance Phase
No specific NICE interval: Follow local weight management service protocols
Continue: Comorbidity management (adjust antihypertensives, antidiabetic agents as needed)
Long-term: Treatment typically available up to 2 years in specialist services

Common Side Effects & Management

Gastrointestinal Side Effects (All GLP-1 Drugs)
Nausea
Most common. Manage with smaller portions, reduced fat intake
Diarrhoea
Increase water and fibre intake. Usually transient
Vomiting
May indicate need for slower titration or dose reduction
Constipation
Increase water intake and dietary fibre. Consider laxatives if needed
Abdominal Pain
Assess for pancreatitis if severe or persistent
Orlistat-Specific Effects
Oily Faecal Spotting
Most common side effect. Use low-fat diet (<30% calories from fat)
Faecal Urgency
Usually resolves within 4 weeks. Keep spare clothing available
Steatorrhea
Fatty, foul-smelling stools. Reduce dietary fat intake
Supplement fat-soluble vitamins (A, D, E, K, beta-carotene) at bedtime to prevent deficiencies due to reduced absorption.
Injection Site & Drug Interaction Considerations
Injection Site Reactions
Rotate injection sites if soreness/pain occurs
Hypoglycaemia Risk
Monitor closely if combined with insulin or sulfonylureas. Consider dose reduction
Increased Heart Rate
Monitor in patients with CVD. Lower dose or discontinue if significant increase
