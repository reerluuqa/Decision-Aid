Quick Threshold Checker

Patient Total Cholesterol (TC) mmol/L
RED FLAG: Suspect FH Immediately
If Total Cholesterol > 7.5 mmol/L (Adult) OR Family history of premature CHD < 60yrs

Identification Criteria

>7.5
mmol/L TC (Adults)
>9.0
mmol/L TC (>30 yrs)

Also suspect if personal or 1st-degree relative history of CHD event < 60 years.

Paediatric Considerations

  • Age 0-10 (At risk): Refer for DNA test ASAP.
  • By Age 10: If testing not done, offer additional opportunity.
  • Homozygous Risk: Measure LDL < 5 years (if 2 affected parents or clinical signs).

Clinical Signs & Diagnosis

Signs: Tendon xanthomata (Achilles, hands), xanthelasma, corneal arcus < 45y. Note: Absence does not exclude FH.

Diagnostic Tools:

  • Simon Broome Criteria: 'Possible' or 'Definite' FH.
  • Dutch Lipid Clinic Network (DLCN): Score > 5.
  • Homozygous Suspect: LDL > 13 mmol/L (Adult) or > 11 mmol/L (Child).

Management: Next Steps

IMMEDIATE ACTION REQUIRED:

  • Refer ALL clinical FH cases to a Specialist.
  • Initiate Cascade Testing (DNA testing of relatives).
  • Manage children in secondary care (Child specialist).

Primary Care Goals (Heterozygous):

  • Lifestyle modification (Diet, Exercise).
  • Statin therapy (High intensity).
  • Annual structured review.

What is it?

Inherited condition causing high serum cholesterol from birth. Autosomal dominant pattern.

Prevalence

1 in 250

(Heterozygous UK Pop)

Medication Note
Statins 1st line. High intensity.
Ezetimibe Add-on if Statin not tolerated or insufficient.
Inclisiran For primary hypercholesterolaemia/mixed dyslipidaemia (NICE TA).
Bempedoic acid With ezetimibe (NICE TA).
Evinacumab Homozygous FH (12yrs+).

Exclude Secondary Causes:

  • Hypothyroidism
  • Cholestatic liver disease
  • Nephrotic syndrome
  • Diabetes / Obesity
  • Alcohol excess
  • Medications (e.g., Ciclosporin)

NICE Quality Standards:

  • LDL reduction > 50% from baseline.
  • Adults with TC > 7.5 assessed.
  • Relatives offered DNA testing.
  • Annual review offered.

Risk Stratification

People with FH are already at high risk. Do NOT use QRISK or Framingham.
