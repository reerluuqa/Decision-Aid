üíâ Injection Technique

  • Leave insulin at room temperature ‚â•30 minutes before injecting
  • Check expiry date and prime pen before each injection
  • Use new needle for each injection
  • Clean site; avoid alcohol wipes unless necessary
  • Pinch-up skinfold for children <6 years or BMI <19
  • Insert needle at 90¬∞ angle, quick insertion
  • Leave needle in skin for 15 seconds (longer for large doses)
  • Apply gentle pressure, do not rub

‚ö†Ô∏è Acute Hypoglycemia Management

Mild-Moderate Conscious, can swallow
Severe Impaired consciousness
  • Immediate treatment: 10-20g fast-acting carbohydrate (liquid preferred) Avoid chocolate/biscuits initially
  • Recheck glucose in 15 minutes; repeat if inadequate response
  • After recovery: 20g complex carbohydrate for maintenance
  • Severe: IM glucagon 1mg (>25kg) or 500mcg (<25kg) Train family/carers
  • Call 999 if no response in 10 minutes or glucagon unavailable
  • Monitor closely for recurrent hypoglycemia (vomiting common)

üíä Adverse Effect Management

  • Painful injections: Numb with ice, use room-temp insulin, new needle, shorter needle
  • Bleeding/bruising: Gentle pressure, review technique, avoid intramuscular injection
  • Local reactions: Usually resolve in days-weeks; consider switching to analogue if persistent
  • Insulin leakage: Leave needle longer, slower withdrawal, pressure on site
  • Altered vision: Temporary during initiation, avoid new glasses until stable
  • Acute painful neuropathy: Self-limiting; simple analgesia first-line
  • Insulin edema: Rare, resolves in 3-4 days; seek specialist advice if persistent

‚ö†Ô∏è Critical Safety Warnings

  • NEVER withdraw insulin from pen devices with syringes Risk of fatal overdose
  • Use devices only as recommended by specialist team
  • Do not change regimen without specialist advice
  • Always use insulin syringes with U100 insulin only
  • High-strength insulins require careful dose verification
  • Contact specialist urgently if unable to use pen device

üöó Driving Safety

Legal Requirement Notify DVLA and insurance company
  • Check glucose ‚â•5 mmol/L before driving
  • Check every 2 hours during long journeys
  • Carry fast-acting carbohydrate in vehicle
  • If hypoglycemic: stop, switch off engine, treat, wait 45 min before resuming
  • Take regular meals/snacks on long journeys
  • Always carry diabetes identification
  • Review DVLA guidance for Group 1/2 entitlements

‚úàÔ∏è Travel & Holidays

  • Carry insulin in hand luggage (climate-controlled)
  • Pack spare supplies in separate bag
  • Obtain travel documentation from specialist team
  • Adjust insulin for time zone changes (>4 hours difference)
  • Obtain comprehensive travel insurance covering diabetes
  • Carry emergency treatment for hypoglycemia
  • Research local healthcare facilities at destination
  • Consider impact of heat, altitude, activity changes

üïå Religious Fasting (Ramadan)

Children Strongly advise against fasting
  • Provide pre-fasting education on dose adjustment
  • Ensure good hypoglycemia awareness before fasting
  • Recommend frequent glucose monitoring during fast
  • Advise breaking fast immediately if hypoglycemia occurs
  • Counsel on nutrition: low-GI foods, limit sweets/fried foods
  • Encourage hydration during non-fasting hours
  • Avoid rigorous exercise before Iftar
  • Seek specialist advice for regimen adjustments

üíº Employment & Shift Work

  • Provide education on insulin dose adjustment for shift changes
  • Advise bringing food/snacks from home
  • Recommend preparing meals in advance
  • Suggest eating main meal before shift starts
  • Counsel on reduced caffeine intake
  • Encourage discussing adjustments with employer/occupational health
  • Refer to specialist team for structured shift work education if needed

üõ°Ô∏è Insurance & Documentation

  • Declare diabetes and insulin treatment for life/loan insurance
  • Ensure travel insurance covers type 1 diabetes
  • Provide documentation for customs/security when traveling
