Gastroparesis

BMJ Best Practice Quick-Scan

Immediate Action Required (Red Flags)

Hospital admission for severe dehydration, electrolyte abnormalities, or uncontrollable vomiting. Correct hyperglycemia (delays emptying). EXCLUDE mechanical obstruction (OGD) before treating as gastroparesis.

Definition

Objectively delayed emptying of solids by the stomach in the absence of any mechanical obstruction.

PREVALENCE

~267.7 per 100,000 adults

Female predominance (4:1).

COMMON CAUSES

  • Diabetes: 57.4% (Type 1 & 2)
  • Idiopathic: 11.3%
  • Post-Surgical: 15%
  • Drug-induced: 11.8% (Opioids, etc.)
Cardinal Symptoms
Early Satiety
Postprandial Fullness
Nausea
Vomiting
Bloating
Epigastric Pain
Exam Sign: Succussion splash (uncommon).
Risk Factors
  • Diabetes Mellitus (esp. Type 1)
  • Previous gastric/pancreatic surgery
  • Female Sex
  • Scleroderma / Parkinson's / MS
  • Opioid / TCA use
Diagnostic Approach
  • 1
    Rule out Organic Disorder & Obstruction FBC, Metabolic Panel, Amylase/Lipase, TSH, HbA1c. Perform OGD to exclude pyloric stenosis/neoplasia.
  • 2
    Gold Standard Test: Gastric Emptying Scintigraphy Solid-phase meal (250kcal). Stop prokinetics 48h prior.
    Diagnostic Criteria: >10% retention at 4 hours OR >60% retention at 2 hours.
  • 3
    Alternative Tests • 13C-GEBT (Breath Test): t1/2 ≥ 79 mins is abnormal.
    • Wireless Motility Capsule: Gastric emptying time >5 hours.
Initial Labs
Test Significance
HbA1c Check diabetes control (≥6.5%)
K+ Low if prolonged vomiting
Glucose >16 mmol/L delays emptying
Amylase/Lipase Exclude pancreatitis
TSH R/o Hypothyroidism

Treatment Goals

1. Correct fluid/electrolyte deficits & hyperglycemia.
2. Nutritional maintenance.
3. Symptom reduction.

Dietary Changes
  • Small, frequent meals (4-6/day)
  • Low insoluble fibre (bezoar risk)
  • Low fat diet
  • Liquidised or high-calorie liquids
Pharmacological Management
Metoclopramide (1st Line) Extrapyramidal Risk

Dopamine antagonist / Serotonin agonist. Improves emptying & nausea.

⚠️ WARNING: Risk of tardive dyskinesia. Limit to 12 weeks (US) or 5 days (Europe). Use drug holidays.
Domperidone (Alternative) Cardiac Risk

Dopamine antagonist. Does not cross BBB (fewer CNS effects).

⚠️ WARNING: QT prolongation risk. Max 30mg/day. Contraindicated in cardiac disease/hepatic impairment.
Erythromycin (Acute/Rescue) Short Term Only

Motilin receptor agonist. Potent stimulant.

Note: Tachyphylaxis develops in days. Limit use to 4 weeks.
Anti-emetics (Adjunct)

Ondansetron (5-HT3), Diphenhydramine (H1). Used for symptom relief; do not improve emptying.

Key Differentials
Condition Differentiating Feature Test Result
Gastric Outlet Obstruction Mechanical cause (pyloric stenosis, cancer) Delayed Emptying + Mechanical finding on OGD/CT
Functional Dyspepsia Similar symptoms, usually less severe vomiting Normal Scintigraphy
Cyclic Vomiting Syndrome Intermittent episodes vs chronic symptoms Normal Scintigraphy
Rumination Syndrome Regurgitation minutes after eating, no nausea Normal Scintigraphy
Acute Pancreatitis Sudden pain radiating to back, fever Elevated Amylase/Lipase
Superior Mesenteric Artery Syndrome Compression of duodenum; pain worse supine CT Angiography: Narrowed aorto-mesenteric angle
