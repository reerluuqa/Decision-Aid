Context

Updated: April 2025

Key Change: Gliflozins (SGLT2i) now 1st line alongside Metformin for high CVD risk.

‚ö†Ô∏è Immediate Actions

  • Symptomatic Hyperglycaemia: Consider 'Rescue Therapy' with Insulin or Sulphonylurea immediately.
  • Severe Hypoglycaemia: Defined as impaired consciousness or requiring medical help. Doubles long-term CVD risk.
  • DKA Risk: Gliflozins carry risk (2 more/1000 over 5y). Ensure Sick Day Rules are understood.

Targets (NICE 2022)

Lifestyle / Non-hypo drug 48 mmol/mol (6.5%)
Hypo-inducing drug (SU/Insulin) 53 mmol/mol (7.0%)
Intensify if HbA1c > 58 mmol/mol (7.5%)

*De-intensify if HbA1c < 48. Always personalise.

Driving (DVLA Summary)

Insulin / Sulphonylureas:

  • Must not have >1 severe hypo in last 12m (and none in last 3m).
  • Check glucose < 2hrs before driving & every 2 hrs.

Non-hypo agents:

  • Group 1: Drive as normal.
  • Group 2: "Regular medical review".

How Drugs Work (Cheat Sheet)

Metformin

Mnemonic: Sucks sugar out of blood into liver, muscle, fat. Good for heart.

Gliclazide (SU)

Mnemonic: Kicks pancreas to make more insulin. Watch out for Hypos.

GLP-1 Mimetics

Mnemonic: Mimic incretins (gut hormones). Augment insulin, slow stomach emptying (weight loss).

Gliflozin (SGLT2i)

Mnemonic: Makes you pee out excess sugar. Great for Heart Failure & Renal protection.

Specific Populations

COPD: GLP-1s reduce exacerbations (admissions). Gliflozins reduce admissions but not moderate exacerbations.

Renal: Gliflozins prevent ‚â•40% fall in eGFR. Check eGFR thresholds before starting.

Therapy Pathway (NICE/SIGN)

1st Line Strategy

Standard: Metformin.
High CVD Risk: Metformin + Gliflozin.
Established CVD: Offer Gliflozin.
Note: Gliflozins can be added at ANY stage if CVD/risk factors develop.

Second Line (After Metformin +/- Gliflozin)

Consider patient factors (frailty, weight, renal, hypo risk).

  • Priority: Gliflozin (SGLT2i)
    Best outcomes: Lower HbA1c, lower BMI, lower BP, fewer HF admissions.
  • Alternative: Gliptin (DPP-4i)
    Pros: Weak but safe (no hypos).
    Cons: Least effective if baseline HbA1c is high.
  • Alternative: Sulphonylurea
    Cons: High hypo risk, weight gain. Use with caution.
  • Specialist Use: GLP-1
    Indication: High BMI (BMI ‚â•35 with comorbidities, or ‚â•30).

Safety, Dilemmas & Special Topics

Metformin & B12

  • Risk of deficiency: 1 in 10.
  • Action: Check B12 if symptoms/signs present.
  • Pragmatic Tip: Add B12 to annual diabetes bloods for those on Metformin.

Sick Day Rules (Gliflozins)

  • Stop Gliflozins if acute illness/dehydration.
  • Risk of euglycaemic DKA.
  • Do not restart until eating/drinking normally for 24-48h.

Drug Dilemmas

Can we combine Gliptin + Sulphonylurea?
Caution: Significant increase in hypoglycaemia (1 extra case per 17 people in first 6 months). BNF recommends reducing SU dose when starting gliptin.
Can we combine Gliflozin + GLP-1?
Yes (beneficial): Dual therapy shows summative benefits. Fewer CV events and renal events compared to monotherapy. (Note: NICE currently limits GLP-1s for obesity/cost, but evidence supports combo).
GLP-1s and Suicidality?
Reassuring: Large cohort studies found NO increased risk of suicide vs other diabetes drugs. Some even showed protective effects.
Gastric Emptying (Rapid vs Delayed)
Emerging Concept: 20% of poorly controlled diabetics have rapid gastric emptying (not just delayed). Rapid emptying worsens post-prandial hyperglycaemia. Treating delayed emptying might worsen sugar control.

Monitoring Guidelines

Who needs Blood Sugar Monitoring?

Monitoring Type Offer To...
Capillary (Finger prick) - On Insulin
- History of hypoglycaemia
- On Steroids
- DVLA requirements
Continuous (Flash/rtCGM) - On Multiple Daily Injections (MDI) + recurrent/severe hypos
- Impaired hypo awareness
- Disability preventing finger pricks

*For all others, routine self-monitoring is NOT indicated (high quality RCTs show no HbA1c difference).

Group 2 (HGV/LGV)
