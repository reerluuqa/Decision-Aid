Type 2 Diabetes: A Clinical Algorithm for Primary Care

A one-page reference for fast decision-making during patient consultations.

1. Diagnosis & Initial Management

Suspect T2DM If:

  • Symptoms: Polydipsia, polyuria, weight loss, blurred vision.
  • Risk Factors: Obesity, family history, ethnicity, history of gestational diabetes.

Confirm Diagnosis:

  • HbA1c ≥ 48 mmol/mol (6.5%)
  • Fasting Glucose ≥ 7.0 mmol/L
  • Random Glucose ≥ 11.1 mmol/L (with symptoms)

If asymptomatic, two separate abnormal readings are required.

Immediate Actions:

  1. Refer to Structured Education (e.g., DESMOND).
  2. Emphasise Lifestyle Modification (Diet, exercise, weight loss).
  3. Set Initial HbA1c Target:
    • 48 mmol/mol (6.5%) for lifestyle/Metformin only.
    • 53 mmol/mol (7.0%) if on drugs causing hypoglycaemia.

2. Pharmacological Treatment Algorithm

Step 1: First-Line Therapy

Offer Standard-Release Metformin.

If contraindicated, consider DPP-4i, Pioglitazone, Sulfonylurea, or SGLT-2i.

↓

Step 2: Assess CV & Renal Risk

  • Established ASCVD / Heart Failure? → Add SGLT-2 inhibitor
  • High ASCVD Risk? → Consider adding SGLT-2 inhibitor
  • CKD? → Add SGLT-2 inhibitor (if eGFR criteria met)
↓

Step 3: Dual Therapy

If HbA1c ≥ 58 mmol/mol, aim for 53 mmol/mol. Add one:

DPP-4i Pioglitazone Sulfonylurea SGLT-2i
↓

Step 4: Triple Therapy

If dual therapy fails, add a third agent from the list above OR consider starting insulin.

↓

Step 5: Further Intensification

If triple therapy fails, consider:

  • GLP-1 RA (if BMI criteria met)
  • Start or intensify insulin therapy.

3. Annual Review: The Circle of Care

HbA1c

6-monthly when stable

Blood Pressure

Target <140/90 mmHg

Kidney Function

Check eGFR & ACR

CVD Risk

Atorvastatin 20mg (Primary) / 80mg (Secondary)

Eye Screening

Refer for Retinopathy Screen

Foot Care

Examine pulses & sensation

4. Key Management Considerations

Sick Day Rules (SADMANS)

During acute illness, advise patients to temporarily STOP:

  • S - SGLT-2 inhibitors
  • A - ACE inhibitors / ARBs
  • D - Diuretics
  • M - Metformin
  • A - Angiotensin II receptor blockers (ARBs)
  • N - NSAIDs
  • S - Sulfonylureas (if not eating)

Key Advice: Maintain fluids, monitor glucose. NEVER stop insulin.

Hyperglycaemic Emergencies

Suspect DKA/HHS in any unwell diabetic patient. Act fast.

  • DKA: Ketones present, can occur with near-normal glucose (esp. with SGLT-2i).
  • HHS: Severe hyperglycemia (>30 mmol/L), dehydration, confusion, no significant ketones.
EMERGENCY HOSPITAL ADMISSION

Hypoglycaemia (<4.0 mmol/L)

Main Risk: Insulin and Sulfonylureas.

Treatment:

  1. Give 15-20g of fast-acting carbohydrate.
  2. Re-check blood glucose in 15 minutes.
  3. Repeat if necessary.
