Key Diagnostic Factors

Common Symptoms

Fatigue/Lethargy
Anorexia
Weight Loss
Nausea/Vomiting
Salt Craving
Dizziness

Hyperpigmentation

The hallmark sign. Look for:

  • Mucous membranes (buccal)
  • Palmar creases
  • Scars / Pressure points
  • Sun-exposed skin

Postural Hypotension

Due to mineralocorticoid deficiency (salt loss).
Check BP: Supine vs. Sitting.

Hair Loss (Women)

Loss of axillary and pubic hair due to adrenal androgen deficiency.

Aetiology & Risk Factors

Autoimmune (80-90%)

Antibodies against 21-hydroxylase. Associated with other autoimmune diseases.

Hashimoto's Type 1 DM Vitiligo

Infectious

  • Tuberculosis (Endemic areas)
  • HIV / CMV / Crypto
  • Septicaemia (Meningococcus)

Haemorrhagic/Infarction

Antiphospholipid syndrome, Heparin therapy, Sepsis. Presents with flank pain.

Drug Induced

Etomidate, Ketoconazole, Mitotane, Rifampicin, Phenytoin.

Metastatic

Lung, Breast, Kidney, Melanoma. (Rare cause of clinical AI).

Management Overview

Replacement Therapy (Lifelong)

Glucocorticoid (Hydrocortisone) + Mineralocorticoid (Fludrocortisone).

Patient Education

Medical alert bracelet required. Dose increase during infection/stress/surgery.

Follow-up

Monitor for over-replacement (Cushingoid features, Hypertension).
