Insulin Rx – Type 2 DM Quick-Scan

Red Flags / Emergencies

  • DKA / HHS: BG >20 mmol/L, ketones >3, pH <7.3
  • Severe hypoglycaemia (<3.0) – unable to self-treat
  • Recurrent nocturnal hypoglycaemia
  • Vision loss / severe oedema after starting insulin

When to Start / Switch

  • Symptomatic hyperglycaemia (polyuria, polydipsia, weight loss)
  • HbA1c >58 mmol/mol on max oral / GLP-1 Rx
  • Contraindication to oral agents
  • Young / high complication risk

1st-line Regimen (Adult)

Basal only: NPH bedtime or QD (or glargine/detemir if ↑ hypo risk, carer injection, or QD preference).

Continue metformin; stop sulfonylurea if hypo risk.

Hypoglycaemia Rx

  • Rule of 15: 15–20 g fast CHO → recheck 15 min → repeat if <3.9
  • Once normal, give long-acting CHO (bread / biscuit)
  • Severe / unconscious: IM glucagon 1 mg → call 999

Insulin Types & Kinetics

Type Onset Peak Duration
Rapid (aspart/lispro) 5–15 min 30–90 min 3–5 h
Short (soluble) 30 min 2–3 h 8 h
NPH 1–2 h 4–12 h 16–24 h
Glargine / Detemir 1 h None 24 h
Degludec / U300 1 h None >24 h

Injection Technique

Needle: 4 mm adult/child; lift skinfold only if thin.

Site rotation: abdomen > thigh > buttock; avoid lumps.

Storage: fridge 2–8 °C; in-use vial/pen 28 days room temp.

Air-shot: prime 2 units before every dose.

Driving & Travel

  • DVLA: group 1 licence – no >1 severe hypo requiring help/12 mo
  • Carry fast CHO in vehicle; test <2 h pre-drive and every 2 h
  • Airport: letter for needles/pens; hand-luggage only

Ramadan / Fasting

  • Generally not recommended if poor control / complications
  • Use basal analogue at sunset; omit daytime short-acting
  • Break fast if BG <3.9 or >16.7
