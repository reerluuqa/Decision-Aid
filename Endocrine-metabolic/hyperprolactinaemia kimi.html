When to Suspect Prolactinoma

Microprolactinoma

<10mm diameter, PRL 100-250 ng/mL

Low Risk

Macroprolactinoma

>10mm diameter, PRL >250 ng/mL

High Risk - Urgent MRI

Giant Prolactinoma

>40mm diameter, PRL >1000 ng/mL

Emergency Referral
Warning: Pituitary apoplexy is a medical emergency. Sudden severe headache, visual loss, or meningismus requires immediate evaluation.
Hook Effect: Extremely high prolactin levels may read falsely low. If discrepancy between tumor size and prolactin level, request 1:100 dilution.

Medical Management

Dopamine Agonists

First-line therapy for prolactinomas

  • Cabergoline: 0.25-1 mg twice weekly
  • Bromocriptine: 2.5-7.5 mg daily
  • Quinagolide: 75-150 mcg daily
First Choice

Drug-Induced Hyperprolactinemia

Consider medication change if possible

  • Switch to prolactin-sparing antipsychotics (aripiprazole, quetiapine)
  • Reduce dose if clinically feasible
  • Add dopamine agonist if necessary
Consult Psychiatrist

Medication Resistance

Mass Effect

Patient Preference

Intolerance to dopamine agonists

Discuss Options
Pregnancy Considerations: Bromocriptine is preferred for fertility. Discontinue dopamine agonists once pregnancy confirmed unless tumor enlargement risk is high.
Treatment Goals: Normalize prolactin, restore gonadal function, reduce tumor size, prevent long-term complications (osteoporosis, cardiovascular risk).

Follow-up & Monitoring

Monitoring Schedule

Initial Phase (First Year)

Every 3-6 months

  • Prolactin levels
  • Symptom assessment
  • Tumor size (MRI at 6-12 months)
  • Side effect monitoring
Close Monitoring

Stable Phase (After Control)

Every 6-12 months

  • Annual prolactin
  • Biennial MRI if indicated
  • Osteoporosis screening
  • Cardiovascular risk assessment
Routine Follow-up

Long-term Remission

Consider gradual taper

  • After 2+ years of normal prolactin
  • No visible tumor on MRI
  • Gradual dose reduction
  • Monitor for recurrence

Long-term Outcomes

Success Rates

Medical Therapy

80-90% achieve normal prolactin

High Success Rate

Surgical Cure

60-80% for microadenomas, 30-50% for macroadenomas

Variable Success

Recurrence Risk

20-30% after dopamine agonist discontinuation

Monitor Closely
Patient Education: Inform patients about the chronic nature of hyperprolactinemia, importance of medication adherence, and need for regular monitoring.
Complications to Monitor: Osteoporosis, cardiovascular disease, tumor progression, medication side effects (nausea, orthostatic hypotension, psychiatric symptoms).
