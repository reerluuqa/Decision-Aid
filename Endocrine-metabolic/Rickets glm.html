üíä Calcium-Deficient Rickets
1

Vitamin D Deficiency (Most Common)

Correct deficiency & ensure Calcium intake.

Calcium: 45-65 mg/kg/day PO (divided).
Vit D3 (Colecalciferol): Prefered over D2.
Alt: Stoss therapy (Single high dose IM/PO).
2

Dietary Calcium Deficiency

Oral Calcium + Vit D2 at recommended daily values.

3

Pseudovitamin D Deficiency (Type I)

Defect in 1-alpha-hydroxylase.

Calcitriol: Physiological dose (Specialist guidance).
Goal: Normalize Ca, P, ALP.
üß¨ Hypophosphataemic Rickets
Note: Caused by renal phosphate wasting (X-linked most common). Treat with specialist.
1

X-linked / Autosomal Forms

Combination therapy needed.

Phosphate Salts: Replace renal losses.
Calcitriol: Counteract GI Ca absorption inhibition.
*Monitor for Nephrocalcinosis.
2

Refractory Cases (XLH)

If conventional therapy fails or complications arise.

Burosumab: Anti-FGF23 monoclonal antibody.
Route: Subcutaneous (every 2 weeks).
Age: >6 months (US) / >1 yr (EU).
3

Tumour-Induced

Primary: Resect tumour.

Calcitriol +/- Phosphate Salts if resection incomplete/metastatic.
