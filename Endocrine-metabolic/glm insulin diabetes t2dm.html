Insulin Therapy in Type 2 Diabetes

Clinical Algorithm & Quick Reference Guide for Healthcare Professionals

When to Consider Insulin Therapy

Indications

Poor glucose control + symptoms
Max oral agents failed
High complication risk
Oral agents contraindicated

Barriers to Consider

Obesity
Physical comorbidities
Psychological issues
Personal preference

Insulin Regimen Selection

1
Start with NPH insulin (once or twice daily) - FIRST LINE
2
If HbA1c ≥75 mmol/mol (9.0%): Add short-acting insulin (separate or premixed)
3
Consider alternatives to NPH if:
  • Needs injection assistance (long-acting analogues reduce frequency)
  • Recurrent symptomatic hypoglycaemia
  • Would otherwise need twice-daily NPH + oral agents
4
Consider premixed analogue insulins if:
  • Prefers injecting immediately before meals
  • Hypoglycaemia is problematic
  • Marked postprandial glucose rise

Insulin Types & Profiles

Type Onset Peak Duration Examples
Rapid-acting analogues 5-15 min 1-2 hr 2-5 hr Humalog®, Novorapid®
Soluble (short-acting) 30-60 min 2-4 hr Up to 8 hr Humulin S®
Intermediate (NPH) 1-2 hr 4-12 hr 16-35 hr Humulin I®
Long-acting analogues 1-2 hr Minimal 20-24 hr Lantus®, Levemir®
Ultra-long-acting 1-2 hr Minimal >24 hr Tresiba®, Toujeo®

Monitoring & Assessment

Self-Monitoring

  • Educate at initiation
  • Assess skills annually
  • Review equipment and technique
  • Evaluate impact on quality of life

Key Review Points

  • Injection sites rotation
  • Adherence to therapy
  • Lifestyle factors
  • Psychosocial issues

Managing Poor Glucose Control

1
Check adherence (common cause of hyperglycaemia)
2
Review injection technique and sites
3
Assess self-monitoring skills
4
Evaluate knowledge of sick-day rules
5
Consider lifestyle factors (diet, exercise, alcohol)
6
Address psychosocial issues
7
Rule out other conditions (CKD, gastroparesis, etc.)

Hypoglycaemia Management

Recognition

Sweating
Tremor
Hunger
Confusion

Acute Management

15-20g fast-acting carbohydrate → recheck in 15 min

Important: Always carry fast-acting glucose source

Prevention

  • Regular meals
  • Consistent carbohydrate intake
  • Appropriate dosing
  • Alcohol moderation

Practical Advice for Patients

Storage

  • Unopened: 2-8°C
  • In use: Room temperature (usually 28 days)
  • Protect from sunlight and heat
  • Never freeze insulin

Injection Technique

  • Rotate injection sites
  • Leave needle in 5-10 seconds
  • Use new needle each time
  • Check insulin appearance before use

Travel & Driving

  • Carry extra supplies
  • Adjust for time zones
  • Check glucose before driving
  • Carry fast-acting carbohydrate
