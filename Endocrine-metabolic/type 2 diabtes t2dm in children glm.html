CRITICAL ALERT: Do not assume T2DM is "mild". DKA and HHS are life-threatening.

Diabetic Ketoacidosis (DKA)

Incidence: 5% - 25% at presentation.

  • Vomiting / Abdominal pain
  • Rapid deterioration
  • Ketonuria present
  • Polyuria & Polydipsia
ACTION: Admit for rehydration & insulin therapy.

Hyperosmolar Hyperglycaemic State (HHS)

Incidence: ‚â§ 2% (Rare but high mortality).

  • Severe Hyperglycaemia
    > 33.3 mmol/L (600 mg/dL)
  • Severe dehydration
  • Little or no ketonuria
  • Altered mental status
ACTION: Medical Emergency. Fluid resuscitation critical.

üß¨ Risk Factors

Key Clinical Profile

Most children with T2DM share a specific cluster of risk factors.

Strong Risk Factors
Obesity (BMI >95%)
High Risk Ethnicity
Family History (1st/2nd degree)
Signs of Insulin Resistance

*90% of children have overweight/obesity.
*Ethnicity: Black, Hispanic, American-Indian, Asian/Pacific Islander.

Supporting Factors
Puberty
Female Sex (64% cases)
Diabetic In-Utero Environment
PCOS

*Puberty increases insulin resistance via growth hormone surge.

üëÅÔ∏è Physical Exam Signs

Acanthosis Nigricans

Present in 90% - 95% of patients. Cutaneous marker of insulin resistance.

Locations: Neck folds, axilla, intertriginous areas.

Appearance: Velvety, hyper-pigmented, thickened skin.

Other Common Findings

  • Hypertension: Frequent comorbidity.
  • Yeast Infections: Vaginal, penile, skin folds.
  • Skin Infections: Cellulitis, abscesses.
  • Obstructive Sleep Apnoea: Snoring, daytime fatigue.
Screening Rec:
Check BP, Lipids, LFTs (MASLD), Eye Exam, Urine ACR at diagnosis.
üíä Management Goals

Treatment Targets HbA1c < 48

Goal: Normalise glycaemia & HbA1c, prevent comorbidities.

  • HbA1c: < 48 mmol/mol (< 6.5%)
  • Weight: Promote weight loss & exercise.
  • BP: Age-appropriate norms.

First-Line Therapy
