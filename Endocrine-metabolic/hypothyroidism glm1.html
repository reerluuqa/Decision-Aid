Hypothyroidism

Adults 18+ (NICE CKS)
Rev: Nov 2024
Guideline: [NICE 2023]

Red Flags & Emergencies

Myxoedema Coma (Rare)

Life-threatening emergency. High mortality (25-60%). Usually elderly with undiagnosed hypothyroidism.

  • Lethargy / Coma
  • Hypothermia
  • Bradycardia / Seizures
  • Precipitant: Heart failure, sepsis, stroke
EMERGENCY ADMISSION

Untreated Overt Hypothyroidism in Pregnancy

Significant risks to mother and fetus if untreated.

  • Risk of miscarriage, pre-eclampsia, stillbirth
  • Adverse neurocognitive development
  • Preterm delivery, low birthweight
URGENT REFERENCE TO SPECIALIST

Definitions

Overt Hypothyroidism (OH)
Primary TSH > Ref Range & FT4 Low
Pregnancy TSH > Trimester Range
Subclinical Hypothyroidism (SCH)
TSH > Ref Range, but T3 & T4 Normal.
Secondary (Central): Pituitary/Hypothalamic disorder. TSH low/normal, FT4 low.

Common Causes

Primary
  • Autoimmune (Hashimoto's/Atrophic)
  • Iodine Deficiency (Global)
  • Post-ablative/Surgery
Drugs
  • Amiodarone
  • Lithium
  • Anti-thyroid drugs
  • Interferon-alpha
Note: Check meds (kelp supplements, biotin) affecting TFTs.

Prognosis

Primary: Good. Full recovery with Levothyroxine (usually lifelong). 5-10% have persistent symptoms.

Subclinical: May normalize (6-35%). Risk of progression to overt ~5%/year. Higher risk if TSH > 10 or antibodies positive.
