Haemochromatosis Reference

CRITICAL ACTION REQUIRED

  • !
    Ferritin > 1000 ng/mL: High risk of organ damage. Screen for cirrhosis & HCC immediately.
  • !
    Cardiac Symptoms: If patient presents with cardiomyopathy/conduction abnormalities, order Cardiac MRI & Echocardiogram.
  • !
    Hepatocellular Carcinoma: Risk is elevated in cirrhosis. If AFP elevated or mass on imaging, refer to Hepatology/Oncology.
Diagnostic Thresholds
Diagnostic

Check Phenotypic Hallmarks. If high, proceed to Genetic Testing (HFE C282Y).

Goal: <45% (F) / >50% (M)
Goal: >300 (M) / >200 (F)
Status Text Here
Confirm with: HFE Genotyping (C282Y Homozygosity) + Liver MRI/Biopsy if fibrosis suspected.
Disease Staging (Type 1)
Classification

Asymptomatic / Biochemical Only

Stage 0: Normal TSAT & Ferritin.

Stage 1: Increased TSAT (>45%), Normal Ferritin.

Action: Monitor annually (Stage 1) or every 3 years (Stage 0).

Iron Overload (No Symptoms)

Increased TSAT (>45%) AND Elevated Ferritin (>300 M / >200 F).

No clinical symptoms.

Action: Start Phlebotomy regimen.

Early Clinical Disease

Elevated TSAT & Ferritin + Symptoms affecting QOL (arthralgia, asthenia, impotence).

Action: Phlebotomy. Symptom management.

Advanced Organ Damage

Elevated Labs + Organ Damage (Cirrhosis, Diabetes, Cardiomyopathy).

Action: Aggressive Iron Depletion. Manage complications. Screen for HCC.
Key Differentials
Condition Clue
Chronic Transfusion History of hematologic disorder, anemic.

Management Protocol
Action Plan

1. Lifestyle Modification (All Patients)

  • Avoid: Iron supplements, Vitamin C supplements (increases absorption), Excess Alcohol.
  • Infection Risk: Avoid raw shellfish / seawater contact (Vibrio vulnificus risk).
  • Vaccinate: Hepatitis A & B if not immune.

2. Induction Phase (Iron Depletion)

Target: Reduce ferritin to 50-100 ng/mL.

  • P Phlebotomy: 7 mL/kg (max 550 mL) Weekly (or biweekly if cardiac issues).
  • M Monitoring: Check Hb/MCV before each session. Pause if Hb < 11 g/dL.
  • F Ferritin Check: Every 10-12 phlebotomies.

3. Maintenance Phase

Target: Maintain ferritin 50-100 ng/mL.

  • P Phlebotomy: Intermittent (usually 2-6 times/year).
  • D Diet: Low-iron diet may reduce frequency of phlebotomy.

4. Alternative (If Phlebotomy Contraindicated)

e.g., Anemia, severe cardiac disease, poor venous access.

  • C Chelation: Deferasirox (Oral) - monitor renal/hepatic function. Desferrioxamine (Parenteral).
  • E Erythrocytapheresis: Alternative procedure, fewer sessions needed.
Follow-up Monitoring
Parameter Action
Liver Fibrosis Non-invasive tests (APRI, FIB-4, Elastography) at diagnosis.
Diabetes Fasting Blood Sugar (HbA1c unreliable during phlebotomy).
Hypogonadism Testosterone, FSH, LH assays.
Heart ECG & Echo if ferritin raised. Cardiac MRI if symptomatic.
Bone Density If risk factors present (1/4 patients have osteoporosis).
Family Screening
Prevention

Siblings: 25% chance of having haemochromatosis.

Recommendation: Test adult first-degree relatives (Genetic testing + Iron studies).
