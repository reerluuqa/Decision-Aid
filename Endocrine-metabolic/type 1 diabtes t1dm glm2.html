Type 2 Diabetes

Quick-Scan Clinical Reference
Critical Flags & Immediate Actions
Check Urine Ketones IF symptomatic

Symptoms Requiring Ketone Check:

  • Polyuria / Polydipsia
  • Volume depletion (dry mucous membranes, poor turgor)
  • Unintentional weight loss

DKA Triggers in T2DM:

  • Acute Infection
  • Cardiovascular event
  • SGLT2 Inhibitors (concomitant use)
Diagnosis Criteria (WHO)

Note: Asymptomatic patients require repeat confirmatory test on subsequent day.

≥7.0 Fasting Plasma Glucose (mmol/L) (≥126 mg/dL)
≥11.1 Random / 2hr OGTT (mmol/L) (≥200 mg/dL)
≥48 HbA1c (mmol/mol) (≥6.5%)

Type 1 vs Type 2 Differentiation

Feature Type 1 (Suspect) Type 2 (Suspect)
Age < 35 years (usually) > 40 years (usually)
BMI Often Normal/Low Overweight/Obese (>25)
Onset Rapid, severe symptoms Gradual, asymptomatic
Autoantibodies Positive (GAD, IA-2) Negative
Ketones Common (DKA) Rare (unless severe stress)
Screening & Risk

STRONG RISK FACTORS

Age > 40 BMI > 25 (23 if Asian) South Asian/Black/Hispanic Gestational Diabetes Hx PCOS Hypertension/Dyslipidaemia CVD

INITIAL INVESTIGATIONS

  • HbA1c
  • Fasting Lipid Profile
  • Urine ACR (Albumin:Creatinine)
  • BP Measurement
  • Refer Eye Screening
  • Foot Risk Assessment
Check FIB-4 Index for liver fibrosis (MASLD)
Management Overview

Lifestyle & Prevention

Structured education + individualized nutrition plan.

  • Weight loss (targets >5-10%)
  • Smoking cessation
  • Physical activity (150 min/week)
  • Mediterranean diet recommended

First Line

Metformin

Standard initial therapy unless contraindicated (e.g., severe CKD).

Targets: Individualized HbA1c.

Review every 3-6 months until stable.

High Risk / Organ Protection

Indicated for patients with ASCVD, Heart Failure, or CKD.

SGLT2 Inhibitors

Renal & Heart Failure benefits.

GLP-1 Agonists

Stroke & ASCVD benefits. Weight loss effect.

DKA Protocol

Indications for immediate action: Polyuria, polydipsia, volume depletion, shock.

Immediate Steps:
  1. Check Urine Ketones.
  2. If positive and symptomatic -> Treat as DKA.
  3. Fluid resuscitation (Saline 0.9%).
  4. IV Insulin infusion.
  5. Treat underlying precipitating cause (Infection, MI).

Note: SGLT2 inhibitors increase risk of euglycaemic DKA. Pause if patient is acutely ill.
